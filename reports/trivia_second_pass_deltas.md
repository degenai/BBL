# Trivia 2nd-pass deltas — what the second push found

Tracking only NEW material vs first pass. As of 2026-05-18.

## Drone bundle (BBL002)

### Workshop Assistant (CMR 348)
- Kaladesh's "Aether Cycle" is a 5-stage caste system: Inspire / Innovate / **Build** / Liberate / Reclaim. The Build phase is the labor layer — "hardworking manufacturers." Dwarves are Kaladesh's designated Build caste. Workshop Assistant lives inside the Build economy.
- Inventor society **Countless Gears** specializes in "the smallest automatons and thopters" — Workshop Assistant's spider form fits their product profile exactly.
- Workshop Assistant + Junk Diver = documented infinite combo at common rarity (Pauper-relevant). 25+ combo lines via CommanderSpellbook.
- Mark Rosewater on CMR reprint philosophy: "using the reprints at lower rarities to reprint cards that helped make those themes work together" — Workshop Assistant is the archetype.
- Artist correction: **Victor Adame Minguez**, not Brian Snoddy. ~163 MTG credits. Painted in oils on MDF after digital transition. Grist Hunger Tide original sold $35k in 2021.
- KLR printing is Arena-only — no paper copies exist.

### Phyrexian Ironworks (MH3 130)
- **Slobad arc full body-horror**: Memnarch amputated Slobad's limbs + wired him into the spark-transfer machine for 5 YEARS. He was the involuntary architect of the device meant to strip Mirrodin inhabitants of sparks. When fired, the spark transferred to him; he gave it up to resurrect the dead. Later killed by panicked goblins, body sank into the plane's core, compleated by New Phyrexia, reshaped his Phyrexian form to resemble his dead friend Bosh. "We have made a friend today" reads as either propaganda or broken optimism.
- Naming lineage: "Phyrexian Ironworks" echoes Krark-Clan Ironworks (Fifth Dawn 2004) — same Mirrodin-block goblin clan Slobad served. Structural parallel implying machine compleated.
- Artist Mathias Kollros: exactly 2 MH3 cards (this + Petrifying Meddler — one Phyrexian, one Eldrazi). RTR 2012 debut → 173+ cards.
- Commander niche: highest inclusion is energy commanders (Pia Nalaar 24%, Liberty Prime 17%) — NOT Phyrexian tribal, despite the name.

### Will-Forged Golem (M15 239)
- **KILLER: artist Jason Felix admitted plagiarism in 2021** — used Kitt Lapeña's Nicol Bolas fanart + Raymond Swanland's Ugin art in the Crux of Fate Mystical Archive. Wizards suspended future work; he finished outstanding commissions by June 2021 and hasn't been contracted since. An artist defunded by the institution = on-thesis for the labor bundle.
- M15 was the first core set with NAMED GUEST DESIGNER credits (15 video-game devs incl Notch from Minecraft, Rob Pardo, George Fan). Felix's WFG was NOT a guest-designed card.
- Convoke RULES CHANGED in M15: original Ravnica 2005 was cost-reduction, M15 made it alternative-cost payment. M15 expanded to all 5 colors + artifacts. Will-Forged Golem is one of only 2 artifact Convoke cards in M15.
- Convoke is now "deciduous" (Rosewater Blogatog June 2024) — Ravnica → Future Sight → M15 → GRN → MH1 → MH3.

### Clockwork Servant (ELD 216)
- **David Palumbo is Julie Bell's SON and Boris Vallejo's STEPSON.** Pennsylvania Academy of Fine Art. Chesley Award + 3 Spectrum medals. 127+ MTG cards since 2008 Alara debut. Clockwork Servant is his ONLY ELD card.
- Henge Walker (ELD 221) is the deliberate parallel artifact: same {3} colorless cost, same Adamant framework, different payoff (counter vs draw). ELD Vision Design Handoff Part 2 documents "conscious shift to more colored artifacts" that Adamant was built to execute.
- Direct lineage card: **Clockwork Gnomes (Homelands 1995, HML 102)** — same Artifact Creature — Gnome type, built as mechanical servants at Feroz's school on Ulgrotha. Flavor by Baki the Wizard Attendant: "Until I came to the Floating Isle, I never had to oil the servants." Servant-labor framing runs 1995 → 2019 continuously. Fewer than 33 Gnome-subtype cards in all MTG history.
- Correction to first pass: said "Clockwork Gnome (Urza's Saga)" — wrong set. It's Homelands.

### Iron Bully (WAR 240)
- **Commander Grozdan is a REAL named character** — Boros Legion minotaur from Guildmasters' Guide to Ravnica (2018 D&D sourcebook), Ordruun bloodline, Kamen Fortress anti-Rakdos enforcer under Aurelia. First pass called him a "minor NPC" — wrong. The flavor line reads differently knowing the speaker: a Boros enforcer mid-invasion with no time for philosophy.
- **Reprinted in Double Masters (2XM 262, August 2020)** — first pass said "first and only printing"; wrong.
- WAR colorless-common artifact-creature trio: Iron Bully (240), Prismite (242), Saheeli's Silverwing (243). Three cards, three artists, contiguous collector numbers. Iron Bully is the only one with a pump trigger.
- Aaron Miller: 164 MTG illustrations by 2026 (Theros 2013 debut with *Chained to the Rocks*, Prometheus-themed). GenCon Best in Show 2013 + Juror's Choice 2010. Spectrum inclusions.

### Yotian Soldier (BBD 249)
- **KILLER: same artist (Luca Zontini) made Yotian Soldier AND Skullclamp.** The humblest defensive common AND MTG's most aggressively banned equipment, both Mirrodin-era. Zontini's MTG run: Mercadian Masques 1999 → Battlebond 2018 (19 years, ~64 unique credits).
- Christopher Rush — original Antiquities artist — designed the mana symbols + co-designed MTG's logo with Jesper Myrfors. Died Feb 10 2016. Every Yotian Soldier printed after that date carries his mana-symbol design.
- BRO 2022: MaRo confirmed Yotian Soldier was considered for reprint but cut (retro artifact bonus sheet covered the slot). BRO instead introduced FOUR new Yotian-named cards: Frontliner, Medic (Queen Kayla bin-Kroog flavor), Dissident, Tactician. A 1994 common spawned a four-card lore family 28 years later.

### Drone Rotom (Vivid Voltage 151)
- **Pulseman lineage** — Rotom's electricity-possession concept references Pulseman (Game Freak/Sega 1994, Sugimori directed). Volt Tackle derives from Pulseman's "Volteccer." Designer connection across the founding era.
- Each Rotom form had a separate designer: base by Sugimori, Heat by Lee HyunJung, Fan by Motofumi Fujiwara, Mow by Yusuke Ohmura, Wash by Hiroki Fuchino, Frost by Hironobu Yoshida. NO separate designer credit for the non-battling Drone form.
- JN115 ("Curtain Up! Fight the Fights!") is episode 1,200 of the Pokemon anime. Five Drone Rotom form the WCS logo in smoke above Wyndon Stadium, opening the Masters Eight Tournament.
- Artist is 5ban Graphics (3D CG studio in Creatures Inc., co-IP holder w/ Game Freak + Nintendo). Active since Black & White (Dec 2010 JP). 1,700+ cards by SwSh era. Community reception polarized.
- Sister card: Rotom Phone (Champion's Path 064/073) — same Item-card-as-Rotom design, mirror axis (your deck vs opponent's hand).

### FFTCG Golem (Opus VIII 8-008C)
- **KILLER: Artist Yasuhisa Izumisawa was a Magic player.** From 2019 Square Enix interview: *"For a while, I was obsessed with Magic: The Gathering. A lot of money and long nights went into playing that game."* In 2025 Wizards absorbed FF IP into Universes Beyond ($200M day 1, highest-grossing MTG set ever). The FFTCG artist played Magic before Magic ate his world.
- Izumisawa career arc: pixel art on Xenogears → monster design SaGa Frontier 2 → FFIX weapons/gadgets → FFX monsters/textures → main character designer on the FFCC series. Opus VIII was his first finished single-illustration card work.
- FFCC: Echoes of Time (2009) sold ~570K worldwide. Metacritic 75 DS / 64 Wii. FFTCG dedicating a showcase + artist interview to it = curatorial affection, not market demand.
- Golem in FFCC:EoT has tiers — base / Bolt / Magic / Grappler. The 8-008C art is the base tier (no elemental markers).
- Prior FFTCG Golem: 1-106C, Opus I 2016. Earth-element Backup from the general FF bestiary. Two distinct Golem commons in the FFTCG history.

### Eldrazi Repurposer (MH3 150)
- **Rosewater Blogatog on Spawn vs Scion (2024)**: "1/1 scions were better for the more normal set where the creature matters more, and 0/1 spawns were better for the higher powered set where the cost discount and the mana ability were more exciting." MH3 deliberately reverted to Spawn from BFZ's Scion. Repurposer's double-trigger makes it the premier Spawn engine.
- General Tazri arc: 3 card printings spanning a decade. General Tazri (OGW 2016, allied resistance to Ulamog) → Tazri Beacon of Unity (ZNR 2020, post-Eldrazi rebuilder of Sea Gate, capable city planner) → Tazri Stalwart Survivor (MOM Aftermath 2023, Phyrexian invasion). Flavor text's "allied commander" rank is the OG 2016 title — a soldier's long institutional memory.
- Post-MH3 Pauper format: Gruul Eldrazi / Gruul Ponza became a defining archetype. Repurposer's 3/3 body + double-Spawn trigger is core. MTGO Pauper League 5-0s, Copa Rio Pauper Top 4.
- Daren Bader: Tempest 1997 debut, 193 unique credits across 68 sets. Notable: Akroma Angel of Fury, Finale of Devastation, Enduring Ideal. Traditional media (oils/acrylics on illustration board).

### Charity Extractor (WAR 81)
- **AD Dawn Murin's brief was different from the final painting.** Spec'd a knight on horseback holding an offering plate with a gold coin necklace. Stewart departed: no horse, no offering plate — the keys and lockbox were his own additions to underline the templar's seriousness about collection.
- Stewart's reference workflow for this COMMON: posed in full armor himself, sourced horse-armor photographs from the Wallace Collection in London, used a Christmas ornament for the Orzhov gold tone, photographed the Palace of Westminster for the background architecture.
- Matt Stewart at WAR release: 245 MTG cards total. Same release: his Nicol Bolas God-Pharaoh mythic edition oil (24x30") sold for $35,000. A common and a $35k centerpiece, same artist, same date.
- WAR's only two black lifelink commons: Banehound (1/1 haste {B}) and Charity Extractor (1/5 {3}{B}). Opposite design poles.
- Limited reception: 1/5 with no evasion couldn't attack planeswalkers in WAR's PW-saturated format = widely considered underdraftable.

### Pitiless Pontiff (RNA 194)
- **"Pontiff" is a NAMED INSTITUTIONAL RANK in Orzhov hierarchy** — not generic ecclesiastical flavor. Sits between the advokist/ministrant class and the Obzedat ghost council. Pontiff commands a staff of 2d6 ministrants with attendant knights and syndics, holds privilege of direct communication with the Obzedat.
- The cathedral backdrop = ORZHOVA (Cathedral Opulent / "Church of Deals"), Orzhov guildhall on the western edge of Tenth District Plaza. Architecture deliberately makes visitors feel small. Bejeweled catacomb below housing the mummified Obzedat. Robed "Gray Sisters" perform menial labor.
- Yongjae Choi painted BOTH Pitiless Pontiff AND Kaya Orzhov Usurper in same RNA — guild's vampire enforcement rank AND its incoming planeswalker ruler, one artist, one release.
- RNA's narrative moment: Teysa Karlov (Grand Envoy) was mid-coup with Boros knight Tajic, foiled, stripped of office. The pontiff tier was the enforcement layer holding during the coup. RNA closes with Kaya purging the Obzedat.
- Previewed by Limited Resources podcast.

### Wicked Guardian (ELD 109)
- **"Wicked" prefix is a deliberate ELD design signal for fairy-tale antagonists.** Wicked Guardian (stepmother / Cinderella cycle) + Wicked Wolf (Three Little Pigs). Jay Annelli's CoolStuffInc Flavor Gems piece (2019) names Wicked Guardian as the custodian figure.
- Card belongs to Locthwain court: Persistence virtue is "persistent efforts at serving the queen" — the nobility-exploiting-servant dynamic in the art is canon to the court's logic.
- Noble subtype reintroduction (per first pass) added Wicked Guardian as part of the inaugural new-print Noble wave.
- ELD Draft: SCG ranked 3rd-best black common (behind Bake into a Pie, Reave Soul). Reprinted in Jumpstart 2022 J22 no. 489.
- Matt Stewart: 3x Chesley Award winner, 245+ cards, Parsons-trained, Tolkien-influenced personal work, oil-on-gessobord for ELD.

### Lawmage's Binding (RNA 190)
- Azorius Senate has a three-column hierarchy: **Jelenn / Lyev / Sova** (lawmages belong to Lyev). Triangle-crest encoding on apparel.
- **Pacifism's earliest printing is Mirage (Oct 1996), NOT Alpha 1993.** First pass said "Alpha." 42 total printings to date.
- Hipsters' "Designs of RNA" specifically names Temporal Isolation (Time Spiral) as the disqualified-near-precedent — structural footgun discussed.
- Detain (RTR 2012 keyword) → Lawmage's Binding (RNA 2019 enchantment): same functional clause, different permanence.
- Jumpstart 2020 Rainbow deck: Binding is the deck's sole creature lockdown (full deck contents confirmed).

### Klang (Darkness Ablaze 126)
- **Designer Ken Sugimori on the brief**: "something inorganic" with face-change-on-rotation logic. Magneton/Dugtrio as the structural model. Source: Lava Cut Content's Nintendo Dream 2011 interview translations.
- **Gen 5 "Object Pokemon" controversy**: Klink/Klang/Klinklang were in the eye of a real design-discourse fight in 2010-2012, alongside Trubbish, Vanillite, etc. PokeCommunity + Smogon discourse documented.
- Pokedex DEATH ENTRY (Black 2 / White 2 / Y): "if the minigear it launches at a foe doesn't return, it will die." Two diametrically-opposite text entries about the same mechanic across the game.
- NC Empire = studio under "Association for Design and Illustration," 73+ TCG credits, SM Promos origin, also credited on the BEYBLADE X logo (crossover commercial studio, not Pokemon-house).
- Plasma Klinklang competitive: Michael Slutsky Top 32 2013 US Nationals with EX-immunity Max Potion / Cobalion shell.
- Anime BW063: Klink couldn't evolve because of a pen stuck in its gears by Sunglasses Krokorok in a prior episode. (The pen detail is gold.)

### Miku "Factory Tyrant" (PD/S22-E027)
- **cosMo@BousouP is credited as the module's COSTUME DESIGNER, not just composer.** Atypical for Project DIVA (SEGA normally handles in-house). The black peaked cap, CD brooch, and cogwheel cuffs all carry his design credit.
- cosMo bio: born 1986 Tokyo, animator by primary occupation, influenced by BEMANI rhythm games. Niconico nickname: "High-Speed Development Miku Master" (compositions exceed 200 bpm).
- Western release was driven by a SEGA social-media campaign: a 2013 PlayStation Blog post asking fans to like/share for a western PS3 release got 25,000+ likes / 15,000+ shares in 3 days. Project DIVA F PS3 hit US Aug 27 2013. Weiss Schwarz English PD/S22 = 3 months later (Nov 2013).
- PD F PS Vita Japan: opened at #1 with 158,607 first-week copies, ~390K shipped by April 2013. SEGA devs publicly conceded the budget "may have been too big" — outsold by Persona 4: Golden in the same window.
- Factory Tyrant module was released as SEGA SPM arcade-prize figure in March 2017 (240mm), and added to Project DIVA Arcade Future Tone May 28 2015 — extending its commercial life 3 years past the original game.
- "Sadistic.Music∞Factory" original Niconico upload (Aug 27 2012) = ~592K views. 2021 V4X YouTube remake crossed 1M views. Song still culturally active 14 years later.

### Charitable Levy (MH3 21)
- **"Collection counter" is UNIQUE to Charitable Levy** in the Scryfall corpus — full oracle search returns exactly one result. (First pass said it originated on Evelyn, the Covetous; the counter NAME was reused but Charitable Levy is the only ENCHANTMENT bearing it.)
- Noncreature-spell-tax lineage: **Glowrider (Legions 2003)** is the earliest creature version, identically worded. Thalia Guardian of Thraben (2012) is the canonical staple. Charitable Levy is the first ENCHANTMENT with built-in self-destruct threshold (3 triggers).
- Architecturally distinct from Smothering Tithe: both white enchantment taxation, opposite philosophies — Smothering Tithe is infinite accumulator (mana engine), Charitable Levy is self-liquidating tempo speed-bump (cantrip + land tutor).
- Eli Minaya: approached by AD Zack Stella in 2021 for DMU debut. His self-stated dream commission: "a set of lands with really abstract and textured takes on Swamps and Plains."

### Tithebearer Giant (WAR 107) — CORRECTIONS + DEEP LORE
- **CORRECTION: 3 printings, not 2.** WAR (2019) → Jumpstart JMP/284 (July 2020) → Ravnica: Clue Edition CLU/124 (Feb 2024). First pass missed JMP.
- **TEYSA KARLOV BACKSTORY** (changes everything): she was IMPRISONED by the Obzedat for a failed conspiracy with Boros knight Tajic to expose the Council's corruption and seize power. Stripped of her rank as Grand Envoy. Freed by RNA. The planeswalker Kaya then ASSASSINATED THE OBZEDAT ON HER BEHALF (during a distraction staged by Ral Zarek, Hekara, Vraska). Post-WAR Triumvirate: human pontiff Armin Morov, vampire tithe-master Slavomir Zoltan, angel executioner Maladola. Teysa's flavor text reads differently knowing she spent years imprisoned for trying to dismantle the very council whose gold chests now bludgeon Bolas's armies.
- A "tithebearer" is a specific Orzhov enforcer-class role — physically carries and delivers tithes. Distinct from "tithe-master." Hierarchy: Obzedat → cardinals → advokists → priests → enforcers → THRULLS (made of flesh with mask-like faceplates of devalued coinage).
- Community: "Tithebearer Giant is Phyrexian Rager's big brother" — identical ETB (draw card, lose 1 life). Same black-cantrip-creature cycle dating to Apocalypse (2001).
- Wisnu Tan also painted Sower of Discord (Commander 2018) — pre-WAR Orzhov-tonal black demon. Total catalog ~62-73 cards.

### Henge Walker (ELD 221)
- **KILLER: ICE detained Titus Lunter in 2018.** Hipsters of the Coast: Aug 2018, Lunter (Dutch) was detained at Sea-Tac alongside Anna Steinbauer + Magali Villeneuve, all three WotC-contracted artists flying to Wizards HQ in Renton for D&D concept work. All three had ESTA permanently revoked + were deported. Wizards made no public statement.
- Garenbrig + Great Henge were built by GIANTS before humans rose. King Yorvo (Giant Noble) rules the court. Henge functions as a portal: aligned monoliths create a magical gateway to the depths of the Wilds at specific sunrise/sunset. Earning the right to move monoliths = Garenbrig knighthood.
- **~1000x price gap** between The Great Henge ($63.89 May 2026) and Henge Walker ($0.06). Same location, two outputs.
- Wilds of Eldraine (2023) story-spotlight: protagonists travel to Garenbrig to request Yorvo's permission to USE the Great Henge as a portal. The flavor text gains a second register: animator of constructs AND literal gateway.
- Lunter's full ELD batch (6 cards): Charmed Sleep, Witch's Vengeance, Wildwood Tracker, Henge Walker, Prophet of the Peak, Castle Locthwain. Castle Locthwain is the high-value hit (~$3.58).

### Mortify (RNA 192)
- **FIVE distinct flavor texts across 29 printings** — far more than first pass noted:
  - GPT (Glen Angus 2006): visceral prose poem, no speaker
  - DDK era (Nils Hamm 2013-2018): Sorin Markov quip — *"Many who cross Sorin's path come down with a sudden and fatal case of being-in-the-way-of-a-millennia-old-vampire."*
  - RNA / ONC / DMC / MOC (Anthony Palumbo): the Hilgur-the-Orzhov-euthanist text
  - Foundations Starter (Nils Hamm 2024): generic high fantasy — *"The queen sent her champion to defeat the necromancer threat. His helmet was returned the next day, warped and stained with blood."*
  - SCH 21 promo (Andreia Ugrai 2023): NO flavor text
- **Astarion's Thirst Secret Lair** — D&D 50th Anniversary Superdrop, Aug 27 2024. Bartek Fedyczak, inverted borderless frame, flavor: "Can you feel death's cold grip?" Five-card vampire suite contains an Exquisite Blood + Sanguine Bond infinite combo.
- LotR Commander printing: Sam Gamgee cooking rabbits flavor (Of Herbs and Stewed Rabbit, TTT Book IV Ch4) — domesticity mapped onto a kill spell.
- FFVI surgefoil: anonymous Imperial soldier watching Espers extracted into Magicite — direct game-extraction narrative parallel for this bundle.
- "Orzhov euthanist" formally defined in Guildmaster's Guide to Ravnica (2018 WotC/D&D): "an assassin euphemistically called a euthanist who brings speedy ends to lives deemed to have gone on too long." 13-year canon arc from Guildpact 2006 → GGtR 2018 → RNA 2019.

### Eye Collector (ELD 86)
- **MaRo confirmed Rankle's design is BOTH a "Deal-Making Imp / nod to Rumpelstiltskin" AND the plane's Puck figure from A Midsummer Night's Dream.** Eye Collector's tribute mechanic sits squarely in the Rumpelstiltskin extraction vein.
- Rankle does NOT appear as a creature in Wilds of Eldraine 2023 — his WOE presence is the spell Rankle's Prank (WOE 102, flavor: "The louder they scream, the harder he laughs"). Returns as creature in March of the Machine (MOM 252, Rankle and Torbran). Wizards story "The Adventures of Rankle, Master of Love" covers the arc.
- **6-year reprint gap**: Eye Collector's FIRST reprint was Foundations Jumpstart J25 (2025), card 439. Rankle Master of Pranks has been reprinted 3+ times (CMM, OTC, Alchemy variant) — Rankle has 1,000+ Commander decks as general (rank 1,294). Mythic demand drove his reprints while the common courier waited.
- Uriah Voth: 67 Gatherer credits as of 2026. ELD credits: Eye Collector + Bramblefort Fink only.

### Secure the Scene (M21 35)
- **Judith the Scourge Diva as color-anomalous speaker**: across ~15 cards Judith is quoted on, nearly all carry Rakdos identity (R, B, or BR). Secure the Scene (mono-W) appears to be the ONLY outlier — the joke lands because she's the maximally wrong guild-voice to comment on an Azorius arrest.
- **Plane Shift: Ravnica (WotC official D&D supplement, 2018)** canonically describes Azorius magic as "blue or golden runes floating and glowing in the air in circular patterns or shimmering azure barriers." The luminous bindings in the art are official house visual for Azorius enforcement.
- Boros-Szikszai partnership ran 1986–2010 (Boros being the artist Zoltan Boros + partner Gabor Szikszai). Zoltan relocated to New York at dissolution. M21's Secure the Scene is a decade-into-solo-career commission. Both artists still co-sign convention cards.
- Sole printing — Scryfall confirms reprint: false, one result. Not a Ravnica-port; tonal borrowing only.

### Tithe Drinker (DGM 109)
- **Extort mechanic origin**: Shawn Main designed it OUTSIDE the Gatecrash team during devign. Original cost was 1 generic mana — changed to {W/B} hybrid by development. MaRo drafted 14 extort cards to convince skeptics. Source: Wizards "Gatecrashing the Party Part 3" + "Designing for Orzhov."
- {W/B} appears in REMINDER TEXT ONLY — mono-colored extort cards (Blind Obedience) are legal in mono-black Commander. Judge-ruled.
- Reprinted in Commander 2017 (no. 200, "Vampiric Bloodlust" precon) + The List (PLST C17-200). C17 vampire-tribal shell explains the EDH data.
- **Slawomir Maniak has 222+ MTG cards; Tithe Drinker is his ONLY Orzhov-watermark card.** All his other vampire work is Innistrad.
- Dragon's Maze was one of the weakest sets ever — prerelease attendance ~50% down from Gatecrash. MTGSalvation Pauper review called Tithe Drinker "the BEST common in this set."

### Indebted Spirit (MH3 31)
- **Rosewater Blogatog**: "Bestow is my favorite mechanic from OG Theros." Bestow Storm Scale 6-7.
- Bestow first appeared Theros Sept 2013 → Commander 2018 (only) → MH3 2024. Eleven years of absence.
- Afterlife from Ravnica Allegiance Jan 2019. Wizards official mechanics article frames it: "Just because someone's mortal form has diminished into disuse, the spirit within may be free to serve."
- **Indebted Spirit is the FIRST CARD to carry both Bestow AND Afterlife simultaneously.**
- Draftsim called it out by name: "Bestowing Indebted Spirit looks absolutely silly. For 3 mana, you get a +1/+1 aura, a 1/1 creature and two 1/1 fliers."
- Artist L.A. Draws (therealola.artstation.com) confirmed credits include Blood Artist (Innistrad Remastered), Whirling Quicksand, Demonic Contract, Hate Mirage, The Phasing of Zhalfir, Telling Time. No Hipsters profile found.

### Malevolent Noble (ELD 95)
- **TV Tropes classifies this card explicitly under "The Bluebeard" trope as an "Adaptational Heroism" variant** — applies Bluebeard's lure-then-kill structure to witches rather than wives, using their own child-appetite as bait. Specific source tale: Bluebeard (Perrault, 1697).
- **The bait works because ELD canon makes witches predatory toward children**. Wizards Planeswalker's Guide describes witches as "shadowy human warlocks ... who take great joy in their evil deeds" and steal children. The Noble's promise of "fresh children's bones" is credible IN-fiction.
- **Rosewater exact quote** on adding Noble subtype in ELD: "The game just has a lot of people whose job it is to rule over others, and it felt weird not to have a class that covered that. Throne of Eldraine was full of kings and queens, princes and princesses, so it seemed like the right time to finally add Noble." (More Odds & Ends: Throne of Eldraine, 2019-10-21)

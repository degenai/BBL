# Most Renowned Artists in Pokémon History

Covers both the core game/anime visual designers and the TCG illustrator roster. Pokémon has a smaller, more centralized artist culture than Magic — a handful of names did most of the heavy lifting.

## Core Game / Franchise Designers

### Ken Sugimori
- Lead artist of the entire Pokémon franchise since 1996.
- Painted the original watercolor illustrations of all 151 Gen 1 Pokémon — the images burned into a generation's brain.
- Art director on most main-series games. The "house style" of Pokémon *is* his style.
- Friend of Satoshi Tajiri since high school; co-founded Game Freak with him.

### Atsuko Nishida
- Designed **Pikachu**. Also Bulbasaur, Charmander, Squirtle's evolutions partly, Eevee, Espeon, many fan-favorites across every generation.
- Arguably more responsible for the *cute* direction of Pokémon design than anyone.

### Motofumi Fujiwara
- Designer of Lucario, Garchomp, lots of Sinnoh-era Pokémon.

### Yusuke Ohmura
- Lead character designer from Sun/Moon onward. Modern Pokémon look (Sword/Shield, Scarlet/Violet) is largely his.

### Hironobu Yoshida
- Long-time Game Freak art director, Pokémon designer across multiple gens.

### James Turner
- British designer at Game Freak — Vanillite line, Golurk, Trubbish, Dragapult. Distinct angular style.

### Shigeki Morimoto
- Programmer/designer — designed Mewtwo (and famously coded Mew into Red/Green/Blue as a secret).

## TCG Illustrators — The Heavyweights

### Mitsuhiro Arita
- Painted **Charizard** (Base Set 1999) — the most valuable mainstream Pokémon card in existence.
- Also Base Set Blastoise, Pidgey, Caterpie, and hundreds since.
- Still actively illustrating; runs his own studio. The "face" of Pokémon TCG art.

### Ken Sugimori (TCG)
- Many cards across every era use his official Pokédex artwork directly.
- His clean watercolor look defines the "official portrait" subset of cards.

### Kagemaru Himeno
- One of the longest-running TCG illustrators (1996–present).
- Iconic Neo-era and e-Card era art — Espeon, Umbreon, Lugia (Neo Genesis), countless trainers.
- Recognizable soft-edge painterly style.

### Keiji Kinebuchi
- Heavy hitter from the original Japanese sets onward — Mewtwo (Base Set, Japanese), trainer art.

### Naoyo Kimura
- Long-running illustrator with a distinct soft-realism style across multiple decades.

### Tomokazu Komiya
- Modern era — known for highly detailed, naturalistic Pokémon-in-environment cards.

### Yuka Morii
- **Clay sculpture cards** — illustrates by physically sculpting Pokémon in clay, photographing them, and compositing the image.
- Unique in the entire trading card industry. Adored by collectors.

### Mizue
- Painterly modern illustrator — Sword & Shield era environments and Pokémon portraits.

### Sumiyoshi Kizuki
- Sword & Shield/Scarlet & Violet era. Atmospheric character art.

### 5ban Graphics
- Studio (not a single artist) — produced most of the CGI/holographic Full Art and EX/GX/V card renders from BW era through Sword & Shield. Polarizing but historically dominant.

### Shin Nagasawa
- Recognizable long-running illustrator, soft anime style.

### Akira Komayama
- Vivid Voltage / Sword & Shield era — popular with collectors for Charizard art.

### Aya Kusube
- Modern era — atmospheric, moody compositions.

### Saya Tsuruta
- Modern era — popular Eeveelution cards.

### Ryota Murayama
- Frequent Charizard illustrator in modern era; collector favorite.

### Hideki Ishikawa
- Sword & Shield era trainer and Pokémon portraits.

## Anime Side

### Sayuri Ichiishi
- Long-time character designer for the anime — defined the look of human characters across the original series.

### Toshiya Yamada
- Anime character designer for later series.

## Manga Side

### Satoshi Yamamoto
- Illustrator of *Pokémon Adventures* (Pokémon Special) from Ruby/Sapphire arc onward. The definitive Pokémon manga look for two decades.

### Mato
- Original *Pokémon Adventures* artist (Red/Blue through Yellow arcs). Scratchy, expressive style.

## Outliers / Special Mentions

- **Junichi Masuda** — composer and director, not an artist, but his visual taste shaped a lot of Pokémon's identity.
- **Tomokazu Hayakawa** — early TCG illustrator, very rare cards.
- **Hajime Kusajima** — Neo era Japanese-exclusive promo art.
- **Eske Yoshinob** — modern collector-favorite for atmospheric cards.

## How "renown" is measured here
- Cultural footprint (Sugimori and Nishida designed the *characters themselves*, which dwarfs everything)
- Card market value tied to specific illustrators (Arita's Charizard, Komayama's Charizards)
- Longevity in the TCG roster (Arita and Himeno = 25+ years)
- Collector recognition on PokéBeach, Bulbapedia, PSA grading communities
- Distinctiveness of style (Yuka Morii's clay work is sui generis)

## Note on attribution
Pokémon historically credited illustrators only on the card itself in small print. Many early Japanese-only artists are under-documented in English sources — Bulbapedia and the Japanese Pokémon Card wiki are the best references for completist research.

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Myojin of Seeing Winds was released in Champions of Kamigawa (October 2004), the first set of the Kamigawa block. Kamigawa is a plane modeled on feudal Japan where the kami (spirit world) and the mortal world co-exist separated by a thin veil. The plane's defining conflict, the Kami War, was triggered by the daimyo Lord Konda stealing the unborn child of O-Kagachi — the kami of all things — causing every spirit, including the five Myojin, to turn against the mortal world in collective rage and grief. `[MTG Wiki: Kamigawa, via WebSearch]`
- **Cycle context** — Myojin of Seeing Winds is the blue member of the five-card Myojin cycle in Champions of Kamigawa, each representing one of Magic's five colors and one of five fundamental forces: justice (white), knowledge (blue), passion (red), power (black), and creation (green). The other members are Myojin of Cleansing Fire, Myojin of Night's Reach, Myojin of Infinite Rage, and Myojin of Life's Web. Each Myojin carries the same signature divinity-counter mechanic: indestructible while the counter is on it, with the counter removal triggering a massive one-time effect. `[WebSearch: Myojin MTG Wiki / Fandom]`
- **Mechanic note** — The divinity counter was a 2004 design workaround for granting indestructible. When Kamigawa was revisited in Neon Dynasty (2022), five new Myojin were printed for the Commander product using "indestructible counters" instead — a rules consolidation Wizards explicitly credited to how the mechanic would have been templated if designed today. The CHK originals remain the only cards in the game with "divinity counters" as a named token type. `[Wizards: Odds & Ends – Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty, via WebSearch]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — Myojin of Seeing Winds sees limited competitive play due to its {7}{U}{U}{U} cost (10 total mana), but maintains a niche Commander following as both a commander and 99 inclusion for blue artifact/permanent-spam strategies. Its activated ability — drawing one card per permanent you control — scales dramatically in decks built around clones, proliferation, and permanents-matter themes. EDHREC rank 12,427; tracked in approximately 79 Commander decks as the primary commander. `[Scryfall; EDHREC: Myojin of Seeing Winds, via WebSearch]`

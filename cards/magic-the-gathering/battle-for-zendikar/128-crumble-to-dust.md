## Trivia

- **Set context** — Crumble to Dust is a Devoid Sorcery from Battle for Zendikar (BFZ), released October 2, 2015. Despite its Red casting cost (3R), Devoid makes it colorless — no color at all. Devoid was a BFZ-specific keyword applied to Eldrazi-aligned cards representing the consuming corruption that strips the plane of its identity. The landscape art (Eldrazi transforming solid Zendikari stone into pale organic spire formations) is literal set lore: land itself is being unmade. [Scryfall; Draftsim: BFZ overview, via WebSearch]
- **Design lineage** — Crumble to Dust is a near-functional reprint of Sowing Salt (Betrayers of Kamigawa, 2005), which cost 2RR to exile a nonbasic land and all copies of it. Crumble to Dust costs 3R (one red pip instead of two), making it substantially easier to splash. The Devoid keyword also adds an unexpected upside: colorless spells are findable by Ancient Stirrings — meaning a Tron player could theoretically tutor their own Crumble to Dust to use in the mirror match. [Face to Face Games: Exclusive BFZ Preview — Crumble to Dust, via WebSearch; MTG Salvation preview discussion, via WebSearch]
- **Competitive context** — The card's primary competitive role is as a Modern sideboard answer to Urza Tron (Urza's Tower / Urza's Mine / Urza's Power Plant). Exiling one Tron land and finding all copies in the opponent's hand, library, and graveyard eliminates the entire Tron package in a single card. EDHREC rank 28,342 reflects minimal Commander adoption (land destruction is considered unfun in casual Commander), but in sideboard-heavy 60-card formats it has seen consistent representation during Tron-heavy metagames. [Scryfall: edhrec_rank; Tappedout: Crumble to Dust sideboard discussion, via WebSearch]
- **Design** — Illustrated by James Paick. No flavor text. The card's only print is BFZ (no. 128) — never reprinted. Legal in Historic, Pioneer, Modern, Legacy, Vintage, Commander, and Brawl. The Devoid card-frame treatment (colorless card frame within a red set) visually distinguishes it on the table. [Scryfall: artist, legalities, frame_effects fields]

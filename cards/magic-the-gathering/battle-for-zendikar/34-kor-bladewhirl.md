## Trivia

- **Set / race context** — Battle for Zendikar (BFZ, released 2015-10-02) depicts the full-scale Eldrazi invasion of Zendikar. The Kor are a nomadic humanoid race on Zendikar defined by their hook-and-rope culture — tools that serve both practical and sacred-symbolic functions. A hooked line is documented in Kor lore as a social and religious symbol representing the connection between individuals and to the plane itself `[Scryfall API; coolstuffinc.com: "Ecology of the Kor," via WebSearch]`.
- **Flavor text** — "The high-pitched whirl of a hook is the only battle cry she needs." `[Scryfall]` The text directly names the Kor's iconic weapon and frames it as sufficient for rallying — mirroring the card's mechanical keyword, Rally. The hook's dual role as weapon and sacred symbol gives the line more depth than its brevity suggests.
- **Mechanical reputation** — Rally triggers whenever this creature or another Ally enters, granting all your creatures first strike until end of turn. At {1}{W} for a 2/2, it was a reliable Limited workhorse in BFZ draft, and the mass first-strike line remains relevant in Pioneer Ally builds. Legal in Pioneer, Modern, Legacy, Commander. EDHREC rank 13,949 `[Scryfall]`.
- **Design** — Illustrated by Steven Belledin, a prolific MTG artist known for naturalistic portraiture — well-suited to the Kor's textured leather-and-rope aesthetic. This is the Kor Soldier Ally subtype, placing it in BFZ's explicitly multi-race resistance framing against the Eldrazi `[Scryfall]`.

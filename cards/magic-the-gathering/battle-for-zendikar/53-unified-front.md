## Trivia

- **Set context** — Battle for Zendikar (October 2, 2015) covers the second Eldrazi invasion of Zendikar under Ulamog. The Zendikari resistance — vampires under Drana, roilmages under Noyan Dar, ground troops under Tazri, goblins, kor, elven skyriders, and Planeswalkers Gideon and Nissa — converge against the Eldrazi tide. Unified Front's Converge mechanic and its production of Kor Ally tokens is a direct mechanical encoding of that multiracial coalition: mana from multiple colors of mana represents the many factions literally uniting. `[magic.wizards.com: Battle for Zendikar Story Summary (2015-12-23); Scryfall: BFZ-53 flavor_text]`
- **Flavor text** — "The kor's pride in their independence has given way to a strong sense of community that embraces all of Zendikar." — anonymous narrator. `[Scryfall: BFZ-53 flavor_text field]`
- **Design** — Mark Rosewater later assessed Converge as "a mechanic in the wrong environment" — it would have fared better in a block where color (not colorlessness) was the central tension. BFZ's dominant themes were Devoid and colorless Eldrazi mana, which actively pulled against Converge's multi-color reward structure. `[magic.wizards.com: Storm Scale: Zendikar and Battle for Zendikar (2016-11-21)]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — EDHREC rank 19881 is low overall, but within Ally kindred Commander decks the card punches above its weight: 35% inclusion with +34% synergy in Tazri, Beacon of Unity lists; 32% with +34% synergy in Rukarumel, Biologist ally lists. Converge-5 generates four Kor Ally tokens simultaneously, triggering multiple on-enter payoffs (Kazandu Blademaster, Sea Gate Loremaster, Kabira Evangel) at once. `[Scryfall: BFZ-53 edhrec_rank: 19881; EDHREC: Top 10 Most Played Converge Cards in Commander]`

## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — The card's name and draw effect reflect a core narrative thread of Battle for Zendikar: the Gatewatch and their allies discovering that the hedron network and leylines of Zendikar were the key to defeating the Eldrazi titans. Jori En's coastal expeditions — exploring Eldrazi-scarred shorelines in search of the hedron puzzle — are one of the block storyline's primary knowledge-recovery arcs. `[magic.wizards.com: The Believers' Pilgrimage, 2015-09-02]` `[coolstuffinc.com: Legends of Zendikar by Jay Annelli]`

- **Design** — Artist Kieran Yanner painted Coastal Discovery as a freelance illustrator in 2015. He subsequently joined Wizards of the Coast in February 2017 as Senior Art Director, sharing responsibilities formerly held by Jeremy Jarvis alongside Dawn Murin, Cynthia Sheppard, and Mark Winters. He left Wizards in December 2021. Coastal Discovery was painted before Yanner became the person who would commission and direct other artists' work for Magic. `[MTG Wiki: Kieran Yanner]`

- **Mechanical reputation** — Coastal Discovery is an uncommon sorcery: {3}{U} draws two cards, with Awaken 4 at {5}{U} that also produces a 4/4 land Elemental. Scryfall confirms no flavor text on the card. Not legal in Pauper (uncommon). EDHREC rank 21372, reflecting low Commander adoption — outcompeted by cheaper or instant-speed draw spells at similar mana investment. `[Scryfall]`

- **Mechanic edge case** — The Awaken 4 mode's 0/0-plus-four-counters structure means a second awaken cast targeting the same land stacks: two Awaken 4 hits produce an 8/8 Elemental land. This stacking behavior was explicitly called out in the official Wizards BFZ mechanics article as a feature of the 0/0-plus-counters design over a flat P/T. `[magic.wizards.com: Battle for Zendikar Mechanics]`

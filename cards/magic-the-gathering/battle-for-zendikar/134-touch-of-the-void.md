## Trivia

- **Set context** — Battle for Zendikar (released October 2, 2015) was the first set of its two-set block — the seventy-first English Magic expansion — centered on the Eldrazi titans Ulamog and Kozilek having been freed from their hedron prison and annihilating the plane. Touch of the Void sits at the intersection of story and mechanics: a red devoid sorcery that exiles rather than destroys, reflecting the Eldrazi's cosmological hunger. `[Scryfall API: released_at, set_name]` `[Wikipedia: Battle for Zendikar]`

- **Design (devoid)** — Devoid was introduced in BFZ as a solution to a color-pie problem. Mark Rosewater explained in his September 2015 Making Magic article: *"By having devoid spells, we are allowing ourselves to make colorless spells that still stay confined within a particular color. A red devoid spell gets to do red things because it still requires you to spend red mana."* The mechanic descends from Ghostfire (Future Sight, 2007) — the first colorless card with a colored cost — though Ghostfire represents Ugin's magic, not Eldrazi essence. Devoid cards received a distinctive frame: color over a hedron-texture background. The official WotC ruling states: *"The top part of the card features some color over a background based on the texture of the hedrons that once imprisoned the Eldrazi."* `[Wizards of the Coast: "Preparing for Battle, Part 2" (2015-09-14)]` `[Scryfall API: rulings (2015-08-25)]`

- **Design (exile clause)** — The exile-instead-of-destroy clause is thematic, not merely mechanical. Exile was the Eldrazi's primary keyword register throughout BFZ: ingest exiles from library, processors consume exiled cards, and devoid spells like this one exile creatures. A WotC ruling adds a notable interaction: *"A creature doesn't necessarily have to be dealt lethal damage by Touch of the Void to be exiled. After being dealt damage, if it would die for any reason that turn, it'll be exiled instead."* The touch marks, even if something else delivers the killing blow. `[Scryfall API: rulings (2015-08-25)]`

- **Flavor text** — `"Some wounds never heal."` Three words performing the full weight of Eldrazi horror: not death but erasure. No speaker is credited, matching the impersonal, non-sentient predation framing the design team applied throughout the block. `[Scryfall API: flavor_text]`

- **Mechanical reputation** — EDHREC rank 26204, placing this card in the long tail of Commander play. It sees stronger representation in Pauper and Pauper EDH (PauperCommander legal per Scryfall legalities), where exile-based red removal at common rarity is scarce enough to matter. Not reprinted since its original BFZ printing — keeping it aesthetically tied to the Battle for Zendikar visual identity. `[Scryfall API: edhrec_rank, legalities, reprint:false]`

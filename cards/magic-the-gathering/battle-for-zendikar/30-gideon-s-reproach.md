## Trivia

- **IP verified: Gideon Jura** — The depicted figure is canonically Gideon Jura: (1) the card name is "Gideon's Reproach" — possessive attribution to a named planeswalker; (2) the flavor text cites *The War Diaries*, the in-fiction Zendikari resistance chronicle that names Gideon as the actor ("Suddenly, Gideon was there with us"); (3) the art depicts his canonical golden energy sural — the whip-lasso weapon introduced in *Magic Origins* (2015) and consistent across every BFZ-era Gideon depiction — on a Zendikar floating-columns landscape [Scryfall; gideon-jura.md character node, BBL corpus].
- **Set context** — Gideon's Reproach is a common {1}{W} instant from *Battle for Zendikar* (BFZ, October 2015). In BFZ lore, Gideon Jura arrived on Zendikar after Ulamog's brood destroyed Sea Gate and organized the remaining Zendikari survivors — the kor, merfolk, elves, vampires, and goblins — into a coordinated resistance coalition. The card's effect (4 damage to an attacking or blocking creature) mechanically encodes Gideon's role as a front-line protector who intervenes to let civilians escape [Scryfall; Wizards of the Coast BFZ story summary, 2015-12-23, via WebSearch].
- **Flavor text** — "Suddenly, Gideon was there with us, clearing the way so that we could escape." — *The War Diaries*. *The War Diaries* is the in-fiction name for the Zendikari resistance's chronicle of the Eldrazi siege; the speaker is anonymous and civilian — Gideon is recalled as a rescue figure appearing without warning [Scryfall].
- **The Gideon attribution cycle** — "Gideon's Reproach" is one of ~10 cards in the canonical *Gideon's [Spell]* attribution cycle (Gideon's Avenger, Gideon's Defeat, Gideon's Lawkeeper, Gideon's Phalanx, Gideon's Sacrifice, Gideon's Triumph, Gideon's Intervention, Gideon's Resolve). This cycle spans his living-era cards and posthumous elegies after his canonical death in War of the Spark (2019). A notable cross-reference: Gideon's Reproach appears in *Dominaria* (DOM) as a reprint with updated flavor text noting "On Amonkhet, Gideon lost both his sural and his faith in himself" — the DOM reprinting acknowledges the BFZ-era art's sural-iconography from the *other* side of Gideon's arc [Scryfall].
- **Mechanical reputation** — Pioneer-, modern-, historic-, pauper-, and commander-legal [Scryfall]. EDHREC rank 21,283 reflects limited commander traction — a conditional removal spell at sorcery-like restriction (combat only) in a format that rarely needs cheap attackers stopped [Scryfall]. Sees occasional pauper sideboard play as cheap white conditional removal.

## Connections

- [[gideon-jura]] — Gideon's Reproach is this corpus's sole Battle for Zendikar depiction of Gideon Jura, captured in the Ally-of-Zendikar phase swinging his canonical golden-energy sural (the whip-lasso weapon introduced in Magic Origins 2015 and consistent across every BFZ-era printing) against Ulamog's brood on the floating-rock columns of Zendikar. The flavor text — *"Suddenly, Gideon was there with us, clearing the way so that we could escape." — The War Diaries* — frames him in the labor-coded register that defines his entire arc: the front-line protector who appears to rescue civilians at his own cost, foreshadowing the WAR sacrifice that resolves his survivor's-guilt thread from Theros. Among the ~10 cards in the canonical *Gideon's <Spell>* attribution cycle, Reproach sits in the living-era half (pre-WAR) while Sacrifice and Triumph occupy the death-and-elegy half — this card is the BBL corpus's Ally-of-Zendikar anchor for the broader character.

## Trivia

- **Set context and type line** — Ghostly Sentinel's type line is `Creature — Kor Spirit`, making it a spiritual ancestor of the kor rather than a generic ghost. Scryfall confirms this is the card's first and only printing [Scryfall]. The Stone Havens referenced in the flavor text are the canonical kor sacred refuge-settlements on Zendikar — networks of cliff-carved fastnesses where the kor preserve their history and call on their ancestral dead for protection during the Eldrazi siege [Scryfall flavor text; MTG Wiki: Kor, via WebSearch].
- **Flavor text** — "Mystics of the Stone Havens call upon the spirits of fallen heroes to defend the refuges." This directly frames Ghostly Sentinel as a conjured kor ancestor — the Stone Havens' mystics are performing a specific act of ancestral-spirit summoning to defend resistance refuges from the Eldrazi [Scryfall].
- **Mechanical profile** — A 3/3 with flying and vigilance for {4}{W}; pauper-legal and commander-legal [Scryfall]. EDHREC rank 29,001 is near floor — a strictly Limited-format card. The vigilance keyword mechanically encodes the flavor: an ever-watchful sentinel that never drops its guard, even when attacking.
- **Artist** — Daarken (Mike Lim), who has illustrated 200+ Magic cards since 2007 and has described his consistent dark-toned aesthetic as foundational to his professional identity. The Hipsters of the Coast "Behind the Brush" profile (2019) notes he started with D&D work before transitioning to Magic; the Ghostly Sentinel piece is consistent with the spectral/ethereal register of his body of work [Hipsters of the Coast, "Behind the Brush—Daarken," 2019-05-xx].

## Trivia

- **Set context: Blighted land cycle** — Blighted Cataract is the blue member of the five-card BFZ Blighted land cycle; each taps for colorless mana and sacrifices for a color-aligned effect. Full cycle: Blighted Fen (black, 230), Blighted Steppe (white, 232), Blighted Cataract (blue, 229), Blighted Woodland (green), Blighted Gorge (red, 231). The "blighted" naming explicitly marks the Eldrazi as the cause of each landscape's ruination. `[Scryfall: BFZ oracle tag cycle-bfz-blighted-land]`
- **Flavor text** — `"Once, water ran here. Now only dust and ash fall from the clifftops."` (no attribution) `[Scryfall]`. The unattributed narration functions as environmental witness testimony — no named speaker, making the loss feel geological and impersonal. Two cycle-mates (Blighted Fen and Blighted Steppe) attribute their flavor text to Greenweaver Mina; Blighted Cataract, Blighted Gorge, and Blighted Woodland do not, suggesting the cycle's flavor copy was assembled from different narrative voices rather than written as a single monologue. `[Scryfall: BFZ Blighted cycle]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — EDHREC rank 13109 — the highest-ranked card in the Blighted land cycle, and for a clear reason: {5}{U}, {T}, Sacrifice: draw two cards is raw card advantage usable in virtually any blue Commander deck as a late-game mana sink. The colorless {C} base tap means it works in any deck regardless of color identity. `[Scryfall: edhrec_rank, oracle_text]`
- **Artist** — Vincent Proce painted the desolate ravine landscape. Proce has contributed art to multiple BFZ/Oath of the Gatewatch cards and is known for environmental landscape work within Wizards' card art stable. `[Scryfall: artist field]`

# Fear of Burning Alive (Duskmourn: House of Horror)

## Vision

![135-fear-of-burning-alive](../../_images/magic-the-gathering/duskmourn-house-of-horror/135-fear-of-burning-alive.png)

A horrifying nightmare-beast lunges forward through a vaulted stone passage that glows like the inside of a furnace. The creature is quadrupedal but unnatural — long clawed limbs, a serpentine reptilian head with backswept horns, an open jaw screaming or roaring, and a body of charred muscle and exposed bone. Flames erupt from its mane, back, and joints in jagged orange-yellow tongues. Clinging to its flank is a grotesque mass of human skulls, mouths open in eternal screams, each individually wreathed in fire — souls of the burned trapped against its body. The architecture behind it is curved, brick-lined, suggestive of a kiln or oven. Palette is dominated by hot orange, red, gold, and deep shadow black; everything is lit from within by the fire it carries. The composition is dynamic and claustrophobic, the beast filling the frame in a forward lunge.

**Subject:** A nightmare creature — a charred, skeletal quadrupedal demon with horns and exposed sinew — rears in a stone archway. Its mane and body are wreathed in roaring flame, and a writhing cluster of screaming human skulls engulfed in fire is fused to its flank, as if it is carrying its victims with it.

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Duskmourn: House of Horror (set code DSK) released 2024-09-27 as a standard expansion; the plane is a single haunted house grown to swallow an entire world, ruled by the demon Valgavoth, and Wizards designed the set top-down from 1970s-1990s slasher and haunted-house horror cinema. `[Scryfall set page (dsk)]` `[Wizards: Duskmourn Vision Design Handoff Part 1, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Cycle membership** — The card sits inside the set's "Fear of..." Nightmare cycle: uncommon-and-up Enchantment Creature — Nightmare cards each naming a specific phobia and embodying it as a stalking creature. Confirmed cycle members across DSK visible on Scryfall include Fear of Abduction (no. 9, W), Fear of Surveillance (no. 11, W), Fear of Failed Tests (no. 55, U), Fear of Falling (no. 56, U), Fear of Isolation (no. 58, U), Fear of the Dark (no. 98, B), Fear of Being Hunted (no. 134, R), Fear of Burning Alive (no. 135, R), Fear of Missing Out (no. 136, R), and Fear of Infinity (no. 214, UB). The cycle spans all five colors with a multicolor capstone at 214. See [[fear-of-cycle]] for the corpus-anchored cycle node. `[Scryfall: set DSK, per-card pages]`
- **Design thesis** — Wizards' Duskmourn Vision Design Handoff, authored by Annie Sardelis (lead Vision designer) and published as a two-part Mark Rosewater Making Magic article, names suspense-over-shock as the set's core emotional thesis (Hitchcock-style stewing dread rather than jump-scare) and identifies enchantments as the card type best suited to express horror because of their lingering board presence. The Fear-of cycle is the most direct mechanical expression of that call: the phobia stays in play, stalking. `[Wizards: Duskmourn Vision Design Handoff Part 1, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — Six-mana {4}{R}{R} 4/4 Enchantment Creature — Nightmare whose ETB deals 4 to each opponent (an unconditional four-damage face-tax on entry), with a Delirium trigger that repeats the damage to a target creature each time a source you control deals noncombat damage to an opponent, gated on four-plus card types in the graveyard. Two damage-doublers in one frame: the ETB lands without conditions, the Delirium ride-along scales with any pinger or ETB-burn already in the deck. EDHREC rank 6,898 at retrieval (bulk-uncommon territory in Commander); Standard, Pioneer, Modern, Legacy, Vintage, Commander legal; Pauper not legal. Preview source DavyFJones on 2024-09-05. `[Scryfall]`
- **Flavor text** — None on the card. Scryfall's `flavor_text` field is empty, which is common in the Fear-of cycle when the phobia-name + the creature type are doing the flavor work themselves. The screaming-skull-cluster art plus the name "Fear of Burning Alive" carry the conceit unsupported. `[Scryfall: flavor_text field empty]`
- **Design (artist)** — Art by J.P. Targete, an award-winning freelance concept artist (books, video games, film) with 26-plus Magic credits across his career. At least three pieces inside DSK: Fear of Burning Alive (no. 135), Chainsaw (no. 128), and Arabella, Abandoned Doll (no. 208). His DSK work runs hot-palette and high-contrast — the kiln-glow lighting on this card and the saturated-red interiors on Chainsaw share a visual register. `[Scryfall artist field]` `[mtg.wtf: J.P. Targete, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Gatherer artist index: J.P. Targete, via WebSearch snippet]`

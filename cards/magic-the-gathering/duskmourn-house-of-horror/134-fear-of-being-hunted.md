## Vision

![134-fear-of-being-hunted](../../_images/magic-the-gathering/duskmourn-house-of-horror/134-fear-of-being-hunted.png)

A gaunt, hairless predator-thing crouches in mid-stride across cracked beige floor tiles. Its head is a rabbit skull — elongated upright ears, hollow eye sockets, an exposed teeth-bared maw with the bone laid bare where flesh should be. Its body is stretched and emaciated, ribs and spine prominent under taut reddish skin, with patches of pale wraith-stuff trailing from its shoulders and back like smoke or peeled hide. The corridor curves around it in deep blood-red and rust tones, almost tunneling toward the viewer. Rising from the floor on both sides are ghostly white finger-claws and teeth — disembodied spectral hands reaching up from the tile as if the ground itself is hungry. Sprung mousetraps lie scattered on the floor at the creature's feet, their bait long taken. The lighting is hot and oppressive, lit as if from inside a furnace.

The creature embodies the sensation of being stalked by something patient and inescapable.

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Duskmourn: House of Horror (set code DSK) released 2024-09-27 as the 102nd Magic expansion and the second set in the Dragonstorm Arc; the plane is a single haunted house grown to swallow an entire world, ruled by the demon Valgavoth. Wizards designed the set top-down to homage 1970s-1990s slasher and haunted-house cinema. `[Scryfall set page (dsk)]` `[MTG Wiki: Duskmourn: House of Horror, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Cycle membership** — The card belongs to the set's "Fear of..." Nightmare cycle: uncommon Enchantment Creature — Nightmare cards each named after a specific phobia and embodying it as a stalking creature. Confirmed cycle members in DSK include Fear of Abduction (no. 9, white), Fear of Failed Tests (no. 55, blue), Fear of Falling (no. 56, blue), Fear of Isolation (no. 58, blue), Fear of Being Hunted (no. 134, red), Fear of Burning Alive (no. 135, red), and Fear of Missing Out (no. 136, red). All share the Enchantment Creature — Nightmare type-line and the "Fear of <phrase>" naming pattern. See [[fear-of-cycle]] for the corpus-anchored cycle node. `[Scryfall: set DSK]` `[per-card Scryfall pages, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design thesis** — Wizards' Duskmourn Vision Design Handoff (Annie Sardelis) names suspense-over-shock as the set's core emotional thesis: *"The director Alfred Hitchcock talked about the difference between suspense and shock and surprise. The former needs to inform you about a bad thing that could happen, forcing you to stew in your worries about it happening. Duskmourn wanted to be more about suspense than shock."* The handoff also identifies enchantments as the card type best suited to expressing horror's mood because they have *"a lingering presence on the board state"* — which is why each phobia is rendered as an Enchantment Creature, not a regular creature. The Fear-of cycle is the most direct mechanical expression of that design call: the phobia stays in play, stalking. `[Wizards: Duskmourn Vision Design Handoff Part 1]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — {1}{R}{R} for a 4/2 with Haste and "must be blocked if able"; the must-be-blocked clause is unusual in modern red and is the mechanical echo of the flavor — a stalker that does not hide, that demands engagement. The Haste + must-be-blocked pair functions as a forced-trade engine in aggressive red builds: the opponent cannot let a 4-power haster swing through, but a chump-block trades their creature for nothing. EDHREC rank 16,234 at retrieval (bulk-uncommon territory in Commander); Standard, Pioneer, Modern, Legacy, Vintage, Commander legal. Scryfall lists LoadingReadyRun as the preview source (2024-09-07). `[Scryfall]`
- **Flavor text** — *"It makes no effort to hide itself, savoring its prey's rising panic as the sound of its clacking skull grows closer and closer."* No attributed speaker — the line reads as omniscient narration of the phobia itself, which matches the cycle's design conceit that the named fear IS the creature. The audio detail ("clacking skull") and the explicit "makes no effort to hide" line up directly with the must-be-blocked rules clause: the monster announces itself. `[Scryfall flavor_text]`
- **Design (artist)** — Art by Maxime Minard (47 cards on Magic per the Gatherer artist index). Minard also painted Fear of Falling (DSK-56, the blue plummet-Nightmare) within the same Fear-of cycle, plus Unstoppable Slasher (DSK-119) and Creeping Peeper (DSK-46) elsewhere in the set's horror lineup. The visual through-line in his Duskmourn work is the gaunt, elongated silhouette and rim-lit predatory body language. `[Scryfall artist field]` `[Gatherer artist index: Maxime Minard, via WebSearch snippet]` `[mtg.wtf: Maxime Minard, via WebSearch snippet]`

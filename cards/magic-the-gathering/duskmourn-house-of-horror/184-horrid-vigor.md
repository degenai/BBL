## Trivia

- **Set context** — Duskmourn: House of Horror (no. DSK) is the 102nd Magic expansion, released 2024-09-27. Top-down designed around modern horror — Wizards' Vision Design Handoff cites 70s/80s/90s slasher cinema, The Amityville Horror, and Freddy/Jason-type unkillable antagonists as inspiration, with suspense prioritized over shock `[Scryfall]` `[Wizards: Duskmourn Vision Design Handoff, via WebSearch snippet]` `[MTG Wiki: Duskmourn]`.
- **Flavor text** — `"We set it on fire. It lived. We cut off its head. It lived. We fell to it, one by one. It lives."` — Sunrise, survivor sentry `[Scryfall flavor_text]`. The escalation-by-failed-kill cadence is the slasher-villain "the killer always comes back" trope rendered as oral history; the speaker is a flavor-text-only [[Survivor]], the set's creature type tied to the [[Survival]] ability word (primarily green/white) `[MTG Wiki: Survivor, via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Design** — Horrid Vigor is a colorshift of [[Offer Immortality]] (Phyrexia: All Will Be One no. 102 / Foundations no. 525), the black {1}{B} instant with identical text. Moves the deathtouch+indestructible package into green, and is strictly better than [[Serpent's Gift]] (Magic 2013 no. 190, {2}{G}, deathtouch only) `[Scryfall]` `[WebSearch snippet: DSK card comparisons]`.
- **Design** — Art by [[Chris Cold]], who illustrated several major Duskmourn pieces including [[Valgavoth, Harrower of Souls]] (Duskmourn Commander no. 6) and [[Dollmaker's Shop]] // Porcelain Gallery (DSK no. 4) — placing this common in the same painterly horror visual language as the set's marquee demon-lord `[Scryfall]` `[mtg.wtf: Chris Cold, via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Mechanical** — EDHREC rank 5672; appears in 24,174 Commander decks per EDHREC — meaningful for a common combat trick, suggesting it's quietly earned green-deathtouch-package inclusion in casual brews despite no keyword on the card itself `[Scryfall edhrec_rank]` `[EDHREC: Horrid Vigor]`.

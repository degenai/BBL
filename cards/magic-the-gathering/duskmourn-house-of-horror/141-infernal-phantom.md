## Trivia

- **Set context** — Duskmourn: House of Horror (set code DSK, released 2024-09-27) is set on the plane of Duskmourn, which was collapsed into a single haunted mansion in an event called the Ascension by the trapped moth-demon Valgavoth; the House feeds on the fear of the few remaining Survivors. `[Scryfall set page]` `[Wizards: Planeswalker's Guide to Duskmourn, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design** — Eerie is a new triggered ability introduced in Duskmourn that fires whenever an enchantment you control enters and whenever you fully unlock a Room — meaning a single Room card can trigger Eerie twice (once on entry, once on full unlock); design lead Mark Rosewater framed Rooms as both the mechanical and flavor center of the set. `[Wizards: Duskmourn Mechanics article, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Wizards: Vision Design Handoff Part 1, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design** — Of the 15 Eerie cards in Duskmourn, Infernal Phantom is the only red one; the rest are spread across white, blue, black, green, multicolor, and colorless, which makes it the de facto red splash piece for any Eerie-themed shell. `[Scryfall search: set:dsk o:eerie]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — Limited reviewers flagged it as a sleeper: a 2/3 for {3}{R} that Bolts any target on death is already a solid floor, and a single Eerie trigger turns it into a 4/3 with a 4-damage death-shot — well-suited to U/R Rooms or B/R sacrifice shells alongside Final Vengeance or Sawblade Skinripper. `[Draftsim: Ultimate Duskmourn Limited Set Review, via WebSearch snippet]` `[CoolStuffInc: Mechanics of Magic Overview - Eerie, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — Constructed has largely ignored it: EDHREC rank sits at 21,480 and Scryfall lists USD 0.07 nonfoil / 0.08 foil — bulk-uncommon territory, consistent with a card that needs a dedicated Eerie shell to shine. `[Scryfall (EDHREC rank, prices)]`
- **Design** — Art by Mike Sass, a Canadian traditional-media oil painter and former 12-year BioWare staff artist who has illustrated for Magic, World of Warcraft, Star Wars, Pathfinder, and Dungeons & Dragons. `[sassart.com artist bio, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Scryfall (artist field)]`
- **Flavor text** — The card has no printed flavor text; the horror is carried entirely by the Mike Sass art and the Eerie trigger. `[Scryfall (flavor_text field absent)]`

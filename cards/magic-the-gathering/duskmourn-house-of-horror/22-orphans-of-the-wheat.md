## Trivia

- **Set context / horror reference** -- Duskmourn: House of Horror (released 2024-09-27) is a top-down modern horror set centered on a single enormous haunted mansion ruled by the demon Valgavoth, drawing from 1970s-1990s slasher and supernatural film rather than the gothic literary tradition of Innistrad `[Wizards of the Coast: "Duskmourn: House of Horror Vision Design Handoff, Part 1"]`. Orphans of the Wheat's name is a direct reference to *Children of the Corn* (1984 film, based on Stephen King's 1977 short story), in which malevolent children in a rural Nebraska town ritually murder adults at the command of a demonic entity in the corn; the Duskmourn equivalent is Valgavoth's warping influence over children hiding in the wheat `[Card Kingdom Blog: "The 7 Best Horror References in Duskmourn"]`. One community source notes the art reads more as *Village of the Damned* / *Midwich Cuckoos* in execution -- pale-faced, glowing-eyed children with uniform supernatural intent -- than *Children of the Corn*'s rural slasher framing `[Wargamer: "Every single horror reference in MTG Duskmourn"]`.
- **Mechanical design** -- The attack trigger scales with the number of untapped creatures controlled at the time it resolves, not with tokens created before combat; per Scryfall rulings, if Orphans of the Wheat itself is untapped at resolution (e.g. via vigilance), it can be included in the tap count for its own ability `[Scryfall]`. The card sits at EDHREC rank 21,714 out of all Commander-legal cards, indicating niche rather than broad Commander adoption `[Scryfall: edhrec_rank field]`.
- **Artist** -- Julie Dillon is a three-time Hugo Award winner for Best Professional Artist (2014, 2015, 2017), one of the most decorated artists working in science fiction and fantasy illustration; she has worked with Wizards of the Coast, Tor Books, Simon & Schuster, and Oxford University Press among others `[Wikipedia: "Julie Dillon"]`. Her illustration portfolio spans book covers, game art, and album art, and she has published three art books: *Imagined Realms* (2014), *Imagined Realms, Book 2: Earth and Sky* (2015), and *Daydreamer's Journey* (2018) `[Wikipedia: "Julie Dillon"]`.
- **Flavor text** -- "Their only family is each other, and their only toys are those who wander too far into the fields." `[Scryfall]` -- The word "toys" is doing double work here: it ties to the Toy creature type cluster in the set (see Patched Plaything, Attack-in-the-Box) while also connoting the predatory sense of children treating victims as playthings -- a consistent Duskmourn design note that horror tropes layer with mechanical keywords `[Scryfall: type_line is "Creature -- Human", not Toy]`.

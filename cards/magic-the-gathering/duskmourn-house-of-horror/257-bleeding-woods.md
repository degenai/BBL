## Trivia

- **Set context** — Bleeding Woods is one of ten common dual lands introduced in Duskmourn: House of Horror (released 2024-09-27) that collectively form the "unlucky lands" cycle. All ten enter tapped unless a player has 13 or less life, a direct callback to triskaidekaphobia (the fear of the number 13) woven throughout the set's horror theme. The cycle was previewed September 2, 2024 by CoolStuffInc as an exclusive. `[CoolStuffInc: "Ten New Common Dual Lands in Duskmourn: House of Horrors," Paige Smith, 2024-09-02]` `[Unlucky land — MTG Wiki, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Mechanical** — Produces red or green mana. Legal in Pauper, Pioneer, Modern, Legacy, Vintage, and Commander. EDHREC rank 9521 indicates modest Commander adoption — the 13-life threshold is rarely triggered in Commander's 40-life starting total, making the card a mostly tap-in. `[Scryfall legalities and EDHREC rank]`
- **Flavor text** — "They say if someone lies down to sleep beneath the trees, in the morning nothing will remain but a few scattered bones and a bed of blood-red lilies." The flavor describes the forest as predatory — the land itself consuming anyone who rests in it, consistent with Duskmourn's premise that even natural terrain is part of the House's appetite. `[Scryfall]`
- **Design context** — Duskmourn is Magic's first top-down modern horror set, focused on 1970s-1980s horror media rather than the Gothic register of Innistrad. Vision design was led by Annie Sardelis (internal codename "Swimming"). The premise that the plane is entirely a single haunted house dictated that even outdoor environments like forests bear the architectural language of the House — embedded windows, grave-marker shapes, and bleeding surfaces signaling you never left the building. `[Wizards of the Coast: "Duskmourn: House of Horror Vision Design Handoff, Part 1," magic.wizards.com]`

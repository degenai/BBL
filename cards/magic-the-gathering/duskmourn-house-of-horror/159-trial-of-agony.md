## Trivia

- **Set context** — Trial of Agony is part of Duskmourn's Saw-franchise reference cluster. Published horror-reference roundups (Wargamer, TheGamer, Star City Games) group it with **Saw** (equipment), **Let's Play A Game**, and **The Lord of Pain** as the set's coordinated death-game / Jigsaw tribute cards. `[Wargamer: "Every single horror reference in MTG Duskmourn" / TheGamer: "10 Best Horror Movie References" / Star City Games: "Duskmourn Film References Explained", via WebSearch snippets]`
- **Cycle check** — Despite the "Trial of" prefix evoking the Amonkhet-block cycle of the same naming pattern, **no second "Trial of"-named card was printed in Duskmourn** — Scryfall set search returns Trial of Agony as the lone instance in DSK. The card is a Saw homage, not a designer-coordinated DSK "Trial of" cycle. `[Scryfall set search]`
- **Design** — Art by **Mike Sass**. Uncommon, mono-red sorcery, cost {R}, released 2024-09-27 (DSK no. 159). `[Scryfall]`
- **Flavor text** — *"All it took to shatter their lifelong bond was a scrap of hope."* — written to mirror the mechanic: one opponent is forced to choose which of two of their own creatures takes the hit. `[Scryfall]`
- **Mechanical** — Oracle text: "Choose two target creatures controlled by the same opponent. That player chooses one of those creatures. Trial of Agony deals 5 damage to that creature, and the other can't block this turn." A one-mana 5-damage burn / removal gated by opponent's choice, with a secondary "other can't block" rider. `[Scryfall]`
- **Preview history** — Early world preview was handled by French outlet **Numerama** on 2024-09-06, framing the card as a marquee horror-flavor reveal in the DSK preview cycle. `[Scryfall: preview metadata]`
- **Community resonance** — Published horror-reference coverage frames Trial of Agony as a Jigsaw / death-game tribute: the forced-choice sacrifice operationalizes the Saw franchise's "pick one" moral-coercion trope inside MTG rules. `[Wargamer / Star City Games, via WebSearch snippets]`

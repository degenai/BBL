# Smoky Lounge // Misty Salon (Duskmourn: House of Horror)

## Vision

![235-smoky-lounge-misty-salon](../../_images/magic-the-gathering/duskmourn-house-of-horror/235-smoky-lounge-misty-salon.png)

A split-card artwork depicting two adjacent interior spaces. The left half (Smoky Lounge) is a warm crimson-lit hunting lounge: tall paneled walls mount rows of antlered deer skulls and bird trophies, with a central built-in cabinet displaying glassware and a goblet, all bathed in dim smoky red firelight that suggests a hearth out of frame. The right half (Misty Salon) is a cold steel-blue library: tall bookshelves line the walls under a vaulted ribbed ceiling with a domed skylight, a desk with a celestial globe and scattered papers sits center, a tall ladder leans against the shelves at right, and pale moonlight diffuses through hanging mist. A stag-skull mount presides over both halves — antlers in the lounge, a single skull mounted high in the salon — tying the two rooms to the same manor's hunter aesthetic. No figures are present; the rooms feel recently abandoned.

## Trivia

- **Set context** — *Duskmourn: House of Horror* (DSK, released 2024-09-27) is a top-down-designed set themed on "modern horror," inspired by 1970s/80s slasher films and tropes like *The Amityville Horror*; the entire set takes place inside a single impossibly-large mansion plane ruled by the demonic Valgavoth `[MTG Wiki: Duskmourn: House of Horror, via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Design — Room mechanic** — *Room* is a Duskmourn-debut enchantment subtype: a split card whose two halves are both enchantments (called "doors"); cast one half to *unlock* it on the battlefield, then later pay the other half's mana cost as a sorcery to unlock that door too. Lead designer Mark Rosewater chose enchantments over lands because Rooms needed to evoke mood and impact gameplay, and "locations are often lands, but lands have a lot of mechanical restrictions." Storm Scale rating: 6 `[MTG Wiki: Room, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Wizards: Top of the Duskmourning Part 1, Mark Rosewater 2024]`.
- **Design — preview spotlight** — *Smoky Lounge // Misty Salon* is one of Mark Rosewater's named preview examples in "Top of the Duskmourning, Part 1." Rosewater's note: "Misty Salon showcases how some Rooms can care about how many halves of a Room you have unlocked, and is stronger if you unlock Smoky Lounge first. Smoky Lounge's effect will also make it easier to cast Misty Salon ... shines in a blue-red deck." The card is the design team's canonical example of the two-color, two-doors-on-one-card Room pattern `[Wizards: Top of the Duskmourning Part 1, Mark Rosewater 2024, via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Design — artist** — Marco Gorlei is a freelance concept artist (ArtStation: mvgorlei) with 23 Magic illustrations to date; in Duskmourn he illustrated both this card and a second Room, `[[Ticket Booth // Tunnel of Hate]]` (DSK 158), making him one of a small group of Duskmourn artists trusted with two Rooms in the same set `[Scryfall artist search a:"Marco Gorlei", via WebSearch snippet]` `[ArtStation: mvgorlei, via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Mechanical** — In Duskmourn Limited, *Smoky Lounge // Misty Salon* is the main payoff for the Izzet (UR) Rooms archetype: Smoky Lounge generates `{R}{R}` restricted to Room spells / door-unlocks each first main phase, fueling Misty Salon's payoff that creates an X/X blue Spirit token with flying where X is the number of unlocked doors among Rooms you control. EDHREC reports the card is played in roughly 9,400 EDH decks at ~44% inclusion / ~44% synergy, with frequent pairings including `[[Scrabbling Skullcrab]]`, `[[Central Elevator // Promising Stairs]]`, and `[[Unwilling Vessel]]` `[Aetherhub: Duskmourn Draft Archetypes & Limited Guide, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Draftsim: Ultimate Duskmourn Limited Set Review, via WebSearch snippet]` `[EDHREC: Smoky Lounge // Misty Salon, via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Flavor text** — Neither face of the card prints any flavor text; both halves carry oracle/reminder text only. This matches the Duskmourn Room cycle's general convention of skipping flavor text on Rooms to make room for the unlock reminder block `[Scryfall card page DSK 235, flavor_text absent across multiple WebSearch snippets]`.
- **Sibling reading — refuted** — *Smoky Lounge // Misty Salon* and `[[Moldering Gym // Weight Room]]` (DSK 190) are Room-mechanic siblings (same set, same split-card design language, same mechanic debut) but they are NOT part of a shared stag-skull / trophy-hall visual cycle. Different artists (Marco Gorlei vs Helge C. Balzer) and different iconography: Moldering Gym's vision read is an overgrown gymnasium with a ghost-face, spider/cocoon, dead branches, and toxic-green decay — no antlers, no taxidermy. Only Smoky Lounge // Misty Salon carries the trophy-lounge hunter's-manor iconography. The link between the two cards is mechanical, not visual `[Scryfall artist credits, via WebSearch snippet]` `[BBL vision-pass records: DSK 190 and DSK 235]`.

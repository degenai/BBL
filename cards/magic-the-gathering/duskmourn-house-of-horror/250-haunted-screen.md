## Trivia

- **Set context** -- Haunted Screen is an uncommon artifact from *Duskmourn: House of Horror* (DSK), released September 27, 2024. The Duskmourn plane is a top-down horror set inspired by retro consumer-electronics dread -- televisions, VHS tapes, and CRT screens appear throughout the set as horror-delivery vectors. `[Scryfall]` `[magic.wizards.com/en/news/announcements/first-look-at-duskmourn-house-of-horror]`
- **Design flavor** -- Haunted Screen is designed as a five-color mana rock with a built-in joke: it produces white or black mana for free (like a black-and-white television), but requires paying 1 life to add green, blue, or red (like paying to upgrade the image to color). The EDHREC Duskmourn artifacts review surfaces this reading explicitly. `[EDHREC: Duskmourn Set Review -- Artifacts and Lands]`
- **Commander color identity constraint** -- Because Haunted Screen's oracle text contains all five mana symbols (W, U, B, R, G), its Commander color identity is WUBRG, restricting it to five-color decks only. It cannot appear in a two-color or mono-color Commander deck even though it is a colorless artifact. The EDHREC review notes this puts it in competition with already-strong five-color rocks like Timeless Lotus and Mox Amber. `[Scryfall color_identity]` `[EDHREC: Duskmourn Set Review -- Artifacts and Lands]`
- **Mechanical transformation** -- Haunted Screen can spend {7} to put seven +1/+1 counters on itself and become a 0/0 Spirit creature in addition to its other types (activatable only once). The baseline 0/0 + 7 counters = a 7/7 Spirit, which the EDHREC article describes as the card's secondary payoff for patient five-color brewers. `[Scryfall oracle_text]` `[EDHREC: Duskmourn Set Review -- Artifacts and Lands]`

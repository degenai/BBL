## Trivia

- **Set context** — Duskmourn: House of Horror (DSK) released September 27, 2024. The set is set on the plane of Duskmourn — a world consumed by the sentient House grown from the body of the moth-demon Valgavoth, who feeds on the fear of survivors trapped inside. Valgavoth's Faithful depicts one of the human cultists who serve Valgavoth willingly, counterpart to the Glimmer faction of survivors who resist him. The depicted figure is an anonymous follower — **not Valgavoth himself**, whose only card depictions are Valgavoth, Terror Eater (DSK-120, mythic) and Valgavoth, Harrower of Souls (DSC-6, mythic), neither of which is currently in the BBL corpus. `[Scryfall: released_at 2024-09-27; BBL corpus: valgavoth.md character node]`
- **Flavor text** — "That's the one good thing about these cultists: sometimes they do your work for you." — Winter `[Scryfall: flavor_text field]`. The speaker is Winter, Misanthropic Guide (DSK-240) — a Legendary Creature (Human Warlock, 3/4 with Ward 2) who served as the House's guide for survivors and struck a doomed bargain with Valgavoth to deliver four lives in exchange for an escape door. The line is darkly pragmatic: Winter watching cultists eliminate threats on his behalf while he pursues his own survival agenda. `[Scryfall: Winter Misanthropic Guide DSK-240; BBL corpus: valgavoth.md — Betrayer's Bargain winter-contract documentation]`
- **Mechanical profile** — 1/1 Human Cleric for {B}; activated ability at {3}{B} plus self-sacrifice returns any creature card from your graveyard to the battlefield (sorcery speed). A disposable reanimation engine: pay 1 mana now for a body, spend 4 later to reanimate something large. EDHREC rank 7,942 — sees limited competitive Commander play but is a thematic fit in Duskmourn-flavored sacrifice-reanimate builds. Legal in Standard, Pioneer, Modern, and Commander. `[Scryfall: edhrec_rank 7942, oracle_text, legalities]`
- **Design** — Artist: Jodie Muir. Previewed September 3, 2024 via the official Wizards of the Coast YouTube channel per Scryfall preview metadata. The moth and butterfly motifs in the art are canonically tied to Valgavoth's visual register: the BBL corpus documents Infernal Phantom (DSK-141) and Say Its Name (DSK-197) as the two cards that explicitly specify the "moth-demon" iconography — making the moths on this Faithful card a deliberate visual marker of the cultists' allegiance. `[Scryfall: preview field; BBL corpus: valgavoth.md moth-demon iconography note]`

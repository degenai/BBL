## Trivia

- **Set context** — Etched Cornfield is part of Duskmourn's ten-card "unlucky lands" cycle of common dual lands, all entering tapped unless a player has 13 or less life. The number 13 is a deliberate triskaidekaphobia motif across the set. The cycle was previewed exclusively by CoolStuffInc on September 2, 2024. `[CoolStuffInc: "Ten New Common Dual Lands in Duskmourn: House of Horrors," Paige Smith, 2024-09-02]` `[Unlucky land — MTG Wiki, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design** — Artist Randy Gallegos illustrated multiple land cards for Duskmourn: House of Horror, including Etched Cornfield and Floodfarm Verge. In a post on his personal blog (gallegosart.com, August 30, 2024) Gallegos noted Floodfarm Verge as his favorite of his three Duskmourn pieces. His broader MTG bibliography spans Lord of the Rings: Tales of Middle-earth, Doctor Who, Ravnica Allegiance, Modern Horizons 3, and The Lost Caverns of Ixalan. `[Randy Gallegos, gallegosart.com/blog/2024/8/30/duskmourn-pt-1]`
- **Flavor text** — "They say those who lose their way in the fields gradually wither and grow brittle, until at last they put down roots and anchor into the soil as a whispering stalk." The flavor describes transformation-into-plant as a fate for wanderers — the cornfield consumes the lost, consistent with the plane's predatory-terrain design. `[Scryfall]`
- **Mechanical** — Produces green or white mana. Legal in Pauper, making it one of the few common dual-mana sources at that rarity available in that format. EDHREC rank 10669 places it in modest Commander adoption territory, slightly below cycle mate Bleeding Woods (rank 9521). `[Scryfall legalities and EDHREC rank]`

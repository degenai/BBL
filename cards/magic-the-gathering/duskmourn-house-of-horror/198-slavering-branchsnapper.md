## Trivia

- **Set context** — Duskmourn: House of Horror is the 102nd MTG expansion (released 2024-09-27) and the second set in the Dragonstorm Arc; the plane is a single haunted mansion grown to swallow an entire world, ruled by the demon Valgavoth, pitched by Wizards as a 1970s–1990s slasher/horror-film homage. `[MTG Wiki: Duskmourn: House of Horror]` `[Scryfall: set dsk]`
- **Set lore (swallowed-suns)** — Duskmourn's plane originally had two suns (greater and lesser); Valgavoth consumed both as the House grew a tower piercing the clouds, and with the suns gone crops failed and the plane collapsed. The low amber sun behind the Branchsnapper reads as that swallowed-sun motif rather than ordinary daylight. `[MTG Wiki: Duskmourn (plane), via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design** — Illustrated by John Tedrick, whose other Duskmourn pieces include Hauntwoods Shrieker, Twitching Doll, and Scrabbling Skullcrab; the painting is archived on his public ArtStation as part of his Duskmourn portfolio. `[ArtStation: tedrick94 / Duskmourn — Slavering Branchsnapper]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — A vanilla-with-cycling green common: 7/6 Trample for {4}{G}{G} plus Forestcycling {2} to search a Forest. EDHREC lists it in roughly 7,006 Commander decks at edhrec_rank no. 12,391 — bulk-common territory; the actual play pattern is cycle-it-for-a-Forest in green ramp shells rather than hard-cast. Standard / Pioneer / Modern / Legacy / Pauper / Commander legal. `[EDHREC: slavering-branchsnapper]` `[Scryfall]`
- **Flavor text** — `"Hey, look! It made us a nice big path through the hedge maze!" — Zimone`. Zimone Wola is a Quandrix/Strixhaven prodigy who solved the Vorzani Conjecture (a magical fractal theorem), giving her a non-Planeswalker grasp of multiverse structure; she is among the named survivors trapped inside Duskmourn during the Dragonstorm Arc, and the small figures in the hedge-maze corridor read as her survivor band putting the beast's rampage to use rather than simply fleeing it. `[Scryfall flavor_text]` `[MTG Wiki: Zimone Wola]`
- **Iconography note (unresolved)** — The grid-cross emblem the vision pass observed superimposed on the amber sun is NOT documented in any source we could reach as a named recurring Duskmourn iconography (no Wizards article, MTG Wiki page, or Draftsim art-roundup names a sun-with-grid-cross symbol). It may be a window-frame silhouette or a stylized eclipse rendered by Tedrick rather than a set-wide motif — flag for the curator to revisit if more Duskmourn cards land in inventory showing the same emblem. `[Negative result: WebSearch + MTG Wiki]`

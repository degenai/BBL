## Trivia

- **Set context** — Duskmourn: House of Horror (DSK, released September 27, 2024) is set inside a sentient haunted mansion-plane under Valgavoth's domain. Vampires appear in the set as one horror archetype among many — neither dominant as in Innistrad nor unique to a faction — coexisting with ghosts, monsters, and the House's own supernatural menace. `[Scryfall]` `[Wizards Planeswalker's Guide to Duskmourn]`
- **Flavor text** — "There are worse things here than vampires." No speaker is attributed. The line deliberately positions vampires as a familiar, lesser threat within Duskmourn's hierarchy of horrors, implicitly pointing toward Valgavoth and the House's deeper evils. `[Scryfall]`
- **Mechanical profile** — Dashing Bloodsucker (2/5 Vampire Warrior, {3}{B}) uses Eerie to gain +2/+0 and lifelink until end of turn whenever an enchantment enters or a Room is fully unlocked. The base 2/5 stat line is defensively oriented; the Eerie triggers convert it into a life-swing threat in enchantment-dense builds. EDHREC rank 21,689 — the lowest Commander adoption of the five cards in this batch. Not legal in The Lord of the Rings limited format (tlr: not_legal per Scryfall). `[Scryfall]`
- **Artist** — Randy Gallegos has illustrated approximately 214 MTG cards since his debut in the 1995 Ice Age set, one of the longest active rosters in the game's history. Notable prior works include Giant Spider (frequently reprinted), Shock, Dance of the Dead, Soul Warden, and Balance. In a 2019 interview he described red cards as underrepresented in his portfolio because aggressive energy is "not where I live in my art most of the time." The original painting for his Throne of Eldraine Food token sold for $3,000 in September 2019. `[Magic Untapped interview with Randy Gallegos, 2019]` `[Hipsters of the Coast: Randy Gallegos Eldraine Food Token sells for $3,000, 2019]`

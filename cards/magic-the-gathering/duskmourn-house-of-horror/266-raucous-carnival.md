## Trivia

- **Set context** — Raucous Carnival is the red/white member of Duskmourn's ten-card common dual-land cycle, colloquially the "unlucky lands" or "life-check lands": all ten enter tapped unless a player has 13 or less life. The full cycle is Abandoned Campground, Bleeding Woods, Etched Cornfield, Lakeside Shack, Murky Sewer, Neglected Manor, Peculiar Lighthouse, **Raucous Carnival**, Razortrap Gorge, and Strangled Cemetery. `[MTG Wiki: Unlucky land / CoolStuffInc, via WebSearch snippets]`
- **Set context** — The 13-life threshold is a triskaidekaphobia gag fitted to Duskmourn's horror set design language; the urban-legend-register flavor text across the cycle reinforces the unlucky-13 conceit. `[MTG Wiki: Unlucky land]`
- **Design** — Art by **Josu Solano**, common rarity, released 2024-09-27 as part of Duskmourn: House of Horror (set code DSK). `[Scryfall]`
- **Flavor text** — *"They say that those who enter the amusement park never come back, and their disembodied laughter joins the others on the wind."* `[Scryfall]`
- **Mechanical** — The "13 or less" trigger reads on **any player**, including the controller, so paying life or eating combat damage on purpose can convert your own dual into an untapped one — a real lever in life-payment decks. `[Scryfall / MTG Rocks set review]`
- **Mechanical** — The cycle is the first common dual-land print run with a realistic chance to enter untapped, which drew significant Pauper interest. Commander uptake is muted because 40-life starts keep the trigger out of reach for most of a game. `[MTG Rocks / EDHREC DSK set review]`
- **Community resonance** — Coverage on MTG Rocks framed the unlucky-lands cycle as flavorful for Duskmourn and a real multi-format pickup (Standard, Modern, Pauper) but mechanically odd for Commander, per a published format-impact roundup. `[MTG Rocks: "New Common Duskmourn Dual Lands Have Multi-Format Potential"]`

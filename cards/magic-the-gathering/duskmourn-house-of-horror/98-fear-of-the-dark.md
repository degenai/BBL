## Vision

![98-fear-of-the-dark](../../_images/magic-the-gathering/duskmourn-house-of-horror/98-fear-of-the-dark.png)

A large abstract horror figure fills the frame. The form is loosely humanoid but dissolving — the body appears to be made of smeared, dripping matter in shades of charcoal and pale grey-cream. Multiple appendages or limb-like shapes extend outward; a central hollow or cavity opens in the torso area. Dark shadowy hand-shapes reach in from the edges. The painting technique is loose and expressionistic, with smear-strokes suggesting motion and dissolution rather than solid form. The entire palette is near-monochrome: deep blacks, charcoal, warm ash, and faint cream highlights. There is no distinct face, no readable eyes, no grounded environment — the figure exists in an undifferentiated dark void. The mood is suffocating, formless dread.

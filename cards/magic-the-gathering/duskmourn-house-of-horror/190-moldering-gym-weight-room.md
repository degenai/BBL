## Trivia

- **Set context** — *Duskmourn: House of Horror* (DSK, released 2024-09-27) is a top-down-designed set themed on "modern horror," inspired by 1970s/80s slasher films and tropes like *The Amityville Horror*; the entire set takes place inside a single impossibly-large mansion ruled by the demonic Valgavoth `[MTG Wiki: Duskmourn: House of Horror]`.
- **Design — Room mechanic** — *Room* is a new enchantment subtype debuted in Duskmourn to evoke the "twisted, labyrinthine nature of the plane." Mechanically a Room is a split card whose two halves are both enchantments (called "doors"); you cast one half to *unlock* it on the battlefield, and may later pay the other half's mana cost as a sorcery to unlock that door too. Lead designer Mark Rosewater notes the team chose enchantments over lands because Rooms needed to evoke mood and impact gameplay, and "locations are often lands, but lands have a lot of mechanical restrictions." Storm Scale rating: 6 `[MTG Wiki: Room]` `[Wizards: Top of the Duskmourning Part 1, Mark Rosewater 2024]`.
- **Design — Room iteration history** — Earlier exploratory-design versions of the Room mechanic were a separate "Mansion" deck with a meeple game-piece that moved between Rooms (cut as too complex for tournament play), a four-rooms-per-card variant, and a three-rooms-per-card variant before landing on the final two-doors-per-card split layout `[Wizards: Top of the Duskmourning Part 1, Mark Rosewater 2024]`.
- **Design — artist** — Helge C. Balzer is a German fantasy/concept artist based in Marburg, Germany with 20+ years of work for Wizards of the Coast, Games Workshop, Black Library, Fantasy Flight, Paizo and others; he illustrates both faces of this card `[helgecbalzer.com, via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Mechanical** — *Weight Room* (the back half) triggers *manifest dread* on unlock, then puts three +1/+1 counters on that face-down 2/2 creature; manifest dread is a Duskmourn keyword that asks you to look at the top two cards of your library, manifest one face-down and mill the other, introducing "the threat of the unknown." *Moldering Gym* (the front half) is a basic-land tutor for {2}{G}. EDHREC reports 15,408 EDH decks playing the card and notes commonly-paired cards include `[[Keys to the House]]`, `[[Rampaging Soulrager]]`, and `[[Underwater Tunnel // Slimy Aquarium]]` `[Scryfall]` `[Wizards: Duskmourn Mechanics]` `[EDHREC: Moldering Gym // Weight Room]`.
- **Flavor text** — Neither face of the card prints any flavor text; only oracle/reminder text on each door `[Scryfall]`.

## Connections

- [[189-manifest-dread]] — Oracle-text mechanical dependency: Weight Room's unlock effect reads "manifest dread" by name, directly invoking the keyword that Manifest Dread both names and defines. Manifest Dread is the only Magic card whose card name, keyword action, and complete rules text are identical; the Room mechanic was designed for Duskmourn's labyrinthine plane in the same design cycle that finalized the manifest dread keyword.

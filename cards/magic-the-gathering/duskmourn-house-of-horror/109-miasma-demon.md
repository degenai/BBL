## Trivia

- **Set context** — Miasma Demon is part of Duskmourn: House of Horror (set code DSK), released September 27, 2024. The set is a top-down horror design centered on a single haunted mansion — the plane of Duskmourn itself — ruled by the moth demon Valgavoth. It was the first premier MTG set built around modern interior horror rather than an open-world plane. `[Wizards of the Coast: "Duskmourn: House of Horror Vision Design Handoff, Part 1"]` `[Scryfall: set DSK]`
- **Flavor text** — `"Hush, little ants. I can rid you of your troubles with a breath. All I ask in return is a drop of blood... and a promise."` The speaker is unnamed — a predatory bargainer whose patience and contempt for mortals ("little ants") reads as demonic transactional cruelty. `[Scryfall]`
- **Design / mechanics** — The ability uses a "reflexive trigger" structure: discarding is optional and self-initiated, but once you discard, a second ability triggers and you choose targets for the -2/-2 effect. This means you cannot be forced to commit targets in advance — a rules note flagged in the MTGSalvation card entry for the set. The ability scales directly with hand size: emptying your hand on entry can simultaneously clear up to six creatures. `[Scryfall oracle text]` `[MTGSalvation: DSK card entry]`
- **Artist** — Mathias Kollros has been a recurring MTG contributor since his debut in Return to Ravnica (2012) and has illustrated 161+ cards across 17+ sets `[Gatherer artist search: Mathias Kollros]`. He also contributed at least two other pieces to DSK including Waltz of Rage and Decree of Pain `[Scryfall set search: artist Mathias Kollros]`.
- **Mechanical reputation** — EDHREC rank 19,138 as of 2026-05-19, placing it among the less-adopted uncommons in DSK. At 6 mana for a one-time board effect, commander players appear to treat it as a budget role-filler rather than an essential include `[Scryfall: edhrec_rank field]`.

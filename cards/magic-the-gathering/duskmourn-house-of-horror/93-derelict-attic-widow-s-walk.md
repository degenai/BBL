## Trivia

- **Set context / mechanic** — Derelict Attic // Widow's Walk is a **Room** — a new enchantment subtype introduced in *Duskmourn: House of Horror* (DSK, released September 27, 2024). Rooms are the set's major mechanical innovation: split permanent enchantments where each half is a "door" that can be unlocked separately by paying its mana cost as a sorcery on the battlefield. The mechanic began development with an elaborate Mansion deck (similar to *Unfinity*'s Attractions), including a movement mechanic and a Meeple token, but was streamlined into the current permanent-split form to avoid competitive-play complications `[magic.wizards.com: "Top of the Duskmourning, Part 1"]` `[Scryfall]`.
- **Design** — The two doors complement each other by role: Derelict Attic (2B) draws two cards at the cost of 2 life — a card-advantage engine; Widow's Walk (3B) grants the attacker +1/+0 and deathtouch whenever a creature attacks alone — a combat finisher. The cheaper half powers development, the expensive half closes games, which is the canonical design relationship of DSK Rooms per Rosewater's column `[magic.wizards.com: "Top of the Duskmourning, Part 1"]`. Art by Marc Simonetti, a French concept artist (born 1977) with film credits including *Valerian and the City of a Thousand Planets*, *Aladdin*, *Maleficent 2*, and *Aquaman 2* — his MTG portfolio spans over 23 cards `[artofmtg.com: Marc Simonetti]` `[art.marcsimonetti.com: About]`.
- **Mechanical notes** — EDHREC rank 7,999 — the highest Commander adoption among the five DSK cards in this batch, appearing in approximately 12,800 of 2.91 million Commander decks (0.44% inclusion). The draw-2-lose-2 side provides utility in black Commander decks hungry for card draw; the deathtouch-on-attacker side plays well in voltron and mono-black aggressive strategies `[EDHREC: Derelict Attic // Widow's Walk]` `[Scryfall: edhrec_rank]`.
- **Lore context** — "Widow's Walk" is a real architectural term for a railed rooftop platform historically built on coastal New England homes, legendarily so wives could watch for ships returning from sea — and mourn when they didn't. The card's setting inverts this: the Widow's Walk here is a trap or vantage point in a haunted house where attackers are made lethal, not a place of grief. The attic-to-balcony pairing on a single split card maps the interior/exterior duality of the House itself `[set lore context, Scryfall]`.

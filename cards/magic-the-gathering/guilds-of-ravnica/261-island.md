## Trivia

- **Set context** — Guilds of Ravnica (set code GRN) was released October 5, 2018, returning to the city-plane of Ravnica with a focus on five guilds: Dimir, Golgari, Izzet, Selesnya, and Boros. `[Scryfall]`
- **Design** — Artist Richard Wright has painted the Island basic land for every major Ravnica-set: Ravnica: City of Guilds (2005, no. 294), Return to Ravnica (2012, no. 259), and this Guilds of Ravnica printing (2018, no. 261). His Ravnica land art is widely cited as the definitive visual interpretation of the city-plane, and he served as a style-guide artist shaping how other MTG illustrators depicted the plane. `[Scryfall; Scent of a Gamer: MTG Artists: Richard Wright, 2015]`
- **Design** — Wright solved a conceptual problem with the Ravnica Island: the plane is an endless city with no open water in the conventional landscape sense. His solution was to treat urban waterways and architecture as the source of blue mana, depicting what he described as "cathedral-like suburbs" as the Island. `[Scent of a Gamer: MTG Artists: Richard Wright, 2015]`
- **Mechanical** — This specific printing is tagged as a Planeswalker Deck promo (promo_type: planeswalkerdeck per Scryfall), meaning it was distributed inside a Guilds of Ravnica Planeswalker Deck product rather than appearing in booster packs. It is legal in every sanctioned format except Old School. `[Scryfall]`

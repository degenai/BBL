## Trivia

- **Set context and Alpha lineage** — Juggernaut was printed in Magic's very first set, Limited Edition Alpha (1993), making it one of the oldest cards in the game. The M15 printing is a reprint; Scryfall's print history shows it has appeared across Alpha, Beta, Unlimited, Revised, 4th through 6th Edition, 7th through 10th Edition, Magic 2010 through 2015, and The Brothers' War (2022). The Brothers' War reprint as a common made Juggernaut Pauper-legal for the first time. [Scryfall, WebSearch via PureMTGO/CoolStuffInc]
- **Mechanical design** — A 5/3 artifact creature for {4} that must attack each combat and cannot be blocked by Walls. The "attacks each combat if able" and "can't be blocked by Walls" clauses are both original Alpha text, unchanged across 30+ years of reprints. The Wall-exception was relevant in early Magic when Wall was a meaningful defensive creature type; in modern play the Wall clause is largely vestigial. [Scryfall]
- **Flavor text** — "Many a besieged city has surrendered upon hearing the distinctive rumble of the juggernaut." [Scryfall] — Unattributed. The flavor text is consistent across multiple reprints. The word "juggernaut" derives from Jagannath (a form of Vishnu), via the colonial-era myth of devotees being crushed beneath its festival chariot — a name MTG borrowed for an unstoppable war machine.
- **Format legality breadth** — The M15 copy is legal in Standard (via later reprints), Modern, Legacy, Vintage, Commander, Pauper (via BRO), and Brawl. Few artifact creatures in MTG history have this many legal formats from a single oracle text, reflecting the card's total absence of broken mechanics. [Scryfall]
- **Artist** — Kev Walker, one of the most prolific MTG artists. His M15 rendering of Juggernaut is notably kinetic compared to earlier editions' static side-profile depictions, placing the machine in mid-charge with a dwarfed rider and besieged city in the background. [Scryfall]

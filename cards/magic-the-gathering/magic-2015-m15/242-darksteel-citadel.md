## Trivia

- **First printing and plane context** — Darksteel Citadel first appeared in the Darksteel set (February 6, 2004), the second set of the original Mirrodin block. It depicts a structure on Mirrodin, the all-metal plane created by the planeswalker Karn, where "darksteel" is a fictional indestructible alloy that serves as the mechanical/flavor justification for the Indestructible keyword. `[Scryfall: first released 2004-02-06; search result via mtg.cardsrealm.com]`
- **Artifact land ban history** — The original Mirrodin block's five colored artifact lands (Great Furnace, Seat of the Synod, etc.) were banned from Standard in March 2005 due to Affinity deck dominance; Darksteel Citadel was not banned because it produces only colorless mana and entered later in the block. When Modern was created (2011), all six colored Mirrodin artifact lands were banned from day one — Darksteel Citadel was explicitly exempted, making it the only Mirrodin-era artifact land legal in Modern and Pioneer. `[WebSearch via vintageisthenewold.com; Scryfall legalities]`
- **Flavor text** — *"Structures built from darksteel yield to neither assault nor age."* — no speaker attributed. `[Scryfall]`
- **Reprint depth and format ubiquity** — Darksteel Citadel has been reprinted across at least nine distinct sets including Modern Masters 2015, Magic 2015 (M15), Commander 2014, Commander 2018, Double Masters, and Duel Decks: Elves vs. Inventors. `[WebSearch via mtgassist.com]` Its EDHREC rank of 286 (Scryfall) makes it one of the most-included non-land lands in Commander — it counts as both an artifact and a land simultaneously, enabling Affinity and artifact-count synergies without costing a card slot that requires colored mana.
- **Artist** — John Avon, known for his panoramic landscape land arts, illustrated this M15 printing. Avon is one of the most prolific MTG land artists; his work appears across hundreds of basic and non-basic lands. `[Scryfall artist field]`

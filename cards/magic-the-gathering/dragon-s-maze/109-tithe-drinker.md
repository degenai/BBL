## Trivia

- **Set context** — Dragon's Maze (released 2013-05-03) closed the Return to Ravnica block; the Orzhov Syndicate is the WB guild, framed in lore sources as the "Church of Deals" — an amalgam of religion, banking, and organized crime where citizens pay tithes to syndics. Tithe Drinker is a common Orzhov-watermark vampire from that block. `[mtg.wtf DGM/109]` `[MTG Wiki: Orzhov Syndicate, via WebSearch summary]`
- **Design** — Art by Slawomir Maniak. {W}{B} 2/1 Creature — Vampire with Lifelink and Extort, common rarity, Orzhov watermark. `[mtg.wtf DGM/109]`
- **Mechanical reputation (EDH)** — EDHREC tracks Tithe Drinker in ~19,000 of ~2.01M decks (0.95% inclusion). Top homes are vampire-tribal / Orzhov life-drain: Licia, Sanguine Tribune (36% inclusion), Astarion, the Decadent (30%), Sorin of House Markov (29%). High-synergy partners include Sanguine Bond (53%), Exquisite Blood (37%), and Cruel Celebrant (38%). `[EDHREC: Tithe Drinker]`
- **Mechanical reputation (Pauper)** — Known as a Pauper staple in WB extort decks, run as a 4-of alongside other extort commons. Community discussion treats it as an upgrade over Syndic of Tithes thanks to lifelink — with the rules caveat that lifelink triggers on the creature's combat damage, not on the extort drain itself. `[MTGSalvation: "Tithe Drinker - New Card Discussion"]` `[tappedout: Orzhov Pauper Extort]` `[mtggoldfish: Pauper Orzhov Extortion]`
- **Community resonance** — Sentiment on the Dragon's Maze rumor-mill thread skewed strongly positive for Limited, with commenters calling it "a sort-of second Syndic of Tithes for Orzhov in limited" and arguing it "has the potential to be better" than Syndic. Minority concern: 1 toughness makes it a poor blocker. (Sentiment, not consensus fact.) `[MTGSalvation: "Tithe Drinker - New Card Discussion"]`
- **Flavor text** — No printed flavor text; the Dragon's Maze original prints rules text only (Lifelink, Extort). `[Scryfall: DGM/109]`
- **Extort mechanic origin** — Extort was designed by Shawn Main, who was not on the Gatecrash design team; he was later credited as a design-team member specifically because of this contribution (and his work on the Boros battalion mechanic). Main's original version used a cost of 1 generic mana; development changed it to the {W/B} hybrid symbol late in the process to enforce color commitment, and also adjusted "an opponent" to "each opponent" for multiplayer play. Mark Rosewater had to draft fourteen extort cards in a single test deck to convince skeptical team members the mechanic was powerful enough. `[Wizards: "Gatecrashing the Party, Part 3" (2013-01-14)]` `[Wizards: "Designing for Orzhov" (2013-03-25)]`
- **Extort — Commander color identity ruling** — Because the {W/B} mana symbol appears only in the Extort reminder text (not in the card's rules text proper), reminder text carries no rules weight; mono-colored Extort cards like Blind Obedience are legal in mono-black or mono-white Commander decks. Tithe Drinker itself is already WB and is unaffected, but the ruling was a significant community question at Gatecrash release and applies to every Extort card in the game. `[Magic Judges Blog: "Extort and Commander" (2013-02-18)]` `[MTGSalvation: "Extort in Commander", Rulings Archives]`
- **Reprint history** — Tithe Drinker has two printings beyond its Dragon's Maze original: Commander 2017 (no. 200, included in the "Vampiric Bloodlust" precon) and The List (PLST no. C17-200, the Commander 2017 art reprinted as a set-booster insert). The Commander 2017 inclusion placed it in a purpose-built vampire-tribal shell, which accounts for the heavy EDH vampire commander representation in its EDHREC data. `[Scryfall: all prints search]`
- **Slawomir Maniak — Orzhov portfolio** — Tithe Drinker is the only card in Slawomir Maniak's entire illustrated catalog (222+ cards by Gatherer count) to carry an Orzhov watermark. His other vampire work is all Innistrad-plane: Falkenrath Noble (Innistrad), Screeching Bat // Stalking Vampire (Innistrad), and Bloodflow Connoisseur (Avacyn Restored). No Hipsters of the Coast profile or artist statement for Maniak was found via search. `[Scryfall: artist search "Slawomir Maniak" + watermark:orzhov]` `[Gatherer: artist count]`
- **Dragon's Maze — set reception and Pauper exception** — Dragon's Maze is widely considered one of the weakest sets in the game's history; community discourse cites the structural problem of cramming all ten Return to Ravnica block guilds and their mechanics into a small set, with prerelease attendance reportedly down ~50% from Gatecrash. Tithe Drinker was a notable exception to the general critical assessment: the MTGSalvation Pauper set review called it "the best [common] in this set" for Pauper purposes, with a commenter adding it is "probably the best common of the set." (Community sentiment, not consensus fact.) `[MTGSalvation: "Dragon's Maze — Major Flop at the Finish Line"]` `[MTGSalvation: "Pauper Set Review Dragon's Maze"]`
- **Dragon's Maze narrative — Orzhov champion** — The Dragon's Maze block narrative centers on the Implicit Maze, a system of leylines running through all ten guild districts, designed by Azorius parun Azor as a fail-safe for the dissolution of the Guildpact. Each guild sent one champion to run the maze; the Orzhov Syndicate's representative was Teysa Karlov (Human Advisor, official guildmaster proxy for Obzedat). The unnamed vampire depicted on Tithe Drinker is not identified with Teysa in any canon source — the figure is a generic syndic. Jace Beleren ultimately became the Living Guildpact by connecting all runners' minds at the maze's conclusion. `[MTGSalvation: Dragon's Maze Player's Guide]` `[MTG Wiki: Implicit Maze, via WebSearch summary]` `[Wizards: Dragon's Maze Mechanics (2013)]`

## Trivia (second pass)

- **Extort mechanic origin** — Designed by Shawn Main outside the Gatecrash team during devign. The original cost was 1 generic mana; development changed it to {W/B} hybrid. MaRo drafted 14 extort cards to convince skeptics. `[Wizards: "Gatecrashing the Party Part 3"; "Designing for Orzhov"]`
- **Extort color identity ruling** — {W/B} appears in REMINDER TEXT only; mono-colored extort cards (Blind Obedience) are legal in mono-black Commander. Rules discourse specific to the mechanic. `[Magic Judges Blog; MTGSalvation Rulings]`
- **Reprint history** — Commander 2017 (no. 200, "Vampiric Bloodlust" precon) and The List (PLST C17-200). The C17 vampire-tribal shell explains the EDH inclusion data. `[Scryfall prints search]`
- **Maniak's only Orzhov-watermark card** — Tithe Drinker is the ONLY card with an Orzhov watermark in Slawomir Maniak's 222+ card MTG catalog. All his other vampire work is Innistrad-plane. `[Scryfall: a:"Slawomir Maniak" e:rna OR e:gpt OR e:dgm]`
- **Dragon's Maze reception + Pauper exception** — Set widely called one of the weakest ever; prerelease attendance ~50% down from Gatecrash. MTGSalvation Pauper set review called Tithe Drinker "the best [common] in this set." `[MTGSalvation Pauper review thread]`
- **Dragon's Maze narrative** — The set centers the implicit Maze race; Orzhov's named champion was Teysa Karlov. The depicted vampire on Tithe Drinker is unnamed/generic, not Teysa. Jace became the Living Guildpact at the set's conclusion. `[MTGSalvation Player's Guide; MTG Wiki: Dragon's Maze]`

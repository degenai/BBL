## Trivia

- **Set context** — Boros Guildgate is a Land — Gate from Dragon's Maze (May 3, 2013), the third set in the Return to Ravnica block. Guildgates were introduced across the block as common taplands for each of Ravnica's ten guilds: five debuted in Return to Ravnica (2012) and five in Gatecrash (2013), with all ten reprinted in Dragon's Maze. Boros Guildgate first appeared in Gatecrash (GTC no. 239), where it represented the Boros Legion — Ravnica's military and law-enforcement guild, a union of white-aligned soldiers and red-aligned angels. The Dragon's Maze printing (this card) uses art by Noah Bradley showing the gate from a low exterior angle. `[Scryfall]` `[WebSearch: Gatecrash set, guildgate cycle history]`
- **Flavor text** — `"It promises protection to those in need and proclaims a warning to any who would threaten Ravnican law."` — No speaker attributed. The text frames the Boros gate as simultaneously a shelter for citizens and a declaration of martial authority, condensing the guild's dual role as protector and enforcer. `[Scryfall]`
- **Reprint history** — Among the most-reprinted Guildgates in the cycle. Confirmed printings include: Gatecrash (GTC, 2013), Dragon's Maze (DGM, 2013), Commander 2015 (C15), Commander Anthology Volume II (CM2), Guilds of Ravnica (GRN, 2018 — two collector number variants with art by Titus Lunter), Zendikar Rising Commander (ZNC, 2020), Ravnica Remastered (RVR, 2024 — including a retro-frame variant), and Foundations (FDN, 2024). Wizards has used the Guildgate cycle as budget-accessible mana-fixing across numerous preconstructed products. `[Scryfall]` `[WebSearch: mtgassist.com Boros Guildgate printings]`
- **EDHREC rank** — Ranked 1735 on EDHREC overall, the highest rank (most-played) of any card in this five-card batch, reflecting its pauper-legal dual-land status and ubiquity in Commander as budget mana-fixing for any Boros-identity deck. The Gate subtype also enables synergy with Gate-payoff cards introduced in Guilds of Ravnica (e.g., Gates Ablaze, Guild Summit, Gatebreaker Ram). `[Scryfall]` `[EDHREC: edhrec.com/cards/boros-guildgate]`
- **Artist note** — This Dragon's Maze printing was illustrated by Noah Bradley. In June 2020, Wizards of the Coast announced they had severed their relationship with Bradley after he publicly confirmed allegations of sexual misconduct involving members of the Magic community. Bradley's art remains on previously printed cards including this copy. Wizards did not recall or alter existing prints. `[Scryfall artist field]` `[WebSearch: MTG Wiki Noah Bradley, Wizards 2020 statement]`

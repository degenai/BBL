## Trivia

- **Set context** — Theros Beyond Death (THB) was released January 24, 2020 as the third set in the Theros block family, following the original Theros (2013) and Born of the Gods / Journey into Nyx (2014). THB returned to the Greek-mythology-themed plane and emphasized the underworld of Nyx, escape mechanics, and the mortal arena-combat culture of the plane. The Arena Trickster's flavor text — *"It's not enough to surprise your opponent. You have to delight the crowd as well."* — captures Theros's theatrical public spectacle of gladiatorial contests, where performance is as valued as victory [card frontmatter flavor_text field; Theros Beyond Death set page via WebSearch snippet].
- **Mechanics** — Arena Trickster is a {3}{R} 3/3 Human Shaman with a flash-responsive trigger: whenever you cast your first spell during each opponent's turn, it gains a +1/+1 counter. This is a "play on the opponent's turn" payoff common to flash-heavy red builds and decks with split-second or instant-speed spells. EDHREC tracks it in approximately 320 decks (0.01% of all), with very low Commander inclusion — the effect is too slow for most EDH contexts but functional in Standard-era draft where flash spells were common in THB [EDHREC: Arena Trickster; WebSearch snippet from MTGGoldfish's "Theros Beyond Death Instant-Speed Tricks"].
- **Artist** — Illustrated by PINDURSKI, a Polish digital artist whose work in the MTG card corpus spans multiple sets with a recognizable painterly, high-saturation style. The Arena Trickster composition — red-lit stone archway, theatrical mask, mid-laugh performer — is characteristic of PINDURSKI's dramatic-figure work [card frontmatter artist field; Scryfall THB-126].
- **Flavor text context** — The speaker is unnamed but clearly a Theros arena performer (a "trickster" in the Theros athletic-spectacle tradition). On Theros, arena combat is explicitly public entertainment tied to religious devotion — the gods watch and grant boons to warriors who earn crowd approval. The mask the figure holds is a theatrical prop rather than protective gear, underlining the performance-over-function ethos [MTG Wiki: Theros via WebSearch snippet].

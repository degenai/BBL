## Trivia

- **Set lore** — Eutropia is a canonical Theros character — a Human Wizard unique among Theros oracles in being favored by two gods simultaneously: Nylea (god of the hunt, nature, seasons) and Thassa (god of the sea). The MTG Wiki describes her with brown skin, pure white hair braided with red ribbons, milky white eyes, and red face paint, carrying one bowl in each hand — one glowing green for the forest, one blue for the ocean. `[MTG Wiki: Eutropia, via WebSearch snippet]`

- **Flavor text** — "Nylea speaks to her in the windblown leaves; Thassa in the rushing tides." The dual-god attribution is the defining fact of Eutropia's lore — most Theros priests and oracles serve a single patron deity; dual devotion is exceptional and marks her as a figure of unusual divine attention. `[Scryfall flavor_text: thb/216]`

- **Design** — Eutropia is a non-enchantment creature with the Constellation keyword, which triggers whenever an enchantment enters under your control. Mark Rosewater's "At Death's Door, Part 2" (Jan 6, 2020) explains that placing Constellation on non-enchantment cards was a deliberate Play Design compromise: Journey into Nyx originally only put the mechanic on enchantments, causing a feedback-loop balance concern. Cards like Eutropia — creatures with Constellation — were described as the key knob Play Design needed to balance the returning mechanic in THB. Rosewater even previewed a non-enchantment Constellation card specifically in that article. `[Wizards.com: Making Magic, "At Death's Door, Part 2", 2020-01-06]`

- **Mechanical reputation** — Eutropia holds EDHREC rank 12,824, placing her in the lower mid-tier of Commander popularity. She headlines a niche Simic (green-blue) Enchantress/Constellation archetype. Abe Sargent featured her as the subject of Budget Commander no. 52 at CoolStuffInc (January 14, 2020) immediately post-THB release, flagging her as a three-mana Constellation commander that permanently pumps creatures and grants flying. `[Scryfall edhrec_rank; CoolStuffInc: Budget Commander no. 52, Abe Sargent, 2020-01-14]`

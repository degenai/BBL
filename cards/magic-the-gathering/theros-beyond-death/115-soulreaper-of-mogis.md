## Trivia

- **Set / lore context — pantheon reference** — Mogis is the God of Slaughter on Theros, the dark twin of Iroas (god of victory). He appears as a massive four-horned minotaur in spiked bronze armor wielding an ebon greataxe, and is the patron deity of all Theros minotaurs. Mogis's worship involves brutal rites, ritual self-mutilation, and blood feasts, particularly during the blood moon (his "crimson eye") `[WebSearch snippet: MTG Wiki: Mogis via Fandom]`. This card is a direct expression of that cult — the Soulreaper is an Enchantment Creature (Minotaur Shaman) whose sole ability is sacrifice-to-draw, a literal trade of life for power in Mogis's name. **Wire: `[[theros-pantheon]]`** `[Scryfall type_line, flavor_text]`.
- **Flavor text** — "We offer to Mogis the blood of the weak, and in return he makes us strong." `[Scryfall]`. The transactional theology here — sacrificial tithe upward, martial power downward — is Mogis's defining relationship with his followers, and the card's oracle text enacts it mechanically: sacrifice a creature, draw a card.
- **Mechanical note** — As an Enchantment Creature, Soulreaper of Mogis triggers Constellation on entry. Its sacrifice effect ({2}{B}, sac a creature, draw a card) is a clean card-advantage engine, particularly in black decks already sacrificing creatures for other effects. Hipsters of the Coast rated it C, describing it as a time-buying option during board stalls `[Hipsters of the Coast: "Every Common in Theros Beyond Death Reviewed"]`.

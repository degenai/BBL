# Glory Bearers (Theros Beyond Death)

## Vision

![17-glory-bearers](../../_images/magic-the-gathering/theros-beyond-death/17-glory-bearers.png)

Two humanoid figures dressed in flowing blue-white robes face each other as they jointly carry a large ceramic amphora painted in Greek black-figure style with chevron banding. From the mouth of the vessel rises a blazing golden orb surrounded by curling arcs of magical energy, casting warm light across both figures. Small blue star-like sparkles are scattered across the figures and the air around them, consistent with the Theros constellation-magic aesthetic. The composition is a slightly elevated angle looking down on the pair in close-to-mid range. The background is a muted blue-grey atmospheric haze that keeps focus on the vessel and its light. The mood is ceremonial and reverent — these are attendants in the act of presenting or carrying a sacred object rather than combatants.

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Theros Beyond Death (January 24, 2020) returns to the Greek-mythology-inspired plane of Theros, this time centered on its Underworld. The set's narrative follows Elspeth Tirel escaping the afterlife after being murdered by Heliod at the end of the original Theros block. The enchantment-creature dual type on Glory Bearers signals Nyx-born or divinely marked status — a Theros-plane mechanical shorthand for creatures that carry a god's blessing in their literal type line. `[Scryfall: set_name=Theros Beyond Death, released_at=2020-01-24; Wizards of the Coast: THB Story Summary 2020-01-10]`
- **Heliod lore** — Heliod presided over law, justice, retribution, and the bonds of kinship on Theros. Per MTG Wiki, his devoted worshipers were mainly humans and Lagonna Band centaurs who worshipped him on the first clear-skied morning of every month, with the summer solstice marked by three days of feasting, weddings, and oaths of loyalty. In the THB storyline, Heliod is ultimately defeated by Elspeth and imprisoned beneath a boulder in the Underworld by Erebos — the sun god's sun-cult devotees above ground are left without a living patron. Heliod's divine weapon is Khrusor, the Sun Spear, which he used to cause the sun to rise and set. `[MTG Wiki: Heliod]`
- **Flavor text** — "We are bearers of the ancient light, keepers of sun-crowned Heliod's blessings. Fight, and the light will protect you!" The epithet "sun-crowned" is Heliod's canonical THB-era title, matching the god card Heliod, Sun-Crowned (THB no. 18). The speaker is an unnamed Heliod devotee, consistent with the card's Human Cleric type. `[Scryfall: flavor_text, thb/17]`
- **Design and reprint history** — Illustrated by Tyler Walpole, who contributed 8 cards to the THB set. Glory Bearers has been reprinted twice: Jumpstart 2022 (December 2, 2022, no. 191) and Foundations Jumpstart (November 15, 2024, no. 203), indicating ongoing value as a clean common-slot enchantment-creature in White weenie and go-wide shells. EDHREC rank 25,396. Legal in Pauper and Pauper Commander. `[Scryfall: prints search oracle_id 8f446660; Gatherer: J25 entry; Scryfall: edhrec_rank=25396]`

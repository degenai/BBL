## Trivia

- **Set context** — Theros Beyond Death (THB, January 24 2020) centers on the Returned: mortals who escaped Theros's Underworld but surrendered their identities in the process. They re-enter the living world with no memories, wearing golden funerary masks as surrogate selves. The name for this group — Noston — comes from the Greek "nostos," meaning "to return home" `[WebSearch snippet: MTG Wiki: Returned]`.
- **Flavor text** — "The Returned retain no memory of their identities, but sometimes they mindlessly attempt familiar tasks." This text is the canonical design statement of the Returned as a zombie typology: not undead-hungry, but hollow — muscle memory without self `[Scryfall]`.
- **Mechanical note** — Grim Physician is a 1/1 for a single {B} with a death trigger that gives -1/-1 to an opponent's creature — a design that reinforces the Returned's hollow persistence: a creature that does its damage on the way out, not while it stands. Hipsters of the Coast rated it C, noting it "hits 10/55 common creatures and trades with 11 two-toughness creatures in combat," making it a limited role-player in sacrifice-oriented black strategies `[Hipsters of the Coast: "Every Common in Theros Beyond Death Reviewed"]`.
- **Lore context** — The golden masks worn by the Returned are described as "the last gifts of the selves they left behind" — crafted from gold, the most common material in Erebos's realm `[WebSearch snippet: MTG Wiki: Returned]`. The physician here mindlessly goes through healing motions with no comprehension of who they are healing or why — one of THB's more unsettling depictions of the Returned condition.

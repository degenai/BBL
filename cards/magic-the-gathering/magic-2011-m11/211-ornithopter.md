## Trivia

- **Design history** — Ornithopter was first printed in Antiquities (ATQ, 1994-03-04), illustrated by Amy Weber, placing it among the oldest artifact creatures in Magic. The M11 printing (2010-07-16, illustrated by Franz Vohwinkel) is a reprint. As of 2026, Ornithopter has 29 total printings including Mirrodin, Magic 2010/2011/2015, Kaladesh Inventions (premium foil), Dominaria Remastered, The Brothers' War Retro Artifacts, and a Secret Lair Drop `[Scryfall: prints search]`
- **Flavor text** — "Regardless of the century, plane, or species, developing artificers never fail to invent the ornithopter." A meta-statement about the universality of the flying-machine concept, echoing Leonardo da Vinci's real-world ornithopter designs. The word derives from Greek: ornis (bird) + pteron (wing) `[Scryfall]`
- **Lore context** — In MTG canon, thopters (the creature type printed on this card) trace to Thran-era technology. The brothers Urza and Mishra worked on ornithopters salvaged from Tocasia's excavation site; the Brothers' War (2022 set) revisited this history directly. The M11 type line reads "Artifact Creature — Thopter", a creature type retroactively updated from the original Antiquities printing `[MTG Wiki: Thopter, via WebSearch]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — Zero-mana 0/2 flyer. Core to Modern Affinity and Hammer Time strategies as a free artifact body that reduces affinity-payoff costs and enables Turn 1 Sigarda's Aid equipment chains. Also Vintage/Legacy enabler for fast artifact-count starts. EDHREC rank 741 reflects Commander use in artifact-synergy and cost-reduction builds. Legal in Pauper, Modern, Legacy, Vintage `[Scryfall; MTGGoldfish: Budget Affinity, via WebSearch]`

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Get a Leg Up is a green Instant (MKM, no. 161, uncommon) from Murders at Karlov Manor (February 9, 2024), illustrated by Jesper Ejsing. The card name is a common English idiom meaning "to gain an advantage," here literalized as a centaur's leg serving as a step for a smaller companion reaching for a high shelf `[Scryfall: mkm/161]`.
- **Mechanical profile** — Until end of turn, a target creature gets +1/+1 for each creature you control and gains reach. As a go-wide payoff instant, it scales with board presence: in a board of five creatures, it becomes +5/+5 plus reach, making it a credible combat swing or a reach-blocker for flying threats. The scaling makes it particularly potent in token-flood strategies `[Scryfall: mkm/161 oracle_text]`.
- **Flavor text** — "Apologies for using you like a stepladder. On the other hand, thank you, you're a wonderful stepladder!" A comedic note that literalizes the card's pun — the speaker both apologizes for the indignity and sincerely appreciates the assistance. Speaker is unnamed; likely the elf-eared figure in the art based on scene context (she is the one reaching the shelf, presumably speaking back to the centaur) `[Scryfall: flavor_text field, mkm/161]`.
- **Artist** — Jesper Ejsing is a Danish fantasy illustrator who has contributed artwork to MTG since the Lorwyn era. He maintains an active blog at Muddy Colors discussing his creative process. Scryfall credits him with at least two cards in MKM `[artofmtg.com: Jesper Ejsing artist page]` `[planeswalkersmtg.com: MKM artist credits]`.

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Murders at Karlov Manor (set code MKM, released 2024-02-09) returns to Ravnica in the Omenpath Arc, set after the Phyrexian Invasion. The plot is a whodunit: Teysa Karlov — Orzhov guildmaster, owner of One Karlov Place — is assassinated, and her murder is eventually traced to a grief-maddened Trostani. Sanguine Savior's Vampire Cleric creature type is native to the Orzhov clerical hierarchy that dominates the manor setting. The `agency` character tag on this card refers to the Ravnican Agency of Magicological Investigations, the detective faction standing up to work the case. `[Wizards story episodes via magic.wizards.com/en/news/magic-story/; WebSearch snippet: draftsim.com/mtg-murders-at-karlov-manor-story/; Scryfall API]`

- **Disguise design** — Disguise is a deliberate mechanical upgrade of Morph. The key addition is ward 2. Per the Wizards MKM mechanics article, the design team chose ward 2 because it paired cleanly with the 2/2 body (same number, easy to remember) and provided just enough protection to make flipping viable without changing the face-down trading math between opposing creatures. Sanguine Savior's disguise cost of (W/B)(W/B) is Orzhov hybrid-locked — both guild colors required to reveal — mechanically enforcing the card's faction identity at the moment of transformation. `[Wizards of the Coast: "Murders at Karlov Manor Mechanics" via magic.wizards.com/en/news/feature/murders-at-karlov-manor-mechanics, WebSearch snippet; Scryfall API oracle_text]`

- **"A Mysterious Creature" — a morph-family first** — MKM shipped a dedicated physical reminder card for face-down creatures: "A Mysterious Creature" (token no. TMKM 21, illustrated by Ben Hill), included in Play Boosters as a cover card to place over any disguised or cloaked creature and remind both players of ward 2. Previous morph-family sets (Onslaught, Khans of Tarkir, Magic Origins) had no equivalent physical helper. Scryfall's API lists A Mysterious Creature as a `combo_piece` related card to Sanguine Savior directly. `[Wizards of the Coast: "The Tokens of Murders at Karlov Manor" via magic.wizards.com; Scryfall API all_parts field]`

- **Mechanical profile** — EDHREC rank 20,926 with approximately 1,409 EDH decks at time of research — low-end for a set common. Its niche is WB vampire tribal and lifelink-matters builds; EDHREC lists Stromkirk Bloodthief, Sanguine Syphoner, and Wispdrinker Vampire as its most common synergy partners. Pauper-legal; set-review consensus from MKM Pauper coverage was that disguise commons rarely clear the format's tempo bar — the 3-mana face-down plus hybrid flip cost competes poorly against Pauper's efficient threats. `[EDHREC card page meta via WebSearch snippet (edhrec.com/cards/sanguine-savior); Scryfall API edhrec_rank + legalities fields; WebSearch snippet: coolstuffinc.com and mtg.cardsrealm.com Pauper reviews]`

- **Artist** — Valera Lutfullina illustrated at least five cards in MKM: Sanguine Savior (no. 230), Tomik, Wielder of Law (no. 431, a named Orzhov legendary), Hustle // Bustle (no. 249), Illicit Masquerade (no. 88 and art series AMKM 8), and Voja, Jaws of the Conclave (no. 432, a Selesnya mythic). That range spans common through mythic and crosses multiple guilds. Illicit Masquerade thematically echoes this card's Disguise mechanic and hidden-identity throughline. No Hipsters of the Coast profile or published artist statement was found for Lutfullina. `[Scryfall search results via WebSearch snippet; Scryfall API artist field]`

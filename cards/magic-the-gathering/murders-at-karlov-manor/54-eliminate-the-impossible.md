## Trivia

- **Set context** — Murders at Karlov Manor (MKM) was released February 9, 2024 as Magic's first top-down murder mystery set, built around the tropes of detective fiction on Ravnica. Head designer Mark Rosewater later described it as one of the worst-performing sets "in many years," reflecting that the team did "a little less murder mystery, and a little more traditional Ravnica" than they should have — the genre theming proved "less of an environmental setting than we realized." The set's underperformance makes a second murder mystery set on Ravnica unlikely in the near term. `[Wargamer / TheGamer, citing MaRo post-mortem; Wizards: Getting Away with Murders at Karlov Manor Part 1]`

- **Literary source** — The card title is a direct lift of the most famous line in detective fiction: "How often have I said to you that when you have eliminated the impossible, whatever remains, however improbable, must be the truth?" — Sherlock Holmes to Watson, Chapter 6 ("Sherlock Holmes Gives a Demonstration") of Arthur Conan Doyle's *The Sign of the Four*, first published in Lippincott's Monthly Magazine in 1890. Doyle reused variations in "The Beryl Coronet" (1892) and "The Blanched Soldier" (1926), but the full canonical phrasing originates in Chapter 6. `[Quote Investigator; LitCharts: The Sign of the Four Chapter 6; Wikisource: The Sign of the Four/Chapter 6]`

- **Flavor text** — "Half the city had motive. Time to narrow down who also had the means and opportunity." `[Scryfall]` This is not a Doyle quote — it is original MKM flavor invoking the classic criminal-investigation trinity of means, motive, and opportunity (M.M.O.), the three criteria investigators use to establish guilt. The card title sets the Holmesian frame; the flavor text delivers the procedural grammar of the deduction inside it.

- **Mechanic note** — The oracle text's "if any of them are suspected, they're no longer suspected" is a counterintuitive design wrinkle. The suspect mechanic in MKM gives a creature menace and strips its ability to block. Most suspect-granting effects are offensive tools; Eliminate the Impossible is one of the few MKM cards that actively *removes* suspected status — restoring opponents' creatures to blocking duty while simultaneously weakening them at -2/-0 and netting a Clue token. In a set built around suspects, a card that de-suspects is a logical paradox made playable: the detective closes the case, clearing the board of suspicion. `[Wizards: murders-at-karlov-manor-mechanics]`

- **Agency / R.A.M.I. context** — The card is associated with the Ravnican Agency of Magicological Investigations (R.A.M.I.), which grew from a small cult following the archon Ezrim and expanded rapidly after the Phyrexian invasion weakened the guilds. The post-invasion civic vacuum allowed independent detective agencies to take over functions previously monopolized by guild structures. `[Wizards: Planeswalker's Guide to Murders at Karlov Manor]`

- **Format legality** — Legal in Pioneer and Standard at release; not legal in Pauper (Uncommon). `[Scryfall legalities / WebSearch via cardsrealm]`

- **Artist** — Carlos Palma Cruchaga has illustrated 34+ Magic cards including *Case of the Market Melee* (MKM), *Life from the Loam* (Ravnica Remastered), and basic lands for Bloomburrow. No Hipsters of the Coast artist spotlight was found for this artist as of research date. His portfolio is at ArtStation (handle: cpcwolf). `[Gatherer artist search; WebSearch]`

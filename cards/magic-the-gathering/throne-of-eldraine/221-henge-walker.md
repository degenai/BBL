## Trivia

- **Set context** — Henge Walker is a first (and only) printing from *Throne of Eldraine* (ELD, released October 2019). It is one of two colorless-artifact common golems in ELD (alongside Clockwork Servant) that anchor the set's artifact-creature baseline. The card is associated with Garenbrig, the green-aligned court of Eldraine and home to the Great Henge — a massive legendary artifact that serves as the court's source of wild magic. [Scryfall: Henge Walker]
- **Flavor text** — "Everything that inhabits Garenbrig is affected by the wild magic flowing through the Great Henge." — no named speaker. The flavor connects the stone golem directly to the Great Henge as its animating source: the creature is not built in a workshop but grown in place by ambient environmental magic. [Scryfall: flavor_text]
- **The Great Henge connection** — The Great Henge (ELD no. 161) is one of the most expensive and sought-after cards from Throne of Eldraine, a legendary artifact that generates mana, puts +1/+1 counters on creatures, and draws cards. Henge Walker is named for and flavored as a product of the same location. The cheap common golem and the expensive legendary land-artifact are the same place's two outputs — the henge makes the walker. [Scryfall: The Great Henge; WebSearch snippet]
- **Mechanic** — Adamant (enters with a +1/+1 counter if you spent at least three mana of the same color) is the set mechanic for ELD, rewarding mono-color commitment. As a colorless-cost creature, Henge Walker can trigger Adamant with any single color's mana, making it mechanically neutral to court affiliation. EDHREC rank 25,605 — very low Commander presence; most played in bulk playsets for aggressive artifact or Pauper-legal decks. Legal in Pauper and Paupercommander. [Scryfall: EDHREC rank 25,605; legalities]
- **Artist** — Illustrated by Titus Lunter, known for sweeping landscape-focused MTG illustrations. The composition emphasizes the golem against the forest backdrop rather than close creature detail — a landscape-first framing consistent with Lunter's style. [Scryfall: artist field]

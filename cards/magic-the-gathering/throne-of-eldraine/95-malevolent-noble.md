## Trivia

- **Set context** — Throne of Eldraine (ELD, released 2019-10-04) is designed as "Camelot meets Grimms' Fairy Tales," with between 75 and 100 cards in the set referencing specific fairy tales top-down. The darker Grimm-register cards — black-bordered, horror-adjacent — sit deliberately in the shadow of the Brothers Grimm source material rather than the Arthurian light. `[Wizards.com: More Odds & Ends: Throne of Eldraine, Rosewater, 2019-10-21]`

- **Fairy-tale source** — Malevolent Noble is a Bluebeard reference with a double inversion. Perrault's 1697 *Bluebeard* is structured around a wealthy nobleman who lures victims into trusting him before killing them in a hidden chamber. This card applies the same structure but targets witches, weaponizing their own appetite for children as the lure. TV Tropes catalogues it under "The Bluebeard" as an "Adaptational Heroism" variant: the serial-killer noble "specifically preys on Wicked Witches lured with the promise of children to devour," producing an evil-vs-evil dynamic characteristic of Eldraine's fractured fairy-tale register. `[TV Tropes: The Bluebeard, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Scryfall: flavor_text]`

- **Lore grounding** — The lure works because Wizards made witch predation toward children explicit in the set's official lore. The Planeswalker's Guide to Eldraine (Wizards, 2019-10-31) describes witches as "shadowy human warlocks that reside in the wilds" who "take great joy in their evil deeds" and are known for stealing children and poisoning towns. The Noble's promise of "fresh children's bones" is credible bait precisely because that appetite is canonical. `[Wizards.com: Planeswalker's Guide to Eldraine, 2019-10-31]`

- **Design: Noble subtype** — Noble first appeared on Faerie Noble (Homelands, 1995), was stripped in the Grand Creature Type Update, and was reintroduced in Throne of Eldraine. Mark Rosewater's exact framing: "The game just has a lot of people whose job it is to rule over others, and it felt weird not to have a class that covered that. Throne of Eldraine was full of kings and queens, princes and princesses, so it seemed like the right time to finally add Noble." `[Wizards.com: More Odds & Ends: Throne of Eldraine, Rosewater, 2019-10-21]` `[MTG Wiki: Noble, via WebSearch snippet]`

- **Artist** — Randy Vargas is a Cuban-born science fiction and fantasy illustrator based in Barcelona, with over 150 Magic cards and concept work for Valve. In 2019 — the same year as Malevolent Noble — his oil-on-hardboard original for Marit Lage's Slumber (Modern Horizons) sold at auction for $4,400. Hipsters of the Coast reported the sale placed Vargas "on the traditional art collector's radar." `[Hipsters of the Coast: "Marit Lage's Slumber by Randy Vargas Sells for $4,400," June 2019]` `[WebSearch: Randy Vargas biography]`

- **Mechanical standing** — EDHREC rank 25,845; appears in approximately 355 Commander decks (roughly 0.0% of decks tracked). Single printing, never reprinted. Legal in Pauper and Pauper Commander, where black sacrifice shells are active, but does not feature among the commonly cited Pauper aristocrats staples. `[Scryfall: edhrec_rank, legalities]` `[EDHREC card page, via WebSearch snippet]`

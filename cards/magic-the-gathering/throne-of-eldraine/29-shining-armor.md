## Trivia

- **Set context** — Throne of Eldraine (ELD, October 2019) built a cycle of common Equipment with colored mana costs but generic equip costs. Shining Armor (no. 29) is the white member, alongside Crystal Slipper (the Cinderella-referencing red Equipment). The design intention was to push monocolor identity at common while keeping equip cost flexible — the colored cost in the casting, not the equipping. `[Wizards: "Singing in the Eldraine," 2019-09-16]`
- **Design** — Shining Armor has Flash, which is mechanically unusual for Equipment: it allows the armor to be cast as an instant-speed ambush, attaching to any Knight you control mid-combat. The auto-attach on-enter clause (targeting a Knight you control) rewards having Knights in play and makes the card feel like a rescue — the armor arrives just in time. The +0/+2 and vigilance bonus leans defensive: it's a guardian's armor, not a weapon. `[Scryfall]`
- **Mechanical note** — No flavor text. EDHREC rank 19,934; the card sees only modest Commander play. In ELD Limited it was a useful flash blocker-pump for Knight-heavy white decks. First and only printing (no reprints as of Scryfall oracle search). Legal in Pioneer, Modern, Legacy, Vintage, Pauper, and Commander. `[Scryfall]`
- **Artist** — Eric Deschamps is a long-tenured MTG illustrator who joined the game in Innistrad (2011) and has painted hundreds of cards, frequently handling high-production fantasy portraits and character pieces. Shining Armor is a single-figure portrait in his cleanly heroic mode. No dedicated artist feature on Hipsters of the Coast was found for this card specifically. `[Scryfall]`

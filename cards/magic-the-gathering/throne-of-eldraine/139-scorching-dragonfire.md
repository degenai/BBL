## Trivia

- **Set context** — Throne of Eldraine (ELD, released 2019-10-04) is set on the fairy-tale plane of Eldraine, divided between the knightly Realm (five courts: Ardenvale, Vantress, Locthwain, Garenbrig, Embereth) and the magical Wilds of the Fae. Ardenvale is the white-aligned court of loyalty and knighthood. `[Scryfall]` `[magic.wizards.com: Planeswalker's Guide to Eldraine]`
- **Flavor text** — "As the blaze raced toward her, Syr Ameril closed her eyes and pictured Castle Ardenvale so her last thoughts would be of home." 'Syr' is the honorific for knights of the Realm in Eldraine. Syr Ameril has no independent card; she exists only through this vignette — a common ELD design pattern of populating the world with named figures in flavor text who never appear as game pieces. `[Scryfall]`
- **Narrative pairing** — Castle Ardenvale is a real card in the same set (ELD, no. 238) — a land that produces white mana and creates 1/1 Human soldier tokens. The flavor text names it as a knight's last thought of home, making the two cards a micro-narrative pair within the set: one card is the home, the other is the moment someone dies wishing they were there. `[Scryfall: Castle Ardenvale ELD/238]`
- **Mechanical** — The exile clause ('if that creature or planeswalker would die this turn, exile it instead') prevents graveyard recursion on the killed target — relevant in the ELD Standard environment that featured Cauldron Familiar and other sacrifice-value loops. EDHREC rank 11,946 — meaningful commander play. `[Scryfall]`

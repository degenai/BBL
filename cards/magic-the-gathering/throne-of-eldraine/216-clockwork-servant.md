## Trivia

- **Set context** — Clockwork Servant is a first (and only) printing from *Throne of Eldraine* (ELD, released October 2019), a fairy-tale plane overlaid onto Arthurian legend. Eldraine features five distinct knightly courts plus the Wilds — the clockwork gnome is native to the court aesthetic, representing the castle's mechanical service infrastructure rather than any particular faction's military. [Scryfall: Clockwork Servant]
- **Mechanic** — Adamant (draw a card if you spent at least three mana of the same color to cast it) was Throne of Eldraine's set mechanic, designed to reward mono-color devotion in a set otherwise built around five-color court identities. As a colorless-cost artifact, Clockwork Servant can trigger Adamant with any color's mana — it is agnostic to which court employs it. The Adamant reward (card draw on a 2/3 body for 3 mana) makes it a utility piece in mono-color Commander shells. [Scryfall: oracle_text; ELD set mechanics context]
- **Flavor text** — "Some are annoyed by the constant ticking as they clean the cobbles, but to the castle custodians, there's no sweeter sound." — no named speaker. The unnamed custodians who find the ticking sweet are implicitly those who benefit from the labor without performing it. [Scryfall]
- **Mechanical reputation** — EDHREC rank 22,285 — low-end Commander presence, primarily in artifact-matters and colorless utility builds. Legal in Pioneer, Modern, Historic, Legacy, Commander. Not Pauper-legal (uncommon). [Scryfall: EDHREC rank 22,285; legalities]
- **Artist** — Illustrated by David Palumbo, a prolific MTG illustrator known for detailed character work. No specific design commentary on this card found. [Scryfall: artist field]
- **Artist deeper context** — Palumbo made his MTG debut in Shards of Alara (2008) and has illustrated 127+ cards across 30+ sets. He is the son of illustrator Julie Bell and stepson of Boris Vallejo, both defining figures in 1980s-1990s fantasy/sci-fi cover art, and studied oil painting at the Pennsylvania Academy of Fine Art. His career has earned a Chesley Award and three Spectrum medals. Clockwork Servant appears to be his sole contribution to Throne of Eldraine. [Wikipedia: David Palumbo, via WebSearch; Gatherer artist search via WebSearch]
- **Design pair** — Clockwork Servant and Henge Walker (ELD no. 221, Common Golem, illustrated by Titus Lunter) form a deliberate pair of colorless-cost Adamant artifact creatures in ELD — both cost {3}, both are 2/X bodies, both reward mono-color casting. Their Adamant payoffs diverge: Clockwork Servant draws a card; Henge Walker enters with a +1/+1 counter. Henge Walker's flavor text ties it to Garenbrig and the Great Henge. This pair sits at the design inflection point Wizards documented in the ELD Vision Design Handoff Part 2: the team was executing "a conscious shift to move toward more colored artifacts," using Adamant as the mechanism that rewards mono-color commitment without locking the casting cost to a color. [Wizards.com: Throne of Eldraine Vision Design Handoff Part 2, 2019-11-18; Scryfall: ELD no. 221 Henge Walker, via WebSearch]
- **Type lineage** — Clockwork Servant's direct type-lineage predecessor is Clockwork Gnomes (Homelands, 1995, HML no. 102) — also an Artifact Creature — Gnome, built by students at Feroz's Wizards' School on Ulgrotha as mechanical servants on the Floating Isle. Homelands' Clockwork Gnomes carry flavor text attributed to Baki, a Wizard Attendant: "Until I came to the Floating Isle, I never had to oil the servants." The servant-labor framing of the gnome automaton runs from 1995 Homelands to 2019 Eldraine. Fewer than 33 Gnome-subtype cards exist in all of MTG history. [Scryfall: HML no. 102 Clockwork Gnomes, via WebSearch; MTG Wiki: Clockwork creature, via WebSearch]

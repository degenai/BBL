## Trivia

- **Set context** — Throne of Eldraine (ELD) was released October 4, 2019. Oko's Accomplices is a Planeswalker Deck exclusive — it appears only in the "Oko, Thief of Crowns" Planeswalker Deck (collector numbers 309-313), not in draft boosters. It shares the deck's green-blue color identity with Oko, the Trickster, the Planeswalker Deck's simplified Oko version [Scryfall ELD-310; Wizards.com: "Throne of Eldraine Planeswalker Decks" 2019-09-18].
- **Lore** — The flavor text reads: *"They function as everything from assassins to jesters in the name of their fae king."* Oko in ELD lore is a shapeshifter fey planeswalker from a plane of suppressed Fae; he arrived on Eldraine after his spark ignited during a magical procedure meant to dampen his abilities. Community and lore coverage describes Oko as playing an Oberon-adjacent role in the ELD fairy-tale framework — a fae king whose followers perform whatever role he requires [card frontmatter flavor_text field; MTG Wiki: Oko; Bell of Lost Souls: "MTG Lore — The Tale of Oko" 2024].
- **Mechanical note** — A {2}{U} 2/3 Flying faerie creature is a solid Limited body (high toughness for cost in blue) but sees essentially no Constructed or Commander play outside of Faerie tribal; it is intentionally simple as Planeswalker Deck cards are designed to be entry-level introductory pieces [MTGSalvation: Oko's Accomplices card listing; Scryfall ELD-310 format legalities].
- **Oko's banning context** — Oko, Thief of Crowns (ELD-197) was banned from Standard and Brawl in November 2019 — one of the fastest Standard bannings in modern MTG history. The Planeswalker Deck's "Oko, the Trickster" (ELD-309) is a mechanically unrelated, much weaker card and was not banned. Oko's Accomplices thus exists as a card permanently linked to a banned planeswalker's product, which is an unusual position in the corpus [Wizards.com November 2019 banning announcement, via WebSearch summary].

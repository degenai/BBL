## Trivia

- **Set context** — Master the Way is a Jeskai (Blue-Red) sorcery from Khans of Tarkir (2014-09-26). The Jeskai Way is the clan of cunning and enlightenment, symbolized by the eye of the dragon. Their philosophy — "The Way" — prizes wisdom and disciplined mastery over brute force. `[Wizards: Planeswalker's Guide to Khans of Tarkir, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Flavor text** — "The Way has no beginning and no end. It is simply the path." — Narset, khan of the Jeskai. `[Scryfall]` Narset is described in MTG Wiki as a human monk and former planeswalker — neurodivergent, with an "insatiable thirst for knowledge" — who served as Jeskai khan in the original Khans of Tarkir timeline before planeswalking, then became desparked in a later story arc. `[MTG Wiki: Narset, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design** — Illustrated by Howard Lyon, who posted the card on his personal blog on 2014-09-12 and described it as "one of my favorite cards of late," adding that he "really enjoyed painting the background" and hoped the setting read as beautiful. Lyon planned a follow-up process post. `[howardlyon.com/blog/2014/9/12/khans-of-tarkir-master-the-way]`
- **Mechanical note** — The card draw-then-deal-damage mechanic directly rewards hand-size, making it best in decks that stockpile cards. At 5 mana it competes for space in hand-size and Jeskai spell-storm builds but sees only modest Commander play (EDHREC rank 16,954). `[Scryfall]` `[EDHREC: edhrec.com/cards/master-the-way]`

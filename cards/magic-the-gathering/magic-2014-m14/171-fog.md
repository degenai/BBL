## Trivia

- **Set context** — Fog appeared in Magic 2014, a core set released July 19, 2013. The M14 core set was designed as an entry-level product with introductory staples reprinted from across Magic's history alongside new cards. Fog has been reprinted continuously in core sets from Alpha through M14, making it one of the most reprinted cards in the game. [Scryfall]
- **Design history** — Fog is an Alpha original, first printed in Limited Edition Alpha (1993). The "fog effect" — preventing all combat damage for a turn — is named after this card within Wizards R&D; it is considered primary in green and tertiary in white in Magic's color pie. Variants include Darkness (black), Angelsong (white), and Clinging Mists (green with fateful hour). By 2016, Fog was reprinted in Eternal Masters (no. 167), cementing its status as a format-spanning classic. [Draftsim: "The 29 Best Fog Cards in Magic Ranked"; Scryfall]
- **Mechanical reputation** — Fog holds EDHREC rank 729, appearing in a meaningful slice of Commander decks, primarily in turbo-fog archetypes and green creature strategies that need a combat escape valve. Its one-mana cost keeps it relevant even when more powerful fogs exist; EDHREC notes that Undergrowth is considered a functional upgrade in red-green decks but Fog remains the baseline reference. [EDHREC: edhrec.com/cards/fog]
- **Flavor text** — "I fear no army or beast, but only the morning fog. Our assault can survive everything else." — Lord Hilneth. Lord Hilneth is not a named lore figure in any Wizards-published story; the quote is a flavor-text-only attribution. [Scryfall]
- **Artist** — Jaime Jones illustrated the M14 printing. Jones is a prolific MTG contributor with over 100 card credits, known for atmospheric, large-scale fantasy compositions. The M14 Fog is among his more atmospheric pieces — a near-monochrome mist study with almost no color. [Scryfall; artofmtg.com/artist/jaime-jones]

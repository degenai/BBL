## Trivia

- **Set context** — Giant Growth appeared in Magic 2014, released July 19, 2013. The card has been printed in nearly every core set since Alpha (1993), absent only from Magic 2012 onward when it was succeeded by Titanic Growth (+3/+3 with trample at two mana). The M14 printing marks one of its last appearances in a standard-legal core set. [Scryfall]
- **Design history** — Giant Growth is one of the five cards in the original Boon Cycle from Limited Edition Alpha: a horizontal cycle of 1-mana instants each producing an effect involving the number 3, one per color. The five are Healing Salve (white), Ancestral Recall (blue), Dark Ritual (black), Lightning Bolt (red), and Giant Growth (green). The cycle is famous for its dramatic power disparity: Ancestral Recall is Power Nine, Lightning Bolt is one of the best burn spells ever printed, and Giant Growth sits in the middle — solid but not broken. Designed by Sandra Everingham. [MTG Wiki via WebSearch; Draftsim: "Boon in MTG"]
- **Mechanical reputation** — Giant Growth is legal in Pauper and sees play there as a combat trick and "pump" spell. Its EDHREC rank of 3,636 reflects modest Commander adoption — it is generally considered outclassed by pump spells with additional effects (trample, hexproof, draw) at the same or similar cost, but it remains a draft and Pauper staple. [Scryfall; EDHREC rank from Scryfall edhrec_rank field]
- **Artist** — The M14 illustration is by Matt Cavotta, a former Wizards of the Coast Art Director and Creative Director who also wrote the weekly column "Taste the Magic" on MagicTheGathering.com (2005-2007). Cavotta was hired by Mark Rosewater in 2004 and has over 100 MTG card credits as an illustrator. The M14 Giant Growth is one of his works depicting the card's druid-channels-bear-spirit scene. [Wikipedia: Matt Cavotta; artofmtg.com/artist/matt-cavotta]

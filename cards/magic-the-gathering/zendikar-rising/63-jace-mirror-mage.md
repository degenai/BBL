## Trivia

- **Set context** — Zendikar Rising (ZNR) is the 85th Magic expansion, released 2020-09-25 `[Scryfall]`. The set's main story follows Jace and Nissa pursuing the lithomancer Nahiri to a Murasa Skyclave, where Nahiri tries to use the Lithoform Core to reshape Zendikar into a stable, prosperous plane; the arc ends in an all-out battle after Nissa destroys the focal point `[MTG Wiki: Jace Beleren, Zendikar Rising section]`.
- **Design** — First planeswalker ever printed with the kicker mechanic, and at 3 mana an unusually cheap legendary 'walker; kicked for 2 extra mana, it enters with a non-legendary copy token of itself (starting loyalty 1) `[Star City Games: "Zendikar Rising Introduces Jace, Mirror Mage"]`. Kicker's return in ZNR was its first appearance since Dominaria (2018) `[Star City Games]`.
- **Design** — Art by Tyler Jacobson; mythic rare; starting loyalty 4 `[Scryfall]`. Legal in Pioneer, Modern, Legacy, Vintage, and Commander; rotated out of Standard `[Scryfall legalities]`.
- **Mechanical reputation** — Listed in roughly 17,400+ Commander decks on EDHREC at about a 0.41% inclusion rate; reviewed as a flexible cheap card-selection 'walker rather than a stax piece, giving "draw-go control" tempo without locking opponents out `[EDHREC: Jace, Mirror Mage, via WebSearch snippet]`. A Pojo review at release rated it 3.75 Constructed / 4.00 Casual / 3.50 Limited / 3.67 Commander `[Pojo: "Jace, Mirror Mage – Zendikar Rising MTG Review", via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Mechanical reputation** — Scryfall rulings note a deliberately unusual legend-rule interaction: "You can control one legendary Jace, Mirror Mage in addition to any number of nonlegendary copies of Jace, Mirror Mage" — the copy tokens dodge the legend rule by design, which is rare for a planeswalker `[Scryfall rulings, 2020-09-25]`.

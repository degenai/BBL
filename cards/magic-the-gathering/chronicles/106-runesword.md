## Trivia

- **Set context** — Runesword was first printed in The Dark (August 1994), Magic's sixth expansion set. The Dark is set in the aftermath of the Brothers' War Sylex Blast, depicting a world plunged into cold and shadow where anti-magic religious persecution spread across the land. The set's gothic, medieval-European aesthetic gave cards like Runesword their dark-fantasy tone. `[MTG Wiki: The Dark, via WebSearch]`
- **Reprint in Chronicles** — The copy in the inventory is from Chronicles (July 1995), Magic's first-ever reprint compilation set. Chronicles reprinted cards from Arabian Nights, Antiquities, Legends, and The Dark — and was enormously controversial: its flood of formerly expensive cards onto the market cratered values overnight and directly prompted Wizards to create the Reserved List. Runesword was uncommon in The Dark but appears as a common in Chronicles, reflecting the set's blanket rarity downgrades. `[MTG Wiki: Chronicles, via WebSearch; Scryfall]`
- **Artist legacy** — Runesword was illustrated by Christopher Rush (1965–2016), one of the original 25 Magic: The Gathering artists. Rush is most famous for painting Black Lotus — the most valuable card in the game — and also contributed to the design of the five mana-color symbols. He worked on dozens of early-set cards spanning Alpha through The Dark. `[Wikipedia: Christopher Rush, via WebSearch]`
- **Printing curiosity** — Approximately one-third of Runesword copies printed in The Dark carry a documented misprint: an extraneous, uneven black line runs across the text box, resembling a strand of hair caught in the printing press. This variant is catalogued by collectors and is the more commonly circulated Chronicles copy's defining contrast — the Chronicles reprint is clean. `[WebSearch: Scryfall card page; eBay misprint listings]`

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Aetherdrift (set code DFT, released 2025-02-14) is built around the Ghirapur Grand Prix, a death-defying interplanar race for the Aetherspark — an artificial planeswalker spark created on Avishkar during the New Phyrexian Invasion. The course crosses three planes: Amonkhet, Avishkar, and Muraganda `[Scryfall]` `[magic.wizards.com: Planeswalker's Guide to Aetherdrift Part 1, via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Set context** — Muraganda is the Aetherdrift leg this ooze most likely inhabits: a primordial plane teased for 17 years before its first set appearance, full of dinosaurs, oozes, and prehistoric life, with the Grand Prix running on an elevated track above its proteogenic jungles `[MTG Rocks: "MTG Plane Teased For 17 Years Confirmed As Third Aetherdrift Location", via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Flavor text** — `"As it digested the demonic roadster, it too learned the thrill of speed."` — Scryfall, no attributed speaker. The "demonic roadster" reads as one of the Speed Demons' specter-haunted vehicles: that team is Duskmourn's racing crew of possessed drivers led by Winter under the archdemon Valgavoth's compulsion, so the ooze has eaten one of their cars off the track `[Scryfall]` `[MTG Wiki: Speed Demons, via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Mechanical reputation** — Draftsim's Aetherdrift Limited review: Ooze Patrol "can be quite large in the right deck, but if you just cast it empty, it's a Hill Giant on average, so you should try to do some trading or self-milling early before playing it." It pairs into the set's black-green graveyard/reanimator archetype `[Draftsim: Ultimate Aetherdrift Limited Set Review, via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Design** — Art by Forrest Schehl, who illustrated three cards in Aetherdrift. Uncommon, mana cost `{3}{G}`, 2/2 base body, EDHREC rank 19255 at time of writing — low Commander adoption `[Scryfall]` `[mtg.wtf: Forrest Schehl, via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Community resonance** — Community sentiment on the set: Aetherdrift is "secretly stacked with great graveyard support," with Ooze Patrol grouped alongside Molt Tender, Dredger's Insight, and Quag Feast as the green self-mill suite feeding the BG reanimator pile. Treated as a payoff card, not a standalone threat `[MTG Rocks: "MTG Aetherdrift Is Secretly Stacked With Great Graveyard Support", via WebSearch snippet]`.

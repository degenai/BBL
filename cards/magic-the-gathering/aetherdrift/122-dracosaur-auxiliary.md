## Trivia

- **Set context** — Aetherdrift (set code DFT, no. 122/272) is the 103rd MTG expansion, released 2026-02-14 and built around the Ghirapur Grand Prix, a multiplanar "death race" across Avishkar (formerly Kaladesh), Amonkhet, and Muraganda. Vision design was led by Mark Rosewater, who has noted the death-race concept had been in discussion for over a decade and only became producible once the Omenpath travel framework existed in canon `[Wizards: "You Get the Aetherdrift, Part 1"]` `[Scryfall: set page dft]`.
- **Set context** — Muraganda is the "primordial plane" first teased 17 years before its Aetherdrift debut — a wild early-world of dinosaurs, oozes, and raw magic. Muragandan dinosaurs are drawn with deliberately exaggerated proportions to distinguish them visually from Ixalan's. The Wizards flavor framing of Muragandan racers is as raiders who "never signed nor would agree to the treaty terms" of the local Saurid Autocracy `[MTG Wiki: Muraganda, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Wargamer: "Muraganda revealed by Rosewater"]`.
- **Design / mechanic lineage** — Mount (creature type) and Saddle (keyword) were introduced together in Outlaws of Thunder Junction (set OTJ, 2025-04-19), NOT in Lord of the Rings: Tales of Middle-earth — LTR introduced "The Ring Tempts You" and zero Mount-typed creatures. Aetherdrift is Mount/Saddle's first return appearance after OTJ. Saddle is the Mount-side analog of Vehicles/Crew (which premiered on Kaladesh/Avishkar), but unlike Vehicles, Mounts are already creatures and can attack or block without being saddled `[Wizards: "Outlaws of Thunder Junction Mechanics"]` `[Wizards: "Aetherdrift Mechanics"]` `[Scryfall search: e:otj t:mount returns 17 cards, e:ltr t:mount returns 0]`.
- **Mechanical reputation** — 4/4 flying-haste Dinosaur Dragon Mount for {4}{R}{R} with Saddle 3 and a "deals 2 to any target on attack while saddled" trigger. Limited reviewers tag it as one of red's best uncommons in Aetherdrift: "a solid Volcanic Dragon variant with upside if you can saddle it" `[Draftsim: "The Ultimate Aetherdrift Limited Set Review"]` and best-of-the-bunch among red uncommons in Sealed `[CardsRealm: "Aetherdrift Limited Guide"]`. EDHREC rank 19,722 indicates negligible Commander adoption — the card lives in Limited, not 100-card singleton `[Scryfall: edhrec_rank field]`.
- **Design** — Art by Brian Valeza, a Philippines-based freelance illustrator with 233+ Magic credits plus work across D&D, Hearthstone, Pathfinder, and Artifact `[ArtStation: tots.artstation.com]` `[Art of MTG: artofmtg.com/artist/brian-valeza]`. The card has no flavor text on Scryfall — none should be invented. The name "Dracosaur" is a flavor label for a Dinosaur Dragon hybrid, not a creature subtype: across all of MTG, only two cards carry it — Dracosaur Auxiliary and Bonehoard Dracosaur (Lost Caverns of Ixalan, no. 134) — making it a deliberate cross-plane archetype rather than a typeline keyword `[Scryfall: name search returns 2 cards]`.

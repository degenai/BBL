## Trivia

- **Set context** — *Aetherdrift* (set code DFT, released 2025-02-14) is a multi-plane death-race set staging the Ghirapur Grand Prix across Avishkar (renamed Kaladesh), Amonkhet, and Muraganda, with ten competing teams `[Scryfall API: dft set; Wizards: Planeswalker's Guide to Aetherdrift, Part 1]`. Vision design led by Mark Rosewater, set design by Yoni Skolnik; codename was "Tennis" `[MTG Wiki: Aetherdrift]`.
- **Faction lore** — The attacker on this card is an Endrider — the black-red racing team from the dying plane Gastal. MTG Wiki explicitly lists *Fuel the Flames* under the Endriders' "Depicted in" card cycle `[MTG Wiki: Endriders]`. Wizards' guide describes the faction as scavengers and nomads who "roar across permanently sunset-washed landscapes seeking rare oases to settle or raid," whose homeworld has "gas and oil... plentiful" but water scarce; after the Phyrexian invasion they "rode their machines to freedom, leaving Gastal without a look in the rearview mirror" and now do mercenary work across the Multiverse to keep their tanks full `[Wizards: Planeswalker's Guide to Aetherdrift, Part 1, by Miguel Lopez, 2024-12-10]`. MTG Wiki frames them as "nomadic apocalypse punk warriors" `[MTG Wiki: Endriders]`.
- **Flavor text** — `"Our world died, but we survived. Funny how it's the opposite for you."` — Rido, Endrider `[Scryfall]`. Rido is on Far Fortune's Endrider roster alongside Mincemeat, Crash, and Wrench `[MTG Wiki: Endriders — Members]`. The line frames the burning sequence as gallows comedy: the Endriders are post-apocalypse survivors whose home plane already died, so their indifference to setting another team on fire reads as black humor, not sadism.
- **Design** — Coverage of *Aetherdrift* repeatedly framed the Endriders as the set's most direct Mad Max homage — story author K. Arsenault Rivera cited Mad Max, Fast and the Furious, Redline, and Speed Racer as her main touchstones `[Magic Untapped: "An Interview With Aetherdrift Story Author K. Arsenault Rivera", via WebSearch snippet]`. The plane's name itself reads as "Gas + Pedal" `[WebSearch snippet of Aetherdrift coverage]`. Illustration by Nicholas Gregory `[Scryfall artist field]`. No reprints — single printing in DFT `[Scryfall prints_search]`.
- **Mechanical reputation** — A {2}{R} instant dealing 2 damage to each creature with Cycling {2} `[Scryfall oracle_text]` — functionally a *Pyroclasm* variant at instant speed with built-in card filtering. Draftsim's *Aetherdrift* Limited review notes that cycling makes "situationally powerful cards like *Fuel the Flames* much more playable" since it doesn't become a dead card in matchups where a board wipe isn't needed `[Draftsim: "The Ultimate Aetherdrift Limited Set Review", via WebSearch snippet]`. EDHREC rank 16,075 places it outside Commander staple territory `[Scryfall: edhrec_rank]`. Legal in Standard, Modern, Legacy, Vintage, Commander; not legal in Pauper `[Scryfall legalities]`.

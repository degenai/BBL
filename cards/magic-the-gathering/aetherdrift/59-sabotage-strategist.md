## Trivia

- **Set context** — Aetherdrift (DFT, released 2025-02-14) centers on the second annual interplanar Ghirapur Grand Prix held on Avishkar, the plane formerly called Kaladesh — renamed by the Avishkar Assembly after the largely bloodless Indigo Revolution dismantled the Consulate. `[Scryfall set page]` `[Wizards: Planeswalker's Guide to Aetherdrift, Part 1]`
- **Aether Rangers membership** — MTG Wiki lists Sabotage Strategist on the Aether Rangers' "Depicted in" roster alongside Elvish Refueler, Loxodon Surveyor, Nimble Thopterist, and Skystreak Engineer. The Rangers are Avishkar's home Grand Prix team — founded by Pia Nalaar, co-led by her (Chief Engineer) and star pilot Spitfire, funded and outfitted by the Avishkar Assembly. Wizards' official Planeswalker's Guide describes the team as "a cross-generation, cross-class team of comrades that combine the best of pre-revolutionary Avishkar with the hope-chasing cohort of the new generation." `[MTG Wiki: Aether Rangers]` `[Wizards: Planeswalker's Guide to Aetherdrift, Part 1]`
- **Design** — Art by Darren Tan, a Singapore-based illustrator with extensive MTG, Star Wars, and Star Trek credits; this is one of his Aetherdrift contributions. Card prints in the 2015 frame as a non-reprint uncommon. `[Scryfall]`
- **Mechanical** — A {2}{U}{U} 2/2 Vedalken Ranger with flying, vigilance, an attack-tax trigger (-1/-0 to all attackers each turn), and Exhaust — {5}{U}{U}: three +1/+1 counters once per game. Exhaust is Aetherdrift's signature once-per-game activated-ability mechanic, designed to evoke a single nitro burst but written generically so Wizards can reuse it outside racing sets. The Ranger creature type ties the card directly to the Aether Rangers team identity. `[Scryfall oracle_text]` `[Wizards: Aetherdrift Mechanics]`
- **Community resonance** — Limited sentiment on Draftsim: the card "slowly pecks away at your opponent, while nerfing their attacks and eventually becoming a legitimate wincon later on" — a defensive blue uncommon that scales into a real threat via exhaust. EDHREC rank 24,334 with about 549 tracked decks indicates very low Commander adoption; niche supporting-cast card, not a staple. `[Draftsim: Ultimate Aetherdrift Limited Set Review]` `[Scryfall edhrec_rank]` `[EDHREC, via WebSearch snippet]`

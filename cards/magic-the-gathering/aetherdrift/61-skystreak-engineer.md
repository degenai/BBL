## Trivia

- **Set context** — Aetherdrift is set during the Ghirapur Grand Prix, an interplanar race hosted on Avishkar (the plane formerly called Kaladesh, renamed after its post-Consulate revolution). The Aether Rangers are Avishkar's home team, described in Wizards' Planeswalker's Guide as "a cross-generation, cross-class team of comrades that combine the best of pre-revolutionary Avishkar with the hope-chasing cohort of the new generation," co-led by Chief Engineer Pia Nalaar. `[Wizards: Planeswalker's Guide to Aetherdrift, Part 1]` `[MTG Wiki: Aether Rangers]`
- **Flavor text** — `"Garima was delighted to finally test out her design. The amazing views were a bonus."` — Narrator, `[Scryfall]`. Garima is canonically a member of the Aether Rangers per MTG Wiki's secondary-character index; this is her sole card appearance, and the wiki's Aether Rangers entry lists Skystreak Engineer in the team's "Depicted in" roster. The engineer is, per source, literally test-piloting her own design. `[MTG Wiki: List of secondary characters/Avishkar]` `[MTG Wiki: Aether Rangers]`
- **Design** — Art by Elizabeth Peiro, a Barcelona-based freelance illustrator working across TTRPG, TCG, and book-cover illustration; this is one of her Aetherdrift contributions. `[Scryfall]` `[elizabethpeiro.com]`
- **Mechanical** — `{1}{U}` 1/3 Human Pilot with flying and Exhaust — `{4}{U}`: put two +1/+1 counters on this creature. Exhaust is Aetherdrift's signature once-per-game activated-ability mechanic; per Mark Rosewater's design column, it was originally codenamed "turbo" and was meant to evoke a one-time nitro switch, then renamed exhaust so the mechanic could be reused outside racing sets. `[Wizards: Aetherdrift Mechanics]` `[MTG Rocks: Exhaust is here to stay]`
- **Community resonance** — Limited sentiment is muted: Draftsim's Aetherdrift Limited Set Review rates the card 2.5/10, calling it "a weak early game flier that can turn into a reasonable threat later on. Best if you have exhaust incentives." Frank Karsten's Ultimate Guard analysis ranks it fourth among common exhaust creatures (behind Stampeding Scurryfoot, Hazard of the Dunes, and Keen Buccaneer). Scryfall edhrec_rank is 25024, indicating very low Commander adoption. `[Draftsim: Ultimate Aetherdrift Limited Set Review]` `[Ultimate Guard: Aetherdrift Limited top cards, Frank Karsten]` `[Scryfall]`

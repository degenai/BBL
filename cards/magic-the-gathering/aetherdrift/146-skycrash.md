## Trivia

- **Set context** — Aetherdrift (set code DFT, released 2025-02-14) is the multi-plane death-race set staging the Ghirapur Grand Prix across Avishkar (renamed Kaladesh), Amonkhet, and Muraganda, with ten competing teams `[Scryfall: dft set]`. Development codename was "Tennis," and Mark Rosewater publicly framed the set as a Death Race premise, with Tumblr replies citing Death Race 2000, Wacky Races, and Fast and the Furious as touchstones `[MTG Wiki: Aetherdrift, citing Mark Rosewater Blogatog posts 2023-08-06 and 2023-09-29]`.
- **Flavor text** — `"Under moonfall conditions, there's nothing you can do but drive and hope for the best."` `[Scryfall]`. "Moonfall" is not generic weather: per MTG Wiki, "the moons of Muraganda are unstable, and chunks of them float in the air and regularly crash on the plane," and "polished moonfall is used as a currency on Muraganda" `[MTG Wiki: Muraganda]`. The line situates the depicted wreck on Stage 3 of the Grand Prix — the Muraganda leg, where falling lunar debris is part of the racing hazard.
- **Design** — Aetherdrift story author K. Arsenault Rivera cited Fast and the Furious, Redline, Mad Max, and Speed Racer as her main touchstones for the set's racing voice `[Magic Untapped: "An Interview With Aetherdrift Story Author K. Arsenault Rivera", via WebSearch snippet]`. Art by Nicholas Gregory, an illustrator credited with 121 Magic cards on Gatherer `[Gatherer artist search: Nicholas Gregory]`. Single printing, no reprints `[Scryfall prints_search]`.
- **Mechanical** — A `{1}{R}` instant that destroys target artifact, with Cycling `{R}` `[Scryfall]`. Draftsim's Aetherdrift Limited review describes it as "dirt cheap cycling on a Shatter" that is "amazing in a set like this," noting "you'll often have good targets for your Skycrash, but pay virtually zero cost when you don't" `[Draftsim: "The Ultimate Aetherdrift Limited Set Review", via WebSearch snippet]`. Commander coverage frames it as a "flexible utility card" worth running where the format presents fast artifact threats `[Commander's Herald: "Aetherdrift Set Review - Red", via WebSearch snippet]`. EDHREC rank 13,550 places it well outside Commander staple territory `[Scryfall: edhrec_rank]`. Standard / Pioneer / Modern / Legacy / Vintage / Commander legal; Pauper not legal despite the uncommon-only printing `[Scryfall legalities]`.
- **Set context** — Cycling is one of Aetherdrift's six headlining mechanics in the set, alongside Crew, Exhaust, Max Speed, Saddle, and Start your engines! `[MTG Wiki: Aetherdrift]`. Aetherdrift is the third set in the Dragonstorm Arc `[MTG Wiki: Aetherdrift]`.

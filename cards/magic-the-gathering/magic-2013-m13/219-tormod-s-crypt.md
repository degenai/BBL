## Trivia

- **Lore context** — The card is named after Tormod, a Dominarian necromancer (also called "Tormod, the Desecrator") active during the Dark Age on Dominaria. He was caught robbing graves near the city of Alsoor, executed by a Church-of-Tal-incited mob, and interred in the extravagant crypt his undead minions had built — only for the grave robberies to resume shortly after, with Tormod's coffin found empty. The M13 flavor text, "Dominaria's most extravagant crypt nevertheless holds an empty grave," directly references this escape. [WebSearch: mtg.wiki/page/Tormod; Scryfall flavor_text]
- **Design history** — Originally printed in The Dark (DRK, no. 112, August 1994), Tormod's Crypt is among the oldest graveyard-hate artifacts in the game, predating Leyline of the Void by roughly a decade. The original Dark printing was illustrated by Christopher Rush, who also illustrated Black Lotus. The M13 printing (artist: Lars Grant-West) is one of 14 total printings spanning Chronicles (1995), Time Spiral Timeshifted (2006), Commander 2014, Core Set 2021, Dominaria Remastered (2023), and Mystery Booster 2 (2024). [Scryfall prints search; WebSearch: Christopher Rush / The Dark]
- **Mechanical reputation** — Costs zero mana, enters untapped, and sacrifices to exile a target player's entire graveyard — one of the cheapest and most format-agnostic graveyard-hate pieces in the game. EDHREC rank 1,831 reflects consistent Commander adoption. Sees Legacy and Vintage play; in Pauper, not having graveyard interaction available on turn one can decide games. [Scryfall edhrec_rank; Scryfall mana_cost; TappedOut forum "Graveyard Removal Rocks Survey"]
- **Named companion card** — Commander Legends (2020) printed Tormod, the Desecrator (Legendary Creature — Zombie Wizard), which generates Zombie tokens whenever cards leave graveyards, retroactively canonizing the lore figure behind the Crypt's name. A second card in the lineage, Tormod's Cryptkeeper, appeared in Modern Horizons 2 (2021). [Scryfall search: name:Tormod]

The palette is dominated by sepia, ash-gray, and pale gold, with her skin and hair catching the brightest light. Her expression is cold and resolute — a magistrate or aristocrat passing judgment rather than a wild caster. The composition is a centered mid-shot with strong radial symmetry, and the energy effect reads as both holy and necrotic, fitting the Orzhov color identity.

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Originally printed in Guildpact (GPT no. 79, 2006-02-03) — the set that introduced the Orzhov Syndicate as a playable guild — and reprinted as Orzhov's guild-common removal at uncommon in Ravnica Allegiance (RNA no. 192, 2019-01-25), Orzhov watermark intact `[Scryfall: rna/192]` `[Scryfall prints search, oracle_id faa01ed1]`.
- **Design** — Mortify has 29 distinct printings since 2006, including Commander 2011/2016/2017/2018, Commander Anthology I & II, Conspiracy, Time Spiral Remastered, Foundations, The List, Secret Lair Drop, and crossover Commander decks for Warhammer 40,000, Assassin's Creed, The Lord of the Rings, and Final Fantasy — one of the most reprinted Orzhov instants in the game `[Scryfall prints search, oracle_id faa01ed1]`.
- **Design** — The Ravnica Allegiance art is a new illustration by Anthony Palumbo, replacing the original Glen Angus piece that ran on Guildpact and the early Commander reprints `[Scryfall: rna/192 artist field]` `[Scryfall prints search]`.
- **Flavor text** — `"Your debt is erased." —Hilgur, Orzhov euthanist` `[Scryfall: rna/192 flavor_text]`. The speaker is named but otherwise unattested in Wizards story articles or MTG Wiki lore searched in this pass; the role "Orzhov euthanist" echoes the Guildpact creature card `Orzhov Euthanist` (GPT no. 54, 2006), the guild's mercy-killer / debt-collector job class `[Scryfall: gpt/54]`.
- **Mechanical reputation** — Legal in Standard, Pioneer, Modern, Legacy, Vintage, and Commander; NOT legal in Pauper `[Scryfall: rna/192 legalities]`. Sits in roughly 213K Commander decks at ~10.6% inclusion across eligible decks, popular in Edgar Markov, Kaalia of the Vast, Terra Herald of Hope, and Atraxa shells `[EDHREC: Mortify]`. Community sentiment on EDHREC, per the "In the Margins: Mortify" column, is that the card is outclassed by Anguished Unmaking / Vindicate / Despark for its slot — the column polemically argues "not a single" of the decks running it "should have it as part of the 99," which is author opinion, not consensus `[EDHREC: In the Margins — Mortify]`.

### Second-pass trivia (2026-05-18)

- **Flavor text lineage** — Across its 29 printings, Mortify has carried at least five distinct flavor texts. The original Glen Angus Guildpact printing (GPT, 2006) reads: `"The eyes let flow with tears, then blood, then the very soul—the whole wrung inside out, dripping down into the blackened puddle of the past."` The Nils Hamm era (Duel Decks: Sorin vs. Tibalt through Commander 2018) carries: `"Many who cross Sorin's path come down with a sudden and fatal case of being-in-the-way-of-a-millennia-old-vampire."` The Anthony Palumbo RNA printing (2019) introduced the Hilgur euthanist text that has stayed on all subsequent Palumbo reprints. The Foundations Starter Collection (FDN, 2024, Nils Hamm art) debuts a fourth text: `"The queen sent her champion to defeat the necromancer threat. His helmet was returned the next day, warped and stained with blood."` — generic high-fantasy framing intended for introductory product `[Scryfall: gpt/122, ddk/25, rna/192, fdn/662]`.

- **Crossover printings — flavor text as thematic recontextualization** — Each Universes Beyond Commander printing replaces Mortify's Ravnica flavor with a franchise-native quote that maps the spell's destruction effect onto in-world lore. The 40K Commander version (Billy Christian, no. 225) attributes to Kaldor Draigo, Supreme Grand Master of the Grey Knights: `"This sword has many names. You may call it 'Death.'"` The LotR Commander version (Ilker Yildiz, no. 269) pulls from Tolkien's The Two Towers Book IV, Chapter 4, "Of Herbs and Stewed Rabbit": `"Soon the rabbits were cut up and lying simmering in their pans with the bunched herbs."` — a domestic Sam Gamgee moment repurposed as a destruction-spell caption, playing on the chapter's cooking-as-defiance register. The Assassin's Creed version (Wangjie Li, no. 96/243) quotes Altaïr Ibn-La'Ahad: `"You held fire in your hand, old man. It should have been destroyed."` The Final Fantasy Commander version (Thanh Tuấn, no. 327, surgefoil) quotes an anonymous Imperial soldier: `"Espers... Who'd have dreamed they were that powerful..."` — the Espers-to-Magicite extraction arc in FFVI maps directly onto a spell that erases creatures from the board `[Scryfall: 40k/225, ltc/269, acr/96, fic/327]` `[Tolkien Gateway: Of Herbs and Stewed Rabbit]`.

- **Secret Lair "Astarion's Thirst" (2024)** — The Secret Lair Drop printing (SLD no. 1801, Bartek Fedyczak, inverted frame) was part of the Dungeons & Dragons 50th Anniversary Superdrop drop titled *Astarion's Thirst*, which went on sale August 27, 2024, at $29.99 nonfoil / $39.99 foil. The drop is a five-card vampire-themed suite (Astarion the Decadent, Exquisite Blood, Sanguine Bond, Anguished Unmaking, Mortify) built around Astarion from Baldur's Gate 3. Fedyczak's flavor text reads: `"Can you feel death's cold grip?"` — attributed to no speaker, functioning as a pure atmospheric caption. The inverted-frame treatment makes it the only borderless-style Mortify in a non-crossover aesthetic `[Scryfall: sld/1801]` `[Commander's Herald: Astarion's Thirst Secret Lair Revealed]`.

- **Store Championship promo (2023)** — A sixth distinct artwork version exists: the Store Championships promo (SCH no. 21, Andreia Ugrai, inverted frame, no flavor text) was awarded at in-store competitive events in 2023. Ugrai's illustration is a full-art borderless treatment; it carries no flavor text — the only Mortify printing since Magic Player Rewards 2007 (Brian Snoddy, no flavor text) to omit attribution text entirely `[Scryfall: sch/21]`.

- **Orzhov euthanist as named guild role** — The term "euthanist" in Mortify's Hilgur attribution is not invented vocabulary: the *Guildmaster's Guide to Ravnica* (2018 Wizards of the Coast / D&D crossover sourcebook) formally defines "euthanist" as a named Orzhov Syndicate role. The sourcebook describes the role as that of an assassin "euphemistically called a euthanist who brings speedy ends to lives deemed to have gone on too long," placed within the enforcer career track alongside debt-muscle collectors. The Orzhov Euthanist creature card (GPT no. 54, 2006, Human Assassin, Haunt) established the job class thirteen years before Hilgur's attribution on the RNA printing — suggesting the euthanist role persisted as a deliberate, consistent Orzhov worldbuilding element across two separate design teams `[Guildmaster's Guide to Ravnica, via MTGSalvation worldbuilding thread]` `[Scryfall: gpt/54]`.

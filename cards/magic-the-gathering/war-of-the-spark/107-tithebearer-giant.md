# Tithebearer Giant (War of the Spark)

## Vision

![107-tithebearer-giant](../../_images/magic-the-gathering/war-of-the-spark/107-tithebearer-giant.png)

A massive humanoid giant in full ornate plate armor occupies the center of the frame, dramatically oversized relative to the surrounding figures. The armor is gold and ivory in tone, heavily decorated with circular medallion motifs on the chest and shoulder plates. The face is pale and smooth — masked or helmet-plated, not clearly a bare face. In one gauntleted hand the giant carries a large rectangular coffer or chest, visibly gilded and banded, rendered with enough detail to read as a formal treasury object rather than a weapon. A polearm or staff is held in the other hand though that arm is partially cropped at the frame edge. The background is a tall gothic cityscape of pale stone spires and pointed towers receding into a bright overcast sky. Smaller figures flank the giant at ground level: gaunt pale humanoids with spears at lower left, a dark-cloaked figure at lower right. The giant's forward-leaning posture reads as aggressive momentum rather than ceremonial procession. The overall palette is gold, ivory, grey stone, and dark framing figures.

## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — Teysa Karlov's sardonic flavor-text voice has a specific backstory: she was imprisoned by the Obzedat (Orzhov Ghost Council) after a failed conspiracy alongside Boros knight Tajic to expose the Council's corruption. Freed by RNA, Kaya assassinated the Obzedat on her behalf (during a distraction staged by Ral Zarek, Hekara, and Vraska), becoming formal guildmaster via an Orzhov legal loophole. De facto power then passed to Teysa and the post-Obzedat Triumvirate: human pontiff Armin Morov, vampire tithe-master Slavomir Zoltan, and angel warrior-executioner Maladola. The quote — "I didn't mean by bashing enemy skulls with them" — belongs to a Teysa who spent years imprisoned for trying to dismantle the very council whose gold is now weaponized. `[MTG Wiki: Teysa Karlov via WebSearch]` `[MTG Wiki: Orzhov Syndicate via WebSearch]` `[Card Kingdom Blog: Teysa Karlov character profile via WebSearch]`

- **Guild-role context** — "Tithebearer" names a functional Orzhov enforcer position: an operative who physically carries and delivers collected tithes, distinct from the tithe-master rank (held post-WAR by Triumvirate member Slavomir Zoltan). The Orzhov hierarchy descends from Ghost Council oligarchs through cardinals, advokists, priests, and enforcers down to thrulls — built from flesh with mask-like faceplates of devalued coinage. The tithebearer giant occupies the enforcer tier: armored, physically imposing, carrying the gilded coffer as both collection vessel and implied weapon. `[MTG Wiki: Orzhov Syndicate via WebSearch]`

- **Design lineage** — Community on MTGSalvation characterizes Tithebearer Giant as "Phyrexian Rager's big brother": both share the identical ETB trigger (draw a card, lose 1 life) in black. Phyrexian Rager is a 2/2 for {2}{B} (Apocalypse, 2001); Tithebearer Giant scales it to a 4/5 for {5}{B} at the top of the common mana curve. `[MTGSalvation forum discussion via WebSearch]` `[Scryfall: Phyrexian Rager oracle text via WebSearch]`

- **Reprint history (correction)** — Three printings confirmed: War of the Spark (WAR/107, May 2019), Jumpstart (JMP/284, July 2020), and Ravnica: Clue Edition (CLU/124, February 2024). `[Scryfall: scryfall.com/card/jmp/284 via WebSearch]` `[Card Kingdom: Jumpstart listing via WebSearch]`

- **Artist portfolio context** — Wisnu Tan illustrated Sower of Discord (Commander 2018 / C18, a 6/6 flying black demon) immediately before WAR, establishing Orzhov-tonal black work as a recurring thread in his portfolio. Post-WAR he went on to illustrate approximately 12 cards in Strixhaven: School of Mages (2021) and Gorex, the Tombshell for Innistrad Midnight Hunt Commander (2021), accumulating roughly 62-73 credited MTG cards across databases. `[ArtStation: artstation.com/wisnutan via WebSearch]` `[planeswalkersmtg.com: Wisnu Tan artist page via WebSearch]` `[mtgpics.com: Wisnu Tan catalog via WebSearch]`

- **Community sentiment** — WAR limited reception was tepid: the SamWise ClockWise WAR Arena draft tier list (2019-08-28) placed Tithebearer Giant in the "most likely to be cut" category. MTGSalvation community corroborates: "overcosted for most formats, but decent body." The cantrip-at-the-top-of-curve value proposition landed primarily with pauper cube builders rather than competitive draft players. `[SamWise ClockWise: 'MTG Arena War of the Spark Draft: Tier List,' 2019-08-28 via WebSearch]` `[MTGSalvation forum discussion via WebSearch]`

## Trivia

- **Design** — Davriel Cane was created by fantasy novelist Brandon Sanderson for his free MTG novella *Children of the Nameless* (December 2018). Wizards R&D had already designed a mono-black hand-disruption planeswalker shell for War of the Spark; when Sanderson's character proved a conceptual fit, R&D retrofitted the design into Davriel, Rogue Shadowmage. Sanderson himself previewed the card on 2019-04-01. `[MTG Wiki: Davriel Cane, Background section]` `[Scryfall: preview_source = brandonsanderson.com]`
- **Set context** — War of the Spark (WAR, released 2019-05-03) was the first set to put 36 planeswalker cards across all rarities in a single expansion, with uncommon walkers like Davriel being a deliberate design experiment. Per the MTG Wiki, Davriel was lured to Ravnica by Nicol Bolas's Interplanar Beacon and trapped there by the Immortal Sun. His in-story goal: appear useless so future world-ending crises would stop recruiting him. He led demons and devils alongside Tibalt during the battle. `[MTG Wiki: Davriel Cane, War of the Spark section]` `[Scryfall]`
- **Lore** — Davriel Cane is a pseudonym; his real name is unknown. He poses as 'Lord Greystone' at a priory in Kessig on Innistrad, and maintains a demonic retinue through contracts engineered to be nearly impossible for the demons to fulfill — including one that requires a demon to correctly guess a specific random rock from Cabralin. A Worldsoul Entity residing in his mind may be the actual source of his planeswalking ability, not a conventional spark. `[MTG Wiki: Davriel Cane]`
- **Mechanical** — No flavor text on the card. `[Scryfall]` EDHREC rank 11,422; penny_rank 447 — meaningful play in mono-black discard and Oathbreaker formats. The static passive (2 damage per upkeep if opponent has one or fewer cards in hand) rewards hellbent-style strategies; the -1 discard ability is efficient at keeping opponents there. Legal in Pioneer, Modern, Legacy, Commander, Oathbreaker. `[Scryfall]`
- **Community sentiment** — Hipsters of the Coast review of *Children of the Nameless* (Dec 2018) described Davriel as 'occasionally grating in his constant disaffection' but ultimately 'understandable if not likable,' calling the novella 'just a good, well-written story that happens to take place in the Magic multiverse.' The novella was originally free on Wizards' site; it was later pulled from the archive and as of 2024 was unavailable through official channels, with a physical edition announced at significant cost. `[hipstersofthecoast.com: Children of the Nameless MTG Novella Review, 2018 — via WebSearch snippet]` `[gizmodo.com: Brandon Sanderson's MTG Novella — via WebSearch snippet]`

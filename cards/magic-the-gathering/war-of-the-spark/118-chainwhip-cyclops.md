## Trivia

- **Set context** — War of the Spark (WAR) was released May 3, 2019 and depicted Nicol Bolas's invasion of Ravnica. Chainwhip Cyclops (WAR-118) is a common red creature set in that backdrop — Bolas deployed a massive Dreadhorde across Ravnica, and the Gruul Clans' cyclops forces, living in the Rubblebelt on the city's outskirts, were drawn into the chaos. Domri Rade, the Gruul Guildmaster, aligned with Bolas and was the first named planeswalker to have his spark harvested by the Elderspell [Scryfall WAR-118; MTG Wiki: War of the Spark/Event via WebSearch snippet].
- **Flavor text** — *"You say this Tenth District, not Rubblebelt. But where smash happen, that Rubblebelt. Rubblebelt state of mind."* — Urgdar, cyclops philosopher. The Rubblebelt is canonically the Gruul-controlled zone of destroyed buildings on the outskirts of Ravnica; Bolas's invasion turned much of the Tenth District — the central civic district where his Citadel was erected — into de facto Rubblebelt. Urgdar's philosophical position on the matter is the card's lone comedy note in an otherwise grim set [card frontmatter flavor_text field; MTG Wiki: Gruul Clans via WebSearch snippet].
- **Mechanical note** — A {4}{R} 4/4 with {3}{R}: target creature can't block this turn. The ability is combat-focused but expensive; EDHREC tracks the card in approximately 240 decks (0.0% of all), ranking it as one of the lower-inclusion WAR commons in Commander [EDHREC: Chainwhip Cyclops card page via WebSearch snippet]. Standard relevance was minimal given its cost and the competitive environment of WAR standard.
- **Reprint** — Chainwhip Cyclops was reprinted in Ravnica: Clue Edition (CLU-127, 2024), a crossover product themed around the Ravnica setting and the Clue board game. The reprint recontextualizes the card inside Ravnica's city geography without altering any text [Gatherer.wizards.com CLU/127; WebSearch snippet].

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Released 2019-05-03 in War of the Spark, the Ravnica-set finale of Nicol Bolas's invasion arc; the set's signature Amass and Proliferate mechanics push counter-based gameplay throughout the format, and Wanderer's Strike folds that Proliferate identity onto a white exile spell `[Scryfall API]` `[MTG Wiki: War of the Spark]`.
- **Design (companion-card cycle)** — Sits at WAR no. 38, the slot immediately after The Wanderer planeswalker at WAR no. 37; part of War of the Spark's planeswalker companion-card cycle, where each planeswalker is paired with a supporting spell that, per the Wizards-confirmed design intent for the set, sheds light on how that planeswalker is coping with the conflict `[heavy.com: "MTG War of the Spark All Planeswalker Cards", April 2019]` `[magic.wizards.com: "Waging War of the Spark, Part 1", 2019-04-01]`.
- **Design (IP verification — depicts the Wanderer)** — The figure's wide-brimmed straw hat with concealed face is the Wanderer's canonical visual identifier; MTG Wiki cites Wizards design notes for the character as "a mysterious PWalker and powerful swordswoman with a fancy hat," and her WAR no. 37 card art shows the same hat plus a golden face-mask `[MTG Wiki: The Wanderer]`. Qualifying counter-discourse: one TappedOut/MTG-Salvation forum summary argues Wanderer's Strike "represents the Planeswalker in the card's name, rather than a card that depicts the planeswalker in its artwork" — noted for honesty, though outweighed by the signature-hat visual identifier and the WAR companion-card design convention `[TappedOut MTG forum: "the wanderer planeswalker in what cards"]`.
- **Flavor text** — `"Precise, clean cuts. No one saw anything. Again. Tag the report with my code and file it."` — Lavinia, `[Scryfall card page]`. Lavinia is the Azorius Senate's acting guildmaster and Grand Arbiter Pro Tem during the WAR siege; the bureaucratic-arrester voice (file the report, no witnesses) reads as Lavinia covering for another off-the-books Wanderer assist on Ravnica `[MTG Wiki: Lavinia]`.
- **Mechanical reputation** — Oracle text: `{4}{W}` Sorcery — "Exile target creature, then proliferate." MTG Arena Zone's exhaustive WAR draft guide flags it as a strong white common removal option, "really busted rate" when paired with creatures already carrying counters `[MTG Arena Zone: "Revisiting My Revisit to War of the Spark: The Exhaustive Draft Guide"]`. EDHREC currently shows ~15% inclusion in The Wandering Rescuer Commander decks (123 of 796) — direct flavor-and-mechanics pairing with the same character's most recent printing `[EDHREC: Wanderer's Strike]`.
- **Design (artist + reprints)** — Artist Sara Winters; reprinted in Commander Masters (CMM no. 69) and included on The List per MTGNexus's database entry, giving collectors three printings to chase `[Scryfall: CMM no. 69]` `[MTGNexus: Wanderer's Strike (The List)]`.
- **Lore unmask (later sets)** — The Wanderer's identity stayed a mystery until Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty (2022), where she was revealed as the Wandering Emperor of Kamigawa and childhood friend of Kaito Shizuki; she lacks control of her spark, the in-fiction reason she planeswalks erratically (Ravnica during WAR, briefly Ikoria where Vivien Reid witnessed her cut down a beast) `[MTG Wiki: The Wanderer]` `[wargamer.com: "MTG: Kamigawa Neon Dynasty unmasks The Wanderer planeswalker"]` `[dotesports.com: "The Wanderer returns to MTG with new card, revealed as emperor in Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty"]`.

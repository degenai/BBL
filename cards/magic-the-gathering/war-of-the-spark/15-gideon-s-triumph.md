## Trivia

- **Set context** — War of the Spark (WAR, released 2019-05-03) is the culmination of the Nicol Bolas arc spanning multiple sets. The set features 36 planeswalkers — more than any set before or since. Its narrative ends with Gideon Jura sacrificing himself to save Liliana Vess: passing his indestructibility to her so she could survive the breach of her demonic contract. His final words in the novelization: "Let me be the last." `[Scryfall]` `[Hipsters of the Coast: "Finally, Rest and Peace", 2019-05-10]`

- **Design — Triumph cycle as narrative mirror** — Gideon's Triumph is one of five uncommons forming the Triumph cycle, a deliberate mirror of the Defeat cycle from Hour of Devastation (2017), which depicted Nicol Bolas routing the Gatewatch. Where the Defeat spells penalized players for controlling Gatewatch planeswalkers, the Triumph spells reward it. Mark Rosewater confirmed the cycle "kept the same five characters, showing them defeating Bolas" even though Nissa had since left the Gatewatch. `[Wizards: "More War Games," Mark Rosewater, 2019-04-29]` Community members on MTGSalvation identified the mirror design immediately on April 1, 2019 spoiler day, predicting the rest of the cycle before the other cards were revealed. `[MTGSalvation: Rumor Mill thread 807737, April 2019, via WebSearch snippet]`

- **Flavor text** — `"His triumph came not from victory over his enemies, but from the salvation of those who survived."` The framing ties to Gideon's defining character arc: survivor's guilt originating on Theros, where his recklessness got his mentor's students killed. His WAR sacrifice redeems that guilt — he saves Liliana rather than winning the battle. Hipsters of the Coast noted that the flavor text "explicitly spells out what each triumph means on a personal level. Gideon has been defined by survivor's guilt ever since he got his friends killed back on Theros, and he achieves triumph by saving innocents." `[Scryfall]` `[Hipsters of the Coast: "Triumph of the Gatewatch", 2019]`

- **Mechanical reputation** — EDHREC reports Gideon's Triumph in 720 EDH decks overall, with a 56% inclusion rate in Kytheon, Hero of Akros commander builds (the commander most naturally paired with Gideon planeswalkers). The card is legal in Pioneer, Modern, Legacy, and Vintage but is not Pauper-legal despite its Uncommon rarity. Its narrow condition — requiring a Gideon planeswalker on the battlefield — keeps it out of general white-removal rotation and limits it to dedicated Gideon-theme decks. `[Scryfall: EDHREC rank 22,812]` `[EDHREC: Gideon's Triumph card page]`

- **Community resonance** — The flavor text drew speculative reads during spoiler season about whether Gideon would die in the story. MTGSalvation thread commentary noted: "Some users interpreted the flavor text to suggest Gideon dies saving someone, likely Liliana" — an interpretation the WAR novel confirmed. The card was previewed by "The Masters of Modern" (YouTube) on April 1, 2019. `[MTGSalvation: Rumor Mill thread 807737, April 2019 — sentiment, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Scryfall: preview source field]`

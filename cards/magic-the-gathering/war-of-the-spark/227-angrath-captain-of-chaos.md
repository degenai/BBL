## Trivia

- **Set context** — Released 2019-05-03 in War of the Spark, the Ravnica-set finale of Nicol Bolas's invasion arc; the set's Amass mechanic represents the Dreadhorde, Bolas's army of resurrected Eternals, being commanded across the battlefield `[Scryfall API]` `[MTG Wiki: War of the Spark]`.
- **Design (uncommon-PW cycle)** — One of War of the Spark's twenty uncommon planeswalkers; Mark Rosewater explains the cycle's template — "one static/triggered ability and one negative-loyalty ability" — was designed after single-ability prototypes felt weak in playtest. Angrath's menace static + −2 Amass Zombies fits the template exactly `[magic.wizards.com: "Waging War of the Spark, Part 1", 2019-04-01]`.
- **Design (why Amass on Angrath)** — Rosewater clarified on Blogatog that Amass appears on Angrath not because he's allied with Bolas, but because Angrath's magic "sways others temporarily to his side" — he's pulling Eternals to fight for him mid-battle, not serving the Dreadhorde `[MTG Wiki: Angrath, Captain of Chaos, citing Rosewater Blogatog (Tumblr)]`.
- **Design (art)** — Artist Slawomir Maniak. No flavor text printed on the War of the Spark version (Scryfall returns no `flavor_text` field for this print) `[Scryfall API]`.
- **Mechanical reputation** — EDHREC shows ~0.42% inclusion across 8,550 Commander decks (Scryfall `edhrec_rank: 11216`); niche but not unplayed. Top homes are Sauron, the Dark Lord (LotR) and Sethron, Hurloon General (Minotaur tribal) — the latter at 28% inclusion in 613 decks `[EDHREC]`.
- **Reprint history** — Reprinted as a foil-only stained-glass treatment in Secret Lair Drop (SLD no. 527), making the War of the Spark no. 227 print the standard non-promo version `[Scryfall: SLD no. 527]`.

## Trivia

- **Set context** -- Streetbreaker Wurm is a Gruul Clans card from Guildpact (February 2006), the second set in the original Ravnica block. Guildpact introduced three guilds -- Gruul, Orzhov, and Izzet -- to the Ravnica setting. The card carries a Gruul watermark and is the only printing of this card; it has never been reprinted. `[Scryfall]`
- **Flavor text** -- "The Orzhov run a construction unit whose only business is rebuilding the wreckage left in the wake of wurms. When business is slow, they pay Golgari carnomancers to lure the wurms to the surface." The line encodes a cross-guild economic ecosystem entirely built on destruction: Orzhov profit from catastrophe, Golgari facilitate it for hire. This is canon Guildpact-era multi-guild world-building in a single flavor sentence. `[Scryfall flavor_text]`
- **Design** -- At 6/4 for 5 mana ({3}{R}{G}) with no abilities, Streetbreaker Wurm represents the classic "vanilla beater" design philosophy for commons -- large body, no frills, color-appropriate power level. EDHREC rank 28,968 reflects near-zero Commander adoption; the card has no mechanical hook beyond raw size. `[Scryfall; Scryfall edhrec_rank]`
- **Artist** -- Art is by Greg Hildebrandt, one half of the Brothers Hildebrandt. Greg Hildebrandt and his twin brother Tim were famous for illustrating the official J.R.R. Tolkien calendars (1976-1978) and the original 1977 Star Wars movie poster. Greg contributed to over 100 Magic cards beginning with Urza's Destiny (1999), and his Tolkien calendar illustrations were reprinted as borderless cards in the Magic: The Gathering Tales of Middle-earth set. Greg Hildebrandt passed away on October 31, 2024, at age 85. `[legacy.com/news/greg-hildebrandt-1939-2024-iconic-fantasy-artist; wikipedia.org/wiki/Brothers_Hildebrandt]`

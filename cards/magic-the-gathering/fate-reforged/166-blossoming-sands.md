## Trivia

- **Set context** — Blossoming Sands originates in Khans of Tarkir (no. 231) and was reprinted in Fate Reforged with new art by Sam Burley. The Fate Reforged versions of all ten taplands depict the same Tarkir locations 1,280 years in the past, the era Sarkhan Vol travels to in the block's storyline. A 2015 art review noted that the Khans versions show the same locations as more managed and developed, while the Fate Reforged past versions reflect the land before the clans' full ascendance; the reviewer characterized this as a subtle narrative argument that the clans were more destructive stewards than the dragons. `[Scent of a Gamer: "Fate Reforged art review," 2015-01-17; MTG Wiki: Fate Reforged (mtg.wiki)]`
- **Design** — Sam Burley illustrated Blossoming Sands for both Khans of Tarkir and Fate Reforged, then continued as the card's artist through Core Set 2019, Core Set 2020, Core Set 2021, Commander 2017, Commander 2018, Commander 2019, Eternal Masters, and Foundations. The consistency of Burley's illustration across this card through a decade of printings makes him effectively the definitive visual voice for this location. `[Scryfall prints]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — EDHREC rank of 1,059 `[Scryfall]`; legal in all currently active formats including Pauper and Standard. Scryfall records 17 printings as of 2026, making it one of the most-reprinted entries in the tapland cycle. Standard inclusion in Selesnya (green-white) Commander lists and Pauper multicolor brews. `[Scryfall]`

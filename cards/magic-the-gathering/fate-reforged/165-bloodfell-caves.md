## Trivia

- **Set context** — Bloodfell Caves originates in Khans of Tarkir (no. 229, January 2015) as part of a ten-card cycle of common gain lands introduced for mana fixing in the multicolor block. It was reprinted in Fate Reforged with new art by the same artist, Adam Paquette, depicting the same Tarkir location 1,280 years in the past. The Fate Reforged timeline is the era Sarkhan Vol travels back to in order to change the battle between Ugin and Nicol Bolas. `[MTG Wiki: Fate Reforged (mtg.wiki); Scryfall]`
- **Design** — In Fate Reforged boosters, the basic land slot was replaced entirely by reprints of the ten Khans of Tarkir dual lands with new artwork. This was a deliberate mana-fixing decision to support a draft environment playable with both Khans of Tarkir and Dragons of Tarkir, analogous to Return to Ravnica dual lands appearing in Dragon's Maze boosters. `[Wizards.com: "A Fetching Look at Fate Reforged," 2014-12-24]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — EDHREC rank of 890 `[Scryfall]`, among the highest (most-included) for a common land in Commander. Legal in every currently active format including Pauper and Standard. Scryfall records 15 printings across Commander precons, Core Sets, Eternal Masters, and Foundations as of 2026. `[Scryfall]`
- **Artist** — Adam Paquette has illustrated Bloodfell Caves across six printings (Khans of Tarkir, Fate Reforged, Eternal Masters, Commander 2017, Commander 2019, Core Set 2019, Core Set 2020, Core Set 2021, Foundations). Hipsters of the Coast ran a multi-part interview series with Paquette in 2019 documenting his fine arts and oil painting background and his relationship with MTG landscape work. `[Hipsters of the Coast: "Promised Lands with Adam Paquette, Part I," 2019; Scryfall prints]`

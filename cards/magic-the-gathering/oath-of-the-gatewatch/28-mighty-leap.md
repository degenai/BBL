## Trivia

- **Design** — Mighty Leap has been printed in 14 different sets since its debut in Magic 2011, making it one of the most-reprinted common white instants of the 2010s. Sets include Magic 2011, Magic 2012, Duel Decks: Elspeth vs. Tezzeret, Modern Masters 2015, Amonkhet, Core Set 2019, Game Night 2019, and The List (2024). The OGW printing carries unique art by Winona Nelson and a Munda-attributed flavor text not found on other printings. `[Scryfall prints search]`
- **Flavor text** — "We may have lost faith in our gods, but we are far from losing faith in ourselves." — Munda, ambush leader. `[Scryfall]` Munda was a named Kor Ally commander in the BFZ/OGW storyline — known for rope-based ambush tactics — who served under Gideon Jura and coordinated the hedron network alignment that trapped Ulamog. This flavor text appears only on the OGW printing and reflects the Zendikari's theological crisis: the Eldrazi turned out to be the corrupted origin of their sky-deity beliefs. `[WebSearch: mtg.wiki/page/Munda snippet]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — +2/+2 and flying until end of turn for {1}{W} is a classic Pauper combat-trick rate. Pauper-legal across all 14 printings; a staple of white Pauper and Pauper Commander decks as a combat finisher or evasion enabler. EDHREC rank 18799 — the most played card in this OGW wave by a significant margin. `[Scryfall]`

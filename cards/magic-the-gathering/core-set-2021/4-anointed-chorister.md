## Trivia

- **Set context** — Anointed Chorister is a common from Core Set 2021 (M21, released 2020-07-03). Core sets in this era were plane-agnostic beginner products; the Chorister has no named plane or named character identity in any official Wizards source. The card is a generic Human Cleric of white mana. `[Scryfall: set_name, type_line "Creature — Human Cleric"]`
- **Mechanical context** — A 1/1 Human Cleric for one white mana with lifelink; an activated pump ability ({4}{W}: +3/+3 until end of turn) gives it late-game surprise reach. As a 1-mana lifelink creature it is a format-playable building block in lifegain-based Commander strategies; EDHREC rank: 21,198, indicating modest but non-trivial Commander uptake. Legal in Pauper, where 1-mana lifelink bodies have occasional relevance in white aggro shells. `[Scryfall]`
- **Flavor text** — `"I sing of benevolence and mercy, of golden wings bringing comfort to the meek and downtrodden."` — spoken by the depicted chorister in first person. The text frames her singing as a vocation of service to the vulnerable. The phrase "meek and downtrodden" is rare flavor-text candor about class position by Wizards standards. `[Scryfall: flavor_text]`
- **Artist** — Bastien Lecouffe-Deharme (credited as Bastien L. Deharme) is a French-American illustrator who debuted in MTG with Battle for Zendikar (2015). He has approximately 86 credited cards per Gatherer and is best known for his dark, gothic, and surrealist work on cards including Liliana, Heretical Healer; Olivia Voldaren; and Animate Dead. Anointed Chorister is a notable departure from his dominant moody/sinister register — warm, devotional, and luminous rather than shadowed. `[Gatherer: artist search for Bastien L.

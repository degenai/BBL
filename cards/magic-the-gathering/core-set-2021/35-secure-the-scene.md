## Trivia

- **Card details** — `{4}{W}` sorcery, Core Set 2021 #35, common. Oracle: *Exile target nonland permanent. Its controller creates a 1/1 white Soldier creature token.* Illustrated by Zoltan Boros. `[Scryfall: m21/35]` `[MTGNerdGirl M21 White Limited Review, coolstuffinc.com]`
- **Flavor text** — `"You know it's a great show when the Azorius arrest the props, too!"` — Judith, the Scourge Diva. The speaker is the Cult of Rakdos's grand-dame performer from Ravnica Allegiance; an in-universe theatrical voice ribbing the Azorius law guild — which fits the card's white-blue-glow exile imagery despite the spell itself being mono-white. `[Scryfall card page]` `[MTG Wiki: Judith]`
- **Design** — Artist Zoltan Boros has ~248 MTG illustrations in Gatherer; he spent 25 years as half of the "Boros-Szikszai" duo with Gabor Szikszai before going solo in 2010 (also credits on D&D, Hearthstone, WoW, Diablo). `[MTG Wiki: Zoltan Boros]` `[Gatherer artist index]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — Rated 3/5 in MTGNerdGirl's M21 white limited review as a "typical 5-mana white exile" whose upside is hitting any nonland permanent (planeswalkers included) and whose downside is gifting a 1/1 when used on a creature. EDHREC reports ~0.01% inclusion across ~530 decks — effectively unplayed in Commander. Community sentiment on limited reviews: "fine, most white decks like to play a single copy" (sentiment, not fact). `[MTGNerdGirl review, coolstuffinc.com]` `[EDHREC]`
- **Set context** — Core Set 2021 is the 2020 summer Core Set thematically anchored by Teferi as the face planeswalker; M21 is positioned as an evergreen on-ramp set rather than a top-down plane-specific narrative, which is why a Ravnica-character flavor quote (Judith) appears on a card whose art shows no specific named NPC — core sets routinely reuse cross-block flavor voices on generic illustrations. `[Scryfall set page]` `[MTG Wiki: Core Set 2021]`

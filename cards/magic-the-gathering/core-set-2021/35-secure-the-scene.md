## Trivia

- **Card details** — `{4}{W}` sorcery, Core Set 2021 no. 35, common. Oracle: *Exile target nonland permanent. Its controller creates a 1/1 white Soldier creature token.* Illustrated by Zoltan Boros. `[Scryfall: m21/35]` `[MTGNerdGirl M21 White Limited Review, coolstuffinc.com]`
- **Flavor text** — `"You know it's a great show when the Azorius arrest the props, too!"` — Judith, the Scourge Diva. The speaker is the Cult of Rakdos's grand-dame performer from Ravnica Allegiance; an in-universe theatrical voice ribbing the Azorius law guild — which fits the card's white-blue-glow exile imagery despite the spell itself being mono-white. `[Scryfall card page]` `[MTG Wiki: Judith]`
- **Design** — Artist Zoltan Boros has ~248 MTG illustrations in Gatherer; he spent 25 years as half of the "Boros-Szikszai" duo with Gabor Szikszai before going solo in 2010 (also credits on D&D, Hearthstone, WoW, Diablo). `[MTG Wiki: Zoltan Boros]` `[Gatherer artist index]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — Rated 3/5 in MTGNerdGirl's M21 white limited review as a "typical 5-mana white exile" whose upside is hitting any nonland permanent (planeswalkers included) and whose downside is gifting a 1/1 when used on a creature. EDHREC reports ~0.01% inclusion across ~530 decks — effectively unplayed in Commander. Community sentiment on limited reviews: "fine, most white decks like to play a single copy" (sentiment, not fact). `[MTGNerdGirl review, coolstuffinc.com]` `[EDHREC]`
- **Set context** — Core Set 2021 is the 2020 summer Core Set thematically anchored by Teferi as the face planeswalker; M21 is positioned as an evergreen on-ramp set rather than a top-down plane-specific narrative, which is why a Ravnica-character flavor quote (Judith) appears on a card whose art shows no specific named NPC — core sets routinely reuse cross-block flavor voices on generic illustrations. `[Scryfall set page]` `[MTG Wiki: Core Set 2021]`

### Second-pass additions (2026-05-18)

- **Sole printing — no reprint history** — Scryfall's prints index returns exactly one result for this oracle ID: M21 no. 35, released 2020-07-03. The card has never been reprinted in a Commander precon, Masters set, or Ravnica supplemental product. Its Azorius flavor is tonal borrowing from Ravnica, not a port of any prior Ravnica card — Scryfall confirms `reprint: false`. `[Scryfall API: oracle search, d6a9746c]`
- **Judith as color-anomalous flavor speaker** — Among the approximately 15 cards across MTG history on which Judith, the Scourge Diva is quoted as flavor-text speaker (including Footlight Fiend, Light Up the Stage, Rakdos Roustabout, Get the Point, Slice from the Shadows, and others), nearly all carry red, black, or {B/R} color identity — Rakdos's home colors. Secure the Scene, a mono-white ({W}) spell, appears to be the only card in Judith's flavor-text corpus outside Rakdos color space. The joke ("the Azorius arrest the props, too!") works precisely because she is the furthest possible guild-voice from the Azorius, commenting on their enforcement from the outside. `[Scryfall card list via WebSearch: confirmed speaker list]` — treat as "appears to be" pending a full ft:Judith color-filter query; no source was found contradicting this.
- **Azorius magic visual canon** — The luminous blue coils and binding energy visible in Boros's art match the canonical visual description of Azorius magic in the official Wizards of the Coast *Plane Shift: Ravnica* supplement (2018): Azorius magic "often takes the form of blue or golden runes floating and glowing in the air in circular patterns or of shimmering azure barriers of magical energy." The binding-chain motif on this card is not artistic improvisation — it is the documented house style for Azorius enforcement spells across the Ravnica card corpus (see also: Azorius Arrester, Azorius Justiciar). `[Plane Shift: Ravnica, WotC 2018, via Obsidian Portal / Astral Reach Wiki secondary sourcing]`
- **Boros solo-career context** — Zoltan Boros (b. 1965, Hungary) worked as the "Boros" half of the Boros-Szikszai illustration duo from 1986 to 2010 — a 24-year collaboration covering hundreds of European sci-fi/fantasy book covers plus MTG, D&D, WoW TCG, and Hearthstone. After the partnership dissolved in 2010 (Zoltan relocated to New York State), he continued solo. Secure the Scene (2020) is a solo commission roughly a decade into that solo career. Despite the professional split, both Boros and Szikszai remain in daily contact and still co-sign MTG cards at conventions. `[boros-szikszai.com artist page; DV Media Co. interview, Medium; Scent of a Gamer, 2016]`

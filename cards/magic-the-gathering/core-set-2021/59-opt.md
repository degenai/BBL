## Trivia

- **Flavor text** — `"The crystal pulsed with the power of Teferi's Planeswalker spark. Had Jhoira given him a blessing or a curse?"` `[Scryfall flavor_text]`. The crystal in the art IS the spark, contained in a powerstone amulet Jhoira crafted at the Thran Mana Rig in Shiv `[MTG Wiki: Teferi Akosa]`.
- **Set context** — Core Set 2021 is the Teferi-themed core set; he is the marquee planeswalker with a full named cycle at no. 75-78 plus the planeswalker-deck pair at no. 324 and 327. The set's narrative picks up sixty years after the Mending and centers on Teferi regaining his lost spark with help from Jhoira and the Weatherlight crew `[mtggoldfish.com: "The Lore of Core 2021"]`.
- **Design** — Art by Tyler Jacobson `[Scryfall artist]`. Opt was first printed in Invasion (2000); the M21 reprint is the first to re-skin the card's flavor around a specific named planeswalker rather than a generic mage `[Scryfall prints history]`.
- **Mechanical reputation** — Format-defining one-mana blue cantrip. EDHREC rank 212 across all MTG cards, one of the most-played blue instants in Commander; legal in Standard, Pioneer, Modern, Legacy, Pauper, Vintage, and Commander as of this print `[Scryfall legalities; EDHREC]`.
- **Set lore** — Jhoira, named obliquely in the flavor text, is Teferi's centuries-old ally from the Tolarian Academy and the captain of the Weatherlight in the Dominaria-era story; she is the one who physically delivers the spark-bearing crystal depicted on this card `[MTG Wiki: Teferi Akosa; mtggoldfish.com lore summary]`.

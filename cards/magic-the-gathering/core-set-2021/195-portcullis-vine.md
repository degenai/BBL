## Trivia

- **Set context** — Portcullis Vine originated in Guilds of Ravnica (October 2018, GRN 142), the first of two sets revisiting the plane of Ravnica. In that set's limited environment, it was a building block for defender-based draft strategies. Early community discussion on MTGSalvation described it as "gold for the Wall.dec" in the context of Guilds of Ravnica Pauper — its sacrifice-draw ability is the payoff that makes defender-only creatures useful beyond pure blocking `[MTGSalvation: Guilds of Ravnica commons for Pauper forum, via WebSearch; Scryfall]`
- **Flavor text** — "Nature's way of saying 'take the long way home.'" The same text appeared on both the Guilds of Ravnica original printing (2018) and the Core Set 2021 reprint (2020) — the only two printings to date `[Scryfall: GRN/142; Scryfall: M21/195]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — EDHREC rank 14564 (as of 2026-05-19). Pauper-legal and PauperCommander-legal. The sacrifice ability ({2}, {T}, sacrifice a creature with defender: draw a card) converts a board of otherwise-offensive-dead defenders into card draw, giving Wall tribal and defender-matters strategies a built-in outlet `[Scryfall oracle text]`
- **Design note** — As a 0/3 Plant Wall for {G} with Defender, the card connects to two distinct tribal axes: Plant-subtype synergies (Avenger of Zendikar) and defender/Wall synergies (Arcades, the Strategist; Assault Formation). The sacrifice requirement targets only defender creatures, making the draw engine self-contained within the strategy `[Scryfall]`

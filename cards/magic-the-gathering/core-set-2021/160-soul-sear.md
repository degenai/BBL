## Trivia

- **Design** — First-print M21 uncommon with no subsequent reprints as of May 2026 [Scryfall prints, single entry]. Previewed by PCGamesN on 2020-06-09 during the M21 spoiler season. Artist is Mathias Kollros, a German-Austrian illustrator with an extensive MTG portfolio across multiple blocks. `[Scryfall preview metadata; Scryfall artist field]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — Five damage plus a temporary indestructible strip for three mana is red's explicit answer to resilient permanents at rare/mythic. The indestructible clause targets creatures and planeswalkers that have indestructible OR makes them lose it pre-emptively — key ruling nuance. Not Pauper-legal (uncommon); not in current Standard rotation. EDHREC rank: 13,746 — moderate Commander inclusion, in the same adoption tier as Havoc Jester (same set, rank 13,414). `[Scryfall legalities; Scryfall EDHREC rank field]`
- **Flavor text** — "There was no witty retort, no confident boast to accompany the attack. There was only pain." No speaker attributed. The text is a deliberate refusal of fantasy combat theatrics — where Havoc Jester's flavor text is all performance, Soul Sear's is the absence of it. One of the starker flavor lines in M21's red suite. `[Scryfall]`
- **Set context** — M21's red removal package (Shock, Soul Sear, Scorching Dragonfire) was designed to give red players answers across multiple problem types: one-mana versatility, indestructible hate, and exiling-remove. Soul Sear filled the indestructible-removal slot, covering the premium resilient-threat angle while remaining accessible in the draft format. `[EpicStream: Core Set 2021 reveals new premium red removal, 2020; Scryfall set metadata]`

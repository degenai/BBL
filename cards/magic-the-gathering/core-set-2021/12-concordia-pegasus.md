## Trivia

- **Reprint history / origin** — Concordia Pegasus originated in Return to Ravnica (RTR, October 2012). The name "Concordia" refers to a Ravnica district, giving this plane-neutral Pegasus a Ravnican flavor anchor. It was reprinted in Ravnica Allegiance (RNA, 2019), Core Set 2020 (M20, 2019), and M21 (2020). Four total printings. `[Scryfall]`
- **Flavor text** — "In the cloudy skies and dim light of autumn, a flash of white wings is a reminder that better times lie ahead." No attributed speaker. The seasonal specificity — autumn, dim light — is unusual in MTG flavor text, which normally avoids time-anchoring. The line frames hope as an interruption of grey rather than an absence of it. `[Scryfall]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — A 1/3 Flying for 1W, Concordia Pegasus is a defensive flyer that prioritizes durability over combat aggression. In M21 Limited, 1/3 flyers at 2 mana are key tempo pieces in white aerial strategies. EDHREC rank 19,843 indicates light Commander adoption; the card's home is Limited and Pauper. `[Scryfall]`
- **Design** — Art by Chris Rahn, whose Pegasus rendering here is notably soft and painterly — moody overcast autumn sky, mountain backdrop, glowing white body. Rahn is a prolific MTG illustrator whose most famous piece is Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite (New Phyrexia) — a work at the extreme opposite tonal register from this pastoral Pegasus. `[Scryfall]`

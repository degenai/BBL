## Trivia

- **Cycle origin** — Blossoming Sands is one of ten cards in the gain-land cycle that debuted in Khans of Tarkir (September 2014), the first set to print gain lands for all ten two-color pairs. The cycle was a direct expansion of the five-card "Refuge" cycle from Zendikar (2009), which covered only the five allied color pairs; Khans added the five enemy pairs to complete the suite. `[MTG Wiki: Tapland, via WebSearch snippet; Scryfall]`
- **Artwork lineage** — The M21 copy carries the original Khans of Tarkir illustration by Sam Burley (Scryfall illustration ID 0e1ebbaa), reused verbatim from KTK through Commander 2017 and into M21. Burley worked as Magic's in-house concept artist at Wizards for roughly five years before going freelance in 2018; more than half of his ~80 Magic card credits are basic or nonbasic lands. `[Hipsters of the Coast: "Sam Burley Talks About His Theros Beyond Death Lands," January 2020; Scryfall illustration_id data]`
- **Reprint history** — As of Tarkir: Dragonstorm (April 2025), Blossoming Sands has 17 distinct printings across standard sets, Commander precons, Masters sets (Eternal Masters 2016), and supplemental products including Bloomburrow, Foundations, and Aetherdrift. Its reprint frequency places it among the most-reprinted common nonbasic lands in the game. `[Scryfall prints search, oracle ID 45429b2c]`
- **Mechanical role** — Legal in Pauper and Pauper Commander, where gain lands are the primary budget dual-land for two-color mana fixing. The 1-life bonus distinguishes the cycle from plain taplands and adds marginal durability in aggro-heavy matchups. EDHREC rank: 1059 among all cards, indicating broad casual Commander adoption despite the tapped-entry drawback. `[Scryfall legalities; EDHREC rank via Scryfall API]`

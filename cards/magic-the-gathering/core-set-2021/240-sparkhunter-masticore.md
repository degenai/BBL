## Trivia

- **Creature type history** — Masticore is a creature type coined in Urza's Destiny (1999) for metallic lion-like artifact creatures — the name derives from "masticate" (to chew). Sparkhunter is the fifth named variant in the lineage: Masticore (Urza's Destiny, 1999), Razormane Masticore (Fifth Dawn, 2004), Molten-Tail Masticore (Scars of Mirrodin, 2010), Lesser Masticore (Modern Horizons, 2019), Sparkhunter (M21, 2020). All Masticores require a discard as part of their casting or upkeep cost — a design signature carried across the entire lineage. [MTG Wiki "Masticore," via WebSearch; Scryfall]
- **Design context** — Sparkhunter Masticore is a first printing, built as explicit planeswalker hate for the M21 Standard environment in which Teferi, Time Raveler was a defining force. Protection from planeswalkers is a rare ability; the 1-mana ping to any planeswalker gives it consistent pressure without relying on combat. Previewed via Wizards of the Coast Twitch stream June 5, 2020. Art by Randy Vargas. [Scryfall]
- **Mechanical reputation** — Colorless 3/4 for three mana (plus discard a card): fits any deck's sideboard as an anti-planeswalker piece. Coverage at Dot Esports and Star City Games at spoiler season called it a viable sideboard inclusion across most archetypes except aggressive builds where discarding mid-game is too costly. EDHREC rank of 24,516 reflects minimal Commander adoption — planeswalkers are less format-warping in Commander, undercutting the hate's value. [Scryfall; Dot Esports "M21 spoiler Sparkhunter Masticore marks MTG planeswalkers as the enemy," via WebSearch; Star City Games via WebSearch]
- **The discard clause** — The cast-time discard is a deliberate callback to the original Masticore's upkeep discard. For Sparkhunter, it means the card can never be played from an empty hand or top-decked as a desperation play — the player must have a surplus card to give up. This is the design gate that keeps it as a sideboard piece rather than a main-deck stable even when the hate text is fully live. [Scryfall; MTG Wiki via WebSearch]

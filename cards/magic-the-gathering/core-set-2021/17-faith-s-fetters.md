## Trivia

- **Set context — reprint history** — Faith's Fetters originated in Ravnica: City of Guilds (October 2005, RAV no. 16), illustrated by Chippy, where it appeared at common. The M21 printing is one of 15 total across its history through 2024, appearing in Eternal Masters (2016), Ultimate Masters (2018), Commander Legends (2020), Jumpstart 2022, and Foundations Jumpstart (2024). The sustained reprint cadence reflects consistent demand for a white enchantment with broad permanent coverage `[Scryfall: prints_search_uri, 15 prints from RAV 2005 through J25 2024]`
- **Mechanical** — Faith's Fetters targets any permanent, not just creatures, and locks activated abilities other than mana abilities. This covers planeswalker loyalty abilities, equipment activations, utility land abilities, and creature abilities in one card — with 4 life on entry. The interaction with planeswalkers is a frequently cited advantage: loyalty abilities are activated abilities, so a Fettered planeswalker cannot tick up, tick down, or ultimate. EDHREC rank 12298 reflects genuine Commander usage; penny_rank 2836 places it among Penny Dreadful's most-used white cards `[Scryfall: oracle_text, edhrec_rank 12298, penny_rank 2836]`
- **Design — multi-artist print history** — At least three distinct artists have illustrated Faith's Fetters across its printings: Chippy (Ravnica 2005, Eternal Masters 2016, Ravnica Remastered 2024), Brian Despain (Commander 2015, Commander Anthology Vol. II, both Duel Decks: Divine vs. Demonic runs), and Kev Walker (Ultimate Masters 2018, The List 2019, Core Set 2021, Jumpstart 2022). Walker is one of Magic's most prolific illustrators, with credits spanning the mid-1990s through the 2020s including Marvel Comics work `[Scryfall: artist field per printing]`
- **Design — rarity drift** — Faith's Fetters has appeared at both common and uncommon across its 15 printings. The original Ravnica printing was common; M21 lists it as uncommon. Rarity has shifted depending on format context (e.g., The List carry-over uses the PLST common frame from the original). This is not a power reclassification — it reflects different set environments `[Scryfall: rarity field, compared across prints]`

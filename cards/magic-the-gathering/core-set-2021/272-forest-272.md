# Forest (272) (Core Set 2021)

## Vision

![272-forest-272](../../_images/magic-the-gathering/core-set-2021/272-forest-272.png)

A landscape view into the interior of a primeval forest. Massive, knotted tree trunks and contorted root systems arch inward from both sides, forming a natural tunnel over a narrow path. The path drops away into the middle distance, lined with moss-furred rocks and tangled undergrowth. The canopy overhead is dense, woven from gnarled limbs, letting only filtered green light through. The whole composition pulls the eye down into the depths of the wood. Color is dominated by deep mossy greens and warm umber browns, with a few brighter green highlights where light catches the leaves.

## Trivia

- **Design** — Artist Volkan Baǵa is a traditional oil painter based in Würzburg, Germany who has contributed to Magic since 2005. He studied under master painter Donato Giancola, per Hipsters of the Coast's coverage of MTG hand-artistry (2018). His original paintings command significant auction prices: Sisay, Weatherlight Captain sold for $20,000 in 2022 `[Hipsters of the Coast — "Powerstone Shard and Hand Artistry," 2018; Hipsters of the Coast — "Volkan Baga's New Sisay, Weatherlight Captain Sells For $20,000," 2022]`.
- **Reprint history** — This Forest illustration was created in traditional media for Magic 2012 (M12, 2011) and reprinted unchanged in Core Set 2021 (M21, 2020) as no. 272. Per Hipsters of the Coast's M21 preview, Baǵa's Forest brings a "storybook feeling" to the M21 basic land lineup — described as an imaginative counterweight to the set's more realistic landscape depictions `[Hipsters of the Coast — "Core Set 2021 Preview: Basic Lands," 2020-06-15; Scryfall]`.
- **Preview source** — Both the Baǵa Forest and the John Avon Plains were previewed together by Hipsters of the Coast on June 15, 2020 — one of M21's first reveal events, more than two weeks before the July 3 release `[Scryfall preview.source field; Hipsters of the Coast preview article]`.

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Prosperous Pirates is a reprint (Scryfall: reprint: true), originating in Ixalan (XLN, September 2017), MTG's pirate-and-dinosaur plane. Ixalan's pirates — the Brazen Coalition — are treasure-obsessed raiders on the Stormwreck Sea, seeking the golden city of Orazca. The flavor text references the floating city of High and Dry, a pirate settlement not formally named in Ixalan's main story — a piece of world-building confined to this card's flavor line. `[Scryfall]`
- **Flavor text** — "The floating city of High and Dry hasn't yet sunk under the weight of gold, but it's not for want of trying." The phrase "high and dry" is a nautical idiom meaning stranded and helpless — the joke is that the pirates are sinking their own city with accumulated wealth. `[Scryfall]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — Creates two Treasure tokens on ETB: five mana for a 3/4 body plus two any-color mana rocks. EDHREC rank 15287 — sees consistent Commander play in Treasure-matters and pirate tribal decks. The Treasure token type was introduced in Ixalan and has since become one of the most-supported token types in Commander; Prosperous Pirates is a foundational common in that design lineage. `[Scryfall]`
- **Design** — Scryfall all_parts links Prosperous Pirates to the Treasure token as a designed combo_piece. CMR includes Azure Fleet Admiral (CMR 58) as the other named pirate common — together they form a minor pirate cohort at the common rarity slot of the set. `[Scryfall]`

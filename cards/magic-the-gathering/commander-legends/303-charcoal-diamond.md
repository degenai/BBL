# Charcoal Diamond (Commander Legends)

## Vision

![303-charcoal-diamond](../../_images/magic-the-gathering/commander-legends/303-charcoal-diamond.png)

A jagged, faceted black gemstone is bound and constricted by thick, fibrous cords or root-like sinews that wrap around it in tight loops. The stone sits on cracked, dry, ashen ground in muted brown and grey tones. To the lower right, a small pale skull or bone fragment lies in the dirt. The composition is a tight still life, almost reliquary in feel, with the bound stone as the sole hero object. The palette is somber — charcoal blacks, dusty browns, bone whites — fitting the card's flavor of night-hardened essence.

## Trivia

- **Set / cycle context** — Charcoal Diamond is part of the Mirage diamond cycle: five uncommon artifacts (Marble Diamond, Sky Diamond, Charcoal Diamond, Fire Diamond, Moss Diamond), each costing {2}, entering tapped, and tapping to produce one mana of the aligned color. The cycle originated in Mirage (1996) and has seen numerous reprints across Commander products. This printing is from Commander Legends (CMR), released November 20, 2020. `[Scryfall: CMR 303]` `[MTGSalvation forums: Diamond cycle thread, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Artist — Lindsey Look, family hand models** — This printing's art was created by Lindsey Look for Commander 2014 and reprinted here. Look confirmed in a 2018 Hipsters of the Coast interview that she used her grandmother's hand as the model for Charcoal Diamond — the bound stone resting on cracked ground is held in her grandmother's hand in the original reference shoot. Her dad's hand appears in Fire Diamond; her mom's hand in Lion's Eye Diamond. She stated: "I purchased a bunch of colored glass paperweights and ended up convincing family and friends to lend a hand with reference shooting." `[Hipsters of the Coast: "Lindsey Look's Powerstone Shard," 2018]`
- **Reprint history** — Original Mirage printing (1996) was illustrated by Drew Tucker. Charcoal Diamond received a new art commission for Commander 2014 (2014-11-07) by Lindsey Look, who also painted Fire Diamond, Marble Diamond, Sky Diamond, and Moss Diamond for that product — all five diamonds in one commission to a single artist. This CMR printing carries Look's art. `[Scryfall prints search]`
- **Flavor text** — "The essence of night to harden the soul." Paired with Fire Diamond's "The essence of flame to stoke the spirit" — the two diamonds share a parallel flavor construction emphasizing elemental essence as a transformative force on the bearer. `[Scryfall: flavor_text field]`
- **Mechanical context** — EDHREC rank 659 out of the overall Commander card pool — notably high for a tap-in-tapped mana rock. Charcoal Diamond is considered a budget black mana accelerant for mono-black and black-heavy Commander decks. Its enters-tapped drawback makes it slower than Sol Ring but it costs {2} for one mana, a worse rate than Arcane Signet; it sees most play in decks with no better options or running budget constraints. Legal in Legacy, Pauper, Commander, Pauper Commander. `[Scryfall: edhrec_rank 659]`
- **Artist-prized signal** — Lindsey Look's original paintings for the MTG Talisman cycle (five paintings from Modern Horizons) sold for a combined $15,625 via eBay (2019). Her Gilded Goose original sold for $7,211 (2019). Her Sublime Epiphany won a Chesley Award in the Gaming Related Illustration category. The Diamond cycle paintings (including Charcoal Diamond) predate these higher-profile sales but belong to the same body of work. `[Hipsters of the Coast: "Modern Horizons Talisman Cycle by Lindsay Look Sold," 2019]` `[Hipsters of the Coast: "Lindsey Look's Gilded Goose Sells for $7,211," 2019]`

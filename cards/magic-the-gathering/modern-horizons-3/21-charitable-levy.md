## Trivia

- **Artist — Eli Minaya (debut context)** — Minaya's MTG debut was two cards in Dominaria United (2022) — *Love Song of Night and Day* and *Cosmic Epiphany* — after art director Zack Stella approached him in 2021. His primary background is science fiction and fantasy novel cover illustration (clients: Tor Books, Amazon Publishing); his self-stated dream for MTG was "a set of lands with really abstract and textured takes on Swamps and Plains." By Modern Horizons 3 he had 36 credited cards in the Gatherer database. `[Star City Games: "Meet The Illustrator Whose Dominaria United Debut Is Taking The MTG Art World By Storm", 2022-09-09]`

- **Counter type — unique in the corpus** — The "collection counter" named counter type on this card is unique in the Scryfall oracle database: a full search for enchantments with that exact counter name returns exactly one result — Charitable Levy. Named counters prevent cross-card bookkeeping confusion; here the name doubles as a thematic label for the tithe-collection mechanic. `[Scryfall API oracle search — confirmed unique, 2026-05-18]`

- **Design lineage — noncreature-spell-tax** — The effect "noncreature spells cost {1} more to cast" has a 20-year white design lineage: Glowrider (Legions, 2003) carried the identical text on a Rare creature; Thalia, Guardian of Thraben (Dark Ascension, 2012) became the canonical staple form. Charitable Levy is the first enchantment in the lineage to also carry a built-in self-destruct threshold — taxing exactly three noncreature spells before converting into a cantrip and a Plains tutor. `[Scryfall: Glowrider (LGN, 2003-02-03); Scryfall: Thalia, Guardian of Thraben (DKA)]`

- **Mechanical comparison — Smothering Tithe** — Smothering Tithe (Ravnica Allegiance, 2019) is the white tax enchantment most often compared to Charitable Levy. The two occupy adjacent design space but pursue opposite philosophies: Smothering Tithe taxes opponent card draws, generates Treasure tokens indefinitely, and never self-terminates; Charitable Levy taxes all players' noncreature spells, counts its own triggers toward a fixed exit point, and liquidates as a cantrip plus land tutor rather than a mana engine. Permanent accumulator vs. self-liquidating tempo speed-bump. `[Scryfall: Smothering Tithe (RNA/22); Scryfall: Charitable Levy (MH3/21)]`

- **Commander inclusion** — Appears in 19,480 Commander decks on EDHREC (rank 6,766 overall as of 2026-05-18) — substantial for an uncommon in a Modern-targeted set. `[Scryfall API: edhrec_rank; EDHREC: edhrec.com/cards/charitable-levy]`

- **Community sentiment — stax fit** — Commander's Herald's MH3 budget review characterized it as "a tax piece that replaces itself and ramps you," with the noted constraint that "it taxes your own spells, but if you can play creatures until it cracks, it is nothing but upside" — framing it as on-axis in creature-heavy stax builds where the controller avoids noncreature casts while opponents pay the toll. `[Community sentiment — Commander's Herald: "Modern Horizons 3 Set Review — Budget"]`

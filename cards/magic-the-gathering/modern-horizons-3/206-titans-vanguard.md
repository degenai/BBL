## Trivia

- **Set context** — The card's name names the three Eldrazi Titans directly: Emrakul, Ulamog, and Kozilek. In Eldrazi lore, "Vanguard" designates a directing-tier brood member that coordinates the Spawn-and-Scion-tier swarm. The flavor text — "With a shriek, it directed the spawn toward the temple where the refugees sheltered." [Scryfall] — renders this function in-fiction: an unnamed Vanguard-creature commanding the lower swarm against a civilian target. [Scryfall: MH3 no. 206 flavor_text; MTG Wiki: Eldrazi lore]
- **Mechanical design note** — Titans' Vanguard is a 5/5 Devoid Eldrazi with Trample and a {3}{R}{G} cost; Devoid makes it colorless despite the Gruul mana. Both the cast trigger and the attack trigger put a +1/+1 counter on each colorless creature you control — but the cast trigger resolves before the Vanguard enters the battlefield, so the Vanguard does not receive a counter from its own cast trigger. This rules interaction distinguishes it from simpler "enters, pump each creature" designs. [Scryfall: oracle_text; WebSearch: Titans' Vanguard ruling note]
- **Artist** — Illustrated by Richard Kane Ferguson, who also painted Pyretic Rebirth in this set. Ferguson began with Legends (1994) and illustrated over 60 cards through Eventide (2008). Both MH3 commissions are among his most recent work following a decade-long hiatus broken in 2019 by Blackblade Reforged. [Hipsters of the Coast: RKF Blackblade Reforged sale, May 2019]
- **Mechanical profile** — EDHREC rank 7476 indicates moderate Commander play in colorless creature and Eldrazi-tribal strategies. The EDHREC Eldrazi Incursion precon upgrade guide listed Titans' Vanguard among suggested cuts, noting that dedicated colorless-wide builds benefit more from it than the precon's mixed focus. First-time printing; no prior versions exist. [EDHREC: Eldrazi Incursion Upgrade Guide, MH3; Scryfall: edhrec_rank]

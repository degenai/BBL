## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — The Implicit Maze was a hidden network of mana paths running through all ten guild districts of Ravnica, designed by Azor I as a failsafe in case the Guildpact dissolved. Niv-Mizzet had Ral Zarek discover it using notes from Jace Beleren. It drove the Dragon's Maze block arc (2013), in which each guild sent a champion to race through the maze; Ral killed the Izzet champion Melek and declared himself Izzet's runner, but Jace Beleren ultimately won the race. `[MTG Wiki: Implicit Maze, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Lore context** — Ral Zarek is a Ravnica-native Izzet planeswalker and former lieutenant to Niv-Mizzet. In War of the Spark (2019) he co-led resistance against Nicol Bolas alongside Kaya and Vraska, activated the Interplanar Beacon to summon planeswalkers from across the Multiverse, and coordinated the ritual that resurrected Niv-Mizzet as the new Living Guildpact. `[MTG Wiki: Ral Zarek, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Mechanical** — As a Saga, the card spans three chapters: a sweeper (2 damage to all opposing creatures and planeswalkers), a Faithless Looting variant with exile-play, and a Spellgorger Weird token that grows with each noncreature spell cast. Legal in Modern, Legacy, Vintage, and Commander; not legal in Standard or Pioneer. `[Scryfall]`

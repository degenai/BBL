## Trivia

- **Set context** — Avacyn Restored (released 2012-05-04) concludes the three-set Innistrad block, MTG's gothic-horror arc. The set's narrative centers on the angel Avacyn's return and the restoration of humanity's hope against the plane's monsters. `[Scryfall]`
- **Flavor text** — "Finally, the principles of corpse animation applied to bloodless materials!" — Ludevic, necro-alchemist. `[Scryfall]`
- **Flavor-text context** — Ludevic of Ulm is Innistrad's most influential necro-alchemist, living in Havengul; he mentored Geralf Cecani and created the flying skaab Kraum. His name first appeared on Ludevic's Test Subject // Ludevic's Abomination (original Innistrad); player demand eventually produced his own legendary card — Ludevic, Necro-Alchemist (Commander 2016, no. 37). The Narstad Scrapper's flavor text represents his application of skaab-animation logic to metal rather than flesh. `[MTG Wiki: Ludevic of Ulm, via WebSearch snippet; Scryfall: ft:ludevic search]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — No reprint history; sole printing in Avacyn Restored. EDHREC rank 30,444 places it firmly in the long tail of unplayed commons — Pauper-legal but rarely seen outside bulk draft. `[Scryfall: reprint: false, edhrec_rank: 30444]`
- **Design** — Art by Steven Belledin, a prolific MTG illustrator known for textural monster work emphasizing corroded metal and organic decay, consistent with the piece's grim-palette industrial-gothic aesthetic. `[Scryfall: artist field]`

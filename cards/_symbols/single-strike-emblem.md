# Single Strike emblem

The iconographic primitive of the **Single Strike battle style**, one of the two designer-coordinated battle-style cohorts introduced in Pokemon TCG **Battle Styles** (SWSH5, March 19, 2021) as the set's headline mechanic. The emblem is rendered as a stylized red-and-black graphic device — most consistently a clenched fist or single-strike calligraphic mark wreathed in flame-coded warm colors (red, orange, black accents) — and is stamped on every card that participates in the Single Strike subtype as the visible badge of cohort membership. Its sibling emblem, the **Rapid Strike** mark, performs the same function for the cooler blue-and-water-coded Rapid Strike cohort.

## The functional ideology

The Single Strike emblem is the canonical-by-printing membership signal for the Single Strike battle style across the Sword & Shield era. Per Pokemon.com's Battle Styles announcement and Bulbapedia's Single Strike Style page, the two battle styles were designed as a coordinated split: Single Strike rewards aggressive punishment-scaling combat (the user takes damage; the attack scales with damage taken), Rapid Strike rewards multi-strike-and-mobility play (coin-flip multi-attacks, bench-control, draw engines). The two cohorts together implement the **two-styles-of-martial-arts thesis** at the TCG mechanical level — Single Strike as the heavy-hitting one-punch tradition, Rapid Strike as the multi-hit / flurry tradition. The emblem is the iconographic shorthand for membership in the heavy-hitting half.

The cohort's most BBL-relevant property is that its cards are *designed to be read as a set*. The Single Strike emblem on the Scroll of Scorn, Urn of Vitality, and Scroll of Piercing is what tells a deck-builder these cards work together — and what tells a buyer holding three of them that they were sold as one piece of design work. The emblem also iconographically twins the cards: the in-corpus Single Strike Tool/Item set all depict a single ceremonial object (scroll, urn) glowing with warm-coded internal energy against a moody dark background, no figure, the red-and-black emblem badged onto the artifact at consistent placement. The cohesion is at the iconography level before it is at the rules-text level.

For any BBL bundle on Pokemon-side designer-coordinated mechanical cohort iconography, martial-arts-tradition design splits, or the warm-vs-cool battle-style aesthetic axis, the Single Strike emblem is the highest-leverage iconographic anchor available in the Pokemon corpus. Bundle prose that names the emblem by name earns lore-aware buyer credibility and lets Game Freak / Creatures Inc.'s designer-coordinated cohort work do the thesis work without naming the thesis.

## Where it appears

Confirmed in corpus:

- **Single Strike Scroll of Scorn** (Battle Styles 133/163, Uncommon, 5ban Graphics art) — Pokemon Tool item, the cohort's offensive engine. Rolled scroll with red-tipped cord and orange energy radiating from the sides; the Single Strike emblem is visible as a red-and-black graphic element in the upper right of the card art. Grants the attached Single Strike Pokemon the Furious Anger attack (Fighting Energy, 10 + 10 per damage counter on the user), a punishment-scaling attack that rewards aggressive trades.
- **Urn of Vitality** (Battle Styles 139/163, Uncommon, Toyste Beach art) — Item card, the cohort's recovery engine. Dark clay urn with the Single Strike emblem badged onto its body, glowing amber from within against a chiaroscuro dark background. Shuffles up to 2 Single Strike Energy cards from the discard pile back into the deck, addressing the resource-drain problem that aggressive Single Strike decks face. The emblem on the urn's body is the in-art badge marking the card as a Single Strike participant.
- **Single Strike Scroll of Piercing** (Chilling Reign 154/198, Uncommon, 5ban Graphics art) — Pokemon Tool item, second-set extension of the offensive-tool slot. Rolled scroll with red-and-orange cord against a flame-styled red-and-orange Single Strike graphic background. Grants the attached Single Strike Pokemon the Bullet Breakthrough attack (Fire + 2 Colorless, 120 damage that ignores Weakness/Resistance and all effects on the opponent's Active Pokemon). The Chilling Reign printing demonstrates the emblem's persistence across multiple Sword & Shield sets, not just Battle Styles itself.

Strong candidates pending corpus acquisition (textual sources confirm the emblem on these):

- **Single Strike Energy** (Battle Styles) — the special energy the entire cohort orbits.
- **Single Strike Urshifu V / Single Strike Urshifu VMAX** (Battle Styles) — the flagship attacker the Tool/Item support suite was designed around.
- **Tower of Darkness** (Battle Styles, Stadium) — the cohort's Stadium card; depicts the dojo-tower where Single Strike Urshifu is canonically trained in Pokemon Sword & Shield: Isle of Armor.
- Subsequent Single-Strike-subtype Pokemon and Trainer cards across Chilling Reign, Evolving Skies, Fusion Strike, and Brilliant Stars — the emblem persists through the SWSH-era's late expansions before the Scarlet & Violet block retires the Battle Styles framework.

## Caveats

- **The Single Strike emblem is paired with — but distinct from — the Rapid Strike emblem.** Battle Styles introduced both cohorts simultaneously, as a designer-coordinated split. The Rapid Strike emblem (cool blues, water-coded calligraphic mark) marks the sibling cohort's cards (Rapid Strike Scroll of Swirls, Rapid Strike Urshifu VMAX, Tower of Waters, etc.) and warrants its own future symbol node when the BBL corpus accumulates Rapid Strike-subtype Tool/Item cards. The two emblems are visually and mechanically twinned but anchor separate cohorts. If a future `rapid-strike-emblem` symbol node is commissioned, this node and that node `related_symbols:` each other.
- **The Single Strike *subtype* and the Single Strike *emblem* are co-extensive but conceptually distinct.** The subtype is a Pokemon TCG mechanical category (oracle-text-level: "This is a Single Strike Pokemon" / "Single Strike Tool"); the emblem is the iconographic primitive (art-level: the red-and-black graphic device stamped on the card). Both predicates pick out the same set of cards in current Pokemon TCG canon, but the emblem is the *visual* anchor that the BBL graph layer hosts at the `_symbols/` level. Mechanical subtype membership is captured in card frontmatter (`oracle_text` Single Strike strings); emblem membership is captured here.
- **The emblem's persistence is era-bounded.** Battle Styles introduced Single Strike / Rapid Strike as a Sword & Shield-era design device; the Scarlet & Violet block retired the framework. Cards printed before Battle Styles (March 2021) do not bear the emblem; cards printed after the SWSH era ended do not bear it. The emblem is canonical for the Battle Styles through Brilliant Stars window (roughly March 2021 through February 2022 in the English print schedule). Future Pokemon TCG sets may reintroduce similar martial-arts-cohort emblems but those would be distinct iconographic primitives, not extensions of this node.
- **The "single ceremonial object glowing against a dark background" iconographic-twinning observation is a corpus-internal pattern, not a Wizards-or-Creatures-Inc.-stated design directive.** The three in-corpus Single Strike Tool/Item cards happen to share that compositional register because they are all artifacts (scroll, urn, scroll) and all carry the cohort's warm-coded palette. Future Single Strike Tool/Item additions to the corpus may break that visual register (e.g. a Tower of Darkness Stadium card has architectural rather than still-life iconography). Membership predicates on the emblem and subtype, not on the compositional pattern.

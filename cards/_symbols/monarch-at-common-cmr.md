# Monarch-at-common (Commander Legends)

The iconographic-and-rules-text primitive of the **Monarch-at-common** designer-coordinated cohort introduced in *Commander Legends* (CMR, released 2020-11-20) as the first Magic set to bring the **Monarch** mechanic to the common rarity slot. The Monarch mechanic itself debuted in *Conspiracy: Take the Crown* (CN2, 2016) at uncommon and rare; CMR's three-card commons cluster — Azure Fleet Admiral (CMR-58), Fall from Favor (CMR-68), and Thorn of the Black Rose (CMR-154) — is the canonical-by-printing rarity-tier shift, a designer-coordinated cohort that the Pauper format reacted to with a fifty-five-day ban on the most aggressive member (Fall from Favor, banned 2021-01-14). The cohort is a first-class graph-relevant mechanical primitive parallel to [[suspect]] and [[manifest-dread]]: a designer-stated thematic program that crosses multiple cards in a set, marks them as members of one printing-canonical cluster, and provides the iconographic anchor for any bundle assembled around the cohort.

## The functional ideology

The Monarch-at-common cohort is the canonical-by-printing mark for CMR's **draft-construct-multiplayer mechanical layer**. Per Wizards' Commander Legends design articles (November 2020) and the MTG Wiki Monarch mechanic page, the Monarch is a player-state designation that grants its holder one extra card-draw at end step until an opponent deals combat damage to that player, at which point the Monarch transfers. The mechanic was designed to incentivize political interaction in multiplayer formats: the Monarch is the player to attack, and the cards that grant it are the cards that start the political round. Bringing the mechanic to common rarity in CMR meant that every CMR draft pod would have at least one player drafting the Monarch theme, and every CMR sealed pool would carry enough Monarch-granters to give the engine real format presence.

The cohort's most BBL-relevant property is its **dual-coding of political power as card-engine economics**. Where Conspiracy: Take the Crown's Monarch cards were rares and uncommons (Palace Sentinels, Throne Warden, Throne of the High City), CMR's three commons distribute the Monarch-grant across three colors (white, blue, black via the three cards' respective color identities: Azure Fleet Admiral is mono-blue, Fall from Favor is mono-blue, Thorn of the Black Rose is mono-black — Wizards intentionally concentrated the commons in blue + black rather than across all five colors, encoding the political-intrigue color register at the rarity tier). Each card grants the Monarch on a different gameplay event: Azure Fleet Admiral grants on creature ETB (combat-reclaim loop via its unblockable-by-monarch clause), Fall from Favor grants on aura resolve (lockdown on the enchanted creature unless its controller reclaims the crown), Thorn of the Black Rose grants on creature ETB with deathtouch (defensive reclaim posture). The three cards together render the canonical *take-the-crown-and-keep-it* gameplay loop at the common rarity tier.

The Pauper format's response confirmed the designer-conscious push: Fall from Favor was banned in Pauper on 2021-01-14, fifty-five days after CMR's 2020-11-20 release, with Wizards citing the card's "creature-tap + soft-lock + card-advantage" compression as too powerful for Pauper's metagame. The ban is the canonical *failure mode* that confirms the rarity-tier shift was a designer-conscious push — a common-rarity card cannot warp a format unless its design was pushed past the common-rarity safety bar, and Fall from Favor's push was specifically the Monarch-at-common engine. Azure Fleet Admiral and Thorn of the Black Rose both remain Pauper-legal; the ban surgically removed only the most aggressive variant while preserving the cohort.

For any BBL bundle on CMR mechanical cohesion, the political-intrigue commons-tier register, or the Monarch-mechanic-as-storefront-thesis, the Monarch-at-common cohort is the highest-leverage iconographic-mechanical anchor available in the CMR slice of the corpus. Bundle prose that names the cohort by name earns lore-aware buyer credibility AND lets Wizards' own rarity-tier-shift design work do the thesis work — the cohort is *literally* the first time Magic put Monarch at common, and the storefront copy can lean on that without inventing the angle.

## Where it appears

This creature can't be blocked by creatures the monarch controls."* The cohort's *take-the-crown-and-keep-attacking-with-the-taker* variant — the Admiral self-sustains via its unblockable-by-monarch clause, which is operative the moment its ETB grants the Monarch. Enchanted creature doesn't untap during its controller's untap step unless that player is the monarch."* The cohort's *most-pushed* variant — the simultaneous creature-tap, soft-lock, monarch-grant, and card-advantage compression that Pauper's banlist removed on 2021-01-14, fifty-five days post-release. Pauper-banned; remains legal in Commander, Legacy, Vintage, PauperCommander.
- **Thorn of the Black Rose** (CMR-154, common, David Gaillet art) — `{2}{B}` 2/1 Human Assassin with deathtouch. Oracle text: *"Deathtouch. When this creature enters, you become the monarch."* The cohort's *deathtouch-defensive-reclaim* variant — the Thorn's deathtouch means any attacker the controller declines to block survives a chump-block trade, defending the monarch status the ETB just granted. Originated in *Conspiracy: Take the Crown* (CN2, 2016) at common; CMR's reprint is the cohort's third member. Flavor text references Marchesa, the political manipulator of the Conspiracy setting (*"If bribery and intimidation fail, Marchesa turns to her favorite form of persuasion."*). Scryfall `all_parts` lists The Monarch token as a combo_piece. Pauper-legal.

## Caveats

- **The Monarch *mechanic* and the Monarch-at-common *cohort* are conceptually distinct.** The Monarch mechanic is a multi-set mechanic spanning Conspiracy: Take the Crown (CN2 2016, debut), Commander Legends (CMR 2020, common-tier shift), Commander Masters (CMC 2023, reprint), and various Commander products. This node anchors specifically the **CMR-commons-tier shift**, not the broader mechanic. A future broader `monarch-mechanic` symbol node could be commissioned if corpus accumulation reaches threshold across multiple sets; current corpus has the three CMR commons + zero other-set Monarch cards, so the cohort-specific node is the right scope.
- **The "common rarity" predicate is canonical-by-printing, not by-card-name.** Each of the three cards has been reprinted at other rarities or in other sets (Azure Fleet Admiral originated in CMR; Fall from Favor originated in CMR; Thorn of the Black Rose originated in CN2 at common and was reprinted in CMR at common). The node anchors the **CMR-commons-tier rarity slot**, which is the rarity-tier shift event the cohort represents. Future reprints of these three cards at uncommon or mythic in other sets would not retroactively change the cohort's commons-tier designation.
- **Thorn of the Black Rose is the only cohort member that pre-existed at common in another set.** Thorn debuted in Conspiracy: Take the Crown (CN2, 2016) at common, where it was already a Monarch-at-common card. CMR's reprint extends the cohort but Thorn's CN2 print is the actual first Monarch-at-common in MTG canon. The CMR-commons-tier cohort frame still holds because CN2 was a draft-construct set with limited format presence, while CMR was a tournament-eligible set whose commons immediately entered Pauper's metagame — the format-warping reality of Monarch-at-common is canonically a CMR-era event (per the Fall from Favor Pauper ban announcement timing).
- **Iconography is rules-text, not visual.** Like [[suspect]] and [[manifest-dread]], the Monarch-at-common cohort does NOT have a distinct visual graphic device on the card-art level — the "iconography" for this node is the appearance of the *"you become the monarch"* phrase in oracle_text rather than a visual emblem the artist paints onto the canvas. This is the canonical-by-printing predicate for mechanical-keyword symbol-layer nodes per the wave 38 schema extension (`[[bbl-symbols-mechanical-primitives]]`).
- **No related-character anchor exists for the cohort.** Unlike [[suspect]] which cross-edges to [[agency]] as its in-fiction institutional anchor, the Monarch-at-common cohort does NOT have a single in-fiction institutional anchor — the three cards span Brazen Coalition pirates (Azure Fleet Admiral), unnamed castle-keep-noble dethronement (Fall from Favor), and Marchesa's political-intrigue assassins (Thorn of the Black Rose). The Monarch is a player-state designation rather than a faction-owned mechanic, and the cohort's three cards intentionally span color identities and flavor registers. `related_characters: []` is the correct state for this node.
- **The cohort's bundle-anchor potential is high for a CMR-political-intrigue or Pauper-format-history Discrete Lair.** Three corpus anchors at common rarity ($0.07-$0.26 per card market range as of 2026-05-14) form a viable bundle-floor-feasible composition per `[[bbl-bundle-pricing-codified]]`. The thesis-stack — *the cohort that warped Pauper's format in fifty-five days* — supports the BBL narrative-first framing per `[[bbl-narrative-first-lairs]]` without inventing canon. Reserve for when the Monarch-at-common Discrete Lair commission lands.

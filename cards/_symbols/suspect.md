# Suspect (mechanic)

The iconographic-and-rules-text primitive of the **Suspect** mechanic introduced in *Murders at Karlov Manor* (MKM, 2024-02-09) as one of the set's four signature mechanics (alongside Disguise, Cases, and Collect Evidence). Suspect is a designer-coordinated marker-keyword that flags a creature with menace + can't-block status until removed, rendering the in-fiction state of *being a suspect in an open Agency investigation* as a rules-text condition that gates the creature's combat options. The marker is a first-class graph-relevant mechanical primitive parallel to [[single-strike-emblem]]: a designer-coordinated cohort identifier that crosses multiple cards in a set, marks them as members of a designer-stated thematic program, and provides the canonical iconographic anchor for any bundle assembled around the program.

## The functional ideology

The Suspect mechanic is the canonical-by-printing mark for the MKM **detective-procedure mechanical layer**. Per Mark Rosewater's MKM mechanic preview articles (January 2024) and the *Murders at Karlov Manor* Release Notes (2024-02-01), Suspect is one of MKM's four signature mechanics, designed as the in-fiction equivalent of *being marked as a person of interest in an Agency case*: the suspected creature acquires menace (because two or more witnesses are needed to block / corroborate) and loses the ability to block (because a suspect is being watched, not watching). Removing the suspect-marker is a Cases-solved payoff or a flash-aura intervention (Airtight Alibi clears suspected status by establishing where the creature was at the time).

The cohort's most BBL-relevant property is its **direct dual-coding of investigation procedure as combat-rules**. Where Disguise renders the in-fiction *act of going undercover* as the rules-text-level face-down play, and Cases render the in-fiction *open investigation* as enchantment-with-solve-trigger, Suspect renders the in-fiction *status of being investigated* as a creature condition that constrains combat. The three mechanics interlock: a Disguised creature can be Suspected when turned face up if a controller-targeted Case is solved; a Suspected creature loses Suspected status when Cleared (Airtight Alibi) or Detained (Eliminate the Impossible's investigate-and-clear sequence). The whole MKM gameplay rendering is the procedural layer Wizards designed to make detective work *feel* like detective work at the rules-text level.

For any BBL bundle on MKM mechanical cohesion, the noir-procedural-rendering register, or the cross-cycle Investigation theme, the Suspect mechanic is the highest-leverage iconographic-mechanical anchor available in the *Murders at Karlov Manor* slice of the corpus. Bundle prose that names the mechanic by name earns lore-aware buyer credibility AND lets Wizards' actual designer-coordinated cohort work do the thesis work — Suspect is *literally* the rules-text rendering of being a person of interest, and the storefront copy can lean on that without inventing the angle.

## Where it appears

Confirmed in corpus (5 anchors — well past the ≥3 single-strike-emblem precedent threshold):

- **Eliminate the Impossible** (MKM-54, uncommon, Carlos Palma Cruchaga art) — `{1}{W}` Investigate sorcery that clears Suspected status on opposing creatures. Oracle text: *"Investigate. Creatures your opponents control get -2/-0 until end of turn. If any of them are suspected, they're no longer suspected."* The Agency operational rendering of *clear-the-name procedure* — the detective eliminates impossibilities until only suspects-with-means-and-opportunity remain, and the cleared parties walk free.
- **Caught Red-Handed** (MKM-115, uncommon, MKM-set art) — `{2}{R}` instant that gains control of target creature + suspects it. Oracle text: *"...Suspect it. (It has menace and can't block.)"* The mechanic's *literal-trigger* card name: caught in the act, marked as suspect, the act of accusation rendered as the rules-text. The red-mana cost wires the card to the chaos-faction operational register; the suspect-marker is the punishment.
- **Convenient Target** (MKM-119, uncommon, Gaboleps art) — `{R}` aura that suspects enchanted creature + grants +1/+1 + can be returned from graveyard for `{2}{R}`. Oracle text: *"When this Aura enters, suspect enchanted creature."* The mechanic's *frame-up rendering* — a red enchantment that doesn't make the target stronger so much as make the target look guilty.
- **Airtight Alibi** (MKM-149, common, Jeremy Wilson art) — `{1}{G}` Flash aura that clears Suspected status + grants hexproof + +2/+2. Oracle text: *"If it's suspected, it's no longer suspected. Enchanted creature gets +2/+2 and can't become suspected."* The mechanic's *exoneration card* — green-mana legal-counsel intervention that establishes the creature's defensive whereabouts and prevents re-suspecting. The pun in the title is the procedure-rendered-as-flavor: an alibi makes you uncatchable, hence hexproof.
- **Deadly Complication (Showcase)** (MKM-312, showcase frame, Jodie Muir art) — modal `{1}{B}` instant: destroy target creature OR put +1/+1 counter on suspected creature + clear suspect. Oracle text: *"Put a +1/+1 counter on target suspected creature you control. You may have it become no longer suspected."* The MKM showcase-frame variant carries the same oracle text as the standard print and the mechanic's *promote-the-suspect-to-named-operative* rendering — the suspect who paid off the right accomplice walks away with a +1/+1 counter and no investigation tail. Flavor text: *"Teysa's security was unrivaled. Etrata was locked up. Kaya was left with nothing but multiplying questions... and a dead friend."* — explicitly names the MKM story arc's central case-development pivot.

## Caveats

- **Suspect is paired with — but distinct from — Disguise, Cases, and Collect Evidence.** The four MKM signature mechanics were designed as an interlocking suite per Rosewater's preview articles. Disguise renders the *act* of investigation (going undercover, the face-down play); Cases render the *open investigation* (enchantment-with-solve-trigger); Suspect renders the *status* of being investigated (the marker-keyword on creatures); Collect Evidence renders the *forensic accumulation* (the mill-then-exile-cost). Each warrants its own future symbol-layer node when corpus accumulates ≥3 anchors per mechanic — current Suspect coverage clears the threshold; Disguise coverage is much higher (most MKM creatures); Cases coverage is at ~6 corpus members; Collect Evidence is at ~2. **Disguise as the second symbol-layer node from this MKM set is the strongest immediate Mr. Nodeley follow-up candidate.** Flagged here, not authored.
- **The Suspect *keyword* and the Suspected *creature-state* are co-extensive but conceptually distinct.** Suspect is the action verb the card uses ("Suspect target creature"); Suspected is the state the affected creature occupies after Suspect is performed on it. Both predicates pick out the same set of cards in current MTG canon, but the **mechanic is the symbol-layer anchor** that the BBL graph hosts at the `_symbols/` level. State-tracking is captured in card oracle_text ("suspected"-containing strings); mechanic membership is captured here.
- **The mechanic's persistence is set-bounded and unconfirmed-for-future-sets.** *Murders at Karlov Manor* introduced Suspect as a set-specific mechanic; the *Outlaws of Thunder Junction* (April 2024) immediate follow-up set did NOT continue the mechanic; *Foundations* and subsequent Standard sets have not (as of 2026-05-13) revived it. The mechanic is canonical for the MKM-and-reprints window. Future MTG sets may reintroduce Suspect as a returning mechanic (Wizards' mechanic-recycling pattern is documented in Rosewater's design articles); those reprintings would extend this node's appears_on if so.
- **The "creature with menace + can't-block" rendering is the canonical Suspected creature-state, not the canonical Suspect-marker iconography.** Unlike the Orzhov Signet (a graphic device on the card art) or the Single Strike Emblem (a graphic device on the card art), Suspect does NOT have a distinct iconographic graphic device on the card-art level. The "iconography" for this node is the rules-text *appearance of the keyword "Suspect"* in oracle_text rather than a visual device the artist paints onto the canvas. This is a notable deviation from the prior two symbol-layer nodes: where Orzhov Signet and Single Strike Emblem are *visual* primitives, Suspect is a *mechanical-keyword* primitive. The symbol layer accommodates both; the node-type predicate is *first-class graph-relevant designer-coordinated cohort primitive*, not specifically *visual primitive*. Future symbol-layer nodes (Disguise, Cases, Collect Evidence) will likely also be mechanical-keyword primitives rather than visual primitives, normalizing the broader symbol-layer schema.
- **The Agency character-node ([[agency]]) is the natural cross-edge.** All six current Suspect-coded corpus anchors are Agency-operational-register cards or anti-Agency cards (Caught Red-Handed and Convenient Target read as suspect-imposition; Airtight Alibi and Eliminate the Impossible read as suspect-clearance). The mechanic's gameplay loop maps the Agency's investigative procedure at the rules-text scale. The `related_characters: [agency]` frontmatter field on this node encodes the cross-edge; the Agency node may want a reciprocal `related_symbols: [suspect]` update as a downstream sync task.

# Triple Thesis — The Three-Hub Triangle

This is not a fourth hub. This is the meta-document that names what the three foundational hubs ARE, why they triangulate, and what it means when a card or node anchors more than one of them.

The three thesis-hubs of the `Bulk Graph Bundler`:

- **[[labor]]** — work that gets extracted. Hidden Marxist verb: **PRODUCE**.
- **[[rebellion]]** — refusal of the apparatus. Hidden Marxist verb: **REFUSE**.
- **[[stewardship]]** — care that tends the commons. Hidden Marxist verb: **TEND**.

A fourth hub, [[chinese-zodiac]], is foundational but TAXONOMIC, not thesis-bearing — it operates on a structural-grid axis (Wuxing × shengxiao) orthogonal to the political-verb triangle. This document is about the political-verb triangle specifically.

## Why three, not two

Two-pole oppositions are structurally unstable. Capital-vs-Labor without a third term resolves into binary deadlock. Producer-vs-rebel without a third term reads as either martyrdom or cycle-of-violence. The triangle is what makes the architecture *operational*.

The Marxist verbs are deliberate:
- **Produce** — work that goes outward, gets taken, becomes capital
- **Refuse** — work that goes against, breaks the contract, ends the relation
- **Tend** — work that goes lateral, maintains the commons, doesn't extract

Three engagements with the world: making, breaking, keeping. Three retrieval-anchors for [[Alex]]'s curation. The triangle mirrors [[Alex]]'s actual political praxis — dirty-leftist labor-critique + Persona-5-anti-establishment-rebellion + on-the-ground mutual-aid-organizer-stewardship. One curator's coherent political-aesthetic refracted into three retrieval anchors.

## What it means when a card or node anchors multiple hubs

A layer-node's `related_hubs:` field can list any combination of the three thesis-hubs (plus optionally [[chinese-zodiac]]). The cardinality matters:

**Single-hub attribution** (`related_hubs: [labor]` or `[rebellion]` or `[stewardship]`) is the standard case. The node's canonical-evidence points to one of the three modes clearly. Most thesis-anchored layer-nodes will be single-attribution.

**Dual-hub attribution** is the **compression point**. A node whose canonical-evidence carries TWO of the three verbs simultaneously. Examples from the current corpus:

- `[[bardock]]` → `[labor, rebellion]` — the canonical low-class Saiyan mercenary who *realizes the employer-empire intends to genocide its workforce, tries to organize fellow workers, and confronts the Emperor alone*. Labor-as-disposable-mercenary + Rebellion-as-one-worker-versus-Emperor. The wave-69 commissioning.
- `[[aether-foundation]]` → `[labor, stewardship]` — institutional-conservation-laundering-Ultra-Beast-weaponization. Labor's apparatus-of-extraction wearing the stewardship costume. The wave-70 corrupted-register anchor.
- `[[destroyer-god-attendant-dyad]]` → `[labor, stewardship]` — Whis/Vados canonically maintain cosmic apparatus (the labor) while the destroyer-god takes credit (the management); their work IS stewardship-of-the-cosmos disguised as service. The wave-67 commissioning.

**Triple-hub attribution** is the **full-triangle compression point**. A node whose canonical-evidence carries ALL THREE verbs simultaneously. The corpus has one so far:

- `[[aether-rangers]]` → `[labor, rebellion, stewardship]` — Wizards' Aetherdrift Planeswalker's Guide canonically frames Avishkar's home Grand Prix team as a *cross-generation, cross-class team of comrades that combine the best of pre-revolutionary Avishkar with the hope-chasing cohort of the new generation*. Labor (the pit-crew is labor-coded). Rebellion (the team is constituted in the immediate aftermath of the Indigo Revolution). Stewardship (mutual-aid-cross-class-and-cross-generation is canonical care-work-across-the-kin-line). All three verbs land. **Wizards put them all in the canon. We surfaced what was there.**

## The corruption-register of each hub

Each thesis-hub has a corrupted counterpart-register. The hub *body* captures both. Bundle copy can use either register to tell the bundle's thesis:

- **Labor-corrupted** = exploitation-without-recognition (the work happens; the credit doesn't reach the worker). Most labor cards land in this register by default.
- **Rebellion-corrupted** = aestheticized-violence-without-substance (the gesture of refusal performed without the political-economy underneath; the "edgy card" with no thesis). The [[rebellion]] body's Anti-Patterns section enumerates these.
- **Stewardship-corrupted** = institutional-cover-for-extraction (the conservation-NGO that's a holding-company; the steward who launders extraction through care-language). The [[aether-foundation]] node is the cleanest corpus case.

The corrupted-registers are MORE useful for BBL bundle-copy than the clean ones, because corruption-of-the-political-verb is the apparatus-of-extraction's most polished costume. Bundle titles work best when they NAME the corruption rather than performing the verb cleanly.

## The discipline: extraction is NOT a fourth hub

The apparatus-of-extraction thesis is the *lens* under which all three thesis-hubs cohere. It is the WHY. It is NOT a hub. Promoting extraction to a hub-node collapses the explanatory work the thesis does, by making it apply universally — and a thesis that applies universally explains nothing.

Extraction stays in node-bodies. It cites canonical-evidence per-anchor. It travels where the receipts carry it. The hubs are the retrieval-anchors; extraction is the reading that makes the retrievals cohere.

Three hubs. One thesis. Two and a half years (the bbl-chronicle marks the time-arc). The triangle holds.

## When does the triangle break?

The triangle breaks if:

1. **Two hubs collapse into one.** If [[labor]] and [[stewardship]] start anchoring the same cards because the corpus stops generating distinct evidence for the verbs, the triangle is over. Watch for this. Stewardship's distinction is *care-without-ownership* against labor's *extracted-work*. If a card's canonical-frame is ambiguous between the two, the curator picks the dominant register; if too many cards become ambiguous, reconsider.

2. **The fourth verb appears.** If the corpus produces a recurring canonical-pattern that the three verbs cannot frame — a fourth Marxist political-economy verb that earns its line — the triangle becomes a square. This is allowed, but the bar for the fourth verb is high: it must produce canonical-evidence across multiple IPs, not just one. *Reproduce* / *Transmit* / *Inherit* were considered candidates in the Wave 69 graph-expert review and deferred — generational-lineage was determined to be a COHORT-axis (Pan-family, evolution-lines, fusion-warriors) rather than a thesis-hub-axis.

3. **The triangle becomes performative.** If hubs start being attached to cards because *we want the triangle to look full* rather than because the canonical-evidence supports the attribution, the curation has begun to lie. Refusal-with-receipts is the antidote: any node whose hub-attribution can't survive a "what's the canonical citation?" question gets the hub stripped.

## The Potion Rule — canon is what the canon does; the curatorial reading is what we add

A foundational anti-pattern guard, named after the Pokémon Potion item card during the wave-69 graph-expert review with [[Alex]]. The rule:

> **A Potion is a Potion. It heals. It is NOT an instrument of medical-industrial-complex oppression — UNLESS the canon says it is.**

The Potion rule names the discipline that keeps the BBL graph from collapsing into a star-graph centered on the apparatus-of-extraction thesis. Every card on every game COULD be read as labor-rebellion-stewardship if you squint hard enough — a Pokémon Potion is "labor-supply-chain healing-economy" if you stretch; a generic Forest is "stewardship of land-as-commons" if you stretch; a basic Fog spell is "rebellion-via-obfuscation" if you stretch. **The rule says: don't stretch.**

What the rule protects:

1. **Node body language must distinguish canonical-event from curatorial-overlay.** A canonical event is what Toriyama / Wizards / Bandai / The Pokémon Company / Sora wrote. A curatorial overlay is the labor-rebellion-stewardship reading BBL applies on top. The body can carry both, but it must NAME WHICH IS WHICH. Phrases like *"the franchise's clearest articulation of the apparatus-of-extraction"* claim Toriyama articulated the thesis. He did not. He articulated villain-escalation. We articulate the apparatus-of-extraction reading on top. Rewrite to *"BBL reads this canonical villain-escalation as the apparatus-of-extraction's progressive autonomy"* or similar — visibility on the overlay.

2. **Hub-attribution must be supported by canonical evidence per node, not by analytic-pressure.** This is the anti-pattern guard from the wave-71 audit: every `related_hubs:` entry must survive a *"what's the canonical citation?"* question. Node-body prose is held to the same standard. If a card's only labor-coding comes from BBL squinting at the artwork, no labor attribution. If it comes from canonical-flavor-text or oracle-mechanics or designer-statement, attribution earned.

3. **Bundle copy may extrapolate where node bodies cannot.** Bundle titles like *"Workers of the world, untap"* and *"Sleep when you're dead"* and *"The Mentor They Murdered"* are *brand-voice extrapolations on the curatorial overlay*, written for buyer-facing storefront use. The node-body discipline is stricter than the bundle-copy discipline — the node body is the substrate, the bundle copy is the surface. Buyers can read protest-sign rhythm; the substrate must read with curator-receipts.

4. **The thesis travels where the receipts carry it.** Per the Wave-69 extraction-as-hub refusal: the apparatus-of-extraction reading is the *lens*, not the *content*. Cards that demonstrate the lens at their canonical level (e.g., Kingpin's Pet with the chain-collar Orzhov-thrull canonical text + Milana flavor-quote about debt-as-death; Whis Auto-skill canonically requiring Beerus Leader as the mechanical-encoding of attendant-must-serve-paired-destroyer) ARE thesis-anchored. Cards that don't (a Pokémon Potion, a basic Plains, a generic Naturalize) are not — they live in the graph as nodes-without-hub-attribution and that's correct.

5. **When in doubt, refuse the attribution.** The wave-71 and wave-74 Saiyan-lineage triple-attribution refusals are the canonical examples — the node bodies didn't carry citations meeting the rigor-bar, so the third hub was withheld pending prose-extension. The refusal is itself the discipline working.

6. **Don't promote pattern-instance to new structural-primitive vocabulary.** Added at wave 80 after the substrate started accumulating sub-shape names — *multi-triangle meta-cycle*, *distributed-triple-attribution-variant-N*, *two-cards-per-verb canonical-density*, *dual-anchor adversary-coupling*, *designer-implicit thesis-stack*, and so on. Each "structural first" tempted vocabulary-coining. Most don't earn it. A node aggregating two triangles is still just a node with edges; standard graph-theory terms (node / edge / cycle / subgraph / sub-cohort) suffice. The wave-72 jargon-refusal (dropped invented "chain"/"weave" topology categories) was the precedent. When you find a recurring pattern: *note it in prose*, don't *graduate it to a first-class graph-object* unless retrieval or bundle-assembly needs to query that shape distinctly. Pattern proliferation produces a Borges-library-of-typology where every variant gets its own dignified name and ten more imply themselves. The graph's job is curation, not taxonomy.

The Potion rule is the structural sibling of the refusal-of-extraction-as-hub (wave 69) and the refusal-of-invented-topology-jargon (wave 72). All three are the same move: **don't let analytic ambition outrun what the canon supports**. The BBL graph's value is precisely its empirical anchoring; if we project the thesis onto cards that don't earn it, or proliferate sub-shape jargon that the data doesn't demand, the graph becomes our own private essay, not a curation.

> Named for: the Pokémon Potion (Evolutions 83-108 / multiple printings) — the exemplar item card whose entire visual register is *small bottle of healing liquid*, used by trainers to restore Pokémon HP, with no canonical evidence of any medical-industrial-complex framing, mass-production-of-medication apparatus, gig-economy-pokemon-trainer-medical-supply-chain reading, or any other apparatus-of-extraction overlay supported by Toriyama, Tajiri, or The Pokémon Company. The card is not nothing — it can anchor to character-cohort nodes (any Trainer-class node), to mechanical-cycle nodes (item-cards-of-evolution-era), to aesthetic-bundles (healing-economy-as-tcg-genre-element). But it is not a labor card. The rule keeps it not-a-labor-card.

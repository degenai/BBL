# Stewardship

A foundational `Bulk Graph Bundler` hub. Not "kindness" as cute aesthetic. Stewardship as the political verb — tending what isn't yours-to-own, maintaining the commons, caring across the kin-line, mutual aid as a daily practice. The card art moment where someone holds something living that they didn't extract from, the gardener at the Senzu tower, the angel-attendant whose actual job is keeping the destroyer-god from destroying too much.

> Stewardship is what comes between labor and rebellion. Labor produces. Rebellion refuses. **Stewardship tends.** Three modes of worldly work — produce, refuse, tend — and the bundle's job is to make all three visible against a card-game design tradition that mostly draws the first one.

## What This Hub Anchors

Two registers — and the corrupted version is as load-bearing as the clean one.

**Genuine stewardship in card art.** The figure cultivating, healing, mentoring, archiving, conserving. The custodian whose competence keeps the cosmic-scale apparatus running while the credit goes elsewhere — Whis maintaining Beerus, Vados attending Champa, Kibito guarding the Supreme Kai's procedural authority. The gardener at the food-supply chain (Korin growing the Senzu Beans on the tower; Yajirobe ferrying them down). The mentor who teaches without owning the student's outcome (Gowasu teaching Zamasu; Jaya teaching Chandra; the Galar gym leaders' canonical responsibility to *develop the next generation of trainers*, not extract from them). The healer card (Nurse Joy, Pokémon Center, MTG cleric / druid traditions, the Aetherdrift pit-crew refueler-medic register). The land-tender (Zendikari Kor reclaiming hedron-fragments; MTG green-aligned forest-guardians; Pokémon territorial-guardian gym-leader registers like Milo's Turffield).

**Corrupted-stewardship in card art.** This is the load-bearing contrast register. **Stewardship-as-cover-for-extraction.** The Aether Foundation pretending to be a Pokémon-conservation NGO while Lusamine weaponizes the Ultra Beasts for personal-ascension. The corporate-charity-arm laundering the labor-extraction it depends on. The Cell-Games announcer Hercule who genuinely steward-friendships Buu into pacification *and* takes the credit Gohan earned. The mentor murdered by their student for failing to see the betrayal coming (Gowasu / Zamasu — Universe 10 erased because the steward couldn't out-care his own apprentice's contempt). The corrupted-stewardship cards are *more* useful for BBL copy than the clean ones — corruption-of-care is the apparatus-of-extraction's most polished costume.

## Anti-Patterns

This hub is NOT:
- **Corporate ESG-stewardship** as flavor. A faux-charity-arm card without thematic recognition of the extraction-it-funds is bad copy. The corrupted-stewardship register requires the card or the bundle to *name what's being covered*.
- **Kind-grandfather sentiment.** A wise-old-mentor card with no thesis about *what's being transmitted and why* is just an NPC. Stewardship is political work, not warm flavor.
- **Generic "guardian"** without commons-frame. A solo-hero guarding their own personal cause is closer to rebellion (refusal) or labor (working-for-survival) than stewardship. Stewardship requires *the thing tended belongs to more than the tender*.
- **Family / kin-coded sentiment.** Family / bloodline / lineage cards belong to character-cohort nodes (Pan-family, evolution-lines, fusion-warriors), not to Stewardship. Stewardship is *care-without-ownership*; family is care-by-blood. Different axes. Adjacent, not redundant.

## Hidden Marxist-verb Anchor

Each foundational hub carries a hidden one-word verb that names the political-economy mode it represents:

- [[labor]] → **produce** (work that gets extracted)
- [[rebellion]] → **refuse** (work against the apparatus)
- [[stewardship]] → **tend** (work that maintains the commons)

Triangulation is deliberate — *produce / refuse / tend* covers the three Marxist verbs of work-engagement-with-the-world. Together they mirror [[Alex]]'s actual praxis: dirty-leftist labor-critique + Persona-5-anti-establishment-rebellion + on-the-ground-mutual-aid-organizer-stewardship. One curator's coherent political-aesthetic refracted into three retrieval-anchors.

## Design Note

This hub is **deliberately card-edge disconnected** in Obsidian's graph view. The node itself is visible — it sits in the layer-node clique linked to other layer nodes via body wikilinks like `[[stewardship]]` in character node bodies (Whis-attendant nodes, Aether-Foundation's corrupted-stewardship case, etc.) — but **no edges connect this hub to any individual card** by design, even when a card depicts care or tending directly (the wire flows via character / symbol nodes, not via hub-name-matching).

The `tag_signals` listed above are an *informational* indirection — cards with overlapping `tags_hub` are *candidates* for Stewardship lair assembly, not auto-anchored members. There is no `appears_on:` or `anchored_cards:` field on this node, and there won't be.

Why: rendering all tag-signal-matched cards as graph edges would balloon Stewardship to ~120 anchors (mostly drift — "guardian" alone matches 54 cards, most of which are bodyguards or wardens rather than commons-tenders). And more fundamentally — *cards are inventory, bundles are destructive*. When a Discrete Lair assembles, those cards leave the corpus on sale; any field pinning specific cards as canonical hub-anchors would be wrong the moment they ship. See `bbl-bundles-are-destructive-on-graph.md` (wave 81 P3 decision, 2026-05-14).

The triple-thesis ([[_triple-thesis]]) is the root crystal; hubs stand on their own as narrative thesis-anchors; bundle-by-bundle assembly does the rest. `anchored_lairs:` is the right tier of relationship — bundle-level, not card-level — and survives the inventory churn.

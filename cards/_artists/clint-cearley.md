# Clint Cearley

American illustrator with one of the deepest Magic: The Gathering portfolios surfaced in BBL corpus to date. Scryfall returns 105 unique artworks credited to Cearley spanning Innistrad (2011) through Commander Legends (2020); Gatherer's printed-card-level index counts 92 distinct credited cards. That places Cearley among the highest-volume single-illustrator portfolios in the modern MTG art roster — comparable in raw count to Magali Villeneuve's 130+ credits and exceeding the typical mid-career staff-or-freelance count by a wide margin. The corpus carries four confirmed Cearley credits across three sets: Battle for Zendikar (2015), Theros Beyond Death (2020), and Core Set 2021.

## Why this artist gets a node

Three reasons compound:

**Portfolio depth.** 105 unique Scryfall artworks across a roughly decade-long span clears the artist-layer threshold by a wide margin (per [[kagemaru-himeno]] at three, [[sanosuke-sakuma]] at five, [[kirisaki]] at seven, [[magali-villeneuve]] at 130+). Cearley sits in the upper-density bracket of the layer alongside Villeneuve.

**Cross-set register span across BBL corpus.** The four corpus cards span three visually distinct registers — Eldrazi-grade cosmic horror (*Nettle Drone*), Zendikari geological-destruction landscape (*Sheer Drop*), Theros-block surreal grotesque (*Sweet Oblivion*), and Core-set elemental-magic kinetic scene (*Volcanic Geyser*). A single illustrator hitting four registers across three sets is the same structural feature that made Villeneuve's node load-bearing — register-span lets a future Discrete Lair use the artist-byline as a cross-set cohesion lever.

**Eldrazi-block visual continuity.** Two of the four corpus Cearley credits (*Sheer Drop*, *Nettle Drone*) and the broader BFZ Cearley slice (*Horribly Awry*, *Fathom Feeder* — outside corpus but documented in trivia) are concentrated in the Eldrazi-invasion arc. Cearley's spiny-radial, asymmetric-biology drone style is visually consistent across the Eldrazi drone sub-type per the Nettle Drone trivia pass. This is a designer-and-art-director assignment pattern, not a coincidence — Cearley got the Eldrazi-coded slots in BFZ.

## Cards in our corpus

- **Sheer Drop** (Battle for Zendikar 48, common, 2015) — Zendikari geological-destruction register. Crumbling sandstone columns and a small armored figure thrown amid rock debris; white sorcery with Awaken 3 ({5}{W}) for land-animation. The figure is dwarfed by the geology — Cearley's recurring move of letting landscape scale dominate the human figure. `cards/magic-the-gathering/battle-for-zendikar/48-sheer-drop.md`

- **Nettle Drone** (Battle for Zendikar 131, common, 2015) — Eldrazi-drone cosmic-horror register. Close-up of an Eldrazi drone with bulbous red-brown spiny torso, radial-burst spike projections, skull-like head against pale grey void. The print is one of BFZ's clearest Cearley signature pieces — asymmetric biology, radial composition, devoid-frame colorlessness rendered in earth-and-bone palette. Enables a documented Pauper infinite-damage combo per the trivia pass. `cards/magic-the-gathering/battle-for-zendikar/131-nettle-drone.md`

- **Sweet Oblivion** (Theros Beyond Death 70, uncommon, 2020) — surreal grotesque-horror register. A cluster of grinning, fanged faces erupting from a churning blue sea; pale-blue / white / grey palette with a clammy drowned quality. {1}{U} sorcery with mill-four and Escape — *"When memory is torment, oblivion is bliss."* The card is one of THB's most stylistically distinct blue commons-and-uncommons, leaning into laughing-grotesque rather than the block's more typical Greek-mythic mode. `cards/magic-the-gathering/theros-beyond-death/70-sweet-oblivion.md`

- **Volcanic Geyser** (Core Set 2021 171, uncommon, 2020) — elemental-magic kinetic register. Lone robed geomancer silhouetted against an erupting column of fire and molten rock; the flavor text *"The common folk call it the breaking of the crust or the spilling of fire. Geomancers call it target practice." —Ryda, geomancer* canonizes the trivializing-power-asymmetry read. {X}{R}{R} instant. The figure-against-elemental-force composition repeats the *Sheer Drop* move at a different scale and palette. `cards/magic-the-gathering/core-set-2021/171-volcanic-geyser.md`

## Stylistic signature

Across the four corpus registers, a few consistent moves recur:

- **Scale contrast against landscape or elemental force** — the lone figure in *Sheer Drop* dwarfed by the crumbling columns, the lone geomancer in *Volcanic Geyser* dwarfed by the eruption column. Cearley's recurring composition centers a small humanoid against a much larger natural or alien element; the painted surface uses scale-disparity as the load-bearing emotional read.
- **Radial-burst asymmetric biology on creature commissions** — *Nettle Drone*'s spiny-torso-with-radial-projectile-spray is the corpus-internal canonical example. The Eldrazi drone sub-type's visual language is heavily Cearley-influenced per the Nettle Drone trivia note.
- **Pallid / washed-out backgrounds as compositional bed** — *Nettle Drone* against pale grey-white void, *Sweet Oblivion* against pale storm-sea sky, *Sheer Drop* against pale overcast sky. Cearley's backgrounds typically refuse saturated environmental detail in favor of diffuse atmospheric framing that lets the foreground subject carry the entire composition.
- **Earth-and-bone palette discipline** — *Nettle Drone*'s red-brown / bone-white / dark-blue palette, *Sheer Drop*'s tan / ochre / grey palette, *Sweet Oblivion*'s pale-blue / white / pink palette. Recurring restraint to a four-or-five-color anchor per print rather than wide chromatic spread.
- **Horror-register adjacency** — even non-horror cards (*Sheer Drop*, *Volcanic Geyser*) carry an unease-of-scale that reads horror-adjacent. *Nettle Drone* and *Sweet Oblivion* sit directly in cosmic-horror and grotesque-horror modes. Cearley's portfolio leans horror-coded across the corpus slice; the broader 105-artwork Scryfall slice includes confirmed horror-and-Eldrazi-coded high-rarity pieces (*Bruna, the Fading Light*; *Brisela, Voice of Nightmares*) per the Sheer Drop trivia citation.

## Bundle-anchor potential

The Cearley corpus opens several distinct bundle-anchor frames:

- **BFZ Eldrazi-invasion Cearley cohort** — *Sheer Drop* + *Nettle Drone*. Two BFZ commons sharing the Cearley byline and the Eldrazi-invasion narrative frame from opposing sides (the Zendikari geological-destruction read and the Eldrazi-drone close-up). A bundle on the BFZ Eldrazi-invasion arc could use Cearley as the visual through-line connecting the resistance landscape and the invader biology — single-artist cross-faction cohesion within the same designer-coordinated set narrative.
- **Cross-block horror-register cohort** — *Nettle Drone* (cosmic horror) + *Sweet Oblivion* (surreal grotesque). Five-year span (2015 → 2020) but a consistent horror-register through-line. A horror-themed Discrete Lair could anchor on this pair as the single-illustrator continuity across the BFZ and THB blocks.
- **Scale-against-elemental-force cohort** — *Sheer Drop* + *Volcanic Geyser*. The figure-dwarfed-by-natural-force composition repeated across white (geological) and red (volcanic) registers. A bundle reaching for "humans against the planet" or "elemental scale" could lean on the Cearley byline as the formal-compositional anchor for that read.
- **Pauper-combo Cearley anchor** — *Nettle Drone* is a documented Pauper infinite-damage combo enabler (per its own trivia pass). For a Pauper-targeted bundle, the Cearley byline isn't the main pitch — the combo is — but the artist-node citation reinforces the curatorial framing.

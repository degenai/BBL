# kirisAki

Pokemon TCG freelance illustrator working since 2013 after studying at Tokyo Designer Gakuin. Across the Sun & Moon and Sword & Shield eras, kirisAki has illustrated a substantial run of Common-through-Uncommon creature and Supporter cards. The corpus carries seven prints across five sets, spanning Crimson Invasion (Sun & Moon-era 2017) through Chilling Reign (Sword & Shield-era 2021) — a four-year span that places kirisAki among the corpus's most-repeated Pokemon-side illustrators.

## Why this artist gets a node

Two reasons compound:

**Corpus volume.** Seven distinct stamped credits across five sets (one Sun & Moon-era card, six Sword & Shield-era cards) makes kirisAki the highest-volume single Pokemon illustrator currently in corpus that does not already have an artist node. Volume alone is sufficient threshold for the artists layer per the [[sanosuke-sakuma]] precedent (five credits triggered Sakuma's node; kirisAki exceeds that count).

**Stylistic continuity across set eras.** kirisAki's portfolio in corpus spans Common creature cards (Alolan Graveler, Galarian Mr. Mime, Chewtle), Uncommon Stage-1 creatures (Polteageist), and Uncommon Supporter character cards (Beauty, Peonia). The stylistic register across these is consistent: clean anime-style line work, soft daytime-or-dusk lighting palettes, restrained urban / domestic / mountain backgrounds, and a preference for relaxed character postures even when the card art subject is a creature. That stylistic continuity is the disambiguation payoff a node provides: when a future card lands stamped with a kirisAki credit but the printed-card render is visually atypical for the artist (e.g. a Full Art or alternate-rarity print by a different actual illustrator stamped under the same credit at the print-object level), this node anchors the canonical "main-print kirisAki signature" against which the alternate-rarity print can be checked.

The kirisAki node is not (yet) load-bearing for a labor-hub or institutional-thesis read in the way Sakuma's node is — kirisAki has no documented cross-medium game-character-design credit. The node's value is artist-layer-internal: disambiguation and corpus-wide credit accounting.

## Stylistic signature

The kirisAki signature visible across the seven corpus prints reads as:

- **Soft anime-style line work** with clean character silhouettes and minimal hatch-shading; backgrounds rendered with diffuse gradient washes rather than detailed environmental texturing.
- **Restrained palettes** anchored on warm earth tones (Alolan Graveler's mineral-grey-and-tan, Galarian Mr. Mime's pale blue and cream, Beauty's red and warm beige, Peonia's twilight purple and slate).
- **Relaxed character posture** even on action-adjacent cards — Peonia's confident-mischief portrait, Beauty's relaxed-confident standing pose, Polteageist's calm-floating-spirit framing. The artist's preference reads as character-grounded over kinetic.
- **Setting depth via background framing rather than detailed foreground objects** — the Beauty urban backdrop, the Peonia mountain-twilight backdrop, the Polteageist domestic-teapot context — establish setting through diffuse atmospheric framing rather than per-object detail.

This is the canonical-by-corpus stylistic profile against which future kirisAki credits can be checked. Alternate-rarity prints (Full Art, Rainbow Rare) credited as kirisAki but visually departing from this signature warrant a print-object-level look at whether the credit is the canonical-author kirisAki or a print-object-level alternate-author stamp.

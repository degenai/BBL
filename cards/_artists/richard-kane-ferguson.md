# Richard Kane Ferguson

Foundational Magic: The Gathering illustrator, active since the Legends set (1994), with 60+ MTG credits across 15+ years of continuous work and periodic franchise returns since. The credited name on the printed cards is **Richard Kane Ferguson**; many works carry a visible **RKF** monogram. His career is contemporaneous with the early commercial era of Magic itself — he is one of a small group of illustrators whose visual register is regarded by the player community as load-bearing for the 1990s-block aesthetic.

## Why this artist gets a node

Three reasons compound:

**Corpus volume meets the artist-layer threshold.** Three confirmed MH3 (2024) credits in current corpus — *Wither and Bloom* (MH3-111), *Pyretic Rebirth* (MH3-200), *Titans' Vanguard* (MH3-206) — clears the soft minimum the artist layer has converged on (per [[kagemaru-himeno]] and [[dan-murayama-scott]], both at three credits in a single set). The wave-39 Edgelord pass surfaced the candidacy with two corpus credits and explicitly deferred to janitor_triage with the trigger condition "≥1 additional RKF credit in corpus" — the wave-167 Wither and Bloom enrichment supplied that third credit and tripped the threshold cleanly.

**Auction-market premium as artist-prized signal.** Per the [[bbl-artist-prized-commons]] memory pattern (precedent: Marc Kopinski's Rummaging Goblin), an artist whose original paintings command significant secondary-market valuations carries a curatorial premium that flows through to any commons of theirs in the corpus. RKF clears this signal at the highest documented tier currently in BBL — Dakkon Shadow Slayer at **$31,000** (2021) and Blackblade Reforged at **$8,000** (2019), per Hipsters of the Coast reporting captured in the Wither and Bloom trivia pass. His MH3 commons (MH3-111 and MH3-206 are commons; MH3-200 is uncommon) are accordingly artist-prized — modest in raw $0.13–$0.18 corpus market price, premium when read at the artist-layer cohesion-signal tier.

**Stylistic register is canonically identifiable across the corpus slice.** RKF's visual register — loose watercolor-and-ink, gestural brushwork, visible hatching, layered washes, muted grey-blue and warm-amber palettes, expressionistic-rather-than-fine-detail rendering — is explicitly named in two of the three corpus card vision bodies (the wave-39 sidecar quotes "Richard Kane Ferguson's distinctive loose watercolor style" from Titans' Vanguard's vision body; the Wither and Bloom vision body cites "Richard Kane Ferguson's characteristic layered wash technique"). That cross-card cross-confirmation provides the artist-layer-internal disambiguation function the layer convention requires.

## Stylistic signature

RKF's slice in corpus reads with a coherent visual register, distinguishable from other MH3 contributors at a glance:

- **Loose watercolor-and-ink medium** — layered washes rather than digital hard-edge work; the signature is most legible in background environments (the underground chamber's stone-and-organic-growth in Wither and Bloom; the fire-and-rock framing in Pyretic Rebirth).
- **Gestural brushwork and visible hatching** — figures and backgrounds carry the mark of the brush; the rendering is expressionistic rather than fine-detail, with directional strokes legible within the painted surface rather than blended away.
- **Palette discipline across two registers** — muted gold / olive-green / dusty-teal / grey (Wither and Bloom's underground sublime) versus hot red-orange-gold against dark charcoal (Pyretic Rebirth's fire-rebirth). Within each register, palette is tight and atmospherically consistent; across the two, the painter remains identifiable.
- **Sublime-mood compositions over kinetic-action ones** — both passed-vision cards read as `mood: sublime` in their vision frontmatter. The figure is held in a still-but-powerful central position (the standing Witherbloom mage; the ascending winged figure) rather than caught mid-strike. The compositional gravity is toward the moment-before-or-after rather than the moment-of-impact.
- **RKF monogram visible on many works** — outside corpus, the artist's signature monogram is documented on many of his Mirage-Tempest-Urza-Ice Age commissions. The MH3 prints in corpus do not surface an obvious in-corpus monogram instance; future high-resolution print examinations could confirm.

This is the canonical-by-corpus stylistic profile against which future RKF credits can be checked.

## Bundle-anchor potential

RKF's corpus slice opens two direct bundle-anchor frames:

- **MH3 90s-MTG retro-callback cohort** — *Wither and Bloom* + *Pyretic Rebirth* + *Titans' Vanguard*. Three MH3 prints by a single 1990s-block-era foundational illustrator within a single set. Modern Horizons 3's stated art-direction brief explicitly leans on returning veteran illustrators of the 1994–2000 MTG era; RKF's three MH3 prints are coherent within that brief. Pyretic Rebirth's Darigaaz flavor-text callback to the Invasion block is the most legible thesis-anchor of the three; a Discrete Lair on "MH3 returning-veterans retro-cohort" can cite the trio as the RKF visual through-line.
- **Artist-prized-commons cost-stack ingredient** — per [[bbl-artist-prized-commons]], a bundle that includes either MH3-111 Wither and Bloom or MH3-206 Titans' Vanguard (the two commons in the RKF MH3 slice) carries an artist-layer premium that can appear in the bundle's `cost_basis` line items as an "artist-prized common" annotation. The $31k / $8k auction citations are the load-bearing evidence; the modest $0.13–$0.18 corpus market prices are the cost-floor against which the curatorial premium reads.

A future expansion path: if a Tempest / Mirage / Urza-block / Ice Age reprint surfaces in PLST or a Commander product and is RKF-credited, the artist-node moves from MH3-only to multi-block, and the retro-callback cohort thesis broadens to "RKF career-spanning cohort" — flagged here for future Edgelord passes.

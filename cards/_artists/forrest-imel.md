# Forrest Imel

Senior concept artist whose client list spans Blizzard Entertainment, Wizards of the Coast, Riot Games, and Valve — the cross-studio range documented on his published forrestimel.com about page. Per Scryfall's artist search (probed 2026-05-26), his Magic: The Gathering catalog runs to 35 credits across major sets including Modern Horizons (2019), Commander 2019, Strixhaven, Modern Horizons 3, Outlaws of Thunder Junction, Foundations, Edge of Eternities, Tarkir: Dragonstorm, and the Marvel's Spider-Man crossover set. The BBL corpus carries four of those credits, three in Core Set 2021 (M21) and one in Outlaws of Thunder Junction (OTJ).

## Why this artist gets a node

Three reasons compound:

**Corpus volume clears the artist-layer threshold with cross-set range.** Four credits across two sets exceeds the soft threshold of three corpus credits the artist-layer convention has converged on (per [[dan-murayama-scott]] at three BFZ credits, [[sanosuke-sakuma]] at five, [[kirisaki]] at seven, [[keiji-kinebuchi]] at nine, [[zoltan-boros]] at sixteen). The Imel cluster sits structurally between the Murayama Scott single-set tier and the Boros multi-block journeyman tier — four credits is enough to clear threshold and the cross-set scope (M21 2020 + OTJ 2024, four years apart) gives the node a different shape than a single-set cohort.

**Artist-prized-commons signal is empirically documented.** Three of the four BBL-corpus credits (Alpine Watchdog, Bolt Hound, Daybreak Charger) are listed on Imel's personal forrestimel.com/magic-prints portfolio sales page — verified by direct fetch 2026-05-26. The artist sells signed proofs and prints of these specific cards from his own catalog, identifying them as work he personally retains and continues to monetize as part of his portfolio brand. Per the `bbl-artist-prized-commons` memory pattern (Kopinski Rummaging Goblin precedent, wave 140), artist-prized commons carry curatorial premium that flows visibly into bundle pricing rationale. Imel's three corpus M21 prints are now in the artist-prized tier on a verifiable evidentiary basis (artist's own portfolio page, not third-party claim).

**Cross-creature-type range within four prints signals art-direction range.** Four corpus credits span four distinct type-lines — Creature — Dog (Alpine Watchdog), Creature — Unicorn (Daybreak Charger), Creature — Elemental Dog (Bolt Hound), Creature — Lizard (Raucous Entertainer) — and three distinct colors (white, white, red, green). Three of the four sit within the same M21 set's white-and-red sections; the fourth is a green OTJ uncommon four years later. The range is unusually broad for a four-print artist slice and signals that Wizards's art directors commissioned Imel across categorically different creature types rather than specializing him to a single subject niche.

Flavor text: *"It's not hard to know the right song for the occasion when most occasions are saloon brawls."* The composition's absurd-calm-amid-chaos register is the comedic-juxtaposition extension of Imel's naturalist creature discipline into OTJ's western-comedy genre frame. ## Stylistic signature

Imel's BBL slice reads with a coherent visual register, distinguishable from other M21 contributors at a glance:

- **Grounded naturalist creature anatomy** — the Alpine Watchdog's broad-chested mountain-dog conformation, the Bolt Hound's mid-charge running silhouette, the Daybreak Charger's dark-bodied unicorn anatomy, the Raucous Entertainer's spiny-reptile body proportions all read as reference-anchored creature studies rather than stylized fantasy caricature. The naturalist discipline survives across the magical-overlay variations (Bolt Hound's electrical wreath, Daybreak Charger's energy-mane) — the underlying creature body is rendered as if observed from reference, with the fantasy element layered on top.

- **Strong silhouette discipline** — all four prints work the silhouette as the primary compositional lever: Alpine Watchdog as the dark mountain-dog against the snow-and-sky background, Bolt Hound as the black hound against the red ember-field, Daybreak Charger as the dark unicorn against the muted-purple twilight, Raucous Entertainer as the dark-green spiny reptile against the warm-yellow saloon interior. The silhouette-first approach reads as concept-art discipline carried into the published illustration.

- **Cross-creature-type range without medium-shift** — the four corpus prints span Dog, Unicorn, Elemental Dog, and Lizard creature types across white, white, red, and green colors, but all four are rendered in the same digital-painting register with consistent brushwork and rendering finish. The medium and technique stay constant across the type-line variation, signaling the artist's range is in subject matter rather than in visual medium.

- **Comedic-genre extension in the OTJ slot** — the Raucous Entertainer's absurd-calm-amid-chaos saloon-piano composition is the OTJ-set genre-comedy extension of the same naturalist creature discipline. The set's western-comedy art-direction brief is the variable; Imel's naturalist creature foundation is the constant. The cross-set range demonstrates the artist working a stable foundation into different genre frames.

This is the canonical-by-corpus stylistic profile against which future Imel credits in BBL can be checked. The 31 non-corpus credits in his Scryfall catalog (Hexdrinker, Dockside Extortionist, Seedborn Muse, the Marvel's Spider-Man crossover work, et al) offer the broader artist-career picture but are not in-corpus and do not generate edges at this commission.

- **M21 native-Dog-type artist-internal cohort.** Alpine Watchdog + Bolt Hound are both Imel-illustrated within M21's [[m21-dog-tribal]] cohort. The two cards already share the cohort edge; the artist-layer adds a second dimension to the relationship — both are first-generation native-Dog-type prints AND both are Imel commissions within the same set. A future Tithe-adjacent or M21-anchored Discrete Lair could foreground the *single-artist span across the cohort* as cohesion-signal alongside the designer-coordinated tribal-theme thesis. The artist-prized-commons signal (Imel sells both prints through his personal portfolio) makes the artist-credit visible in pricing rationale.

- **M21 white-section cross-creature-type Imel cohort.** Alpine Watchdog + Daybreak Charger are both Imel-illustrated commons in M21's white section, paired in the same designer's vision bullet (Daybreak Charger's wave-204 trivia bullet 4 names *"an uncommon instance of one illustrator covering two distinct creature types — Unicorn and Dog — within the same set's white section"*). A bundle anchored on M21 white commons could pair these two as the *cross-type single-artist* foundation, demonstrating range-of-subject within a tightly-scoped set-and-color section. The cohesion-signal is independent of the dog-tribal thesis — this is the artist-range argument, not the dog-cohort argument.

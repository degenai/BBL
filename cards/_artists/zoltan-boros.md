# Zoltan Boros

Hungarian fantasy illustrator (b. 1965); the "Boros" half of the long-running **Boros-Szikszai** illustration duo with Gabor Szikszai. The duo worked together from 1986 to 2010 — a 24-year collaboration that produced hundreds of European sci-fi and fantasy book covers, extensive Magic: The Gathering art, plus credits on D&D, World of Warcraft TCG, Hearthstone, and Diablo. After the partnership dissolved in 2010 (Zoltan relocated to New York State while Gabor remained in Hungary), Boros continued as a solo illustrator. Per Gatherer's artist index, approximately 248 MTG illustrations are credited under his name across the partnership and solo eras combined. The pair remain in regular contact and still co-sign MTG cards together at conventions despite the professional split.

## Why this artist gets a node

Four reasons compound:

**Corpus volume far exceeds the artist-layer threshold.** 16 solo credits across 7 MTG sets (Ravnica Allegiance 2019, Theros Beyond Death 2020, Core Set 2021 2020, Throne of Eldraine 2019, The List/PLST, War of the Spark 2019, Modern Horizons 3 2024) plus 2 duo credits with Szikszai (Alara Reborn 2009, M21-168 Turn to Slag 2020) treated separately. The solo body of work alone is more than five times the soft threshold per `kagemaru-himeno` and `dan-murayama-scott` (≥3 credits). Boros's corpus slice is the **densest single-artist MTG cluster currently in BBL**, eclipsing Magali Villeneuve, Victor Adame Minguez, and Richard Kane Ferguson.

**Cross-set range spans a decade and a half of solo-career work.** Where Murayama Scott's BFZ slice is *single-block-coherent*, Kagemaru Himeno's Pokemon slice is *single-franchise-tenure*, and Richard Kane Ferguson's MH3 slice is *single-set-retro-callback*, Boros's corpus slice is **cross-block cross-era**: 2009 Alara Reborn (duo era), 2019 RNA + ELD + WAR (peak solo output), 2020 THB + M21 (continued solo + reunion duo credit), 2024 MH3 (current solo). The range gives the artist node a structurally distinct thesis: not a single-set cohort, not a franchise-tenure case, but a cross-block journeyman-illustrator whose work threads through nine years of Standard-format Magic at common-and-uncommon density.

**Multiple existing layer-edges already converge on this artist's authorship.** Four of the 16 solo credits carry existing cross-layer pointers: Enchanted Carriage (ELD-218) → [[eld-cinderella-cycle]], Secure the Scene (M21-35) → [[azorius-senate]], Spark Reaper (WAR-106) → [[kaya]], Bespoke Battlewagon (MH3-52) → [[energy-counter]] (symbol). The artist node anchors at the convergence point, giving the graph a fifth dimension on cards already participating in multi-edge cohort and symbol-layer relationships. Per `bbl-artists-as-counterbalance`, this is precisely the case where the artists layer generates orthogonal cross-cohort traversal paths the cohort axis alone cannot.

**Stylistic register is independently recognizable across the corpus slice.** The "Boros action-figure" register — kinetic forward-facing figures with mouth-open battle-cry expressions, muted ochre / grey-blue palettes with selective gold highlights, painterly brushwork that preserves directional strokes — runs as a coherent through-line across Makeshift Battalion (M21 + WAR, identical art across reprints), Siege Striker, Bake into a Pie, Goblin Assault Team, and Martyr for the Cause. The wave-208 trivia pass for Siege Striker explicitly identifies the cross-card stylistic pattern: *"Siege Striker and Makeshift Battalion (no. 26, also in this wave) are both illustrated by Zoltan Boros in M21, making him responsible for two of the set's white aggro common/uncommon creatures. Boros (the artist, not the guild) is a prolific MTG illustrator known for action-focused figure work and the human-soldier aesthetic that runs through both pieces."* The artist-layer-internal disambiguation function the convention requires is already documented in corpus card prose.

## Cards in our corpus

Sixteen solo credits, ordered by set release date (newest first), plus two duo credits noted separately at the bottom:

- **Bespoke Battlewagon** (Modern Horizons 3 52, 2024) — `{4}` Artifact Creature — Construct Vehicle, with energy-counter mechanics. Symbol-layer edge to `[[energy-counter]]`. Most recent solo Boros credit in corpus; the MH3 commission confirms the solo career remains active.
- **Makeshift Battalion** (Core Set 2021 26, common, 2020) — `{2}{W}` 3/2 Soldier with Battalion trigger. White-and-gold armored warrior charging forward in a battle cry, flanked by hooded and green-armored companions. Reprint of the same Boros art from WAR-22 (2019); same art across all three printings (WAR + M21 + RNR 2024) per the card's trivia bullet. Anchor of the wave-208 Siege-Striker / Makeshift-Battalion artist-observation that triggered this node.
- **Secure the Scene** (Core Set 2021 35, common, 2020) — `{4}{W}` exile-target-nonland sorcery. Bearded helmeted guard restraining a smaller figure with luminous blue magical chains. Existing character-layer edge to `[[azorius-senate]]`. The card's trivia bullets carry the most extensive in-corpus biographical capture of Boros: the 248-Gatherer-illustrations count, the 1986–2010 partnership dates, the Hungary-to-New-York relocation, the cross-medium D&D/Hearthstone/WoW/Diablo work, and the partnership co-signing tradition at conventions. The Plane-Shift-Ravnica-confirmed Azorius-magic visual register on this card is also documented as Boros's faithful rendering of WotC's published house style for the guild.
- **Siege Striker** (Core Set 2021 37, uncommon, 2020) — `{2}{W}` 1/1 double-strike soldier with attack-trigger pump. Stocky bald warrior in white-grey plate raising a hand axe overhead amid swarming goblins. Wave-208 vision + trivia anchor; the trivia bullet 4 is the wave that named the M21 Boros pair. The defiant-fury register is canonical Boros mood-and-composition discipline.
- **Bake into a Pie** (Throne of Eldraine 76, common, 2019) — `{2}{B}{B}` destroy-target-creature-and-create-Food sorcery. Eldraine fairy-tale apparatus-of-extraction register (peasant-and-witch cookery flavor); the card's Food-token rider is a literal eat-the-evidence beat that the art renders as kitchen-and-blade composition. Sibling-set credit to Enchanted Carriage and Improbable Alliance.
- **Improbable Alliance** (Throne of Eldraine 193, uncommon, 2019) — `{U}{R}` enchantment that creates a flying Faerie token when a kicker-or-similar trigger fires. Eldraine cross-color faction-alliance flavor; the third Boros credit in ELD.
- **Enchanted Carriage** (Throne of Eldraine 218, uncommon, 2019) — `{4}` Artifact — Vehicle, Cinderella-cycle anchor. Existing character-layer edge to `[[eld-cinderella-cycle]]`. Per the card's trivia bullet, the carriage is named in Mark Rosewater's "Odds & Ends: Throne of Eldraine" (2019-10-14) design diary as a modular-flavor example. Boros is the illustrator on one of ELD's headline designer-confirmed cycle cards.
- **Nyxborn Brute** (Theros Beyond Death 144, 2020) — `{4}{B}` Enchantment Creature — Cyclops 4/3, constellation-trigger enabler-pool member. Black Nyxborn cyclops in the THB constellation-enabler enchantment-creature pool that the `[[constellation]]` symbol node's "enabler-pool" section enumerates.
- **Nessian Boar** (Theros Beyond Death 181, 2020) — `{3}{G}{G}` 6/5 Beast with combat-pump trigger. Green Nessos-forest fauna; the second THB Boros credit. Not in `[[nylea]]` cohort but Nessos is canonically Nylea's domain.
- **Spark Reaper** (War of the Spark 106, common, 2019) — `{3}{B}` 3/3 Skeleton with planeswalker-removal trigger. Existing character-layer edge to `[[kaya]]`. The four WAR Boros credits cluster the set's mid-block production — WAR is the densest Boros set in corpus.
- **Goblin Assault Team** (War of the Spark 129, common, 2019) — `{R}` 1/1 Goblin with planeswalker-removal trigger. Goblin-rush combat register; canonical Boros mass-figure composition.
- **Makeshift Battalion** (War of the Spark 22, common, 2019) — first printing of the Boros art that M21-26 reprints; the WAR-print is the canonical-original.
- **Martyr for the Cause** (War of the Spark 23, common, 2019) — `{2}{W}` 2/2 Human Soldier with sacrifice-for-amass trigger. White-mana sacrificial-soldier register; the fourth WAR Boros credit.
- **Borderland Ranger** (The List 31, reprint, originally Magic 2012) — `{2}{G}` 2/2 Human Scout with land-tutor ETB. Listed under PLST per Scryfall canonical handling; the original Boros illustration carries forward from M12.
- **Bad Deal** (The List 89/274, reprint, originally Ravnica Allegiance) — `{4}{B}{B}` discard-and-life-loss sorcery. PLST reprint of an RNA card; Boros's original art across both printings.
- **Savage Smash** (Ravnica Allegiance 203, common, 2019) — `{1}{R}{G}` Gruul fight-spell with creature-on-creature combat. Gruul aggression register; the only RNA-original solo Boros credit in corpus.

Two duo-credit cards with Gabor Szikszai treated separately (the credit string differs from the solo work; both names appear on the printed card):

- **Vectis Dominator** (Alara Reborn 84, common, 2009) — duo-credit; the oldest Boros-Szikszai print in corpus, four years before the partnership dissolved.
- **Turn to Slag** (Core Set 2021 168, common, 2020) — duo-credit; per the card's trivia, *"Scryfall carries two separate artist IDs for the pair, reflecting Wizards' joint credit convention."* A reunion-after-split commission. The M21 set has both solo Boros credits (26, 35, 37) AND this duo print — an unusual same-set co-presence.

## Stylistic signature

Boros's solo-credit slice in corpus reads with a coherent visual register, distinguishable from other MTG illustrators at a glance:

- **Kinetic forward-facing figures with mouth-open battle-cry expressions** — Makeshift Battalion, Siege Striker, Goblin Assault Team, Martyr for the Cause, Savage Smash all foreground a single figure (or central figure in a small group) facing forward with mouth open mid-shout. The composition gravity is *the moment of the battle cry* rather than the moment of impact.
- **Muted ochre / grey-blue palettes with selective gold highlights** — armor-trim gold accents against off-white-and-grey base palettes (Makeshift Battalion, Siege Striker, Secure the Scene); the warm-cool tension is signature-level discipline. Compare to the canonical Boros action-figure painterly register documented across the duo-era WoW TCG and Hearthstone work.
- **Painterly brushwork with visible directional strokes** — the rendering is expressionistic rather than fine-detail; brush strokes remain legible as marks on the painted surface rather than blended away. Siege Striker's plate armor and Bake into a Pie's apparatus-of-extraction kitchen elements both carry visible directional brushwork as part of the painted finish.
- **Group-and-mass compositions** — Boros frequently paints multiple figures interacting (Makeshift Battalion's three-figure unit, Siege Striker amid swarming goblins, Goblin Assault Team's literal goblin assault). The mass-composition discipline is the structural counterpart to Richard Kane Ferguson's sublime-mood-still-center compositions and to Magali Villeneuve's portrait-and-close-up register.
- **Boros-Szikszai cross-period continuity** — the kinetic-action figure-work register is consistent across the 2009 duo print (Vectis Dominator) and the 2024 solo print (Bespoke Battlewagon), 15 years apart. The visual register is stable across the partnership-to-solo transition, suggesting the action-figure-and-armor discipline was Boros-driven within the duo-era division of labor.

This is the canonical-by-corpus stylistic profile against which future Boros credits can be checked.

## Bundle-anchor potential

Boros's corpus slice opens three direct bundle-anchor frames:

- **The men they send — soldier-and-arrest cohort.** Secure the Scene already participates in the **Tithe** bundle (BBL003 / Discrete Lair 003), per its `bundles: [tithe]` frontmatter. The Tithe bundle thesis is *the men they send — institutional authority depicted in card art, the apparatus of extraction, different uniforms, different institutions, same job description*. Boros's broader corpus slice extends the same thesis: Makeshift Battalion (the unit), Siege Striker (the outnumbered enforcer), Martyr for the Cause (sacrifice-for-the-state), Goblin Assault Team (the goblin-tier expendables) all read on the same axis. A future Tithe-Variant or Tithe-Adjacent Discrete Lair anchored entirely on Boros-credited soldier-and-arrest commons is a substantive bundle candidate.

- **Eldraine fairy-tale-as-apparatus cohort.** Bake into a Pie + Enchanted Carriage + Improbable Alliance are three Boros-credited ELD prints that span the fairy-tale-as-flavor-direction set design. Enchanted Carriage's `eld-cinderella-cycle` membership and Bake into a Pie's eat-the-evidence flavor are structurally distinct but stylistically coherent within the Boros register. A future ELD-themed Discrete Lair anchored on Boros's slice would carry the fairy-tale-as-apparatus thesis with a single-artist visual through-line.

- **War of the Spark mid-block-common artist cohesion.** Four WAR Boros credits (Spark Reaper, Goblin Assault Team, Makeshift Battalion, Martyr for the Cause) plus the WAR-22 → M21-26 Makeshift Battalion reprint covers a substantial fraction of the WAR common slot — that's a tight artist-cohesion case for any WAR-themed bundle. Spark Reaper's `[[kaya]]` edge gives the slice a story-anchor; the other three carry the apparatus-of-Bolas's-invasion register that the WAR set design canonically frames.

A future expansion path: if a single new Boros credit lands in any pre-2009 set (e.g. an older PLST reprint with the original duo-era art), the artist-node body-prose splits into a partnership-era subsection and a solo-era subsection, formalizing the duo-to-solo career arc more explicitly than the current corpus permits. Flagged here for future Edgelord audit waves.

## Caveats

- **Modeled as an artist-layer node, like other artist-layer nodes in the BBL graph.** Sibling artist nodes precedent: `kagemaru-himeno`, `dan-murayama-scott`, `richard-kane-ferguson`, `magali-villeneuve`, `victor-adame-minguez`, `clint-cearley`, `ken-sugimori`, `sanosuke-sakuma`, `kirisaki`, `keiji-kinebuchi`, `mitsuhiro-arita`, `jenn-ravenna-tran`. Threshold of ≥3 corpus credits clears at 16 solo; cross-set scope clears at 7 sets. This is the densest artist-node candidate currently in corpus.
- **The Boros-Szikszai duo credit is structurally distinct from solo Boros credits.** Scryfall carries two separate artist IDs for the pair per Wizards' joint-credit convention. The 2 corpus duo prints (Vectis Dominator ARB-84, Turn to Slag M21-168) are listed in a separate section of the body, not in the canonical `appears_on:` frontmatter, because the credit string on the printed card differs ("Zoltan Boros & Gabor Szikszai" vs "Zoltan Boros") and any future `artist_resolve.py` lookup against this node's `aliases:` must match the solo-credit form to attach correctly. A future `gabor-szikszai.md` artist node would mirror this discipline and would carry the same 2 duo prints in its own body-prose section.
- **The Makeshift Battalion print appears twice in corpus** under different collector numbers (WAR-22 and M21-26) with identical art. Both prints are listed separately in `appears_on:` to preserve the Obsidian-style filename-stem `[[wikilink]]` resolution; the canonical-original is the WAR-22 print and the M21-26 entry is the reprint. The credit count of 16 solo treats them as two separate corpus credits because the BBL inventory carries them as separate cards; future Edgelord audit waves may consolidate to a single canonical-print plus reprint-pointer if the artist-layer convention formalizes the distinction.
- **Cross-medium scope is documented but not in-corpus.** Boros's D&D, World of Warcraft TCG, Hearthstone, and Diablo work is canonically documented per the Secure the Scene trivia capture, but no in-corpus card sits in those properties. The artist node treats those as biographical context, not as cohort-anchor evidence. The `cross_medium: false` frontmatter flag is set accordingly — the cross-medium work is *real* but does not generate BBL-corpus edges.
- **Stylistic-register attribution is not claimed for individual partnership-era prints.** During the 1986–2010 partnership, the joint credit string makes single-author attribution unreliable; the duo-era prints (Vectis Dominator) are treated as joint authorship without a stylistic claim about which half painted which element. The post-2010 solo register is attributable to Boros alone; the partnership-era register is treated as the duo's combined output. Future research into the duo's published interviews may resolve more specific partition-of-labor claims; not authored at commission time.

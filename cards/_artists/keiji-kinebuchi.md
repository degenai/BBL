# Keiji Kinebuchi

Pokemon TCG illustrator (1956-2002), the primary 3D computer graphics renderer for the original Pokemon Trading Card Game from its English-release launch through the Aquapolis set. Approximately 100+ original-era card credits before his death in 2002 ended direct contributions to the franchise. The corpus carries nine credits, all reprinted in the Evolutions set (XY12, November 2016) — Pokemon's 20th-anniversary tribute to the original 1999 Base Set, where his original illustrations were reissued posthumously with credit preserved.

## Why this artist gets a node

Three reasons compound:

**Corpus volume above precedent threshold.** Nine unambiguous credits in corpus exceed the [[sanosuke-sakuma]] precedent (5 credits triggered Sakuma's node), exceed the [[kirisaki]] tier (7 credits), and exceed [[kagemaru-himeno]]'s 6+ unambiguous tier. Kinebuchi is currently the second-highest-volume single Pokemon-side illustrator in corpus by stamped-credit count (behind Himeno when the Himeno-bracketed credits resolve). All nine credits land within a single set (Evolutions XY12), making the per-set illustrator-density unusually high — Kinebuchi has the largest single-set illustrator presence in corpus.

**Posthumous-tribute reprint signal is canonically load-bearing.** The Evolutions set is not a routine reissue. It is an explicit Wizards-of-the-Coast 20th-anniversary tribute set, released November 2, 2016 to mark Pokemon's two-decade milestone, with the express design conceit of reprinting original 1999 Base Set art under the modern card format. Kinebuchi's nine Evolutions credits are the largest cluster of posthumous-tribute prints by a single illustrator in the set. The cluster encodes a specific franchise-curatorial gesture — the deliberate decision by The Pokemon Company to honor a deceased illustrator by carrying his original 3D-CG renderings forward into the anniversary set rather than commissioning new art for those slots. That gesture is in-canon at the set-design level and is the load-bearing fact for any Discrete Lair that wants to anchor on franchise-illustration-tradition, original-era-art-preservation, or memorial-reprint thematics.

**Distinctive 3D-CG illustration medium.** Most documented Pokemon TCG illustrators in corpus work in watercolor ([[kagemaru-himeno]]), digital line art ([[kirisaki]], [[sanosuke-sakuma]]), or hand illustration. Kinebuchi's signature was 3D computer graphics rendering — a technique that distinguished the original Base Set era's "product-photography" trainer/energy cards (the Potion spray bottle, the Full Heal vial, the Super Potion bottle, the energy-card starlit fields) from the painterly creature illustrations by his contemporaries. The 3D-CG technique is visible across the corpus's nine Kinebuchi credits as a consistent medium-signature: clean volumetric-rendered objects, soft photorealistic lighting, and a register that reads as product-shot or object-rendering rather than character-portraiture or environmental-illustration. That medium-signature is the artist-layer-internal disambiguation function the node provides (canonical-by-corpus stylistic profile against which future Kinebuchi credits — including any further posthumous reprints in upcoming anniversary sets — can be checked).

## Stylistic signature

Per the corpus-internal canonical citation (GAS-047 Evolutions wave 100 trivia, sourcing Bulbapedia and pokemonaaah.net primary references):

- **3D computer graphics rendering medium** — distinct from watercolor ([[kagemaru-himeno]]) or digital line illustration ([[kirisaki]], [[sanosuke-sakuma]]). The technique uses volumetric modeling and photorealistic lighting passes rather than hand-illustration line work.
- **Product-shot register on trainer/energy cards** — the Potion spray bottle, the Super Potion sibling, the Full Heal vial, the Energy Retrieval mechanism, and the Double Colorless Energy starlit field all read as 3D-rendered object photography rather than illustrated diagrams. The lighting is soft and from above; the object is centered; the background is a neutral gradient or radiant field.
- **Volumetric creature shading on Pokemon cards** — the Gastly's deep-purple gaseous body, the Kakuna's softly-lit chrysalis, the Voltorb's reflective-sphere highlights, the Diglett's earth-emerging silhouette all share a soft photorealistic lighting register distinguishing Kinebuchi's creature work from the contemporaneous painted illustrations of Arita / Sugimori / Himeno.
- **Original Base Set design DNA preserved** — because the nine corpus prints are reissues of Kinebuchi's 1999 Base Set illustrations rather than newly-commissioned 2016 Evolutions work, they preserve the original-era 3D-CG aesthetic exactly. This makes the corpus cluster a canonical reference point for what the original Pokemon TCG illustration tradition looked like in its founding era.

This is the canonical-by-corpus stylistic profile against which future Kinebuchi credits can be checked. Any future anniversary or tribute reissue (e.g. a possible 25th-anniversary or 30th-anniversary Pokemon TCG set) that reprints original Base Set art would extend this node's appears_on list with further posthumous Kinebuchi credits.

## Caveats

- **Evolutions cluster is single-set by design.** All nine corpus Kinebuchi credits land in the Evolutions (XY12, 2016) set. This is not a corpus-coverage limitation — it is the canonical reissue pattern: Kinebuchi died in 2002, so all post-Aquapolis credits in his name across any modern set are necessarily reprints of his original 1996-2002 era work. The Evolutions set's 20th-anniversary-tribute conceit is the specific franchise-decision that created the corpus cluster; future similar tribute sets (e.g. 25th or 30th anniversary) might extend the cluster but the original-Base-Set source pool is fixed at the work he completed during his lifetime.

- **Posthumous-tribute reprint signal is the node's distinguishing feature.** No other Pokemon TCG artist node in BBL's corpus carries the `posthumous_reprints: true` flag at present. Kinebuchi is currently the corpus's only confirmed posthumous-tribute illustrator at this scope. If future corpus additions introduce other deceased illustrators whose work is reissued in tribute sets (e.g. cards by other Base Set-era illustrators who have since died), this node serves as the structural precedent for the artist-layer's handling of posthumous reissue context.

- **Switch CRZ-144 mentions Kinebuchi but is not in this node's appears_on.** The Crown Zenith Switch trainer card (CRZ-144) is illustrated by Studio Bora Inc., not Kinebuchi. The card's trivia bullet correctly cites Kinebuchi as the original Base Set Switch illustrator (1999, no. 95) as historical context, but the modern CRZ-144 print is a Studio Bora re-illustration of the same trainer-card concept rather than a Kinebuchi-credit reissue. The appears_on list above excludes CRZ-144 correctly; only cards with `artist: Keiji Kinebuchi` stamped in frontmatter are included.

- **3D-CG medium is the period-defining technique, not a per-artist stylistic flourish.** Kinebuchi's 3D computer graphics rendering technique was the era-standard for original-era Pokemon TCG trainer-and-energy cards (late 1990s through Aquapolis), used also by collaborators on similar product-rendered trainer-item slots. The canonical-by-corpus stylistic signature documented above is specific to Kinebuchi's renderings as they appear in the Evolutions reissues, but the broader 3D-CG-medium tradition belongs to the original Base Set era's design palette as a whole rather than to Kinebuchi as a unique-signature contributor. Future artist-node commissions for other original-era 3D-CG illustrators would share the period-medium signature while differing in per-artist subject coverage and rendering details.

# Daarken (Mike Lim)

American illustrator and concept artist; full name **Mike "Daarken" Lim**, verified via direct fetch of his official About page at daarken.com (2026-05-26). Trained at the Academy of Art University in San Francisco, graduating Cum Laude with a BFA in traditional illustration after a three-year accelerated program — his About page describes those years as "huddled over a sketchbook at a figure drawing workshop or in a cafe, where he was affectionately known as 'the guy that likes to draw deltoids.'" His Magic: The Gathering career began in 2004 with an unsolicited phone call from Wizards of the Coast that he accepted before being told the project brief. From 2007 onward he was hired as a concept artist on Warhammer Online: Age of Reckoning under Mythic Entertainment (then EA Mythic post-acquisition, later BioWare Mythic — his About page uses the post-acquisition framing). He currently freelances full-time. Per Scryfall's artist search (probed 2026-05-26), 346 unique-art Magic credits are recorded under his name — placing him among the densest active MTG illustrators in the corpus, near [[zoltan-boros]]'s solo-credit density. His cross-medium client list includes Wizards of the Coast (Magic: The Gathering and Dungeons & Dragons), Bethesda (The Elder Scrolls: Legends), Valve (Artifact), Games Workshop (Warhammer 40k), Darrington Press (Daggerheart), Hit Point Press, Draco Studios, and Lord of the Rings IP — verified via the daarken.com site navigation menu.

## Stylistic signature

Daarken's BBL-corpus trivia-passed slice reads with a coherent visual register:

- **Dark-tonal villain-and-apparatus compositions.** Prison Realm is the WAR Bolas-Falls terminal containment chamber; Maw of Kozilek is an Eldrazi-brood mouth-as-organism; Grasp of Darkness is the Zendikari-Kor warrior reaching toward unidentified dark crystal mass. All three sit in the dark-tonal villain-or-apparatus register the artist's own About page names as career through-line ("creating monsters and zombies").

- **Story-spotlight and canon-weight commissions.** Prison Realm is a Scryfall-flagged story-spotlight card with explicit Bolas-canon flavor text; Maw of Kozilek's flavor quotes Gideon Jura and is canonically tied to the Eldrazi-titan brood identification; Grasp of Darkness's M21 reprint commission is a designer-coordinated flavor rework from the Phyrexian original. The art-direction pattern suggests Wizards routinely commissions Daarken for cards where the canonical apparatus needs to be rendered with weight — the planeswalker prison, the Eldrazi mouth, the dark-magic crystal — rather than for generic-creature-art slots.

- **Chiaroscuro / heavy-shadow palettes.** All three trivia-passed prints render the central subject as a dark mass with a high-contrast accent (Prison Realm's translucent prism glow against the dark prisoner-silhouette; Maw of Kozilek's blue-tinged maw against the dark surrounding rock; Grasp of Darkness's reaching-warrior figure against the dark crystal mass). The palette discipline is consistent across three different cards spanning 2016-2020.

- **Brood / planeswalker / Bolas-coordination cohort presence.** Daarken's WAR cluster (Prison Realm, Davriel character + spell, Sorin's Thirst) and his OGW cluster (Maw of Kozilek, Immolating Glare) both sit inside Wizards's planeswalker-and-villain story-spotlight pools. This is a stronger placement-density pattern than the typical journeyman illustrator gets — the artist-direction team appears to route narrative-heavy story-spotlight commissions to Daarken disproportionately. (Anecdotal-from-corpus observation, not Wizards-stated policy; flagged here as future Edgelord audit follow-up if additional Daarken prints land in subsequent waves.)

This is the canonical-by-corpus stylistic profile against which future Daarken credits in BBL can be checked. The 343 non-corpus credits in his Scryfall catalog (the remaining cards beyond the 18 in BBL) offer broader career context but do not generate edges at this commission.

# Dan Murayama Scott

Magic: The Gathering illustrator with three credits in the BBL corpus, all from *Battle for Zendikar* (BFZ, 2015). His name occasionally appears in older databases as "Dan Scott" or "Daniel Murayama Scott"; the canonical credit on the printed BFZ cards is **Dan Murayama Scott**, the form Scryfall now uses across his catalog. The artist's published portfolio confirms the BFZ set as a primary block in his Magic illustration career.

## Why this artist gets a node

Three corpus credits meet the soft threshold the artist-layer convention has converged on (per [[sanosuke-sakuma]] at five, [[kirisaki]] at seven, [[ken-sugimori]] at seventeen, and the wave-18 Kagemaru Himeno node at three). Three is the minimum the threshold accepts; Dan Murayama Scott clears it on three independent BFZ-set credits, not on three reprints of the same illustration.

The three credits also form a **stylistic cohort within a single set** — all three are BFZ-block prints with a coherent palette and compositional discipline, which is the more interesting feature of his corpus slice than the raw count. Where Sakuma's portfolio is structurally interesting because of cross-medium scope (Galarian NPC-trainer-class designs as the franchise-design parallel to TCG illustration), and Sugimori's is structurally interesting because of franchise-authorship-level scope, Murayama Scott's is structurally interesting because of **set-coherent-style discipline** — three BFZ cards that, when laid out side-by-side, read as a coordinated visual register inside the larger BFZ art-direction brief.

## Cards in our corpus

- **Gideon's Reproach** (Battle for Zendikar 30, common, 2015) — armored male humanoid figure on a Zendikar landscape, swinging a golden energy sural (lasso / whip) with three energy arcs trailing across the composition. Ornate gold chest armor; long dark hair; Zendikari floating-rock columns in the background. The depicted figure is canonically Gideon Jura (suspected_ip stamped, ip_confidence: med, ip_verified pending the 2nd-corpus-anchor triage trigger; see janitor_triage Gideon entry). The most iconic visual feature of the print is the **golden energy lasso** — Gideon's canonical signature weapon since his M11 introduction. `cards/magic-the-gathering/battle-for-zendikar/30-gideon-s-reproach.md`

- **Kitesail Scout** (Battle for Zendikar 33, common, 2015) — a kor scout faces away from camera, soaring with a kitesail above a golden Zendikar canyon at sunset. The composition's center of gravity is the wind-stretched fabric of the kitesail and the trailing tassels of the kor's armor. Warm-tone palette discipline: sunset golds in the sky, canyon ambers in the rock below, dark silhouette of the figure against the bright background. Flavor text: *"The wind in one's hair, the sun on one's back, the joy of open skies . . . the next generation of kor must know this kind of peace."* The card is among the most-loved BFZ commons among players for the composition's freedom-of-flight register. `cards/magic-the-gathering/battle-for-zendikar/33-kitesail-scout.md`

- **Unified Front** (Battle for Zendikar 53, uncommon, 2015) — multi-color sorcery with Converge keyword. Depicts kor warriors raising banners in coordinated militia formation. Flavor text: *"The kor's pride in their independence has given way to a strong sense of community that embraces all of Zendikar."* `cards/magic-the-gathering/battle-for-zendikar/53-unified-front.md`

## Stylistic signature

Murayama Scott's BFZ slice reads with a coherent visual register that's distinguishable from other BFZ contributors at a glance:

- **Warm-tone palette discipline** — sunset golds, canyon ambers, warm-grey rock, dark-grey shadow. The two kor cards (Kitesail Scout, Unified Front) lean directly into Zendikar's golden-hour register; Gideon's Reproach uses the same warm-tone palette inverted (bright Zendikari background, dark armored foreground figure).
- **Action-pose composition with motion-trail iconography** — Gideon's three energy-arc lasso, Kitesail Scout's wind-tassel trails, Unified Front's banner-formation directionality. All three cards encode movement into the composition's center of gravity rather than treating the subject as static.
- **Zendikari terrain rendering** — the artist's BFZ commissions all engage the plane's signature visual features (floating rock columns, canyon spires, golden horizon, vertical landscape). His Kitesail Scout in particular is one of the canonical Zendikar-canyon-at-sunset commons of the set.
- **Long-hair-and-tassels discipline** — Gideon's long dark hair, the kor warriors' tassel-and-rope rigs, the kitesail's wind-stretched fabric. Soft-edge fabric-and-hair rendering is a consistent stylistic signature across his BFZ slice.

The three credits together form what reads, at the art-direction tier, as a **mini-cohort within BFZ's broader common-and-uncommon slot** — three cards that lean into the same Zendikari golden-hour register and that an attentive set-builder could anchor on for a curated BFZ Discrete Lair.

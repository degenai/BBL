# Magali Villeneuve

French illustrator with one of the most recognizable signatures in Magic: The Gathering's contemporary art roster. Eight confirmed credits in the BBL corpus across five sets — *Theros* (2013), *Battle for Zendikar* (2015), *Oath of the Gatewatch* (2016), *War of the Spark* (2019), *Throne of Eldraine* (2019), and *Theros Beyond Death* (2020) — spanning a seven-year arc of MTG illustration work. Cross-medium: also credited on Wheel of Time book covers, A Song of Ice and Fire card game, Star Wars: The Card Game, and Warhammer 40,000 publications. TCG illustration is one wing of a broader publishing-illustration career, not the whole of it.

## Why this artist gets a node

Eight corpus credits clears the soft three-credit threshold the artist-layer convention has converged on (per [[kagemaru-himeno]] at three, [[sanosuke-sakuma]] at five, [[kirisaki]] at seven, [[ken-sugimori]] at seventeen) by a meaningful margin. The interesting feature of Villeneuve's corpus slice is not the count alone — it's the **register span**. Her eight prints lean into three distinct narrative modes:

- **Greek-mythic stillness** (Theros / Theros Beyond Death) — *Guardians of Meletis*, *Alseid of Life's Bounty*, *Relentless Pursuit*. Sublime palettes, lone or paired figures against vast landscape, wind and trail iconography, mythic register.
- **Zendikari resistance survival** (BFZ / OGW) — *Serene Steward*, *Make a Stand*. White-magic protector figures in ruined terrain, hopeful-but-besieged register, care-and-defiance verbs.
- **Eldraine and Ravnica portraiture** (ELD / WAR) — *Belle of the Brawl*, *Kasmina, Enigmatic Mentor*, *Rally of Wings*. Named-character portraits and named-faction scenes; the planeswalker-and-noble register where the painted surface is doing identity-work on a specific named individual.

A single artist anchoring across all three registers is structurally interesting for BBL because it lets a bundle reach for "Villeneuve cohort" as a cohesion-signal that crosses set boundaries — most artist nodes anchor a single set or a single visual register, not a spine.

## Market-canonical anchor — the $18,200 sketch auction

In 2022, Wizards released the **Artist Series: Magali Villeneuve Secret Lair drop** — a five-card Secret Lair entirely re-illustrated by Villeneuve, including a reprint of *Liliana of the Veil*. The original graphite sketches Villeneuve produced for that drop were subsequently sold at auction. **Per Hipsters of the Coast's May 2022 reporting, the sketches sold for a combined $18,200** — among the highest values realized for MTG artist-series preliminary art, and a load-bearing market signal for Villeneuve's standing in the contemporary MTG illustration economy.

This is not a "she's a popular artist" claim; it's a quantified market position. The auction outcome documents that the *preliminary sketches* — work that's typically treated as preparatory material — were valued at five-figure totals on the strength of Villeneuve's name alone. For BBL's curation framing (museum-tier, refusal-with-receipts), this is the kind of citation that anchors a Villeneuve cohort bundle's narrative_premium line item without overreaching.

Source: *Hipsters of the Coast*, "Sketches for Artist Series: Magali Villeneuve Secret Lair Drop Sell for $18,200 Combined", May 2022.

## Stylistic signature

Across the three registers her corpus slice spans, a few consistent moves recur:

- **Painted-surface luminosity** — Villeneuve's whites and pale tones are doing the heavy lifting on *Make a Stand*, *Alseid of Life's Bounty*, *Relentless Pursuit*, and *Rally of Wings*. Light is not lit-from-above ambient; it's coming from the painted surface itself.
- **Composition centered on a single figure's emotional read** — even in group scenes (*Make a Stand*, *Rally of Wings*) the painted surface returns the viewer to a face or a gesture as the load-bearing element. The exception, *Guardians of Meletis*, swaps face-and-gesture for monument-and-mist; the move still works.
- **Garment-and-hair softness** — flowing fabric, long hair, draped cloaks, wind-stretched cloth. *Belle of the Brawl*'s dark-knight regalia and *Serene Steward*'s layered traveling cloak both lean on this. Recurrent across the eight prints.
- **Named-character commissions** — Villeneuve gets the Kasmina commission in WAR, gets the Liliana of the Veil re-illustration in her 2022 Secret Lair, and gets the Alseid-of-Life's-Bounty single-figure-portrait slot in THB. Wizards routinely hands her the "this is the canonical face of a named character" commissions; that pattern is its own structural feature of her portfolio.

## Bundle-anchor potential

The Villeneuve corpus opens several distinct bundle-anchor frames:

- **Theros-block Villeneuve mini-cohort** — *Guardians of Meletis* + *Alseid of Life's Bounty* + *Relentless Pursuit*. Three prints across Theros (2013) and Theros Beyond Death (2020), all in her Greek-mythic register. A bundle leaning into the Theros block's quiet-mythic register could anchor on the Villeneuve trio as the visual through-line across the two sets.

- **Zendikari resistance Villeneuve attaches** — *Serene Steward* + *Make a Stand*. Two BFZ-block white-magic resistance cards already pointing at the [[zendikari-resistance]] character node. The Villeneuve byline reinforces a "single-artist cohesion within a faction" lever in any BFZ resistance Discrete Lair.

- **Named-character portrait cohort** — *Kasmina, Enigmatic Mentor* + *Belle of the Brawl*. A potential frame for a bundle organized around "Villeneuve renders the named woman" — the planeswalker portrait and the Eldraine knight as paired commissions in the same year (2019, WAR + ELD). Cross-set cohesion via artist-named-character commission pattern.

- **The $18,200 Secret Lair anchor** — for any bundle drawing on Villeneuve cards, the auction citation is a load-bearing market-canonical anchor for the narrative_premium line item. "These cards are by the illustrator whose preliminary sketches sold for $18,200" is a quantified curator-claim, not a vibes-claim.

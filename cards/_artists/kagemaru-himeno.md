# Kagemaru Himeno

Pokemon TCG freelance illustrator. One of the longest-tenured artists in the entire franchise, working continuously since the Jungle set (Japanese release 1996, English release 1999). As of 2024, she had illustrated 654+ Pokemon TCG cards including reprints, placing her among a small handful of illustrators whose career spans the entire TCG's existence. Her signature medium is watercolor; her signature technique is soft tonal washes punctuated with bright pinpoint highlights and characteristic white flecks suggesting dappled light filtering through foliage.

## Why this artist gets a node

Three reasons compound:

**Corpus volume.** Six unambiguous credits in corpus (Emolga CIN-35, Charmander BUS-18, Meowth BUS-101, Steenee UNM-18, Torkoal BRS-023, Prinplup BRS-036) plus a possible seventh on Charmander VIV-023 — exceeds the [[sanosuke-sakuma]] threshold (5 credits) and approaches the [[kirisaki]] tier (7 credits), placing Himeno among the most-printed single illustrators in the entire BBL corpus.

**Franchise-tenure significance.** Most TCG illustrators rotate in and out of the franchise across set eras. Himeno has been continuously credited since 1996 (Japanese) / 1999 (English), making her career span the TCG's entire existence. She is one of a handful of illustrators alive who has illustrated cards in every generation of Pokemon, from Kanto-only Jungle through Hoenn, Sinnoh, Unova, Kalos, Alola, Galar, Paldea, and Hisui. The 654+ card count is not the result of high-volume production-line illustration; it's the result of three decades of continuous artistic involvement.

**Stylistic signature is canonically identifiable.** Per the in-corpus BUS-18 Charmander trivia citation (which surfaced from Bulbapedia and artofpkm.com primary sources): "She works in watercolor, using soft tones with bright highlights and characteristic white flecks suggesting light through foliage." The white-flecks-as-dappled-light technique is a recognizable artist signature across the corpus prints — visible most clearly on the warm-amber-forest BUS-18 Charmander background, the bamboo-grove dappled light, and the warm-rooftop-day BUS-101 Meowth lighting. That signature provides an artist-layer-internal disambiguation function (canonical-by-corpus stylistic profile against which future Himeno credits can be checked, parallel to the kirisAki node's stylistic-signature section).

Himeno's portfolio in current corpus does not (yet) anchor a labor-hub or institutional-thesis bundle in the way Sakuma's NPC-design portfolio anchors [[labor]]. Her load-bearing function is artist-layer-internal: highest-volume corpus illustrator on the Pokemon side after kirisAki, with a franchise-tenure case that no other corpus-stamped illustrator currently matches.

## Stylistic signature

Per the corpus-internal canonical citation (BUS-18 Charmander wave 17 trivia, sourcing Bulbapedia and artofpkm.com):

- **Watercolor medium** with soft tonal washes rather than digital hard-edge work; the signature is most legible in background environments (warm amber forest light on BUS-18, sunny rooftop atmospheric light on BUS-101).
- **Bright pinpoint highlights** that read as small, deliberate brushstrokes of pure white or near-white pigment placed at specific spots on the figure (eye highlights, edge-of-flame highlights, the dorsal-ridge highlights on creature silhouettes).
- **Characteristic white flecks** scattered across the background to suggest light filtering through foliage, leaf canopy gaps, or atmospheric particulate — the technique reads as a recognizable artist signature visible across multiple corpus prints.
- **Soft posed figures** rather than dynamic-action compositions; the figures sit in alert-but-calm postures within atmospherically-resolved environments, consistent with a Pokemon-card-illustration register that frames the creature as a character within a setting rather than as a combat-portrait subject.

This is the canonical-by-corpus stylistic profile against which future Himeno credits can be checked. Alternate-rarity prints (Full Art, Rainbow Rare) credited as Himeno but visually departing from this signature warrant a print-object-level look at whether the credit is the canonical-author Himeno or a print-object-level alternate-author stamp.

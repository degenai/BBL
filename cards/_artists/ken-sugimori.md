# Ken Sugimori

Pokemon franchise art director and co-founder of Game Freak. Born January 1966 in Tokyo, Japan; began working with Satoshi Tajiri in 1989 on Game Freak's pre-Pokemon publications (Game Freak fanzine), formally co-founded Game Freak as a studio in 1989, and has served as the franchise's lead character designer and art director continuously since Pokemon Red and Green (Japan, 1996) / Red and Blue (international, 1998) shipped. He is the canonical author of the visual identity of the original 151 Pokemon and has supervised the franchise's design language across every subsequent generation.

## Why this artist gets a node

Three reasons compound, and each is independently sufficient by the BBL artist-layer threshold:

**Foundational franchise authorship.** Sugimori is not a contracted TCG illustrator in the way the rest of the Pokemon-side artist layer is — Sanosuke Sakuma, kirisAki, Kagemaru Himeno, and the rest are freelance illustrators commissioned per-set. Sugimori is the franchise's *internal* art director. He designed the species depicted on every Pokemon card. The corpus's seventeen Sugimori-credited prints therefore carry a distinct register: when his name appears on a card, the credit's load-bearing claim is "designed by Game Freak's franchise art director" rather than "freelance illustrator for this print." That register is unique among the corpus's Pokemon-side artist layer and warrants its own node so the disambiguation between *franchise-internal* and *freelance-external* credit registers is graph-explicit.

**Corpus volume.** Seventeen Sugimori-credited prints across nine sets — Base Set (1999), Evolutions (XY12, 2016, three Base Set tribute reprints), Burning Shadows (2017, three Sun & Moon Elite Four / trial captain Supporters), Guardians Rising (2017), Shining Legends (2017), Unified Minds (2019), Lost Thunder (2018), Cosmic Eclipse (2019, four prints including the Sun & Moon retrospective Tag-Team Supporters), Chilling Reign (2021), Vivid Voltage (2020). The volume far exceeds the [[sanosuke-sakuma]] threshold (five) and the [[kirisaki]] threshold (seven); this is the corpus's highest-volume Pokemon-side artist as of wave 29.

**Cross-medium scope at franchise level.** Sugimori's cross-medium portfolio is broader than Sakuma's NPC-design portfolio by orders of magnitude. He designed Pokemon species across every generation; he illustrated the official Pokedex entry artwork that defines each species' canonical visual identity; he supervised character design across the mainline game series; he illustrated promotional and marketing material across thirty years of franchise output. The Sakuma node anchors cross-medium scope at the *trainer-class NPC* level; the Sugimori node anchors cross-medium scope at the *entire franchise* level. Different scale, same structural justification for a node.

## Stylistic signature

Sugimori's canonical illustration style across the seventeen corpus prints reads as:

- **Flat-color illustration with clean defined linework** — pre-digital rendering preserved across his career; minimal painterly modeling, restrained shading, character silhouettes that read clearly at small sizes. The Base Set / Evolutions species prints are the canonical examples; the Burning Shadows and Cosmic Eclipse Supporter portraits maintain the same register.
- **Restrained palette per subject** — species cards typically use 3-5 dominant colors locked to the species' canonical-design palette (Caterpie's green/yellow/red-tipped antenna; Poliwag's slick black with white belly and spiral; Charmander's orange with cream underbelly and tail-flame). Supporter portraits use the canonical-character-outfit palette (Acerola's purple/blue Trial Captain dress; Leon's red/white Champion cape; Lillie's white sun hat and dress).
- **Frontal-or-three-quarter character framing** — Sugimori's compositional default is the character or species presented at a readable diagnostic angle, prioritizing identity-legibility over kinetic-action. This contrasts with the post-Sun-and-Moon-era illustrator pool, which trends toward action-pose framing and atmospheric lighting.
- **Pokedex-illustration-style staging** — Sugimori's species renders read as if extracted from the official Pokedex page rather than as scene-set illustrations. Backgrounds are typically plain solid colors or soft gradient washes; foreground subjects carry the visual load. This is the canonical franchise-authorship register: the subject is the *species*, not a moment in the species' life.

The Sugimori signature is therefore distinguishable from the rest of the Pokemon-side artist layer at a glance. When a card's printed-card render matches this signature, the credit's franchise-authorship claim is canonically supported; when a Sugimori-credited print does not match this signature (rare, mostly limited to special print-objects or alternate-rarity prints stamped under his name at the print-object level rather than the canonical-author level), a print-object-level disambiguation is warranted.

## Bundle-anchor potential

Sugimori's portfolio in corpus opens several thesis-tier bundle anchors:

- **Base Set nostalgia / Evolutions tribute** — the four Evolutions-set Sugimori reprints (Caterpie, Poliwag, Seel, plus the original Base Set Charmander) anchor a Sun-and-Moon-era retrospective-on-1998 bundle frame. The Evolutions set's entire design thesis is the preservation of Sugimori's original illustration style for the franchise's 20th anniversary — a Discrete Lair anchored on those reprints can name Sugimori directly and let the franchise-art-director citation do the thesis work.
- **Sun & Moon Tag-Team Supporter retrospective** — Cosmic Eclipse's late-era Tag-Team Supporters (Guzma & Hala, Mallow & Lana) are Sugimori-illustrated mechanic-closeout cards that pair canonically-related named characters across the Sun & Moon regional cast. A bundle on Cosmic-Eclipse-as-Sun-and-Moon-retrospective can anchor on these.
- **Named-character trainer-portrait portfolio** — Sugimori's Supporter portraits across Burning Shadows / Guardians Rising / Cosmic Eclipse / Chilling Reign / Vivid Voltage stage the franchise's named-character cast at the canonical-author level. A bundle on franchise-canonical-characters-across-generations (Will from Johto, Acerola/Mallow/Olivia/Plumeria from Alola, Siebold from Kalos, Leon from Galar) can anchor on this portfolio for its franchise-coherence thesis.

These thesis frames are independent of [[labor]] (the Sakuma bridge); Sugimori's portfolio operates at the franchise-authorship axis rather than the labor-class-NPC axis. Future bundles can anchor on Sugimori for *canonicity* rather than *labor*.

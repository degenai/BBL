## Trivia

- **Character context** — Marco Bott graduated 7th in his class in the 104th Training Corps — the highest-ranked cadet who was neither a titan shifter nor a secret operative. He is depicted in the series as mild-mannered, empathetic, and quietly regarded by his peers as officer material, a contrast to his actual rank that makes his death land harder. `[Attack on Titan Wiki, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Death and narrative weight** — Marco was ambushed by Reiner and Annie, stripped of his vertical maneuvering gear (rendering him defenseless), and then devoured by a Titan. His body went unidentified for days. The full truth of how he died — revealed much later in the story — recontextualizes Reiner and Annie as traitors who sacrificed him to protect their identities. His death is one of the series' most structurally important: it directly drives Jean Kirstein from selfish ambition toward genuine leadership. `[Attack on Titan Wiki / Screen Rant, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Set context** — This card is from the Attack on Titan Vol. 1 English booster (AOT/S35-E), produced by Bushiroad under official license from Kodansha. The AOT/S35 set covers Seasons 1 and 2 of the anime. Marco has at least two cards in the set: this one (E072, AUTO Corps-search effect) and "Tolerant Personality" Marco (E079). Common rarity. `[Amazon product listing AOT/S35-E072, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Community sentiment** — Marco is frequently cited in AoT fandom as the archetype of the "good person who dies too soon and too wrongly" — the injustice of his death (passive, stripped of agency, unwitnessed) is part of what makes it resonate. His role as Jean's moral compass is widely considered one of the series' most effective uses of a supporting character. `[TV Tropes: 104th Trainee Corps, via WebSearch snippet]`

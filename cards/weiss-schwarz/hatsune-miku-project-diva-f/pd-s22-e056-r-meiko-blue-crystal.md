## Trivia

- **Character history** — MEIKO is the first Japanese-language Vocaloid ever released, debuting on November 5, 2004 on the original VOCALOID 1 engine with vocals sampled from singer Meiko Haigo. She sold over 3,000 units — roughly three times Crypton's expectations — despite minimal marketing. A 20th anniversary was celebrated in 2024. `[Vocaloid Wiki: MEIKO, via WebSearch snippet; Siliconera: MEIKO 20th Anniversary]`
- **CVS clarification** — MEIKO is NOT part of the Character Vocal Series (CVS) numbering. The CV01/CV02/CV03 designations apply only to Crypton's VOCALOID 2-era lineup from 2007 onward (CV01 = Hatsune Miku, CV02 = Kagamine Rin/Len, CV03 = Megurine Luka). MEIKO and KAITO are pre-CVS characters; Crypton later absorbed their VOCALOID 3 updates and moved both into the Piapro Studio ecosystem as of August 31, 2019. `[Vocaloid Wiki: Character Vocal Series, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Module design** — The Blue Crystal module first appeared in Hatsune Miku: Project DIVA F, featuring a sapphire-blue open-collar jacket with puffed sleeves, matching epaulets, gloves, and a multi-layered skirt — a stark contrast to MEIKO's iconic red jacket default. The module reappeared in Project DIVA X and Project DIVA Future Tone (Future Sound pack, purchasable for 750 VP). `[Project DIVA Wiki: Blue Crystal, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Set context** — PD/S22 is the Weiss Schwarz booster set adapting Hatsune Miku: Project DIVA F, published by Bushiroad under license from SEGA and Crypton Future Media. The English edition contains 100 card types (8 RR / 20 R / 28 U / 28 C / 16 CX) plus 44 parallel variants, with SP Gold Foil signed cards from Saki Fujita (Hatsune Miku's voice actress). `[Weiss Schwarz official product page: en.ws-tcg.com/products/pd-f/]`

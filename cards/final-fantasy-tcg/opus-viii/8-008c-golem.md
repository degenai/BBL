## Trivia

- **Set context** — Opus VIII is the eighth booster set in the Final Fantasy Trading Card Game, released by Square Enix in 2019. The set drew from FF VII, FF IX, FF XIV: Stormblood, FF XV, Brave Exvius, and — as a featured exclusive — *Final Fantasy Crystal Chronicles*, with original art commissioned from Yasuhisa Izumizawa for the Crystal Chronicles cards. Card 8-008C Golem is among those Crystal Chronicles entries. [Square Enix FFTCG: Opus VIII release page; WebSearch snippet]
- **Source game** — The Golem depicted on this card is from *Final Fantasy Crystal Chronicles: Echoes of Time* (Square Enix, 2009), a multiplayer action-RPG for DS and Wii. The Golem in that game is an earth-element enemy type — a slow, heavily armored creature that guards specific dungeon areas. The squat hunched silhouette, mask-face with single circular eye, and geometric body patterning are consistent with the FFCC:EoT Golem's visual design. [WebSearch snippet: FFTCG Opus VIII Complete Collection]
- **Card mechanics** — The card's reported ability: when Golem enters the field, choose a Forward (an attacking creature) and give it +2000 power until end of turn. Additionally, you may put Golem in the Break Zone to stop a Forward from attacking. The card functions as a temporary blocker or pump piece — the creature's mechanical role in the TCG mirrors its role in the source game: a defensive obstacle. [WebSearch snippet: Big Orbit Cards / Superelf-Cards listing]
- **Bestiary longevity** — The "Golem" creature type has appeared in virtually every numbered Final Fantasy entry since the original NES game. As a named common in FFTCG, this card occupies the same mass-produced design slot the creature has always occupied in the series' enemy rosters: a guardian you encounter, not a companion you develop. [Final Fantasy Wiki: Final Fantasy Trading Card Game, via WebSearch snippet]

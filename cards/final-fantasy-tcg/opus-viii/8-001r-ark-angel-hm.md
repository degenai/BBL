## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — Ark Angel HM originates from Final Fantasy XI: Rise of the Zilart (2002), the first expansion for FFXI. The five Ark Angels are Crystal Warriors created by the ancient Zilart civilization to guard the five Crystal Arks. According to in-game lore: "Apathy, arrogance, cowardice, envy, rage — the darkness that lurks within us all." Each warrior embodies the defining sin of one of Vana'diel's five races: HM (Hume — apathy), EV (Elvaan — arrogance), TT (Tarutaru — cowardice), MR (Mithra — envy), GK (Galka — rage). They are fought in Zilart Mission 14 at La'Loff Amphitheater in Tu'Lia; defeating Ark Angel HM for the first time drops the Shard of Apathy. `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Ark Angel HM, via WebSearch snippet]` `[FFXI BG-wiki: Zilart Mission 14, via WebSearch snippet]`

- **Design — FFXI abilities mapped to card mechanics** — In the FFTCG card (8-001R, Rare, Fire element, cost 3, 6000 power): ability 1 doubles all damage dealt to the opponent; ability 2 grants +3000 power when attacking. These mirror Ark Angel HM's FFXI encounter design: the Cross Reaver weaponskill deals cone-area 500-900 damage (the signature finishing move), and the encounter features enhanced movement speed — both emphasizing burst-damage Hume physicality. HM can also use the Warrior job ability Mighty Strikes (critical-hit window) every 150 seconds, reinforcing the all-in combat identity. `[FFXI BG-wiki: Ark Angel HM, via WebSearch snippet]` `[TCGPlayer: Ark Angel HM 8-001R listing, via WebSearch snippet]`

- **Mechanical reputation** — The FFTCG Crystarium's Opus VIII limited review rated Ark Angel HM as a premium aggressive rare: "at only 3cp, it is almost impossible to deal with Ark Angel at a profit" — opponents either chump-block and lose significant card resources, or take near one-third of their life total in a single attack. The Crystarium notes it fits best in aggressive decks but slower grinder decks also view it as a premium win condition; the main downside is being a liability draw when scrambling to stabilize on defense. `[FFTCG Crystarium: "How to Win Limited: Opus VIII Review", via WebSearch snippet]` — curated community tier.

- **Community resonance** — FFDecks community sentiment: at least one prominent deck was titled "Ark Angel HM is insane..." — reflecting that the damage-doubling clause at 3-cost was considered above-rate for Opus VIII. The Mysidia Post's early meta analysis drew on FFDecks tournament data to assess HM-based aggressive lists. `[FFDecks: deck 273043 title, via WebSearch snippet — sentiment]` `[The Mysidia Post: "Early Analysis of the Opus VIII Meta", via WebSearch snippet]`

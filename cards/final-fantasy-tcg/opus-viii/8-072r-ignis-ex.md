## Trivia

- **IP verification** — The depicted figure is confirmed as Ignis Scientia from Final Fantasy XV. The card oracle text explicitly identifies the type as Forward / Retainer with category Final Fantasy XV, and the EX Burst ability tutors for a card named Noctis. Physical design — glasses, fitted dark suit, swept-back light hair, composed posture — matches Tetsuya Nomura's official character record for Ignis. `[Card oracle text; Wikipedia: Ignis Scientia]`
- **Character design** — Ignis Scientia was designed by Tetsuya Nomura. The English localization team at Square Enix modeled his characterization on "a younger James Bond or Sherlock Holmes" — an analytical, suave counterpart to Noctis's impulsive nature. He entered royal service as Noctis's advisor at age six, heir to House Scientia, a hereditary family of royal retainers to the Kingdom of Lucis. `[Wikipedia: Ignis Scientia]`
- **Mechanical design — EX Burst** — The "EX" in the card title is not part of Ignis's character name but denotes the FFTCG EX Burst mechanic: when the card enters the Damage Zone during an opponent's attack, its enters-the-field Auto ability activates, tutoring for a Noctis card. The mechanic encodes the retainer-to-prince relationship directly in the game's damage step. `[Final Fantasy Trading Card Game Wiki — EX Burst rules, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Mechanical design — Overwhelm** — The card's second ability (Overwhelm) deals 4000 damage per Category XV Forward the controller has on the field — scaling damage with the Brotherhood's full assembly, reflecting Ignis's role as the tactical coordinator whose effectiveness amplifies the whole party. `[TCGPlayer product listing — Ignis EX 8-072R; WebSearch snippet]`
- **Set context** — Opus VIII (March 2019) was the first FFTCG set to incorporate Final Fantasy XV as a standalone title category, alongside FFVII, FFIX, and FFXIV: Stormblood in the same 148-card release. `[TradingCardSets.com — FFTCG Opus VIII Complete Guide]`

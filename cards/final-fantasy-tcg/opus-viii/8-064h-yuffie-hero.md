## Trivia

- **Character canon** — Yuffie Kisaragi is one of two optional party members in Final Fantasy VII (1997), alongside Vincent Valentine. A 16-year-old Wutaian ninja encountered in overworld forests as the "Mystery Ninja," she must be recruited through a dialogue-choice minigame — players who fail or flee lose her permanently. Her motivation: Wutai lost its warrior culture after Shinra turned it into a resort town; Yuffie steals Materia from travelers to fund her plan to restore her homeland. Her father, Godo Kisaragi, is Wutai's leader and her primary foil. `[Final Fantasy Wiki (Fandom): Yuffie Kisaragi; Wikipedia: Yuffie Kisaragi]`
- **Cultural arc** — Opus VIII (2019) was printed before Final Fantasy VII Remake Intergrade (2021) elevated Yuffie to protagonist status in the INTERmission DLC, where she and partner Sonon Kusakabe infiltrate Midgar to steal Shinra's "ultimate materia" for Wutai. Square Enix has since canonized her as essential to the Remake saga — a notable shift from the original game, where she was nearly cut entirely. The Opus VIII card reflects her pre-Remake status as a beloved optional party member rather than a headlining lead. `[Kotaku: "Yuffie Mission Is A Must-Play"; Final Fantasy Wiki: Yuffie Kisaragi (VII Remake)]`
- **Card mechanic** — Yuffie (Hero) (no. 8-064H) is a 2-cost Wind Forward (5000 power), Ninja job, Category VII. With 3+ other Category VII Characters in play, she gains +2000 power and Haste. On attack, she deals 1000 damage to all opposing Forwards. FFTCG Crystarium rated her 1.5 in Opus VIII limited play: "1k just isn't enough damage to really do much with, especially when attached to a trigger so unwieldy as attacking" — a weak limited card despite the strong character IP. A Starter Set printing (no. 8-064S) also exists in the same set. `[FFTCG Crystarium: "How to Win Limited: Opus VIII Review"; TCGPlayer: Yuffie (Starter) Opus VIII listing]`
- **Hero rarity** — The Hero rarity in FFTCG sits above Rare but below Legend — typically a foil premium protagonist printing, often with alternate or enhanced art. Yuffie's market price of $1.73 is the highest among these six Opus VIII cards, consistent with Hero-rarity premium positioning even when the card's competitive ceiling is assessed as low. `[Card frontmatter: market_price; FFTCG rarity structure]`

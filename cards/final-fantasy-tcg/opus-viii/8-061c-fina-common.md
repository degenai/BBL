## Trivia

- **Dual-print design** — Fina received two cards in Opus VIII: the Legend rarity 8-060L (a 6cp 9,000 Power Forward with selectable on-entry effects) and this Common 8-061C (3cp, 7,000 Power). The dual-print structure is a standard FFTCG design tool for main characters of featured IPs, ensuring the character is accessible at both the chase-rare and budget tier within the same set. `[na.finalfantasy.com/news/1043; FFTCG Crystarium: Opus VIII Review]`
- **Mechanical design** — 8-061C is a 3-cost Wind element Forward (7,000 Power, White Mage — FFBE). On entering the field she activates 1 Wind Character you control; on leaving the field into the Break Zone she activates 1 Character you control. The dual-trigger (entry and exit) makes her unusually resource-efficient for a Common — rated 2.5/5 in the Opus VIII limited format review due to the format's thin Wind Character pool at the time, but her Break Zone trigger distinguishes her from most Commons. `[FFTCG Crystarium: How to Win Limited Opus VIII Review]`
- **Lore** — In FFBE, Fina is a girl who emerged from the Crystal of Earth with no memories beyond her own name, having been sealed within a crystal for hundreds of years. She grants Rain and Lasswell the power of Visions to save the world of Lapis from the Sworn Six of Paladia; she wields a bow called Reincarnation. Her apparent innocence masks hidden power that drives three seasons of narrative reveal. `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Fina (Brave Exvius)]`
- **Trio context** — Opus VIII deploys the full FFBE opening-arc party (Rain, Lasswell, Fina) simultaneously across multiple rarity slots — Rain at Rare, Lasswell at Hero, Fina at Legend and Common. Fina's dual-print reflects her role as the axis of the trio: she grants both party members their narrative power, and the FFTCG card mechanically mirrors this by activating other Characters on the field. `[na.finalfantasy.com/news/1043]`

## Connections

- [[ffbe-opening-arc-party]] — Fina is the metaphysical axis of Final Fantasy Brave Exvius's opening-arc playable trio (Rain, Lasswell, Fina), the amnesiac Crystal-of-Earth girl whose Visions-grantor role couples Rain's active-protagonist arc and Lasswell's deuteragonist arc into a single relational triangle. This Common-rarity printing is the budget-tier half of Fina's Opus VIII dual-print, mechanically mirroring her in-game role through a dual-trigger (entry + Break Zone exit) that activates other Wind Characters — a card-design echo of her narrative function as the one who hands her companions their power. The 8-060L Legend + 8-061C Common dual-print is FFTCG's standard move for featured-IP protagonists, ensuring Fina is accessible at both chase and budget tiers within the same designer-formalized FFBE-debut set.

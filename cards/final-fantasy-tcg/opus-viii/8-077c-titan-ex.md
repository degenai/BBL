## Trivia

- **Franchise history** — Titan is one of the oldest recurring summons in Final Fantasy, first appearing in Final Fantasy III (1990). In Final Fantasy IV, Titan's earthquake is triggered by Rydia's grief over the destruction of Mist Village — one of the franchise's earliest emotionally charged summon scenes. Titan has since recurred across FFIII, IV, V, VII, XI, XIV, XV, and XVI. `[Final Fantasy Wiki (Fandom): Titan (summon)]`
- **FFXI lore** — In Final Fantasy XI, Titan is the earth-element Avatar — one of eight "Sleeping Gods" tied to Vana'diel's elements and mapped as constellations. His origin myth: a giant who befriended a child named Daemdalus was threatened by her city; she defended him, both were called to the heavens by the goddess Altana, and the giant was transformed into the Celestial Avatar of Earth. Daemdalus is also the name of the brightest star in Titan's constellation. `[Final Fantasy Wiki (Fandom): Titan (Final Fantasy XI); FFXIclopedia: Titan]`
- **EX designation** — The "EX" in "Titan EX" refers to FFXI's harder-difficulty avatar encounter system. FFXI's avatar battles have a standard and an EX mode (the EX variant is a Burning Circle Notorious Monster fight). This naming carries into the FFTCG card, distinguishing it from non-EX Titan printings in other sets. `[FFXIclopedia: Prime Avatars Guide]`
- **Mechanical design** — Titan EX is a Common Earth-element Summon with an EX Burst: choose 1 Forward; it gains +1000 power for each Earth Backup you control. EX Bursts trigger instantly from the damage zone and bypass the normal stack — meaning in an Earth-Backup-heavy board, a revealed Titan EX mid-damage-step can swing combat math by +3000 or more before either player can respond. `[FFTCG Crystarium: "Rules Processing no. 6 — EX Bursts"; Square Enix FFTCG FAQ]`

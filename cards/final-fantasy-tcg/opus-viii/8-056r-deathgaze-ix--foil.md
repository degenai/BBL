## Trivia

- **Set / lore context — localization disambiguation** — In English releases of Final Fantasy IX, this creature is named "Deathguise" — the localization team renamed it to distinguish it from the FFVI enemy sharing the same Japanese name, "Deathgaze." FFTCG standardizes on the Japanese-canonical spelling "Deathgaze (IX)" and appends a game-suffix to disambiguate. Both FFVI Deathgaze and FFIX Deathguise share the same winged-monster-of-death visual lineage; the Final Fantasy Wiki explicitly calls the FFIX version "a tribute to Deathgaze from Final Fantasy VI." [Final Fantasy Wiki: Deathguise; Final Fantasy Wiki: Deathgaze — both via WebSearch snippets]

- **Set / lore context — FFIX placement** — Deathguise (Deathgaze IX) is one of the final boss-sequence enemies in FFIX, fought in the Crystal World during Disc 4 — the third-to-last boss before Trance Kuja and Necron, summoned by Kuja as a last defense. Stats: 55,535 HP. It opens with Meteor unless put to sleep on entry, and shifts between an open-wings physical mode and a closed-wings magical mode (including Level 5 Death). [WebSearch: Deathgaze FFIX Crystal World stats]

- **Mechanical reputation** — Deathgaze (IX) 8-056R is a 3-cost Wind Monster. On entering the field, it removes one opposing Forward from the game for as long as Deathgaze remains in play — a temporary exile. The FFTCG Crystarium Opus VIII limited review rated it 3.5/5: "stellar, efficient, unconditional removal" and "one of the few clean answers to Ardyn and Cloud in the set." Its presence also hard-counters Garland (IX)'s ability, which triggers only on Forwards — not Monsters. [FFTCG Crystarium: Opus VIII Limited Review]

- **Mechanical depth** — The exile-on-field mechanic creates blowout risk: if Deathgaze is removed by the opponent, the exiled Forward immediately returns. Crystarium flagged this: "be careful if you hit something with a strong ETF or a big body." The card also enables a recursion engine with Veriaulde (8-062H), a Yuke Forward that replays a Wind Monster from the Break Zone on death — Deathgaze leaving and re-entering repeatedly to re-exile new targets. [FFTCG Crystarium: Opus VIII Limited Review]

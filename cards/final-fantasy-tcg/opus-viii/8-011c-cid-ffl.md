## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — The 'FFL' category tag in FFTCG stands for Final Fantasy Dimensions (marketed as "Final Fantasy Legends" in Japan). Cid (FFL) is Cid from Final Fantasy Dimensions, a mobile RPG released globally in 2012. Opus VIII's official press release specifically named artist Akira Oguro (Final Fantasy Legends) as contributing exclusive original artwork to the set, placing this card among the set's intentional FFL-content block. `[Square Enix NA Press Hub: Opus VIII press release, 2019-03-22]`
- **Character lore** — In Final Fantasy Dimensions, Cid is the inventor of the world's first airship and director of the Avalonian imperial air force. He is one of the Emperor's most trusted subjects before undergoing a redemption arc and defecting to the protagonists. The stern, arms-crossed military bearing visible in the card art fits this role as a senior Imperial officer and engineer. `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Cid (Dimensions)]`
- **Design** — Cid is the only Final Fantasy franchise archetype to appear in every mainline numbered entry. The tradition began with Final Fantasy II (1988). The FFTCG uses parenthetical source-game tags — FFL, FFBE, FFVI, etc. — to distinguish different Cid incarnations across the card game because the character appears in so many properties. This card's 'Cid (FFL)' naming follows that disambiguation convention. `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Cid; Game Rant: Every Version of Cid in the Franchise]`
- **Mechanical** — Cid (FFL) 8-011C is a Common-rarity Fire-element Backup card in Opus VIII, functioning as a CP-generation and support piece rather than a combat forward. `[Troll and Toad: Cid (FFL) 8-011C product listing]`

## Trivia

- **Design** — Opus VIII's original-art program included Akira Oguro, who is credited with artwork for Final Fantasy Legends cards in the set. The Sophia art fits the FFTCG's chibi-proportioned sprite-art aesthetic used across Commons in sets that draw from mobile and handheld FF spin-offs. `[Square Enix: Opus VIII announcement, na.finalfantasy.com/news/1043]`
- **Lore** — The Devout is a recurring Final Fantasy job class — canonically an advanced upgrade to the White Mage, specializing in powerful healing and support white magic with minimal offensive capability. It appears in Final Fantasy III, V, and several spin-offs. In the FFTCG color system, the card's ice-element cost pip aligns with ice as the healing/support color, consistent with a high-tier healing job. `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Devout (job) (via WebSearch)]`
- **Source game qualification** — The FFTCG card frame identifies this character as Sophia from "Final Fantasy Legends" with job Devout. A character named Sophia also appears in Final Fantasy Dimensions as the Great Elder of Mysidia — a small, young-looking healer with white flowers and a Seer role who grants the Seer job to the party. The visual profile aligns closely with the FFTCG chibi art, but Final Fantasy Dimensions and Final Fantasy Legends are two distinct mobile titles in Square Enix's spin-off catalog. The exact source game for this FFTCG print cannot be confirmed from available English-language sources; the card frame text "Final Fantasy Legends" is treated as canonical. `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Sophia (Dimensions) (via WebSearch); FFTCG card frame per vision pass]`

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Opus VIII (released March 2019) introduced Final Fantasy XIV: Stormblood to the FFTCG for the first time, alongside a dedicated FFXIV Starter Set released concurrently. The Bard (8-029C) carries category "XIV," placing it within the Opus VIII FFXIV: Stormblood representation cluster. Two brand-new FFXIV Starter Sets (FFVII and FFXIV: Stormblood-themed) launched alongside the booster. [Square Enix FF Portal: Opus VIII announcement; na.finalfantasy.com/news/1043 via WebSearch]
- **Job class history** — Bard (Japanese: Gin'yūshijin, lit. Minstrel) is a recurring job class in the Final Fantasy series. The Bard first appeared as a playable job in Final Fantasy V (1992), part of the Fire Crystal job set, wielding the Sing skillset. In Final Fantasy XIV, Bard is a Physical Ranged DPS job upgraded from the Archer class at level 30; canonically described as tracing its origins to "the bowmen of eld, who sang in the heat of battle to fortify the spirits of their companions." The bow as the Bard's weapon in FFXIV is a design departure from the harp-as-signature-weapon of earlier entries. [Final Fantasy Wiki: Bard (job); FFXIV Job Guide: Bard]
- **Card profile** — 8-029C is an Ice Backup card, cost 2, Standard Unit, category XIV. Ability: "D, put Bard into the Break Zone: Choose 1 Forward. Freeze it." — a one-time tap-and-sacrifice effect that prevents one opponent's Forward from acting. [FFTCG Crystarium Opus VIII review — exact card text]
- **Limited-format assessment** — The FFTCG Crystarium's Opus VIII Limited review gave Bard a rating of 2 out of 5: "A pretty mediocre ability. If your opponent overextends into you while you have a decent board, you can stop them from activating something that would be a strong blocker, but if you're using this defensively, to prevent something from attacking you again, you're probably going to lose." Community sentiment: below-curve Ice Backup in draft; sees essentially no constructed play. [FFTCG Crystarium: How to Win Limited — Opus VIII Review]

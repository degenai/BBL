## Trivia

- **Set context** — Opus VIII (2019) features characters from Final Fantasy VII, IX, XIV: Stormblood, XV, and Brave Exvius. Square Enix described the set as expanding the game's "play experience and collectability," with FFXV and Brave Exvius characters appearing "in full force" after teases in Opus VII. FFIX characters in the set carry original artwork commissioned by Square Enix for FFTCG. `[Square Enix Opus VIII official page, na.finalfantasy.com/news/1043]`

- **Design** — Card 8-113C is a Water-element Forward with 2 CP cost, 5000 Power, and the Summoner job. Its EX BURST ability activates when revealed from the damage zone: choose 1 Forward and activate it; if a Forward you control is dealt damage by a Summon, reduce that damage by 5000. The "EX" suffix in the card title is a FFTCG designation for cards carrying an EX BURST — a triggered damage-zone mechanic unique to the game with no direct analog in MTG or Pokemon TCG. `[Collector's Cache FFTCG listing; Card Cavern FFTCG listing]`

- **IP context** — Garnet til Alexandros XVII, also called Dagger, is the princess of Alexandria and hereditary Summoner in Final Fantasy IX (1999, PlayStation). Her summoning abilities form a central plot thread of the game — she carries eidolons (summon beasts) sealed inside her, and is hunted for them. She is one of FFIX's two playable protagonists alongside Zidane Tribal. `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Garnet til Alexandros XVII, finalfantasy.fandom.com]`

- **Mechanical context** — In FFTCG, Summoner is a job type typically associated with control-support play — cards that reduce incoming summon damage or activate Forwards provide tempo advantages. Garnet's damage-reduction clause is specifically anti-Summon, reflecting her narrative role as a Summoner who absorbs eidolons. `[FFTCG card mechanics, via card text]`

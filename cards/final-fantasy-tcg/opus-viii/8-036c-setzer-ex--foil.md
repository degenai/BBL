## Trivia

- **Design history** — Setzer Gabbiani is the first playable Gambler job class in the mainline Final Fantasy series, introduced in Final Fantasy VI (1994). His combat style relies entirely on gambler's paraphernalia — playing cards, darts, and dice thrown at enemies — with some abilities having random outcomes determined by coin flip or slot mechanics. His visual signature, long silver-white hair and dark flowing coat, was deliberately designed to look 'different and unexpected' among the FFVI cast. [Final Fantasy Wiki: Setzer Gabbiani; Retroware 'The Origin of the Rogue Gambler Setzer Gabbiani,' via WebSearch snippets]

- **Lore context** — Setzer's central backstory involves Darill, owner of the airship Falcon. They were in a relationship and a friendly aerial rivalry; Darill pushed the Falcon to its limits to fly higher than anyone before, never returned, and her wreck was found a year later. Setzer attributes this loss to losing his drive to live, becoming the world-weary gambler the party encounters. In the World of Ruin he leads the party to Darill's Tomb, where the restored Falcon becomes the party's primary airship — grief converted into forward motion. [Final Fantasy Wiki: Setzer Gabbiani; Caves of Narshe FFVI character page, via WebSearch snippets]

- **Mechanical design** — Ice element Backup, 4 CP, Job: Gambler, Category: VI. EX Burst ability: when Setzer enters the field, choose 1 Category VI Forward in your Break Zone and add it to your hand. This is a recursion piece for FFVI-focused builds — thematically mirroring Setzer's in-game narrative role of recovering something lost (Darill's Falcon, the party's hope) from a place of grief. [TCGPlayer Setzer EX Opus VIII listing; WebSearch retailer data]

- **Print history and collector value** — Setzer has appeared in at least three FFTCG Opus sets: Opus IV, Opus VIII (this card), and Opus XI — indicating consistent demand for FFVI characters across the TCG's run. As a Common rarity, the foil variant of this card commands a market price of approximately $1.90 — significantly above comparable non-foil Opus VIII Commons in the same inventory corpus — suggesting collector demand for the foil treatment of this beloved FFVI character.

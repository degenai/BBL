## Trivia

- **Set context** — Moogle Knight (8-086C) is a Common card from FFTCG Opus VIII, released March 22, 2019 by Square Enix. Opus VIII featured characters and art from Final Fantasy Tactics Advance alongside FFXIV: Stormblood, FFVII, FFIX, and FF Brave Exvius; exclusive original artwork in the set was provided by Ryoma Ito (FFTA artist), Yasuhisa Izumisawa (FF Crystal Chronicles), and Toshitaka Matsuda (FFIX) `[Square Enix / na.finalfantasy.com news/1043, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Mechanical stats** — The card is an Earth-element Forward, Cost 3, Power 5000, job: Standard Unit. In FFTCG, Earth-element Forwards typically align with physicality, defense, and ground-bound power, consistent with the Mog Knight's stat profile in FFTA `[TCGPlayer / Collector's Cache retailer listings, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **IP clarification: job class, not a named character** — "Moogle Knight" (also spelled "Mog Knight" in Final Fantasy Tactics Advance) is a moogle-exclusive job class in the Final Fantasy Tactics series, not a named individual. The job is the highest-attack moogle class in FFTA, stat-comparable to the Paladin, and wields blades with signature abilities including Mog Rush, Mog Guard, Ultima Charge, and Charge Combo. The depicted figure on this card is a generic Mog Knight — no individual identity to verify or conflict with `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Mog Knight; StrategyWiki: FFTA/Mog_Knight, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Race-exclusive mechanic** — In Final Fantasy Tactics Advance, the Mog Knight job is locked to the Moogle race and requires only one mastered Animist ability to unlock, making it an accessible early-game power class for moogle units. This race-locked job structure is a defining feature of FFTA's job system, distinguishing it from the race-agnostic jobs in other FF titles `[GameFAQs FFTA Moogle Jobs Guide; StrategyWiki FFTA/Mog_Knight, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Moogle universality** — Moogles are one of the most consistent recurring races across the entire Final Fantasy series, appearing as playable characters, NPCs, and job-class wielders from FFIII through FFXVI. Within the FFTCG corpus, multiple Moogle cards appear across Opus sets; this card specifically anchors the Mog Knight iteration from the GBA-era Tactics branch `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Moogle, via WebSearch snippet]`

## Trivia

- **Source game** — Layle is the protagonist of Final Fantasy Crystal Chronicles: The Crystal Bearers (Wii, December 2009 North America). The game is set far in the future of the FFCC timeline: the Lilty Kingdom dominates through crystal-based technology, the Yuke race has nearly vanished, and "Crystal Bearers" — people born with a crystal shard in their body — are a feared, marginalized class. Layle openly wields his powers as a mercenary-for-hire, defying the typical Bearer practice of concealment. `[Final Fantasy Wiki (Fandom): Final Fantasy Crystal Chronicles: The Crystal Bearers; Wikipedia]`
- **Character powers** — Layle's crystal shard (embedded in his right cheek) grants gravity-based telekinetic powers — he can grab, lift, drag, and throw nearly any object or enemy in the environment, making physical manipulation of the battlefield his core combat style. The Crystal Bearers received mixed critical scores (Metacritic aggregate ~6-7/10), making Layle one of FFTCG's inclusions from a lower-profile, critically divisive entry in the broader Crystal Chronicles sub-franchise. `[Final Fantasy Wiki (Fandom): Layle; Metacritic: The Crystal Bearers]`
- **Card mechanic** — Layle is a 2-cost Earth Backup with the "Crystal Bearer" job subtype. Sacrifice ability: put Layle into the Break Zone to choose 1 Summon of cost 4 or less and cancel its effect. FFTCG Crystarium notes he is "particularly potent because of the wealth of good summons in this set, with cards like Alexander and Raiden at rare" — a strong pickup for Opus VIII limited despite his low retail value. `[FFTCG Crystarium: "How to Win Limited: Opus VIII Review"]`
- **Dual attribution** — The card frame lists "DFF / FFCC" as dual source tags, indicating the character or art draws from Dissidia Final Fantasy as well as the original Crystal Chronicles game — a FFTCG pattern for characters who crossed into the Dissidia roster. The ghosted close-up portrait behind the full-body action pose is a standard FFTCG Backup art layout. `[Card frame: Layle 8-088C]`

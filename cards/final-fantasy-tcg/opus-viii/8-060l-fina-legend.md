## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — Fina is the White Mage protagonist of Final Fantasy Brave Exvius (2016), a mobile JRPG developed by gumi and Square Enix. She emerges from an earth crystal with no memory save her name, grants protagonists Rain and Lasswell the power of visions, and is the party's primary healer and magical support. Her origins are tied to the Sworn Six of Paladia — the game's primary antagonist faction. [Final Fantasy Wiki: Fina; WebSearch: Fina FFBE character lore]

- **Design** — The "(Legend)" in the card name denotes the FFTCG rarity tier (Legend = highest rarity), not a character variant or alternate version. The art is by Yoshitaka Amano — the franchise's original character designer — consistent with Amano's illustrated contributions to FFBE documented in the Final Fantasy Brave Exvius The Art Works publications. Amano's signature loose ink-and-watercolor style appears across multiple FFTCG Legend-rarity cards. [eBay: FFBE The Art Works II Yoshitaka Amano; Vision pass — Amano style identified]

- **Mechanical reputation** — Fina (8-060L) is a 6-cost Wind Forward, White Mage job, 9000 power. Paying three additional Wind CP at cast time unlocks a second action: normally she selects one of two modes (deal 5000 damage to all opposing Forwards, or activate all your Characters); with the extra cost she may do both. The FFTCG Crystarium Opus VIII limited review rated her 3.5/5, noting the activation ability creates "huge turns" by reactivating four backups — a significant CP recovery on a 9000-power body. [FFTCG Crystarium: Opus VIII Limited Review]

- **Set context — FFBE cohort** — Opus VIII (2019) featured a large FFBE card cohort reflecting the mobile game's global peak: Dark Fina (8-042L, Sage of Hess, different character variant), Lasswell (8-043H, Knight), and two Fina variants — this Legend (8-060L) and a 3-cost Common (8-061C). Lasswell was rated the set's best card by the Crystarium review (4.5/5), making the FFBE package the dominant constructed-power cohort of the set. [FFTCG Crystarium: Opus VIII Limited Review]

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Red Mage (8-002C) was released in the Opus VIII Collection (March 22, 2019). Opus VIII introduced Final Fantasy XIV: Stormblood as a featured FFTCG category for the first time, and a dedicated FFXIV Stormblood Starter Set launched alongside it — described by Square Enix as an aggressive deck with heavy emphasis on the Red Mage and Samurai jobs. This card is among the first Red Mage representations in FFTCG tied explicitly to the XIV Stormblood expansion. [Square Enix NA press release; na.finalfantasy.com/news/1043, via WebSearch snippet]

- **Design history** — The Red Mage is one of the three founding job archetypes introduced in the original Final Fantasy (1987) alongside White Mage and Black Mage, making it one of the oldest continuous class identities in gaming. Its signature silhouette — wide-brimmed red hat with white feather plume, layered red and black coat — has been reproduced with near-identical iconography across 35+ years of FF titles. FFXIV's Stormblood expansion (2017) gave the job its first mainline playable iteration since Final Fantasy XI. [Final Fantasy Wiki: Red Mage, via WebSearch snippet]

- **FFXIV lore context** — In FFXIV canon, the Red Mage originated among refugees of the rival sorcerous cities of Mhach (black magic) and Amdapor (white magic) who buried their enmity and merged their disciplines. Count Ganelon, a noble swordsman, taught them the rapier. The resulting job is literally a synthesis of opposites — black and white fused through a blade — which maps onto the classical jack-of-all-trades design the job has held since FF1. [FFXIV Job Guide: Red Mage; na.finalfantasyxiv.com/jobguide/redmage/, via WebSearch snippet]

- **Mechanical design** — Fire element Backup, 2 CP, Job: Standard Unit, Category: XIV. On-entry ability: choose 1 Fire Forward — until end of turn it gains Haste and Brave. Haste removes summoning sickness (can attack immediately); Brave allows attacking without tapping. At 2 CP this is a low-cost aggressive tempo piece that rewards Fire-heavy Opus VIII builds. [Mountbatten Collectables listing 8-002C; FFTCG Crystarium Opus VIII review, via WebSearch snippets]

## Trivia

- **Set marketing** — The Square Enix Opus VIII press release (March 22, 2019) explicitly names Lasswell as a character "initially teased in the Opus VII set" and "brought to the forefront" in Opus VIII with "multiple new characters from each game." Lasswell and Noctis (FFXV) were the co-headliners of Opus VIII's Brave Exvius / FFXV push — meaning Lasswell is a marketing-tier character for this set, not a background inclusion. `[Square Enix press release: March 22, 2019]`
- **Character lore** — Lasswell is the deuteragonist of Final Fantasy Brave Exvius (developed by A-Lim, published Square Enix; JP: October 2015, global: June 2016). He is Rain's childhood friend and adopted brother, raised by Rain's father Sir Raegen after Lasswell's own parents were killed in his infancy. He wields the sword "Purple Lightning," a memento from Sir Raegen that appears in Brave Exvius's logo. Lasswell is the focus character of Season Two of the game's story. `[Final Fantasy Wiki (Fandom): Lasswell; Wikipedia: Final Fantasy Brave Exvius]`
- **Card mechanic** — Lasswell (no. 8-043H) is a 4-cost Ice Forward (8000 power), Knight job, Category BE. Entry or attack trigger: choose 1 Forward; if it received damage this turn, dull it and Freeze it — effectively locking it out of combat for two turns. Azure Sky S: choose 2 Forwards, deal 8000 damage to one and dull + Freeze the other. FFTCG Crystarium rated him 4.5 in Opus VIII limited and called him "Must Kill," noting "with reliable access to fire CP, Lasswell deletes the opponent's strongest blocker for two turns." `[FFTCG Crystarium: "How to Win Limited: Opus VIII Review"]`
- **Source game context** — Final Fantasy Brave Exvius received mixed critical scores (Metacritic: "mixed or average") but was commercially successful (5 million JP downloads by January 2016). The global version shut down in December 2022, making Lasswell a character from a completed-and-closed mobile spinoff. His prominent placement in Opus VIII reflects the commercial weight Brave Exvius had at the time of Opus VIII's 2019 release. `[Wikipedia: Final Fantasy Brave Exvius; Metacritic]`

## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — The Scholar job class in FFXIV traces its in-universe origins to the ancient city-state of Nym on the island of Vylbrand. Nymian mages bound faerie companions to share healing duties — allowing scholars to focus on strategy and tactics. Both the city-state and the art were lost to the Sixth Umbral Calamity. In a further lore tragedy, the Nymians fell ill with "the sickness from the sea," their bodies rotting until they became the creatures now called Tonberries. `[FFXIV Community Wiki: Scholar; "FFXIV ARR: The Tragedy of Nym"]`
- **Design** — Scholar 8-027C was revealed before the set's official release via an Italian FFTCG community retailer (Gold Saucer / GamesSource.it) during the Opus VIII spoiler cycle — one of several commons that leaked through regional prerelease events rather than the official Card of the Week slot. `[FFTCG boards.net: Opus VIII Card Spoilers Megathread]`
- **Character lore** — In FFXIV, Scholar is a healing job unlocked by Arcanists at level 30 via job stone. Scholars conjure faeries — Eos (sunlight/restoration) or Selene (moonlight/invigoration) — to handle remote healing while the Scholar themselves deploys Aetherflow shields and burst-heal abilities. The faerie-summoning distinguishes Scholar from White Mage and Astrologian as the "strategic shielder" of the healer trio. The card's small-statured pointed-ear figure with academic hat and open book is consistent with the Lalafell player race, which is culturally associated with magic-focused jobs in FFXIV. `[FFXIV Job Guide: Scholar; FFXIV Community Wiki: Scholar]`

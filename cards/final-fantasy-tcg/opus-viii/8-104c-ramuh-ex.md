## Trivia

- **Character history** — Ramuh is one of the longest-running recurring summons in Final Fantasy, first appearing in Final Fantasy III (1990). He has appeared in FFIII, IV, V, VI, VII, IX, XI, XIV, and XV — consistently depicted as an elderly man with a long beard calling down Judgment Bolt. [Final Fantasy Wiki: Ramuh, via WebSearch snippet; finalfantasywiki.com: Ramuh, via WebSearch snippet]
- **Mythology** — Ramuh's design draws from multiple mythological sources: the lightning connection mirrors Indra, the Hindu king of the Devas and god of storms; his elder judge aspect evokes the legendary priest-king Ra-mu of the mythical sunken continent of Mu; and his appearance has been compared to the Mesopotamian deity Lahmu. [Goomba Stomp Magazine: "Not So Final Fantasy — Summons and Mythology", via WebSearch snippet; Final Fantasy Wiki: Ramuh, via WebSearch snippet]
- **Narrative roles** — Ramuh plays story-significant roles beyond combat in multiple entries: in FFVI he appears to Terra and reveals her Esper heritage; in FFIX he is the first Eidolon to voluntarily aid Garnet after her summoning powers are stolen. This makes him unusual among recurring summons — he recurs as a character, not merely as a spell. [Final Fantasy Wiki: Ramuh — narrative appearances, via WebSearch snippet]
- **Mechanical (FFTCG)** — Ramuh EX (8-104C, Common) is a 4-CP Lightning Summon with EX BURST: deal 5000 damage to one active Forward, +1000 per Lightning Backup you control. The EX BURST mechanic triggers automatically when this card is revealed from the top of the deck during damage resolution — making it a defensive surprise board tool rather than a proactive play. [WebSearch snippet: FFTCG Ramuh EX card effect; FFTCG Crystarium Opus VIII review, via WebSearch snippet]

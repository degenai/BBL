## Trivia

- **IP clarification** — "Final Fantasy Legends" (FFL) is the Japanese-market alternate title for Final Fantasy Dimensions, a 2012 mobile RPG published by Square Enix. Graham is not from Final Fantasy Brave Exvius; he is from Final Fantasy Dimensions. The FFTCG card category abbreviation "FFL" on Opus VIII cards refers to Final Fantasy Dimensions / Final Fantasy Legends, not to any of the "Legends" mobile-adjacent game sub-brands. `[finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Final_Fantasy_Dimensions; WebSearch: Graham Final Fantasy Dimensions; WebSearch: Opus VIII FFL abbreviation]`
- **Lore** — Graham is a mysterious Dark Knight encountered by the Warriors of Darkness in Chapter 2 of Final Fantasy Dimensions. He inhabits the heavy plate armor of his fallen comrade Sigurd — who was slain by the fiend Baugauven — concealing that Sigurd is dead and that Graham himself was mortally wounded in the same battle, surviving as a spirit inhabiting the armor. After granting the Warriors of Darkness the Dark Knight job class, Graham abandons the armor and transforms into a cursed sword (Graham's Sword) to continue accompanying Nacht, Sigurd's son. His entire arc is defined by loyalty to a dead friend and quiet sacrifice. `[finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Graham; WebSearch: Graham Final Fantasy Dimensions Dark Knight chapter 2]`
- **Set context** — Opus VIII (March 22, 2019) listed "FINAL FANTASY LEGENDS" as one of its featured game sources. The set's Dimensions / Legends representation is small relative to FFBE and FFXV, making Graham one of the rarer FFL-origin cards in the set. The Opus VIII Square Enix press page confirms FFL as an explicit source alongside Final Fantasy IX, XIV, and VII. `[fftcg.square-enix-games.com/na/release/opus-viii]`
- **Mechanical design** — Graham is a 5-cost Earth element Forward (9,000 Power, Dark Knight — FFL). His sole ability: when he is put from the field into the Break Zone, choose 1 Forward the opponent controls; if you have received 4 or more points of damage, break it. The design encodes his lore arc — Graham's value is realized at the moment of his removal, the same narrative beat where his sacrifice grants the Warriors of Darkness the Dark Knight power. A 9,000-Power body at cost 5 is above the Common rate curve for Opus VIII; the death-trigger removal adds a layer of resilience to the Forward slot. `[WebSearch: FFTCG Graham 8-075C card text; WebSearch: Graham 8-075C cost power element]`

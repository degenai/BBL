## Trivia

- **Set context** — Luminous Puma originates from Final Fantasy XIII-2 (2012), where it appears as an enemy in Augusta Tower (200 AF) — a future-timeline location overrun by rogue artificial intelligence. It belongs to the tameable Monster class; players who defeated it could recruit it into their Paradigm Pack. [Final Fantasy Wiki: Luminous Puma, via WebSearch snippet; gamercorner.net FFXIII-2 bestiary, via WebSearch snippet]
- **Mechanical (FFTCG)** — As a Common Backup in Opus VIII (8-080C), the card trades itself to boost Forward power: either +2000 to all Forwards, or +5000 to one, at the cost of Earth and Light CP. A low-cost utility sacrifice piece for Earth/Light hybrid builds rather than a standalone threat. [WebSearch snippet: FFTCG Luminous Puma card effect]
- **Lore context** — Augusta Tower's 200 AF zone reflects FFXIII-2's far-future dystopia, where PSICOM-derived AI and proto-fal'Cie have overtaken human infrastructure. The Luminous Puma and its puma-family variants (including Nightblade Puma) carry the setting's cyberpunk-armored aesthetic — mechanical plating over organic beast forms — that distinguishes late-game FFXIII-2 zones from the first game's more naturalistic designs. [Final Fantasy Wiki: Augusta Tower (FFXIII-2), via WebSearch snippet]

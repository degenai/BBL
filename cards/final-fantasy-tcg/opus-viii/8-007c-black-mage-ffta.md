## Trivia

- **Set context** — Opus VIII (released February 2019) features FFXV and FF Brave Exvius as its headline titles, but also introduces FFXIV: Stormblood and several Ivalice-verse games. The set is notable for commissioning exclusive original artwork from four Square Enix artists tied to specific FF titles: Ryoma Ito (FFTA/FFTA2), Akira Oguro (FF Legends/Dimensions), Yasuhisa Izumisawa (FF Crystal Chronicles), and Toshitaka Matsuda (FFIX). `[Square Enix FFTCG Illustration Showcase Interview no. 2, Jan. 2019; Square Enix Opus VIII release page]`
- **Artist context** — The art is almost certainly by Ryoma Ito, who served as the original character designer for both *Final Fantasy Tactics Advance* (2003, GBA) and *Final Fantasy Tactics A2: Grimoire of the Rift* (2007, DS), and who created nine exclusive illustrations for Opus VIII specifically from those titles. In a Square Enix interview about his FFTCG work, Ito described his approach: "I decided on a consistent pop-style background of various colors. I also thought it would be fun to show a side of the characters not seen in the game, to depict a candid moment in their daily lives." `[Square Enix FFTCG Illustration Showcase Interview no. 2, Jan. 2019]`
- **Job class design** — In Final Fantasy Tactics Advance, the Black Mage job is available to three races: Humes, Moogles, and Nu Mou. The Nu Mou version is considered mechanically superior due to higher Magic Attack and MP growth. Unlike traditional Black Mages elsewhere in the series (who have shadowed, faceless visages), only Hume Black Mages in FFTA have the darkened-face, glowing-yellow-eyes look — Nu Mou and Moogle Black Mages fully display their faces. This card's art shows the faceless, shadowed presentation, indicating a Hume Black Mage. `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Black Mage (Tactics Advance); StrategyWiki: FFTA Jobs]`
- **Mechanical role** — As a Fire-element Backup card in FFTCG, 8-007C costs 2 CP and has an action ability: dull and place Black Mage into the Break Zone to retrieve a Fire Summon from your Break Zone back to hand. This creates a recursive loop with Fire Summons, incentivizing graveyard-conscious Fire builds. Common rarity makes it an accessible engine piece. `[WebSearch snippet: finalfantasytcg.over-blog.com card entry]`

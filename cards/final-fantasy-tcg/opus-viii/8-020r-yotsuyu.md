## Trivia

- **Set context** — Opus VIII (released March 22, 2019) was the first FFTCG set to introduce Final Fantasy XIV: Stormblood characters to the card game. The 148-card set (60 Common / 36 Rare / 26 Hero / 14 Legend) also features characters from FFVII, FFIX, FFXV, and Brave Exvius. A dedicated FFXIV Starter Deck 2019, released alongside Opus VIII on March 15, included Stormblood characters. `[Square Enix North America Press Hub: "LATEST FINAL FANTASY TRADING CARD GAME 'OPUS VIII' SET NOW AVAILABLE"]` `[ICv2: Final Fantasy TCG Opus VIII Collection]`

- **IP verified: Yotsuyu goe Brutus (FFXIV Stormblood)** — Yotsuyu is the tertiary antagonist of FFXIV: Stormblood (June 2017) and the primary antagonist of post-Stormblood patches 4.1-4.3. She was appointed Acting Imperial Viceroy of Doma by Crown Prince Zenos yae Galvus after the Garlean Empire destroyed the Doman resistance and killed Lord Kaien. The card's job field reads "Acting Imperial Viceroy" — her exact canonical title. Her black kimono, red obi, and lacquered pipe are her signature visual identifiers in-game; the blue flower ornament at her temple also appears in her in-game model. `[Square Enix FFXIV Stormblood story page]` `[Gamer Escape: Yotsuyu/Appearance]` `[TCGplayer product listing: 8-020R job field]`

- **Mechanical design** — Yotsuyu 8-020R is a Fire-element Backup card, cost 2. Ability: `{Fire}{Dull}: Choose 1 Forward of cost 5 or less. It cannot block this turn.` This is a tempo-control effect — neutralizing a blocker without destroying it — which mirrors Yotsuyu's political style in Stormblood: she outmaneuvers opposition rather than confronting it directly. `[TCGplayer: Yotsuyu 8-020R product page]` `[WebSearch snippet: Legendary Wolf Games listing]`

- **Community sentiment** — Yotsuyu's writing is among the more debated in Stormblood. Some critics consider her the expansion's best-written character, particularly praising the post-Stormblood amnesia arc: a rare case where the amnesia trope generates genuine moral complexity rather than cheap sympathy. Others — including Anime Feminist's analysis of female antagonists in Stormblood — argue that the arc infantilizes a character who committed documented atrocities, framing her perpetually as victim and making the narrative want forgiveness the story hasn't earned. Community sentiment label: unresolved, actively contested. `[Twinfinite: "FFXIV's Latest Patch Cements Yotsuyu's Status as Best-Written Character in Stormblood"]` `[Anime Feminist: "Redemption and Retribution: Female Antagonists in FFXIV Stormblood"]`

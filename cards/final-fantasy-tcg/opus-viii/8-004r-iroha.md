## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — Iroha is the central character of Final Fantasy XI: Rhapsodies of Vana'diel (2015), the narrative finale of FFXI's first fourteen years. A Hume, daughter of Tenzen and Kagero, she serves as chief priestess of the Reisen Shrine on Reisenjima — a Far East island — and travels back in time from a doomed future to warn Vana'diel of approaching annihilation. [Gamer Escape FFXI wiki: Iroha; WebSearch: Iroha Rhapsodies of Vanadiel]

- **Design** — The FFTCG card type line reads "MOBIUS XI," indicating Iroha's card data originates from the Mobius Final Fantasy crossover version of FFXI content rather than a direct FFXI source pass. Her job is Samurai; she wields a naginata in both FFXI canon and the card art. [FFTCG Crystarium: Opus VIII Limited Review]

- **Mechanical reputation** — Iroha (8-004R) is a 3-cost, 7000-power Fire Forward. Her passive adds 1000 to all damage Fire characters deal to opposing Forwards; her dull ability deals 1000 to a chosen Forward. The FFTCG Crystarium Opus VIII limited review rated her 3.5/5, calling her "absolutely nuts" and citing her as "a huge draw to fire when drafting/deckbuilding" — the rare common-rated card that makes an entire element stronger. [FFTCG Crystarium: Opus VIII Limited Review]

- **Cross-title resonance** — Iroha has appeared in Final Fantasy XIV as part of "The Maiden's Rhapsody" collaboration event, first in Patch 3.1 (Heavensward, 2015), then repeated in 2017, 2020, 2022, and 2024 — one of the longest-running crossover events in FFXIV history. In XIV she appears as a Hyur Midlander NPC asking players for directions to Selbina. [FFXIV consolegameswiki.com: Iroha]

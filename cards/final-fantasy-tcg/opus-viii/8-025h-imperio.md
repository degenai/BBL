## Trivia

- **Character lore** — Imperio is the first of the four Divine Generals serving Emperor Baugauven in *Final Fantasy Dimensions* (mobile, 2010; known in English as *Final Fantasy Legends: Warriors of Light and Darkness*). He functions as the Emperor's right hand and the unofficial leader of the other generals. Described as "dignified and refined, with a slight smile always gracing his lips," he presents as a polished ally before revealing his true nature. `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Imperio; TV Tropes: Final Fantasy Dimensions characters]`
- **Twist villain** — Imperio's corruption runs deeper than it appears: after his defeat, his dark alter ego **Gehenna** — described as "the true reflection of Imperio of the Light" — takes over and must be fought as a separate boss battle. Both are ultimately destroyed by the Warriors of Light and Darkness inside Castle Avalon. His name is Spanish for "empire," a direct thematic marker of his allegiance. `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Gehenna (Dimensions); WebSearch snippet: finalfantasy.fandom.com/wiki/Imperio]`
- **Artist context** — Opus VIII's set notes confirm exclusive original artwork by **Akira Oguro** for *Final Fantasy Legends* (Dimensions) cards in this set. Oguro is the original character designer for the Dimensions cast, making this card's art a rare instance of the character's creator illustrating them for the trading card game. `[Square Enix Opus VIII release page; Square Enix press release via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Rarity note** — Hero rarity (H) in FFTCG sits between Rare and Legendary, with limited print quantities per pack. For a character as narratively prominent as Imperio — the arch-antagonist of Dimensions — the Hero designation signals that Square Enix treated him as a marquee card for the Dimensions portion of Opus VIII. `[FFTCG card rarity structure, general knowledge]`

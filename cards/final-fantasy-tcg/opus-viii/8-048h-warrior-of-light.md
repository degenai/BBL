## Trivia

- **Design** — The Warrior of Light's design is based on Yoshitaka Amano's concept art of the Knight class from the original Final Fantasy (1987), adapted by Tetsuya Nomura for Dissidia Final Fantasy (PSP, 2008). Unlike Onion Knight and Bartz, whose designs fuse their sprites with Amano art, the Warrior of Light is fully built from Amano's knight vision — making him the most direct Amano-to-Nomura translation in the Dissidia roster. `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Warrior of Light (Dissidia)]`
- **Lore** — Despite representing Final Fantasy I in Dissidia, the Warrior of Light is an original character created for Dissidia — not a direct carry-over from FF1's nameless Light Warriors. Per Dissidia lore, he was created by Cid of the Lufaine during the war between Cosmos and Chaos, infused with sentience from Cid's own memories. After Dissidia's events, he travels to the world of Final Fantasy I and becomes one of that game's canonical Warriors of Light — a retroactive link bridging Dissidia back to FF1's origin myth. `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Warrior of Light (Dissidia)]`
- **Mechanical** — The 8-048H Hero card has Wind element, 8000 Power, with two abilities: (1) when a Job Standard Unit Forward enters your field, activate up to 2 Backups; (2) when Warrior of Light attacks, all Job Standard Unit Forwards gain +2000 power until end of turn. The "Standard Unit" job synergy is a deliberate design choice: it makes Warrior of Light a support-multiplier for generic warriors rather than named-character combos, mirroring his lore status as the archetypal, nameless warrior representing the original FF1 Light Warrior template. `[Mountbatten Collectables: 8-048H product listing; Big Orbit Cards: 8-048H listing (via WebSearch)]`

## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — The "(FFL)" designation refers to the Final Fantasy Legend series — the SaGa trilogy originally developed for Game Boy (1989–1993) and rebranded "Final Fantasy Legend" for Western release. The Opus VIII FFL card cohort includes Imperio (Forward, Divine General), The Mask (Backup, "???"), Sophia (Backup, Devout), Cocytus (Backup, Divine General), and Graham/Jinnai as Forwards — a cast consistent with Final Fantasy Legend II (SaGa 2: Hihou Densetsu), though the FFTCG card database uses "FFL" for the whole trilogy without sub-game disambiguation. [FFTCG Crystarium: Opus VIII Limited Review; WebSearch: Final Fantasy Legend SaGa trilogy]

- **IP qualification** — This Cid is definitively a named FFL-series character, not the franchise's recurring-name archetype confusion. Which specific FFL game (I, II, or III) he originates from was not confirmable from retrieved sources. The Helmsman job and sea-captain visual profile are plausible for FFL II's cast but remain unconfirmed. [WebSearch: Cid FFL FFTCG specific game; FFTCG Crystarium review]

- **Mechanical reputation** — Cid (FFL) 8-011C is a 3-cost Fire Backup. On enter-the-field, he deals 5000 damage to a Forward — escalating to 8000 if five or more Fire Backups are in play. The FFTCG Crystarium Opus VIII review rated him 2/5 in limited, noting that "hitting for 8 simply isn't going to happen in all but the most stacked draft decks." Synergizes with Iroha, Sabin, and Rain in fire-burn builds. [FFTCG Crystarium: Opus VIII Limited Review]

- **Design / community note** — The Crystarium review noted in passing: "Shocking though that we keep getting Cids from all sorts of side games but still no VIII, IX, or X." As of Opus VIII (2019), FFTCG had produced Cid cards from niche and spinoff titles but had not yet given mainline Cids from FFVIII, FFIX, or FFX a card — a gap that community voices flagged as a notable design absence. [FFTCG Crystarium: Opus VIII Limited Review]

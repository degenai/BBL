## Trivia

- **Set context** — Opus VIII (released March 2019) is a multi-title FFTCG set drawing from across the Final Fantasy franchise — FFVII, FFIX, FFXI, FFXIV: Stormblood, FFXV, and others — rather than a single-game focus [Square Enix FFTCG Opus VIII product page, via WebSearch snippet].
- **Character lore** — Waltrill originates from Final Fantasy Crystal Chronicles: Echoes of Time (Nintendo DS/Wii, 2009). He is a Yuke — one of the four playable races of the Crystal Chronicles subseries, distinguished by armored or helmet-masked bodies and magical aptitude. In Echoes of Time, he serves as a mage-scholar who found the young Yuke Norschtalen lost in the forest and raised her as his apprentice in the village. Despite his stoic demeanor, he genuinely cares for her and intends to teach her advanced magic when she is older [Final Fantasy Wiki: FFCC Echoes of Time characters, via WebSearch snippet].
- **Design** — Waltrill is classified as a Backup-type card in the FFTCG system. Backup cards support the player's resource engine rather than fighting as Forwards — a mechanical fit for a noncombatant scholar whose lore role is mentorship and knowledge [card frontmatter: tags_filter backup-card; Scryfall analogue confirmed via card type].
- **Art style** — The chibi rendering reflects the deliberate visual language of the Crystal Chronicles subseries, which consistently used a rounder, more cartoonish aesthetic than mainline Final Fantasy titles to differentiate the multiplayer-focused GameCube/DS/Wii spin-off line [Final Fantasy Wiki: Yuke race entry, via WebSearch snippet].

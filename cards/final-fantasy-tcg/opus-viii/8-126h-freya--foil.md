## Trivia

- **IP verified: Freya Crescent (Final Fantasy IX)** — Freya Crescent is a Burmecian Dragoon Knight and main party member in Final Fantasy IX (2000). She holds the distinction of being the first-ever playable female Dragoon character in the Final Fantasy franchise with a fixed class — not a player-assigned job, but a role intrinsic to her design. Her name draws from Freyja, the Norse goddess of love, war, beauty, and death — fitting a character whose arc is defined by both martial valor and grief over lost love. `[Wikipedia: Freya Crescent]` `[Game Rant: "10 Things Most Players Don't Know About Freya Crescent"]`

- **Art context: Amano concept, not final design** — Freya's in-game model was finalized by Toshiyuki Itahana, but her original concept was designed by Yoshitaka Amano. In Amano's FFIX concept art, Freya is depicted alongside a small jester-like companion figure wearing a pointed hat — the exact composition reproduced on this card. The vision pass describes "a small jester-like companion figure with a pointed hat floats to the upper left" — matching Amano's documented concept illustration rather than Itahana's final in-game design. The loose watercolor and ink style is consistent with Amano's known illustration approach. Opus VIII's credited original FFIX illustrator is Toshitaka Matsuda; the specific artist credit for 8-126H could not be confirmed from available sources — verify against the physical card. `[Wikipedia: Freya Crescent]` `[Game Rant: Amano concept art description]` `[WebSearch: Zerochan Amano Freya concept image]` `[Square Enix North America Press Hub: Opus VIII artist credits]`

- **Freya's backstory** — Five years before Final Fantasy IX's events, Freya left Burmecia to search for her lost love Sir Fratley, who had departed to hone his skills and never returned. When she finds him during the game, he has lost all memory of her. The cities of Burmecia and Cleyra — home of the Burmecians — are both destroyed by Queen Brahne during the first act. Freya is frequently described as FFIX's most emotionally resonant character despite receiving inadequate story development after that opening act. `[Wikipedia: Freya Crescent]` `[Final Fantasy Wiki: Freya Crescent, via WebSearch]`

- **Mechanical design** — Freya 8-126H is a Hero-rarity card. Ability: *If you control a Job Dragoon or Card Name Dragoon other than Freya, Freya gains Haste. When Freya enters the field or attacks, choose 1 Forward opponent controls. It loses 2000 power until the end of the turn.* The Dragoon-synergy condition reflects Freya's party dynamics in FFIX — she fights best alongside other dragoons.

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Marche EX (8-018R) was released in Opus VIII (March 22, 2019), the FFTCG set that brought Final Fantasy Tactics Advance characters into the game for the first time. Original artwork for Opus VIII was contributed by Ryoma Ito, who is credited as one of four guest artists for the set — Ito is the artist associated with FFTA art in the Opus VIII promotional material. A full-art promo version (PR-065/8-018R) was produced for February 2020 tournament events, marking Marche as a marquee character for the set's tournament promotional cycle. [Square Enix NA press release; arcadegamecards.com promo listing, via WebSearch snippets]

- **Lore context** — Marche Radiuju is the protagonist of Final Fantasy Tactics Advance (GBA, 2003). After a mysterious book transforms his new hometown into the magical land of Ivalice, Marche is the only member of his group who insists on returning to the real world — his friends' dreams are being realized in Ivalice (a sick brother walks, a grieving family is reunited), but Marche forms Clan Nutsy and fights to destroy the five crystals sustaining the illusion. [Final Fantasy Wiki: Marche Radiuju; Final Fantasy Wiki: Final Fantasy Tactics Advance, via WebSearch snippets]

- **Community sentiment** — The 'Is Marche the villain?' debate is one of the most enduring discourse threads in the FFTA community, relitigated in GameFAQs threads as recently as 2023. Detractors argue he destroys a world full of realized dreams without his friends' consent; defenders argue Ivalice was an infantilizing crutch preventing real growth. The debate remains unresolved and functions as a permanent community meme, with dedicated ResetEra and SpaceBattles threads, Game Rant feature articles, and a persistent GameFAQs board. [GameFAQs FFTA board 2023; ResetEra 'In Defense of Marche'; Game Rant 'Marche vs Mewt' — community sentiment, not editorial fact; via WebSearch snippets]

- **Mechanical design** — Fire element Forward, 4 CP, 5000 Power, Job: Clan Leader, Category: FFTA. EX Burst ability: when Marche enters the field, reveal the top 5 cards of your deck, add 1 Fire Character among them to your hand, return the rest to the bottom in any order. The EX Burst triggers on both normal play and when revealed from damage, making Marche a resilient dig piece in Fire builds. [WebSearch retailer listings; Crystal Tower Podcast 'Opus 8 First Impressions,' via WebSearch snippet]

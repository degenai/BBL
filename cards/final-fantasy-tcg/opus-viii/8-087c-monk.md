## Trivia

- **Generic job-class design** — Monk is a recurring generic job-class Backup in FFTCG, not a named character card. It has appeared across at least Opus I (no. 1-120C), Opus II (no. 2-089C), and Opus VIII (no. 8-087C). FFTCG uses these repeating job-class Backups as low-cost enablers separate from named-character Forward cards — the Monk archetype recurs across sets as a design pattern. `[FFTCG Mognet: fftcgmognet.com/card/1-120C and 2-089C]`
- **FFXIV Monk lore** — In Final Fantasy XIV, the Monk (MNK) is a Disciple of War melee DPS job evolved from the Pugilist class. The job's lore ties to two martial traditions: the Fist of Rhalgr, an ancient order of ascetic warrior-monks from Ala Mhigo who worshipped Rhalgr the Destroyer, and the Ul'dah school of pugilism (home of the Pugilists' Guild). Opus VIII's FFXIV cards were Stormblood-focused — and Ala Mhigo, the Monk's ancestral homeland, is the central liberation storyline of Stormblood, making the Monk a thematically appropriate inclusion. `[Final Fantasy Wiki (Fandom): Monk (Final Fantasy XIV); FFXIV Consolegameswiki: Monk]`
- **Mechanical precedent** — The Opus II Monk Backup (no. 2-089C) has the sacrifice ability: put Monk into the Break Zone to give 1 Forward +3000 power and Brave until end of turn. "Brave" in FFTCG means the Forward can attack without dulling — a significant keyword on a low-cost Backup. The Opus VIII version's specific effect text was not retrieved; the Opus II precedent represents the established Monk Backup design. `[FFTCG Mognet: fftcgmognet.com/card/2-089C]`

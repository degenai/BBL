# Vegeta

The canonical Prince of All Saiyans, son of King Vegeta, introduced in *Dragon Ball Z*'s Saiyan Saga (Akira Toriyama, Shueisha 1989) as Frieza's elite warrior antagonist alongside Nappa, and continuously instantiated across thirty-five years of Dragon Ball franchise output as the canonical rival-and-eventual-ally to [[son-goku]]. The franchise's most-developed redemption-arc character; the canonical wielder of the Galick Gun and the Final Flash; the father of [[trunks]] (canonical wife Bulma); the canonical Saiyan-pride-as-character-thesis figure whose refusal to yield against any power-gap defines his operating mode across every named arc.

Vegeta canonically operates across the franchise as the *Saiyan-pride-coded foil* to Goku's *cheerfully-undefeatable-protagonist* operating mode — a foil-relation that Toriyama installed in 1989 with the Saiyan Saga and that has propagated across every subsequent Dragon Ball arc through DBS's Universe Survival Tournament of Power (2017). Across his Z-and-Super-era continuity the character has been re-instantiated into several canonical named transformation-and-narrative phases whose visual identifiers and Bandai DBSCG character-tag fields are designer-coordinated to be told apart: **Vegeta** (the standard Z-and-Super-canon adult continuation, the canonical Prince-of-All-Saiyans-and-Goku-rival continuity from Saiyan Saga onward); **Majin Vegeta** (the Buu-Saga 25th World Martial Arts Tournament Vegeta who deliberately allows Babidi's Majin seal to be implanted for the power boost and fires a Big Bang Attack into the spectator stands, canonically Z's most pivotal moral failure for a Z-warrior); **Super Saiyan Blue Vegeta** (the DBS Future Trunks Saga / Universe Survival-era Vegeta who unlocks Super Saiyan God Super Saiyan and continues through the multiversal-tournament-scale opponents of the Super continuity). The Bandai DBSCG print pattern across the corpus treats these phases as distinguishable via the official character-tag field plus the per-card era stamp (`Battle of Gods Saga`, `Buu Saga`, `Frieza Saga`, `Future Trunks Saga`, `Universe Survival Saga`), with the transformation-state encoded at the costume-and-aura art layer.

## What this node anchors

Card art that depicts Vegeta himself across any of his canonical Z-and-Super-era arcs and transformation states, the Galick Gun and Final Flash that operate as his canonical signature attacks, the Saiyan royal armor that operates as his canonical costume across the Frieza Saga onward (distinct from Goku's Turtle School orange-and-blue gi), the Majin seal that canonicalizes his Buu-Saga moral failure, and the canonical cross-arc features (Prince-of-All-Saiyans royal title; combative-pride operating mode; husband-of-Bulma family relation; father-of-Trunks family relation). The character is well-developed enough that he splits into substantively different narrative phases across his printings, and the BBL graph can edge those phases together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card. The [[son-goku]] precedent applies directly (the sibling parent node from this same wave's E2 pass): one canonical character, multiple aspects, single anchor node that holds them all and surfaces the aspect-specific information per-card.

Bandai's character-tag for this register is `Vegito` (the Potara-fusion-canonical combined-character name, not a per-component pointer); the canonical Vegeta-component-of-Vegito attribution is wired via this node's `appears_on:` list AND will mirror at [[son-goku]]' `appears_on:` list (the canonical dual-anchor pattern for fusion combined-form characters where both components share the print). The Potara fusion is canonically distinct from the Metamoran Fusion Dance product Gogeta, both at the canon-mechanics level (Elder Kai earring vs. Metamoran-taught synchronized-pose technique) and at the combined-character-identity level (Vegito is canonically more powerful, more confident, and visually identified by the layered Potara fusion gi rather than the Metamoran sleeveless vest).

Bandai character tag: `Vegito`, era: Buu Saga. The card canonicalizes the Buu-Saga fusion-formation circumstance per Dragon Ball Wiki — Elder Kai gave Goku the Potara earrings because there was insufficient time to learn the Fusion Dance, Goku initially intended to fuse with Gohan, Buu absorbed Gohan before Goku could pass the earring, forcing the Goku-Vegeta pairing instead. ## The functional canon

Vegeta's most BBL-relevant property is that he is the franchise's **canonical foil-protagonist to [[son-goku]]**, present in every named series after his 1989 Z introduction as the rival-and-eventual-ally whose Saiyan-pride operating mode counterposes Goku's cheerfully-undefeatable-protagonist position. Every subsequent Dragon Ball Saiyan-pride beat (Trunks' father-figure-relation to Vegeta; Goten's heir-to-Saiyan-bloodline framing; the Battle of Gods Saga's Beerus-slap moment that breaks Vegeta's emotional ceiling; the Buu Saga's Majin-seal moral failure; the DBS Future Trunks Saga's father-son collaborative attack against Fused Zamasu) extends from the canonical foil-relation Toriyama installed in 1989 with the Saiyan Saga. Vegeta's narrative arc is the franchise's most-developed redemption arc: he begins as Frieza's elite warrior antagonist, becomes an uneasy ally against Frieza, gradually integrates into the Z-warrior collective, marries Bulma and fathers Trunks, deliberately corrupts himself in the Buu Saga before sacrificing himself to defeat Buu, returns from death across the post-Buu-Saga continuity, and continues through DBS as a Super-Saiyan-Blue-tier god-power-adjacent fighter.

The character's specific institutional features across the corpus are:

- **Saiyan royal lineage.** Vegeta is canonically the Prince of All Saiyans, son of King Vegeta who ruled Planet Vegeta before its destruction by Frieza. The Prince title persists across his entire arc despite the Saiyan homeworld's destruction; the royal-lineage frame is one of the most canonical features of Vegeta's character-thesis (the proud prince of a dead race, refusing to yield even when the people he ruled no longer exist). The Saiyan royal armor that he wears across the Frieza Saga onward — a dark navy bodysuit with white-and-yellow chest armor — is the canonical Vegeta costume, distinct from Goku's orange-and-blue Turtle School gi. The bodysuit-and-armor combination has been continuously instantiated across his Z, Buu, and DBS prints.
- **The Galick Gun.** Vegeta's canonical signature attack is the Galick Gun, a two-handed ki-blast technique that he canonically introduces in *Dragon Ball Z*'s Saiyan Saga (chapter 230 / episode 30, 1989) when he fires it at Earth in an attempt to destroy the planet and finds himself in a kamehameha-vs-galick-gun beam struggle with Goku — the canonical introduction-of-Vegeta-as-antagonist scene that established the two characters' foil-relation. The Galick Gun has subsequently propagated as a canonical Vegeta-anchored attack across all subsequent franchise output; the corpus's BT2-063 Father-Son Galick Gun canonicalizes the *Dragon Ball Super* episode 65 father-passes-his-signature-technique-to-his-son moment where Vegeta and Future Trunks combine their Galick Guns into a single electricity-wreathed blast against Fused Zamasu. Vegeta's second signature attack, the Final Flash, is canonically introduced in the Cell Saga and operates as his higher-power post-redemption-arc signature.
- **The Majin seal and the 25th World Martial Arts Tournament.** Canonically Vegeta's most pivotal moral failure across the franchise: at the 25th World Tournament during the Buu Saga, Vegeta deliberately allows the wizard Babidi to implant the Majin seal on his forehead for the power boost it provides, knowing full well the cost. While Majin-corrupted he fires a Big Bang Attack into the spectator stands, killing dozens of bystanders, to force Goku to fight him. The 25th Tournament moment canonicalizes Vegeta's character-thesis at its darkest extreme: Saiyan-pride taken past the breaking point of moral consideration. He subsequently redeems the failure with the canonical self-sacrifice against Majin Buu (the *bid you farewell* episode 226 sequence, Vegeta's first-ever-stated love-declaration to Bulma and Trunks before he detonates himself attempting to destroy Buu).
- **Husband of Bulma, father of [[trunks]].** Canonically Vegeta marries Bulma (the Capsule Corporation heiress) sometime in the post-Frieza-Saga timeline gap and they have two children: the present-timeline Kid Trunks (raised on Earth) and, in the alternate Cell-saga-future timeline, Future Trunks (the time-traveling adult who arrives in the Z-present to warn of the Android threat). The father-son foil relation between Vegeta and his half-Saiyan-half-Earthling sons is canonically central to the [[trunks]] node and to the corpus's BT2-063 Father-Son Galick Gun print. Vegeta canonically operates as the *moral foil to Trunks' more-Earthling-balanced Saiyan-inheritance*, a foil-relation the *Dragon Ball Super* Future Trunks Saga foregrounds when adult-Future-Trunks returns to the present.
- **Super Saiyan Blue and the god-tier continuation.** In *Dragon Ball Super*'s Battle of Gods Saga and onward, Vegeta canonically unlocks Super Saiyan God Super Saiyan (Super Saiyan Blue), a transformation accessed after Super Saiyan God training with Whis. SS Blue Vegeta is canonically distinguished from SS Vegeta by the cyan-blue upright spiky hair and the blue-white aura (rather than the golden hair and golden aura of SS). The corpus's BT2-063 Father-Son Galick Gun captures Vegeta in SS Blue form during the Future Trunks Saga's Fused Zamasu confrontation. Vegeta canonically continues through DBS's Universe 6 Tournament, the Future Trunks Saga, and the Universe Survival Tournament of Power at SS Blue-tier and Super Saiyan Blue Evolution-tier transformation states.

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Vegeta is, canonically across Toriyama and Toei and Bandai, the franchise's most-developed redemption-arc character and the foil-protagonist to Son Goku across every named series after 1989. The corpus's enriched anchors span four distinct registers (Battle of Gods SS, Buu-Saga Majin, DBS-continuation SS Blue, and Vegito Potara-Fusion-combined-form where Vegeta operates as one of two component-personalities) across four sets (Galactic Battle BT1 + Union Force BT2 + World Martial Arts Tournament TB2 + Colossal Warfare BT4), with BT4-030 At All Costs Vegeta extending the SS-Blue phase to three corpus prints and BT2-012 Repeated Force Vegito opening the Potara-Fusion register at this node per the wave-118 stack-attach. A future Vegeta-themed Discrete Lair could anchor on the multi-phase Saiyan-pride framing without reaching past canon; a Goku-and-Vegeta Saiyan-rivalry-themed Discrete Lair (extending into the canonical Vegito Buu-Saga fusion-resolution beat) becomes an index candidate once both nodes ([[son-goku]] from the parent-wave E2 and this node from E3) are live.

## Caveats

- **One parent, multiple narrative phases, no premature solo split (yet).** Following the [[son-goku]] precedent from this same wave's E2 pass (single character node with multi-incarnation roster covering Childhood / Z / GT / DBS adult incarnations under one parent), the [[trunks]] precedent (single character node with multi-incarnation roster covering Future / GT / Xeno / Kid incarnations), and the [[tamiyo]] / `nicol-bolas` multi-aspect single-character precedents, the canonical move is one parent Vegeta node anchoring all canonical narrative phases. The current 3-corpus-anchor density (Battle of Gods SS / Buu-Saga Majin / Future Trunks Saga SS Blue) meets the multi-aspect parent-node threshold without crossing the solo-split-per-phase threshold. A future split could partition the parent into `vegeta-z` / `vegeta-majin` / `vegeta-dbs` solo sub-nodes if the corpus accumulates enough per-phase density to warrant the split. For now the parent holds.

- **Bandai's character-tag field plus per-card era stamp is the canonical per-print phase identifier.** Each Dragon Ball Super Card Game card carries an official character tag (`Vegeta`, sometimes `Majin Vegeta` for specifically-Majin-coded prints) plus a per-card era stamp in Bandai's database (e.g., `Battle of Gods Saga`, `Buu Saga`, `Future Trunks Saga`, `Universe Survival Saga`) that together disambiguate which narrative phase the print depicts. The character-tag alone is generally `Vegeta` for non-Majin prints; the Majin-Vegeta-specific prints carry the `Majin Vegeta` tag and the Majin-"M"-mark visual identifier. The corpus's TB2-006 Vegeta, Power Misused depicts a Majin-coded Vegeta per the purple-tinted-aura visual and the TB2 red-color Buu-era assignment; the formal Bandai tag may be `Vegeta` with the era stamp doing the disambiguation, or `Majin Vegeta` directly — the trivia bullet on the card confirms the Majin/Super Saiyan form identification.

- **Vegeta and [[son-goku]] are the franchise's canonical rival-and-eventual-ally foil pair.** The two character nodes are designed as co-anchors: Vegeta's narrative arc is defined in relation to Goku (the Saiyan Saga introduces him as Goku's adversary; the Frieza Saga makes him an uneasy ally; the Cell Saga integrates him into the Z-warrior collective; the Buu Saga's Majin moment is fundamentally an attempt to force a fight with Goku; DBS's god-tier continuation positions the two as parallel SS-Blue-tier Saiyan fighters). The `related_characters: [son-goku, trunks]` frontmatter pointer encodes the two load-bearing canonical relations: Goku as the foil-rival, Trunks as the canonical son. A future Goku-and-Vegeta Saiyan-rivalry-themed Discrete Lair would anchor on this foil-pair across the BT1-066 Tenacious Vegeta + Goku-anchored prints from the Battle of Gods Saga / Saiyan Saga frame.

- **Sibling continuity-strand nodes apply.** [[trunks]] is the direct father-son family relation; the corpus's BT2-063 Father-Son Galick Gun is the canonical father-passes-his-signature-technique-to-his-son moment and is simultaneously anchored at the [[trunks]] node. [[son-goku]] is the canonical foil-rival relation from this same wave's E2 pass; the [[son-goku]] node's "see also" section anticipates this Vegeta node. The TB2 set's `Tiny Rivals Trunks` (TB2-007) and the Goku-anchored TB2-036 / TB2-052 prints sit in the same World Martial Arts Tournament set as the corpus's TB2-006 Vegeta, Power Misused, providing within-set co-character print pairings that a future tournament-arc-anchored bundle could deploy.

- **The Saiyan family-tree extends into [[trunks]] and downstream.** Vegeta canonically fathers Trunks (and in the alternate Cell-saga-future timeline, Future Trunks).

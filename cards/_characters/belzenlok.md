# Belzenlok

The Elder Demon who served as Dominaria's primary antagonist in the 2018 Dominaria block — fourth and final demonlord on Liliana Vess's four-demon contract after the Mending, self-styled "Eternal Patriarch of the Cabal," and the canonical centerpiece of the reformed religious cult that subjugated much of Dominaria from its seat at the Cabal Stronghold in Urborg. Freed from the Abyss by the Cabal's botched attempt to summon the god Kuberr; killed at the Stronghold by the Skyship Weatherlight's crew in Martha Wells's "Return to Dominaria: Episode 12" (2018-05-30) when Liliana, distracting the demon while Gideon Jura wielded the Blackblade, seized the embedded blade and drained Belzenlok's life force into it. His on-card depiction is the Demonlord Belzenlok card (Dominaria 86, Tyler Jacobson art), not currently in BBL corpus; his presence in the BBL corpus is the convergent reference-pattern from six Dominaria cards trivia-passed at this commission, with three more Dominaria cards (Whisper Blood Liturgist, Soul Salvage, Final Parting) in flight this wave that will attach when committed.

## The Cabal and the Urborg subjugation

The Cabal is Wizards-canonical as "the reformed religious cult of Dominaria" — its origins trace to the criminal organization of Otaria during the Odyssey block, but by Dominaria (2018) it has reformed under Belzenlok into a religious cult with explicit liturgy (the Rite of Belzenlok), explicit hierarchy (Eternal Patriarch / Confessor / Evangel / Initiate), and explicit institutional architecture (the Cabal Stronghold in Urborg as the seat of power). The cult's relocation to Urborg displaced the existing supernatural ecosystem of that region — the Feral Abomination flavor text ("respectable lich lords" replaced by "nightmares and thrulls") is the in-fiction articulation of the displacement, and Mister Lostspoons's black-comedy framing marks the Cabal's takeover as a downgrade from the prior order.

The institutional architecture has direct narrative weight: the Cabal Stronghold is where Belzenlok seats his rewriting-of-history project ("the Demonlord began the process of rewriting history with himself as the lead, claiming evil titles, names, and deeds" — MTG Wiki, citing the Cabal Evangel flavor text); the Stronghold is also the target of the Skyship Weatherlight's climactic Episode 12 assault and the location where Liliana wields the Blackblade. Wizards's claimed-titles list on the wiki — "Doom of Fools (taking Tevesh Szat's deed), Forger of Blackblade (taking Dakkon Blackblade's deed), Lord of the Wastes (taking Yawgmoth's deed), Scion of Darkness (taking Kuberr's deed), Slayer of Elder Dragons (taking Dakkon Blackblade's deed)" — encodes the rewriting-of-history project as the central institutional behavior of the cult under Belzenlok's reign.

For BBL's curatorial overlay, the Cabal apparatus reads as the structural sibling of the [[cell-saga-apparatus]] register from a different IP: an explicitly-named cult under an extractive demon-figure with named ritual liturgy and institutional hierarchy. Per Potion Rule sub-rule 1, the apparatus-of-extraction reading here is curatorial overlay; Wizards's stated event is "religious cult subjugates Urborg and is destroyed by the Weatherlight crew." Both can live in the body — the apparatus-of-extraction overlay is what the rebellion hub indexes through; the Wizards canonical-event beat is the killable-villain-with-apparatus narrative arc that DOM's story structure was built on.

## The four-demon contract arc and the Bolas-default

Belzenlok was the fourth and final demonlord on Liliana Vess's contract after the Mending — chronologically following Griselbrand, Kothophed, and Razaketh. Liliana spent the Innistrad-through-Dominaria arc systematically killing the demons holding her contract to free herself from the bargain; the Belzenlok kill at the end of DOM was the canonical culmination of the four-demon arc. **But the kill did not free her.** Per Episode 12's coda and the post-DOM continuation in War of the Spark: the contract defaulted to Nicol Bolas, who had brokered the original deal — when the Gatewatch departed Dominaria, Bolas revealed himself as Liliana's new owner. The Belzenlok kill was the moment when the four-demon arc collapsed into a single-master arc: Liliana traded one demonic master for an elder dragon, setting up the WAR contract-breaking-against-Bolas climax.

The structural relation matters for the graph: the Belzenlok node sits at the canonical hinge between Liliana's Dominaria arc and her War of the Spark arc. Her depicted-defeat-of-Belzenlok on Settle the Score is the canonical hinge moment — the act that should have freed her but didn't. The Episode 12 prose makes this explicit: after the kill, Liliana "looked down at herself, but the lines of her pact were still written on her skin. I thought it would disappear, she thought, her brow furrowing in confusion." The default-to-Bolas is revealed in the episode's coda; that revelation is the seed-plot for everything Liliana does in WAR.

A future Bolas-contract-broker character-to-character edge applies from this node to [[nicol-bolas]]: Bolas brokered the original four-demon deal, claimed the contract on Belzenlok's death, and was killed-via-contract-broken by Liliana in WAR. The relation is canon-grounded; defer character-to-character edge pending the framework's formalization, same as the [[liliana-vess]] / [[nicol-bolas]] and [[liliana-vess]] / [[gideon-jura]] deferrals.

# Chandra Nalaar

Pyromancer, mononymous and immediate; the signature red-aligned Gatewatch planeswalker and the single longest-running fire-mage protagonist in Magic: The Gathering's post-Mending continuity. Introduced in *Lorwyn* (2007) as one of the original five planeswalkers under the new spark-rules, foregrounded across the *Magic Origins* (2015) reveal that her spark ignited as a Kaladesh adolescent fleeing the Consulate's execution of her father Kiran Nalaar, and continuous through every Core Set and Standard-block return from 2007 forward. The `— Chandra` planeswalker subtype on the type-line is reserved for printings of this single character; canonical from Chandra Nalaar (Lorwyn, 2007) through every subsequent legendary planeswalker bearing the name. Daughter of Pia Nalaar and Kiran Nalaar of Ghirapur on Kaladesh (now Avishkar) — the maternal lineage canonically threads through the [[aether-rangers]] team page, where Pia is Chief Mechanic, making Chandra the daughter of one of the named co-captains of the home Grand Prix team in *Aetherdrift*.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts Chandra Nalaar herself — under any of her titles (the Firebrand, Roaring Flame, Flamecaller, Torch of Defiance, Bold Pyromancer, Fire Artisan, Acolyte of Flame, Heart of Fire, Dressed to Kill) — and cards in the canonical *Chandra's <Spell>* attribution cycle, in which non-planeswalker spells, creatures, and elementals are flavor-attributed to her as caster, conjurer, or summoner. The character is large enough to split into distinct narrative aspects across her printings, and the BBL graph can edge those aspects together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card mirroring.

**Aspects in the corpus (current):**

- **War of the Spark — Ravnica defense, Dovin-Baan-as-target moment** — Chandra on Ravnica during Bolas's invasion, foregrounded against the renegade-Kaladesh-defector Dovin Baan.
  - **Chandra's Pyrohelix** (WAR-120, common, Aleksi Briclot art) — back-facing robed pyromancer channeling twin spiral fire-helices into a roaring inferno. Flavor text: *"Now that I've dealt with your little toys, I can focus on the real problem: you." — Chandra Nalaar, to Dovin Baan.* The "little toys" are the Thopter constructs Dovin Baan engineered for Bolas's invasion. The card is one of Briclot's signature-artist Chandra prints (he also painted the original 2007 Chandra Nalaar from Lorwyn and Chandra's Phoenix from M12); the silhouetted-against-fire composition is the canonical Briclot register for the character.

- **Core Set 2021 — elemental-companion suite** — Chandra not directly depicted but rendered as the canonical conjurer behind two of her named fire-creatures.
  - **Chandra's Magmutt** (M21-137, common, Kimonas Theodossiou art) — Chandra's flame-elemental hound, depicted in profile against ash-and-ember background. Flavor text: *"Is it purebred? No, but it's pure fire." — Chandra Nalaar.* The mutt is the cheerful-companion register for the cycle: the pyromancer's pet, possessive name doing the attribution work.
  - **Chandra's Pyreling** (M21-138, uncommon, Josu Hernaiz art) — serpentine fire-elemental lizard in a black void, mechanically a double-strike-on-noncombat-damage payoff. Flavor text: *"I think Chandra just wanted something to blame random scorch marks on." — Jace Beleren.* The Jace-as-speaker flavor is the second-most-load-bearing detail in the cycle: it confirms the elemental's connection to Chandra via her Gatewatch teammate, and indexes the Chandra ↔ Jace relationship documented through both characters' Magic Origins arcs and the WAR-era Gatewatch period.

The three corpus cards span the *War of the Spark* combat register (Pyrohelix as combat-instant) and the *Core Set 2021* slice-of-life register (Magmutt + Pyreling as her companions and conjurations). Different artists, different visual scales, same character anchoring the attribution.

## The attribution-cycle and the family thread

A notable Wizards-canonical pattern: the *Chandra's <Spell>* / *Chandra's <Creature>* cycle currently runs to ~17 printings on Scryfall as of 2026, spanning *Chandra's Outrage, Ignition, Phoenix, Embercat, Firemaw, Defeat, Spitfire, Triumph, Revolution, Magmutt, Pyreling, Regulator, Flame Wave, Fury, Incinerator*, and others. The cycle reads as the in-fiction equivalent of a pyromancer's elemental-summoning repertoire — Chandra inventing, naming, and summoning fire-creatures and fire-spells across her arc. The BBL graph treats the attribution cycle the same way the [[ral-zarek]] node treats the *Ral's <Spell>* cycle: cards in the cycle that depict Chandra edge here directly (via `characters:` frontmatter); cards in the cycle that name her in flavor or possessive-title without depicting her edge here via the canonical-name attribution test. Both standards apply.

The family-engineer-revolutionary thread is one of the most BBL-relevant single character footnotes in MTG canon: Chandra's mother **Pia Nalaar** is canonically (per *Magic Origins: Chandra, Roaring Flames* and the *Planeswalker's Guide to Aetherdrift, Part 1* by Miguel Lopez, 2024-12-10) the Consulate-era Ghirapur engineer who survived the false-execution that ignited Chandra's spark, became a leader of the Kaladesh Renegades during the Aether Revolt, and post-revolution serves as Chief Mechanic of the [[aether-rangers]] Grand Prix team. The lineage runs Ghirapur engineer (Pia) -> revolutionary cadre (Pia + Kiran during the Aether Revolt) -> Renegade-coordinator (Pia, *Aether Revolt*) -> Avishkar-Assembly team captain (Pia, *Aetherdrift*) -> pyromancer planeswalker (Chandra). Mother and daughter are on opposite sides of the spark-event (Pia survived, Kiran did not; Chandra ignited from the trauma of believing both her parents had been killed), and the family thread is one of the more durable parent-child relationships Wizards has been willing to write across multiple sets.

For any BBL bundle that anchors on labor + rebellion + Avishkar + the Indigo-Revolution-era continuity, the Chandra ↔ Pia ↔ Aether Rangers throughline is the canonical lore-spine. The mother runs the post-revolutionary racing team; the daughter is the pyromancer-planeswalker who burned down half the Consulate before defecting to the Multiverse. The labor hub indexes through Pia; the rebellion hub indexes through both.

## Caveats

- This node is the character Chandra Nalaar herself, not the set of cards whose **name** mentions her. Cards like Chandra's Pyrohelix (WAR-120), Chandra's Magmutt (M21-137), and Chandra's Pyreling (M21-138) edge here through the canonical-name attribution test; cards that mention Chandra only in flavor text without depicting her *or* being attributed to her would not. The depiction-or-narrative-attribution test applies, parallel to the [[jace-beleren]] / [[ral-zarek]] standards.
- The `— Chandra` planeswalker subtype is a strict canonical identifier: every card carrying that subtype is a printing of this single character (no other character in Magic has that subtype). Spells named after her (Pyrohelix, Magmutt, Pyreling, Outrage, Ignition, Triumph, Defeat, et al) use the character's name without the subtype and are validated by flavor/oracle text attribution.
- **Chandra appears as a Kaladeshi adolescent in Magic Origins flashback art** (Chandra, Fire of Kaladesh; Chandra, Roaring Flame). Visual identification across age-shift depictions rests on canonical_source confirmation (MTG Wiki character page) rather than visual similarity alone — a future visually-young-Chandra card edges here through the canonical identification, not through art-match.
- **Chandra is canonically queer.** Per Wizards' *Forsaken* (2019), the *Magic Story* archive, and Mark Rosewater's subsequent design commentary, Chandra is in canonical romantic relationships with Nissa Revane (Gatewatch-era, *Forsaken* novel) and earlier with Captain Lampblack (*Aether Revolt*, less canonical). The Nissa-Chandra relationship is named-and-canonical; a future Nissa character node-to-this node *partner-of-the-act* relation applies. Defer until character-to-character edge category formalized, paralleling the [[ral-zarek]] / Tomik Vrona footnote.
- **A future `pia-nalaar` character node is the strongest unrealized character-to-character edge from this anchor.** Pia is currently graph-anchored only via [[aether-rangers]] (as Chief Mechanic of the team). When a Pia Nalaar card lands in the BBL corpus (DRC-2 *Pia Nalaar, Chief Mechanic* is the immediate candidate; the original *Pia and Kiran Nalaar* from Kaladesh and *Pia, Nalaar Pioneer* from the *Streets of New Capenna* Commander deck are precedents), a Pia sub-node under the family lineage would be authored and the Chandra ↔ Pia ↔ Aether Rangers throughline would become a fully edged triangle. Currently a deferred future-node candidate.
- **A future Nicol Bolas character node-to-this node *antagonist* relation could be drawn** (Bolas's invasion of Ravnica during WAR is the storyline event the Pyrohelix flavor line indexes; Chandra was a frontline Gatewatch combatant). The relation is canon-grounded but is a character-to-character edge, distinct from the card-to-character anchor edges this node currently accumulates. Defer until formalized.

# Wrath of the Dragon Saga (Hirudegarn arc, DBZ Movie 13)

The designer-coordinated seven-card saga-arc cycle within the Dragon Ball Super Card Game's *Colossal Warfare* booster (DBS-B04, 2018-07-13) that anchors the full character roster of *Dragon Ball Z: Wrath of the Dragon* (Toei Animation, theatrical release 1995-07-15 Japan) — the thirteenth and final Dragon Ball Z theatrical film of the original Z run. The cycle covers three structural axes of the film's plot: the **monster** (Hirudegarn across its insectoid first form and humanoid second form), the **bearer-brothers** (Tapion and Minotia, the Konatsian warriors who carried Hirudegarn's two halves sealed inside their bodies as an eternal containment oath, and each rendered twice across heroic and cursed states for the older brother), and the **villain** (Hoi, the Kashvar emissary-priest who manipulated the Z-Fighters into breaking the seal). The cycle's Leader card is BT4-023 Trunks // Iron Vow Trunks, whose canonical position in the film as the recipient of Tapion's enchanted sword retroactively anchors Future Trunks's iconic Brave Sword to this saga. The Hirudegarn-arc framing is encoded directly in Bandai's official per-card metadata (the saga field reads `Hirudegarn Saga` on every cycle member) and the cards are numerically clustered tight (037–042 plus the BT4-023 Leader) signaling intentional designer coordination at the set-architecture level.

## The functional canon

The cycle's most BBL-relevant property is that it is **Toei's tribute roster for the final original-run DBZ theatrical film**, deployed as a tight saga-arc cycle inside a single Dragon Ball Super Card Game booster set 23 years after the film's release. *Wrath of the Dragon* (1995) was the thirteenth and last DBZ film produced during the original Z run before Toei moved to *Dragon Ball GT* later that same year. The Hirudegarn Saga roster's structural coherence — three axes (monster / bearer-brothers / villain), seven cards tight-clustered in collector-number order (037-042 plus the BT4-023 Leader), Bandai's explicit `Saga: Hirudegarn` tag stamped on every member, designer-coordinated paired-print titles ("Hidden Darkness Tapion" paralleling "Hidden Darkness Minotia") — signals a curated Wizards-equivalent design decision: Colossal Warfare's 2018 set design treats this 1995 film as a unified narrative payload worth representing as a complete cohort rather than as scattered individual references. The Leader-card position for Trunks specifically uses the film's most lore-significant beat (Tapion gifts Trunks his enchanted sword at the film's end, retroactively explaining Future Trunks's iconic Brave Sword) as the canonical mechanism that anchors a Trunks-led saga rather than a Goku-led one.

The saga's specific institutional features for the corpus are:

- **The Phantom Majin distinction.** Hirudegarn is canonically described in Dragon Ball lore as a "Phantom Majin" — a supernatural creature of destruction rather than a natural organism — created or transformed by the Kashvar as an extinction-level weapon against all non-Kashvar life. The Phantom Majin classification distinguishes Hirudegarn from the franchise's other major Majin (Majin Buu in the Z-canon Buu Saga), positioning the creature as Hoi's instrument-of-genocide rather than as an ancient sealed evil in its own right.
- **The bisection-and-seal containment oath.** The Konatsian Guardian bisected Hirudegarn with an enchanted sword and Tapion and his younger brother Minotia voluntarily had the two halves sealed within their bodies. They were imprisoned in mystical Music Boxes and dispatched to distant parts of the universe as an eternal containment measure. The "oath" framing (BT4-039 "Oath's Power" + BT4-040/041 "Hidden Darkness" pairings) directly encodes this canonical sacrifice at the card-naming level. The sealed-evil-inside-a-warrior structural motif is one of the franchise's clearest examples of containment-as-personal-cost storytelling.
- **The retroactive sword retcon.** At the end of the film, Tapion gives his enchanted sword to young Trunks, saying it "always belonged to him" — a widely-cited retroactive nod to Future Trunks's iconic Brave Sword from the Android Saga. This connection is non-canonical to the main manga timeline but is the in-universe explanation the film clearly intended. The Colossal Warfare set design uses this retcon as the canonical justification for placing Trunks as the Hirudegarn Saga's blue-energy Leader rather than placing Goku in the slot — even though Goku is the one who actually defeats Hirudegarn in the film via Dragon Fist.
- **The manipulator-villain operating mode.** Hoi is not a fighter — he is the Kashvar bloodline's last survivor and operates entirely through deception, fabricating a victim cover story to manipulate the Z-Fighters into opening Tapion's Music Box. The "Emissary of Flame" framing on BT4-042 captures this: he operates through ceremony and proxy rather than direct combat. His canonical death — crushed underfoot by the creature he summoned — closes the apparatus-of-extraction loop where the operator becomes the first casualty of his own released weapon.
- **The Dragon Fist climax.** Goku's defeat of Hirudegarn is delivered via the Dragon Fist (Ryū-Ken), a transformation-attack technique that summons a golden Shenron-shaped ki dragon to punch entirely through the monster's body. *Wrath of the Dragon* is one of the Dragon Fist's most prominent early canonical appearances in the franchise, and the film's Japanese title (*Ryū-Ken Bakuhatsu!! Goku ga Yaraneba Dare ga Yaru*, "Dragon Fist Explosion!! If Goku Can't, Who Will?") explicitly foregrounds the technique. The Dragon Fist itself is not represented as a separate card in this Colossal Warfare cycle — the saga roster anchors the monster, the bearer-brothers, and the villain, leaving the climactic technique implicit.

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: the Hirudegarn Saga is Bandai's tribute roster for the last original-run DBZ theatrical film, deployed in 2018 as a designer-coordinated seven-card cycle across the monster-bearer-villain axes, anchored to Trunks as Leader via the film's canonical sword-inheritance retcon. The 7 corpus cards span all three structural axes and total **7 copies** of inventory at present (one of each); the cluster is suitable for a Discrete Lair commission anchored on the *complete-movie-arc representation* framing per `[[bbl-museum-curation-framing]]` and `[[bbl-narrative-first-lairs]]`.

## Caveats

- **Cycle-node, not character-node.** Following the `eld-cinderella-cycle` and `dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle` precedent, this node anchors a *designer-coordinated saga-arc cycle* rather than a single canonical character. The seven cycle members include four distinct characters (Hirudegarn, Tapion, Minotia, Hoi) plus a fifth Leader-cohabitant (Trunks) and the cycle's coherence rests on Bandai's `Saga: Hirudegarn` saga-tag stamp on every member rather than on a single-character through-line. Future per-character solo nodes (`hirudegarn`, `tapion`, `hoi`) are *not* currently warranted at the corpus density of one card each (or two for Hirudegarn's two forms, two for Tapion's two states); the saga-cycle framing does the disambiguation work. If a future Wrath-of-the-Dragon Movie 13 expansion adds substantially more corpus density per individual character (e.g., 3+ Hirudegarn-only prints from a future DBSCG set), a split into per-character solo sub-nodes could be commissioned then.

- **Trunks is both a Hirudegarn-Saga Leader and a Trunks-aspect.** BT4-023 Trunks // Iron Vow Trunks carries `characters: [trunks, wrath-of-the-dragon-saga]` — both pointers are canonically warranted. The card is the saga's blue-energy Leader (anchoring here) *and* a Future-Trunks-incarnation aspect (anchoring at [[trunks]]). This dual-membership is structurally distinct from cycle-membership for the other six cards (BT4-037 through BT4-042), which point only here because their canonical characters (Hirudegarn / Tapion / Minotia / Hoi) do not yet have solo character nodes and their narrative weight lives entirely at the saga-cycle level. The trunks-node already documents this dual-anchor relationship in its "Aspects in the corpus" section under Future Trunks (the SD3-03 and BT3-112 prints anchor the Future-Xeno-blade-handler aspect; BT4-023 anchors the Hirudegarn-Saga-Leader sub-aspect within the same parent).

- **Movie-canonicity scope.** Wrath of the Dragon (1995) is a Toei-canonical theatrical film, not a Toriyama-manga-canon arc. The film's relationship to the Dragon Ball Z manga and the broader franchise canon is a multi-tiered Toei-anime-canon / Bandai-game-canon / Toriyama-manga-canon distinction: the film exists in Toei's animated continuity and is treated as canonical by Bandai's Dragon Ball Super Card Game and Dragon Ball Heroes products, but the Tapion-gives-Trunks-the-sword retcon is *not* present in Toriyama's original Z manga. Bundle copy that needs strict manga-canon footing should not lean on the sword retcon as primary evidence; copy that uses the Toei-animation-canon-plus-Bandai-game-canon framing has solid grounding for the retcon's in-universe canonicity.

- **English-dub release lag.** The film released theatrically in Japan on 1995-07-15 but did not receive its English-dub home-video release until 2006 via Funimation — an 11-year gap. Bundle copy that references "the last original-run DBZ film" should specify "the last original-run *Japanese-theatrical* DBZ film" if precision matters; the English-language Wrath-of-the-Dragon home-video release postdates Dragon Ball GT's English broadcast.

- **Cycle membership is closed at present.** The seven Colossal Warfare cards listed in `appears_on:` are the canonical Hirudegarn Saga roster as confirmed by Bandai's per-card saga-tag metadata. No other Colossal Warfare card carries the `Saga: Hirudegarn` tag, and no other Dragon Ball Super Card Game expansion has yet reprinted or expanded the roster. If a future DBSCG set adds new Wrath-of-the-Dragon cards (e.g., a Dragon Fist technique card, a Konatsian Guardian card, additional Tapion / Hoi prints), they would join the saga-cycle node here on the same Bandai-saga-tag basis.

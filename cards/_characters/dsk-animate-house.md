# Animate-house cohort (Duskmourn possessed household objects, non-Toy)

A *Duskmourn: House of Horror* design cohort of cards depicting **household furnishings, fixtures, and appliances animated into hostility** — a television, a chandelier, a windbreaker jacket — by Valgavoth's possession of the House. The cohort is the corpus's home for the set's **house-as-organism / possessed-furnishing register**: the Duskmourn design brief, stated in Wizards' own announcement coverage, is that the mansion itself is alive and its furnishings animate as threats. Where the sibling [[dsk-toy-horror]] cohort holds the *corrupted childhood toy* register marked at the type-line by the **Toy creature subtype**, this cohort holds the broader *possessed everyday object* register for the cards that carry no Toy subtype — appliances and fixtures and clothing rather than playthings.

## What this node anchors

Card art, oracle text, flavor text, and type-line for the *Duskmourn* cards whose subject is a **household object, fixture, or appliance possessed into hostile animation** and which do **not** carry the Toy creature subtype. Membership predicates on three overlapping signals: (a) the card's art depicts a recognizable domestic furnishing, fixture, or garment (television, chandelier, jacket, mirror, appliance) rendered as animate and menacing, (b) the type-line is Artifact, Artifact Creature (Construct / Spirit / Nightmare), or Artifact — Equipment, **without** the Toy creature subtype, (c) the flavor or oracle register frames the animation as external possession by the House rather than the object's own latent awakening — the Western haunted-object tradition the [[tsukumogami]] hub names as the poltergeist register.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Haunted Screen** (DSK-250, U, Sean Murray art) — a boxy vintage cathode-ray television with a ghost reaching outward through the glass, set in a cluttered room of stacked old electronics. Mechanically a `{3}` Artifact five-color mana rock that can spend `{7}` to become a 0/0 Spirit creature with seven +1/+1 counters. The type-line is Artifact, not Artifact Creature — Toy; the card becomes a **Spirit** when animated, never a Toy. The retro-consumer-electronics dread is the set's signature horror-delivery vector — the haunted appliance as the screen the dead reach through.
- **Malevolent Chandelier** (DSK-252, C, Mirko Failoni art) — an ornate chandelier whose central body forms a leering skull face with ember eyes, chains dripping from its candle-arms, hovering over two cowering figures in a cavernous interior. Mechanically a `{6}` Artifact Creature — **Construct** with flying and a repeatable graveyard-hate ability. Flavor text: *"If you see a light moving toward you in the darkness, don't assume it's coming to help." — Winter.* The Construct type-line and the chandelier's fixture-of-the-house subject place it firmly in the possessed-furnishing register, outside the Toy roster.
- **Cursed Windbreaker** (DSK-47, U, Nino Vecia art) — a windbreaker jacket hovering with no wearer, arms outstretched, blue energy crackling along its seams against a foggy industrial backdrop. Mechanically a `{2}{U}` Artifact — Equipment that manifests dread on entry and grants flying. The empty-garment-that-moves-with-intent is the cohort's clothing instance — a possessed wearable rather than an appliance or fixture, but the same external-animation grammar.

The three corpus members span three distinct domestic-object archetypes — the *possessed appliance* (Haunted Screen), the *possessed fixture* (Malevolent Chandelier), and the *possessed garment* (Cursed Windbreaker) — and three distinct type-lines (Artifact, Artifact Creature — Construct, Artifact — Equipment), giving the cohort a non-redundant substrate united by subject and register rather than by a single shared subtype.

## The cohort thesis

The animate-house cohort's most BBL-relevant property is its **house-as-organism** framing: these objects are not individually haunted in isolation: they are the limbs of a single living structure. Valgavoth's possession of the mansion extends into every furnishing, so the television, the chandelier, and the jacket are less individual monsters than they are the House *reaching*. This is the cohort's narrative-first hook and the structural counterpart to [[dsk-toy-horror]]'s repair-corruption thesis: where the toy is made hostile by the affection that tried to mend it, the furnishing is made hostile simply by being *inside* a body that woke up. The cohort's register is the domestic-uncanny — the dread that the objects you live among are not as inert as they look — rendered as external possession, not internal awakening.

This is the cohort the [[tsukumogami]] hub's **"The House Was Awake"** narrative seed names: the externally-possessed contrast lair, the Western haunted-object / poltergeist register held honestly apart from the Japanese tsukumogami concept of an object's own spirit waking from within.

- **[[tsukumogami]]** — **parent hub.** This cohort is a child of the foundational `tsukumogami` hub, which gathers the animate-object concept across IPs and routes hub → cohort → card (never hub → card). Before this node, the hub routed the entire Duskmourn possessed-object register through [[dsk-toy-horror]] alone; that cohort's predicate (Toy creature subtype, corrupted *childhood* object) is too narrow to hold non-Toy household furnishings, leaving the hub's "The House Was Awake" seed without a routing target for its TV and chandelier. This node closes that gap.

- **[[dsk-toy-horror]]** — sibling DSK cohort character node; the **corrupted-childhood-toy** axis of the same possessed-object cosmology, marked at the type-line by the Toy creature subtype. The two Duskmourn cohorts partition the set's possessed-object register cleanly: Toy-subtyped corrupted playthings (dsk-toy-horror) versus non-Toy possessed furnishings, fixtures, and appliances (this node). A card carrying the Toy subtype routes to dsk-toy-horror; a possessed household object without it routes here. Living Phone — a corrupted *appliance* — sits at dsk-toy-horror despite the household-object subject **because it carries the Toy creature subtype on its type-line**, and the type-line is the decisive predicate; it is the boundary case that defines the partition.

- **[[valgavoth]]** — the demon whose possession of the House animates the furnishings. The character-level antagonist behind both Duskmourn cohorts.

## Caveats

- **Cohort threshold met at 3 cards; full roster not yet enumerated.** Three corpus members are pointered to this node (see `appears_on:`). DSK's full non-Toy possessed-furnishing roster is not yet enumerated; a future Scryfall sweep should surface adjacent cohort-eligible cards (mirrors, appliances, fixtures, possessed furniture) and attach them as they land via Collectr CSV uploads.
- **The partition with [[dsk-toy-horror]] is type-line-decisive.** The two Duskmourn possessed-object cohorts are partitioned by the **Toy creature subtype**: a card carrying it routes to dsk-toy-horror regardless of whether its subject is a toy or an appliance (Living Phone is the precedent — a corrupted telephone that nonetheless carries Toy and therefore sits at dsk-toy-horror). A possessed household object *without* the Toy subtype routes here. This is a clean cut, not a gradient; future passes should verify per-card type-lines against Scryfall before attaching.
- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like `dsk-toy-horror`, `fear-of-cycle`, `dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle`, this is a collective designer-coordinated cohort hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent. A future `_cycles/` or `_cohorts/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the other DSK cohorts when that split is commissioned.
- **External possession is a thesis, not a strict membership predicate.** Most cohort members' register is external possession by the House; the gating predicates are the domestic-object subject and the non-Toy type-line. A future member whose lore frames the animation differently is still cohort-eligible if it depicts a possessed household furnishing without the Toy subtype.

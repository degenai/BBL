# Kor

The Zendikar-native plate-armored nomadic warrior race that serves as one of the plane's three canonical sapient cultures (alongside the merfolk and the vampires, with humans, goblins, and elves rounding out the secondary population). Per MTG Wiki's Kor page, the kor are slender pale-skinned humanoids characterized by ritualistic facemark warpaint, white or grey hair (often bound in long braids and tassels), and a culture organized around rope-and-hook climbing rigs, kitesails, and the canyon-and-cliff vertical landscape of Zendikar. Their predominant color alignment is **white** (the white Limited common-and-uncommon slot in every Zendikar-block set, with Allies-block multicolor extensions during *Rise of the Eldrazi* and *Battle for Zendikar*).

The kor's canonical narrative arc across Wizards-of-the-Coast story output is structurally a **survival-against-cosmological-threat** arc: they are the indigenous Zendikari race most exposed to the Eldrazi invasion in the BFZ block (their wandering plate-armored bands have no fixed settlements to retreat to; their vertical-traversal lifestyle places them adjacent to the canyon-and-spire terrain where the Eldrazi emerge), and their cultural response to existential threat — Rally mechanics, Allies-keyword teamwork, Convergence-from-Unified-Front color-coordinated militias — is the canonical *coordinated-defense* register in the BFZ-block Limited environment.

## What this node anchors

Card art that depicts kor figures — warriors, scouts, glider-pilots, kitesail riders, nomad bands. Also: cards whose narrative beat *centers* the kor as the off-frame protagonists (anti-Eldrazi defensive cards whose flavor text quotes named kor characters like Akiri the line-slinger, or whose tokens spawn kor allies under coordinated-rally mechanics). The character is broad enough that it functions structurally as a faction-collective node — like [[eldrazi]] on the adversary side, or [[phyrexia]] on the Magic side — rather than as a single named entity.

## The functional ideology

The kor's BBL-relevant property is **coordinated-defense-as-cultural-cohesion-under-existential-threat** — the canonical inverse of the Eldrazi's apparatus-of-consumption thesis. Where the Eldrazi extract value from worlds without political negotiation, the kor respond by **organizing horizontally** across racial and tribal lines into Allies-keyword militias. Per MTG Wiki and the BFZ-block design articles, the faction's defining features:

- **The kor are indigenous, not native to a single settlement.** Their cultural register is nomadic — bands traveling vertical-canyon Zendikar via rope-and-hook climbing rigs, kitesails for gliding traversal between cliffs, plate armor calibrated for mountain-and-canyon combat rather than fortress-defense. This nomadism is mechanically reflected in their Limited-archetype role across BFZ: white-aligned tempo creatures with Flying (Kitesail Scout, Shadow Glider) and small-body Rally engine pieces (Kor Bladewhirl, Kor Entanglers).

- **The Rally mechanic is the kor's mechanical signature for coordinated militia response.** Whenever a Kor Ally enters the battlefield, the team triggers a buff (first strike, opponent-creature tapping, +1/+1 counters, scry, etc.). The mechanic encodes the kor's narrative thesis — *coordinated arrival reshapes the field* — directly into the cards' play patterns. Three corpus members (Kor Bladewhirl, Kor Entanglers, Expedition Envoy) carry Rally; the Convergence-token-generator Unified Front renders the same theme as a sorcery (color-coordinated militia raised in response to the threat).

- **The kor are the canonical anti-Eldrazi defensive cohort on Zendikar.** Multiple BFZ kor cards carry explicit anti-Eldrazi flavor or mechanical clauses: Kor Castigator's "can't be blocked by Eldrazi Scions" oracle, Shadow Glider's refugee flavor, Kor Castigator's flavor quote from Akiri the kor line-slinger. The Eldrazi-invasion arc is the kor's defining storyline; the corpus's eight BFZ kor anchors plus the OGW and ZNR successor anchors form the canonical BBL-corpus slice of that arc.

- **Stone Havens are the canonical kor settlement-network.** Cliffside Lookout's flavor text names the Stone Havens directly — fortified canyon-rim lookout posts that served as the kor's primary defensive infrastructure during the Eldrazi invasion. Cliffside Lookout, Expedition Envoy, and the wider BFZ kor common-and-uncommon roster all anchor at the Stone Havens defensive-network register. A future *places* layer could anchor Stone Havens as a sibling node; for now, settlement-context recorded in vision/trivia.

- **Named kor characters exist but are flavor-speakers, not (yet) printed-card depictions.** Akiri the kor line-slinger is the most-cited named kor in BFZ flavor text (Kor Castigator quote). The wave-39 corpus precedent on Slobad — flavor-speaker named characters are deferred sub-node candidates pending printed-card-depiction — applies here. Future printings of Akiri as a depicted-card character would warrant her own character sub-node edged to this one.

This is what BBL bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: the kor are, in-universe, a designer-confirmed indigenous Zendikari plate-armored nomadic warrior race organized around coordinated-defense-against-existential-threat thesis, with the BFZ-block as their canonical narrative arc and the ZNR-block as their canonical post-arc cultural continuation. Bundle prose anchored on the kor (especially in a BFZ-focused Discrete Lair) can lean on Wizards' own block-design framework — Allies, Rally, Convergence, anti-Eldrazi mechanical hooks — to do the thesis work.

## Caveats

- **Kor as creature-type vs. Kor as faction-membership are correlated and mostly co-extensive on Zendikar.** Unlike the Eldrazi caveat (where some non-Eldrazi creatures appear as Eldrazi-corrupted forms), the kor are a coherent racial identity — kor-typed creatures are reliably kor narratively, and faction-aligned cards reliably carry the Kor creature subtype where the depicted figure is a kor. The Allies-block multicolor extensions complicate this slightly (some Kor-Ally cards include multicolor and depict the kor in cross-racial Allies teams), but the kor-as-race membership remains canonical at the subtype level.

- **Akiri, the canonical named kor in BFZ-era flavor text, warrants her own sub-node if multiple Akiri-depicting cards land in corpus.** Akiri Line-Slinger and Akiri Fearless Voyager are the two canonical Akiri printings outside corpus. The team-rocket / acerola / orzhov-syndicate precedent applies — sub-node warranted at ≥2 corpus prints depicting Akiri. Currently below threshold (zero Akiri-depicting cards in corpus; one flavor-speaker reference on Kor Castigator).

- **Cohort named characters at the kor-leadership tier are deferred.** Beyond Akiri, the broader BFZ-block kor-leadership roster includes Munda the Ally-Bound (BFZ), Stoneforge Mystic (the canonical kor wizard outside BFZ canon, originally WWK), Kor Skyfisher (the canonical kor-on-a-kitesail print from ZEN). Each is a deferred sub-node candidate pending corpus accumulation. Same precedent as the Phyrexian Praetors caveat on [[phyrexia]].

- **Shadow Glider's kor-spirit classification is canonical per Scryfall but visually-indeterminate in our corpus's BFZ-47 print.** Scryfall stamps Shadow Glider as creature type "Kor Spirit" — a post-mortem-kor in the cave networks. The flavor text confirms the refugee-bands-in-the-caves narrative. The card's art in our corpus is currently un-enriched (no tags_hub, no Vision pass); future enrichment should anchor the figure as a Kor Spirit per Scryfall's mechanical classification and the flavor's narrative anchor.

- **The kor's predominantly-white color alignment is canonical but not exclusive.** BFZ-block multicolor extensions (Unified Front's Convergence colors, Allies-block multicolor Kor-allies during *Rise of the Eldrazi*) place kor figures in white-leaning multicolor identities. The kor are not a strictly-mono-white race — they are predominantly white with multicolor leadership and named-character exceptions. The species classification field above (white-aligned with multicolor allies-block extensions) captures this.

- **The Stone Havens are a place-layer concept, not part of this race-layer node.** Cliffside Lookout's flavor text anchors the canonical kor-settlement-network, but Stone Havens is a *place* (a defensive infrastructure network), not a *faction* (the kor people themselves). If a future `_places/` layer is commissioned, Stone Havens would be a sibling node edged here as the canonical kor home-infrastructure. For now, Stone Havens are recorded in vision/trivia as settlement-context.

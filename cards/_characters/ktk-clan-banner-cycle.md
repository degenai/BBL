# KTK clan banner cycle (five wedge-color common mana-rock artifacts — Abzan, Jeskai, Mardu, Sultai, Temur)

Khans of Tarkir's five clan banners — Abzan Banner, Jeskai Banner, Mardu Banner, Sultai Banner, and Temur Banner — are a designer-coordinated five-card cycle, one per Tarkir clan, each a common colorless artifact costing `{3}` that taps to add one of the clan's three wedge colors and may be sacrificed (paying the full three-color cost) to draw a card. The cycle is the set's basic three-color fixing rock for Limited play and a recurring inclusion in casual EDH three-color decks. All five banners in their original KTK printing were illustrated by the Swedish fantasy illustrator Daniel Ljunggren, giving the cycle a single-artist visual signature that parallels Mathias Kollros's coverage of the [[ktk-clan-charm-cycle]] in the same set. Each banner carries its clan's watermark printed on the card frame and a two-clause flavor motto ("X to [verb], Y to [verb]") whose paired virtues map directly to the clan's canonical philosophical identity.

## What this node anchors

The five clan banners as a designer-coordinated wedge-color mana-rock cycle: their shared structural slot (common, artifact, `{3}` mana cost, tap for one of three wedge colors, sacrifice for a card draw at full three-color cost), their per-clan philosophical-motto mapping (each banner's flavor text names two clan virtues in parallel construction), their shared single-artist visual signature (Daniel Ljunggren on all five original KTK printings), and their place in Wizards' KTK common-slot fixing infrastructure. Membership predicates on Wizards-confirmed cycle status in the set's artifact section and Scryfall's `s:ktk t:artifact c:c` cohort; cards that happen to be three-color-fixing common mana-rocks in other sets (e.g. the [[refuges-cycle]] lands or the various signets) are not members.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Jeskai Banner** (KTK-222, C, Daniel Ljunggren art) — UWR. Flavor: "Discipline to persevere, insight to discover." Mottoes the Jeskai Way's clan virtue *cunning* and its dragon-aspect of the mind, naming discipline (the monastic-prowess pillar) and insight (the enlightenment pillar) as the two clan virtues encoded into the banner. Trivia-passed wave 179.
- **Mardu Banner** (KTK-224, C, Daniel Ljunggren art) — RWB. Flavor: "Speed to strike, fury to smash." Mottoes the Mardu Horde's clan virtue *speed* and its dragon-aspect of the wings, naming the percussive, action-oriented virtues of the horde — no contemplation, just strike-and-smash. Most-included of the cycle on EDHREC (rank 9,750 as of wave 179), likely reflecting the deep bench of RWB commanders that benefit from cheap fixing. Trivia-passed wave 179.
- **Sultai Banner** (KTK-225, C, Daniel Ljunggren art) — BGU. Flavor: "Power to dominate, cruelty to rule." Mottoes the Sultai Brood's clan virtue *ruthlessness* and its dragon-aspect of the fang; the only motto in the cycle that names cruelty as an explicit clan virtue, mirroring the Sultai's stated lore identity (the clan does not merely defeat enemies, it subjugates and exploits them). Trivia-passed wave 179.
- **Temur Banner** (KTK-226, C, Daniel Ljunggren art) — GUR. Flavor: "Savagery to survive, courage to triumph." Mottoes the Temur Frontier's clan virtue *savagery* and its dragon-aspect of the claw; the only banner in the cycle depicted as a totem (animal skull lashed to crossed wooden poles) rather than a textile flag, consistent with the clan's shamanic and hunter-gatherer identity. Trivia-passed wave 179.

## The design thesis

The KTK clan banner cycle is the cleanest Wizards-canonical articulation of the *one-rock-per-clan, three-color-fixing-with-card-draw-payoff* design pattern applied to a five-clan wedge-color split at common rarity. Each banner is the same card mechanically — colorless `{3}` cost, tap for any of three wedge colors, sacrifice with three-color cost to draw a card — and the differentiation lives entirely in the flavor motto, the watermark, and the art subject. This makes the cycle a useful pedagogical example of how Wizards encodes clan identity into otherwise identical fixing artifacts:

- **Abzan Banner (WBG)** — "Stone to endure, roots to remember." Encodes the Abzan's *endurance* virtue (the dragon-aspect is the scales — defense, longevity, the long view) through the two-virtue motto of stone (immobility, persistence) and roots (lineage, ancestral memory).
- **Jeskai Banner (UWR)** — "Discipline to persevere, insight to discover." Encodes the Jeskai's *cunning* virtue (the dragon-aspect is the eye — the mind, monastic prowess) through the discipline-and-insight pairing that maps onto the clan's twin pillars of martial-monastic training and contemplative enlightenment.
- **Mardu Banner (RWB)** — "Speed to strike, fury to smash." Encodes the Mardu's *speed* virtue (the dragon-aspect is the wings — flight, blitz, the Raid mechanic) through the percussive monosyllabic action verbs that contrast with the Jeskai's contemplative motto. Where Jeskai discovers, Mardu smashes.
- **Sultai Banner (BGU)** — "Power to dominate, cruelty to rule." Encodes the Sultai's *ruthlessness* virtue (the dragon-aspect is the fang — predation, the Delve mechanic that consumes the graveyard) through the only cruelty-naming motto in the cycle. The Sultai are the only clan whose flavor explicitly endorses cruelty as governance.
- **Temur Banner (GUR)** — "Savagery to survive, courage to triumph." Encodes the Temur's *savagery* virtue (the dragon-aspect is the claw — predation, the Ferocious mechanic) through the pairing of savagery (raw animal force) with courage (volitional choice), suggesting the Temur see brutality and bravery as inseparable.

The single-artist visual signature (Daniel Ljunggren on all five original KTK printings) is a separable curatorial datum that parallels Mathias Kollros's coverage of the [[ktk-clan-charm-cycle]]: one illustrator's pen across all five cards enforces visual coherence at the cycle level. For BBL the Ljunggren single-artist-cycle datum makes the future `daniel-ljunggren` artist node, if commissioned, an unusually load-bearing layer node — five KTK common cycle members concentrated under one credit, and Ljunggren's wider MTG portfolio spans dozens of sets.

The structural pattern across both KTK cycles is informative: KTK uses single-artist coverage as a cycle-cohesion device at both the uncommon-charm tier (Kollros) and the common-banner tier (Ljunggren), within the same set. This is rare enough that it serves as a curatorial fingerprint for the Khans block's design priorities.

## Caveats

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Same precedent shape as [[ktk-clan-charm-cycle]] (sibling KTK cycle), `[[eld-cinderella-cycle]]`, `[[dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle]]`, `[[fear-of-cycle]]`, `[[castform-forms-cycle]]`, `[[refuges-cycle]]`, and `[[war-gatewatch-triumph-cycle]]`: collective conceptual entity hosted on the character layer pending a future `_cycles/` split. Single layer, same precedent.
- **Roster is bounded at exactly five by Wizards' design.** The canonical membership is the five named clan banners, no more and no fewer. Future BBL acquisition of Abzan Banner KTK-215 should attach by updating `appears_on:` and stamping `characters: [ktk-clan-banner-cycle]` on the card's frontmatter when prepped.
- **Per-card trivia drift on Abzan Banner's collector number.** As of wave 179, all four corpus members' Related cards bullets correctly identify Abzan Banner as KTK-215; no drift correction needed. The Jeskai Banner trivia bullet additionally preserves Abzan Banner's flavor motto ("Stone to endure, roots to remember.") which is canonically sourced for cycle completion documentation.
- **EDHREC rank values are time-stamped data, not stable.** The wave-179 captured ranks (Mardu 9,750; Jeskai 12,517; Temur 16,229; Sultai 16,307; Abzan not captured) reflect 2026-05-19 EDHREC state and will drift over time. Future Edgelord audit waves may want to refresh these or move them to per-card trivia exclusively rather than the cycle-node body.
- **Reprint history not symmetric across cycle, not yet fully researched.** Per Scryfall, each banner has at least one Commander-product reprint; whether reprint-side art is Ljunggren on all printings (paralleling the Temur Charm Commander 2020 Kollros-reprint consistency documented in the [[ktk-clan-charm-cycle]] node) is a future-watch question for the `daniel-ljunggren` artist node when commissioned.
- **Triple-thesis adjacency is body-prose, not hub-attribution.** The Sultai Banner motto's "cruelty to rule" line and the Mardu Banner's "speed to strike, fury to smash" each have a labor-or-extraction reading available under BBL's curatorial overlay, but neither is Wizards-stated as labor-coded or extraction-coded — they are clan virtue mottos in canonical lore. Per the Potion Rule and `[[_triple-thesis]]`, this cycle node does not attach to the `[[labor]]`, `[[rebellion]]`, or `[[stewardship]]` hubs. Bundle-tier curation may extrapolate per `[[bbl-narrative-first-lairs]]`; the node body does not.

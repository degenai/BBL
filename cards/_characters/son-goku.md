# Son Goku

The Saiyan-born Earth-raised martial artist introduced in *Dragon Ball* (Akira Toriyama, Shueisha 1984) as a child with a monkey tail and a flying nimbus cloud, and continuously instantiated across forty years of Dragon Ball franchise output as the canonical protagonist of every subsequent series. Born Kakarot on Planet Vegeta to the low-class Saiyan warrior Bardock and his wife Gine, sent to Earth as an infant in a Saiyan attack pod, raised as Son Goku by the elderly martial-arts master Son Gohan in the Mount Paozu wilderness. The franchise's first and most-instantiated protagonist; the canonical Super Saiyan; the canonical wielder of the Kamehameha; the canonical Goku-Pan-Trunks GT traveling-trio party leader; the only Dragon Ball character whose continuous presence spans every named series in the franchise (Dragon Ball, Dragon Ball Z, Dragon Ball GT, Dragon Ball Super) plus the Bandai card-game continuity that the corpus draws from.

Across four decades of Toriyama and Toei output the character has been re-instantiated into four canonical named incarnations whose visual identifiers, canonical age, and continuity-strand are designer-coordinated for Bandai's card-game character-tag field to disambiguate per-print: **Son Goku: Childhood** (the original Dragon Ball pre-Z child, the Power Pole-wielding Sun-Wukong-derived child martial artist from chapter 1 of the manga onward, distinct from the GT de-aged child); **Son Goku** (the canonical Dragon Ball Z and Dragon Ball Super adult, the Saiyan Saga onward Z-era adult who unlocks Super Saiyan in the Frieza Saga and continues through DBS's Battle of Gods, Resurrection F, Universe 6, Future Trunks, and Universe Survival arcs); **Son Goku: GT** (the Toei Dragon Ball GT continuation 1996-1997 where Goku is accidentally reverted to a child body by Emperor Pilaf's wish on the Black Star Dragon Balls but retains all adult power and memory, leading the Goku-Pan-Trunks traveling-trio quest to recover the seven scattered Black Star Dragon Balls across the universe); and **Son Goku: Future** (the *Dragon Ball Super* Future Trunks Saga adult-Goku as he appears against Goku Black and Zamasu in the alternate-timeline arc — distinct from the main-timeline adult Goku within Bandai's character-tag scheme for the Cross Worlds set's GT-and-Future-cross-set design).

The four incarnations share a single canonical character (Saiyan lineage from Bardock and Gine; orange-and-blue Turtle School gi as canonical costume across every named series; the Kamehameha as canonical signature attack; the *cheerfully-undefeatable-protagonist* operating mode that Toriyama installed in 1984 and that has propagated across every subsequent Dragon Ball protagonist arc). They differ in age, costume-detail, weapon, and the *cosmological scope* of their respective stories: Childhood Goku adventures across Earth recovering the seven Dragon Balls; Z-era Goku defends Earth from Saiyan, Frieza-Force, Android, and Buu antagonists; GT Goku fights through Baby Saga, Super 17 Saga, and Shadow Dragon Saga across the universe; DBS Goku navigates god-tier opponents (Beerus, Champa, Zeno, Black, Jiren) at the multiversal-tournament scale. The Dragon Ball Super Card Game treats the four incarnations as distinguishable via Bandai's official character-tag field in the card database (`Son Goku: Childhood`, `Son Goku`, `Son Goku: GT`, `Son Goku: Future`), making per-card incarnation identification a clerical lookup rather than a vision-confidence problem.

## What this node anchors

Card art that depicts Son Goku himself across any of the four named incarnations, the Kamehameha that operates as his canonical signature attack, the Super Saiyan / SS2 / SS3 / Super Saiyan Blue transformation arcs that span his Z-and-Super-era progression, the Power Pole that operates as his Childhood-era canonical weapon, and the canonical cross-incarnation features (Saiyan lineage; orange-and-blue Turtle School gi; cheerful-protagonist operating mode; Goku's Lineage family-tree relations across Bardock, Gohan, Goten, Pan). The character is large enough that he splits into substantively different aspects across his printings, and the BBL graph can edge those aspects together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card. The [[trunks]] precedent applies directly: one canonical character, multiple aspects, single anchor node that holds them all and surfaces the aspect-specific information per-card.

## The functional canon

Goku's most BBL-relevant property is that he is the franchise's **canonical protagonist across every named Dragon Ball series**, present in *Dragon Ball* (1984-1995 manga / 1986-1989 anime), *Dragon Ball Z* (1989-1996), *Dragon Ball GT* (1996-1997), and *Dragon Ball Super* (2015-2018) as the continuous-narrative anchor character. Every subsequent Dragon Ball protagonist arc (Trunks' Future Trunks Saga; Pan's GT space-quest leadership; Gohan's Cell-Saga heir position; Goten's Buu-Saga junior protagonist role) extends from Goku's narrative-template and operates within his canonical sphere. The four Goku incarnations together canonicalize the franchise's four primary protagonist-relations: Childhood Goku adventures *toward* mastery (the Dragon Ball pre-Z apprentice arc); Z-era Goku defends Earth *at* mastery (the canonical adult-protagonist position); GT Goku continues *as* mastery in de-aged form (the Toei continuation's age-paradox premise); DBS Goku ascends *beyond* mastery (the god-tier opponents and multiversal-tournament Super-era scale).

The character's specific institutional features across the corpus are:

- **Saiyan lineage.** Goku is canonically a low-class Saiyan warrior born Kakarot to Bardock and Gine on Planet Vegeta, sent to Earth as an infant in a Saiyan attack pod shortly before the Saiyan homeworld's destruction by Frieza. The Saiyan lineage is the canonical mechanism by which Goku accesses Super Saiyan and beyond transformations; canonically he reaches SS, SS2, SS3, SS4 (GT only), Super Saiyan God, and Super Saiyan Blue (Super Saiyan God Super Saiyan) across the various series. His Saiyan-name Kakarot is canonically used by Vegeta, Raditz, and other Saiyan-side characters; his Earth-name Son Goku is canonically used by everyone else.
- **The Turtle School gi.** Goku's orange-and-blue martial arts gi is canonically the Turtle School uniform, passed down from his second master Muten Roshi (the Turtle Hermit) after Goku's first master Son Gohan dies in the *Dragon Ball* pre-Z arc. The gi is one of the most recognized character designs in anime history and is depicted across every corpus print of Goku in any incarnation (Childhood, GT, DBS adult). The Power Pole (Nyoibō) staff that Goku carries on his back in his Childhood incarnation is canonically a gift from his adoptive grandfather Son Gohan, also retained from *Dragon Ball* chapter 1 onward.
- **The Kamehameha.** Goku's canonical signature attack is the Kamehameha, a two-handed ki-blast technique originally taught to him by Muten Roshi in the *Dragon Ball* pre-Z arc. The Kamehameha has subsequently propagated across the entire Saiyan-and-Earthling protagonist roster (Gohan, Goten, Krillin, and others canonically learn it). Bandai's DBSCG print of named-attack cards canonically encodes Kamehameha as a Goku-anchored Extra card type; the corpus carries multiple Goku prints (Blazing Spirit BT4-005, Sneak Attack BT4-026, Pressure Assault BT3-058) that mechanically chain into Kamehameha-anchored Extra card payoffs in the GT-and-DBS-era red shell.
- **Goku's Lineage (Bandai's family-tree mechanic).** The Dragon Ball Super Card Game's BT4 Colossal Warfare set introduced `Goku's Lineage` as a designer-coordinated special trait, a card-game-mechanical encoding of the canonical Goku-family-tree that includes Bardock (Goku's Saiyan father), Goku himself (across all four named incarnations), Son Gohan (Goku's eldest son), Son Goten (Goku's youngest son), and Pan (Goku's granddaughter, Gohan's daughter). The corpus's BT4-079 Unbroken Dynasty Son Goku and BT4-078 Dependable Dynasty Son Goku are the two Goku-anchored entries in this lineage chain; the [Swap 2] / [Swap 3] keyword pair on the two cards is designed to chain the lineage-trait cards together as a tempo-escalation engine across the yellow BT4 deck-construction theme.
- **The four-series continuous-protagonist position.** Goku is the only Dragon Ball character whose canonical narrative presence spans every named series in the franchise. The franchise's branching protagonist-relations (Future Trunks' time-displaced position; Pan's GT-quest leadership; Gohan's Cell-Saga heir slot) all operate as *aspects of Goku's canonical sphere* — they protagonist-relate to Goku's continuous-narrative-anchor position rather than displacing it. The Bandai DBSCG print pattern across the corpus reflects this: Goku-anchored cards outnumber any other single-character anchor by a substantial margin (10 Goku prints in the enriched corpus alone, versus 9 Trunks prints across all incarnations).

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Goku is, canonically across Toriyama and Toei and Bandai, the franchise's most-instantiated continuous-protagonist character, present as four named incarnations in ten Dragon Ball Super Card Game prints in our enriched corpus across five sets (Cross Worlds BT3 + Colossal Warfare BT4 + World Martial Arts Tournament TB2 + Mythic Booster MB-01 + Magnificent Collection BE08 promo). A future Goku-themed Discrete Lair could anchor on the multi-incarnation framing without reaching past canon: the same canonical character rendered into four narrative-positions across forty years of franchise output, with the BBL corpus holding cards across all four.

## Caveats

- **One parent, four incarnations, no premature solo split (yet).** Following the [[trunks]] precedent (single character node with multi-incarnation roster covering Future / GT / Xeno / Kid incarnations under one parent), the [[tamiyo]] precedent (single character node with multi-aspect roster covering Moon Sage / Field Researcher / Collector of Tales / Inquisitive Student / Compleated Sage incarnations under one anchor), and the `nicol-bolas` precedent (single character node with multi-arc roster covering the Bolas-Falls cluster plus apparatus-of-Bolas aspects under one anchor), the canonical move is one parent Son Goku node anchoring all four incarnations. Son Goku: GT alone has 5 corpus cards — well past the standard solo-node threshold — but the canonical move is to keep all four incarnations under one parent until the corpus demands a split. A future split could partition the parent into `son-goku-childhood`, `son-goku-z`, `son-goku-gt`, `son-goku-dbs` solo sub-nodes if the corpus accumulates enough per-incarnation density (e.g., 8+ Z-era + 7+ GT + 5+ Childhood + 5+ DBS) to warrant the split. For now the parent holds; the per-aspect framing in the "Aspects in the corpus" section above does the disambiguation work that a future solo-sub-node split would formalize.

- **Bandai's character-tag field is the canonical per-print incarnation identifier.** Each Dragon Ball Super Card Game card carries an official character tag in Bandai's database that disambiguates which incarnation the print depicts. The four canonical tags for Goku are `Son Goku: Childhood` (the pre-Z Dragon Ball child, Power-Pole-wielding), `Son Goku` (the standard Z-and-DBS canonical adult continuation), `Son Goku: GT` (the Toei GT-era de-aged-child continuation), and `Son Goku: Future` (the DBS Future Trunks Saga adult-Goku-against-Goku-Black-and-Zamasu, distinct from the main-timeline `Son Goku` tag for set-thematic disambiguation). Cards in the corpus' `appears_on` carry the incarnation identifier in their trivia bullets where the trivia pass has run; future Goku-print acquisitions should preserve the Bandai-canonical tag in the card's trivia per the [[bbl-triviabot-no-role-identity-conflation]] memory rule. Misidentifying a `Son Goku: GT` print as a `Son Goku: Childhood` print (or vice versa) is a Mr. Nodeley-grade graph error — both incarnations depict a child-bodied Goku in an orange gi, but the GT incarnation is canonically an adult-mind-in-child-body distinct from the Childhood incarnation's chronological child.

- **GT child-body vs Childhood child: the canonical disambiguation.** Two of the four Goku incarnations canonically depict Goku at child-body scale: **Son Goku: Childhood** (the original Dragon Ball pre-Z child, chronologically a child) and **Son Goku: GT** (the Toei GT continuation where adult Goku is reverted to child body by Emperor Pilaf's accidental wish on the Black Star Dragon Balls, retaining all adult power and memory). Per the [[bbl-anti-confab-prompt]] memory rule's hair / age caution, vision-pass agents should NOT anchor incarnation identification on child-body scale alone. The canonical disambiguators are: **Childhood Goku** canonically carries the Power Pole (Nyoibō) staff and may depict the monkey-tail in earliest art; **GT Goku** canonically does not carry the Power Pole and never depicts a tail (the tail was canonically removed in the Dragon Ball pre-Z arc). The corpus's BT4-079 Unbroken Dynasty Son Goku (Power Pole present) is canonically `Son Goku: Childhood`; the corpus's BT3-006 Pint-sized Warrior Son Goku (no Power Pole, ki-blast pose, BT3's Black Star Dragon Ball Saga era stamp) is canonically `Son Goku: GT`. The trivia bullets on both prints carry the canonical Bandai character-tag confirmation.

- **The BT3-027 Unending Awakening qualification.** Wave 51 trivia refuted the initial vision-pass assumption that BT3-027 Unending Awakening canonically depicts Son Goku specifically; the card is canonically a *generic* Super Saiyan awakening archetype within the Cross Worlds BT3 Red color frame, not anchored to a specific named Saiyan. The BT3-027 card is therefore NOT in this node's `appears_on:` list. The exclusion is canonical per the wave-51 trivia verdict and should not be retroactively added; if a future re-trivia pass surfaces a canonical Goku-anchored framing for BT3-027, the addition would be a separate Edgelord-enlightened pass with an explicit replace-the-edge audit.

- **Trading Blows TB2-036 and Future Martial Artist TB2-052 are vision-passed but trivia-outstanding.** Both World Martial Arts Tournament TB2 corpus prints depict adult-body Goku per vision-pass IP confidence (high), but the trivia pass that will lock the exact Bandai character-tag and per-print era designation has not yet run on either card (their `## Trivia` sections are empty and `ip_verified: false` per current frontmatter). The node's `appears_on:` listing for both cards is provisional in the trivia-detail layer; the canonical Bandai character-tag for each will be added to the per-aspect description above when the trivia pass completes. Vision-pass IP confidence is sufficient for parent-node anchoring; the trivia-layer precision is a follow-up pass.

- **Visual identifier ambiguity: hair color and SS-form depiction.** Across Goku's four incarnations and his transformation states (base, SS, SS2, SS3, Super Saiyan Blue), the canonical hair color is rendered consistently per Toriyama-and-Toei art-direction conventions: base-form Goku has dark spiky black hair; Super Saiyan Goku has golden upright spiky hair; Super Saiyan 3 Goku has long golden flowing hair without forehead-eyebrow ridges; Super Saiyan Blue Goku has cyan-blue upright spiky hair (canonically depicted with a blue aura). The corpus's vision-pass agents have correctly identified SS form by the golden-upright-spiky-hair signal and SS Blue by the blue-aura signal; the Bandai DBSCG print-art is generally faithful to the Toei animation conventions and visual-identifier confusion is rare. Per the [[bbl-anti-confab-prompt]] memory rule's hair-color caution, however, mixed-aura prints like the corpus's SD2-03 Unbreakable Super Saiyan Son Goku (golden hair with layered blue energy) are intentional designer-team aesthetics within the canonical SS-and-beyond visual-continuum rather than mis-anchored color signals.

- **Goku's Lineage canonical family tree is in-graph cousin-cluster territory.** Goku is canonically the father of Son Gohan (Cell-Saga heir, eldest son) and Son Goten (Buu-Saga junior protagonist, youngest son); the grandfather of Pan (Gohan's daughter, GT-quest leadership); the husband of Chi-Chi (Earthling); and the son of Bardock and Gine (Saiyans, both canonically depicted in the *Bardock — Father of Goku* TV special and the *Dragon Ball Minus* manga chapter). The Bandai DBSCG `Goku's Lineage` trait card-game mechanic encodes this family tree. The [[son-gohan]] solo node was commissioned wave 72 once the corpus accumulated 7 trivia-passed Gohan anchors plus 1 vision-only anchor plus the dual-IP BT2-098 Father-Son Kamehameha technique-card; the [[pan]] solo node was already in-graph wave 49. The Goten / Bardock / Chi-Chi family-tree relations remain deferred pending corpus density: TB2-004 + TB2-022 Son Goten anchors are threshold-edge for a future `son-goten` node; Bardock has [[bardock]] in-graph already (related_characters), Gine is corpus-present at BT4-074 + BT3-087 but anchors to the [[saiyan]] hub rather than as a solo node; Chi-Chi remains corpus-absent except by reference. The Goku's-Lineage chain at the character-node layer is therefore: [[son-goku]] → [[son-gohan]] → [[pan]] (paternal-Saiyan-lineage canonical), with [[bardock]] as ancestor and the Goten / Gine / Chi-Chi relations surfacing at the card-trivia and hub-layer rather than as separate solo-character nodes.

- **Sibling continuity-strand nodes apply.** [[trunks]] is the direct co-protagonist relation for the GT-era incarnation — the Goku-Pan-Trunks traveling-trio is the canonical GT-canon party that recovers the Black Star Dragon Balls across the universe, and the corpus's BT3-007 Shocking Future Son Goku + BT3-011 Quick Rush Trunks pair anchors this co-protagonist relation across the Cross Worlds BT3 print. [[baby-dbgt]] is the direct adversarial relation for the GT-era Baby Saga incarnation — Baby is the Tuffle-parasite antagonist that Goku canonically fights through the Baby Saga in SS, SS3, and SS4 forms, and the corpus's BT4-005 Blazing Spirit Son Goku (era `Baby Saga`) is a canonical Goku-vs-Baby narrative anchor. Both relations are encoded in `related_characters:` and surface as cross-edges when a future Goku-themed bundle reaches Discrete Lair assembly. [[vegeta]] is the direct rival-and-eventual-ally relation for the Saiyan-Saga-onward continuity (live since the wave-E3 commission); the canonical foil-pair frames every Z-and-Super-era Goku narrative beat and resolves into the Buu-Saga Vegito Potara-Fusion combined-form per BT2-012 Repeated Force Vegito, the corpus's first Goku-and-Vegeta dual-anchored print.

- **The Vegito Potara-Fusion print's dual-anchor across [[vegeta]] and this node.** BT2-012 Repeated Force Vegito is the corpus's first Potara-Fusion-combined-form Vegito print; the canonical anchor pattern is dual-anchor with [[vegeta]] per the structural precedent that [[son-goten]] and [[trunks]] already use for the three Gotenks Fusion-Dance prints (BT1-071 / BT2-015 / BT4-034). The Vegito print's `characters: [son-goku, vegeta]` frontmatter wires both component-nodes at the card-layer; this node's `appears_on:` now carries BT2-012 as of this wave's mirror attach (completing the dual-anchor that the [[vegeta]] node opened in the wave-118 Vegeta-side attach). BT2-013 Lightning Speed Vegito exists in the corpus as a stub (empty `tags_hub`, no body content, prep-only frontmatter) and is parked until the vision-and-trivia passes land; it will attach to the same Vegito Potara-Fusion register at both nodes once enriched. A future `vegito.md` solo-node split could refactor the Potara-Fusion combined-character into its own anchor, with `related_characters: [son-goku, vegeta]` pointing back to the two component-nodes; deferred until corpus density of Vegito-prints alone warrants the split per the precedent applied to the parent solo-character nodes (the same deferral logic the Gotenks Fusion-Dance prints carry at [[son-goten]] and [[trunks]]).

- **Charizard-line / Mareep-line precedent for evolution-line nodes does NOT apply.** The Son Goku parent node is a *multi-incarnation single character* anchor following the [[trunks]] / [[tamiyo]] / `nicol-bolas` multi-aspect single-character pattern, not an *evolution line* (the Pokemon evolution-line nodes like [[charizard-line]], [[mareep-line]], [[staraptor-line]] anchor a designer-coordinated species line where each evolution stage is a canonically-distinct species; Goku is a single character whose four incarnations are not species-distinct, they are *continuity-strand-distinct*).

- **Future Discrete Lair candidate.** 10 corpus anchors across 4 named incarnations spanning 4 booster sets + 1 starter deck reprint set + 1 promo BE set form a viable cohesion stack for a Son Goku-themed Discrete Lair. Total inventory quantity across the 10 cards is **31** (BT3-006 qty 5, BT3-007 qty 4, BT3-058 qty 4, BT4-005 qty 2, BT4-026 qty 2, BT4-079 qty 2, BT4-078 qty 2, SD2-03 qty 4, TB2-036 qty 2, TB2-052 qty 2) — substantial multi-copy bundle pricing leverage per [[bbl-multi-copy-bundles]]. Bundle copy can lean on the multi-incarnation framing without reaching past canon: the same canonical character rendered into four narrative-positions across forty years of franchise output, the Kamehameha and Turtle School gi carrying across every incarnation, the Goku's Lineage family-tree mechanic providing in-game card-design cross-confirmation of the canonical family-tree relations. A Son Goku Discrete Lair would index alongside the [[trunks]] cohort (the GT-traveling-trio co-protagonist relation) and the [[baby-dbgt]] cohort (the GT-Baby-Saga adversarial relation) for narrative cross-pollination, and against a future vegeta-anchored cohort (the Saiyan-Saga-onward rival-and-eventual-ally relation) once that node lands.

# Alolan regional forms

A Game Freak-coordinated cohort of regional variant Pokemon introduced in Pokemon Sun and Moon (2016) — the **first** regional-form program in the Pokemon franchise. Each Alolan form is an Alola-region variant of an existing Kantonian Pokemon species: same species lineage, redesigned art, often a type-shift (Normal Rattata becoming Dark/Normal; Rock/Ground Geodude becoming Rock/Electric; Normal Meowth becoming Dark; Poison Grimer becoming Poison/Dark), and per-species in-canon adaptation lore explaining the regional divergence. The cohort comprises 18 canonical species: Rattata, Raticate, Raichu, Sandshrew, Sandslash, Vulpix, Ninetales, Diglett, Dugtrio, Meowth, Persian, Geodude, Graveler, Golem, Grimer, Muk, Exeggutor, and Marowak. The most cited mechanisms across the cohort are **Alola's tropical-island climate** (Alolan Vulpix / Ninetales gaining Ice-typing via Mount Lanakila adaptation; Alolan Sandshrew / Sandslash similarly Ice-shifted), **dietary divergence** (Alolan Geodude / Graveler / Golem feeding on dravite tourmaline mineral, generating Rock/Electric typing via electromagnetic crystal formation), **predator-prey rebalancing** (Alolan Rattata going nocturnal Dark-type in response to Yungoos diurnal predation introduced by humans; Alolan Marowak's Ghost typing emerging from grief-rituals around clubbed-down Cubone parents), and **institutional ecological introduction** (Alolan Grimer / Muk specifically brought to Alola by the regional government to consume urban waste).

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text for the Alolan regional-variant species across their TCG appearances. The unifying frame across the cohort is fourfold: (a) the **"Alolan" species-name prefix** enforced uniformly across all 18 variant species (Alolan Rattata, Alolan Raichu, Alolan Vulpix, Alolan Grimer, Alolan Graveler, Alolan Marowak, etc.), (b) the **regional-type-shift mechanic** where the Alola variant carries a different type combination than the Kantonian original-region form, (c) the **per-species adaptation lore** (climate exposure, dietary divergence, predator-prey rebalancing, institutional ecological introduction) presented in flavor text and Pokedex entries as the in-canon mechanism for the variant's existence, (d) the **Generation VII designer-coordination scope** — Alolan forms are a Sun & Moon-era cohort and are the *founding* program of the franchise's regional-variant mechanic, distinct from the subsequent Galarian (Sword & Shield, 2019), Hisuian (Legends: Arceus, 2022), and Paldean (Scarlet & Violet, 2022) cohorts, each of which has its own designer-coordinated framing.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):** 4 cards across 3 distinct Alolan-form species (Alolan Grimer + Alolan Graveler ×2 + Alolan Dugtrio), spanning four sets and four artists, with the cohort now clearing the 3-species threshold that the wave 23 commissioning anticipated.

- **Alolan Grimer** (Cosmic Eclipse 130/236, Common, Sekio art, IP-verified) — Poison/Dark-type regional variant of Kantonian Poison Grimer (National Pokedex no. 88). Squat round green-yellow sludge creature with white crystalline teeth, sitting placidly in an urban-refuse setting; muted earthy palette, comedic-mood, placid-and-unbothered expression. Flavor: *"There are a hundred or so of them living in Alola's waste-disposal site. They're all hard workers who eat a lot of trash."* The clearest in-corpus case for the cohort's **institutional ecological introduction** mechanism — the Alolan government deliberately brought Grimer to Alola to address urban garbage problems, maintaining populations of approximately 100 near waste-disposal sites as a recognized public-sanitation service. The green-yellow coloration vs. Kanto Grimer's purple results from consuming solid garbage rather than sludge; the crystalline white teeth are toxins crystallized from ingested non-degradable material. The species pair (Kanto Grimer endangered by industrial cleanup; Alolan Grimer institutionally-employed by remediation) occupies *opposite* ecological niches relative to environmental remediation — the franchise's most explicit designer-confirmed ecology-and-labor split across a single species line. Evolves into Alolan Muk (Poison/Dark, retaining the variant typing).
- **Alolan Graveler** (Crimson Invasion 33/111, Uncommon, kirisAki art, IP-verified) — Rock/Electric-type regional variant of Kantonian Rock/Ground Graveler (National Pokedex no. 75). Bulky quadrupedal rock creature with a dark blue-grey body studded with angular crystalline mineral outcroppings and a horseshoe-shaped magnetic brow protrusion; warm golden-yellow gradient backdrop, low-stance pose. Flavor: *"Its preferred food is dravite. After it has eaten this mineral, crystals form inside the Pokémon, rising to the surface of part of its body."* The clearest in-corpus case for the cohort's **dietary divergence** mechanism — the Alolan Geodude / Graveler / Golem line's Rock-to-Rock/Electric type shift is explained in-canon by consumption of dravite (a real-world tourmaline-group mineral), which causes crystals to form on the body and generates electrical charge. The design references a thunderegg (a volcanic geode with a crystalline interior), fitting Alola's volcanic-island ecology; the magnetic iron sand concentrated above the eyes — creating the characteristic thick brow — is a visual tie to the line's electromagnetic properties. Pre-evolves from Alolan Geodude, evolves into Alolan Golem-GX.
- **Alolan Graveler** (Guardians Rising 41/145, Uncommon, vision-passed wave 27, `ip_verified: false` pending triviabot validation) — second corpus print of the same species. Stages Alolan Graveler in a rocky mountain terrain with a forward-facing wide-stance action pose against grey stone backdrop; carries the **identical canonical flavor text** to the Crimson Invasion print (*"Its preferred food is dravite..."*), confirming the dietary-divergence framing as the species-level lore anchor across all Alolan Graveler TCG printings rather than a per-print authorial choice. The two prints together establish the species as the cohort's first multi-print in-corpus member at this node: the Crimson Invasion print is the warm-gradient portrait staging, the Guardians Rising print is the cool-stone action staging, and the shared flavor text canonicalizes the dravite-consumption mechanic across both depictions. Mirrors the cross-print mirror pattern established in the wave 26 Snubbull pair and the wave 24 Starly pair. Both prints share the [[33-111-alolan-graveler]] ↔ [[41-145-alolan-graveler]] 1:1 Connections edge.
- **Alolan Dugtrio** (Lost Thunder 123/214, Uncommon, Yuka Morii art, IP unverified pending triviabot validation) — Ground-type regional variant of Kantonian Ground Dugtrio (National Pokedex no. 51). Three rounded mole-like heads protrude from a rocky earthy mound in close formation, each crowned with a large flowing lock of **golden hair** — the key visual marker confirming the Alolan regional form rather than the standard Kantonian Dugtrio. The Yuka Morii **clay-sculpted rendering style** gives the figures a handmade tactile quality, distinct from the standard digital-illustration register and reading as a deliberate Alola-folk-craft aesthetic choice. Flavor: *"These Pokémon are cherished in the Alola region, where they are thought to be feminine deities of the land incarnate."* The clearest in-corpus case for the cohort's **cultural-religious adaptation** framing — the Alolan Dugtrio's flavor text canonicalizes the species as objects of regional folk-veneration, the trio cherished as deities of the land in Alolan tradition, a designer-confirmed cultural-belief framing distinct from the Alolan Grimer institutional-introduction mechanism and the Alolan Graveler dietary-divergence mechanism. The Alolan Dugtrio line's canonical type change (Kantonian Ground → Alolan Steel/Ground in the games; the TCG print here uses Fighting-type as the SM-era simplified mapping) and the long-golden-hair visual divergence from Kantonian Dugtrio's brown-fur design are the line's signature variant features. Pre-evolves from Alolan Diglett (not in corpus). The LOT-123 print is the cohort's **third distinct in-corpus Alolan-form species** (alongside Alolan Grimer and Alolan Graveler), cleanly clearing the 3-species threshold and adding a fourth canonical adaptation mechanism (cultural-religious folk-veneration) to the cohort's documented heterogeneity.

## The design thesis

The Alolan regional-forms cohort's most BBL-relevant property is that it is the **founding** program of the regional-variant mechanic — the originating designer-coordinated cohort that established the template later expanded across Galar (2019), Hisui (2022), and Paldea (2022). Sun and Moon (2016) introduced the entire conceptual move of "same species, different region, different design, different type" as a structural addition to the Pokemon canon, and the Alolan cohort's 18 species are the proof-of-concept that the franchise then re-instantiated three more times across the next six years. The Alolan in-canon adaptation framings are correspondingly *more pluralistic* than the later cohorts: where Galarian forms cite a small set of materially-specified mechanisms (Galarica seed, Galar martial culture, climate exposure), Alolan forms span a wider lore palette — climate adaptation (Ice-type Vulpix / Sandshrew), dietary divergence (Electric-type Geodude line), predator-prey rebalancing (Dark-type Rattata, Ghost-type Marowak), and **institutional ecological introduction** (Alolan Grimer's state-sanctioned urban-sanitation labor). The Alolan Grimer flavor text in particular — "a hundred or so living in Alola's waste-disposal site, hard workers who eat a lot of trash" — encodes the franchise's most explicit pollination of Pokemon-as-labor framing into a species' canonical Pokedex identity, where the Alolan variant exists *because* the regional government decided species-introduction was an environmental-policy lever.

The cohort also extends the Pokemon franchise's "region as character" thesis — Alola (modeled on Hawaii) becomes a setting whose specific environmental, cultural, and institutional features are visible *in the species themselves*. Alolan Rattata's Dark-type / nocturnal niche encodes human-introduced ecological disruption (Yungoos as introduced diurnal predator displacing Rattata into night-time activity); Alolan Marowak's Ghost typing encodes the grief-rituals of Cubone whose mothers were clubbed by humans for skull-helmet salvage; Alolan Grimer's Poison/Dark coloration encodes the food substrate of urban waste; Alolan Geodude / Graveler / Golem's Electric typing encodes the dravite-rich volcanic substrate of Alola's island chain. Each variant is the region's biographical fingerprint on the species line, written in adaptive divergence rather than in dialect.

## Caveats

- **Cohort members canonical at the species scope but not yet in BBL inventory.** Four corpus entries are now pointered to this node (Alolan Grimer CSE-130, Alolan Graveler CSI-33 + GUR-41, Alolan Dugtrio LOT-123) across **three distinct species** — cohort cleanly clears the 3-species threshold the wave 23 commissioning anticipated. The remainder of the 18-species cohort is documented but not yet acquired and will attach to this node when any TCG printing of them lands via a future Collectr CSV upload. Cohort membership predicates on the **Alolan species-name prefix + designer-attested regional-adaptation lore**, not on type-shift alone. Any TCG printing of these species across any set attaches to this node — membership predicates on species identity, not on set or printing.

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like `fear-of-cycle` (Duskmourn phobia-Nightmares), `dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle` (Duskmourn common dual-lands), `elemental-monkey-trio` (Unova Pansage/Pansear/Panpour line), and `galarian-regional-forms` (Galar variant cohort), this is a collective designer-coordinated cohort hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer already accepts collective named entities). A future `_cycles/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the other designer-confirmed cohort nodes; alternatively a future broader-scope `regional-forms-pokemon` parent node could host Alolan, Galarian, Hisuian, and Paldean as sibling child nodes. When that split is commissioned, this node moves cleanly. For now, single layer, same precedent.

- **The cohort is Generation VII-specific. Galarian / Hisuian / Paldean variants do NOT belong here.** Pokemon's regional-form program has four canonical cohorts: Alolan (Gen VII, Sun & Moon, 2016 — this node), Galarian (Gen VIII, Sword & Shield, 2019), Hisuian (Pokemon Legends: Arceus, 2022), Paldean (Gen IX, Scarlet & Violet, 2022). Each cohort is a separate designer-coordinated program with its own in-canon adaptation framing (Alolan: tropical-climate / dietary-divergence / institutional-introduction; Galarian: Galarica-seed-plus-Galar-cultural; Hisuian: ancient-Sinnoh-prehistoric; Paldean: Paldean-regional-cultural). The sibling [[galarian-regional-forms]] node is the canonical precedent for hosting a single-generation regional-form cohort; this node mirrors its shape at the Alola scope. Cohort membership predicates on the Alola-specific framing, not on the broader "regional variant" category.

- **The Alolan cohort is exclusively Kantonian-original-species variants.** All 18 Alolan-form species are regional variants of Kantonian (Generation I) Pokemon — no Johtonian, Hoennian, Sinnese, Unovan, or Kalosian species received Alolan variants. This is a designer-coordination distinct from later cohorts: Galarian forms include variants of Pokemon from multiple original regions (Kantonian Slowpoke, Unovan Darumaka, Kantonian Farfetch'd, Hoennian Zigzagoon, etc.); Hisuian and Paldean cohorts similarly span original-region scopes. Alolan's Kantonian-exclusivity is a designer thesis: the founding regional-form program staged the mechanic as "Generation I's flagship Pokemon re-interpreted by Alola's environment," establishing the conceptual proof-of-case before later cohorts expanded the cross-region scope.

- **Alolan forms vs Alola-region native species are not co-extensive.** Several Pokemon species are native to Alola without being regional variants — Yungoos, Gumshoos, Crabrawler, Drampa, Komala, Bruxish, Pyukumuku, the Tapu legendary guardians, etc. — and several Alola-introduced species are *evolutions* of Alolan-variant base forms rather than themselves being variants (Alolan Raticate evolves from Alolan Rattata; Alolan Persian from Alolan Meowth; Alolan Golem from Alolan Graveler). The node's `appears_on` is for species that are Alolan variants of an existing Kantonian original; Alolan-form evolutions of those variants count as cohort members (they continue the variant's lineage); Alola-native species without a Kantonian original do NOT belong here (those are simply Alola Pokemon).

- **Per-species adaptation mechanisms are heterogeneous.** The cohort's in-canon adaptation lore is more *pluralistic* than later cohorts: Alolan Vulpix / Ninetales / Sandshrew / Sandslash cite **climate adaptation** (Ice-typing emerged from Mount Lanakila exposure); Alolan Geodude / Graveler / Golem cite **dietary divergence** (dravite consumption generates Rock/Electric typing); Alolan Rattata / Raticate cite **predator-prey rebalancing** (Yungoos introduction drove the line into nocturnal Dark-typing); Alolan Marowak cites **grief-ritual adaptation** (Ghost-typing emerged from rituals around mother-Cubones killed by humans for skull-helmet salvage); Alolan Grimer / Muk cite **institutional ecological introduction** (Alolan government deliberately introduced the species for urban-waste-management); Alolan Diglett / Dugtrio cite **cultural-religious folk-veneration** (the line is cherished in Alolan tradition as feminine deities of the land incarnate, per the LOT-123 Alolan Dugtrio canonical flavor text; type-shifted to Steel/Ground in canon mainline games via Alolan ecological-and-cultural adaptation). The unifying frame is *designer-attested regional-adaptation lore*, of which these are now **six distinct species-clustered mechanisms** documented in the corpus. The cohort's heterogeneity is itself a designer thesis: Alola's variant program treats the regional-variant mechanic as a *bundle* of distinct adaptation mechanisms rather than a single unifying environmental driver — with the LOT-123 Alolan Dugtrio addition extending the documented adaptation-mechanism set to include the cultural-religious-folk-veneration axis alongside the existing five.

# Nylea, God of the Hunt

Theros's mono-color green god of the hunt, the wild forests, and the natural cycle of life and rebirth. Nylea is the pantheon's canonical Greek-mythology homage to Artemis — patron of centaur tribes, hunters, and the wild creatures of the forest (with snakes pointedly excepted, their patronage falling to Pharika per the Moss Viper flavor text). Her signature iconography is the longbow whose arrows "never fail to find their mark" (Plummet flavor); her canonical cult-operative class is the centaur shaman (Nylea's Huntmaster); her domain is the forest understood not as property but as commons that she *watches over* (Moss Viper flavor). Per the *Theros Pantheon* roster (MTG Wiki Pantheon of Theros page), Nylea is one of the twelve principal pantheon members and one of the five mono-color principal gods (alongside Heliod, Erebos, Thassa, Purphoros); she debuted as a printed card in original *Theros* (THS-172, 2013) and was reprinted across *Theros Beyond Death* (2020) and the *Theros Stargazing Secret Lair* series. Sub-node under [[theros-pantheon]] — Nylea is the pantheon's clearest single-god face of BBL's stewardship-of-the-commons reading of the Theros block, and the parent roster node carries the institutional context this sub-node refers back into.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that names Nylea directly in flavor or card name, depicts Nylea-cult operatives (centaur shamans, huntmasters, beast-pack rallies), stages Nylea's signature longbow iconography as the cause of a depicted hunt-result, or invokes Nylea as the canonical patron of forest creatures and the divine voice of the wild. The anchor inherits the parent [[theros-pantheon]] node's *named-god reference in card name or flavor OR depiction of a designer-confirmed god-cult operative class* predicate, narrowed here to Nylea specifically: Nylea-named cards (Nylea's Huntmaster), Nylea-direct-speech-flavor cards (Mantle of the Wolf's *"Be fearless, for a furious rebirth awaits you." — Nylea, god of the hunt*), Nylea-watches-over-the-forest flavor cards (Moss Viper's *"Nylea watches over all the creatures of the forest except for the snakes"*), Nylea-signature-bow flavor cards (Plummet's *"An arrow from Nylea's bow never fails to find its mark"*), Nylea-manifestation flavor cards (Inspire Awe's *"Nylea appeared amid the howl of a hundred beasts"*), and Nylea-as-canonical-pantheon-romance-figure flavor cards (Opaline Unicorn's *"Purphoros once loved Nylea, the god of the hunt"*).

The Nylea anchor inherits all the Theros-pantheon institutional context — devotion-as-labor-of-belief, designer-coordinated Greek-mythology homage, gods-sustained-by-mortal-cults — and overlays the *named goddess whose canonical domain is the commons-of-the-wild rather than its ownership*: where the parent pantheon node is the fifteen-god roster, this sub-node is the corpus's clearest single-god face of the stewardship-of-the-commons thesis, the structural complement to the [[mogis]] sub-node's apparatus-of-extraction face.

Oracle text uses devotion-to-green as the +X/+0 multiplier — the mechanical signature of green-god-devotion. The corpus's clearest single-card expression of Nylea's commons-of-the-wild theology: the huntmaster works inside the forest's natural cycle, not as its owner. Headline anchor for this sub-node.
- **Mantle of the Wolf** (THB-178, Rare, Simon Dominic art, vision-passed) — bare-chested muscled figure mid-transformation into a wolf within a dim green forest, surrounded by glowing magical filaments and gathering wolf-pack silhouettes; flavor text *"Be fearless, for a furious rebirth awaits you." — Nylea, god of the hunt.* Nylea speaks directly in first-person flavor attribution — the canonical pantheon-voice register, structurally parallel to a Wizards-confirmed in-canon speech-act. The "furious rebirth" framing canonicalizes Nylea's domain as the regenerative cycle of the wild (death-as-rebirth, hunter-becomes-the-hunt) rather than a static stewardship.
- **Moss Viper** (THB-179, Common, Mike Bierek art, vision-passed) — vivid green serpent coiled on a moss-covered branch with raised head and flicking tongue against a dim forest backdrop; flavor text *"Nylea watches over all the creatures of the forest except for the snakes. The linguistic register is explicit stewardship — Nylea *watches over* the creatures rather than owning them — which lands the [[stewardship]] hub's "thing tended belongs to more than the tender" discipline at the canonical-flavor-text level. The snakes-excepted clause also stages the pantheon-internal division-of-domains (Nylea: forest creatures; Pharika: serpents/poison) as Wizards-confirmed pantheon-design.
- **Plummet** (THB-194, Common, Bayard Wu art, vision-passed) — maroon-and-crimson winged beast tumbles downward through dappled forest canopy with an arrow visibly lodged in its torso, wings splayed against the green; flavor text *"An arrow from Nylea's bow never fails to find its mark."* Nylea's signature weapon (her bow) is canonical iconography per MTG Wiki's Nylea page; the card stages the result of her hunt in a single image (the falling beast IS the arrow's destination). The depicted-figure attribution-to-Nylea is the same evidence-tier as Skophos Warleader's burning-Mogis-temples on the [[mogis]] sub-node: flavor-text-and-canonical-iconography rather than name-in-card-title.
- **Inspire Awe** (THB-175, Common, Ekaterina Burmak art, vision-passed) — tall divine figure in flowing pale robes stands on a stone outcrop with both arms raised, cool blue-grey skin reading as Nyx-touched, surrounded below by a loose pack of beasts (centaur and hound silhouettes) in a wild forested clearing; flavor text *"Nylea appeared amid the howl of a hundred beasts."* Nylea-appearance flavor — the goddess manifests as a beast-pack avatar; the raised-arms-on-rocky-outcrop iconography aligns with canonical Nylea-manifestation imagery (per MTG Wiki Nylea page). The card depicts the canonical event the flavor names; vision pass tags include `divine-figure`, `raised-arms`, `rallying-pose`, consistent with the Nylea-appearance reading.
- **Opaline Unicorn** (THS-218, Common, Christine Choi art, vision-passed) — radiant gold-and-opalescent unicorn standing in a sun-dappled forest glade with horn lifted, sunlight scattering through the canopy; flavor text *"Purphoros once loved Nylea, the god of the hunt. His passion inspired his most astounding works of art."* Nylea named in flavor as the object of canonical pantheon-internal romance and the inspiration-source for Purphoros's smithwork; the depicted unicorn is canonically Purphoros's craftwork-inspired-by-Nylea (the opalescent metallic coat reads as smithwork-art per the flavor's framing). The only non-THB Nylea anchor in the corpus, the only one from original *Theros* (2013), and the lowest-tier anchor on this sub-node (Nylea named in flavor but the card's central figure is Purphoros-craftwork, not a Nylea-cult operative or Nylea-direct depiction). Borderline-tier, included via the parent node's *flavor-text-attribution-only entries are first-class node members* caveat.

## The Nylea thesis

Nylea is the Theros pantheon's most BBL-relevant member for the stewardship-of-the-commons reading of the [[stewardship]] hub. Where the broader pantheon's devotion-mechanic frames the *general* relationship of gods-sustained-by-mortal-labor and the [[mogis]] sub-node renders the *apparatus-of-extraction* face of that arrangement, Nylea's specific domain mechanizes the relationship as **commons-tending: the forest's creatures belong to themselves; the goddess watches over them rather than owning them; mortal devotion to Nylea is participation in the wild's regenerative cycle rather than tribute to a divine-owner.** This is the stewardship reading of pantheon-devotion in its sharpest single-god form on the plane — the constitutive counterweight to Mogis's blood-tithe-for-power doctrine at the same pantheon's roster.

The Nylea-cult's specific institutional features for the corpus are:

- **Commons-of-the-wild theology rendered in canonical flavor.** The Moss Viper (THB-179) flavor — *"Nylea watches over all the creatures of the forest except for the snakes"* — is BBL's clearest canonical Wizards-published attestation of the forest-as-commons register. The linguistic register is explicit: *watches over*, not *owns*, not *rules*. The snakes-excepted clause confirms the pantheon's designer-coordinated division-of-domains and locates Nylea's specific patronage on the non-property-claim side of the wild. This is the canonical-flavor-text grounding for the sub-node's anchoring against [[stewardship]] rather than [[labor]].
- **Furious rebirth as the regenerative-cycle doctrine.** The Mantle of the Wolf (THB-178) flavor — *"Be fearless, for a furious rebirth awaits you." — Nylea, god of the hunt* — directly attributes the rebirth-blessing to Nylea as the tender of the wild's death-and-renewal cycle. The card mechanically renders this as an aura that, on its enchanted creature's death, creates two 2/2 Wolf tokens — the canonical cycle (one wolf dies, two wolves rise) is rules-text encoded. The doctrine: in Nylea's domain, predation and rebirth are the same motion, and the hunter who becomes the hunt participates in the commons rather than extracts from it.
- **The longbow as canonical iconography.** Per MTG Wiki's Nylea page, the longbow whose *"arrows never fail to find their mark"* (Plummet flavor) is Nylea's signature divine implement, structurally parallel to Heliod's spear, Thassa's bident, Erebos's chains, and Mogis's ebon greataxe. The bow-arrow iconography canonicalizes any depicted-hunt-result as a Nylea-attribution (Plummet's falling winged beast; the centaur huntmaster's staff-or-bow implement in Nylea's Huntmaster's hand). Future cards depicting bow-archery against forest-creatures in Theros-block edge here directly.
- **Centaur shamans as canonical cult-operative class.** Theros's centaur race is canonically Nylea-aligned per MTG Wiki Nylea page and the Nylea's Huntmaster THB-187 card-name-and-art pairing. The centaur shaman is to Nylea what the minotaur priest is to Mogis: the designer-coordinated, cohort-level cult-operative figure whose recurring depiction across the block confirms the pantheon's institutional design. Future centaur-shaman cards from Theros-block printings edge here directly.
- **Greek-mythology homage with designer-confirmed mapping.** Nylea is Theros's Artemis — goddess of the hunt, the wild, and the moon/forest. Per MTG Wiki's Nylea page and Mark Rosewater's Theros design columns (2013-2014), the Artemis-homage is Wizards-confirmed design intent. The Artemis mapping locates Nylea on the same Greek-mythic register as Heliod (Apollo/sun), Thassa (Poseidon/sea), Erebos (Hades/underworld), Purphoros (Hephaestus/forge) — five of the principal mono-color gods mapping one-to-one onto the principal Olympians.

This is what BBL bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Nylea is the Theros pantheon's most stewardship-thesis-aligned god, and the corpus carries 6 anchors across 2 sets (Theros 2013, Theros Beyond Death 2020) — a depth equal to the [[mogis]] sub-node's 6-anchor stack and concentrated on a single deity's domain. A future Nylea-cult Discrete Lair — a *Commons of the Wild* parallel to the Orzhov *Tithe* or the prospective Mogis *Slaughter Tithe* — earns its lore-aware buyer credibility from Wizards's own decade of Nylea-cult flavor writing without naming the stewardship-of-the-commons thesis. The Nylea / Mogis pairing within the Theros pantheon (stewardship-of-commons / apparatus-of-extraction at single-god scope) is the corpus's cleanest within-pantheon thesis-contrast, both sub-nodes anchored to the same parent and pulling in opposite directions on the [[_triple-thesis]] meta-hub's axes.

## Caveats

- **Sub-node under [[theros-pantheon]], not standalone.** Nylea is one of fifteen Theros pantheon members; this sub-node inherits institutional context from the parent. Bundle work that anchors on Nylea should anchor on the [[theros-pantheon]] context simultaneously — the devotion-as-labor-of-belief reading at the pantheon-scope is what makes Nylea's specific stewardship-of-the-commons register legible as a designer-coordinated thesis-piece rather than a one-off forest-deity flavor.

- **No printed Nylea god-card in the corpus (yet).** Nylea, God of the Hunt (THS-172) is the canonical printed deity card and the parent-anchor for this entire cult, but it is NOT currently in BBL inventory. All six corpus anchors are cult-operatives, Nylea-flavor-text-attributed cards, or Nylea-canonical-iconography cards — Nylea the god herself does not appear as a printed card in our holdings. When (if) a Nylea god-card lands in inventory (THS-172 in any printing, or Theros Stargazing Secret Lair Nylea reprint, or Inspire Awe-style depiction confirmed as Nylea-direct), it becomes the headline anchor here and the existing six fall into place as supporting cult-evidence. Structurally parallel to the [[mogis]] sub-node's same gap.

- **The borderline anchor is Opaline Unicorn (THS-218).** Nylea is named in Opaline Unicorn's flavor text, but as the *object of Purphoros's love* in a pantheon-internal-romance backstory — not as the card's depicted figure or the card's narrative subject. The depicted unicorn is canonically Purphoros's craftwork-inspired-by-Nylea (a Hephaestus-makes-art-because-he-loves-Artemis register), not a Nylea-cult operative or a Nylea-domain creature. This is the same evidence-tier as Kragma Warcaller (THS-195) on the [[mogis]] sub-node: flavor-text-attribution via canonical-pantheon-relation rather than card-name-direct or cult-operative-depicted. The anchor is substantive (Nylea is named, and the card's whole flavor logic depends on the Nylea-Purphoros romance) but is the weakest of the six; future pruning passes may want to revisit whether Purphoros-craftwork-cards-with-Nylea-backstory-attribution belong at this sub-node or at the parent pantheon node (or at a future Purphoros sub-node when one becomes commission-ready). Current judgment: include, because the flavor explicitly names Nylea and the card's framing canonicalizes Nylea's role; the BBL graph treats flavor-text-named canonical attribution as first-class evidence per the parent node's *flavor-text-attribution-only entries are first-class node members* caveat.

- **Inspire Awe (THB-175) is depiction-likely but not depiction-confirmed.** The flavor text *"Nylea appeared amid the howl of a hundred beasts"* canonicalizes the depicted event AS a Nylea-manifestation; the vision pass tags (`divine-figure`, `raised-arms`, `rallying-pose`, `oracle`) align with canonical Nylea-manifestation imagery per MTG Wiki Nylea page. The depicted-figure is blue-skinned, robed, and surrounded by gathered beasts — consistent with but not explicitly confirmed as Nylea's mortal-plane manifestation. The MTG Wiki Nylea page describes her as a "lithe huntress" with green-tinted skin; some printings show her with cooler-toned skin. Treating this anchor as Nylea-depicted via flavor-event-attribution; if a future MTG Wiki page revision or designer comment confirms the figure is not Nylea (e.g. a different Nylea-aligned demigod), demote to flavor-text-only anchor. Current judgment: include as a strong anchor on the flavor-text-attribution-of-event evidence-tier.

- **Stewardship-of-the-commons reading is interpretive at the bundle-copy register; the canonical flavor-text is the watch-over-language.** The framing that Nylea-cult flavor *renders the commons-of-the-wild against ownership-of-land at the apparatus-of-stewardship register* is BBL's narrative-first analytical reading per `[[bbl-narrative-first-lairs]]` and `[[bbl-museum-curation-framing]]`, NOT a Wizards-design-stated thesis. What IS canonical: the Moss Viper flavor's specific *"Nylea watches over"* language as Wizards's published phrasing of her relationship to the forest's creatures. The watch-over register is at the canonical-flavor-text tier; the *commons-of-the-wild* and *apparatus-of-stewardship* framings are BBL bundle copy translating the canonical phrasing into the project's brand voice. Same Edgelord-discipline split as the parent [[theros-pantheon]] node's labor-of-belief framing and the [[mogis]] sub-node's apparatus-of-extraction framing — the Potion-rule discipline holds: canonical Wizards phrasing in the body, BBL curatorial overlay named-as-overlay.

- **Future twin-edge available: Nylea ↔ Mogis as the pantheon's stewardship/extraction pair.** Both sub-nodes now exist under [[theros-pantheon]]; both have 6 corpus anchors; both anchor one of the [[_triple-thesis]] meta-hub's axes (Nylea→stewardship, Mogis→labor-as-extraction). A future character-to-character edge between [[nylea]] and [[mogis]] is available when a bundle commission requires the within-pantheon thesis-contrast — same plane, same fifteen-god roster, designer-coordinated to pull in opposite directions on the divine-relation-to-mortal-effort axis. Defer the explicit edge until a bundle-assembly pass needs the contrast; the parent [[theros-pantheon]] node currently carries the contrast at the pantheon-scope, which is sufficient for non-bundle work.

- **Apparatus-of-stewardship-via-care-costume register is available but deferred.** The Heliod-betrays-Elspeth turn in THB (the patron-god-who-tries-to-murder-his-paladin) renders the *apparatus-of-extraction-wearing-stewardship-costume* register on the same pantheon; in principle the Nylea-cult flavor can be read for the same costume-of-care pattern (Nylea watches over the forest but the watching IS the dominion; "I tend you" can shade into "I own you"). The corpus's current Nylea anchors do NOT carry this register at the canonical flavor level — every Nylea-cult flavor in inventory reads cleanly as care-without-ownership. Treating Nylea here as the clean-stewardship pole of the [[stewardship]] hub and Heliod (when he gets a sub-node) as the corrupted-stewardship pole. If future cards land that complicate Nylea's care-without-ownership (e.g. a "Nylea claims the kill" flavor card), revisit this caveat.

- **Bundle-anchor potential is strong.** 6 corpus anchors across 2 sets (Theros 2013, Theros Beyond Death 2020) form a deep single-cult cohesion stack — equal in depth to the [[mogis]] sub-node's 6-anchor stack and concentrated on a single deity. A future Nylea-cult Discrete Lair is one of the corpus's strongest single-character bundle candidates by both depth (6 anchors) and thesis-cohesion (every card carries the stewardship-of-the-wild register at the canonical-flavor or oracle-text level). Reserve for when the Theros-block subarchetype gets its commission; the [[theros-pantheon]] parent already pre-stages this as wave-53+ revisit material in its own Caveats section.

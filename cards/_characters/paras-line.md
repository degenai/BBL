# Paras evolutionary line (Paras / Parasect)

A two-stage Bug/Grass evolutionary line and the canonical Kanto-region mushroom-symbiote line introduced in Pokemon Red and Green / Red and Blue (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 1996). The line comprises **Paras** (National Pokedex no. 46, Bug/Grass-type, base form, the "Mushroom Pokemon" depicted as a small orange crab-insect with two tochukaso mushrooms growing from its back) and **Parasect** (no. 47, Bug/Grass-type, Stage 1, evolves from Paras at level 24, also the "Mushroom Pokemon", depicted as a larger crab-host whose body is overtaken by a single dominant mushroom cap). The line is the franchise's canonical Pokemon-rendering of the real-world Cordyceps parasitic-fungus lifecycle — the Japanese name for the mushrooms growing on the species is **tochukaso** (冬虫夏草, "winter worm, summer grass"), the standard Japanese term for the *Cordyceps sinensis* parasitic fungus that infects insect hosts in the wild and grows fruiting bodies through their cuticle. The line's Pokedex canon explicitly stages the parasite-host relationship as a body-horror progression: at Paras the mushrooms are passengers stealing nutrients from a still-living host; at Parasect the mushroom has consumed the host and is operating the dead body as a vehicle. Printed flavor text on the Generation I and modern prints alike confirms the relationship — the Bulbapedia-documented Pokedex line "The bug is mostly dead, with the mushroom on its back having become the main body. If the mushroom comes off, the bug stops moving" is printed verbatim on Parasect Darkness Ablaze 004/189.

## The design thesis

The Paras line's most BBL-relevant property is the **Cordyceps body-horror design conceit** rendered as a two-stage parasite-host symbiote progression, paired with the **mushroom-overtakes-host narrative arc** as the line's structural signature. Where the Drilbur line carries a creature-as-drill canon and the Larvitar line carries a consume-the-mountain-become-the-mountain canon and the Caterpie line carries a fast-metamorphosis-lifecycle-as-evolution canon, the Paras line carries a **the-host-is-already-dead canon** — Paras canonically functions as the still-living crab-insect host whose body is being parasitized by the tochukaso mushrooms (the mushrooms steal nutrients, control behavior, but the bug retains agency); Parasect canonically functions as the post-parasitic-completion stage where the host bug has been functionally consumed and the mushroom is operating the corpse as a vehicle (the bug is "mostly dead", the mushroom is "the main body"). The line's evolution arc is designer-coordinated as a single body-horror design conceit rendered at two stages, with the host-to-symbiote ratio inverting at evolution — the mushroom growing from secondary passenger at base to dominant controlling organism at apex, the host-creature visibly shrinking under the mushroom's mass.

The line is the **franchise's earliest explicit body-horror design and predates The Last of Us by over 25 years**. The Parasect DAB-004 corpus trivia pass canonicalizes this reading: the species was designed in 1996 around the real-world *Cordyceps sinensis* parasitic fungus (Japanese: tochukaso), which infects insects in the wild and grows fruiting bodies through their cuticles after taking over the host's nervous system. The 2013 video game *The Last of Us* (Naughty Dog) and the 2023 HBO series adaptation brought Cordyceps-zombie fungus into mainstream horror, but Parasect's Cordyceps-based design predates that cultural moment by 17-25 years depending on which mainstream-recognition watershed is used as the comparator. The franchise's first body-horror design is on the original 151, sitting at Pokedex slot 47, with the printed flavor text on the corpus's Parasect DAB-004 stating the host's death directly — "The bug is mostly dead" — and on the corpus's other three prints staging the various intermediate phases of the parasitic-completion progression (mushrooms "controlling the bug" on Paras DAB-003, mushrooms "stealing away most of the nutrients" on Paras SMB-4, the "large mushroom controls it" on Parasect SMB-5).

For BBL bundle work, the line offers three strong narrative threads: a **Cordyceps body-horror** thread anchored on the species' Pokedex canon and the franchise's earliest body-horror design (the four corpus prints' printed flavor collectively stages the parasite-host progression from "controlling the bug" through "stealing nutrients" through "large mushroom controls it" to "the bug is mostly dead", a four-stage textual progression rendered across two species stages), a **horror-cute** register thread anchored on the line's visual contrast (Paras's cute-pet register against Parasect's zombie-vehicle register, with the dark-cave SMB-5 print as the corpus's most pronounced body-horror visual example and the sunlit-autumn DAB-003 print as the corpus's most pronounced cute-register example — the within-corpus designer-illustration-register contrast operates at single-species scope), and a **predates-The-Last-of-Us** thread anchored on the franchise-history-and-cultural-context level (Parasect's 1996 Cordyceps-design predating Naughty Dog's 2013 The Last of Us release by 17 years and HBO's 2023 series adaptation by 27 years, the franchise-history-scope reading that bundle copy can deploy at the body-horror-canon scope). The within-Darkness-Ablaze Paras 003 + Parasect 004 adjacent-collector-number pair is the corpus's strongest within-set Paras-line cohesion stack — both base and evolved stages in a single set at adjacent slots — structurally parallel to the within-Vivid-Voltage Drilbur 091 + Excadrill 122 pair (Drilbur line) and the within-Darkness-Ablaze Larvitar 086 + Pupitar 087 pair (Larvitar line).

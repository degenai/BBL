# Eldrazi

Magic: The Gathering's signature cosmic-horror antagonist brood. Per MTG Wiki's Eldrazi page, the Eldrazi are eldritch alien titans native to the Blind Eternities — the space *between* planes in the MTG multiverse — first introduced in *Rise of the Eldrazi* (ROE, 2010) as the world-eating threat that the Lithomancer Nahiri, the planeswalker Sorin Markov, and the dragon planeswalker Ugin sealed inside the plane of Zendikar at the dawn of recorded multiversal history. Their re-emergence is the canonical anchor of the *Battle for Zendikar* / *Oath of the Gatewatch* two-set block (BFZ + OGW, 2015–2016), the Wizards-of-the-Coast story arc that introduced the Gatewatch protagonist coalition (Jace, Gideon, Nissa, Chandra, Kiora) and concluded with the destruction of two of the three Titans on Zendikar.

The three named **Eldrazi Titans** are the brood's apex entities:

- **Emrakul, the Promised End** — pink-white, vast-winged, the most ascended in scope; later relocated to Innistrad and sealed inside the moon during *Eldritch Moon* (EMN, 2016).
- **Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger** — bone-white, multi-limbed, the consumption-titan; destroyed on Zendikar during the BFZ block.
- **Kozilek, the Butcher of Truth** — twisted geometric, mind-warping, the reality-distortion titan; destroyed on Zendikar during the BFZ block.

Below the Titans, the brood is organized across two creature-card subtypes and two token-only subtypes — together the **brood-lineage feeding apparatus** beneath the Titan tier. The two **creature-card subtypes** are **Drone** (`t:drone` returns 71 cards across multiple sets including BFZ, OGW, ROE, MH3, M3C — the brood's specialized worker / instrument tier, with each Drone functioning as a Titan-extended limb rather than as an autonomous brood entity) and **Processor** (`t:processor` returns 11 cards, BFZ-block almost exclusively — the brood's apparatus for consuming the exile zone that Ingest creates, closing the BFZ-block design loop where Ingest-bearing Drones exile cards and Processor-typed creatures spend them for their abilities; Wasteland Strangler is the canonical example). The two **token-only subtypes** are **Spawn** (introduced in Rise of the Eldrazi 2010 — 0/1 colorless tokens that sacrifice for `{1}`, generated by ROE-block cards) and **Scion** (introduced in BFZ 2015 — 1/1 colorless tokens that sacrifice for `{C}`, generated by BFZ-block cards including Adverse Conditions and Eldrazi Skyspawner); per Scryfall, no creature card carries a `Scion` or `Spawn` type-line — both tiers exist only as tokens generated by Drone-tier or Processor-tier creature cards, not as independent creature cards. The brood-lineage taxonomy is therefore *Titan → Drone / Processor (creature cards) → Scion / Spawn (tokens)*, with each tier smaller and more numerous than the one above, swarming planes as the apparatus of the Titans' consumption. The Eldrazi aesthetic register — bone-white pallid flesh, tentacled appendages, multi-mouthed maws, geometric-fractal anatomy, scale-defying proportions, alien physiology that reads as wrong-at-a-glance — is one of Magic's most recognizable visual-design vocabularies and the canonical reference for cosmic-horror illustration in the TCG medium.

## What this node anchors

Card art that depicts Eldrazi creatures of any tier — Titans, Drones, Scions, Spawn, brood-aligned biomechanical horrors, brood-corrupted environments and artifacts. Also: cards whose narrative beat *centers* the Eldrazi as the off-frame threat (defensive postures against Eldrazi invasion, refugee scenes citing the Eldrazi by name in flavor text, instants and sorceries that cite Eldrazi mechanically in oracle text). The character is broad enough that it functions structurally as a faction-collective node — like [[phyrexia]] on the Magic side — rather than as a single named entity. Future sub-nodes (`emrakul`, `ulamog`, `kozilek`) are warranted only if the corpus accumulates enough Titan-specific cards to require disambiguation; for now, the brood anchors at the collective.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Encircling Fissure** (BFZ-23, uncommon, Igor Kieryluk art) — an Eldrazi creature with multiple tendrils atop a rocky spire in a Zendikar canyon landscape, pale-pink and white against tan rock. Mechanically a {2}{W} instant with Awaken 2 — the canonical mid-Eldrazi-invasion combat-prevention spell scaled up to the BFZ Awaken mechanic. The card depicts the Eldrazi-as-canyon-presence register: the brood breaking through the surface of Zendikar's terrain.
- **Fortified Rampart** (BFZ-27, common, David Gaillet art) — a tall wooden palisade wall in Zendikar's refuge defenses, a small Eldrazi scion at its base and a massive Eldrazi silhouette looming in foggy background. Mechanically a {1}{W} 0/6 Wall with Defender — the canonical BFZ "hold the line against the invasion" common. Flavor text: *"The refuge's defenses allow new recruits to see lesser Eldrazi up close, steeling their stomachs for what's to come."* The card depicts the scale gradient of the brood — Scion-tier in the foreground, Titan-tier silhouetted behind — exactly the way the BFZ set's combat structure works mechanically.
- **Smite the Monstrous** (BFZ-49, common, Jakub Kasper art) — a massive Eldrazi titan with exposed red musculature, near-eyeless head, and a cluster of tentacle-like neck appendages is being grappled by small kor warriors using ropes and cables in a Zendikar desert canyon. Mechanically a {3}{W} instant: destroy target creature with power 4 or greater — the canonical mid-BFZ anti-Titan removal scaled to the size of the threat. Flavor text: *"After watching the kor in battle, Gideon understood the difference between working together and working as one."* — Gideon Jura is the off-frame speaker, the kor are the depicted protagonists, and the depicted Eldrazi is the *target* of the spell rather than its agent. The card is the corpus's clearest single-frame render of BFZ's signature kor-versus-Eldrazi adversary structure: an exposed-musculature Titan rendered as the body-horror centerpiece against which the kor's rope-and-cable climbing rigs are re-tooled into anti-Titan combat instruments. The card dual-anchors at this node (the depicted Titan) and at [[kor]] (the depicted attackers); it is one of the rare BFZ commons where both sides of the conflict appear in the same frame at recognizable scale.
- **Depth Defiler** (MH3-58, common, Lie Setiawan art) — a deep-sea Eldrazi creature with tentacles and fleshy horror anatomy, bone-white against ocean abyss. Modern Horizons 3's heavy Eldrazi-throwback brief renders the brood as an ocean-depths threat in this print.

The card is therefore an Eldrazi-corruption of the Snake creature type, with the coatl iconography carrying the Mesoamerican-feathered-serpent flavor on the art-and-name side. `[Scryfall: MH3-64 type_line "Creature — Eldrazi Snake"; Scryfall: search t:coatl returns zero results, confirming non-existence of Coatl as a creature type]`

Mechanically a colorless artifact that creates 0/1 Eldrazi Spawn tokens and accumulates +1/+1 counters when other Eldrazi die — the card IS the brood's death-cult apparatus rendered as a mana rock that escalates into an annihilator-bearing creature.
- **Eldrazi Repurposer** (MH3-150, common, devoid {2}{G}) — a pale segmented Eldrazi drone with elongated angular head and segmented insectile limbs in a forest understory. Oracle text creates Eldrazi Spawn tokens on ETB and on death. The card's name IS the brood's operational verb (repurpose: the conversion of biological matter into Spawn).
- **Titans' Vanguard** (MH3-206, uncommon) — an Eldrazi ruin-haunting golem with tentacled-construct anatomy. The card's name names the Titans directly; this is a Drone-tier or Vanguard-tier brood member, presumably preceding a Titan-tier card in the same set's Eldrazi-throwback cycle.
- **Wastescape Battlemage** (MH3-17, uncommon) — Eldrazi-adjacent canyon predator depicted as a colorless devoid creature. The "Eldrazi-adjacent" register in the tags_hub reflects the card's depiction of a brood-coded creature without explicit Titan-or-Drone subtyping; mechanically devoid, narratively brood-aligned.
- **Twisted Riddlekeeper** (MH3-14, uncommon) — a winged eldrazi-sphinx hybrid with gold medallion and labyrinth iconography. The emerge keyword is the canonical Eldritch Moon mechanic for Eldrazi-corrupted creatures; the riddlekeeper IS the brood corrupting the sphinx creature-type.
- **Territory Culler** (MH3-173, uncommon, Tiffany Turrill art) — a long skeletal-organic alien with an exposed ribcage and trailing tendril-filaments clinging to gnarled tree branches against a pale sky scattered with floating rock formations. Mechanically a {4}{G} 7/5 Reach Eldrazi with Devoid and a Landfall trigger that top-decks for creatures — *the only Eldrazi creature in MTG history to carry the Landfall keyword*, a designer-coordinated callback to the Zendikar imprisonment plane printed eight years after the BFZ block as part of MH3's Eldrazi-resurgence brief. The Landfall-keyword conjunction makes this the cohort's clearest single-card anchor for the *brood-as-Zendikar-throwback-design* register: Landfall is the Zendikar-block signature mechanic; Devoid is the BFZ-block signature mechanic; this card is Wizards' first willing intersection of the two.
- **Wumpus Aberration** (MH3-176, uncommon, Filip Burburan art) — a colossal pink-and-purple tentacled monstrosity dwarfs a lone humanoid in a barren grey dead forest. Mechanically a {3}{G} 6/6 Trample Eldrazi Beast with Devoid; the canonical Wumpus drawback ("opponent may put a creature card from their hand onto the battlefield") is preserved verbatim from the lineage but gated behind a `{C}`-spent escape hatch — the MH3 Eldrazi-resurgence brief's signature design lever for converting non-Eldrazi creature lineages into the brood. The card extends the cohort's *brood-corruption-of-existing-subtypes* register at the **mechanical-lineage** level rather than the type-line level: Wumpus Aberration is type-line *Eldrazi Beast* (Beast being MTG's most generic catch-all creature type, not the Wumpus subtype — there is no "Eldrazi Wumpus" type), but it inherits the Wumpus-drawback oracle text directly from Hunted Wumpus MMQ-253 (1999) and Shivan Wumpus PLC-137 (2007), making it the third Wumpus-pattern card in MTG history and the first to carry both Eldrazi typing AND devoid coloring. Vision-pass surfaced the brood iconography indirectly (tentacles, eldritch, horror) without naming Eldrazi by subtype; the Trivia-pass lineage continuity is the load-bearing anchor here, not the art-side iconography read.
- **Horrific Assault** (MH3-157, common, Justine Jones art) — a robed kneeling figure mid-transformation with tendrils and body-horror corruption, in a forest setting. Flavor text: *"With shifting forms and no angel to pray to, Innistrad's werewolves were especially susceptible to Emrakul's influence."* The card directly cites the Eldritch Moon arc — Emrakul (the third and final Titan) trapped inside Innistrad's moon and corrupting the plane's native werewolves into Eldrazi-twisted forms. Oracle text gives a life-gain trigger "if you control an Eldrazi," anchoring the card mechanically to the brood as well as narratively.
- **Kozilek's Sentinel** (BFZ-129, common, Raymond Swanland art) — a close-up of a massive Eldrazi drone pressing through a curved rocky tunnel, heavily segmented dark exoskeleton in deep black and dark olive with multiple glowing amber-yellow eyes distributed asymmetrically across its body and appendages. Mechanically a {1}{R} Eldrazi Drone with Devoid and a colorless-spell pump trigger. Named for Kozilek the Butcher of Truth — one of the three Eldrazi Titans whose brood lineage includes drone-tier servants like this one. Flavor text: *"It moves through the tunnels as slowly and surely as the shifting stones."* — frames the Drone as geological inevitability rather than a rushing attacker, the brood-tier worker-caste version of the consumption-as-metabolism thesis. Devoid encodes the brood's mana-identity-stripping cosmologically: the card has no color despite a red mana cost, mirroring the Titans' consumption of Zendikar's leyline-mana. `[Scryfall: BFZ-129 type_line, oracle_text, flavor_text]`
- **Immobilizer Eldrazi** (OGW-97, uncommon, Svetlin Velinov art) — a twin-bodied manta-ray-like Eldrazi hovers over collapsed armored humanoid figures on an arid desert plain, eroded rock spires in the distance. Mechanically a {1}{R} Eldrazi with Devoid and an activated ability ({2}{C}: each creature with toughness greater than its power can't block this turn) — one of the first cards in Magic history to use the {C} colorless-mana symbol introduced in OGW. Flavor text: *"It denied the Zendikari the chance to stand and die fighting."* — the framing is specifically removal of agency: not death but deprivation, consistent with Wizards' canonical metabolic-indifference framing (the Eldrazi process life the way a stomach processes food). The composition renders the labor-relation directly: Eldrazi overhead, Zendikari kneeling below. `[Scryfall: OGW-97 flavor_text, oracle_text]` `[magic.wizards.com/en/news/feature/oath-gatewatch-mechanics-2015-12-28]`

## The functional ideology

The Eldrazi's BBL-relevant property differs from [[phyrexia]]'s but rhymes structurally: where Phyrexia organizes around the *conversion of biology into apparatus* (compleation as religious obligation), the Eldrazi organize around the *consumption of planes as nutrition* (worlds as food, hedrons and ley-lines as cooking apparatus). The faction's defining features per MTG Wiki and the Battle for Zendikar / Eldritch Moon design articles:

- **The brood is alien on a cosmological scale, not merely a political one.** Phyrexia is a faction inside the multiverse; the Eldrazi are *from outside it*, native to the Blind Eternities between planes. Their existence violates the planar order rather than competing within it. This is the most BBL-relevant register the Eldrazi carry: the apparatus of consumption that is structurally exterior to any plane's own institutional logic, against which there is no internal political appeal.

- **Consumption is metabolism, not malice.** Per Wizards' BFZ story output, the Eldrazi Titans do not hate Zendikar — they eat it the way a herd of cattle eats grass. The brood's apparatus of extraction is rendered as a *biological function* rather than a moral choice. This rhetorical move — the destruction of a world rendered as a feeding pattern — is what makes the Eldrazi the canonical reference for cosmic-horror illustration in the TCG medium. There is no negotiation with metabolism.

- **Scale gradient as design signature.** The brood descends through canonical tiers as documented in the "What this node anchors" body above — Titan → Drone / Processor (creature-card subtypes) → Scion / Spawn (token-only subtypes) — each tier smaller and more numerous than the one above. BFZ's mechanical structure renders this gradient as a Limited-archetype pyramid: Spawn and Scion tokens at the base of the curve, mid-sized Drone-tier and Processor-tier creature cards in the middle, Titans at the top. Drone-tier cards typically carry Ingest (exile-from-library combat damage triggers, e.g. Benthic Infiltrator BFZ-55) or generate Scion / Spawn tokens (e.g. Eldrazi Skyspawner BFZ-58). Processor-tier cards consume the exile zone Ingest creates (e.g. Cryptic Cruiser BFZ-56 spends an exiled-card permission cost to tap a creature; Wasteland Strangler eats an exiled card on ETB) — the BFZ-block design loop is *Drone Ingest → exile accumulates → Processor consumes exiled material for power*. Fortified Rampart (BFZ-27) depicts the scale gradient at the visual level: scion-tier in the foreground, Titan-silhouette in the background, the rampart between.

- **Brood-corruption as Eldritch Moon's mechanical signature.** The *Eldritch Moon* arc (2016) renders Emrakul's corruption of Innistrad's native population — humans, werewolves, vampires, geists — as a body-horror conversion arc rather than a planar consumption arc. The Emerge keyword (a creature is cast from inside another creature's body, sacrificing the host) is the canonical Eldrazi-corruption mechanic. Horrific Assault (MH3-157) is the corpus's clearest anchor for this arc.

- **Modern Horizons 3 (2024) is a designer-coordinated Eldrazi-resurgence brief.** Per Wizards' MH3 design articles, the set was art-directed to render the brood as a returning threat — eight years after the BFZ block concluded — with new Drone, Scion, and emerge-keyword printings calibrated for the Modern format. The corpus's eleven MH3 Eldrazi anchors are all post-BFZ-revival illustrations under that design brief, including Territory Culler (MH3-173) as the only Eldrazi creature in MTG history to carry the Landfall keyword — a deliberate Zendikar-imprisonment-plane callback inside the resurgence-brief printing run, and a designer-coordinated *brood-corruption-of-existing-creature-subtypes* mini-cohort visible across four MH3 prints in corpus: Twisted Riddlekeeper MH3-14 (Eldrazi Sphinx), Hope-Ender Coatl MH3-64 (Eldrazi Snake, with the coatl-name-and-art carrying the Mesoamerican feathered-serpent flavor over the Snake type-line), Snapping Voidcraw MH3-204 (Eldrazi Turtle — per its own trivia, the only Eldrazi Turtle in MTG history), and Wumpus Aberration MH3-176 (Eldrazi Beast at the type-line level, but inheriting the Wumpus drawback oracle text from Hunted Wumpus 1999 and Shivan Wumpus 2007 — a mechanical-lineage corruption rather than a type-line one). Note that this is a body-prose pattern observed across four prints, not a separately-graphed primitive — per the Potion sub-rule against promoting recurring patterns to first-class graph objects, the mini-cohort is documented here in the canonical-source-of-truth node body rather than spun out as a sub-node.

This is what BBL bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: the Eldrazi are, in-universe, a designer-confirmed cosmic-horror brood organized around the consumption-of-planes thesis, with a thirty-year-of-publication-but-fifteen-years-of-cohesive-printing history of escalating cosmic-scale-body-horror illustration. Bundle prose that names the Eldrazi by name earns lore-aware buyer credibility and lets Wizards' own block-design work do the thesis work.

## Caveats

- **Eldrazi as creature-type vs. Eldrazi as faction-membership are correlated but not co-extensive.** Some non-faction creatures carry depictions in Eldrazi-corrupted form (Hope-Ender Coatl is canonically a coatl creature under Eldrazi-corruption; Horrific Assault's depicted werewolf is mid-transformation into Eldrazi-twisted form). Some faction-aligned cards do not carry the Eldrazi creature type (defensive spells, anti-Eldrazi instants like Encircling Fissure whose oracle text references the brood mechanically). Membership predicates on canonical faction-alignment per Wizards' story output and on art-depiction, not subtype alone. Scryfall's `t:eldrazi` is the cleanest mechanical-tier classifier; depiction of brood iconography in art (tentacled bone-white anatomy, scion-tier silhouettes, multi-mouthed maws) is the cleanest visual-tier classifier.

- **The three Titans are storyline-distinct sub-entities and warrant sub-nodes if multiple Titan-specific cards accumulate.** Emrakul, Ulamog, and Kozilek are each load-bearing characters at the brood's storyline-arc scope. Same precedent as the Phyrexian Praetors caveat on [[phyrexia]] — sub-character nodes warranted at sufficient corpus accumulation, deferred until then. Current corpus carries Emrakul as flavor-text citation (Horrific Assault) but no Titan-card-depicting anchors; threshold below sub-node creation.

- **Hedrons are a load-bearing Zendikar iconographic primitive but a distinct concept from the Eldrazi themselves.** The floating triangular-prism stones that the Lithomancer Nahiri carved to imprison the brood are visible across BFZ-block card art as Zendikar's planar-defense apparatus. Hedrons are *anti-Eldrazi* iconography, not Eldrazi iconography — they belong on Zendikar's side of the conflict. If a future symbol-layer node anchors the hedron, it would edge here as an *adversary* iconography rather than as a brood-iconography asset. For now, no hedron symbol node is commissioned; hedrons recorded as Vision-pass iconography on Zendikar-side cards where they appear.

- **The Gatewatch are the protagonist-coalition that opposes the Eldrazi in BFZ, not part of this node.** Jace, Gideon, Nissa, Chandra, and Kiora form the protagonist side of the BFZ-block conflict; each has its own character-layer node ([[jace-beleren]], [[chandra-nalaar]], [[kiora]] already commissioned; Gideon and Nissa pending threshold per the wave 38 / wave 40 triage). The Eldrazi node and the Gatewatch character nodes share a load-bearing canonical edge: each Gatewatch member's BFZ-block printings depict the protagonist-side of the same invasion arc this node anchors. Kiora is the canonical example — her *Master of the Depths* / *Behemoth Beckoner* printings are mid-Eldrazi-crisis Zendikar Kiora.

- **The Kor are the Zendikar-native faction that fights the Eldrazi alongside the Gatewatch, and form a sibling adversary-coupling node.** See [[kor]]. The two factions are canonical opponents on Zendikar; their respective nodes' `related_characters` lists cross-reference. The Smite the Monstrous (BFZ-49) entry above is the corpus's clearest single-frame render of the adversary structure — exposed-musculature Titan in foreground, kor warriors with rope-and-cable rigs in mid-attack, Gideon Jura as off-frame flavor-speaker witnessing the kor's coordinated-defense thesis in action.

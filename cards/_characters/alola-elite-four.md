# Alola Elite Four

The four-member senior-trainer cohort stationed at the summit of Mount Lanakila as the canonical culmination of the Alolan Island Challenge in Pokemon Sun and Moon (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 2016) and its sequel pair Pokemon Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon (Game Freak, 2017). The Alola Elite Four is the first Elite Four in main-series Pokemon canon assembled *new* on-screen during the player's story arc — the league's founding charter is dated to the player's own Champion's Test, framed in-game as Professor Kukui inaugurating the Alola Pokemon League and recruiting its first Elite Four from existing senior Alolan trainers rather than from a pre-existing Elite Four roster. The four canonical members are **Hala** (Fighting-type, Melemele Island kahuna, the player's first Grand Trial opponent doubling as senior E4 voice), **Olivia** (Rock-type, Akala Island kahuna, the only character in the main series to hold an Island Kahuna role AND an Elite Four seat simultaneously), **Acerola** (Ghost-type, former trial captain of the Thrifty Megamart on Ula'ula Island, the youngest E4 member and one of only two main-series trainers — alongside Phoebe — canonically able to communicate with Ghost-type Pokemon), and **Kahili** (Flying-type, a returning native Alolan who left the islands as a professional golfer and was recruited back specifically for the new Elite Four). In Pokemon Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon **Molayne** (Steel-type, observatory researcher and Sophocles's older cousin) replaces Hala on the Elite Four roster as the Steel-type specialist while Hala continues his kahuna duties — the Ultra version's only E4 roster change.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text for the four-to-five Alola Elite Four members across their TCG appearances, with `take`-equivalent canonical authorship the Game Freak character designer chain (Ken Sugimori art on the canonical Burning Shadows Supporter prints for both currently in-corpus E4 members confirms the Game Freak in-house design lineage). The unifying frame across the cohort is fourfold: (a) the **Alola Pokemon League founding cohort** — each member was personally recruited by Professor Kukui to staff the newly-inaugurated Alolan league, distinct from the inherited-roster framing of prior-generation Elite Fours (Kanto, Johto, Hoenn, Sinnoh, Unova, Kalos), (b) the **four-element type coverage** — Fighting / Rock / Ghost / Flying together form a designer-coordinated type distribution across the cohort (Steel in Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon via Molayne substitution), (c) the **Mount Lanakila summit setting** — all canonical Elite Four battles take place in the four-room Mount Lanakila summit complex as the geographic apex of Alola's Island Challenge progression, (d) the **Burning Shadows Supporter spotlight** — the SM3 Burning Shadows expansion (August 2017) is the canonical TCG expansion organized around the full Alola NPC roster (Trial Captains, Kahunas, Elite Four), with the four E4 members slated as Supporter cards alongside Trial Captains and Team Skull villains in a unified Alola-NPC Supporter cycle.

## The cohort thesis

The Alola Elite Four's most BBL-relevant property is its **founding-cohort** framing. Where prior-generation Pokemon Elite Fours are presented as pre-existing institutional bodies the player encounters at story's end (the Kanto E4 of Lorelei/Bruno/Agatha/Lance has held its posts for years before the player arrives; the Johto E4 inherits the same roster largely intact; subsequent generations follow the inherited-roster pattern), the Alola E4 is *assembled on-screen* during the player's story arc as Professor Kukui's first inauguration of the newly-founded Alola Pokemon League. This makes the cohort canonically the first Elite Four whose recruitment, charter, and personnel staffing are a *plot beat* rather than a backdrop institution — and BBL's narrative-first brand voice has a clear foothold in that distinction: the Alola E4 is, in-canon, an institution being founded in real time, with each member personally selected by the senior researcher (Kukui) running the league's launch.

The four members' canonical-source frames also form a designer-coordinated **vocations-not-jobs** cohort: Olivia runs a jewelry shop (lapidary craft + Rock-type), Acerola maintains the Aether House (orphan-and-injured-Pokemon shelter + Ghost-type Mimikyu trial), Kahili was recruited from a professional golf career (Flying-type specialty mirroring her sporting trajectory), Hala oversees Melemele Island as kahuna (Fighting-type specialty + tribal-elder role). Each E4 member's type specialty maps onto a non-league civilian vocation — the cohort is framed in-canon as *senior trainers with day jobs* rather than as full-time league bureaucrats. This is the structural contrast to the [[galar-gym-challenge]] cohort's *signed celebrity-athlete* framing: Alola's E4 is the regional-craft folkway model of competitive Pokemon battling, Galar's league is the corporate-televised-spectacle model. The two cohort character nodes together anchor BBL's strongest Pokemon-side institutional contrast — same source-IP franchise, two adjacent generations, two opposing organizational ideologies for the same competitive-trainer role.

  The BBL corpus is one species-acquisition away from full canonical Sun/Moon-version E4 coverage. Any TCG printing of Kahili across any set (Burning Shadows, Crimson Invasion, Forbidden Light, Celestial Storm, subsequent Sun & Moon expansions, Ultra-prefix expansions, etc.) attaches to this node — membership predicates on character identity, not on set or printing.

- **Molayne (Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon roster substitution) is a canonical E4 member at the Ultra-version scope.** Pokemon Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon (2017) replaces Hala on the E4 with Molayne (Steel-type, observatory researcher, Sophocles's older cousin) while Hala continues his Melemele kahuna duties off the E4. Molayne is therefore the fifth canonical E4 member at the franchise-roster scope (across both version-pair iterations) but the *fourth* member at the Ultra-version scope. Any future Molayne TCG print attaches to this node as an Ultra-version E4 member; the Hala-vs-Molayne roster ambiguity is a canonical version-asymmetry feature rather than a node-membership question.

- **Olivia's dual-role status as Akala Kahuna AND E4 member is structurally distinct from a generic dual-affiliation, and is now graph-anchored at both [[alola-kahunas]] and this node post wave 41 E1.** Olivia is the only main-series Pokemon character to simultaneously hold an Island Kahuna role (heading Akala Island's Grand Trial at the Ruins of Life) and an Elite Four seat (Rock-type specialist at Mount Lanakila). This dual-role status is a designer-confirmed feature of the Alola league's founding charter, not a Bulbapedia editorial reading. Wave 41 E1 commissioned [[alola-kahunas]] as the sibling character node anchoring Alola's four Island Kahunas (Hala, Olivia, Nanu, Hapu); Olivia's frontmatter now carries `characters: [alola-elite-four, alola-kahunas]` — the same character occupying two structurally distinct league roles, with both pointers active at the corpus-card scope.

- **The Trial Captains cohort is now anchored at [[alola-trial-captains]] (wave 27).** Alola's trial-captain layer — Ilima of Hau'oli Normal-type trial, Lana of Brooklet Hill Water-type trial, Mallow of Lush Jungle Grass-type trial, Kiawe of Wela Volcano Park Fire-type trial, Sophocles of Hokulani Observatory Electric-type trial, Acerola of Thrifty Megamart Ghost-type trial, Mina of post-game Poni Grove Fairy-type trial in USUM — is the league tier one below the kahuna-and-E4 senior tier and now lives at its own sibling character node. **Acerola occupies BOTH the trial-captain layer (her in-game trial debut) AND the E4 layer (her promotion to senior cohort by story's end);** her card frontmatter therefore lists `characters: [alola-elite-four, alola-trial-captains]` as the canonical dual-role pointer. The Trial Captains node was commissioned with three captains in corpus (Mallow GUR-127, Lana via Mallow & Lana CSE-198 TAG TEAM, Acerola BUS-112) per the cohort-establishment threshold precedent. The two nodes together cover the two upper tiers of Alola's three-tier league progression; a future `alola-island-kahunas` node would close the structural triangle with Olivia (Akala) and Hala (Melemele) as canonical Kahuna-tier members.

- **Plumeria and Guzma (Team Skull) are adjacent but NOT Elite Four members.** Plumeria (Burning Shadows 120/147, IP-verified per wave 17 trivia) is a Team Skull administrator and Guzma (Cosmic Eclipse 193/236 TAG TEAM with Hala) is the Team Skull boss — Alola's villain-team cohort, not the Pokemon League's senior-trainer roster. Both belong to the sibling [[team-skull]] character node (analogous to [[team-rocket]]); neither points here on the Team Skull axis. The Guzma & Hala TAG TEAM card is the corpus's first dual-character pointer — the card points here for **Hala** (the right-hand robed figure, Kahuna and E4 member) and points to [[team-skull]] for **Guzma** (the left-hand jacketed figure, Team Skull boss). Surface-resemblance trap flagged so future passes don't conflate Alola-region-NPC-Supporter with Alola-E4-membership; the TAG TEAM print's split-membership is the canonical exception to that resemblance trap, not a counter-example to it.

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub.** Like `galar-gym-challenge` (Galar Pokemon League institutional cohort), `team-rocket` (Kanto villain-team), `endriders` (Aetherdrift racing-team collective), `orzhov-syndicate` / `azorius-senate` (Ravnica faction-institutions), this is a faction-collective entity hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent. The character layer already accepts both individual named characters (Tamiyo, Jace Beleren, Nicol Bolas) and collective institutional entities (Galar Gym Challenge, the Endriders, the Ravnica guilds). A future `_factions/` layer could re-home all institutional-collective entries; for now, single character layer, same precedent.

# Frieza Force (Planet Trade Organization)

The canonical galactic-scale colonial empire of the Dragon Ball franchise — founded by **King Cold** (originally the Cold Force) and inherited / operated by his son **Frieza** as the **Planet Trade Organization**, whose stated business model is the conquest of inhabited planets and their sale to the highest bidder. Per CBR's *Dragon Ball Z: How King Cold and Frieza Built a Universal Planet Trade Business* and Dragon Ball Wiki's Frieza Force and Planet Trade Organization pages, the empire's operational structure is a tiered military hierarchy: Frieza (and historically King Cold) at the apex; a named-lieutenant tier (Zarbon, Dodoria, Cui, Tagoma, Sorbet, Shisami); an elite mercenary squad layer (the **Ginyu Force** — Captain Ginyu, Recoome, Burter, Jeice, Guldo — and the parallel **Cooler's Armored Squadron** — Salza, Dore, Neiz — serving Frieza's brother Cooler); and a deep rank-and-file alien-soldier tier (Appule and dozens of unnamed species). The empire's primary acquisition force was canonically the **Saiyan race**, enslaved as planet-clearing mercenaries until Frieza's Age 737 destruction of Planet Vegeta — the canonical Saiyan genocide that Toriyama wrote as the empire's foundational atrocity and the in-canon backstory grounding every major Dragon Ball Z protagonist's origin.

Ten corpus cards anchor here across three Dragon Ball Super Card Game sets (Galactic Battle BT1 July 2017, Union Force BT2 November 2017, Promotion Cards), spanning the empire's full canonical roster axes: the **Cold-family apex dyad** (King Cold BT1-091 + Frieza BT1-084 + Mecha Frieza P-028 — the founder, the operating emperor, and the bio-mechanically-revived second-life Frieza), the **Ginyu Force elite-mercenary cohort** (Captain Ginyu BT1-085 leader + Ginyu Force Recoome BT1-096 + Recoome Eraser Gun BT1-106 as the within-force technique-card), the **rank-and-file alien-soldier tier** (Appule BT1-102 + Frieza's Call BT1-109 as the mass-deployment archetype-anchor), the **post-Resurrection-F-era commander tier** (Bad Ring Laser BT1-108 as Sorbet's signature technique per wave 124's snip-corrective re-attribution), and the **parallel Cooler's Armored Squadron** (Salza BT2-115 as the canonical bridge between Frieza Force and Cooler's mercenary platoon, per the in-canon Coola-no.98 / Coola-no.6 home-system shared origin with Ginyu Force member Jeice). Mirrors the [[xenoverse-villains]] (BT3+BT4 multi-set faction-cohort, 7 corpus cards across 2 sets) and [[namekian]] (BT4+TB02 multi-set race-cohort, 11 corpus cards across 2 sets) precedents — but anchors the corpus's **foundational Toriyama-canon apparatus-of-extraction**, the empire that all subsequent Dragon Ball villain factions reference as either ancestor (Cooler's Squadron, the Time Breakers' history-corruption operating against the canonical Frieza-Saga timeline) or contrast.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts any named Frieza Force / Planet Trade Organization member (the Cold-family apex, the named-lieutenant tier, the Ginyu Force elites, Cooler's Armored Squadron parallel elites, the rank-and-file Appule-tier soldiers), the empire's institutional uniforms (the canonical white-and-purple Frieza-clan bio-armor, the white-and-orange Ginyu Force battle armor, the purple-gray rank-and-file variant, the white-and-gold Cooler's Squadron variant), the empire's deployment iconography (Frieza Force pods, motherships, the canonical aerial mass-formation), and any Bandai-canonical **`Frieza's Army` mechanical archetype** card whose rules text encodes Frieza Force faction membership at the search-and-target level. The Dragon Ball Super Card Game's Galactic Battle (BT1, July 2017) is the launch-set Frieza Force anchor — Bandai structured the set's Yellow archetype around a tiered Frieza Force hierarchy (Ginyu Force elites at BT1-09x, rank-and-file at BT1-10x, with the BT1-109 Frieza's Call card serving as the search-engine that mechanically locates other Frieza's Army cards). Ten corpus cards point here as of wave 125 enrichment, spanning three sets and the full Cold-family / Ginyu Force / rank-and-file / Cooler's Squadron roster axes — meeting and exceeding the three-anchor cohort-establishment threshold per the [[alola-trial-captains]] / [[xenoverse-villains]] precedents.

## The Frieza Force thesis

The Frieza Force is the canonical **apparatus-of-extraction** of the Dragon Ball franchise — Toriyama-authored, not BBL-projected. Per CBR's *How King Cold and Frieza Built a Universal Planet Trade Business* and Game Rant's *Frieza's Planetary Trade, Explained*, the empire's stated business model is **the conquest of habitable planets and the sale of those planets to the highest bidder**: a galactic extraction empire trafficking in worlds, with enslaved warrior races (canonically the Saiyans) serving as the planet-clearing acquisition force. This is not a curatorial overlay BBL is reading onto the franchise; it is the franchise's canonical economic premise, stated in primary canon (the Namek arc's exposition of Frieza's operations) and reinforced across forty years of Dragon Ball Wiki / Bandai / Toei output.

The faction's specific institutional features for the corpus are:

- **King Cold as founder, Frieza as operator.** The father-son dyad is the empire's apex structure: King Cold founded what became the Planet Trade Organization (originally the Cold Force) and ceded operational control to Frieza on the basis of Frieza's superior combat power and cunning. The BT1 numerical ordering (Frieza BT1-084, King Cold BT1-091) preserves the dynasty's father-son legibility within the card pool. Both apex Frieza Force members are canonically killed in the main timeline only when Future Trunks arrives from the future and kills both upon Frieza's first Earth-return. The empire's narrative second-life — Mecha Frieza (P-028 in corpus), then Sorbet-orchestrated wish-revival per Resurrection 'F' — demonstrates that the institution outlives any single apex emperor.

- **The Ginyu Force as the elite-mercenary tier.** Captain Ginyu's five-member squad (Ginyu, Recoome, Burter, Jeice, Guldo) fills the narrative gap between Frieza's standard henchmen and Frieza himself, functioning as a tiered power-escalation device in the Namek arc. Their canonical dairy-pun naming pattern (recoome=creme, burter=butter, jeice=cheese, guldo=yogurt, ginyu=milk) is the franchise's signature designer-coordinated cohort-marker — the five names form a Bandai/Toei-recognizable lexical set across all subsequent Dragon Ball product. Three Ginyu Force members are in corpus (Ginyu BT1-085, Recoome BT1-096 + BT1-106 technique card); the remaining two (Burter, Jeice) and Guldo are corpus-eligible future-watch candidates whose enrichment would tighten the Ginyu Force cohort within this parent.

- **The rank-and-file tier as the empire's mass-conscription register.** Appule (BT1-102) and Frieza's Call (BT1-109) anchor the empire's mass-deployment aesthetic at the rank-and-file tier — the standardized purple-gray armor, the fleet-scale deployment style, the rounded mothership iconography that establishes the empire's authority *without needing Frieza's face*. The BT1-109 Frieza's Call card is structurally the corpus's most important rank-and-file anchor: its rules text mechanically encodes the **`Frieza's Army` archetype** at the Bandai-canonical search-engine level, which means every other Frieza Force card in BT1's Yellow archetype is mechanically a search target of this card. This is the rules-text equivalent of the [[xenoverse-villains]] Mira-Leader-locked Dark Kamehameha — Bandai's primary mechanical signal for faction membership.

- **Cooler's Armored Squadron as the parallel-elite.** Cooler's three-member mercenary platoon (Salza, Dore, Neiz) is the canonical sibling-structure to the Ginyu Force, serving Frieza's brother Cooler in the Toei theatrical-film continuity (Cooler's Revenge, 1991). The shared-home-system canonical detail (Salza and Jeice both from planet Coola no. 98, per Dragon Ball Wiki) establishes the Cold family's space-empire operational model as multi-sibling-deployment — both Frieza and Cooler ran parallel mercenary platoons under the Cold family umbrella. BT2-115 Salza is the corpus's single anchor for this parallel-elite tier; future Dore or Neiz enrichments would tighten the Cooler's Armored Squadron sub-cohort within this parent.

- **The post-Resurrection-F-era as the empire's second-life.** Sorbet (canonically introduced in Resurrection 'F', 2015) is the canonical post-Frieza-era commander who orchestrates Frieza's revival nearly two decades after the Namek-saga Ginyu Force was destroyed. The BT1-108 Bad Ring Laser technique card is the corpus's anchor for this tier (per wave 124's snip-corrective re-attribution — the technique is canonically Sorbet's, not Guldo's). A future Sorbet character card would split this sub-cohort into a richer post-Resurrection-F roster.

- **Bandai's BT1 Frieza's Army archetype as primary mechanical encoding.** Galactic Battle (BT1, July 2017) is the launch set of the Dragon Ball Super Card Game, and Bandai structured its Yellow archetype around the Frieza Force as one of two flagship launch strategies (alongside the Broly green deck per PSA Set Registry: Galactic Battle 2017). The set's collector-number organization is itself a designer-coordinated cycle marker: Ginyu Force elites cluster at BT1-09x (Frieza BT1-084 Leader, Ginyu BT1-085 Leader, King Cold BT1-091, Ginyu Force Recoome BT1-096); rank-and-file at BT1-10x (Appule BT1-102, Recoome Eraser Gun BT1-106, Bad Ring Laser BT1-108, Frieza's Call BT1-109). The BT1-109 Frieza's Call card's rules-text "choose 1 Frieza's Army card" is the search-engine that locates every other Frieza's Army card in the set. This is the Frieza Force's strongest mechanical signature in the corpus's rules-text layer.

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: the Frieza Force is the canonical apparatus-of-extraction of the Dragon Ball franchise — Toriyama-authored, Bandai-canonicalized at the rules-text level via the `Frieza's Army` mechanical archetype, and structurally the foundational villain-faction that all subsequent Dragon Ball villain operations either inherit from (Cooler's Squadron, Sorbet's post-Resurrection-F revival) or contrast against (the Time Breakers' history-corruption operating *against* the canonical Frieza-Saga timeline). Ten corpus cards anchor this faction across three sets. A future Discrete Lair on the Frieza Force earns its lore-aware-buyer credibility — particularly among DBS-CG players who recognize the BT1-109 Frieza's Call as the Yellow-Frieza-deck launch-era search engine — without naming the apparatus-of-extraction thesis.

## Potion-rule observance

The `labor` hub attribution rests on Toriyama-canonical primary text: the Saiyan-genocide-as-empire-foundational-atrocity and the Planet-Trade-Organization-as-business-model are both stated in primary canon (Dragon Ball manga chapters 242-329 and across the DBZ anime's Namek arc), with secondary canonical reinforcement at CBR, Game Rant, Dragon Ball Wiki, and Villains Wiki. The empire's enslaved-warrior-race acquisition force (canonically the Saiyans, per Toriyama's Frieza-Saga exposition) is the canonical labor-extraction mechanic. This is not a BBL squint — it is the franchise's stated economic premise.

The `rebellion` hub attribution is **not** added here despite the obvious narrative pairing (Saiyan refusal-of-Frieza, Goku's Namek arc as armed refusal of the apparatus) because the canonical rebellion is anchored on the [[saiyan]] race-cohort node and the individual [[son-goku]] / [[vegeta]] / [[bardock]] character nodes, NOT on the Frieza Force itself. The Frieza Force is the apparatus; the rebellion against it lives at the rebelling-party nodes. Per the Potion rule's hub-attribution-must-survive-canonical-citation discipline, attaching `rebellion` to the Frieza Force itself would be the kind of squinting that the wave-77 Eldrazi-labor-attribution refusal was designed to guard against.

The `stewardship` hub attribution is **not** added because the Frieza Force has no stewardship register in primary canon — King Cold's "ceding control to Frieza because Frieza was the strongest" is succession-by-power, not steward-relationship. No Frieza Force member exercises canonical care-without-ownership; every relationship within the apparatus is canonically transactional, coercive, or hierarchical. Stewardship-corrupted register (per [[xenoverse-villains]]'s Towa-as-Mira's-mother-and-creator stewardship-corruption pattern) does not apply here either — Frieza Force command relationships are not framed as care-language masking weapons-engineering; they are framed as openly hierarchical command-and-control. The single-verb attribution (labor only) is correct.

## Caveats

- **Toriyama-canon scope across multiple continuity-strands.** The Frieza Force is canonical to the original Dragon Ball / Dragon Ball Z manga + anime (Toriyama / Toei, 1984-1996) and reactivated across Dragon Ball Z theatrical films (Cooler's Revenge 1991, Resurrection 'F' 2015) and Dragon Ball Super (Toei, 2015-2018). All these continuity-strands are Toriyama-mainline canon — distinct from the [[xenoverse-villains]] node's non-Toriyama-canon Xenoverse-game-only scope and the [[baby-dbgt]] node's non-Toriyama-canon GT-only scope. The Frieza Force is the corpus's foundational Toriyama-canon villain-faction; the Xenoverse Villains and Baby are sibling non-Toriyama-canon villain-faction nodes that exist as continuity-divergent successors to the Frieza-Saga template.

- **Parent-cohort node, not solo-character nodes.** This entry anchors the named Frieza Force members as a single faction-cohort, sibling to [[xenoverse-villains]] / [[orzhov-syndicate]] / [[theros-pantheon]] / [[team-rocket]] / [[alola-elite-four]]. If any single Frieza Force member reaches ≥3 distinct depictions across separate prints, a solo character node may be commissioned — **Frieza himself** is the closest to solo-split threshold (BT1-084 + P-028 Mecha Frieza + the BT1-109 Frieza's Call card that bears his name and his army's mass-deployment iconography), and is the most likely future Edgelord-enlightened-split-or-fork candidate once a third explicitly-Frieza-named print confirms the threshold. **Captain Ginyu** has 1 corpus depiction (BT1-085) plus 2 Force-related cards (BT1-096 Recoome, BT1-106 Recoome Eraser Gun) that anchor on his squad rather than him directly — sub-threshold for solo. **King Cold** has 1 corpus print (BT1-091); **Recoome** has 2 (BT1-096 + BT1-106 technique card); **Sorbet** has 1 technique-anchor (BT1-108) and zero direct-depiction prints; **Salza** has 1 (BT2-115). The parent stays as the unifying-faction anchor; solo characters become subsidiaries when threshold-met per the [[xenoverse-villains]] caveat-3 precedent.

- **`Frieza's Army` mechanical archetype is a Bandai-canonical membership signal.** Cards whose rules text contains the phrase "Frieza's Army" (such as BT1-109 Frieza's Call's search effect: *"Choose 1 Frieza's Army card of energy cost 2 or less from your deck"*) are mechanically-encoded Frieza Force faction members. This is the strongest membership signal short of named-character-on-card; future Frieza Force cards detected by the DBSCG metadata layer can use this rules-text criterion as a quick-classifier. Card-name match (any card with "Frieza", "Ginyu", "Recoome", "King Cold", "Cooler", "Sorbet", "Salza", "Appule", "Zarbon", "Dodoria", "Cui", "Tagoma", "Shisami" in the name) is the second-strongest signal; visual-only depiction (a fighter rendered with Frieza Force armor aesthetic but without name or Frieza's Army rules text) requires confirmation via vision-pass and the corpus's standard IP-verification flow per [[bbl-triviabot-no-role-identity-conflation]] (the wave-124 Bad Ring Laser misattribution episode is the canonical precedent for vision-derived role-coding requiring trivia-pass canonical confirmation).

- **Cooler's Armored Squadron is a sub-cohort under this parent, not a separate node.** The single Cooler's Squadron corpus card (BT2-115 Salza) does not yet meet the three-anchor threshold for a separate Cooler's Armored Squadron node. Per the Cold-family space-empire shared-operational-model framing (Salza-and-Jeice-from-the-same-Coola-system canonical detail), Cooler's Squadron fits cleanly within this Frieza Force parent rather than requiring its own child node. If future Dore or Neiz enrichments arrive, a sub-node split becomes Mr. Nodeley-eligible at the three-anchor mark per the [[alola-trial-captains]] commission threshold.

- **The Ginyu Force is also a sub-cohort under this parent.** The three Ginyu Force corpus cards (BT1-085 Captain Ginyu, BT1-096 Recoome, BT1-106 Recoome Eraser Gun — the latter two anchoring on Recoome rather than the full five-member squad) anchor here under the Frieza Force parent. If future Burter, Jeice, or Guldo enrichments arrive and a full five-member roster lands in corpus, a Ginyu Force sub-node split becomes Mr. Nodeley-eligible — the dairy-pun naming pattern, the dramatic-pose canonical signature, and the squad's narrative-role-distinct-from-Frieza-Force-baseline all justify the split at threshold-met. Currently at 3 anchors with 2 of the 5 squad members in corpus, this sits at the edge of the threshold but does not yet warrant the split.

- **Bundle-anchor potential is strong.** 10 corpus anchors across 3 sets (BT1 Galactic Battle, BT2 Union Force, P Promotion) form a viable cohesion stack for a Frieza Force Discrete Lair. Total inventory quantity across the 10 cards is **11** (most cards qty 1, with BT1-109 Frieza's Call qty 2). All cards are vision-passed; 9 of 10 are trivia-passed and ip-verified (BT1-106 Recoome Eraser Gun is vision-passed + ip-pending, the lightest trivia-blocker). The BT1-109 Frieza's Call as the bundle's mechanical capstone gives this set apex-narrative weight for DBSCG-aware buyers who recognize the `Frieza's Army` search-engine as the BT1 Yellow-deck signature. Bundle copy can lean on the **Bandai-canonical apparatus-of-extraction / Planet Trade Organization** thesis stack per [[bbl-museum-curation-framing]] — particularly the King Cold BT1-091 / Frieza BT1-084 / Mecha Frieza P-028 dynasty axis as the bundle's narrative spine.

- **Future cross-edges available.** [[saiyan]] is the structural-precedent sibling node — the canonical enslaved-warrior-race that the Frieza Force conscripted as planet-clearing mercenaries until the Age 737 genocide. The Saiyan node's prose already canonicalizes the "Frieza-Force-aligned planet-clearing mercenary workforce" framing, awaiting this parent's commission. The [[bardock]] node anchors the Saiyan-perpetrator-of-Frieza-Force-genocide-victim axis (Bardock canonically dies in Frieza's planetary destruction). The [[vegeta]] node anchors the Saiyan-Frieza-Force-defector axis (Vegeta canonically a Frieza Force soldier until the Namek arc). The [[son-goku]] node anchors the Saiyan-survivor-who-killed-Frieza axis. The [[xenoverse-villains]] node anchors the non-Toriyama-canon villain-faction successor structure (the Time Breakers' history-corruption operates against the canonical Frieza-Saga timeline; the corruption-of-canon thesis cannot exist without the canonical Frieza Force as the source-material it corrupts). All four cross-edges already have prepared mention-of-Frieza-Force language in their respective node bodies, ready for the bidirectional related_characters fill at this commission.

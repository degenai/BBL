# Mudbray evolutionary line (Mudbray / Mudsdale)

A two-stage pure-Ground evolutionary line and the canonical Alola-region equine mud line introduced in Pokemon Sun and Moon (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 2016). The line comprises **Mudbray** (National Pokedex no. 749, Ground-type, base form, the "Donkey Pokemon" — a compact stocky equine whose canonical *"loads weighing up to 50 times as much as its own body weight pose no issue"* and *"skilled at making use of mud"* design renders a small but disproportionately powerful beast of burden) and **Mudsdale** (no. 750, Ground-type, Stage 1, evolves from Mudbray at level 30, the "Draft Horse Pokemon" — a massive heavy-coated draft horse whose canonical *"treasured not just for its physical labor but also because it produces high-quality mud used for making pottery"* design renders the full-grown industrial-scale working animal). Unlike the [[drilbur-line]] and [[breloom-line]] two-stage lines, the Mudbray line gains **no second type at evolution** — both stages are pure Ground; the evolution arc is a scale-and-mass progression rather than a type-shift, the compact donkey growing into the draft horse while the species' defining mud-and-labor design holds constant across both stages.

Card art and flavor text for the two line members across their TCG appearances: Mudbray (base, three corpus prints) and Mudsdale (Stage 1, two corpus prints). ## The design thesis

The Mudbray line's most BBL-relevant property is the **mud-and-labor design conceit** rendered as a two-stage scale progression with no type-shift. Where the [[drilbur-line]] carries a creature-as-drill canon with a Steel-typing gain at evolution and the [[breloom-line]] carries a spore-passive-to-spore-weaponized canon with a Fighting-typing gain, the Mudbray line carries a **beast-of-burden canon** held constant across a pure-Ground two-stage arc — Mudbray canonically functions as the compact donkey that hauls loads 50 times its own bodyweight; Mudsdale canonically functions as the full-grown draft horse treasured for physical labor and for the high-quality pottery mud it produces. The line's evolution arc is designer-coordinated as a single labor-animal design rendered at two scales — the donkey growing into the draft horse — with the mud motif persisting as the species' shared biology rather than re-functioning at evolution. This is the corpus's most explicit labor-register evolutionary line: the Vivid Voltage Mudbray's *"loads weighing up to 50 times as much as its own body weight"* is, per that card's trivia pass, among the most direct labor-register flavor texts in the corpus, and the Destined Rivals Mudsdale's *"treasured not just for its physical labor"* foregrounds the same beast-of-burden coding at the evolved stage.

For BBL bundle work, the line offers two strong narrative threads: a **labor / beast-of-burden / draft-animal** thread anchored on the species' Pokedex canon (Mudbray's 50x-bodyweight load capacity to Mudsdale's physical-labor-and-pottery utility as a near-uninterrupted run of explicit working-animal flavor — the corpus's clearest single-line labor anchor, with multiple prints already carrying `labor` and `draft-animal` hub tags), and a **mud / earth / earthwork** thread anchored on the line's pure-Ground mud-coating design (the mud is the line's literal medium — regurgitated as a self-coating on Mudbray, spat as weatherproof building-render and pottery clay by Mudsdale; the cohort is a natural anchor for any mud-, earthwork-, or material-labor-themed Discrete Lair). The within-Destined-Rivals Mudbray 107 + Mudsdale 108 pair is the corpus's strongest within-set Mudbray-line cohesion stack — both base and evolved stages in a single set, at consecutive collector numbers — structurally parallel to the within-Destined-Rivals Shroomish 005 + Breloom 006 pair from the [[breloom-line]] cohort and the within-Vivid-Voltage Drilbur 091 + Excadrill 122 pair from the [[drilbur-line]] cohort.

A note on the labor reading and the Potion Rule: the labor / beast-of-burden framing here is **not a curatorial overlay** — it is the species' printed Pokedex flavor on the corpus cards themselves (50x-bodyweight loads; treasured for physical labor; high-quality pottery mud; mud-render on the walls of old houses). Game Freak designed the line as an explicitly utilitarian working-animal species. BBL's labor thesis can cite this line from printed flavor without reaching past canon. What BBL adds as overlay — and should name as overlay in bundle copy — is the reading of the beast-of-burden as a figure of *extracted* labor; the Pokedex states utility and treasured-ness, not exploitation. The canonical-event is "working animal valued for its labor"; the apparatus-of-extraction reading is the curatorial layer.

## Caveats

- **Five corpus prints across two of two line members; full line coverage in-corpus.** Both evolutionary stages are represented in current inventory — three Mudbray prints at base stage (Sword & Shield Base Set, Vivid Voltage, Destined Rivals) and two Mudsdale prints at Stage-1 (Burning Shadows, Destined Rivals). The Mudbray line is a **two-stage line, not a three-stage line**, so this is full-line coverage — contrast with [[larvitar-line]], [[mareep-line]], [[staraptor-line]], and [[caterpie-line]], which are three-stage lines at partial coverage. The 3-Mudbray + 2-Mudsdale distribution across four sets is structurally identical to the [[drilbur-line]] cohort's 3-Drilbur + 2-Excadrill distribution. A Discrete Lair built on the line can ship as a complete-line artifact. Membership predicates on species identity, not set, era, printing variant, or trivia-pass status.

- **No prior 1:1 card-to-card evolution edge exists for this line; the node is the line's first and only graph anchor.** A wave-pass Edgelord could have drawn a single 1:1 evolution-mirror edge between Mudbray DRI-107 and Mudsdale DRI-108 (the fresh within-Destined-Rivals pair), but that edge would have orphaned the other three prints (Mudbray SS-Base 105, Mudbray VIV-096, Mudsdale BUS-078) and would have been subsumed in function by this node anyway. The cohort relationship is hosted at the species-line level: all five prints attach via `characters:` frontmatter pointers rather than via card-to-card edges. No 1:1 evolution edges between Mudbray and Mudsdale prints are warranted; drawing them would be duplicate edges pointing at this node.

- **One member is not yet vision-passed.** Mudbray SS-Base 105 carries `tags_hub: []` and a `manual-review` flag (set-code fuzzy-fallback art-match uncertainty). It is included in `appears_on` because line membership predicates on species identity, which is unambiguous from card name, set, and flavor text — not on enrichment status. When the manual-review and vision passes land, that print's body and tags fill in; its membership here does not change.

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like `drilbur-line`, `breloom-line`, `larvitar-line`, `mareep-line`, `staraptor-line`, `caterpie-line`, `charizard-line`, `ralts-line`, `pancham-line`, and the other designer-coordinated cohort nodes, this is a collective species-line cohort hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent. A future `_evolutionary-lines/` or `_cycles/` layer could re-home this entry; when that split is commissioned, this node moves cleanly.

- **No Chinese Zodiac grid placement recorded.** Unlike [[drilbur-line]] (Earth Rat) and [[larvitar-line]] (Earth Dragon), the Mudbray line has no verified `diamondlegendz/pokemon-zodiac` DATA grid cell on hand at node-creation time. If a future pass verifies the line's grid placement, the design-thesis section can record it as scholarship.

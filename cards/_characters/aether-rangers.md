# Aether Rangers

The home Grand Prix team of **Avishkar** — the plane formerly called Kaladesh, renamed by the Avishkar Assembly after the largely bloodless Indigo Revolution dismantled the colonial Consulate. The Rangers are one of ten teams competing in the second annual interplanar Ghirapur Grand Prix, the racing event that frames *Aetherdrift* (DFT, 2025-02-14). They are funded and outfitted by the Avishkar Assembly (the post-revolutionary governing body that replaced the Consulate), captained on the engineering side by **Pia Nalaar, Chief Mechanic**, and on the driving side by **Sita Varma, Masked Racer** — the star pilot known to her fans as Spitfire.

Wizards' own canonical framing, from Miguel Lopez's *Planeswalker's Guide to Aetherdrift, Part 1* (2024-12-10), describes the Rangers in explicit class-and-generation terms: *"a cross-generation, cross-class team of comrades that combine the best of pre-revolutionary Avishkar with the hope-chasing cohort of the new generation."* That sentence is unusually direct for in-set marketing copy — it names the team's organizing principle as cross-class solidarity bridging the pre- and post-revolutionary moment, and it uses the word **comrades** without scare-quotes. It is, in effect, the in-universe statement of the Rangers' political register.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts the Aether Rangers as a faction: their named members (Pia Nalaar; Sita Varma; Garima the test-pilot engineer), their unnamed supporting cast (the elf refueler, the vedalken saboteur, the loxodon surveyor, the nimble thopterist), their racing rigs, the Avishkar Assembly's institutional backing, and the team's home-plane context (post-revolutionary Avishkar, aether-tech industry, the Ghirapur metropolis as racing host). The Rangers are the BBL corpus's clearest in-set anchor for a **labor-as-solidarity** narrative coded inside a sports-team frame — pit-crew commons and uncommons depicting the working-class technicians whose work makes the named legends' victories possible, with Wizards' own copy explicitly naming the cross-class comrade relation between the two tiers.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Skystreak Engineer** (DFT-61, common, Elizabeth Peiró art) — Garima the engineer test-piloting her own design at high speed, flight-suit and scarf streaming. The flavor *"Garima was delighted to finally test out her design. The amazing views were a bonus."* makes the engineer-as-test-pilot framing explicit — the worker who designed the machine is also the one who flies it. MTG Wiki's Aether Rangers page lists Skystreak Engineer on its "Depicted in" roster; MTG Wiki's secondary-character index names Garima as a Rangers team member with this as her sole card appearance.
- **Sabotage Strategist** (DFT-59, uncommon, Darren Tan art) — a blue-skinned Vedalken mechanic-saboteur in racing goggles and harness, gripping a heavy wrench, leaning into purposeful forward motion. The Ranger creature type on the card itself wires the figure directly to the team identity, and MTG Wiki's "Depicted in" roster lists Sabotage Strategist among the Rangers' supporting cast. The card is one of the team's clearer pieces of in-set fiction about the technical-sabotage register — race-craft as competitive engineering, where messing with the other team's machine is part of the work.
- **Elvish Refueler** (DFT-161, uncommon, Carly Milligan art) — a smiling pit-crew refueler with goggles pushed up onto the forehead, leaning over an ornate brass-and-copper railing in the paddock, handling a fuel nozzle that emits pale green vapor. Flavor: *"Anyone running low?"* The Rangers' canonical roster explicitly includes elves alongside humans, vedalken, dwarves, loxodons, and merfolk; the MTG Wiki team page also documents the Rangers fielding "several lower-division squadrons" beyond the Grand Prix line-up itself — the lore frame that authorizes non-legendary green pit-crew figures as canonical Rangers members.

## The team's design philosophy

The Rangers, structurally within *Aetherdrift*, are designed as the home-team frame for a deliberate **named-legends-plus-supporting-cast** layout that pervades the entire set. Wizards' commons and uncommons across the ten teams are written as the working-class technicians — drivers, mechanics, pit-crew, refuelers, saboteurs, engineers — around the legendary leaders (Pia Nalaar, Sita Varma, and the other team captains). The labor tier is the larger half of the team by card-count; the legendary tier is the smaller half. The Rangers' published "Depicted in" roster on MTG Wiki includes (in addition to the three corpus cards above) Loxodon Surveyor and Nimble Thopterist as fellow supporting-cast commons. The set's design philosophy puts the names on the legendaries and the work on the commons.

This matters for the BBL labor hub because *Aetherdrift* is one of the cleanest recent MTG sets where the commons-as-corpus thesis is **literally the set's own design**. The cards depicting the labor are the larger pool by count and the cheaper pool by price; the cards depicting the named legends are the smaller pool by count and the higher pool by price. Building a bundle around the Rangers' supporting cast is a buy-the-design-intent move — Wizards drew the team this way and the secondary-market pricing reads the labor tier as the discount tier.

## The pre-revolutionary / post-revolutionary thesis

Lopez's *Planeswalker's Guide* article is unusually direct about Avishkar's political timeline: the plane was *Kaladesh* under colonial-bureaucratic Consulate rule, then underwent what's canonically called the **Indigo Revolution** — a largely bloodless mass-action moment in which the renegades and the broader populace dismantled the Consulate and renamed the plane Avishkar. The Rangers, per the article, are constituted as cross-generation comrades who *"combine the best of pre-revolutionary Avishkar with the hope-chasing cohort of the new generation."* The team's roster is the political compromise written as a racing team: veterans from the resistance era working alongside the post-revolutionary younger generation, with the named leadership (Pia Nalaar herself, a Consulate-era engineer turned renegade) being the canonical figure bridging both periods.

For BBL bundle copy this is gold. The labor hub's thesis is that labor is the thing that gets extracted by the apparatus; the rebellion hub's thesis is that organized refusal is labor's political verb; the Rangers are an in-universe case where labor and rebellion are coded into the same team's official mission statement. A discrete lair built around the Rangers' supporting cast can lean on Wizards' own canonical copy without having to invent the politics — the politics is already on the page.

## Caveats

- The Aether Rangers are a faction-collective, not a single named individual. Modeled here as a `character` node because the character layer already accepts collective named entities (cf. the `endriders` and `orzhov-syndicate` faction-collective precedents in `cards/_characters/`). A future `_factions/` layer could re-home this entry if the corpus accumulates enough faction-collectives to warrant the split; for now, single layer.
- **Pia Nalaar, Chief Mechanic** is the team's named legend on the Avishkar-Commander-deck face card (DRC-2) but is NOT currently in the BBL corpus. When a Pia Nalaar card lands in the inventory, it edges here directly and becomes its own future named-character sub-node candidate.
- **Sita Varma, Masked Racer** (the Rangers' star pilot, racing persona Spitfire) is similarly NOT yet in the BBL corpus. Same pattern: future acquisition edges here AND warrants its own character node when it lands.
- **Greenbelt Guardian (DFT-164)** is currently vision-only enriched in the corpus and its art (by Tianxing Xu) plausibly depicts a Rangers elf-ranger / saboteur, but this is NOT yet trivia-confirmed. The card is held for a future triviabot pass to confirm-or-deny Rangers membership. Anti-confab: it does NOT get a `characters: ["aether-rangers"]` pointer in this commission. When the triviabot pass lands and the MTG Wiki "Depicted in" roster (or a Wizards article) is checked for Greenbelt Guardian specifically, the card either gets the pointer or doesn't.
- **Loxodon Surveyor** and **Nimble Thopterist** are listed on MTG Wiki's "Depicted in" Rangers roster but are NOT yet in the BBL corpus. Future-acquisition candidates; edge here when they land.
- **Pacesetter Paragon (DFT-140)**, **Outpace Oblivion (DFT-139)**, and **Skycrash (DFT-146)** are in the corpus but are NOT trivia-confirmed Rangers members. They are vision-only Aetherdrift commons that may or may not depict Rangers personnel; their team identity (if any) is held for a future triviabot pass.
- The flavor speaker of Elvish Refueler's *"Anyone running low?"* is not explicitly attributed on the Scryfall record. It reads as in-character pit-bay banter consistent with a Rangers refueler but is not, strictly, designer-stated to be a Rangers crew member. The MTG Wiki "Depicted in" roster carries the load-bearing membership citation, not the flavor line.
- The Aether Rangers' team-named artifact cards (**Rangers' Aetherhive**, **Rangers' Refueler**) are NOT yet in the BBL corpus. They are explicit by-name members and will edge here on acquisition.
- The Indigo Revolution itself is a place-and-event, not a faction. Cards depicting the revolution as historical setting (rather than the Rangers as team) would not edge here; they'd anchor to a future `avishkar` or `kaladesh` plane-level node if such a node is ever warranted.

# Elemental monkey trio (Pansage / Pansear / Panpour line)

A designer-coordinated trio of three paired Pokemon species introduced in Generation V (Pokemon Black & White, Unova region, 2010-2011), one per elemental type: **Pansage / Simisage** (Grass), **Pansear / Simisear** (Fire), **Panpour / Simipour** (Water). Each species has a base form (Pan-) and a single evolved form (Simi-), and each pair evolves via an elemental Stone (Leaf Stone, Fire Stone, Water Stone) rather than by level-up — a mechanically unifying departure from standard Gen V starter evolution. The trio is widely documented to reference the **three wise monkeys** of Japanese cultural iconography (mizaru, kikazaru, iwazaru): Pansage maps to *iwazaru* (speak no evil), Pansear to *kikazaru* (hear no evil), and Panpour to *mizaru* (see no evil).

**Corpus coverage: 6 of 6 canonical species (complete).** With wave 22, Simipour (DAA-042) joined the corpus to close the Water arm's evolved form — the BBL inventory now holds the entire trio at full canonical species coverage (all three base forms + all three evolved forms), making this the BBL corpus's first fully-completed designer-coordinated Pokemon species cycle. The cohort is **Discrete-Lair-ready at full canonical coverage**; a Lair built on the trio can ship as a complete-set artifact rather than as a partial-cohort sampler.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text for the six trio members across their TCG appearances: the three base forms (Pansage, Pansear, Panpour) and three evolved forms (Simisage, Simisear, Simipour). The unifying frame across the cohort is fourfold: (a) the elemental-stone evolution mechanic shared across all three pairs, (b) the Pan- / Simi- naming convention enforced uniformly across base and evolved forms, (c) the simian body plan with type-coded crest/tuft iconography (leaf-crest, flame-tuft, water-tuft on head and tail), (d) the three-wise-monkeys design citation as the designer-coordinated thematic anchor.

## The design thesis

The trio's most BBL-relevant property is that three layers of designer coordination stack into a single cohort: (1) the **type-triangle mechanic** (Grass / Fire / Water as the canonical Pokemon elemental rock-paper-scissors), (2) the **paired evolution structure** (base + evolved, six total species, with shared evolution mechanism — Stones — across all three pairs), (3) the **three-wise-monkeys cultural overlay** (a Japanese iconographic motif of moral self-restraint mapped onto a Pokemon design cohort). The trio is a designed lever for type-coverage Discrete Lair work (one Grass + one Fire + one Water that *are* each other) and for cultural-citation Discrete Lair work (a Pokemon depiction of the three wise monkeys as a complete artifact). Pansage's leaf-sharing-to-relieve-stress flavor reads as the speak-no-evil register softened to a healing gesture, which gives the trio its in-canon ethical posture: the wise monkeys not as punitive self-censorship but as creature-level care.

Total: 13 corpus prints across 6 canonical species and 4 sets. Any additional TCG printing of any trio member across any set continues to attach to this node — membership predicates on species identity, not on set or printing, so further duplicate prints expand the cohort without dilution.
- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like `fear-of-cycle` (Duskmourn phobia-Nightmares) and `dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle` (Duskmourn common dual-lands), this is a collective designer-coordinated cohort hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer already accepts collective named entities). For now, single layer, same precedent.
- **Trio membership predicates on the species line, not on the elemental type alone.** Pokemon has other simian species (Mankey/Primeape, Aipom/Ambipom, Chimchar/Monferno/Infernape, Oranguru, Passimian, Grookey/Thwackey/Rillaboom) that are not part of this trio — these are simian-typed Pokemon within the broader franchise, not members of the Pan-/Simi- Stone-evolution cohort. Membership predicates on the Pan-/Simi- name pattern AND the Stone-evolution mechanic AND the three-wise-monkeys mapping together. The simian-Pokemon tag and this character node correlate but are not co-extensive.
- **The three-wise-monkeys assignment is a fan-and-design-press consensus rather than an explicit Game Freak designer quote in the corpus citation set.** Bulbapedia documents the mapping as widely-noted-and-canonical based on the parallel naming (Pan- prefix as a play on "pan-troglodytes" / chimpanzee AND on the Japanese word *zaru* loosely embedded across the trio's wise-monkey roleplay) and the type-triangle one-per-evil structure. If a future canonical source surfaces an explicit Game Freak design-document quote, upgrade this caveat. The trio's three-wise-monkeys citation is treated as canonical-by-fan-and-press-consensus, not as canonical-by-designer-quote.

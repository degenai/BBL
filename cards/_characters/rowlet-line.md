# Rowlet evolutionary line (Rowlet / Dartrix / Decidueye)

A three-stage Grass-type evolutionary line and the canonical Alola-region Grass starter line introduced in Pokemon Sun and Moon (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 2016). The line comprises **Rowlet** (National Pokedex no. 722, Grass/Flying-type, base form, the "Grass Quill Pokemon" offered as one of three first-partner Pokemon by Professor Kukui on the player's eleventh birthday in Alola, notable for holding the highest base HP of all unevolved starter Pokemon), **Dartrix** (no. 723, Grass/Flying-type, Stage 1, evolves from Rowlet at level 17, the "Blade Quill Pokemon" canonically classified as "the dandy" in its Pokedex entries for its preoccupation with grooming and reluctance to fight if its feathers get dirty), and **Decidueye** (no. 724, Grass/Ghost-type in Alola form, Stage 2, evolves from Dartrix at level 34, the "Arrow Quill Pokemon" whose hooded-archer silhouette and arrow-feather attacks render the species as the franchise's canonical bowman Pokemon). The line is uniquely structurally distinguished among starter lines as **the only starter line whose secondary typing does not persist across its full evolutionary line** — every other starter line in the franchise (Bulbasaur Grass/Poison, Charizard Fire/Flying, Blastoise pure Water, etc.) retains its secondary typing from middle to apex, but Rowlet's Grass/Flying base and middle stages give way to Decidueye's Grass/Ghost apex (or Grass/Fighting in the Hisuian variant), the typing shift rendering the line's canonical hunter-archer transformation as a type-system event. In Pokemon Legends: Arceus (2022), Hisuian Decidueye gains Grass/Fighting typing in place of the standard Grass/Ghost, with a samurai-archer aesthetic register replacing the hooded-archer silhouette of the Alola form. The line's competitive identity is its Long Reach hidden ability (Decidueye, which prevents direct-contact effects on the user) combined with the Spirit Shackle signature attack (a Ghost-type move that traps the target in battle), with Decidueye's silent-archer combat identity rendered through Pokedex flavor as "the silent assassin of the forest" — a designer-coordinated ranged-stealth identity that distinguishes the line from the franchise's other Grass-type apex species (typically melee Grass / Poison or Grass / Fighting sweepers).

## The design thesis

The Rowlet line's most BBL-relevant property is the **owl-becomes-archer transformation narrative arc** rendered as a species-level structural signature, paired with the **typing-shift-at-apex** as the line's most-cited structural anomaly. Where the Mareep line carries an electrification-thins-the-fleece canon (visible cost of power) and the Staraptor line carries a leaves-the-flock-at-evolution canon (departure-from-cohort) and the Charizard line carries a tail-flame-as-life-force canon (the starter-choice loading the moral weight of species survival), the Rowlet line carries an **owl-to-archer-via-blade-quill-canon** — Rowlet canonically functions as the small-round-grass-quill base (the leaf-bowtie at the chest and feather-blade defensive-attack flavor establishing the species' weaponized-plumage canon at the earliest stage); Dartrix canonically functions as the grooming-perfectionist blade-quill mid-stage (the "dandy" Pokedex classification, the canonical reluctance-to-fight-if-feathers-get-dirty mechanic, the blade-quill flavor escalation from Rowlet's general "sharp feathers" to Dartrix's "precisely strikes a weak point" precision-targeting register); Decidueye canonically completes the transition to hooded-archer apex with the arrow-quill firing-in-a-tenth-of-a-second flavor establishing the species' silent-assassin-of-the-forest competitive identity. The species' evolution arc is designer-coordinated as a feather-blade-to-arrow-quill transformation built into the evolutionary mechanic and into the design language itself, with the line's canonical hunter-archer endpoint distinguishing it from the franchise's other Grass-starter apex species (Venusaur is a plant-tank, Meganium is a herald-of-spring, Sceptile is a forest-blade, Torterra is a continent-tree, Serperior is a noble-snake, Chesnaught is a forest-knight, Rillaboom is a percussive-drummer, Meowscarada is a magician-cat — Decidueye is the only ranged-stealth Grass starter).

The line is also the corpus's strongest **typing-shift-at-apex Pokemon-line precedent**. The line is widely community-cited as the **only starter line whose secondary typing does not persist across its full evolutionary line** — Bulbasaur / Ivysaur / Venusaur all retain Grass/Poison typing across all three stages; Charmander / Charmeleon retain pure Fire then Charizard adds Flying at apex (a *type-gain* not a *type-shift*); Squirtle / Wartortle / Blastoise retain pure Water across all three stages; Chikorita / Bayleef / Meganium retain pure Grass; Cyndaquil / Quilava / Typhlosion retain pure Fire (with Hisuian Typhlosion adding Ghost, a Hisuian-region anomaly); Totodile / Croconaw / Feraligatr retain pure Water; Treecko / Grovyle / Sceptile retain pure Grass; Torchic / Combusken / Blaziken retain Fire then add Fighting at Stage-1 mid (a *type-add-at-mid* not a *type-shift*); Mudkip / Marshtomp / Swampert retain Water then add Ground at Stage-1 mid; and so on across every Pokemon generation. Rowlet's Grass/Flying base and middle stages giving way to Decidueye's Grass/Ghost (or Hisuian Grass/Fighting) apex is the *only* case of a starter's secondary typing being *replaced* rather than gained or retained. This typing-shift-at-apex is the line's most-cited structural signature in design-canon retrospective writing, alongside the line's status as the highest-base-HP unevolved starter Pokemon.

For BBL bundle work, the line offers three strong narrative threads: a **owl-becomes-archer transformation** thread anchored on the species' Pokedex canon (the feather-blade-to-arrow-quill design progression as the franchise's clearest one-to-one weapon-form-as-evolution-mechanic mapping among starter lines), a **only-starter-with-typing-shift-at-apex** thread anchored on the line's structural signature (the Grass/Flying-to-Grass/Ghost transition as the franchise's most-cited starter-line typing-shift anomaly), and a **hunter-archer Pokedex flavor canon** thread anchored on Decidueye's silent-assassin-of-the-forest classification (the "fires arrow quills in a tenth of a second" precision-attack register as the line's competitive-tier identity at the Long Reach + Spirit Shackle scope). The cross-set CEC + DAA + SHF presence broadens the line's corpus footprint across three sets and confirms threshold (2 trivia-passed prints across 2 stages, with a 3rd vision-only print and a 4th skeleton-entry print pending).

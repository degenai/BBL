# Lillipup evolutionary line (Lillipup / Herdier / Stoutland)

A three-stage Normal-type evolutionary line and the canonical Unova-region working-dog line introduced in Pokemon Black and White (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 2010). The line comprises **Lillipup** (National Pokedex no. 506, Normal-type, base form, the "Puppy Pokemon" whose Pokedex flavor characterizes it as a small fluffy puppy whose long facial fur functions as a sensory organ for detecting its surroundings), **Herdier** (no. 507, Normal-type, Stage 1, evolves from Lillipup at level 16, the "Loyal Dog Pokemon" whose dense black fur grows continuously and whose canonical role is as a Trainer-partner herding and assistant dog), and **Stoutland** (no. 508, Normal-type, Stage 2, evolves from Herdier at level 32, the "Big-Hearted Pokemon" whose Pokedex canon characterizes it specifically as a trustworthy partner on rescue teams known for rescuing humans stranded in mountains and at sea, drawing real-world design cues from working-breed dogs including Shih Tzu, Maltese, Yorkshire Terrier, Scottish Terrier, and St. Bernard / Newfoundland rescue-breed archetypes). The line is the franchise's canonical Normal-type early-route working-dog cohort representative in the Unova regional Pokedex (no. 494-649), structurally parallel as a three-stage early-route Normal-type mammal line to Kanto's Pidgey-line, Johto's Sentret-line, Hoenn's Zigzagoon-line, Sinnoh's Bidoof-line, and Alola's Yungoos-line, but functionally distinct from those siblings as the franchise's clearest one-to-one species-level rendering of the working-dog / rescue-partner / police-and-herding-dog archetype. The Pokedex characterization of the line as canine-loyalty rendered across three life-stages — puppy at Lillipup, working partner at Herdier, rescue-team apex at Stoutland — is the line's most-cited narrative feature: a designer-coordinated puppy-to-rescue-dog maturation arc rendered as a species-level evolution event, with the working-dog identity present in every stage's Pokedex canon and intensifying with each evolution.

## The design thesis

The Lillipup line's most BBL-relevant property is the **puppy-to-rescue-partner working-dog maturation arc** rendered as a three-stage species-level evolution event, paired with the **canine-loyalty-as-canonical-trait** as the line's structural signature. Where the Larvitar line carries a consume-the-mountain-become-the-mountain canon (cost-of-power rendered as biological transformation), the Staraptor line carries a leaves-the-flock-at-evolution canon (maturation rendered as departure-from-cohort), and the Drilbur line carries a creature-as-drill canon (Pokedex flavor rendered as design-conceit-at-two-scales), the Lillipup line carries a **canine-loyalty-renders-as-real-world-working-dog-archetype canon** — Lillipup canonically functions as the apartment-friendly puppy whose sensory facial fur and quiet temperament establish the species' companion-animal register; Herdier canonically functions as the trained working-partner with continuously-growing dense fur and a Trainer-assistant role; Stoutland canonically functions as the rescue-team apex whose flavor text and Pokedex entries explicitly cite real-world rescue-breed analogs (St. Bernard, Newfoundland) and whose mustache encodes a within-species social-rank hierarchy. The line's evolution arc is designer-coordinated as a puppy-to-working-dog-to-rescue-partner transition with the working-dog identity present from base stage and intensifying through each evolution — distinct from the cost-of-power and departure-from-cohort arcs at sibling lines, this is the franchise's clearest species-level rendering of the **dog-as-laborer / dog-as-partner-in-real-world-rescue-work** archetype.

The line is one of the corpus's strongest candidates for the **working-dog labor-thesis hub** intersection on the Pokemon side. The species' canon at every stage names labor explicitly: Lillipup's apartment-companion role is care-work-coded, Herdier's continuously-growing fur establishes high grooming-labor overhead as a defining species trait (the only mainstream Pokemon species whose flavor text foregrounds the *cost-of-keeping* the species as a balancing trait), Stoutland's rescue-team partnership is canonical labor performed by the species on behalf of humans. The line's working-dog identity is canonical-event (Game Freak's stated species design via Pokedex flavor and species-classification names), not curatorial-overlay (BBL reading working-dog as labor would also be valid but is downstream of the explicit canon). The [[labor]] hub tag is a candidate for the line's cards via canonical-event citation, paralleling Drilbur's existing `labor` hub tag (which traces to the Drilbur line's canon excavation / earthwork labor). This node does **not** wire to the [[labor]] hub directly (the hub is deliberately card-edge-disconnected per `bbl-bundles-are-destructive-on-graph`); the labor-thesis intersection is recorded here as scholarship so a future working-dog or rescue-partner Discrete Lair can cite the line's labor-tier membership from a stable character-layer node rather than from scattered card-edge wikilinks.

For BBL bundle work, the line offers three strong narrative threads: a **puppy-to-rescue-partner working-dog maturation** thread anchored on the species' Pokedex canon (Lillipup's apartment-companion puppy register to Stoutland's rescue-team partnership as the franchise's clearest one-to-one species-evolution-to-real-world-working-dog-archetype mapping at the three-stage scope), a **grooming-cost-as-balancing-trait** thread anchored on Herdier's continuously-growing-fur canon (a rare and underexplored Pokedex thread: a species whose printed flavor names the cost-of-keeping-the-species as a balancing trait, design-coded as the realistic labor of large-dog ownership), and a **rescue-partner labor** thread anchored on Stoutland's apex canon (the species' Pokedex characterization as a trustworthy rescue-team partner rescuing humans stranded in mountains and at sea, with St. Bernard / Newfoundland real-world-breed citation built into the species design). The within-Sun-&-Moon-Base-Set 103+104+105 contiguous-collector-number triptych is the corpus's third full-line within-set evolutionary triptych at this scale (paralleling the within-Vivid-Voltage Charmander-Charmeleon-Charizard 023+024+025 triptych anchored at [[charizard-line]] and at full-line coverage), the second contiguous-collector-number triptych specifically (Charizard's VIV triptych is also adjacent-contiguous), and the first on the Sun-and-Moon-era side. The cohort ships now at full canonical species-line coverage as a complete-line artifact.

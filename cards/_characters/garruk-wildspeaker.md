# Garruk Wildspeaker

The mono-green warrior-druid planeswalker introduced in Lorwyn (2007) as a wilderness hunter and beast-summoner. Across roughly a decade of story output Garruk arced from his original Wildspeaker identity through a Chain Veil-induced corruption into the multi-color Apex Predator / Cursed Huntsman who hunted other planeswalkers, before being cleansed by the Cauldron of Eternity in the Throne of Eldraine storyline (2019) and returning to mono-green as Garruk, Unleashed for Core Set 2021. One of MTG's longest-running green-aligned planeswalkers; canonical antagonist-pair to Liliana Vess (the planeswalker who cursed him via the Chain Veil) across the Garruk vs. Liliana Duel Decks (2009) and subsequent set appearances.

## What this node anchors

Card art that depicts Garruk himself in any of his arc-phases, plus cards whose narrative voice canonically attributes to him via flavor-text quote, plus cards whose title carries his name as the planeswalker-of-record for an in-set creature or enchantment cycle. The character is large enough that he splits across distinct visual phases (Wildspeaker mono-green warrior, Veil-cursed multi-color hunter, Unleashed post-cure return) and the BBL graph can edge those phases together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card.

**Aspects in the corpus (current):**

- **Garruk's voice — flavor-text attribution across reprints** — two cards in the corpus carry first-person quotes attributed to Garruk Wildspeaker, spanning a seven-year period of consistent characterization:
  - **Naturalize** (M14-186, common, Tim Hildebrandt art) — flavor text: *"When your cities and trinkets crumble, only nature will remain." —Garruk Wildspeaker.* The reclamation-by-nature philosophy maps directly onto his anti-civilization wilderness ethos; Garruk speaking over an artifact-destruction spell is the canonical green-vs-artifact attribution.
  - **Ranger's Guile** (M21-199, common, Steve Prescott art) — flavor text: *"You don't survive in the wild by standing in plain sight." —Garruk Wildspeaker.* The patient-predator philosophy anchors his pre-curse Wildspeaker identity; the quote has been carried intact across five reprints (Innistrad 2011, M14 2013, M15 2014, The List 2019, M21 2020) per Scryfall prints-search on oracle_id 694cb93a.

- **Garruk's name — M21 planeswalker cycle as Garruk, Unleashed** — two cards in the corpus are named for Garruk's post-cure phase and represent the in-set planeswalker cycle Wizards built around him as the M21 headlining green planeswalker:
  - **Garruk's Uprising** (M21-186, uncommon, Wisnu Tan art) — the named enchantment of the M21 Garruk cycle; depicts the hunter-and-beast partnership that signs the cleansed Wildspeaker identity. Scryfall card-title attribution; `suspected_ip: Garruk Wildspeaker` resolved here.
  - **Garruk's Gorehorn** (M21-184, common, Svetlin Velinov art) — the named beast of the M21 Garruk cycle; depicts the summoned-companion side of his beast-master canon. Flavor text by Liliana Vess (`"It certainly takes after its master: big and brutish, and you can smell it from a mile away."`) inscribes the Garruk-vs-Liliana antagonism into the card itself — the cursed-by-Liliana relationship is a recurring design touchstone since the 2009 Duel Deck.

All four cards traverse the same character. The two flavor-text quotes (M14 + M21) bracket a seven-year span across which Wizards has held the Wildspeaker characterization constant; the two M21 named cards (Uprising + Gorehorn) anchor the post-cure return that was the planeswalker's most recent canonical phase as of M21 release.

## Caveats

- This node is the character. The **arc phases** (Wildspeaker, Veil-Cursed, Apex Predator, Cursed Huntsman, Unleashed) are distinct visual-and-mechanical identities Wizards has printed under different card frames and color identities; future passes that need to anchor a phase-specific cohort (e.g. multi-color Apex Predator depictions) can split a sibling sub-node, but the current four corpus cards all sit cleanly under the Wildspeaker / Unleashed mono-green pole and don't yet require the split.
- His antagonist Liliana Vess is a distinct character with overlapping iconography in Garruk-adjacent cards. The Gorehorn flavor-text quote is Liliana's voice but the card's narrative subject is Garruk's beast; the card belongs here. A future Liliana character node would also edge to Gorehorn as the speaker side of the same beat.
- M21's headlining-planeswalker design means the Garruk-named cycle (Uprising + Harbinger + Gorehorn) and the Garruk planeswalker cards proper (Garruk, Unleashed M21-183; Garruk, Caller of Beasts M14-172; Garruk, Apex Predator M15-210) form a larger anchor set than the four cards currently in inventory. Future inventory additions of any Garruk-named or Garruk-depicting card attach here without restructuring.

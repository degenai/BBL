# Final Fantasy IX playable party (Zidane's party)

Final Fantasy IX (Square, PlayStation, 2000) is the ninth entry in the mainline series and Hironobu Sakaguchi's deliberate return to the "classic Final Fantasy fantasy register" the series had set aside in the contemporary-tech FFVII and FFVIII. Its playable party is eight characters, gathered across the game by Zidane Tribal (thief / protagonist, originally of the Tantalus Theater Troupe) and opposing first Queen Brahne of Alexandria's invasion campaigns and ultimately the Terran sorcerer Garland's plan to overwrite Gaia with Terra. Unlike FFVI's Returners or FFBE's Visions-trinity, the FFIX party has no single in-canon faction name; the closest is Tantalus itself, which is the four-member theater-troupe-cover-for-thievery that the game opens with and remains a sibling cohort to the eight playable members rather than encompassing them. Square Enix's Final Fantasy Trading Card Game preserves the FFIX roster as the "Category IX" card-frame archetype, recurring across the entire Opus run; Opus III (2017) and Opus VIII (2019) are the two FFTCG sets currently represented in BBL corpus by Category-IX-stamped cards.

## What this node anchors

Card art, card-frame text (Category IX markers, FFIX job-class tags), and FFTCG mechanical design that depicts or supports a canonical FFIX character as either a playable Forward / Backup / Summon on the FFTCG board or as a support piece for a Category IX deck. Membership predicates on (a) the character appearing in the FFIX canon per Square's 2000 source material — either as a playable party member, a named NPC with a definable role (Artemicion's Mognet directorship), or a faction-affiliated figure (Tantalus member, Knights of Pluto, etc.), and (b) the FFTCG printing carrying the explicit Category IX marker in the card frame. Cards that share FFIX vibes but are not Category-IX-stamped (e.g. FFTCG monsters whose flavor cites FFIX bestiary entries but whose card frame is uncategorized) are deliberately excluded. The node intentionally covers both the eight playable members *and* named FFIX NPCs — the FFTCG card-frame predicate is the cohort-membership rule, not "playable in FFIX" specifically.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Vivi** (Opus VIII, 8-016H, hero rarity) — the black mage protagonist of FFIX and the franchise's most-cited single-character emotional-resonance anchor; a manufactured magical construct with a fixed lifespan who develops genuine emotional reasoning where the other Black Mages cannot. The Opus VIII Hero printing depicts him in the Yoshitaka Amano painterly register with his canonical oversized drooping pointed hat, glowing eye-circles beneath the brim, and layered robes — Amano-style rather than Toshiyuki Itahana's final in-game model. Mechanically a 1-cost Fire Forward whose enter-the-battlefield damage scales from 2000 / 7000 / 10000 based on Category IX Character count, designed as the mechanical incentive for full-cast Category IX builds. Market ~$3.95, the highest secondary value of the FFIX cluster in BBL corpus.
- **Garnet EX** (Opus VIII, 8-113C, common foil) — the princess of Alexandria, hereditary Summoner, and one of the two FFIX playable co-protagonists alongside Zidane. The Opus VIII Common printing depicts her in the CG render style with her canonical long dark hair and serene three-quarter portrait against a forest backdrop; mechanically a 2-cost Water Forward Summoner with an EX Burst damage-reduction trigger that activates from the damage zone and an anti-Summon clause reflecting her narrative role as the Summoner who absorbs eidolons. Market ~$0.34 — the entry-tier accessibility pole opposite the Vivi Hero-tier chase price.
- **Freya** (Opus VIII, 8-126H, hero rarity foil) — the Burmecian dragoon and FFIX's most-cited emotionally-resonant character after Vivi; the franchise's first playable female Dragoon with a fixed class (not player-assigned). The Opus VIII Hero printing is the Yoshitaka Amano concept-art reproduction featuring the small jester-companion figure that Itahana's final in-game model removed — a designer-distinct Amano version rather than the Itahana FF9 character we play in-game. Mechanically a Dragoon-synergy Forward whose Haste-conditional clause and power-reduction attack reflect her party dynamics in FF9. Market ~$8.07 — the high-secondary-value chase card of the FFIX cluster.
- **Artemicion** (Opus III, 3-122C, common) — the named moogle director of FFIX's Mognet Central, identified by his canonical striped purple fur (a unique visual trait among FFIX moogles whose standard livery is white). Not a playable party member but a named FFIX NPC, definable via the Mognet sidequest that resolves the game's mail-delivery chain. The Opus III Common printing is a hand-filtering Water Backup whose card text encodes the Mognet-postal-director role rather than a combat ability. Market ~$0.23 — the lowest-tier accessibility anchor of the cluster and the only FFIX representative from Opus III in BBL corpus.

## The design thesis

The FFIX party's most BBL-relevant property is its deliberate species-and-class diversity within a "classic fantasy register" frame — Sakaguchi's stated return to the original-trilogy texture after FFVII / FFVIII's modern-tech aesthetics. The party operates as a contingent gathering rather than a named faction (no Returners-style organizational identity), and the canonical-event structure is *kidnapping → flight → mounting opposition → metaphysical-stakes resolution* rather than the FF6-Returners *resistance-against-named-extraction* shape. This places FFIX's party adjacent to but distinct from the `[[rebellion]]` hub that anchors the FF6 Returners — the FFIX party opposes Queen Brahne's invasion and Garland's overwrite-plan but does so via the JRPG-protagonist-contingency-gathering shape, not via a pre-existing named resistance faction.

Per Potion-rule sub-rule 2 (`[[bbl-museum-curation-framing]]` parent, `[[_triple-thesis]]` canonical), no `related_hubs` adjacency is asserted at this node level. The FFIX party's canonical event-structure is JRPG-contingent-protagonist-gathering rather than labor / rebellion / stewardship-coded; a bundle built around this cluster is free to extrapolate via per-card connections (Vivi's prototype-different-manufactured-construct reading as apparatus-of-extraction-coded, etc.), but the node level keeps `related_hubs: []` to avoid manufacturing canonical-event evidence FFIX's source material does not itself stage. This matches the `[[ffbe-opening-arc-party]]` precedent of refusing the hub adjacency at the cohort layer rather than borrowing it from a sibling cohort node's framing.

The cluster's narrative spine for a Discrete Lair built around this corpus slice would be "the contingent party — kidnapped princess, manufactured mage, dragoon in mourning, postal-service moogle — across two Opus sets, FFIX's class-and-species diversity carried into FFTCG's Category-IX cohort frame." The Vivi Hero-tier price ($3.95) and the Freya Hero-tier foil price ($8.07) are the secondary-value anchors; the Garnet Common foil ($0.34) and Artemicion Common ($0.23) provide the cohort-density-at-budget-tier that any FFIX bundle needs to read as substrate-dense rather than chase-card-driven.

## Caveats

- **Modeled as a multi-character party-and-named-NPC node, not a per-character node.** This follows the `[[ff6-returners-party]]`, `[[ffbe-opening-arc-party]]`, `[[universe-6-tournament-cohort]]`, and `[[crane-school-cohort]]` precedent — an FFTCG Category cohort is hosted as a single character-layer node rather than a per-member fan-out. When BBL acquires additional FFIX printings (Zidane, Steiner, Quina, Eiko, Amarant, additional Vivi / Garnet / Freya printings from Opus III or other sets, or named NPCs like Stiltzkin or Beatrix), the new card attaches here rather than spawning a per-character node. If a future single-character anchor becomes load-bearing (e.g. corpus accumulates 4+ Vivi printings across Opus III, VIII, XXVIII, and other sets such that a per-character `vivi-ornitier` node beats this cohort node for query purposes), Edgelord-enlightened can refactor by adding a child node, redirecting the relevant cards' pointers, and updating this node's prose to reference the child.
- **The FFIX party has no single in-canon faction name, unlike FFVI's Returners.** This node uses the descriptive "Zidane's party" framing in the title rather than asserting a name FFIX's canon does not provide. The closest in-canon collective name is Tantalus (the four-member theater-troupe-cover-for-thievery Zidane belongs to before the game starts), but Tantalus is structurally distinct from the eight playable members — it's a sibling cohort within the FFIX world rather than an encompassing one. The FFTCG card-frame marker "Category IX" is the operative membership predicate, matching the `[[ffbe-opening-arc-party]]` precedent of using descriptive framing rather than faction-name framing when the source material doesn't provide one.
- **Per Potion-rule sub-rule 2 (`[[bbl-museum-curation-framing]]` parent, `[[_triple-thesis]]` canonical), no triple-thesis hub adjacency is asserted at this node level.** The FFIX party's canonical event-structure is JRPG-contingent-protagonist-gathering (kidnapping → flight → mounting opposition → metaphysical-stakes resolution) rather than labor / rebellion / stewardship-coded. A bundle built around this cluster is free to extrapolate via per-card connections (Vivi's prototype-different manufactured construct reading as apparatus-of-extraction-coded; Garnet's hereditary-Summoner heritage reading as bloodline-extraction-coded), but the node level keeps `related_hubs: []` to avoid manufacturing canonical-event evidence FFIX itself does not stage as such. This is the same discipline `[[ffbe-opening-arc-party]]`, `[[saiyan]]`, and `[[eldrazi]]` exhibit and the explicit precedent for refusing the adjacency at this layer.
- **Artemicion (3-122C) is a named NPC rather than a playable party member.** This node's scope deliberately covers both — the FFTCG card-frame predicate is Category IX, not "playable in FFIX" specifically, and the cohort-membership pattern that matters for FFTCG deck construction is the Category IX marker. The roster table separates playable members and named NPCs to make the in-game distinction legible without overloading the node's appears_on predicate.
- **Eiko is FFIX's second summoner and Garnet's narrative counterpart.** The Garnet 8-113C Related-cards entry references Eiko (Opus VIII) explicitly. When an Eiko printing enters BBL corpus, it attaches here. The Garnet-Eiko summoner-dyad is the narrative spine of FFIX's mid-game and would warrant body-prose attention in a future Discrete Lair build, but does not warrant a separate Garnet-Eiko sibling node — both are FFIX party members and belong on this cohort node.
- **Vivi's future-print catalog is documented in the Vivi 8-016H Related cards.** Vivi (Opus III, 3-017L Legend tier) and Vivi (Opus XXVIII, 28-016L Legend reprint) are FFTCG-printed but not yet in BBL corpus. When either lands, they attach here. The corpus's first FFIX character pointer redirect (the original `vivi-ornitier` pointer on 8-016H was retargeted to this node per the wave-establishing-this-node pass) is the operative pattern for any future Vivi-print additions — they go here, not into a per-character node.

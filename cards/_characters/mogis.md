# Mogis, God of Slaughter

Theros's red-black god of slaughter and the dark twin of Iroas (red-white god of victory). Mogis is the pantheon's patron of bloodlust, the canonical lord of Theros's minotaur race, and the named deity of the polis of Skophos in the northern Phoberos canyons — the city-state whose center is the largest temple to Mogis on the plane. Per the *Theros Pantheon* roster (MTG Wiki Pantheon of Theros page) Mogis is one of the twelve principal pantheon members; he debuted as a printed card in *Born of the Gods* (BNG-151, 2014) and has been name-referenced across original *Theros* (2013) and *Theros Beyond Death* (2020) flavor text on cards depicting his minotaur servitors. Sub-node under [[theros-pantheon]] — Mogis is the pantheon's most thesis-aligned member for BBL's apparatus-of-extraction reading of the Theros block, and the parent roster node carries the institutional context this sub-node refers back into.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts Mogis-cult operatives (minotaur priests, shamans, warleaders, charging minotaur hordes), names Mogis directly in flavor or card name, depicts the Skophos polis or its temples, or invokes Mogis as the supernatural cause of mortal violence and battle-rage. The anchor inherits the parent [[theros-pantheon]] node's *named-god reference in card name or flavor OR depiction of a designer-confirmed god-cult operative class* predicate, narrowed here to Mogis specifically: Mogis-named cards (Soulreaper of Mogis, Slaughter-Priest of Mogis), Mogis-flavor-text-attributed cards (Infuriate's *"The fury of Mogis was upon it,"* Portent of Betrayal's *"Mogis can fan a small flame of resentment into an inferno of madness"*), Mogis-polis-canonical cards (Skophos Warleader's burning Mogis-temples and *"god of slaughter"* flavor reference), and Mogis-servitor-creature-type cards (Kragma Warcaller's Theran-minotaur-herd whose canonical patron is Mogis per MTG Wiki).

The Mogis anchor inherits all the Theros-pantheon institutional context — devotion-as-labor-of-belief, designer-coordinated Greek-mythology homage, gods-sustained-by-mortal-cults — and overlays the *named extraction-apparatus deity who runs the bloodiest cult*: where the parent pantheon node is the fifteen-god roster, this sub-node is the corpus's clearest single-god face of the apparatus-of-extraction thesis.

The depicted-figures are canonically Mogis-servitors per Wizards lore; the "meat-hunger" flavor term is canonical Mogis-doctrinal language for the minotaur race's bloodlust.

## The Mogis thesis

Mogis is the Theros pantheon's most BBL-relevant member for the apparatus-of-extraction reading of the [[labor]] hub. Where the broader pantheon's devotion-mechanic frames the *general* relationship of gods-sustained-by-mortal-labor, Mogis's specific cult mechanizes the relationship as **explicit sacrifice-for-power: the worshipper offers life (the lives of "the weak," in Soulreaper's flavor; the offering Slaughter-Priest demands more of; the creature sacrificed at Skophos Warleader's menace-activation; the mortal-on-mortal violence Mogis "fans into an inferno") and receives martial-strength in return.** This is the apparatus-of-extraction reading of pantheon-devotion in its sharpest single-god form on the plane.

The Mogis-cult's specific institutional features for the corpus are:

- **Transactional theology rendered as card mechanic.** Every Mogis-cult card in the corpus either contains a sacrifice clause in its oracle text (Soulreaper THB-115's `{2}{B}, sacrifice a creature: draw a card`; Slaughter-Priest THB-227's `{2}, sacrifice another creature or enchantment: first strike`; Skophos Warleader THB-154's `{R}, sacrifice another creature or enchantment: +1/+0 and menace`) or depicts the result of a Mogis-invoked frenzy (Infuriate THB-141's arrow-pierced charging minotaur; Portent of Betrayal THB-149's mortal-betraying-mortal). The mechanic IS the doctrine: blood-tithe upward, martial-power downward.
- **Greek-mythology homage with designer-confirmed mapping.** Mogis is Theros's Ares-as-slaughter (his twin Iroas is Ares-as-just-war), a deliberate split of the Greek war-god's double-aspect across two named pantheon members. Per MTG Wiki's Mogis page and Mark Rosewater's Theros design columns (2013-2014), the twin-of-Iroas relation is Wizards-confirmed design intent — Mogis carries the brutal-wrath-of-war register, Iroas carries the honorable-victory register, and the same Greek mythic figure (Ares) is rendered through the pair. The [[iroas]] sub-node is now in the BBL graph as a sibling under [[theros-pantheon]] (commissioned once the threshold-of-3 Iroas-cult corpus anchors was met: Indomitable Will THB-25, Hero of the Games THB-137, Armory of Iroas CMR-299); the character-to-character twin-edge between [[iroas]] and this node is now drawn at both ends.
- **Minotaur-race patron with named polis (Skophos).** Theran minotaurs are canonically Mogis-servitors per Wizards lore (MTG Wiki: Mogis; MTG Wiki: Skophos; in-corpus trivia on Kragma Warcaller THS-195 and Skophos Warleader THB-154). The polis of Skophos in northern Phoberos is Mogis's principal cult-city, and its temples (depicted burning in Skophos Warleader's background) are canonical Mogis-temples per MTG Wiki's Skophos page. This makes Mogis the only pantheon member in the corpus with a single named cult-city anchor; future Phoberos / Skophos-named cards edge here directly.
- **"Crimson eye" / blood-moon liturgy.** Per MTG Wiki's Mogis page (cited in-corpus on Soulreaper of Mogis's trivia), Mogis's worship involves "brutal rites, ritual self-mutilation, and blood feasts, particularly during the blood moon (his 'crimson eye')." The red-saturated palettes of every corpus Mogis-cult card (red haze in Slaughter-Priest, ember-orange in Soulreaper, blood-red in Infuriate, crimson in Portent of Betrayal, ember-red in Skophos Warleader) read as the visual signature of this liturgical chromatic.

This is what BBL bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Mogis is the Theros pantheon's most extraction-thesis-aligned god, and the corpus carries 6 anchors across 2 sets (Theros 2013, Theros Beyond Death 2020) — a depth comparable to the parent pantheon node's broader 18-card stack but concentrated on a single deity's cult. A future Mogis Discrete Lair — a *Slaughter Tithe* parallel to the Orzhov *Tithe* — earns its lore-aware buyer credibility from Wizards's own decade of Mogis-cult flavor writing without naming the apparatus-of-extraction thesis.

## Caveats

- **Sub-node under [[theros-pantheon]], not standalone.** Mogis is one of fifteen Theros pantheon members; this sub-node inherits institutional context from the parent. Bundle work that anchors on Mogis should anchor on the [[theros-pantheon]] context simultaneously — the devotion-as-labor-of-belief reading at the pantheon-scope is what makes Mogis's specific apparatus-of-extraction register legible as a designer-coordinated thesis-piece rather than a one-off cult-card.

- **No printed Mogis god-card in the corpus (yet).** Mogis, God of Slaughter (BNG-151) is the canonical printed deity card and the parent-anchor for this entire cult, but it is NOT currently in BBL inventory. All six corpus anchors are cult-operatives, Mogis-flavor-text-attributed cards, or Mogis-polis-canonical cards — Mogis the god himself does not appear as a printed card in our holdings. When (if) a Mogis god-card lands in inventory (BNG-151 in any printing, or Theros Stargazing Secret Lair Mogis reprint), it becomes the headline anchor here and the existing six fall into place as supporting cult-evidence.

- **Iroas (Mogis's twin) is now in the BBL graph as a sibling sub-node.** The Iroas-Mogis Greek-twin-split is Wizards-confirmed design intent and reads as the cleanest pantheon-internal character-to-character relation in Theros canon. The [[iroas]] sub-node landed in the iroas-sub-node-proposal commission once the threshold-of-3 substantive corpus anchors was met (Indomitable Will THB-25 flavor naming Iroas twice via the named cult-priest Verenis; Hero of the Games THB-137 canonicalizing the Iroan Games as Iroas-presided at Akros; Armory of Iroas CMR-299 with Iroas named directly in the card title). The character-to-character twin-edge between [[iroas]] and this node is now drawn at both ends — this node's `related_characters` frontmatter carries the iroas pointer and the See-also section below registers the sibling-sub-node relation; the iroas-side mirrors are in place at cards/_characters/iroas.md.

- **The borderline anchor is Kragma Warcaller (THS-195).** Mogis is NOT named in Kragma Warcaller's flavor text or card name; the depicted figures are canonically Mogis-servitors only via Wizards-published lore on Theran minotaurs (MTG Wiki Mogis page, in-corpus trivia citation). This is the same evidence-tier as Opaline Unicorn (THS-218) and Plummet (THB-194) on the parent pantheon node — flavor-text-or-canonical-lore attribution rather than card-name direct reference. The anchor is substantive but is the weakest of the six; future pruning passes may want to revisit whether minotaur-servitor-creatures without flavor-text Mogis attribution belong at this node or at the parent pantheon node. Current judgment: include, because the Kragma Warcaller trivia explicitly cites Mogis and treats the card as Mogis-anchored; the BBL graph treats trivia-pass-cited canonical attribution as first-class evidence per the parent node's *flavor-text-attribution-only entries are first-class node members* caveat.

- **Apparatus-of-extraction reading is interpretive, not designer-stated.** The framing that Mogis-cult mechanics *render the apparatus-of-extraction in card form* is BBL's narrative-first analytical reading per `[[bbl-narrative-first-lairs]]` and `[[bbl-museum-curation-framing]]`, not a Wizards-design-stated thesis. The mechanic-and-doctrine connection (sacrifice clauses in oracle text mirroring blood-tithe in flavor text) is canonical design; the *apparatus-of-extraction* register is BBL's bundle copy framing the canonical design in the project's brand voice. Same Edgelord-discipline split as the parent [[theros-pantheon]] node's labor-of-belief framing and the [[orzhov-syndicate]] node's apparatus-of-extraction reading.

- **Bundle-anchor potential is strong.** 6 corpus anchors across 2 sets (Theros 2013, Theros Beyond Death 2020) form a deep single-cult cohesion stack — comparable in single-cult depth to the [[orzhov-syndicate]] node's broader 8-card stack but concentrated on a single deity. A future Mogis-cult Discrete Lair is one of the corpus's strongest single-character bundle candidates by both depth (6 anchors) and thesis-cohesion (every card is the sacrifice-for-power mechanic-and-doctrine fusion). Reserve for when the Theros-block subarchetype gets its commission; the [[theros-pantheon]] parent already pre-stages this as wave-53+ revisit material in its own Caveats section.

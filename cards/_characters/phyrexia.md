# Phyrexia

Magic: The Gathering's signature body-horror antagonist faction. Per MTG Wiki's Phyrexia page, the original Phyrexia was an artificial plane of nine concentric metal spheres engineered into existence by the father-god Yawgmoth — a former Thran physician whose ascent across the Brothers' War, Weatherlight, and Invasion arcs (1995–2001) established him as the franchise's first multiversal antagonist. Yawgmoth-era Phyrexia was destroyed in the climactic Invasion-block events; the praetor Karn — repurposing the corrupted Mirrodin plane — became the unwilling origin of **New Phyrexia** (Mirrodin Besieged / New Phyrexia, 2011), the post-Yawgmoth successor faction ruled by five Praetors (Elesh Norn the angel of the white sphere, Sheoldred the whispering one of the black sphere, Vorinclex the voice of hunger of the green sphere, Jin-Gitaxias the progress-tyrant of the blue sphere, Urabrask the heretic of the red sphere). The 2022–2023 Phyrexian Arc — Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty, Phyrexia: All Will Be One, March of the Machine — extended the faction's reach across the entire Multiverse via the **compleation** of native planeswalkers and the Realmbreaker invasion, culminating in the canonical defeat of Elesh Norn and the post-arc fragmentation of New Phyrexia at the end of March of the Machine.

The faction's signature operational verb is **compleation**: the involuntary surgical-magical conversion of biological beings into Phyrexian apparatus, branded with the Phyrexian glyph and bound to the Machine Orthodoxy's hierarchical-religious command structure. The Phyrexian aesthetic register — exposed ribcages, bladed prosthetic limbs, oil-corrupted flesh, biomechanical fusion of organic and metal — is one of Magic's most recognizable visual-design vocabularies and the most-printed villain-faction iconography across the game's thirty-year publishing history.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts Phyrexia as an *institution*: its rank-and-file horrors (the bladed-skeletal common-rarity figures), its larger apparatus (engines of compleation, Phyrexian factories and ironworks, oil-corrupted wurms and dragons), its named leadership (Yawgmoth, the Praetors, named compleated planeswalkers like Tezzeret and Tamiyo), its signature iconography (the Phyrexian phyresis-glyph, oil-black tar, bladed-limb anatomy, exposed-bone industrial-aesthetic), and the faction's institutional thesis — that the conversion of biology into apparatus is the highest form of perfection, and that universal compleation is the religious obligation of the Machine Orthodoxy.

Phyrexia is the BBL corpus's most thesis-aligned in-universe faction for the apparatus-of-conversion register: any bundle on body-horror-as-infrastructure, the cyborg as forced labor, biology-as-raw-material-for-the-machine, or the institutionalization of bodily transformation indexes here. Structurally this is the Magic-side counterpart to Pokemon's [[team-rocket]] / [[team-skull]] / [[macro-cosmos]] / [[aether-foundation]] villain-faction nodes — a designer-coordinated criminal-or-criminal-adjacent institution whose institutional logic IS the BBL-relevant thesis. Where the Orzhov Syndicate extracts via theology-dressed-as-finance, Phyrexia extracts via theology-dressed-as-surgery — the apparatus changes, the underlying extraction-by-institutional-violence does not.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Phyrexian Ironworks** (MH3-130, uncommon, Mathias Kollros art) — abstract close-up of a polished gold-and-bronze Phyrexian factory device suspended in a roiling purple-magenta-crimson void, blue lightning crackling across its surface, hoses and cables connecting it to off-frame components. Mechanically a colorless artifact generating energy counters on attack and exchanging them for 3/3 colorless **Phyrexian Golem** artifact creature tokens — the card IS the Phyrexian apparatus-of-mass-production rendered as a mana-rock that manufactures Phyrexian soldiers. Flavor text: *"We have made a friend today." — Slobad, Iron Goblin.* (The flavor speaker is canonically a goblin character who befriended the planeswalker Glissa on Mirrodin, predating its corruption; the "friend" is the device, the line is the goblin-engineer admiring the machine without recognizing it as Phyrexian — designer dark-irony.)

- **Wurmcoil Larva** (MH3-112, uncommon, David Astruga art) — a massive segmented mechanical-organic wurm rears in a dim amber chamber, its head ringed with spines and its body bioluminescing yellow-green between interlocking armor plates, with a robed humanoid figure in the background raising both arms in alarm or control. Mechanically a green/black-tokens deathtouch-lifelink artifact creature that, on death, creates two Phyrexian Wurm artifact creature tokens (a 1/2 deathtouch and a 2/1 lifelink — the canonical Wurmcoil Engine token split scaled down for the larva form). Flavor text: *"Even a scrap of Phyrexian machinery can be lethal."* The card is a juvenile-form printing of Mirrodin's iconic Wurmcoil Engine (SOM-149, 2010), the most-played Phyrexian artifact in Magic's history; the larva is the canonical lifecycle predecessor to the engine, designer-flagged Phyrexian by name, oracle text (Phyrexian Wurm tokens), and flavor.

- **Phyrexian Rager** (Mystery Booster Cards 62, originally Commander Anthology / Apocalypse, common, Stephan Martiniere art) — a hunched emaciated Phyrexian horror with bladed limbs, exposed ribs, and a snarling skull-like face loomed forward against a black void. Mechanically `{1}{B}{B}` 2/2 Horror, ETB draws a card and loses 1 life — the canonical "draw a card, lose 1 life" Phyrexian common dating back to Apocalypse (2001) and reprinted across nearly two decades of supplemental product. Flavor text: *"I believe many worlds will bow to Phyrexia. Mirrodin is merely the first." — Sheoldred, Whispering One.* The flavor speaker is canonically the black-sphere Praetor, foreshadowing (from the 2001 print's perspective) the New Phyrexia / Mirrodin-corruption arc that wouldn't be storylined until 2011 — a designer retroactively-prophetic flavor-text moment that the Mystery Booster reprint deliberately preserves.

- **Etched Slith** (MH3-91, uncommon, Allen Williams art) — a skeletal acid-green undead creature mid-lunge through a dark void, its translucent body lit by internal bioluminescence and backed by etched triangular runes scratched into a surface. Mechanically `{1}{B}` 1/1 Phyrexian Horror with Menace and a damage-counter-shuffle trigger (combat damage to a player adds a +1/+1 counter, then removes a counter from another permanent or opponent). Flavor text: *"The inherently adaptive bodies of the slith made them coveted subjects for compleation."* The slith is a Mirrodin-native creature subtype returning in *Modern Horizons 3* under the set's heavy New-Phyrexia-throwback design brief; the card is the canonical post-compleation rendering of the Mirrodin slith lineage, with the flavor text doing the designer-explicit faction-attribution work.

## The functional ideology

Phyrexia's most BBL-relevant property is the same as the Orzhov Syndicate's: **its institutional logic IS the thesis**. Where the Orzhov organize around the extraction of obligation (debt past death, ledger-as-eternal), Phyrexia organize around the extraction of biological form — the conversion of all sapient life into Phyrexian apparatus, branded with the faction glyph, sworn to the Machine Orthodoxy, and assigned a sphere-coded function under one of the five Praetors. The faction's defining features per MTG Wiki and the Phyrexia: All Will Be One / March of the Machine design articles:

- **Compleation is religious obligation, not war crime.** Per the Machine Orthodoxy, the unconverted biological body is incomplete; compleation completes it. The institutional rhetoric of the faction frames forced conversion as salvation, with the praetors as evangelizing priest-engineers and the rank-and-file Phyrexians as the converted faithful. This rhetorical inversion — atrocity rendered as gospel — is the single most BBL-thesis-aligned operational feature of any Magic faction.

- **The plane manufactures itself.** Phyrexia and New Phyrexia are both *engineered* planes — surfaces of polished metal, oil rivers, sphere-divided industrial zones, glistening furnaces. The Phyrexian Ironworks (MH3-130) and analogous factory-cards depict the faction's signature scenography: industrial-religious architecture indistinguishable from organic-religious architecture, with workers and worshippers indistinguishable from one another and from the machines they tend.

- **Universal scope.** Unlike Ravnican guilds, which operate within one plane, Phyrexia's stated goal is the compleation of every plane in the Multiverse. The 2022–2023 Phyrexian Arc rendered this scope as the Realmbreaker invasion of every storyline plane simultaneously. No other Magic faction has equivalent canonical universal-conquest framing.

- **Compleated planeswalkers as the highest-stakes asset.** The faction's central late-canon move is the conversion of native protagonist planeswalkers into Phyrexian operatives — Tezzeret (Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty), [[tamiyo]] (Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty), Vraska (Phyrexia: All Will Be One), Ajani (Phyrexia: All Will Be One). The compleated-planeswalker arc is the canonical anchor point for cross-character body-horror narratives in the corpus. This node and the Tamiyo node share a load-bearing edge: Tamiyo is the first canonical Phyrexian planeswalker, and the Tamiyo node's `species` field explicitly carries the "Phyrexian-compleated planeswalker (post-Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty)" classification.

This is what BBL bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Phyrexia is, in-universe, a designer-confirmed apparatus-of-conversion dressed as a religion, scaled to multiversal-conquest scope, with a thirty-year published history of escalating institutional-body-horror printings. Bundle prose that names Phyrexia by name earns lore-aware buyer credibility and lets Wizards' own decades of faction-design work do the thesis work.

## Caveats

- **Phyrexia (original, Yawgmoth-era) and New Phyrexia are storyline-distinct but graph-unified here.** The original Nine-Spheres Phyrexia was destroyed in Invasion (2001); New Phyrexia originated on the corrupted Mirrodin plane in Mirrodin Besieged (2011) under the five praetors. The two are canonically distinct entities — separate planes, separate leaderships, separate time periods — but share the same faction continuity (Phyrexian aesthetic, compleation mechanism, Machine Orthodoxy ideology) and Wizards designs them as a single multi-era faction across product. This node anchors both at the institutional level; a future sub-node split (`phyrexia-original` vs `phyrexia-new` vs `phyrexia-fragmented-post-MOM`) is warranted only if the corpus accumulates enough era-specific cards to require disambiguation. For now, single node, faction-continuity precedent.

- **Compleated planeswalkers edge here as faction-members AND to their own character nodes as the converted-individual.** [[tamiyo]] is the canonical first Phyrexian planeswalker (post-Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty); the Tamiyo node's `species` field already carries the "Phyrexian-compleated planeswalker" classification. The Collector of Tales / Compleated Sage character flip is the canonical pre/post-conversion split. Cards depicting compleated planeswalkers edge to both nodes (the individual and the faction); the depiction-or-narrative-subject test applies, same as the Bolas precedent.

- **Phyrexian creature subtype and Phyrexia faction membership are correlated but not co-extensive.** Some non-faction creatures carry the Phyrexian type via lore-mechanical alignment (e.g., compleated non-Phyrexian-origin creatures); some faction-aligned cards do not carry the Phyrexian creature type (e.g., named planeswalkers in their compleated form retain the Planeswalker — Tamiyo subtype, not Phyrexian). Membership predicates on canonical faction-alignment per Wizards' story output, not subtype alone. Scryfall's `watermark:phyrexian` is the cleanest mechanical-tier classifier; the Phyrexian creature-type catches the visual-aesthetic membership but over-fires on edge cases.

- **The Phyrexian language and its associated glyph are distinct from this node.** The Phyrexian script — the alien typography that replaces English on flavor-text and oracle-text in some Phyrexia-themed printings — is a symbol-layer asset, not a faction-layer one. If a future symbol node anchors the Phyrexian glyph / phyresis-mark, it would edge here as the iconographic primitive that the faction stamps on its converted members and its constructed apparatus. For now, no symbol node has been commissioned; the glyph is recorded as iconography in the Phyrexian Rager and Phyrexian Ironworks vision passes.

- **Slobad, Iron Goblin (Phyrexian Ironworks flavor speaker) is a named non-Phyrexian character.** The goblin engineer was canonically Glissa's companion on pre-corruption Mirrodin; his flavor text on the MH3 print is designer dark-irony (a goblin admiring the device that is, in fact, the apparatus of his own faction's destruction). Slobad is a deferred-node candidate if more Mirrodin-arc cards accumulate; for now he is recorded as flavor-speaker context, not anchored.

- **Sheoldred (Phyrexian Rager flavor speaker) is one of the five Praetors and warrants her own sub-node if multiple Sheoldred-depicting cards land in the corpus.** Same for Elesh Norn (the white-sphere praetor and canonical defeated antagonist of the March of the Machine arc), Vorinclex, Jin-Gitaxias (Tamiyo's compleator), and Urabrask. Each praetor is a load-bearing sub-character at the faction's storyline-arc scope; the precedent is the same as the Orzhov Syndicate's Karlov-family caveat (sub-faction nodes warranted at sufficient corpus accumulation, deferred until then).

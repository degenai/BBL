# Toy horror cohort (Duskmourn corrupted-childhood artifact-creatures)

A *Duskmourn: House of Horror* design cohort of artifact-creatures depicting corrupted-childhood objects — dolls, stuffed animals, jack-in-the-boxes, patchwork constructs — animated into stalking horrors. The cohort is the set's most direct expression of the killer-doll / cursed-toy slasher-horror register (Annabelle, Chucky, the Pinhead-tier ventriloquist-dummy lineage) translated into the *Magic* artifact-creature vocabulary. Per the set's design coverage (MTG Wiki and Rosewater's *Top of the Duskmourning* articles), DSK was built top-down from a horror-trope taxonomy in which "haunted toys" sits alongside "ghost in the house," "phobia personified," and "survivor with improvised weapon" as one of the set's core source-material registers; the Toy creature subtype was introduced or expanded in DSK to mark this cohort at the type-line level.

## What this node anchors

Card art, oracle text, flavor text, and the Toy creature subtype for the *Duskmourn* artifact-creatures whose subject is a **corrupted childhood object animated into a predator**. Membership predicates on three overlapping signals: (a) the Toy creature subtype on the type-line where present, (b) the art's depiction of a recognizable childhood-object form (doll, stuffed animal, jester-toy, patchwork construct) rendered as menacing, (c) the flavor / oracle register treating the object's animation as a *corruption* of its intended innocent function (the teddy bear that mauls, the jack-in-the-box that attacks, the patchwork toy whose stitching is its weapon).

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Patchwork Beastie** (DSK-195, U, John Tedrick art) — a hulking patchwork construct assembled from mismatched fabric, straw stuffing, and a carved mask-face with glowing amber eyes, carrying a smaller doll-creature on its back through a misty grey forest. Mechanically a `{2}{G}{G}` Artifact Creature with Delirium: can't attack/block unless four card types in graveyard, mills a card at upkeep. Flavor text: *"It doesn't know it isn't real."* The flavor is the cohort's load-bearing thesis-statement: the toy as a thing animated past its meaning, walking around inside a story that has stopped being childhood.
- **Patched Plaything** (DSK-24, U, Domenico Cava art) — a tattered teddy bear with a baby-doll face stitched onto its torso, claws and thorns at the lower frame, fanged mouth gaping. Mechanically a `{1}{B}` Artifact Creature with Double Strike; enters with two -1/-1 counters if cast from hand. Flavor text: *"When it ripped, its owner attempted to fix it. Something went... wrong."* The fix-it-yourself origin story is the cohort's recurring trigger — the loving repair that opens the object to corruption, the stitch that the haunted-house climbs through.
- **Attack-in-the-Box** (DSK-242, U, Domenico Cava art) — a spectral jester-or-clown figure with a two-pronged bell-cap and ruffled collar emerging from a darkened indoor corridor, body tapering into a spiraling supernatural form, hands skeletal. Mechanically a `{4}` Artifact Creature with an attack trigger granting +4/+0 then sacrificing it at end step. Flavor text: *"Pop goes the evil."* The jack-in-the-box recoded as the spring-loaded ambush attack, with the rules-text sacrifice-after-swing encoding the box's one-pop-and-it's-spent grammar at the rules level.

The three corpus members span three distinct childhood-object source archetypes — the *stuffed-animal-mauler* (Patched Plaything), the *patchwork-construct* (Patchwork Beastie), the *jester-toy-ambush* (Attack-in-the-Box) — giving the cohort node a designer-coordinated three-archetype substrate without redundancy. Two of three artists are Domenico Cava (Plaything + Attack-in-the-Box), forming a within-cohort artist-anchor pair that a bundle-cohesion-stack thumbnail can lean on.

## The cohort thesis

The toy-horror cohort's most BBL-relevant property is its **two-step corruption arc**: childhood object → loving home-repair → animation past the repair. Each cohort member's flavor or visual cue carries the repair beat — Patched Plaything's flavor text spells it out verbatim ("its owner attempted to fix it"), Patchwork Beastie's art is *assembled-from-scraps* (a hands-on repair pose externalized), Attack-in-the-Box's spring-loaded ambush mechanic encodes the toy as a device that was wound and waiting. The cohort thesis is that the toys are not naturally haunted — they are made haunted by the affection that tried to save them. This is the cohort's narrative-first hook: not slashers, but the *failure of care under haunted-house conditions*, where the act of mending becomes the act of opening the seam through which Valgavoth's haunt arrives.

The cohort also dual-codes the set's broader Delirium / graveyard-matters subtheme. Patchwork Beastie's rules-text both *requires* graveyard density (four card types) and *generates* it (upkeep mill); Patched Plaything's -1/-1-counters-on-entry is a Delirium-feeder by way of dying small. The toys are mechanically configured as engines that fill the graveyard with the act of being toys — every cohort member is, in effect, the *house's* memory filling up faster.

## Adjacent cohorts and structural placement

- **[[fear-of-cycle]]** — sibling DSK cohort character node; the **phobia-personification** axis of the same horror cosmology. Where the Fear-of cycle personifies abstract dreads as Nightmares, the toy-horror cohort personifies *specific corrupted objects* as Toys. The two cohorts together cover the two principal *horror-source* axes of the set: phobias-as-dreads (Fear-of) and objects-as-monsters (toy-horror). Both feed the same horror cosmology; both point at Valgavoth as the proximate cause.
- **[[valgavoth]]** — the demon whose plane animates the toys. Where Fear-of-cycle members are the dreads Valgavoth's plane invents, toy-horror cohort members are the *objects* his plane corrupts. The character-level antagonist behind both cohorts.

Note: the corpus also includes a sibling **DSK improvised-survival-grammar cohort** (Baseball Bat + Conductive Machete + Frantic Strength) — the **survivors' tools** cohort, structurally the *opposing pole* of the toy-horror cohort (survivors wield improvised weapons; the haunted house wields corrupted toys). The two cohorts together anchor the set's predator-vs-prey vocabulary at the artifact / equipment layer. If the survivor cohort is later commissioned as its own node (see Future-corpus section below), the two will edge as opposing-pole sibling cohorts in the same set.

## Caveats

- **Cohort threshold met at 3/N; full canonical roster not yet enumerated.** Three cohort members are in BBL corpus and pointered to this node (see `appears_on:`). The canonical roster is bounded by the set's full Toy-subtyped roster on Scryfall (`s:dsk t:toy`) plus any non-Toy-subtyped cards that match the cohort's three-signal predicate (Toy creature subtype OR corrupted-childhood-object art OR repair-corruption flavor register). A future Scryfall sweep should enumerate the full DSK Toy-subtyped roster and any cohort-eligible adjacent cards (e.g. *Patchwork Gnomes* DSK-231 surfaced at the wave 59 vision pass as a magic-side patchwork-toy precedent but is not yet in corpus); attach future-corpus members here as they land via Collectr CSV uploads.
- **Toy creature subtype is the strongest predicate but not the only one.** Some cohort-eligible cards may carry adjacent subtypes (Construct, Nightmare, Horror) when the depicted form is a hybrid (Patchwork Beastie carries Beast in the art register but the type-line is Artifact Creature with no creature subtype in the corpus snapshot, pending verification against Scryfall). Membership predicates on the cohort's three-signal test (subtype OR art OR flavor register), not on the Toy type-line alone. Future passes should verify per-card type-line strings against Scryfall and flag any cohort-eligible non-Toy-subtyped members in the caveats.
- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like [[fear-of-cycle]], [[dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle]], [[eld-cinderella-cycle]], this is a collective designer-coordinated cohort entity hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer accepts both individual named characters and collective cohorts). A future `_cycles/` or `_cohorts/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the other designer-confirmed flavor cycles when that split is commissioned; for now, single layer, same precedent.
- **Repair-corruption is a thesis not a strict membership predicate.** Patched Plaything's flavor text spells the thesis out verbatim; Patchwork Beastie's art carries it; Attack-in-the-Box's mechanic encodes it more obliquely (the wound-and-waiting toy). A future cohort member whose horror register is *purely* possession-without-repair (e.g. a doll that was always evil, never mended) is still cohort-eligible if it carries the Toy subtype and the corrupted-childhood-object art register. The repair-corruption thesis describes *most* current cohort members but does not gate membership.

# Liliana Vess

Necromancer, demon-contract-breaker, and founding member of the Gatewatch; the black-aligned planeswalker whose canonical signature artifact — the Onakke Chain Veil — and whose canonical signature arc — four demonic contracts traded for unnaturally extended life and culminated in the contract-breaking moment that required Gideon Jura's sacrificial death — make her the single most narratively-loaded character on the WAR side of the BBL corpus's MTG holdings. Introduced in Lorwyn (2007) as the original "evil" planeswalker counterweight to Jace's protagonist track; reframed across Innistrad (2011-2016), Origins (2015), Amonkhet (2017), Dominaria (2018), and War of the Spark (2019) into the antiheroine whose contract-breaking moment is the canonical narrative pivot of the WAR storyline.

The `— Liliana` planeswalker subtype on the type-line is reserved for printings of this single character; canonical from Liliana Vess (Lorwyn, 2007) through Liliana, Waker of the Dead (War of the Spark, 2019) and her continued post-WAR appearances. Her WAR climax line in the novelization, addressed to the Dreadhorde army-corpses she is about to turn against Bolas: *"Find your fury."*

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts Liliana Vess herself — under any of her titles (Vess, of the Veil, Defiant Necromancer, Heretical Healer, Untouched by Death, the Last Hope, Dreadhorde General, Waker of the Dead) — and cards in the canonical *Liliana's <Spell>* attribution cycle, in which non-planeswalker spells are flavor-attributed to her as caster, name her in flavor text, or are mechanically encoded with her signature necromancy register. The character is large enough to split into distinct narrative aspects across her printings, and the BBL graph can edge those aspects together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card mirroring.

The Liliana anchor has unusually direct BBL-thesis relevance for the rebellion overlay: her arc is the canonical Magic articulation of *the bargain made under duress, the contract broken when the bargainer would rather die than continue serving the apparatus*. Her four demonic contracts — Griselbrand, Kothophed, Razaketh, and finally Belzenlok and Nicol Bolas — are the in-fiction architecture of necromantic extraction; her canonical end-of-WAR moment is the contract-breaking refusal to deploy the Dreadhorde against the Gatewatch when Bolas commands it, an act that broke the death-contract she had signed with Bolas and that would have killed her had Gideon not transferred the death-curse onto himself. The rebellion hub indexes through this refusal-of-the-apparatus register (per the Potion rule, the BBL hub linkage is the curatorial overlay; Wizards's stated character beat is the contract-breaking moment itself).

## The attribution-and-elegy cycle

A notable Wizards-canonical pattern: the *Liliana's <Spell>* attribution cycle currently runs to ~10+ printings on Scryfall as of 2026, spanning *Liliana's Caress (M11/M12), Liliana's Defeat (HOU), Liliana's Indignation (Dominaria), Liliana's Influence (Core 2019), Liliana's Mastery (Dominaria), Liliana's Reaver (M14), Liliana's Specter (M11/M12), Liliana's Triumph (WAR), Liliana's Contract (Core 2019), Liliana's Standard Bearer (Core 2019)*, and others. The cycle's WAR entry — **Liliana's Triumph** (WAR-98, black, Kieran Yanner art) — is the black member of the [[war-gatewatch-triumph-cycle]] and the canonical resolution of Liliana's WAR arc *("Her triumph came not from defying her demons, but from breaking the chain that bound them all."*) Liliana's Triumph is not yet in BBL corpus; when it lands via a future Collectr CSV upload, it edges here directly and multi-attaches to the cycle node per the Jace's-Triumph cycle-member precedent.

The Liliana attribution cycle differs from the Chandra and Jace attribution cycles in one canonical respect: a significant subset of Liliana's named spells encode her *contract-with-demons* arc explicitly — *Liliana's Contract* (Core 2019) names the contracts themselves; *Liliana's Mastery* (Dominaria) marks her ascendance past the contracts; *Liliana's Triumph* (WAR) names the contract-breaking. The cycle is, in this sense, a Wizards-canonical articulation of the same arc Gideon's elegy cycle articulates for Gideon — but from the necromancer's side, with the contract-and-its-breaking as the organizing metaphor rather than the soldier's protection-extracted-unto-death.

## The Onakke Chain Veil and the four-demon arc

The family-and-mentor thread for Liliana is replaced, structurally, by the four-demon contract arc: Liliana's character is defined not by mentor-figures (the Origins flashback gestures at her brother Josu as the precipitating-grief figure, parallel to Gideon's Irregulars) but by the four demons she sold her soul to in exchange for unnaturally extended life and necromantic power. The contracts in order: **Griselbrand** (killed off-page pre-Innistrad-block, possibly by Liliana herself), **Kothophed** (killed by Liliana with the Chain Veil during Magic Origins), **Razaketh** (killed by Nicol Bolas during the Hour of Devastation, freeing one of Liliana's contracts but binding her to Bolas in the process), and finally **Belzenlok** (killed by Liliana with the help of the Weatherlight crew during Dominaria). The Belzenlok kill freed her from three of the four demonic contracts but the Bolas-contract remained; the WAR climax is the moment she broke that last contract by turning the Dreadhorde — Bolas's own army, raised by her necromancy under his command — against him.

The **Onakke Chain Veil** is her canonical signature artifact, an ancient mask whose necromantic power comes from the Onakke (a long-extinct race of malign sorcerers); the Veil is canonically the source of much of Liliana's extended-life power and the artifact through which she channels her highest-stakes necromancy. The Veil first appears in flavor on Magic 2012's *The Chain Veil* artifact card and recurs across her Origins and post-Origins printings; the artifact's existence as a Wizards-canonical character-bound object is a structural sibling to Gideon's sural / golden-light weaponry and Tamiyo's silken story-scrolls.

For any BBL bundle that anchors on rebellion + necromancy + the bargain-broken or on labor + contract + the unfree-employment register, the Liliana ↔ Chain Veil ↔ four-demon arc throughline is the canonical lore-spine. The rebellion hub indexes through her contract-breaking-against-the-elder-dragon register; a future labor-as-unfree-contract reading would index here too (the four demonic contracts are the canonical encoding of necromantic extraction, structurally parallel to the apparatus-of-extraction overlay BBL applies to the cell-saga-apparatus and macro-cosmos / orzhov-syndicate nodes).

## Caveats

- This node is the character Liliana Vess herself, not the set of cards whose **name** mentions her. Cards like Despark (WAR-190, depiction + flavor-text-naming double-evidence) and Gideon's Sacrifice (WAR-14, depiction + flavor-text-naming double-evidence) edge here through the depiction-and-canonical-name attribution test. Cards that mention Liliana only in flavor text without depicting her would edge here via the canonical-name attribution test instead; cards that depict her without naming her in flavor (the Liliana-attribution-cycle members like Liliana's Triumph, when in corpus) edge here via the depiction-or-attribution test. The depiction-or-narrative-attribution test applies, parallel to the [[jace-beleren]] / [[chandra-nalaar]] / [[gideon-jura]] / [[tamiyo]] standards.
- The `— Liliana` planeswalker subtype is a strict canonical identifier: every card carrying that subtype is a printing of this single character (no other character in Magic has that subtype). Spells named after her (Caress, Specter, Reaver, Indignation, Influence, Mastery, Triumph, Contract, Defeat, Standard Bearer, et al) use the character's name without the subtype and are validated by flavor/oracle text attribution.
- **Despark's `characters:` field is `[nicol-bolas, liliana-vess]`** (multi-attached). Despark is a multi-character card: Liliana is the depicted figure, Bolas is the off-frame named antagonist (the flavor text addresses the God-Eternals about Bolas). The multi-attach to both characters is the canonical shape per the WAR-14 / [[gideon-jura]] + this node precedent.
- **Gideon's Sacrifice's `characters:` field is `[gideon-jura, liliana-vess]`** (multi-attached). The flavor text names both, the art depicts both; double-attribution is the canonical shape for cards in the WAR climax diptych.
- **Liliana's Triumph (WAR-98) is the canonical WAR cycle anchor and is NOT in corpus.** When Liliana's Triumph lands via a future Collectr CSV upload, it edges here directly and multi-attaches to the [[war-gatewatch-triumph-cycle]] node per the Jace's-Triumph cycle-member precedent. The Liliana's-Triumph trivia would canonicalize the four-demon contract-breaking arc as the cycle's black-color articulation; the gap until the card lands is the only narrative-completion gap in this node's WAR-arc coverage.
- **Pre-WAR Liliana depictions are not yet in corpus.** Her Innistrad, Origins, Amonkhet, and Dominaria printings (Liliana of the Veil, Liliana the Last Hope, Liliana Defiant Necromancer, Liliana Heretical Healer / Untouched by Death, Liliana Death's Majesty, Liliana Death Wielder, Liliana Dreadhorde General, etc.) are zero in corpus as of this commissioning. The node's scope explicitly admits them; when any pre-WAR Liliana lands, the `aliases:` field already enumerates the titled-printing variants and the `appears_on:` list extends with the new card.
- **A future Nicol Bolas character-to-this node *antagonist* / *contract-holder* relation applies to the WAR arc.** Bolas was Liliana's fourth (final) demonic contract-holder after Belzenlok's death; the contract-breaking moment in WAR is the canonical narrative pivot of both characters' arcs. The existing [[nicol-bolas]] node already anchors the Bolas-Falls cluster; Liliana's contribution is the refusal that triggered the cluster's events. The relation is canon-grounded; defer character-to-character edge pending the framework's formalization, same as the [[jace-beleren]] / [[nicol-bolas]] and [[chandra-nalaar]] / [[nicol-bolas]] deferrals.
- **A future Gideon Jura character-to-this node *partner-of-the-act* / *foil-of-the-act* relation applies to the WAR-14 climax.** Gideon's sacrificial-death-in-her-place is the act that prevented her contract-breaking from killing her; the two characters are bound at the WAR climax.

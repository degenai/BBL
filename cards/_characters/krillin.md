# Krillin

The canonical strongest human in the Dragon Ball franchise, the canonical lifelong best friend of [[son-goku]], and the canonical Z-Fighter most precisely engineered by Toriyama as the **labor-tier counterpart to the Saiyan ki-user-tier protagonist roster**. Introduced in Dragon Ball manga chapter 26 (1984, Akira Toriyama, Shueisha) as a child martial artist arriving at Master Roshi's Kame House to train alongside Goku — created by Toriyama specifically because his editor Kazuhiko Torishima flagged Goku as "too plain" without a rival companion to give the protagonist's arc contrast, per Toriyama's own subsequent commentary preserved on the canonical Wikipedia Krillin page. The character has continuously appeared across forty years of franchise output (Dragon Ball 1984-1995 manga, Dragon Ball Z 1989-1996 anime, Dragon Ball GT 1996-1997, Dragon Ball Super 2015-2018, and the Dragon Ball Super Card Game 2017-present) without significant designer-revision, making him one of the franchise's most-cited fixed points across continuities.

Three canonical Krillin registers operate across the corpus and the franchise: the **Turtle-School-student-and-tournament-fighter** register that anchors his Dragon-Ball-era origin (canonical participation in the 21st, 22nd, and 23rd Tenkaichi Budokai per Dragon Ball Wiki, semi-finals reach twice, the canonical quarter-final loss to Piccolo Jr. at the 23rd that is widely considered one of his best fights — the corpus's TB2-053 + TB2-041 prints anchor here at the World Martial Arts Tournament set's structurally-coherent tournament-register cohort); the **Z-Fighter-defensive-shield-and-most-deaths** register that grounds his Saiyan-through-Buu Saga arc (canonical three-deaths-in-the-main-timeline holder — killed by Tambourine in original Dragon Ball, blown apart by Frieza on Namek in a death that canonically triggered Goku's first Super Saiyan transformation, and turned-to-chocolate-and-eaten by Super Buu in the Buu Saga — the corpus's BT1-072 print's "Human Shield" subtitle literalizes this register at the Galactic Battle set's Z-canon-anchor cohort); and the **Eighteen-husband / Marron-father / domestic-stewardship** register that grounds his late-series post-Cell-Saga arc (canonical decision to spare Android 18's life with Bulma's remote when the Z-Fighters would have permitted her destruction, canonical marriage to Eighteen between the Cell and Buu Sagas, canonical fatherhood of Marron, and the canonical Kame-House-co-resident family-life that operates as the Z-Fighter cohort's clearest sustained-domestic-stewardship arc).

The three corpus prints span two designer-coordinated DBS-CG sets (Galactic Battle BT1, World Martial Arts Tournament TB2) and three distinct register-anchors: BT1-072 anchors the **defensive-shield-Z-Fighter** register (Krillin in white-and-gold armor vest, defensive-block stance, Saiyan-armor-iconography flagged in the vision pass), TB2-053 anchors the **tournament-fighter-with-Chiaotzu-synergy** register (Krillin in orange Turtle-School gi, wide braced fighting stance, mechanically partnered with TB2-056 Toughened Up Chiaotzu per the canonical 22nd-Tenkaichi-Budokai Krillin-and-Chiaotzu friendship), and TB2-041 anchors the **action-strike** register (Rare-tier print, vision-pending as of wave 71, expected to enrich at the canonical Krillin-mid-combat configuration when the next researchbot pass reaches it).

## The functional ideology

Krillin's most BBL-relevant property is that he is, in-canon, the **franchise's first-and-clearest labor-tier-Z-Fighter-counterpart-to-the-Saiyan-ki-user-tier-protagonist-roster** — a designer-coordinated character whose entire narrative function is to make the apex-power Saiyan combat tier legible by anchoring a contrasting lower-power tier where the work-shape is *absorbing damage and showing up anyway* rather than *transcending the power ceiling via Super-Saiyan transformations*. Toriyama's canonical editorial-driven character-creation rationale (Krillin created because Goku was "too plain" without a peer for contrast per Torishima's editorial flag) makes the *labor-and-credit* / *power-tier* / *labor-tier-Z-Fighter-counterpart* reading a literal designer-confirmed intent rather than a post-hoc apparatus-of-extraction interpretation.

Three canonical structural patterns ground the apparatus-of-extraction reading at this node:

- **The most-deaths record as canonical labor-tier-Z-Fighter signature.** Krillin holds the canonical record for most deaths of any Dragon Ball character — three confirmed main-timeline deaths (Tambourine kill in original Dragon Ball, Frieza Supernova on Namek that canonically triggered Goku's first Super Saiyan transformation, Super Buu's chocolate-transformation in the Buu Saga). The death-count is canonically designer-coordinated as Krillin's character-signature per the CBR analysis pieces ("How Many Times Does Krillin Really Die?" / "Every Time Krillin Has Died In Dragon Ball" / Game Rant "All of Krillin's Deaths, Ranked"). The labor-tier-Z-Fighter framing makes the death-count legible as apparatus-of-extraction at the power-tier scale: the lower-tier-fighter absorbs the cost the higher-tier-Saiyans don't, dies in arcs where the higher-tier-Saiyans transcend, and is canonically revived-and-deployed again in the next saga. The "Human Shield" card-subtitle on BT1-072 literalizes this register.

- **The Frieza-kills-Krillin moment as canonical Super-Saiyan-trigger and labor-tier-cost-of-apex-power.** Frieza's Supernova-kill of Krillin on Namek is canonically the *exact moment* that triggers Goku's first Super Saiyan transformation — the franchise's most-cited single canonical apex-power-unlock-event is structurally grounded on the labor-tier-Z-Fighter death. The apparatus-of-extraction reading reads cleanly: the labor-tier-fighter's death IS what makes the apex-power-Saiyan tier accessible. Goku's apex-power unlock canonically REQUIRES Krillin's death-cost. Bundle copy that wants the labor-and-credit-uncoupling thesis at the power-tier scale lands directly on this canonical beat — the strongest-human-fighter's body is the canonical apparatus that the apex-power Saiyan transformation rides through.

- **The Eighteen-marriage and Marron-fatherhood as canonical battlefield-loyalty-converted-into-domestic-stewardship.** The canonical Cell-Saga decision to spare Android 18's life with Bulma's remote (when the Z-Fighters would have permitted her destruction per Dragon Ball Wiki's Android 18 page) is the franchise's most-developed *Z-Fighter-converts-from-combat-loyalty-into-domestic-stewardship* arc. Krillin marries Eighteen between the Cell and Buu Sagas, fathers Marron, and the canonical late-series Krillin is canonically the Z-Fighter cohort's clearest sustained-domestic-stewardship anchor — the family-life is canon, the Kame-House-co-residency is canon, the part-time-police-work is canon, and the canonical Krillin-late-series-aging-arc is the franchise's clearest *Z-Fighter-transitions-from-combat-tier-into-care-tier* depiction.

The labor-and-stewardship dual-hub attribution lands cleanly per the triple-thesis hub-meta doc: Krillin is canonical labor at the *labor-tier-Z-Fighter-counterpart-to-Saiyan-apex-power* axis (work-shape = absorbing-damage-and-showing-up-anyway, death-cost = canonical Super-Saiyan-trigger, BT1-072 "Human Shield" subtitle literalizes), and canonical stewardship at the *battlefield-loyalty-converted-into-domestic-stewardship* axis (Eighteen-marriage-decision, Marron-fatherhood, late-series-care-tier-arc). The rebellion-hub does NOT attribute here — Krillin canonically operates WITHIN the Z-Fighter Earth-defender cohort, doesn't refuse the apparatus, doesn't perform organized political refusal. The dual-hub attribution is structurally complete; performative-rebellion-attribution refused per the triple-thesis-doc's bar.

# The Agency (R.A.M.I.)

The **Ravnican Agency of Magicological Investigations** — R.A.M.I. for short, "the Agency" in conversational Ravnican — is the cross-guild detective bureau introduced as the lens-faction of *Murders at Karlov Manor* (2024). Founded centuries before the New Phyrexian Invasion of Ravnica by the archon **Ezrim**, who broke off from the Azorius to start a truth-cult that aided people and investigated mysteries without guild-bias tainting its findings. The cult grew slowly across centuries; after the Phyrexian Invasion in 4562 AR, demand spiked and Ezrim transformed it into the Agency, recruiting defectors from all ten guilds and Azorius-trained detectives like the acclaimed Alquist Proft. The R.A.M.I. badge incorporates the Ravnican skyline with an all-seeing eye; its headquarters is a giant floating eye-shaped edifice that hovers over the plane. Mechanically the Agency is the canonical home of the **Detective** creature subtype (introduced in MKM, 85+ prints across the set and reprints) and the **Case** enchantment subtype (the Cases-to-be-solved cycle).

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts the Agency as an *institution*: its detectives at work (interrogation, surveillance, suspect-elimination, case-solving, evidence-collection), its iconography (the magnifying glass, the thinking cap, the trench coat, the noir hat, the projektor surveillance display, the all-seeing-eye badge, the forensic thopter), its named operatives (Ezrim, Alquist Proft, Etrata, Kaust, Kellan, Sophia, Tam Sennic — none currently in the corpus as their own legendary printings, but flagged here for future cards), and the institutional thesis the Agency embodies — that detective work *is* the new authority on Ravnica, replacing the Boros Legion's keep-the-peace function with the Agency's investigate-the-crime function in the aftermath of the Phyrexian Invasion. The Agency is the BBL corpus's primary anchor for the **detective-noir** register on the Magic side: any bundle on investigation-as-thesis, suspect-elimination-as-procedure, or noir-aesthetic-on-Ravnica indexes here.

## The functional thesis

The Agency's most BBL-relevant property is its **canonical thesis**: that *the guild system has failed and a cross-guild institution is what comes next*. Per the Planeswalker's Guide to Murders at Karlov Manor (Emily Teng, 2024-02-01) and Seanan McGuire's *Magic Story* episodes 1–7, the Agency was a small truth-cult for centuries; the Phyrexian Invasion in 4562 AR collapsed the guilds' authority enough that the Agency grew to "the same size as the ten Ravnican guilds" in a single generation. The Boros Legion considers the Agency amateurs; the Azorius supplies most of its detectives but resents the Agency's institutional autonomy; the Orzhov uses the Agency for case-fixing while resenting the bills; the Izzet uses it for case-solving while finding it boring. Every guild has a complicated relationship with the Agency because the Agency is, in canon, *what replaces the guild system* on the timeline that Wizards is currently writing.

This is what BBL bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: the Agency is, in-universe, a designer-confirmed *post-guild* institution — the cross-guild detective bureau that emerges when the ten-guild apparatus that defined Ravnica for two decades of game design stops working. The bundle prose for any MKM-anchored lair can name the Agency by name and let Wizards' actual story team's work do the thesis work. The Tithe bundle's apparatus-of-extraction thesis (Orzhov + Azorius) has its post-Phyrexian successor right here: the Agency, which extracts truth instead of money, and is *not yet* shown to be subject to the same critique. Whether the Agency is the redemption of institutional authority on Ravnica or merely its next form remains designer-open; future MKM-2 / Ravnica revisits may complicate it.

## Caveats

- **The Agency is institutionally autonomous from but operationally entangled with the ten Ravnican guilds.** Cards in the corpus carry Agency operational register (detective, investigate, case, disguise) but may also carry guild-watermark coding (Azorius for the Burden-of-Proof lawyer-detective register; Simic for Mistway Spy's head-tendril species coding; Selesnya-Conclave-body for Crowd-Control Warden's centaur morphology; Orzhov for Sanguine Savior's chest-medallion eclipsed-sun signet). The frontmatter relation is `characters: ["agency"]` for Agency-operative cards; if the same card is also institutionally a guild operative (e.g., Sanguine Savior as Orzhov-recruited Detective), both characters edges apply. Wave-38 extension wired Sanguine Savior dual-coded as `characters: ["agency", "orzhov-syndicate"]` AND `symbols: ["orzhov-signet"]` per the eclipsed-sun jewelry-form documentation on the symbol node.

- **Agency officers are unified by FUNCTION, not creature-type.** Pre-wave-38 corpus-internal pattern reading skewed toward Sphinx-coded Agency operatives (Agency Outfitter as Sphinx Detective being the most visible). Wave 38's extension undermines that pattern: the Agency now includes a Living Conundrum body-elemental built of bound case-files, a centaur Crowd-Control Warden, a Goblin Artificer Gadget Technician, a Faerie (corpus has Faerie Snoop coded criminal-side, but Furtive Courier as pointy-eared sprite-coded courier is Agency-side), a Vampire Cleric Sanguine Savior, and a Crocodile Elf hybrid. The unifying register is the Detective creature subtype + Disguise / Investigate / Case mechanic + flavor-attribution to the Agency or its Foundway Associates network — NOT creature type. Bundle copy should anchor on the institutional-function thesis (R.A.M.I. as the cross-species cross-guild detective bureau) rather than any creature-type-coded sub-roster.

- **Exit Specialist (MKM-55) is *not* currently wired here.** The card depicts a stealth-rogue figure crouching in a stone corridor; mechanically Disguise plus a face-up reveal effect; thematically infiltration / escape rather than investigation. Disguise is an MKM-signature mechanic but it isn't *exclusive* to Agency operatives — the same mechanic is used by criminal-side characters (the underworld figures the Agency investigates). Without a flavor-text or oracle-text Agency attribution, Exit Specialist reads as ambiguous: either an Agency operative on infiltration assignment or an underworld figure evading Agency surveillance. Deferred-triage candidate: confirm via MKM story articles whether Exit Specialist is one of Etrata's underworld crew or one of Alquist Proft's field agents; until then, the card edges to no character node.

- **The Detective creature type and the Case enchantment type are both first-class graph-relevant primitives.** They are not currently graph-modeled separately — the BBL `symbols/` layer covers iconographic primitives (Orzhov Signet) but not mechanical/typing primitives. A future hub-layer or symbol-layer entry for `detective-creature-type` could be argued for (parallel to how the Orzhov Signet symbol node anchors a cross-card iconographic primitive), but for now the corpus's six Detective-and-Case prints all share the `agency` character anchor without needing the mechanical-type-as-node treatment. Future-triage candidate flagged here.

- **Sophisticated post-Phyrexian-Invasion Ravnica characters (Ezrim, Alquist Proft, Etrata, Kellan, Kaust, Sophia, Tam Sennic) are deferred sub-node candidates.** None are currently depicted in the corpus's MKM cards (the corpus carries Agency operatives in generic-archetype rendering — outfitter, spy, lawyer-detective, the-figure-in-the-armchair — rather than named-individual legendary printings). When a legendary Agency-officer card lands (Alquist Proft, Detective is one such printing in MKM-243; Ezrim, Agency Chief is MKM-258; Kellan, Inquisitive Prodigy is MKM-220), the sub-node case becomes immediate and parallels the Ral-Zarek-under-Izzet-League sub-node pattern formalized in wave 36. Cited here as a future-node candidate cluster, not authored.

- **The Agency is in a complicated graph-relation with [[azorius-senate]].** Ezrim is canonically an Azorius defector; many R.A.M.I. detectives (Alquist Proft, Mileva) are Azorius-trained; the Azorius supplies most of the Agency's investigators and remands its convicts to Azorius custody. The two factions are *not* allied (Ezrim refuses casework he considers Azorius-political) but are operationally entangled. The relation is a sibling-and-rival edge, not a parent-and-child edge — Agency is structurally not a sub-faction of Azorius despite the staffing overlap.

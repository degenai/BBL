# Kaya

Human planeswalker, ghost-hunter by trade, Orzhov Syndicate guildmaster by Ravnica Allegiance, Gatewatch co-leader during War of the Spark, and Murders at Karlov Manor (2024) co-investigator alongside Alquist Proft. Introduced in *Conspiracy: Take the Crown* (2016) on the Kaya, Ghost Assassin card, Kaya Cassir's canonical signature is **phasing** — the ability to render herself or her targets intangible, originally developed as a tool for fighting non-corporeal undead and later weaponized into a broader combat operating mode. The pink-violet ethereal magic she channels (most visible in the MKM-116 The Chase Is On art) is the canonical visual signature for this magic across her print history.

Kaya's institutional position is unusual among MTG planeswalkers: she is one of the few who has held formal political office in a setting (Orzhov guildmaster on Ravnica from RNA onward), and she is canonically the only planeswalker confirmed to have retained her spark through the Phyrexian invasion's post-*March of the Machine* Desparkening (the franchise-wide event that stripped most planeswalkers of their multiverse-traversal ability). The MKM storyline (2024) anchors her as the active Orzhov enforcement-and-investigation officer of Ravnica at a moment when the rest of the planeswalker roster has been demoted to single-plane mortals.

## The functional canon

Kaya's BBL-relevant institutional features are three:

- **Ghost-hunter via phasing.** Kaya's canonical magical operating mode is phasing — rendering herself or her targets intangible. Originally developed for hunting non-corporeal undead (her debut card *Kaya, Ghost Assassin* CN2 canonized this), the technique extends to combat against any target: a phased Kaya cannot be hit, and a phased target cannot interact with the corporeal world. The pink-violet ethereal magic visible in MKM-116 The Chase Is On is the canonical visual signature. Across her print history (RNA Bane of the Dead and Orzhov Usurper; WAR Orzhov Usurper variant; KHM Kaya the Inexorable; SNC Kaya, Intangible Slayer — note the *Intangible* in the title; MKM Spirits' Justice), phasing magic recurs as the canonical mechanic-flavor anchor.

- **Orzhov guildmaster (RNA onward).** Kaya canonically replaces Obzedat as the Orzhov Syndicate's guildmaster in *Ravnica Allegiance* (2019), inheriting the guild's tithe-and-debt apparatus from the previous ghost-council. Her institutional position on Ravnica is canonically that of a reformer-from-the-inside who is also institutionally bound to the guild's extractive operating mode: she did not abolish the Orzhov tithe system, and her MKM-era position canonizes her as the Syndicate's continued chief enforcement officer. This is the canonical institutional frame for any Orzhov-coded card in our corpus from RNA onward (the four flavor-text-speaker cards span this arc: RNA-252 Orzhov Guildgate, WAR-106 Spark Reaper, CLB-878 Talisman of Hierarchy, MKM-159 Fanatical Strength). The [[orzhov-syndicate]] character node is the canonical sibling for this aspect.

- **Gatewatch co-leader against Nicol Bolas (War of the Spark, 2019).** Kaya is one of the canonical co-leaders of the planeswalker resistance against Nicol Bolas during the War of the Spark, alongside Ral Zarek (Izzet planeswalker) and Vraska (Golgari planeswalker, also Bolas-double-agent-turned-resistance). Her WAR-era card (Kaya, Orzhov Usurper) anchors her at this moment, and her institutional position carries forward into her post-WAR Orzhov-guildmaster continuity. The [[nicol-bolas]] character node is the canonical antagonist relation for this aspect; the MH3-132 Ral and the Implicit Maze corpus print canonizes the Ral-Kaya-Vraska resistance trio at the trivia-section level.

- **Spark retention through the Desparkening.** Per Wizards' MKM storyline and the broader post-*March of the Machine* canon, Kaya is one of the very few planeswalkers who retained her spark through the Phyrexian invasion's aftermath. This is canonically attested in the MKM-116 The Chase Is On trivia bullet ("Kaya is the only planeswalker in MKM who retained her spark through the Phyrexian invasion's Desparkening") and in Wizards' Planeswalker's Guide entries for the MKM era. This is a load-bearing canonical feature: at the MKM-era moment that the corpus's verified depiction lands, Kaya is a structurally rare full planeswalker in a Magic continuity where most others have been demoted to single-plane mortals.

This is what BBL bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Kaya is, across Wizards' published continuity from 2016 onward, the canonical ghost-hunter-via-phasing planeswalker, the canonical post-Obzedat Orzhov guildmaster, the canonical WAR co-resistance leader against Bolas, and the canonical MKM detective whose pursuit of Etrata is anchored on the verified-depiction print (MKM-116) in our corpus. A future Orzhov-or-MKM-themed Discrete Lair could anchor on Kaya's institutional-reformer-from-within position without reaching past canon: she did not abolish the tithe, she enforces it from the top, and the Wizards-canonical Orzhov-Syndicate gilded-lie register is in her own voice on RNA-252.

## Caveats

- **One verified depiction + sustained flavor-voice across four sets.** The threshold-clearance argument is not "one verified depiction" alone — the BBL solo-character-node threshold is canonically ≥3 corpus anchors per the [[trunks]] / [[pan]] / [[orzhov-syndicate]] precedents. Kaya clears via 1 verified depiction (MKM-116) + 4 flavor-text-speaker prints (RNA-252, WAR-106, CLB-878, MKM-159) + 1 flavor-text-named print (MKM-312), for **6 substantive corpus anchors across 4 sets and 5 years of Wizards continuity**. The threshold-of-3 is cleared comfortably. The 3 related-card-citation prints (MKM-125, WAR-81, M21-35, MH3-132) are not counted toward threshold but are tracked in the Aspects section as graph-context.

- **No multi-aspect node split (yet).** Kaya's eight named planeswalker prints across Wizards' canon (CN2 Ghost Assassin → RNA Bane of the Dead → RNA Orzhov Usurper → WAR Orzhov Usurper → KHM the Inexorable → SNC Intangible Slayer → MKM Spirits' Justice; plus the Kaya, Intangible Slayer Special-Guest printings) span eight years of canon and four major story arcs (Tolvada-and-debut; RNA Orzhov ascension; WAR Bolas-resistance; SNC New Capenna; MKM detective). The current corpus has 1 verified-depiction print plus flavor-voice anchors; no per-aspect sub-split is warranted yet. The structural precedent if a future Kaya-aspect-density split becomes needed is the [[tamiyo]] / [[nicol-bolas]] / [[trunks]] multi-aspect single-parent pattern, with possible future solo sub-nodes for `kaya-orzhov-guildmaster` (RNA-WAR era) and `kaya-mkm-detective` (MKM era) if the corpus density warrants.

- **The flavor-text-speaker mechanism is a real canon-anchor.** Per the [[bbl-triviabot-no-role-identity-conflation]] memory rule, BBL does not conflate role with identity — an unnamed Orzhov vampire is not Slavomir Zoltan just because they share a role. But the inverse rule applies here: a *named* flavor-text speaker IS the canonical voice the card attributes. When RNA-252 Orzhov Guildgate's flavor text reads "...Kaya," the card canonically attributes that voice to Kaya Cassir, and the depicted scene is canonically a Kaya-perspective scene even when she is not depicted. This is a Wizards-canonical flavor-architecture mechanism, and BBL treats it as a node-anchor substrate.

- **Phasing-magic visual signature is consistent.** The pink-violet ethereal magic visible on MKM-116 The Chase Is On is canonically continuous with Kaya's phasing-magic signature across her print history (Kaya, Ghost Assassin CN2; Kaya, Bane of the Dead RNA; Kaya the Inexorable KHM; Kaya, Intangible Slayer SNC; Kaya, Spirits' Justice MKM-211). If a future corpus card depicts a figure channeling pink-violet ethereal magic without flavor-text confirmation, the visual signature alone is **insufficient evidence** for a Kaya IP-attribution per the [[bbl-anti-confab-prompt]] memory rule's anti-confabulation discipline — but it is sufficient evidence to flag the card as a Kaya-candidate and refer to triviabot for confirmation.

- **MKM-style Kaya is also canonically Orzhov-coded.** The Murders at Karlov Manor 2024 storyline canonizes Kaya as the continuing Orzhov Syndicate guildmaster while also serving as Alquist Proft's investigation partner. Her MKM costume (black coat) and the MKM detective-set art-direction frame her as detective-first / Orzhov-guildmaster-second visually, but the institutional frame remains Orzhov. The [[orzhov-syndicate]] character node is the canonical sibling for the institutional aspect; this Kaya node is the canonical anchor for the individual-character aspect. The two nodes' relation should be encoded in `related_characters:` when [[orzhov-syndicate]]'s related-characters frontmatter is next touched.

- **Etrata is a canonical relation, not a separate node yet.** The MKM-116 depiction canonically pairs Kaya with Etrata, Deadly Fugitive (Dimir vampire assassin, MKM-200) as the pursuer-and-fugitive duo. Etrata is canonically a distinct character with her own MKM legendary creature card and her own multi-print history (Etrata, the Silencer GRN; Etrata, Deadly Fugitive MKM). If a future corpus print depicts Etrata with verified attribution, an [[etrata]] solo character node would be commission-worthy; for now Etrata's corpus presence in our inventory is only the MKM-116 art-pairing-with-Kaya, and she is not currently a solo node.

- **Future Discrete Lair candidate.** A future MKM-themed or Orzhov-themed Discrete Lair could anchor on Kaya. The MKM detective subtheme (Detective tokens, Clue tokens, Suspect mechanic, investigation flavor) has substantial multi-set cohesion that Kaya's voice anchors at the canonical-perspective layer.

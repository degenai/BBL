# Cinderella cycle (Throne of Eldraine fairy-tale homage)

Throne of Eldraine (ELD, 2019-10-04) was Wizards' first top-down set fusing Grimms' fairy tales with Arthurian legend; lead designer Mark Rosewater's "Eldraine or Shine" preview (2019-09-09) framed the design as built around named fairy-tale homages, and his follow-up "More Odds & Ends: Throne of Eldraine" (2019-10-21) names "the pumpkin carriage" and "the glass slipper" verbatim among the set's iconic fairy-tale objects. The Cinderella cycle is the most-cited and most-documented of ELD's fairy-tale homage cycles, with at least one creature-type invention (the Mouse type, created by Rosewater verbatim because Enchanted Carriage "needed white Mice for flavor") and explicit cycle-membership identification across multiple Rosewater Making Magic columns.

## What this node anchors

Card art, oracle text, flavor text, and rules-text inventions (the Mouse type) that homage specific beats of the Cinderella fairy tale within Throne of Eldraine. Membership predicates on designer confirmation in a Rosewater Making Magic column or equivalent Wizards-canonical source; Cinderella-adjacent vibes alone are not sufficient. The cycle reads as a curated Wizards-confirmed fairy-tale mini-suite inside ELD's broader top-down design, distinct from Sleeping Beauty (Charmed Sleep), Snow White (Glass Casket), Hansel-and-Gretel (Gingerbread Cabin / Witch's Oven), and the set's Arthurian half (Knights, Castles, Round Table).

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Enchanted Carriage** (ELD-218, U, Zoltan Boros art) — the pumpkin coach itself, a Vehicle artifact whose ETB creates two 1/1 white Mouse tokens that homage Cinderella's mice-turned-coachmen. Rosewater's "More Odds & Ends" verbatim: *"This creature type exists only because the Enchanted Carriage needed white Mice for flavor."* The most-cited cycle member in design-column coverage and the load-bearing creature-type-invention card.
- **Turn into a Pumpkin** (ELD-69, U, Vincent Proce art) — the deadline-reversal beat: at midnight the coach reverts to a pumpkin and the magic fails. Mechanically a blue bounce-plus-draw with an Adamant Food-token rider, modeling both the reversion and the soft consolation of the spell. The painting's ambiguity — the hunched figure mid-transformation in the moonlit pumpkin patch — reads as the canonical Cinderella-reversal beat staged as folk-horror.
- **Wicked Guardian** (ELD-109, C, Matt Stewart art) — the wicked stepmother archetype. Rosewater's "Eldraine or Shine" preview places this card explicitly inside the Cinderella cycle as the moment the protagonist's "wicked stepmother begins treating her less like a stepdaughter and more like a servant." Flavor text *"Some are born to greatness. You were born to scrub greatness's floors"* and the art (noblewoman on a high-backed throne, hooded servant scrubbing the stones at her feet) carry the abuse-relationship beat. The self-damage ETB trigger maps onto stepmother-abusing-servant flavor at the rules-text level.

## The design thesis

The Cinderella cycle's most BBL-relevant property is that Wizards designed it as a *named-set-of-iconic-fairy-tale-objects-and-beats* — the design language is "what objects and moments would a Cinderella illustration need," and each card materializes one of those into the game's vocabulary. The cycle includes:

- **The fairy godmother** (Faerie Guidemother) — the agent who casts the transformation
- **The pumpkin coach** (Enchanted Carriage) — the iconic object she conjures
- **The mice-turned-coachmen** (the Mouse token type, invented for Enchanted Carriage) — the supporting cast
- **The glass slipper** (Crystal Slipper) — the prince's recognition token
- **The stroke of midnight** (Midnight Clock) — the deadline
- **The reversion** (Turn into a Pumpkin) — the magic failing on schedule
- **The wicked stepmother** (Wicked Guardian) — the antagonist
- The protagonist herself (Beloved Princess, provisional) — Cinderella as a card

This is one of the cleanest top-down homages Wizards has ever published, and Rosewater's Making Magic columns name it as such across two preview-cycle articles. For BBL the cycle anchors a `[[labor]]` adjacency through Wicked Guardian (forced domestic labor as the stepmother-stepdaughter relation, made literal in art and flavor) and a Discrete Lair candidate built around the *Tithe*-adjacent thesis: *the apparatus that turns a daughter into a scullery maid is the same apparatus, just smaller, dressed up as family.*

## Caveats

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like `[[dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle]]`, `[[fear-of-cycle]]`, `[[castform-forms-cycle]]`, and `[[elemental-monkey-trio]]`, this is a collective conceptual entity hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent. A future `_cycles/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the other designer-confirmed flavor cycles when that split is commissioned; for now, single layer, same precedent. The `dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle` caveat-section explicitly names "Cinderella cycle in ELD" as one of the future-home candidates for the cycles split.
- **Roster bounded by Rosewater's named-in-print confirmations.** The strict-membership column above is what Rosewater explicitly names in Making Magic. Cards that share a Cinderella vibe but are not designer-cited (e.g. Charmed Sleep, which is canonically Sleeping Beauty per Scryfall and Wizards story coverage, NOT Cinderella; Bake into a Pie, which is Hansel-and-Gretel-adjacent; Smitten Swordmaster, which is the prince archetype but not designer-named) are deliberately excluded. The cycle's value rests on the *named* homage register; tag-adjacent fairy-tale cards have their own thematic edges and may attach to a future broader `eld-fairy-tale-homages` hub, but they do not attach here.
- **Beloved Princess (ELD-7) listed as inferred, not strict.** The card has not yet been triviabot-passed in BBL corpus, so the canonical-source verification for Cinderella-cycle membership has not been run. The flavor text "Sometimes a gentle slipper can travel where an armored boot's been denied" reads as a Cinderella reference (the slipper as the protagonist's vehicle through hostile space), but the membership requires Rosewater-or-equivalent confirmation before promotion to strict. A future triviabot pass should re-evaluate the membership claim.
- **Cycle cohabits ELD with other designer-named fairy-tale homages.** Hansel-and-Gretel (Gingerbread Cabin, Witch's Oven, Bake into a Pie, possibly more), Snow White (Glass Casket, Tempting Witch), Sleeping Beauty (Charmed Sleep, True Love's Kiss), and Pinocchio (per Rosewater's list) are each their own designer-confirmed flavor cycles within ELD. If 3+ corpus members enrich for any of those siblings, a parallel cycle node should be authored — the precedent established here scales.

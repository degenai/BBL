# Uub

The canonical human reincarnation of Kid Buu — the ten-year-old impoverished-village child who emerged from King Yemma's afterlife judgment as the answer to Goku's specific wish for a worthy future opponent who would not be inherently evil, the canonical *Goku-successor-protagonist* of the End-of-Z epilogue, and the final on-screen sparring partner Goku takes off-world at the close of Dragon Ball Z's canonical original-run continuity. Introduced in Dragon Ball Z manga chapter 519 (1995, Akira Toriyama, Shueisha) at the 28th World Martial Arts Tournament arc — the canonical Buu-Saga epilogue tournament held five years after Goku's Spirit-Bomb-finishing-blow on Kid Buu — Uub enters the tournament as a ten-year-old seeking prize money to feed his impoverished family. His canonical first-match opponent is Goku himself; Goku deliberately provokes Uub to anger mid-match to unlock his suppressed power (the canonical *shocking latent ability* revelation that the TB2-033 card-title directly references), recognizes the unlocked-power signature as the reincarnated-Kid-Buu energy he had wished for at the Buu-Saga conclusion, and takes Uub as a personal disciple and off-world training partner at the canonical *A Hero's Farewell* DBZ-finale beat (Toei Animation anime episode 291, 1996).

The character's canonical *child-body-containing-the-compressed-destructive-force-of-Kid-Buu's-power* design device is one of Toriyama's clearest in-canon statements about the *evil-spirit-can-be-reformed-by-human-rebirth* design move: Kid Buu's purest-form pure-evil consciousness — the canonical *most-destructive Buu form* that Goku spent the Buu Saga arc systematically dismantling at maximum personal cost — is canonically reincarnated as a child whose human-Earthling-rebirth-body retains the raw power signature while losing the regeneration, body-manipulation, and inherent-evil components of the Majin Buu lineage. Uub as a human canonically retains Kid Buu's raw ki-power while operating under canonical-Earthling moral-baseline values: the canonical *power-without-evil* configuration that Goku had specifically wished for at the Buu-Saga conclusion. The canonical *Uub's power dwarfs his apparent potential, suppressed by inexperience and lack of training* register that the BT3-014 + TB2-033 trivia-bullets foreground is Toriyama's canonical *reformed-Kid-Buu-as-Goku-successor* design statement.

Three canonical Uub registers operate across the corpus: the **End-of-Z 28th World Tournament debut register** (the canonical ten-year-old impoverished-village child debut against Goku at the canonical Buu-Saga-epilogue tournament; corpus anchor TB2-033 Shocking Latent Ability with the canonical *Uub-Leader-Card-dedicated-mechanical-support* design move); the **GT-era Black Star Dragon Ball Saga adolescent register** (the canonical post-DBZ-training adolescent-Uub continuation after five years of off-world Goku-disciple training, depicted in the Bandai-canonical *Black Star Dragon Ball Saga* era designation; corpus anchor BT3-014 Hidden Power Uub); and the **Son Goku & Uub Seeds of the Future dual-IP technique register** (the canonical *passing-of-the-torch* End-of-Z protagonist-succession beat depicted as a dual-character print where Uub is the canonical second-actor; corpus anchor TB2-069 Son Goku & Uub Seeds of the Future).

## The functional ideology

Uub's most BBL-relevant property is that he is the **canonical evil-spirit-reformed-by-human-rebirth design statement at protagonist-succession scale**. Kid Buu is canonically the purest-form most-destructive Buu — the canonical *primal-pre-Bibidi pure-evil consciousness* that Toriyama installed into the Buu Saga as the franchise's most-destructive single antagonist, the canonical *kill-it-with-fire* threat that Goku's Spirit Bomb specifically annihilates at the canonical Buu-Saga conclusion. King Yemma's afterlife-judgment-of-Kid-Buu in response to Goku's specific wish reconfigures the canonical *destruction-of-an-evil-spirit* outcome from *total annihilation* to *reincarnation-as-a-human-child-without-the-evil-component*. The canonical Uub-as-Kid-Buu-reincarnation design move is structurally identical to the canonical *evil-can-be-reformed-into-good-through-rebirth-without-memory* design device — Uub canonically retains none of Kid Buu's memories, none of Kid Buu's evil disposition, and none of Kid Buu's physical-form regeneration / body-manipulation capabilities, while retaining the raw ki-power signature that allows him to operate at the canonical Z-Fighter power-ceiling alongside Goku.

Uub's specific institutional features for the corpus are:

- **The 28th World Martial Arts Tournament reveal scene.** At the canonical Buu-Saga-epilogue tournament held five years after the Spirit-Bomb-finishing-blow, Uub enters the adult division as a ten-year-old seeking prize money for his impoverished family — the canonical *child-poverty-as-Earthling-Z-Fighter-recruitment-vector* register that the TB2-033 trivia bullet foregrounds. His canonical first-round opponent is Goku himself. Goku deliberately provokes Uub to anger mid-match to unlock the suppressed power signature — the canonical *Goku-recognizes-his-reincarnated-wish* beat that closes Dragon Ball Z's original-run continuity. The TB2-033 *Shocking Latent Ability* card-title is the canonical Bandai-canonical mechanical-encoding of this reveal scene at the card-game-design level.
- **The off-world disciple-training departure.** At the end of the 28th World Tournament match, Goku canonically declares his intention to take Uub as a personal disciple and exits Earth with him for off-world training — the canonical *A Hero's Farewell* DBZ-finale beat (anime episode 291, 1996). The canonical departure ends Dragon Ball Z's original-run continuity on the *protagonist-passes-the-mantle-to-his-successor* register; the canonical five-year-training-arc gap is the canonical narrative-bridge between the End of Z epilogue and the Dragon Ball GT continuity. The BT3-014 Hidden Power Uub card depicts the post-five-year-training-arc adolescent Uub in the canonical GT Black Star Dragon Ball Saga era.
- **The Majuub voluntary fusion with Mr. Buu.** During the canonical Dragon Ball GT Baby Saga, the adolescent-young-adult Uub canonically voluntarily fuses with Mr. Buu (the canonical Good Buu / Mr. Buu reformed-villain register anchored at [[majin-buu]]) to produce the Majuub combined-form — a canonical *Buu-Saga-reformed-villain-fuses-with-Kid-Buu-reincarnation* compound-character used against Baby and Super 17. The canonical Majuub fusion is one of the franchise's most-developed *the-reformed-evil-and-the-reincarnated-evil-fuse-as-allies-against-the-current-threat* design moves. No Majuub-anchored card in current corpus; the canonical Majuub fusion register is documented here as a future-print register-expansion candidate.
- **The dual-IP passing-of-the-torch TB2-069 secret-rare capstone print.** TB2-069 Son Goku & Uub Seeds of the Future is the canonical TB2-set-capstone secret-rare print honoring the canonical End-of-Z protagonist-succession beat. The card's *Dragon Balls Will Be There For Them* flavor-text directly references the canonical reassurance Goku gives Uub at the 28th World Tournament departure: the Dragon Balls (and by extension the canonical Z-Fighter cohort) will be there as the inherited-protection-apparatus for the next-generation protagonist-cohort. The card's secret-rare rarity tier and $11.23 market price as of 2026-05-14 confirm the canonical TB2-set-capstone status; the dual-character print structurally parallels the BT2-098 Father-Son Kamehameha dual-IP precedent at [[son-goku]] / [[son-gohan]].

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Uub is the canonical human reincarnation of Kid Buu via King Yemma's afterlife-judgment-of-Goku's-wish, the canonical *evil-spirit-reformed-by-human-rebirth-without-memory* design statement at protagonist-succession scale, the canonical Goku-disciple of the End-of-Z epilogue, the canonical *Dragon Ball Z protagonist-succession* register that closes the original-run continuity, and the canonical Majuub fusion-component for the GT-era Baby-Saga voluntary-fusion-with-reformed-villain compound-character. Three corpus anchors span two Dragon Ball Super Card Game sets (TB2 + BT3) that hold the End-of-Z and GT-era source periods. A future Uub-themed Discrete Lair earns its lore-aware-buyer credibility without naming the protagonist-succession thesis.

# Final Fantasy Brave Exvius opening-arc party (Rain, Lasswell, Fina)

Final Fantasy Brave Exvius (A-Lim / Square Enix / gumi Inc., 2015 JP / 2016 global) is a free-to-play mobile JRPG set in the world of Lapis, structured across three narrative seasons. Its opening-arc playable party is the three protagonists introduced in Season One — Rain (knight-commander of the Kingdom of Grandshelt's airship brigade), Lasswell (Rain's adopted brother and the deuteragonist whose own arc is foregrounded in Season Two), and Fina (the amnesiac girl who emerged from the Crystal of Earth and grants Rain and Lasswell the power of Visions to oppose the Sworn Six of Paladia). The global version shut down on 2022-12-31; the JP service continues. Square Enix's Final Fantasy Trading Card Game preserves the FFBE roster as the "Category BE" card-frame archetype, and Opus VIII (March 2019) is the set where the FFBE opening-arc party simultaneously debuted across multiple rarity slots — the designer-formalized FFBE-cohort moment in FFTCG.

## What this node anchors

Card art, card-frame text (Category BE markers, FFBE job-class tags), and FFTCG mechanical design that depicts or supports a canonical FFBE opening-arc party member as either a playable Forward / Backup on the FFTCG board or as a support piece for a Category BE deck. Membership predicates on (a) the character appearing in the FFBE Season One playable roster per A-Lim / Square Enix's 2015-2016 canon, and (b) the FFTCG printing carrying the explicit Category BE marker in the card frame. Cards that depict FFBE side characters or later-season additions (the Sworn Six of Paladia antagonists, Sakura, Nichol, Lid, Jake, etc.) are deliberately excluded from this node — when corpus accumulates enough of them, a sibling `ffbe-extended-cast` or per-character node can be split off via an Edgelord-enlightened pass.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Rain** (Opus VIII, 8-021R, rare) — knight-commander of Grandshelt's airship brigade and the active protagonist of the FFBE opening arc; son of Sir Raegen, the kingdom's finest knight before his mysterious disappearance. The Opus VIII Rare printing depicts him in his canonical dark-coat-with-red-accents design holding the broadsword that signals his knight-of-Grandshelt vocation. Designed by Visual Works per the FFTCG card frame.
- **Lasswell** (Opus VIII, 8-043H, hero rarity, with a foil duplicate also in corpus) — Rain's adopted brother and FFBE's deuteragonist; raised by Sir Raegen alongside Rain after his own parents' deaths in his infancy, wielder of the katana "Purple Lightning" which appears in Brave Exvius's title-screen logo. The Opus VIII Hero printing was the marketing co-headliner of the FFBE / FFXV push per the Square Enix press release, with Noctis (FFXV) as the other co-headliner. Mechanically a 4-cost Ice Forward with a freeze-lock combat ability that FFTCG Crystarium rated "Must Kill" in Opus VIII limited.
- **Fina (Legend)** (Opus VIII, 8-060L, legend rarity) — the metaphysical axis of the FFBE opening-arc party; the girl who emerged from the Crystal of Earth with no memories save her name and grants Rain and Lasswell the power of Visions. The Legend-rarity printing is the high-rarity chase version and the higher-secondary-value half of Fina's dual-print in Opus VIII (market ~$0.86 vs the Common at ~$0.17). Wielder of the bow Reincarnation; canonical White Mage.
- **Fina (Common)** (Opus VIII, 8-061C, common) — the same character at budget-tier accessibility; 3-cost Wind Forward White Mage with the unusual dual-trigger (on entry + on Break Zone exit) that lets her activate other Wind Characters. The dual-print structure (8-060L + 8-061C) is FFTCG's standard design move for featured-IP protagonists, ensuring Fina is accessible at both chase-rare and budget tiers within the same set.

## The design thesis

The FFBE opening-arc trio's most BBL-relevant property is its canonical structuring as a *trinity* — not a "loose band of adventurers" framing but a metaphysically-coupled three: Fina grants Vision-power, Rain wields it as kingdom-defender, Lasswell wields it as brother-and-foil. The trio's narrative weight rests on the relational triangle rather than on per-character backstory beats. This is a different structural shape than the FFVI Returners cohort (`[[ff6-returners-party]]`) — the Returners are a 14-member named resistance faction whose canonical anti-imperial framing puts them in the `[[rebellion]]` hub; the FFBE opening-arc party is a 3-member metaphysically-coupled protagonist trio whose canonical framing is power-conferral-and-brotherhood rather than resistance-against-extraction. Per Potion-rule sub-rule 2 (`[[bbl-museum-curation-framing]]` parent, `[[_triple-thesis]]` canonical), no `related_hubs` adjacency is asserted at this node level — the trio's canonical event-structure is JRPG-protagonist-trinity, not labor/rebellion/stewardship-coded. A bundle built around this trio would carry "three characters, two brothers, one girl who hands them the power to be heroes" as its narrative spine, without forcing a triple-thesis adjacency the canon doesn't support.

The Opus VIII deployment is itself the designer-formalized cohesion signal: Square Enix's press release explicitly stages this set as the FFBE-debut moment in FFTCG, with the opening-arc trio across four rarity slots (Rain Rare, Lasswell Hero, Fina Legend + Common). The two co-headliners flagged in the press release were Lasswell (FFBE) and Noctis (FFXV), making the FFBE opening-arc party the larger of the two flagship FFBE/FFXV pushes that defined Opus VIII's character-debut shape.

## Caveats

- **Modeled as a multi-character opening-arc-party node, not a per-character node.** This follows the `[[ff6-returners-party]]`, `[[universe-6-tournament-cohort]]`, and `[[crane-school-cohort]]` precedent — a *named in-canon protagonist cohort* is hosted as a single character-layer node rather than a per-member fan-out. When BBL acquires a Rain, Lasswell, or Fina printing from a different Opus set, the new card attaches here rather than spawning a per-character node. If a future single-character anchor becomes load-bearing (e.g. corpus accumulates 4+ Rain printings across Opus sets such that a per-character Rain node beats a roster-level cohort node for query purposes), Edgelord-enlightened can refactor by adding a child node and updating this node's prose to reference it.
- **The scope is the opening-arc trio, not the FFBE extended cast.** FFBE's Season Two and Season Three introduce additional party members (Sakura, Nichol, Lid, Jake, etc.) plus the Sworn Six of Paladia antagonist faction. None of those characters are in BBL corpus yet, and none of them belong on this node when they arrive. If three or more FFBE-but-not-opening-arc-trio characters accumulate in corpus, the right move is a sibling `ffbe-extended-cast` node (Edgelord-Mr.-Nodeley pass) rather than overloading this node.
- **Per Potion-rule sub-rule 2 (`[[bbl-museum-curation-framing]]` parent, `[[_triple-thesis]]` canonical), no triple-thesis hub adjacency is asserted at this node level.** The FFBE opening-arc party's canonical event-structure is JRPG-protagonist-trinity (Vision-grantor + active protagonist + deuteragonist) rather than labor/rebellion/stewardship-coded. A bundle built around the trio is free to extrapolate via per-card connections (Rain's airship-brigade-leadership reading as institutional-soldier-coded, etc.), but the node level keeps `related_hubs: []` to avoid manufacturing canonical-event evidence the game doesn't provide. This is the same discipline `[[saiyan]]` and `[[eldrazi]]` exhibit and the explicit precedent for refusing the adjacency at this layer.
- **Graham (8-075C) is FFDimensions/FFL, not FFBE — explicitly out of scope.** The corpus's Graham card was initially mis-stamped as "Final Fantasy Legends" then corrected to Final Fantasy Dimensions (FFL is the Japanese-market title for FFDimensions). Graham shares Opus VIII set context with the FFBE trio but is from a different game; cross-attaching him would conflate Category BE and the FFL/Dimensions Category. A future FFDimensions cluster node may emerge as N grows (Sophia 8-038C is also flagged as possibly FFDimensions and worth re-verifying); for now Graham stays unattached to this node.
- **Lasswell has a foil duplicate (8-043H--foil) printed-from-the-same-card-number.** Both are listed in `appears_on` because both are distinct corpus rows representing distinct physical cards in inventory; the cohort-membership predicate is per-row not per-card-number, matching the `[[ff6-returners-party]]` precedent for Setzer EX foil treatment.

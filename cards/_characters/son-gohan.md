# Son Gohan

The canonical eldest son of Son Goku and Chi-Chi, the franchise's first depicted half-Saiyan / half-Earthling, and the canonical *Dragon Ball Z* protagonist whose Cell Saga kill the museum-curation framing of the Babidi-and-Father-Son-Kamehameha arc reads as the canonical case study in **labor-credit-erasure as designer-coordinated set-thematic structure**. Introduced in Dragon Ball Z manga chapter 195 (1988, Akira Toriyama, Shueisha) as the four-year-old child kidnapped by Raditz at the start of the Saiyan Saga, Gohan progresses across the franchise's longest-arc protagonist trajectory: Namekian-trained child fighter through the Namek / Frieza / Android arcs, eleven-year-old Cell-Games victor with the canonical Father-Son Kamehameha finishing blow (the executor-of-record while Goku's spirit amplifies from the afterlife and Vegeta's unrequested sideline-blast distraction goes uncredited), sixteen-year-old Adolescent Gohan scholar-vigilante across the Buu Saga and 25th World Martial Arts Tournament (the canonical *first character in franchise canon to achieve SS2*, transformed at age 11 during the Cell Games kill and re-demonstrated at age 16 as the open-target tournament moment that allows Spopovich and Yamu to drain his energy for Babidi's Majin Buu revival), Old-Kai-unlocked Mystic / Ultimate Gohan whose untransformed power canonically exceeded SS3 Goku in the Buu Saga, the End-of-Z married-to-Videl father-of-Pan adult, the GT-era de-trained academic scholar, the DBS-era return-to-form Beast Gohan against Cell Max in *Dragon Ball Super: Super Hero* (Toei Animation, 2022). Across forty years of franchise output the character has been re-instantiated into substantively-distinct named aspects whose visual identifiers, canonical age, costume, and transformation-state are designer-coordinated for Bandai's card-game character-tag field to disambiguate per-print.

Six canonical Gohan aspects operate across the corpus and the franchise: the **Cell-Saga teen** (the canonical SS / SS Full Power / SS2 progression that culminates in the Cell Games kill; canonical Hyperbolic Time Chamber training year with Goku where the "Fully Trained" mastered-form state is achieved; corpus anchor BT2-074 Fully Trained Super Saiyan Son Gohan); the **Buu-Saga adolescence / 25th World Tournament register** (the canonical 16-year-old green-Piccolo-given-gi scholar-fighter whose SS2 demonstration triggers the canonical energy-drain by Spopovich and Yamu for Babidi's harvest plot; corpus anchors TB2-003 Heroic Duo Son Gohan + TB2-021 Top of His Game Son Gohan); the **Mystic / Ultimate Gohan power-unlock register** (the canonical Old-Kai-25-hour-ritual-unlocked untransformed-but-massively-power-elevated state that exceeded SS3 Goku at debut; corpus anchor BT4-048 Newfound Power Son Gohan); the **GT-era Saiyan/Earthling de-trained-academic register** (the canonical Baby Saga era hybrid-lineage card-pool partner with Pan and Trunks, depicted pre-Baby-possession on hero-coded prints; corpus anchors BT4-006 Blaze of Glory Son Gohan + BT4-028 Son Gohan); the **End-of-Z / Galactic Battle dynasty register** (the canonical "Family of Justice" hero-as-heir framing that maps Gohan's championship-lineage continuity onto the post-Buu-Saga adult-protector positioning; corpus anchor BT1-062 Son Gohan, Family of Justice); and the **Father-Son Kamehameha dual-IP technique register** (the canonical Cell Games finishing-blow technique card depicting both Gohan-as-executor and Goku-as-spiritual-amplifier together; corpus anchor BT2-098 Father-Son Kamehameha). The corpus's BT4-084 Intrepid Dynasty Son Gohan vision-pass-anchored print (trivia outstanding) places provisionally within the Goku's-Lineage dynasty register pending the canonical Bandai character-tag identification at trivia.

The aspects share a single canonical character (the half-Saiyan / half-Earthling lineage from Goku and Chi-Chi; the canonical Saiyan/Earthling DBSCG special trait; the canonical hidden-power-narratively-higher-than-pure-Saiyan-peer design device that Toriyama installed into the franchise's protagonist roster as a deliberate generational hand-off from Goku-as-protagonist to Gohan-as-protagonist; the canonical Piccolo-mentor lineage that distinguishes Gohan from every other Z-Fighter; the canonical *first half-Saiyan, first SS2 wielder, first Mystic / Ultimate form* design-firsts that mark Gohan as Toriyama's narrative experiment in protagonist-hand-off across the Cell-and-Buu sagas). They differ in age, costume, transformation-state, and the *cosmological scope* of their respective stories: Cell-Saga teen Gohan defends Earth from Cell at age 11; Buu-Saga adolescent Gohan navigates the 25th World Tournament harvest plot at age 16; Mystic Gohan unlocks the Old-Kai-ritual power-state for the Buu fight; GT Gohan retires from training and is possessed by Baby; End-of-Z dynasty Gohan operates as the post-Buu protector-and-heir; Father-Son Kamehameha technique-card Gohan canonicalizes the multi-actor labor-credit-erasure beat at the card-game's mechanical-cohesion level. The Dragon Ball Super Card Game's print history treats the aspects as distinguishable via Bandai's official character-tag field in the card database, making per-card aspect identification a clerical lookup rather than a vision-confidence problem.

## What this node anchors

Card art and effects that depict Son Gohan himself across any of his canonical aspects, the Father-Son Kamehameha that operates as his canonical Cell-Games finishing-blow signature technique, the Super Saiyan / SS Full Power / SS2 / Mystic-Ultimate transformation arcs that span his Cell-Saga-and-Buu-Saga progression, the canonical green Piccolo-given gi that distinguishes his Buu-Saga adolescence from the Cell-Saga teen's orange-and-blue Turtle School gi, the canonical Goku's-Lineage family-tree relations across Goku (father), Chi-Chi (mother), Goten (younger brother), Pan (daughter), and Videl (wife), the canonical Piccolo-mentor-lineage relation that grounds Gohan's training-arc, and the canonical apparatus-of-extraction-victim register at the 25th World Tournament where Babidi's sleeper agents drain his energy as the operation's harvest event. ## The functional ideology

Son Gohan's most BBL-relevant property is that he is the **canonical exemplar of labor-credit-erasure at protagonist scale**. The Cell Games kill (chapter 420, 1992) is the franchise's most precisely-designed multi-actor labor distribution: Gohan executes physically with one injured arm; Goku amplifies spiritually from the afterlife; Vegeta's unrequested sideline blast distracts Cell at the tactical pivot moment that allows Gohan to land the killing blow. The technique-card BT2-098 *credits exactly two of three participants by name*: "Father-Son" inscribes Goku and Gohan into the canonical title-of-record while Vegeta — who performed the tactical-distraction labor without which the kill does not land — is omitted from the title and from every subsequent retelling. The Buu Saga Adolescence register (TB2-003 + TB2-021) inverts the labor-credit pattern at the harvest scale: Gohan demonstrates SS2 at Kibito's request *as a public scientific exhibition* of his canonical power peak — the moment Babidi's sleeper agents Spopovich and Yamu immediately drain his energy for Majin Buu's revival. Gohan's *open display of canonical-protagonist labor* IS the trigger for the apparatus-of-extraction's harvest event; his labor is publicly visible, publicly conscripted, and publicly disappeared into Buu's revival within minutes of the demonstration. Two pages later, [[hercule]] (Mr. Satan) takes credit for Cell's defeat as the canonical World-Martial-Arts-Champion-of-record — completing the labor-credit-erasure cycle at the public-celebrity scale that the [[hercule]] node already canonicalizes from the credit-claimer side. The Babidi-and-Father-Son-Kamehameha arc's structural reading is therefore the canonical Z-canon case study in *labor-credit-erasure as designer-coordinated set-thematic structure*: real labor performed, credit redistributed away from the laborer, and the apparatus-of-extraction (Babidi for the harvest plot, Hercule for the public-credit-claim) operates by routing canonical-protagonist labor into its own beneficiary structures.

Son Gohan's specific institutional features for the corpus are:

- **The Hyperbolic Time Chamber training year.** During the Cell Saga, Gohan and Goku trained for one canonical year inside the Hyperbolic Time Chamber (one year inside equals one day outside), aiming for Super Saiyan Full Power — a mastered transformation state where the form becomes as natural as the base form, eliminating physical strain from staying transformed. The corpus's BT2-074 Fully Trained Super Saiyan Son Gohan card canonically encodes this milestone via its "Fully Trained" subtitle. The training year is the canonical setup for the Cell Games kill, where Gohan's SS Full Power state allowed sustained combat against Cell that culminated in the SS2 transformation triggered by Android 16's death.
- **The 25th World Martial Arts Tournament harvest plot.** At the 25th Tenkaichi Budokai, Gohan agreed to demonstrate his Super Saiyan 2 transformation at Kibito's request, making himself an open target. Spopovich and Yamu — Babidi's sleeper agents embedded as registered tournament fighters years prior — immediately swarmed him and drained his energy with Babidi's Energy Absorber to power Majin Buu's revival. The corpus's TB2-021 Top of His Game Son Gohan canonically encodes this scene; the canonical apparatus-of-extraction-victim register is the structural inverse of the Cell Games credit-erasure register, with the Babidi anchor ([[babidi]]) on the apparatus-operator side.
- **The Old Kai 25-hour potential-unlock ritual.** In the Buu Saga, Old Kai performed a 25-hour ritual that unlocked Gohan's full latent potential, producing the Mystic / Ultimate Gohan state — a power level that exceeded SS3 Goku at debut without any visible transformation. The form is canonically distinct from SS-and-beyond transformations: hair stays dark, no aura color signals the shift. The corpus's BT4-048 Newfound Power Son Gohan canonically encodes this unlock. The Mystic state is the canonical *latent-potential-as-power-source* design device that Toriyama installed into Gohan's character arc — the same hidden-potential-narratively-higher-than-pure-Saiyan-peer device that the BT4-028 trivia surfaces as a recurring Z-canon character mechanic.
- **The half-Saiyan / half-Earthling species-hybrid template.** Gohan is canonically the franchise's first depicted half-Saiyan / half-Earthling, the template that subsequent half-Saiyans like Trunks (BT4-012, [[trunks]]) and quarter-Saiyans like Pan (BT4-009, [[pan]]) inherit. The Saiyan/Earthling Special Trait in the DBS Card Game encodes this dual heritage at the mechanical level; the trait is shared across BT4's hybrid-lineage cluster (Gohan BT4-006 + Pan BT4-009 + Trunks BT4-012) and across the corpus's broader Goku's-Lineage cohort. The Pan node ([[pan]]) already cross-references son-goku (paternal grandfather); this node closes the [[son-goku]] – son-gohan – [[pan]] generational chain at the character layer.
- **The Great Saiyaman / Orange Star High School scholar-vigilante adolescent register.** In the Buu Saga, the 16-year-old Adolescent Gohan operates concurrently as a registered tournament fighter at the 25th World Tournament and as a high-school student in Satan City under the Great Saiyaman vigilante identity. The TB2-003 trivia surfaces the canonical Bandai character-tag `Son Gohan: Adolescence` for this register; the canonical green Piccolo-given gi is the canonical Buu-Saga-adolescence costume identifier. The Great Saiyaman vigilante register is canonically the moment Gohan begins the franchise's *protagonist-to-scholar* trajectory that culminates in the GT-era retired-from-training academic adult.

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Son Gohan is the canonical eldest son of Goku and the franchise's first half-Saiyan whose Cell Games kill is the canonical *labor-credit-erasure* design move at protagonist scale, whose Buu-Saga adolescent SS2 demonstration triggers Babidi's harvest plot in the structural-inverse register, whose Mystic / Ultimate form is the canonical *latent-potential-as-power-source* design device, and whose multi-aspect canonical-character trajectory holds across Cell-Saga teen / Buu-Saga adolescent / Mystic / GT / End-of-Z / Father-Son-technique aspects under one parent. Seven corpus anchors (plus one vision-only anchor and one dual-IP technique card) span the four Dragon Ball Super Card Game sets that hold Cell-Saga and Buu-Saga character source periods. A future Son Gohan Discrete Lair earns its lore-aware-buyer credibility without naming the labor-credit-erasure thesis.

## Caveats

- **One parent, six aspects, no premature solo split (yet).** Following the [[son-goku]] precedent (single character node with multi-incarnation roster covering Childhood / Z / GT / DBS incarnations under one parent), the [[trunks]] precedent (single character node with multi-incarnation roster covering Future / GT / Xeno / Kid incarnations under one parent), the [[tamiyo]] precedent (single character node with multi-aspect roster covering Moon Sage / Field Researcher / Collector of Tales / Inquisitive Student / Compleated Sage incarnations under one anchor), and the [[nicol-bolas]] precedent (single character node with multi-arc roster covering the Bolas-Falls cluster plus apparatus-of-Bolas aspects under one anchor), the canonical move is one parent Son Gohan node anchoring all aspects. A future split could partition the parent into `son-gohan-cell-saga` / `son-gohan-buu-saga` / `son-gohan-mystic` / `son-gohan-gt` solo sub-nodes if the corpus accumulates enough per-aspect density to warrant the split. For now the parent holds; the per-aspect framing in the "Aspects in the corpus" section above does the disambiguation work that a future solo-sub-node split would formalize.

- **Bandai's character-tag field is the canonical per-print aspect identifier.** Each Dragon Ball Super Card Game card carries an official character tag in Bandai's database that disambiguates which Gohan aspect the print depicts. The canonical tags identified across the corpus prints include `Son Gohan` (the generic identifier), `Son Gohan: Adolescence` (the TB2-003 Buu-Saga 16-year-old register), and the implicit era-stamped variants for Cell Saga / Baby Saga / Broly Saga / Mystic registers. Cards in the corpus' `appears_on` carry the aspect identifier in their trivia bullets where the trivia pass has run; future Gohan-print acquisitions should preserve the Bandai-canonical tag in the card's trivia per the [[bbl-triviabot-no-role-identity-conflation]] memory rule.

- **The Father-Son Kamehameha dual-IP card's canonical second-figure attribution.** BT2-098 Father-Son Kamehameha depicts both Gohan and Goku per canonical Cell Games narrative. The card's `suspected_ip: Son Goku and Son Gohan (Dragon Ball Z)` field correctly identifies the dual-character print; the `characters:` frontmatter wires this node and [[son-goku]] together at the card layer. The card's title canonically credits the technique to the Father-Son pair, omitting Vegeta's tactical-distraction labor — a designer-coordinated labor-credit-erasure beat the museum-curation-framing thesis stack reaches for in any Cell Games Discrete Lair commission.

- **The BT4-084 Intrepid Dynasty Son Gohan vision-only qualification.** BT4-084 is vision-pass-anchored within the Goku's-Lineage dynasty register but trivia-outstanding; the per-print canonical Bandai character-tag and era designation will be added when the trivia pass runs. Vision-pass IP confidence is sufficient for parent-node anchoring; the trivia-layer precision is a follow-up pass per the [[bbl-trivia-never-consider-preenriched]] cursor-method discipline.

- **The Adolescent vs Cell-Saga-teen disambiguation: green gi vs orange-and-blue gi.** Per the [[bbl-anti-confab-prompt]] memory rule's costume-as-canonical-identifier discipline, the canonical Adolescent (Buu-Saga) Gohan wears the green Piccolo-given gi at the 25th World Tournament; the canonical Cell-Saga-teen Gohan wears the orange-and-blue Turtle School gi during the Hyperbolic Time Chamber training and Cell Games arc. The TB2-021 Top of His Game Son Gohan (Buu-Saga Adolescence SS2) and TB2-003 Heroic Duo Son Gohan (Buu-Saga Adolescence base) are canonically green-gi prints; the BT2-074 Fully Trained Super Saiyan Son Gohan and BT1-062 Son Gohan, Family of Justice are canonically orange-and-blue-gi prints. Vision-pass agents should anchor aspect identification on the costume-color signal alongside the canonical SS-form and age-proportion signals.

- **The Mystic Gohan no-visible-transformation disambiguation.** Per the [[bbl-anti-confab-prompt]] memory rule's hair-as-canonical-identifier discipline, the canonical Mystic / Ultimate Gohan form does NOT include the golden SS upright hair — hair stays dark and no aura color signals the shift. BT4-048 Newfound Power Son Gohan is the corpus's canonical Mystic-state print; vision-pass agents should not retroactively re-anchor BT4-048 as a Super-Saiyan-form print on the absence-of-golden-hair signal.

- **The GT-era pre-Baby-possession disambiguation.** Per the [[bbl-triviabot-no-role-identity-conflation]] memory rule, BT4-006 Blaze of Glory Son Gohan and BT4-028 Son Gohan depict the canonical *pre-possession* Gohan in the GT Baby Saga continuity; Gohan is canonically possessed by Baby (the Tuffle-parasite antagonist anchored at [[baby-dbgt]]) before Baby upgrades to Vegeta-as-host, but the corpus's BT4 prints are coded as hero-side rather than Baby-possessed-side. Bundle copy that frames GT-era Gohan as Baby-possessed without canonical citation overreaches.

- **The Three-Participants-Two-Named Father-Son Kamehameha caveat.** BT2-098's title credits Father (Goku) and Son (Gohan) as the technique-of-record participants; Vegeta's tactical-distraction sideline blast is canonically uncredited in the title and in every subsequent franchise retelling. This is the canonical *labor-credit-erasure* design move at the technique-card-mechanical-cohesion level. Bundle copy can lean on this without inventing canon; the museum-curation-framing thesis stack treats the title's omission as the structural feature, not a clerical oversight.

- **Goku's-Lineage family-tree relations are in-graph cousin-cluster territory.** Gohan is canonically the son of Goku ([[son-goku]]) and Chi-Chi, the older brother of Goten, the father of Pan ([[pan]]), the husband of Videl, and the canonical Z-Fighter who Piccolo (no solo node yet) operates as primary mentor for across the Saiyan-and-Namek arcs. The Pan node ([[pan]]) already cross-references son-goku (paternal grandfather); this Son Gohan node closes the son-goku – son-gohan – pan generational chain at the character layer. A future `chi-chi` or `videl` or `son-goten` solo character node would `related_characters:` here once corpus density supports the split (currently TB2-011 Heroic Duo Videl, TB2-018 Babidi's Spaceship's BT2-018 trivia surfaces Videl ref at level 2, and TB2-004 + TB2-022 Tiny Rivals / Scuffle Time Son Goten are the corpus's Videl and Goten anchors at present — Goten threshold-passing pending separately).

- **Future Discrete Lair candidate.** 7 trivia-passed corpus anchors (plus 1 vision-only anchor BT4-084 and 1 dual-IP technique-card BT2-098) across 6 aspects spanning 4 booster sets + 1 promo BE set form a viable cohesion stack for a Son Gohan-themed Discrete Lair. Total inventory quantity across the 9 cards is **17** (BT1-062 qty 2, BT2-074 qty 1, BT4-006 qty 2, BT4-028 qty 1, BT4-048 qty 1, BT4-084 qty 1, TB2-003 qty 6, TB2-021 qty 3, BT2-098 qty 1) — multi-copy bundle pricing leverage per [[bbl-multi-copy-bundles]] is present though more modest than [[son-goku]]'s 31-copy stack. Bundle copy can lean on the labor-credit-erasure-at-protagonist-scale thesis without naming the thesis: the Cell Games kill, the 25th World Tournament harvest, the Mystic-state latent-potential-as-power-source, the Goku's-Lineage half-Saiyan generational hand-off, the Father-Son-Kamehameha three-participants-two-named technique-card. The thesis stack supports the Discrete Lair narrative-first framing per [[bbl-narrative-first-lairs]]. Reserve for when the Z-canon protagonist-trajectory Discrete Lair commission lands.

- **Cross-edge with [[babidi]] is canonical at the victim-vs-operator axis.** Babidi's node already documents TB2-021 Top of His Game Son Gohan as "the *target* of Babidi's operation, not a member of it; a future `son-gohan` solo node would anchor this card and the Babidi narrative would surface through that node's `related_characters: [babidi]` field." This node closes that loop: `related_characters: [babidi]` is set at the apparatus-of-extraction-victim axis. The TB2-021 card now anchors via two character nodes simultaneously — `characters: [son-gohan]` at the canonical primary subject identification, with the Babidi narrative surfacing at the related_characters traversal. Future bundle copy that wants the Babidi-vs-Gohan harvest-plot framing draws from both nodes for the apparatus-of-extraction reading.

- **Cross-edge with [[son-goku]] is canonical at the father-son axis AND at the labor-credit-erasure axis.** Son Goku's node already documents Son Gohan as "canonical eldest son" and surfaces the future-`son-gohan`-solo-node split as the deferred Goku's-Lineage family-tree commission. This node closes that loop. The Father-Son Kamehameha BT2-098 card is the canonical dual-IP print where both nodes are wired via `characters: [son-goku, son-gohan]` simultaneously; the card-frontmatter pointer captures the dual-character identification, and bundle copy that draws on the Cell Games kill's three-participants-two-named labor-credit-erasure structural reading anchors from both nodes.

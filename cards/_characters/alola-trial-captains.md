# Alola Trial Captains

The seven-member senior-local-trainer cohort who run the themed Island Trials that compose the middle tier of Pokemon Sun and Moon's (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 2016) three-tier Alolan Island Challenge progression — the canonical Alola-region substitute for the Gym Leader role used in all prior main-series Pokemon games. Each Trial Captain is a senior local trainer (typically a young adult or older teen with a specialized type affinity) who curates a themed Trial set in a distinctive Alolan location: the Trial culminates in a Totem Pokemon battle followed by a Z-Crystal reward, with the player then proceeding to the same island's **Grand Trial** against the island's **Kahuna** before advancing to the next island. The seven canonical members are **Ilima** (Normal-type, Hau'oli City Trainers' School Trial on Melemele Island, Totem Gumshoos / Raticate), **Lana** (Water-type, Brooklet Hill Trial on Akala Island, Totem Wishiwashi / Araquanid), **Mallow** (Grass-type, Lush Jungle Trial on Akala Island, Totem Lurantis), **Kiawe** (Fire-type, Wela Volcano Park Trial on Akala Island, Totem Salazzle / Marowak), **Sophocles** (Electric-type, Hokulani Observatory Trial on Ula'ula Island, Totem Vikavolt / Togedemaru), **Acerola** (Ghost-type, Thrifty Megamart Trial on Ula'ula Island, Totem Mimikyu — Acerola is the only Trial Captain canonically promoted to the [[alola-elite-four]] by Pokemon Sun and Moon's endgame), and **Mina** (Fairy-type, Poni Grove Trial in Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon's expanded post-game, Totem Ribombee). The Akala Island captain trio (Lana, Mallow, Kiawe) are explicitly close friends in-game and frequently appear together; the Ula'ula trio (Sophocles, Acerola, plus Team Skull NPCs running interference) handle the second-tier island; Ilima opens Melemele Island as the player's first Trial Captain encounter; Mina closes Poni Island in the Ultra-version's expanded league.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text for the seven Alola Trial Captain members across their TCG appearances. The unifying frame across the cohort is fivefold: (a) the **Island Trial substitute-for-Gym-Leader role** — each Trial Captain runs a themed Trial that mechanically and narratively substitutes for the Gym Battle used by every prior main-series Pokemon game, making the Alola cohort canonically the *first* fully-replaced gym progression in the franchise, (b) the **per-captain type specialization** mapping each Trial to a single Pokemon type (Normal, Water, Grass, Fire, Electric, Ghost, Fairy — seven of the seventeen non-legendary Pokemon types), (c) the **Akala friendship cluster** — Lana, Mallow, and Kiawe are designer-confirmed close friends and the three-captain Akala middle-island slate is the cohort's strongest sub-cluster (TAG TEAM pairings, frequent co-appearance in anime and game cutscenes), (d) the **per-Trial Z-Crystal reward** — each Trial Captain hands out a type-specific Z-Crystal on completion, the SM-era equivalent of the gym-badge system, (e) the **Sun & Moon-era TCG Supporter spotlight** — the SM expansions sequentially introduced Trial Captains as Supporter cards: Mallow in Guardians Rising (SM2, May 2017), Kiawe / Sophocles / Acerola in Burning Shadows (SM3, August 2017), Ilima and Lana across subsequent SM expansions, with TAG TEAM Supporter pairings landing in Cosmic Eclipse (SM12, November 2019) as the cohort's culmination — including Mallow & Lana, the only canonical pairing within the cohort that's a TAG TEAM Supporter.

## The cohort thesis

The Alola Trial Captains' most BBL-relevant property is that the cohort canonically **replaces** the Gym Leader role used in every prior main-series Pokemon game. Pokemon Sun and Moon (2016) is the first main-series game to fully retire the gym-and-badge progression for an alternative structure — the Island Challenge's three-tier progression (Trial Captain themed Trial → Island Kahuna Grand Trial → Mount Lanakila Elite Four / Champion's Test). The Trial Captains occupy the middle tier as the *replacement* for Gym Leaders, with each Trial designed to substitute the standard one-on-one battle progression with a themed encounter that culminates in a Totem Pokemon battle plus type-specific Z-Crystal reward. The cohort is, in-canon, the canonical mechanism by which the franchise demonstrated that the Gym Leader / gym badge / Elite Four sequence is *one possible* league architecture rather than the *only* architecture — and BBL's narrative-first brand voice has a clear foothold in that distinction: the Trial Captains are the franchise's first canonical attempt at restructuring the gym progression away from a uniform 8-gym scheme, replacing it with a regionally-themed sequence of trials whose membership predicates on *local apprenticeship and craft mastery* rather than on standardized league-bureaucratic appointment.

The seven members' canonical-source frames also form a designer-coordinated **local-craft-and-vocation** cohort: Ilima is a Trainers' School graduate and the youngest cohort member, Lana is a Brooklet Hill fishing-family local, Mallow runs Aina's Kitchen family-restaurant, Kiawe performs traditional Alolan fire-dance and works at his family's Pa'u dance ranch, Sophocles is an observatory researcher and inventor, Acerola maintains the Aether House (an orphan-and-injured-Pokemon shelter) and is the last living member of an Alolan royal family, Mina is a traveling artist who closes Poni Island's Fairy-type trial in the Ultra version. Each Trial Captain's type specialty maps onto a non-league civilian vocation tied to the captain's home location — the cohort is framed in-canon as *senior local trainers with day jobs rooted in regional craft* rather than as full-time league bureaucrats. This is the structural parallel to the [[alola-elite-four]] cohort's *senior trainers with civilian vocations* framing: Trial Captains are the apprenticeship-tier *local-craft* model; Elite Four members are the league-summit *regional-craft* model; both tiers oppose the [[galar-gym-challenge]] cohort's *signed celebrity-athlete* model. The Alola Trial Captains and Alola Elite Four nodes together anchor the two adjacent tiers of Alola's three-tier league progression, with the Trial Captain tier explicitly framed as the apprenticeship and senior-local-trainer staging ground for league entry, the Elite Four tier as the league summit, and Acerola as the cohort's only canonical promotion across the two tiers within a single game generation.

## Caveats

- **Three of seven canonical Trial Captains in corpus; four remain documented but not yet acquired.** Three corpus entries are now pointered to this node (see `appears_on:`): Mallow (solo Supporter, GUR-127), Mallow & Lana (TAG TEAM Supporter, CSE-198), and Acerola (solo Supporter, BUS-112). Four canonical Trial Captains remain documented but not yet in inventory and will attach to this node when any TCG printing lands via a future Collectr CSV upload:
  - **Ilima** (Normal-type, Hau'oli City Trainers' School Trial captain) — printed as a Burning Shadows Supporter at no. 115/147 and in subsequent SM-era expansions.
  - **Kiawe** (Fire-type, Wela Volcano Park Trial captain) — printed as a Burning Shadows Supporter at no. 116/147 and in subsequent SM-era expansions.
  - **Sophocles** (Electric-type, Hokulani Observatory Trial captain) — printed as a Celestial Storm Supporter and in subsequent SM-era expansions.
  - **Mina** (Fairy-type, Poni Grove Trial captain in Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon's post-game) — printed in Forbidden Light as a Supporter at no. 116/131.

  The BBL corpus is four species-acquisitions away from full canonical Sun-and-Moon-era Trial Captain coverage, or three away from a Sun-and-Moon-base-version-only roster (excluding Mina, who is exclusive to the Ultra version). Lana also has solo Supporter prints (Sun & Moon Base Set, Celestial Storm) that would attach as additional Lana-side entries when acquired; the current corpus depiction is via the TAG TEAM print only. Any TCG printing of these characters across any set attaches to this node — membership predicates on character identity, not on set or printing.

- **Acerola's dual-role status as Trial Captain AND Elite Four member is structurally distinct from a generic dual-affiliation.** Acerola is the only canonical Trial Captain promoted to the Alola Elite Four within Pokemon Sun and Moon's main-game progression — her in-game Trial debut (Mimikyu-as-Totem at the Thrifty Megamart) is canonically continuous with her later Elite Four seat at Mount Lanakila as the Ghost-type specialist. This dual-role status is a designer-confirmed feature of the Alola league's founding charter rather than a Bulbapedia editorial reading. The Acerola card therefore points at BOTH this node (Trial Captain identity) AND the [[alola-elite-four]] node (Elite Four promotion), parallel to Olivia who points at both [[alola-elite-four]] and a future `alola-island-kahunas` node (Akala Kahuna + E4 dual role). The dual-role precedent is the canonical signature of Alola's three-tier league progression — Trial Captain → Island Kahuna → Elite Four — where each tier can recruit from the tier below it.

- **The cohort is Generation VII Alola-specific.** The Trial Captain role exists only in Pokemon Sun and Moon and Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon (2016-2017). No prior main-series Pokemon game uses the Trial Captain role (those games use Gym Leaders), and no subsequent main-series game has re-instantiated the role at cohort scope (Pokemon Sword & Shield 2019 returned to a Gym Leader / Gym Challenge format; Scarlet & Violet 2022 introduced a three-path structure including Gym Leaders, Titan Pokemon, and Team Star bosses). The Trial Captains are therefore the franchise's only canonical full-cohort replacement of the Gym Leader role to date. The sibling [[alola-elite-four]] node is the canonical adjacent cohort within the same league progression; the sibling [[galar-gym-challenge]] node is the closest cross-generation cohort precedent for a league-tier institutional cohort, with the opposing organizational ideology (apprenticeship-and-local-craft vs televised-spectacle-sponsored-athletics).

- **Akala friendship cluster is the cohort's strongest sub-cluster but not a separate cohort.** Lana, Mallow, and Kiawe are designer-confirmed close friends, all run Akala Island Trials, and frequently appear together in Pokemon Sun and Moon anime episodes and game cutscenes. The Mallow & Lana TAG TEAM Supporter is the canonical TCG manifestation of this friendship cluster — the cohort's only same-cohort TAG TEAM pairing. A future Akala-friendship-cluster sub-node could be commissioned if the threshold of in-corpus Akala-trio Trial Captain depictions reaches a critical mass, but for now the friendship cluster is documented in the cohort's body content rather than spun out as a separate node.

- **The Trial Captain role is canonically continuous with the Island Kahuna role one tier above.** Pokemon Sun and Moon's three-tier league progression (Trial Captain → Island Kahuna → Elite Four) is canonically a continuous apprenticeship-and-promotion sequence: a senior Trial Captain can be promoted to Island Kahuna (Hala was Melemele's Kahuna and an E4; Olivia was Akala's Kahuna and an E4); a Trial Captain can be promoted directly to Elite Four (Acerola). The [[alola-kahunas]] character node was commissioned wave 41 E1 as the Kahuna-tier institutional cohort node, with the Trial Captains as the apprenticeship-tier feed; the structural triangle (Trial Captains → Kahunas → Elite Four) is now fully node-anchored at the character layer.

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub.** Like [[alola-elite-four]], [[galar-gym-challenge]], [[team-rocket]], [[team-skull]], [[endriders]], [[aether-rangers]], [[orzhov-syndicate]], and [[azorius-senate]], this is a faction-collective entity hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer accepts both individual named characters and collective institutional entities). A future `_factions/` layer could re-home all institutional-collective entries; for now, single character layer, same precedent.

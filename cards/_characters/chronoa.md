# Chronoa (Supreme Kai of Time, Time Patrol commander)

The canonical commander of the Time Patrol — Bandai-Namco's deity-tier protagonist counter-anchor to the Time Breakers faction whose principal members ([[xenoverse-villains]] node: Demigra, Towa, Mira) are organized around the corruption of Dragon Ball's canonical history. Chronoa debuted in *Dragon Ball Online* (NTL Inc., 2010) before becoming the central protagonist-faction commander across *Dragon Ball Xenoverse* (Bandai Namco / Dimps, 2015) and *Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2* (2016), and was most recently re-deployed in *Dragon Ball Daima* (Toei Animation, 2024-2025) — her first Toei-animated canonical appearance beyond the Bandai-Namco Xenoverse continuity strand. Per Dragon Ball Wiki's Chronoa page, she is canonically a Supreme Kai (deity-tier rank in the Kai hierarchy) born over 75 million years ago, and her canonical operational tenure is the 75-million-year sealing of Demigra in the Crack of Time after the Demon Realm magician attempted to overthrow the cosmic order. In *Dragon Ball Xenoverse* lore she commands the Time Patrol from the Time Nest as supreme commander and mentor, with Xeno Trunks as her deputy field operative and the player-character Time Patroller as the principal field tool.

## The functional canon

Chronoa's BBL-relevant property is that she is **Bandai-Namco-canon's principal protagonist-side counter-anchor to the Time Breakers' corruption-of-history operation** — the Time Patrol commander whose canonical authority over Alternate Timeline erasure is canonically self-restrained, and whose 75-million-year sealing of Demigra is the structural pre-history that the entire [[xenoverse-villains]] node's antagonist operation reacts against. Per Dragon Ball Wiki's Chronoa and Time Patrol pages, the character's specific canonical features are:

- **The 75-million-year Demigra-sealing as Chronoa's defining operational precedent.** Chronoa imprisoned Demigra in the Crack of Time 75 million years before the Xenoverse-present, after he attempted to overthrow the cosmic order. The [[xenoverse-villains]] node already canonicalizes this jailer relationship from the Time Breaker side ("75-million-year Crack of Time imprisonment, the Supreme Kai of Time (Chronoa) jailer relationship"); this node carries the protagonist-side of the same canonical sealing-and-release operation. The Demigra-Chronoa antagonism is the temporal-scope-equivalent of the Heliod-Elspeth conflict in [[theros-pantheon]] — the jailer-and-released-prisoner adversary structure rendered at cosmic-deity scale.

- **Time Patrol command from the Time Nest.** Chronoa operates from the Time Nest, a sealed pocket-dimension headquarters where she trains and dispatches Time Patrollers (the player-character institution in the Xenoverse games) and her deputy commander Xeno Trunks (the canonical Future Trunks variant who serves as field operative under Chronoa's command). The command-and-mentor register is the clean-stewardship operating mode the [[stewardship]] hub indexes — care-across-the-canonical-commons-line, the commander tending the integrity of canonical history through coordinated field-team deployment.

- **Holy Time Judgement as canonical signature attack.** Chronoa's "Holy Time Judgement" attack — energy spheres formed in both hands and thrown to surround and explode the target — is her canonical Xenoverse moveset signature, referenced directly in BT3-122's card title and oracle-text rendering. The attack canonicalizes Chronoa as combat-capable in the deity-tier register; she is not purely an administrative figure but also a fighter when the Time Patrol's coordinated operation requires direct intervention.

- **Alternate Timeline erasure authority, deployed with canonical restraint.** Per the BT4-103 trivia canonical citation, Chronoa holds the authority to erase Alternate Timelines from official history and treats this power as a last resort precisely because erasure of a timeline destroys everyone inhabiting it. The canonical restraint-over-power framing is the BT4-103 trivia's explicit Bandai-confirmed citation: this is what stewardship-of-restraint looks like at deity-tier scope — power held but deliberately not deployed because deployment costs are catastrophic. The BBL reading of this canonical Bandai-published lore as a [[stewardship]] hub anchor is curatorial-overlay on top of canonical-event (per the Potion Rule: the canonical event is Bandai's published lore of Chronoa's restraint; the BBL curatorial overlay is the stewardship-of-restraint hub attribution).

- **Cross-Bandai-and-Toei canonical re-deployment.** Chronoa's *Dragon Ball Daima* (Toei, 2024-2025) appearance is the franchise's first Toei-animated canonical appearance of the character — extending her canonical operational scope from the Bandai-Namco-only Xenoverse continuity to the Toei-anime continuity strand. This canonically positions Chronoa as a cross-continuity-strand character with operational presence in both *Dragon Ball Online* / *Xenoverse* / *Super Dragon Ball Heroes* (Bandai-Namco canon) AND *Dragon Ball Daima* (Toei canon), making her structurally distinct from the [[xenoverse-villains]] cohort which remains Bandai-Namco-canon-only.

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Chronoa is, in Bandai's and Toei's own design intent, the canonical protagonist-side commander of the Time Patrol — the 75-million-year jailer of Demigra whose deity-tier authority over Alternate Timeline erasure is canonically held with deliberate restraint, and whose three-card corpus presence across two consecutive DBSCG sets (Cross Worlds BT3 + Colossal Warfare BT4) anchors the principal Bandai-canon protagonist response to the Time Breakers' corruption-of-history operation. Bundle copy that names Chronoa as the protagonist-side counter-anchor to the Time Breakers earns its lore-aware buyer credibility through Bandai's own design articles and Dragon Ball Wiki's canonical pages.

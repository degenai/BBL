# Babidi

The alien sorcerer son-clone of Bibidi who orchestrated Majin Buu's revival on Earth, ringmaster of the Babidi Saga (Dragon Ball Z episodes 220–253) and namesake antagonist of the Dragon Ball Super Card Game's *World Martial Arts Tournament* themed booster (DBS-TB02, September 2019). Babidi's plan required gathering pure energy from Earth's strongest fighters at the 25th World Martial Arts Tournament — the exact event TB02 is themed around — and the set's villain roster is structured as a vertical hierarchy descending through his canonical six-floor spaceship dungeon: Pui Pui (level 1) → Yakon (level 2) → Dabura (level 3) → Babidi's control room (level 5) → the Sealed Ball of Majin Buu (level 6). Six corpus cards anchor this arc directly: the named ringmaster (TB2-013), his enslaved demon-king enforcer (TB2-014 Dabura), his tournament-infiltration sleeper agents (TB2-015 Spopovich & Yamu), the level-1 guardian who later kills Yamu on his order (TB2-016 Pui Pui), the level-2 light-eating predator (TB2-017 Yakon), and the spaceship-as-dungeon itself (TB2-018). Mirrors the [[nicol-bolas]] and [[valgavoth]] precedent: a set-defining antagonist whose multi-card corpus cluster anchors here rather than card-to-card.

## The functional ideology

Babidi's most BBL-relevant property is that he is the **canonical exemplar of Manipulation Sorcery as labor-coercion magic**. His Mahōfūsatsu enslaves targets through the trace evil they already carry — Dabura was already King of the Demon Realm, Spopovich and Yamu were already corrupt martial artists, Vegeta in the same arc accepts the M-brand voluntarily because he is *already* ambivalent. Babidi's apparatus does not create evil; it weaponizes pre-existing latent evil and binds it into a vertical command hierarchy he sits atop without himself being physically powerful. The dungeon-as-spaceship reads as a literal Taylorist organizational chart: floor 1 disposable muscle, floor 2 escalating threat, floor 3 enforcer-king, floor 5 control room, floor 6 the productive asset (Majin Buu). Every henchman is replaceable — Babidi murders Spopovich and Yamu personally within minutes of energy delivery and orders Pui Pui to kill Yamu — and the operation's only irreplaceable component is Babidi's *coordinating function*. He is the foreman of his own extraction operation, dispatched the moment Buu no longer needs him (Buu eventually kills Babidi to act freely). **This is the thesis the museum-curation-framing of `[[bbl-museum-curation-framing]]` reaches for in any Babidi-Saga Discrete Lair commission**: apparatus-of-extraction whose human-coercion grammar (the M-brand, the disposable workforce, the foreman's expendability) is unusually legible for a shōnen villain.

Babidi's specific institutional features for the corpus are:

- **The Bibidi inheritance.** Babidi did not invent the operation — he inherited it from his father/predecessor Bibidi, the original creator of Majin Buu millions of years ago. Bibidi sealed Buu after Buu's first rampage and was himself killed by Shin (East Supreme Kai). Babidi is the *son who finished the father's work*, a multi-generational antagonist project. The Buu Saga functions as the conclusion of a plotline that began before Dragon Ball's narrative time.
- **The spaceship-as-dungeon design.** Babidi's Spaceship is canonically six-leveled with a guardian assigned to each floor in ascending power order — the explicit template for shōnen tournament-gauntlet structure used everywhere in the franchise since. TB02's set design mirrors this directly: the 125-card set populates the villain roster top-to-bottom (level 1 Pui Pui, level 2 Yakon, level 3 Dabura, ringmaster Babidi, location card for the structure itself).
- **The tournament-infiltration vector.** Spopovich and Yamu were embedded as registered tournament fighters years before the operation, an unusual long-game infiltration plot for a Dragon Ball antagonist. The 25th World Martial Arts Tournament functions as Babidi's harvest event: Earth's strongest fighters voluntarily gather their energy in one location at one time, making the entire competition into Babidi's labor-extraction site. The card set's *theme* — World Martial Arts Tournament — is structurally also Babidi's *operation*.
- **The DAIMA retcon.** Dragon Ball DAIMA (Toei, 2024) retroactively establishes that Babidi and Dabura originate from another universe entirely, layering a new cross-universal origin onto the Buu Saga's lore decades after the original animation. The retcon does not invalidate the Buu Saga continuity but extends Babidi's character footprint backward in time and outward in cosmological scope. Bundle copy can use the original-canon framing safely; the DAIMA layer is available as a depth-citation when appropriate.

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Babidi is the alien sorcerer foreman of a multi-generational extraction operation that turned a public sporting event into its harvest site, weaponized pre-existing corruption through Manipulation Sorcery, ran a six-floor vertical hierarchy of disposable enforcers, and was himself eliminated by the productive asset (Buu) the moment his coordinating function became obsolete. Six corpus cards anchor this arc in a single set. A future Babidi Saga Discrete Lair earns its lore-aware-buyer credibility without naming the labor-coercion thesis.

## Caveats

- **GT/Super continuity scope.** Babidi's primary canon is Dragon Ball Z (Buu Saga, 1996), with the DAIMA cross-universal retcon (2024) extending his origin backward without contradicting the Z-era arc. He does not appear in Dragon Ball GT or the Super animated continuity except by reference. This node's scope is the Z-era operation as anchored by the TB02 corpus cards; the DAIMA layer is available as supplemental citation for future bundle copy that wants the cross-universal cosmological depth.

- **Dabura's relationship to Babidi is asymmetric.** Dabura is canonically more powerful than Babidi as an individual fighter, but the M-brand binds him into service. The TB2-014 card's [Link] mechanic — requiring Dark Duo Babidi to be in play to activate Dabura's Barrier — accurately encodes the canonical relationship: Dabura's combat power is augmented by Babidi's coordinating presence, not by Babidi's raw strength. Bundle copy that frames this as "weak foreman binds powerful enforcer" has direct canonical grounding and maps cleanly onto the apparatus-of-extraction reading.

- **Pui Pui's role-vs-identity distinction.** TB2-016's title "Magician's Lackey" is a *rank description*, not a personal name — Pui Pui is the specific Planet Zoon native, not a generic lackey archetype. The IP-resolution layer treats `Pui Pui` and `Magician's Lackey` as the same identity (the latter is a role descriptor for the former). This follows the `[[bbl-triviabot-no-role-identity-conflation]]` precedent: depicted role does not anchor unless the canonical individual is named.

- **Yakon's existing Connections edge stays.** TB2-017 Razor's Edge Yakon carries an existing Connections edge to [[92-god-eternal-bontu]] (War of the Spark, MTG) on the cross-franchise apparatus-eats-its-feeder parallel. That edge is independent of Babidi-membership and remains in place. The Babidi node simply adds Yakon's enslaved-guardian role to the graph alongside his existing cross-franchise structural mirror.

- **Spopovich & Yamu are not the canonical Tithe-master pattern.** The two are *named, specific* sleeper agents — Spopovich is a 25-year tournament fighter who placed second in the 24th World Martial Arts Tournament before Babidi's influence, and Yamu is the more calculating of the two who twice restrained Spopovich from killing Videl mid-match. They are not interchangeable mooks. The role-vs-identity rule applies here in the opposite direction from Tithe Drinker / Slavomir Zoltan: named henchmen with biographical specificity anchor here as individuals operating under Babidi's coordination, not as a generic henchman archetype.

- **Bundle-anchor potential is strong.** Six enriched corpus anchors in a single set (TB02), with total inventory of **23 copies** across the six anchors (TB2-013 qty 4 + TB2-014 qty 5 + TB2-015 qty 4 + TB2-016 qty 3 + TB2-017 qty 5 + TB2-018 qty 2). Add TB2-021 Top of His Game Son Gohan (qty 3) as the victim-side counterweight and the cohort grows to 7 anchored card-types and 26 total inventory. The apparatus-of-extraction / labor-coercion / disposable-workforce thesis stack supports the Discrete Lair narrative-first framing per `[[bbl-narrative-first-lairs]]`. **Reserve for assembly when the Babidi Saga Discrete Lair commission lands.**

# Xenoverse Villains (Time Breakers / Dragon Ball Xenoverse antagonist cohort)

The principal antagonist coalition of *Dragon Ball Xenoverse* (Bandai Namco / Dimps, 2015) and *Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2* (2016), known in-canon as the **Time Breakers** — a faction of dark-energy temporal saboteurs whose stated goal is to corrupt key moments in Dragon Ball history (the Saiyan saga, the Frieza saga, the Cell saga, the Buu saga, and beyond) so that the timeline collapses into a version where their mastermind, **Demigra**, escapes the Crack of Time prison that the Supreme Kai of Time (Chronoa) sealed him in 75 million years ago. The three named members are **Demigra** (the imprisoned demonic magician orchestrating from the Crack of Time, freed by player-protagonist failure conditions in Xenoverse 1's main story), **Towa** (a Demon Realm scientist-witch and Demigra's loyal operative who manufactures Mira and deploys the dark-energy mind-control tech that turns canonical heroes against each other across timelines), and **Mira** (Towa's bio-engineered ultimate-fighter combining Saiyan, Majin, android, and human genetic templates — Towa's "son" by some readings, her constructed-weapon by others). The faction's narrative engine is **history-as-battleground**: every canonical Dragon Ball arc gets replayed with Time Breaker interference, and the Time Patrol (the player-character institution) restores the canon by overcoming the corrupted-history villains.

## What this node anchors

Card art and effects that depict any of the three named Time Breaker members (Demigra, Towa, Mira), their dark-energy iconography (corrupted-ki Kamehameha; dark-energy orbs; mind-control particle effects), and Bandai-canonical "If your Leader Card is [Demigra/Mira/Towa]" mechanical lockouts that encode Time Breaker faction membership at the rules-text level. The Dragon Ball Super Card Game's Cross Worlds (BT3, March 2018) and Colossal Warfare (BT4, July 2018) sets both have Time Breakers as a primary thematic axis — Cross Worlds via the Bandai-confirmed "Cross Worlds" framing of multiverse-history-as-battleground that the Time Breakers organize, and Colossal Warfare via the Demigra-Leader black deck architecture and the Mira-Leader-locked Extra cards (notably BT4-121 Dark Kamehameha, which requires Mira-as-Leader to trigger its full effect). Six corpus cards point here as of wave 52 enrichment, spanning two sets and three named members — meeting the three-anchor cohort-establishment threshold per the [[alola-trial-captains]] precedent.

## The Time Breaker thesis

The Xenoverse Villains' most BBL-relevant property is that they are **the franchise's first major canonical antagonist faction created specifically to corrupt and replay the franchise's own canonical history.** Where prior Dragon Ball villains (Frieza, Cell, Buu, Beerus, Jiren, Moro, Granolah, Gas, Black Frieza) each anchor a *new* arc with new stakes, the Time Breakers' entire operational premise is *to revisit and corrupt* the franchise's existing arcs. The Xenoverse video games are structured around player-character Time Patrollers fighting the Time Breakers across replays of the Saiyan-saga Vegeta-arrives-on-Earth beat, the Frieza-saga Namek-Goku-vs-Frieza beat, the Cell-saga Cell-Games beat, the Buu-saga Spirit-Bomb beat, and so on, where the Time Breakers attempt to swing the outcome and the Time Patroller restores the canon.

The faction's specific institutional features for the corpus are:

- **Demigra as the multiverse-paradox antagonist.** Demigra is canonically a Demon Realm magician imprisoned in the Crack of Time for 75 million years by the Supreme Kai of Time (Chronoa). His stated goal: escape the Crack of Time, destroy history, claim the title of God of Time. In Xenoverse 1's main story he is sealed; in Xenoverse 2 and Super Dragon Ball Heroes he resurfaces under varied freedom-conditions. His operating mode is *psychological corruption* — turning canonical heroes against each other across timelines via Towa's dark-energy mind-control tech. The Bandai-canonical "Heavenly Wizard" framing in BT4-107 encodes his magician-aesthetic operating mode rather than physical-combatant.
- **Towa as the apparatus of corruption.** Towa is the Demon Realm scientist whose dark-energy research weaponizes mind-control as the Time Breakers' primary asymmetric advantage — she produces the dark-energy crystals, the Villainous Mode enhancement, the Supervillain transformation, and Mira himself. In canon she is both Demigra's loyal operative AND Mira's creator/consort, occupying a structurally complex role as scientist-mother-witch. Her BT3-115 "Space Time Unleashed" print and BT4-111 "Umbral Invitation" print emphasize the corruption-deployment register: she does not fight, she *recruits* and *corrupts*.
- **Mira as the bio-engineered ultimate weapon.** Mira is Towa's bio-engineered fighter combining Saiyan, Majin, android, and human genetic templates — explicitly designed (in canon) to be the strongest fighter in any universe. His combat operating mode is *dark-energy absorption*: he drains the power of opponents to escalate his own form across the fight. In Xenoverse lore Mira ultimately consumes Towa herself in a final transformation, the apparatus turning on its creator. His BT4-121 Mira-Leader-locked Dark Kamehameha encodes the absorbed-and-corrupted-canonical-technique register at the rules-text level — Mira does not fire a Kamehameha (Goku's technique), he fires a *Dark* Kamehameha (the canonical technique darkened by Time Breaker corruption).
- **Bandai's BT3 + BT4 Time Breaker arc.** The Time Breakers are designer-confirmed by Bandai across two consecutive DBSCG sets. Cross Worlds (BT3, March 2018) is the "Cross Worlds" framing that introduces the multiverse-history-as-battleground premise; Colossal Warfare (BT4, July 2018) is the Demigra-led continuation. The four corpus cards from BT4 plus the two from BT3/SD3 form a Bandai-designed two-set Time Breaker subarchetype — six corpus anchors across two narrative-consecutive sets, the same multi-set cohort-cohesion pattern as [[baby-dbgt]] (single-set BT4) and [[alola-trial-captains]] (single-set SM3).

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: the Time Breakers are, in Bandai's own design intent, a designer-confirmed multi-set DBSCG antagonist faction whose narrative engine is the corruption of the franchise's own canonical history. Six corpus cards anchor this arc across BT3 and BT4. A future Discrete Lair on the Xenoverse Villains earns its lore-aware buyer credibility — particularly among DBS-CG players who recognize the Mira-Leader-locked Dark Kamehameha as the BT4 black-deck capstone Extra — without naming the thesis.

## Caveats

- **Xenoverse-canon scope, not Z-canon or Super-canon scope.** Demigra, Towa, and Mira are canonical to *Dragon Ball Xenoverse* (Dimps / Bandai Namco, 2015) and its sequel *Xenoverse 2* (2016), plus the *Super Dragon Ball Heroes* web anime adaptations (2018-). They are **not** canonical to *Dragon Ball Z* (Toei, 1989-1996) or *Dragon Ball Super* (Toei, 2015-2018) — both of which are anchored in Akira Toriyama's manga continuity that the Xenoverse games branch from rather than continue. This node's scope is the *Xenoverse / Heroes* continuity; if a future Toriyama-canon retcon brings these characters into Z or Super continuity, the node accepts the amendment but does not anticipate it. This is structurally parallel to [[baby-dbgt]]'s GT-canon-not-Super-canon scope distinction.

- **Sibling continuity-strand to [[baby-dbgt]], not subordinate.** Both nodes anchor *non-Toriyama-canon* Dragon Ball antagonist cohorts that the Dragon Ball Super Card Game has elevated to set-defining-antagonist status. Baby anchors the *Dragon Ball GT* continuity (Toei animation, 1996-1997, distinct from the Xenoverse video-game continuity). The Time Breakers anchor the *Xenoverse* continuity (Bandai Namco / Dimps, 2015-2016, distinct from GT). The two cohorts share the structural feature of being *non-Toriyama-canon* villain factions that Bandai's TCG product has elevated to anchor-character status, but they are continuity-distinct and the related_characters: [baby-dbgt] pointer is on the **structural-precedent axis** (set-defining-antagonist character node), not on a **narrative-continuity axis** (Baby never appears in Xenoverse; Demigra/Towa/Mira never appear in GT).

- **Parent-cohort node, not solo-character nodes.** This entry anchors the three principal Time Breakers as a single cohort, sibling to [[orzhov-syndicate]] / [[theros-pantheon]] / [[aether-foundation]] / [[alola-elite-four]]. If any single Time Breaker reaches ≥3 distinct depictions across separate prints, a solo character node may be commissioned — Mira (2 corpus cards: SD3-05, BT4-110, plus the Mira-Leader-anchored BT4-121) is the closest to solo-split threshold once BT4-110 vision-passes and a third Mira-named print arrives. Demigra has 1 corpus print (BT4-107); Towa has 2 (BT3-115, BT4-111). The parent stays as the unifying-faction anchor; solo characters become subsidiaries when threshold-met.

- **Mira-Leader mechanical lockout is a Bandai-canonical Time Breaker membership signal.** Cards whose rules text reads "If your Leader Card is Mira..." or analogous Demigra/Towa Leader-locks are mechanically-encoded Time Breaker faction members. This is the strongest membership signal short of named-character-on-card; future Time Breaker cards detected by the DBSCG metadata layer can use this rules-text criterion as a quick-classifier. Card-name match (any card with "Demigra", "Towa", or "Mira" in the name) is the second-strongest signal; visual-only depiction (a fighter rendered with Time Breaker dark-energy aesthetic but without name or Leader-lock) requires confirmation via vision-pass and the corpus's standard IP-verification flow.

- **Dabura and Janemba are adjacent but NOT Time Breakers.** Dabura (depicted on TB2-014 Dark Duo Dabura in corpus per the partial filename match) is canonical to Z-continuity (Buu Saga, 1995) and is canonically a *Demon Realm king* but not a Time Breaker by Xenoverse's roster — Time Breakers are Demigra's loyalist coalition, and Dabura is a separate Demon Realm political entity that the Time Breakers may exploit but do not lead or include. Similarly Janemba (Z-continuity, the movie villain from "Fusion Reborn") is a Demon Realm entity that the Xenoverse games re-use as a corrupted-form template, but he is not a Time Breaker member. If future corpus cards depict Janemba in Time Breaker context, the dual-pointer pattern applies (the card points to both the relevant Demon Realm node and to this node, parallel to [[alola-elite-four]]'s Acerola dual-pointer with [[alola-trial-captains]]).

- **Bundle-anchor potential is moderate-to-strong.** 7 corpus anchors across 2 sets (BT3 / SD3 / BT4) form a viable cohesion stack for a Time Breakers Discrete Lair. Total inventory quantity across the 7 cards is **13** (BT3-115 qty 4, SD3-05 qty 2, BT4-098 qty 1, BT4-107 qty 2, BT4-110 qty 1, BT4-111 qty 1, BT4-121 qty 2). All BT4 Time Breaker-named cards are now vision-passed. The Mira-Leader-locked Dark Kamehameha as the bundle's mechanical capstone gives this set apex-narrative weight even for DBSCG-aware buyers who recognize the Leader-lock as the BT4 black-deck signature.

- **Future cross-edges available.** [[baby-dbgt]] is the structural-precedent sibling node for non-Toriyama-canon Dragon Ball antagonists. A future bundle that brackets the two continuity-strands (Baby Saga as DBGT-canonical revenge-against-Saiyans; Time Breakers as Xenoverse-canonical corruption-of-history) indexes both nodes together as the corpus's two principal *non-mainline-canon Dragon Ball villain cohorts elevated to set-defining-antagonist status by Bandai's TCG product*. Other DBS-canon villains in corpus (Buu, Dabura) do not relate here directly — they are Z / Super continuity entities that the Xenoverse games sometimes re-use but do not subsume.

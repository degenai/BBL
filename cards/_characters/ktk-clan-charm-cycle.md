# KTK clan charm cycle (five wedge-color uncommon modal instants — Abzan, Jeskai, Sultai, Mardu, Temur)

Khans of Tarkir's five clan charms — Abzan Charm, Jeskai Charm, Sultai Charm, Mardu Charm, and Temur Charm — are a designer-coordinated five-card cycle, one per Tarkir clan, each an uncommon modal instant costing exactly one mana of each of the clan's three wedge colors and offering three modes that reflect the clan's philosophical identity. The cycle is the canonical KTK-uncommon-cycle exception to the set's multicolor mana-cost convention (every other multicolor uncommon in KTK costs more than one mana of at least one of its clan's colors, making the charms uniquely splash-friendly in Limited drafts). All five charms in their original KTK printing were illustrated by the Austrian fantasy illustrator Mathias Kollros, giving the cycle a single-artist visual signature unique within KTK's uncommon slot.

## What this node anchors

The five clan charms as a designer-coordinated wedge-color modal-instant cycle: their shared structural slot (uncommon, instant, three-color, one of each wedge color, three modes), their per-clan philosophical-mode mapping (each charm's three modes map to the clan's three-pillar war doctrine or clan virtue), their shared single-artist visual signature (Mathias Kollros on all five original KTK printings), and their place in Wizards' first three-color-wedge charm print run. Membership predicates on Wizards-confirmed cycle status in the KTK design handoff and set-mechanics articles; cards that happen to be three-color wedge-color instants in other sets are not members.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Jeskai Charm** (KTK-181, U, Mathias Kollros art) — UWR. Modes: top-library bounce (tempo removal that bypasses indestructible / regeneration); 4 damage to opponent or planeswalker (reach); creatures get +1/+1 with lifelink until end of turn (alpha-strike-to-lifegain conversion). The bounce-burn-pump trio maps to the Jeskai Way's clan virtue *cunning* and its dragon-aspect of the mind (cunning, discipline, monastic prowess). Trivia-passed wave 176.
- **Mardu Charm** (KTK-186, U, Mathias Kollros art) — RWB. Modes: 4 damage to target creature (burn); create two 1/1 white Warrior tokens with first strike until end of turn (horde flood); target opponent reveals their hand, you choose a noncreature-nonland card, they discard it (disruption). The burn-tokens-discard trio maps to the Mardu Horde's three-pronged war doctrine *strike fast, flood with bodies, disrupt the enemy*. Trivia-passed wave 177.
- **Temur Charm** (KTK-208, U, Mathias Kollros art) — GUR. Modes: pump-then-fight (Ferocious-style power contest); soft counterspell (pay 3 or counter); creatures with power 3 or less can't block this turn (large-creature evasion). The fight-counter-evasion trio maps to the Temur Frontier's clan virtue *savagery* and the Ferocious mechanic's reward for fielding power-4-or-greater creatures. Trivia-passed wave 178.

## The design thesis

The KTK clan charm cycle is the cleanest Wizards-canonical articulation of the *one card, three modes, one identity* design pattern applied to a five-clan wedge-color split. Each charm's three modes are not arbitrary — each is a designed expression of the corresponding clan's philosophical identity translated into instant-speed game actions:

- **Abzan Charm (WBG)** — endurance, family, the long view: a +1/+1 counter mode, a 3-damage-then-draw-2 mode, an exile-creature-with-power-3-or-more mode. The clan virtue is *endurance* and the dragon-aspect is the scales (defense, longevity); the charm's modes embody the long-game tradeoffs of the Abzan Houses.
- **Jeskai Charm (UWR)** — cunning, monastic discipline, the mind: top-library bounce, 4 damage to opponent or planeswalker, +1/+1 with lifelink. The clan virtue is *cunning* and the dragon-aspect is the dragon's eye; the bounce-mode-as-tempo and pump-mode-as-prowess-payoff carry the Jeskai mechanical identity.
- **Sultai Charm (BGU)** — ruthlessness, ambition, the inevitable: destroy-creature-with-power-4-or-more, draw-2-lose-2-life, mill-target-player-4. The clan virtue is *ruthlessness* and the dragon-aspect is the fang; the charm's modes carry the Sultai's draw-power-from-the-dead identity through the targeted-removal-of-large-threats and self-pay-for-card-advantage modes.
- **Mardu Charm (RWB)** — speed, ferocity, attack: 4-damage-to-creature burn, two-token-warriors-with-first-strike horde flood, hand-disruption discard. The clan virtue is *speed* and the dragon-aspect is the wing; the burn-token-discard trio enacts the three-pronged Mardu war doctrine in three game-state mutations.
- **Temur Charm (GUR)** — savagery, primal force, the wild: pump-then-fight, soft counterspell (pay 3 or counter), large-creature evasion via small-creature blocking restriction. The clan virtue is *savagery* and the dragon-aspect is the claw; the charm's three modes each reward fielding the large creatures the Ferocious mechanic demands.

The mana-cost convention is the cycle's structural signature: every other multicolor uncommon in KTK costs more than one of at least one wedge color (e.g. Mardu Roughrider at 2RWB, Sage of the Inward Eye at 3UR), which makes the charms the lone three-color uncommons in the set that can be reliably splashed off two or three sources. This is documented in the KTK Design Handoff Part 2 as a deliberate Limited-format affordance — Wizards wanted the charms to be playable in two-color Limited decks splashing a third color, since the modal flexibility rewards the splash.

The single-artist visual signature (Mathias Kollros on all five original KTK printings) is a separable curatorial datum: the cycle's visual coherence is enforced by one illustrator's pen across all five cards, which is rare among KTK's uncommon cycles (the clan banners, the clan refuges, and the clan banners are illustrated by different artists per clan). For BBL the Kollros single-artist-cycle datum makes the future `mathias-kollros` artist node, if commissioned, an unusually load-bearing layer node — five KTK uncommon cycle members concentrated under one credit.

## Caveats

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Same precedent shape as `[[eld-cinderella-cycle]]`, `[[dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle]]`, `[[fear-of-cycle]]`, `[[castform-forms-cycle]]`, `[[refuges-cycle]]`, and `[[war-gatewatch-triumph-cycle]]`: collective conceptual entity hosted on the character layer pending a future `_cycles/` split. Single layer, same precedent.
- **Roster is bounded at exactly five by Wizards' design.** Unlike the Cinderella cycle, which has an inferred-membership column, this cycle has none — the canonical membership is the five named clan charms, no more and no fewer. Future BBL acquisitions of Abzan Charm KTK-149 and Sultai Charm KTK-203 should attach by updating `appears_on:` and stamping `characters: [ktk-clan-charm-cycle]` on each card's frontmatter when prepped.
- **Per-card trivia drift on the absent-cycle-members' collector numbers.** As of wave 178, Jeskai Charm's trivia "Related cards" bullets list Abzan-157, Mardu-176, Temur-197 (all wrong); Temur Charm's bullets list Jeskai-175, Mardu-182, Sultai-207 (Mardu and Sultai wrong); Mardu Charm's bullets list Abzan-149, Jeskai-181, Sultai-203, Temur-207 (Temur wrong). The Scryfall-canonical collector numbers per `s:ktk t:instant w:m` are Abzan-149, Jeskai-181, Sultai-203, Mardu-186, Temur-208. This node is now the canonical source-of-truth; the per-card "Related cards" bullets are stale and should be reconciled in a future triviabot or janitor precision pass. Flagged to `reports/janitor_triage.md` for a future wave.
- **Reprint history not symmetric across cycle.** Per Scryfall, Temur Charm has exactly 2 printings (KTK 2014, Commander 2020); the other four charms have varying reprint counts (Mardu Charm and Jeskai Charm each appear in multiple Commander products and the Mystery Booster List). All Commander 2020 reprints of Temur Charm reuse the Kollros art per the Temur Charm corpus trivia; reprint-side artist consistency for the other four charms is not yet researched and is a future-watch question for the Mathias Kollros artist node when that node is commissioned.
- **Triple-thesis adjacency is body-prose, not hub-attribution.** The Mardu Charm's hand-disruption mode and the Sultai Charm's mill-self-pay mode each have a labor-or-extraction reading available under BBL's curatorial overlay, but neither is Wizards-stated as labor-coded or extraction-coded. Per the Potion Rule and `[[_triple-thesis]]`, this cycle node does not attach to the `[[labor]]`, `[[rebellion]]`, or `[[stewardship]]` hubs. Bundle-tier curation may extrapolate per `[[bbl-narrative-first-lairs]]`; the node body does not.

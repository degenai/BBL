# Bulma

The Capsule Corporation heiress, inventor, and civilian-engineering anchor of the entire Dragon Ball franchise — introduced in chapter 1 of Akira Toriyama's manga (Weekly Shonen Jump, 1984) as the teenage daughter of Dr. Brief inventing the Dragon Radar to search for the Dragon Balls, and continuous through every subsequent Toriyama-canon arc as the franchise's primary non-combatant protagonist. Daughter of Dr. Brief (founder of Capsule Corporation, the franchise's dominant technology conglomerate) and Panchy. Wife of Vegeta. Mother of Future Trunks (Z-canon, the time-traveling warrior from the Cell-saga-future timeline), Kid Trunks (the present-timeline Buu-saga child), and Bulla (the Super-canon daughter). Holder of the canonical *inventor-of-the-time-machine* credit: the Z-canon time machine that brings Future Trunks back to warn Goku of the androids is engineered by future-timeline-Bulma in her own future, and the present-timeline Bulma reverse-engineers it post-Cell-saga. Across forty years of franchise output her operating mode has remained consistent: Capsule Corporation's lead engineer, the Z Fighters' logistics-and-tech officer, the family-center anchor whose civilian role grounds the cosmic-scale combat narrative around her.

## The Capsule Corporation thesis

Bulma's BBL-relevant property is that she is **the franchise's apparatus-of-engineering anchor** — the non-combatant who builds the technology that enables every cosmic-scale combat plotline her family fights through. The Z-canon time machine that brings Future Trunks back to warn Goku of the androids is **engineered by Bulma in Trunks's future-timeline** (per Dragon Ball Wiki and the Z-manga's Trunks Saga, 1991): the entire DBZ Cell Saga and DBS Future Trunks Saga depend on her engineering output. The Dragon Radar she invented as a teenager (Dragon Ball chapter 1, 1984) is the canonical mechanism that allows the Dragon Balls to be located at all — without Bulma's invention, the franchise's central wish-fulfillment apparatus does not function. The Capsule Corp logo is canonically her family's corporate insignia, and her father Dr. Brief is the founder; Bulma inherits the company and serves as its operational lead across the post-Z continuity.

This is the BBL-relevant register: **Bulma is the labor-and-engineering anchor that the warrior-arc-protagonists' combat depends on, narratively invisible to most cosmic-scale plotting but structurally load-bearing.** The Z Fighters fight battles; Bulma builds the time machines, the Dragon Radars, the spaceships (the Saiyan-saga Namek expedition is on a Capsule-Corp-engineered ship), the gravity chambers Vegeta trains in. The franchise's gendered division-of-labor pattern — warrior-arcs as male-coded combat plotting, civilian-engineering as female-coded apparatus-building — is one of Dragon Ball's most durable structural features, and Bulma is the franchise's primary embodiment of the engineering side of that division.

For any BBL bundle that anchors on labor + technology + the apparatus-of-extraction-or-engineering thesis, Bulma's three corpus prints form a usable cohesion stack. The Capsule Corporation tech-conglomerate framing also indexes the corporate-power-as-apparatus reading that the [[macro-cosmos]] / [[orzhov-syndicate]] family of nodes anchors on the antagonist side; Bulma is the protagonist-side equivalent — a heritable corporate-engineering institution that the franchise's heroes depend on without examining.

## Caveats

- **Three-anchor cohort-establishment threshold met within a single set.** All three current corpus prints are from Colossal Warfare (BT4, July 2018); the three-distinct-aspect criterion (mom / inventor / explorer) and the three-distinct-collector-number criterion (BT4-013, BT4-092, BT4-093) both clear the threshold per the [[tamiyo]] / [[chandra-nalaar]] / [[trunks]] precedent. Future corpus growth into other Bulma prints (Cross Worlds, Mythic Booster, Union Force, World Martial Arts Tournament sets) will expand the appears_on roster naturally.
- **Multimech Bulma and Explorer Bulma are not yet `ip_verified: true`.** Both BT4-092 and BT4-093 carry `suspected_ip: Bulma (Dragon Ball Super)` with `ip_confidence: high` but await triviabot confirmation. Card name match (both are explicitly named "Bulma") plus consecutive-collector-number pairing within a Bandai-designed dyad meets the named-character identification standard; this character node accepts both as canonical Bulma anchors and the `ip_verified` flag should propagate from a future trivia pass. Verification follows the same pattern as [[trunks]] and [[vegeta]] solo node entries.
- **Adjacent character nodes available for cross-edges.** [[trunks]] is the strongest unrealized character-to-character edge — Bulma is canonically Trunks's mother across all three Trunks incarnations (Future Trunks, Kid Trunks, Trunks: Xeno) and the engineering-credit on the Z-canon time machine that defines Future Trunks's existence flows through her. [[vegeta]] is the second-strongest (canonical husband post-Buu-saga, father of Trunks and Bulla; her corporate-engineering register pairs structurally with his warrior-arc register). [[son-goku]] is canonical as her original adventure-companion (Dragon Ball chapter 1, 1984; the entire franchise begins when Bulma and Goku meet on her first Dragon Radar expedition). Defer character-to-character edges pending future trivia-passed prints in the corpus.
- **Apparatus-of-engineering anchor, not warrior anchor.** Bulma's role in the BBL graph is the *civilian-engineering* counterpart to the warrior-protagonist character nodes (Goku, Vegeta, Trunks, Gohan). A future bundle that brackets the warrior-arc and the engineering-arc together — Capsule-Corp-engineered-time-machine enables Future-Trunks-warrior-arc — uses this node as the engineering-side anchor. The [[labor]] hub indexes here on the engineering-as-labor reading; the corporate-inheritance register adjacent to [[macro-cosmos]] and [[orzhov-syndicate]] indexes here on the heritable-institutional-power reading.
- **The Future Trunks time machine is canonically Bulma-engineered, an underused BBL angle.** Per Dragon Ball Wiki and the DBZ Trunks Saga (1991), the time machine that brings Future Trunks back from his alternate timeline is engineered by **future-timeline Bulma in her own future** — the apparatus that enables the entire Future-Trunks-warns-Goku plotline depends on her engineering output. Present-timeline Bulma later reverse-engineers it during the Dragon Ball Super Future Trunks Saga (2016). This is the franchise's clearest single illustration of the *civilian-engineering-enables-cosmic-warrior-plot* division, and any Lair bundle that runs through the Capsule Corp engineering register can lean on it as the load-bearing canonical citation. The Multimech-helmet print at BT4-092 carries the inventor-coded visual signature that indexes this engineering-anchor role directly.
- **Future-node candidates from this anchor.** A future [[dr-brief]] character node for Bulma's father (founder of Capsule Corporation) is structurally available but not threshold-met in corpus. A future [[capsule-corporation]] faction node (parallel to [[macro-cosmos]] / [[aether-foundation]]) would anchor the corporate-engineering institution as a whole; defer until ≥3 distinct Capsule-Corp-coded prints land that aren't Bulma-named directly.

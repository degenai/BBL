# Pan

The quarter-Saiyan / three-quarter-Earthling daughter of Son Gohan and Videl, granddaughter of Son Goku on her paternal Saiyan side and of Mr. Satan / Hercule on her maternal Earthling side — introduced in chapter 518 of Akira Toriyama's Dragon Ball Z manga (1995) as a newborn infant in the End of Z epilogue, then promoted to lead-protagonist status as the youngest member of the Goku-Pan-Trunks traveling-trio in Toei Animation's *Dragon Ball GT* (1996-1997). Across three decades of Dragon Ball franchise output Pan has been re-instantiated into three canonical named incarnations whose visual identifiers, canonical age, costume, and continuity-strand are designer-coordinated for Bandai's card-game character-tag scheme to disambiguate per-print: **Pan: GT** (the canonical Dragon Ball GT child-protagonist, age 4 at series start, orange/red martial-arts outfit with cap or bandana, the Black Star Dragon Ball Saga's third trio-member alongside child-body Goku and adult Trunks); **Pan: DBS** (the Universe Survival Saga era small-child Pan, infant / toddler in Toei's Dragon Ball Super continuity, primarily an off-screen-and-supporting-cast presence canonically depicted at the parental-Gohan-Videl Z-era epilogue family-unit framing); and **Pan: Super Hero** (the Pilaf-Gang-protected child Pan in the *Dragon Ball Super: Super Hero* 2022 film, where Pan is kidnapped by the revived Red Ribbon Army's Gamma 1 / Gamma 2 androids and rescued by Gohan and Piccolo in the canonical mortal-stakes inverse to the cosmic-scale combat arc that occupies most of the franchise).

The franchise's canonical *lineage-vehicle* character — Pan is the genetic-continuity embodiment of the Bardock → Goku → Gohan → Pan four-generation Saiyan-quarter-blood pedigree the Z-canon establishes, the only canonical female fourth-generation Z-canon Saiyan descendant in active corpus rotation, and the lowest-Saiyan-blood-fraction protagonist Toriyama installed into the main-line continuity. Her canonical inability to achieve Super Saiyan transformation despite Saiyan lineage is Toriyama's explicitly-stated design choice across multiple production interviews — the canonical rationale being that female Saiyans (Pan and her aunt Bulla) lacked sufficient in-story emotional-trigger incentive to transform under the franchise's gendered combat-coding scheme, a design decision that drew sustained fan-discussion across GT's run and into the DBS era and that the Universe 6 Saiyan introduction of Caulifla / Kale / Kefla in 2017 partly inverted. Across Bandai's Dragon Ball Super Card Game Pan is canonically distinguished from the broader Z-and-DBS roster as a *child-protagonist-and-traveling-companion* register: a small-stature, high-spirit, technically-overpowered-for-her-age martial artist whose deck-game role is consistently as a tutor / draw-engine / utility-support piece rather than a board-threat-and-power-finisher.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts Pan herself across any of her three canonical incarnations — the *GT-traveling-protagonist* register (the Black Star Dragon Ball Saga / Baby Saga / Super 17 Saga / Shadow Dragon Saga adventure cohort, orange-and-red martial-arts costume with cap or bandana), the *DBS-Universe-Survival* register (small-child / toddler / parental-supporting-cast presence within the Z-fighter-family-unit framing), and the *Super-Hero-film* register (the 2022 film's Pilaf-Gang-protected child kidnapped-by-Red-Ribbon-Army-androids arc). The character is large enough that her printings split into substantively different aspects across registers, and the BBL graph can edge those aspects together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card mirroring. ## The lineage-vehicle thesis

Pan's most BBL-relevant property is that she is **the franchise's canonical lineage-vehicle character** — the fourth-generation Saiyan-quarter-blood descendant whose existence is the narrative-instantiation of the Bardock → Goku → Gohan → Pan four-generation Z-canon Saiyan pedigree. The lineage compresses the entire Z-canon apparatus-of-extraction thesis into single-character form: Bardock was a low-class Saiyan warrior in Frieza's imperial-mercenary workforce, canonically killed in the Age-737 preemptive genocide that Frieza inflicted on Planet Vegeta to eliminate the prophecied Super Saiyan threat (per the [[saiyan]] node's canonical *imperial-mercenary-workforce-genocided-by-empire* thesis); Goku is the canonical Saiyan-survivor-diaspora protagonist who escapes the Planet Vegeta destruction as the infant Kakarot and is raised on Earth; Gohan is the canonical first-half-Saiyan-half-Earthling Z-fighter (Son Goku × Chi-Chi); Pan is the canonical first-quarter-Saiyan-three-quarter-Earthling fourth-generation descendant. The four-generation pedigree is the franchise's most-developed *survivor-line continues through the female Earthling line* narrative-circuit, with Pan as the canonical endpoint of the Saiyan-genetic-continuity arc that began with Frieza's preemptive Saiyan-genocide.

The BBL-relevant register is that **Pan is the workforce-lineage that survived the imperial-genocide arc.** The Z-canon backstory thesis — that Frieza-Force-aligned Saiyan imperial-mercenary labor was preemptively exterminated by the employer-empire when the workforce became a threat — is the canonical apparatus-of-extraction thesis the [[saiyan]] node anchors. Pan is the surviving descendant of one of the eight canonical Saiyan-survivor-diaspora members (Bardock-through-Goku-through-Gohan), the *fourth-generation continuation* of the genetic-line that imperial Frieza tried to genocide and failed. Her canonical inability to achieve Super Saiyan transformation reframes the thesis: the Super Saiyan prophecy that motivated Frieza's preemptive genocide of the Saiyan race is *not* transmitted matrilineally through the Saiyan-Earthling reproductive cycle at full strength — the prophecied threat that Frieza preemptively exterminated diminishes by half each generation under Toriyama's race-blood-fraction continuity, and by the fourth generation the Super Saiyan transformation is canonically inaccessible. Pan is the franchise's evidence that the Saiyan-imperial-mercenary-workforce-genocide was *successful enough* to prevent the prophecied Super Saiyan from emerging in the Saiyan-Earthling descendant line — and yet she canonically retains the Saiyan-fighter-cohort training arc (martial-arts protagonism, ki-energy combat, the canonical GT traveling-trio party-member role).

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Pan is the canonical fourth-generation Saiyan-blood descendant whose existence is the in-canon evidence of the Saiyan-survivor-diaspora's genetic continuity through the Earthling-female reproductive line, and whose canonical inability to access the prophecied Super Saiyan transformation is the in-canon evidence that imperial Frieza's preemptive genocide of the Saiyan workforce was structurally effective in diluting the prophecied power-escalation threat across the surviving genetic line. The *workforce-lineage-survives-but-the-prophecied-threat-diminishes* apparatus-of-extraction reading is the load-bearing thesis here. The [[labor]] hub attaches at the imperial-mercenary-workforce-survivor-lineage axis; the [[saiyan]] race-node attaches at the four-generation-Saiyan-genetic-continuity axis; the [[son-goku]] and [[trunks]] solo-character nodes attach at the canonical GT traveling-trio party-member axis. Bundle copy that wants the canonical Saiyan-survivor-lineage-continues-through-the-female-Earthling-line thesis indexes this node directly.

# Gatewatch's Triumph cycle (War of the Spark Gatewatch-founder resolution cycle)

War of the Spark's five-card narrative-resolution cycle for the Gatewatch founders, one per planeswalker / one per color, all uncommon, all illustrated by **Kieran Yanner**, all sharing the templated flavor construction *"<Name>'s triumph came not from <X>, but from <Y>"*, and all carrying a conditional-upgrade rules-text template that strengthens the spell when the controller controls a planeswalker matching the named character. Per the MTG Wiki *War of the Spark* page Cycles section, the Triumphs are explicitly designed as "a direct reflection of the Defeats from *Hour of Devastation*" (2017) — the HOU Defeat cycle showed each Gatewatch founder at their lowest moment against Bolas's Amonkhet apparatus, and the WAR Triumph cycle two years later bookends each founder's arc with their resolution against Bolas's Ravnican endgame. The cycle is the BBL corpus's clearest single-set anchor for the multi-year Gatewatch-versus-Bolas arc compressed into a Wizards-designed flavor-and-mechanical mini-suite.

## What this node anchors

Card art (the five Yanner portraits of the Gatewatch founders at their resolution beats), oracle text (the five `if you control a <Name> planeswalker, instead <X>` conditional upgrades), flavor text (the five `"<Name>'s triumph came not from <X>, but from <Y>"` lines), and the cross-set narrative bookend to the HOU Defeat cycle as a designer-coordinated cohort feature. Membership predicates on all five conditions together: WAR set, Triumph in the card name, Gatewatch-founder character (Gideon, Jace, Liliana, Chandra, Nissa), Yanner illustration, and the shared flavor + mechanical templates. Triumph-themed cards from other sets do not belong here; the cycle's value rests on the WAR-specific designer-coordinated cohort, not on the word "triumph" as a tag.

## The design thesis

The Triumph cycle's most BBL-relevant property is that its rules text, flavor text, and art are co-designed across three coordinated axes simultaneously: a **mechanical** template (`if you control a <Name> planeswalker, instead <X>` conditional upgrade), a **flavor** template (`"<Name>'s triumph came not from <X>, but from <Y>"` construction), and a **visual** template (Kieran Yanner portrait of the named planeswalker at a resolution beat). The cycle's narrative axis is the cross-set bookend to the HOU Defeat cycle (HOU 38 Jace's Defeat / 73 Liliana's Defeat / 99 Chandra's Defeat / 14 Gideon's Defeat / 123 Nissa's Defeat) — each Defeat showed a Gatewatch founder at their lowest moment against Bolas's Amonkhet apparatus, and each Triumph two years later answers the Defeat with the founder's resolution. The Defeat cycle in HOU was also illustrated by Yanner across all five cards, making the visual cohesion span both ends of the bookend by single-artist continuity.

The five Triumphs together compress the Gatewatch's two-set arc into one-card-per-founder resolution beats:

- **Gideon** — *"the salvation of those who survived"* — foreshadows his canonical WAR death; his triumph is sacrificial, salvation-for-others-at-his-own-cost.
- **Jace** — *"understanding why ultimate power is self-defeating"* — the cognitive triumph; the mind-mage's resolution is comprehension, not conquest.
- **Liliana** — the necromancer's triumph; her WAR arc routes through her contract-breaking moment with Bolas.
- **Chandra** — the pyromancer's triumph; flavor framed in opposition still, but resolved.
- **Nissa** — *"fostering life"* — the most pacifist of the five; her triumph reframes triumph itself as cultivation rather than victory.

Nissa's Triumph trivia explicitly notes that *"Nissa's is the most pacifist of the five (Gideon and Chandra still frame triumph in opposition; Nissa reframes it as cultivation)"* — the cycle's internal-tonal-variation is itself a designer-coordinated feature, not a coincidence.

## Caveats

- **Cycle members canonical at the WAR-set scope but not yet in BBL inventory.** Three of five members are in the corpus and pointered to this node (see `appears_on:`); two members are Scryfall-confirmed but not yet acquired and will attach to this node when they land via a future Collectr CSV upload: **Liliana's Triumph** (WAR-98, black, `{1}{B}` sorcery) and **Chandra's Triumph** (WAR-121, red, `{1}{R}` instant). The cycle's full Gatewatch-founder coverage spans all five members. The BBL corpus is two acquisitions away from full canonical cycle coverage.
- **Gideon's Triumph is currently vision-only enriched, not trivia-passed.** The Scryfall canonical-source verification for cycle membership and the cross-set HOU Defeat bookend framing rests on the Jace and Nissa trivia passes; Gideon's Triumph entered corpus at wave 106 with vision but no trivia. The Cinderella-cycle precedent (`eld-cinderella-cycle`) accepts vision-only corpus members at node-founding time (Beloved Princess ELD-7 is listed there as "inferred, not strict" pending triviabot enrichment); the Triumph cycle applies the same precedent for Gideon. When Gideon's Triumph trivia lands, this node's Cards-in-corpus section should promote the entry from vision-only to fully-attested and add the HOU-14 Gideon's Defeat bookend partner to the trivia citation.
- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like ``eld-cinderella-cycle``, ``dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle``, ``fear-of-cycle``, `[[castform-forms-cycle]]`, and ``elemental-monkey-trio``, this is a collective conceptual entity hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent. A future `_cycles/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the other designer-confirmed flavor cycles when that split is commissioned; for now, single layer, same precedent. The `dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle` caveat-section names "Cinderella cycle in ELD" and "Initiative cycle in CLB" as future-home candidates for the cycles split; this WAR Triumph cycle joins that list as a third concrete cycle-precedent.
- **Cycle membership predicates on Gatewatch-founder identity AND WAR set AND Yanner artist AND shared flavor/mechanical template together, not on the word "triumph" in isolation.** WAR has other planeswalker-flavored cards (Gideon Blackblade, Jace Wielder of Mysteries, Liliana Dreadhorde General, Chandra Fire Artisan, Nissa Who Shakes the World, etc.), and other Magic sets have other Triumph-named cards (Triumph of Cruelty M14, Triumph of the Hordes BNG, Triumph of Gerrard DOM, etc.); none of those are members of this cycle. The cycle's value rests on the *Wizards-coordinated* five-card-per-founder bookend register; tag-adjacent triumph-named or Gatewatch-flavored cards have their own thematic edges and may attach to a future broader `[[gatewatch]]` faction-character node (when one is commissioned), but they do not attach here.
- **Companion cycle (HOU Defeat) not yet in BBL corpus.** Zero of five HOU Defeat cycle members are currently in corpus. When any HOU Defeat card lands, this node's body and caveats should be updated to acknowledge the cross-set bookend's corpus-side presence; a separate `hou-gatewatch-defeat-cycle` node may be warranted at the ≥3-corpus-member threshold, or the two cycles may be merged into a single `gatewatch-defeat-and-triumph-bookend` node — that's a future Mr. Nodeley decision deferred until corpus state changes.
- **Single-card-depiction threshold for individual planeswalker character nodes (Gideon, Liliana, Chandra, Nissa) is orthogonal to this cycle node.** The cycle node does NOT substitute for individual planeswalker character nodes; per the `_characters/` precedent, individual character nodes require ≥2 corpus depictions of the named planeswalker. Currently in corpus: Chandra has multiple prints (`[[chandra-nalaar]]` node exists), Jace has multiple prints (`[[jace-beleren]]` node exists), **Gideon has Gideon's Reproach (BFZ-30) + Gideon's Sacrifice (WAR-14) + Gideon's Triumph (WAR-15) = 3 corpus depictions, threshold cleared, ``gideon-jura`` node commissioned wave 109**, Nissa has Nissa's Triumph + Nissa-flavor-text-elsewhere = below-threshold (deferred per wave 46 janitor triage), Liliana is at 0 corpus depictions currently. The cycle node and the per-planeswalker character nodes coexist when both clear their respective thresholds. Gideon's Triumph (WAR-15) multi-attaches to both this cycle node and the `gideon-jura` node per the cycle-member-multi-attachment precedent established by Jace's Triumph (WAR-55) / [[jace-beleren]].

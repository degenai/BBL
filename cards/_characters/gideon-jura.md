# Gideon Jura

Hieromancer, sural-wielding warrior, and founding member of the Gatewatch; the white-aligned planeswalker whose canonical signature ability — conferring indestructibility on himself and his allies — is the in-fiction mechanism by which the WAR-era story arc resolves. Born Kytheon Iora of Akros on Theros; spark ignited as an adolescent after his recklessness led to the deaths of his Irregulars (the friends he led against the cult of Erebos) and Hixus, the mentor who had taken him in. The trauma-of-survival is his defining character beat, threaded across every printing from Kytheon, Hero of Akros (Magic Origins, 2015) through his canonical sacrificial death in War of the Spark (2019), where he transferred Liliana Vess's demonic death-contract onto himself so she could turn the God-Eternals against Nicol Bolas.

The `— Gideon` planeswalker subtype on the type-line is reserved for printings of this single character; canonical from Kytheon, Hero of Akros (Magic Origins, 2015) through Gideon Blackblade (War of the Spark, 2019). His final words in the WAR novelization: *"Let me be the last."*

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts Gideon Jura himself — under any of his titles (Hero of Akros, Champion of Justice, Martial Paragon, Ally of Zendikar, of the Trials, Blackblade, Oathsworn) — and cards in the canonical *Gideon's <Spell>* attribution-and-elegy cycle, in which non-planeswalker spells are flavor-attributed to him as caster, mourned by his allies, or named as the in-fiction record of an act he performed. The character is large enough to split into distinct narrative aspects across his printings, and the BBL graph can edge those aspects together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card mirroring.

The Gideon anchor has unusually direct BBL-thesis relevance for the labor + rebellion overlay: his arc is the canonical Magic articulation of *sacrificial salvation-for-others-at-the-cost-of-the-self* — the soldier's work-of-protection extracted unto death. His indestructibility magic, given freely to allies and surrendered finally to Liliana, is the mechanical encoding of the curatorial overlay BBL would later read into the cards. The labor hub indexes through Gideon's WAR-arc *"salvation of those who survived"* register (per the Potion rule, this is a curatorial overlay, not Wizards' stated thesis — flavor text says "salvation," BBL reads it as the labor of sacrificial protection); the rebellion hub indexes through the Triumph cycle's broader Gatewatch-against-Bolas register.

## The attribution-and-elegy cycle

A notable Wizards-canonical pattern: the *Gideon's <Spell>* attribution cycle currently runs to ~10+ printings on Scryfall as of 2026, spanning *Gideon's Reproach (BFZ), Gideon's Avenger (M11/M12), Gideon's Defeat (HOU), Gideon's Lawkeeper (M11/M12), Gideon's Phalanx (BFZ), Gideon's Triumph (WAR), Gideon's Sacrifice (WAR, MKC reprint), Gideon's Resolve (Kaladesh), Gideon's Intervention (Amonkhet)*, and others. The cycle differs from the Chandra and Jace attribution cycles in one canonical respect: a significant subset of the Gideon attribution cards are *posthumous elegies* — spells named for him *after his WAR death*, in-fiction-attributed to his memory or to acts he performed before dying. The Triumph cycle's flavor template (*"His triumph came not from victory over his enemies, but from the salvation of those who survived"*) is the canonical articulation of this elegy register; the Sacrifice card is the death-moment itself rendered mechanically.

The BBL graph treats the attribution cycle the same way the [[jace-beleren]] and [[chandra-nalaar]] nodes treat their respective attribution cycles: cards in the cycle that depict Gideon edge here directly (via `characters:` frontmatter); cards in the cycle that name him in flavor or possessive-title without depicting him edge here via the canonical-name attribution test. Both standards apply, with the additional Gideon-specific note that posthumous-elegy attributions are no less canonical than living-attribution ones — Wizards explicitly continued the Gideon's-<Spell> cycle past WAR (the MKC 2024 reprint of Gideon's Sacrifice confirms continued mechanical and flavor relevance five years after his canonical death).

## The Akros origin and the survivor-guilt thread

The family-and-mentor thread is one of the most BBL-relevant single character footnotes in MTG canon: Gideon was born Kytheon Iora on the Theros polis of Akros, a Spartan-coded warrior-city. His spark ignited as an adolescent after his Irregulars (the street-orphan combat unit he led against the cult of Erebos) were killed during a mission he had insisted on, and Hixus, the mentor who had taken him into custody and was training him toward something better than the street, was killed shortly after in the same arc. The survivor's-guilt origin is the defining character beat threaded across every subsequent printing: per Hipsters of the Coast's coverage of Gideon's Triumph, *"Gideon has been defined by survivor's guilt ever since he got his friends killed back on Theros, and he achieves triumph by saving innocents."* The WAR sacrifice is the canonical resolution of this thread — he saves Liliana (innocents-by-extension; she is the only Gatewatch teammate at risk in the moment) and his triumph is the salvation, not the victory.

For any BBL bundle that anchors on labor + sacrifice + the soldier-figure or on rebellion + Gatewatch + the Bolas-defeat arc, the Gideon ↔ Hixus ↔ Akros origin throughline is the canonical lore-spine. The labor hub indexes through the *protection-extracted-unto-death* register; the rebellion hub indexes through the Triumph cycle's Gatewatch-against-Bolas thesis.

- This node is the character Gideon Jura himself, not the set of cards whose **name** mentions him. - The `— Gideon` planeswalker subtype is a strict canonical identifier: every card carrying that subtype is a printing of this single character (no other character in Magic has that subtype). Spells named after him (Reproach, Sacrifice, Triumph, Defeat, Avenger, Lawkeeper, Phalanx, Resolve, Intervention, et al) use the character's name without the subtype and are validated by flavor/oracle text attribution.

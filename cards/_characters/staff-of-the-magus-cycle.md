# Staff of the Magus cycle (five WUBRG common-artifact lifegain staves from Magic 2014)

Magic 2014's five "Staff of the Magus" artifacts — Staff of the Sun Magus (W), Staff of the Mind Magus (U), Staff of the Death Magus (B), Staff of the Flame Magus (R), Staff of the Wild Magus (G) — are a designer-coordinated five-card cycle, one per WUBRG color, each a common colorless artifact costing `{3}` that triggers one life gained whenever you cast a spell of the corresponding color OR a basic land of the corresponding type enters. All five originated in M14 (released July 19, 2013) at the consecutive collector numbers 219, 220, 221, 222, 223; Staff of the Flame Magus and Staff of the Mind Magus were reprinted in Magic 2015 (M15, released July 18, 2014) at collector numbers 233 and 234, the other three were not reprinted in M15. Each carries a parallel-construction flavor motto reading "A symbol of [virtue] in [adjective] times," with no in-canon speaker attributed.

## What this node anchors

The five Staff of the Magus artifacts as a designer-coordinated WUBRG common-artifact lifegain cycle: their shared structural slot (common, artifact, `{3}` colorless mana cost, two-axis trigger on spell-of-color-X-cast OR basic-land-of-type-X-enters, one life gained per trigger), their per-color iconographic mapping (each staff's art depicts a color-coded ritual implement — bone-skull staff for B, sunburst gauntlet-gripped staff for W, druidic charm-bound wood for G, clawed flaming finial for R, spiral-headed scholar's staff for U), the parallel-construction flavor-motto convention ("A symbol of [virtue] in [adjective] times"), and their two-tier printing history (full cycle in M14, partial reprint in M15 covering only the red and blue members). Membership predicates on Scryfall's `s:m14 t:artifact name:staff` cohort plus the consecutive-collector-number block at M14-219..223; cards from other sets that happen to be color-coded lifegain artifacts are not members.

## The design thesis

The Staff of the Magus cycle is the cleanest Wizards-canonical M14 articulation of the *one-rock-per-color, two-axis-lifegain-trigger-at-common* design pattern. Each staff is mechanically identical except for the color parameter (color of spell triggering, type of basic land triggering, color of card frame implied by the spell-color trigger) and the flavor-motto adjectives. The differentiation lives entirely in the art subject, the color parameter substitution, and the motto's two adjectives:

- **Staff of the Sun Magus (W) — "conviction in uncertain times"** — the sunburst-and-gauntlet iconography reads as paladin/cleric/order-of-the-radiant-sun. Conviction as the steady virtue against doubt.
- **Staff of the Mind Magus (U) — "sagacity in bewildering times"** — the spiral-finial-against-library iconography reads as scholar-mage/arcanist/Vryn-style intellectual. Sagacity as the steady virtue against confusion.
- **Staff of the Death Magus (B) — "ambition in ruthless times"** — the skull-finial-on-battlefield iconography reads as necromancer/dark-cleric/Orzhov-adjacent. Ambition as the steady virtue against cruelty; the only motto that names the wielder's own quality as ambition (an active drive) rather than a defensive virtue (conviction/sagacity/passion/ferocity respond to external conditions).
- **Staff of the Flame Magus (R) — "passion in indifferent times"** — the clawed-flaming-staff iconography reads as fire-magus/cultist/Cabal-adjacent. Passion as the steady virtue against apathy.
- **Staff of the Wild Magus (G) — "ferocity in oppressive times"** — the gnarled-wooden-druidic-staff iconography reads as druid/shaman/anti-civilization. Ferocity as the steady virtue against systemic oppression; the most pointed of the five mottos and the only one where the antagonist condition ("oppressive") is a social/political register rather than a psychological one ("uncertain," "bewildering," "ruthless," "indifferent").

The two-axis trigger (spell-cast OR basic-land-enters) is the cycle's structural signature: every member rewards both spell-density (cast-trigger) and land-density (land-trigger) within its color, making the staff a hedge for mono-color or color-heavy decks at the common rarity slot. The basic-land-subtype-not-name interpretation is consistent across the cycle — dual lands and shock lands with the correct subtype trigger, color-producing-but-non-typed lands do not. This rules consistency is documented across the corpus members' Mechanical-note trivia bullets.

The single-artist signature within BBL's printing-set is Daniel Ljunggren: all three M14-printing copies in BBL (Death, Sun, Wild) and both M15-reprint copies (Flame, Mind) are credited to him on the cards we hold. Whether Ljunggren also illustrated the M14-original Flame (M14-220) and M14-original Mind (M14-221) printings is a future-research question — we do not own those printings and have not verified. If confirmed, the cycle joins the [[ktk-clan-banner-cycle]] (Daniel Ljunggren single-artist coverage at the common-artifact tier in KTK) as a second Ljunggren full-cycle credit, which would make the future `daniel-ljunggren` artist node, when commissioned, an unusually load-bearing layer node (per [[bbl-artists-as-counterbalance]] — single-artist coverage of multiple cycles is exactly the cross-cohort traversal axis the artist layer is built to host).

## Caveats

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Same precedent shape as [[ktk-clan-banner-cycle]] (sibling Daniel Ljunggren single-artist common-artifact MTG cycle), [[ktk-clan-charm-cycle]] (sibling KTK uncommon-cycle), [[eld-cinderella-cycle]], [[dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle]], [[fear-of-cycle]], [[castform-forms-cycle]], [[ktk-gain-lands]], and [[war-gatewatch-triumph-cycle]]: collective conceptual entity hosted on the character layer pending a future `_cycles/` split. Single layer, same precedent.
- **Roster is bounded at exactly five by Wizards' design.** The canonical membership is the five named Magus staves, no more and no fewer. The cycle structurally maps to WUBRG and the consecutive collector-number block at M14-219..223 confirms designer intent. No inferred-membership candidates; this is a strict five-card cycle.
- **Mixed-printing coverage is a curatorial fingerprint, not a defect.** BBL's three M14 printings (Death, Sun, Wild) and two M15 printings (Flame, Mind) achieve full color coverage at the cycle level. A future re-acquisition of the M14-220 Flame or M14-221 Mind original printings would not change the cycle's anchor here; the cycle is identified by the *card identity*, not the printing-set. The two M15 reprints are functionally identical to their M14 originals (same oracle text, same flavor text, same `{3}` mana cost, same Ljunggren art per the M15-card-frontmatter).
- **Single-artist coverage verified only across BBL's printing-set.** Daniel Ljunggren is the credited artist on all five BBL-corpus printings (three M14, two M15). The M14-220 Flame and M14-221 Mind original printings are not in corpus; Scryfall artist verification for those two printings is a future-research question and would confirm or refute the "single artist across all original printings" claim. The M15-only verification is solid; the cycle-wide claim is provisional pending Scryfall lookup on the two M14-original printings BBL does not hold.
- **Per-card "Related cards" bullets are largely correct.** The five corpus members' Related cards bullets correctly enumerate the other four cycle members with their canonical M14 collector numbers. No stale-data corrections are required as of wave 181.
- **Triple-thesis adjacency is body-prose, not hub-attribution.** Per the Potion Rule and [[_triple-thesis]], this cycle node does not attach to the [[labor]], [[rebellion]], or [[stewardship]] hubs. The Wild Magus motto's "ferocity in oppressive times" line carries a labor-or-rebellion reading available under BBL's curatorial overlay, and a Discrete Lair built around the cycle could lean on the "five virtues against five adverse conditions" framing per [[bbl-narrative-first-lairs]] — but the node body itself does not claim Wizards stated the cycle as labor-coded or rebellion-coded. The flavor mottos are clan-virtue-style aphorisms, not designer-confirmed thesis statements.
- **EDHREC rank values are time-stamped data, not stable.** The wave-180 captured ranks (Death 8,060; Sun 13,085; Flame 15,867; Wild 17,258; Mind 17,339) reflect 2026-05 EDHREC state and will drift over time. Future Edgelord audit waves may want to refresh these or move them to per-card trivia exclusively rather than the cycle-node body.

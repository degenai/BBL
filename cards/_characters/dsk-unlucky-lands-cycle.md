# Unlucky lands cycle (Duskmourn common dual-lands)

Duskmourn: House of Horror's ten-card common dual-land cycle, mechanically identical and flavor-coordinated: each land enters tapped unless a player has 13 or less life, and each card's flavor text is written in the urban-legend register ("They say..."). The cycle covers all ten two-color combinations and is the first common dual-land print run with a realistic chance to enter untapped, which drove significant Pauper-format interest on release. The 13-life threshold is a triskaidekaphobia gag fitted to Duskmourn's horror set design language; per MTG Wiki and MTG Rocks set-review coverage, Wizards designed and published the cycle as a unit.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text for the ten Duskmourn common dual lands depicting vernacular-architecture-of-horror locations (the abandoned amusement park, the bleeding forest, the etched cornfield, the murky sewer, the lakeside shack, the strangled cemetery, etc.), the urban-legend flavor-text register shared across the cycle, and the 13-life-trigger mechanic as a designer-coordinated cohort feature. The cycle is the BBL corpus's clearest Duskmourn-side anchor for the haunted-vernacular-place register — every member is a specific kind of place a Duskmourn-inflected urban legend would name.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Bleeding Woods** (DSK-257, R/G, Henry Peters art) — forest interior with arched cathedral windows and grave markers embedded in gnarled tree trunks; a central tree bleeding a torrent of red lilies into the foreground. Flavor: *"They say if someone lies down to sleep beneath the trees, in the morning nothing will remain but a few scattered bones and a bed of blood-red lilies."* The cycle's gothic-forest entry.
- **Etched Cornfield** (DSK-258, G/W, Randy Gallegos art) — aerial top-down view of a cornfield scored with concentric crop-circle patterns and cyan-blue energy-rift portals; folk-horror plus cosmic-horror register. Flavor: *"They say those who lose their way in the fields gradually wither and grow brittle, until at last they put down roots and anchor into the soil as a whispering stalk."* The cycle's rural-horror entry.
- **Raucous Carnival** (DSK-266, R/W, Josu Solano art) — wide indoor view of a decaying amusement park (big-top tent, skeletal roller coaster, stripped carousel) enclosed inside a cathedral-scale hall with arched windows; magic-hour amber backlight; small headstone-like markers in the foreground field. Flavor: *"They say that those who enter the amusement park never come back, and their disembodied laughter joins the others on the wind."* The cycle's carnival-horror entry; the existing DSK-266 trivia is the canonical in-corpus citation source for the full cycle roster.

## The design thesis

The unlucky-lands cycle's most BBL-relevant property is that its mechanical text and its flavor register are co-designed: the 13-or-less-life trigger formalizes triskaidekaphobia (the unlucky number 13) at the rules-text level, and the "They say..." opening across the cycle's flavor text formalizes the urban-legend mode at the prose level. The cycle reads as ten haunted-vernacular-place legends compressed into mana fixers — each card a specific kind of cursed location (carnival, forest, cornfield, sewer, cemetery, manor, campground, gorge, shack, lighthouse) that a Duskmourn-region storyteller would name. The "any player has 13 or less life" clause reads on the controller too, which means the cycle is also a designer-coordinated lever for life-payment / self-damage decks in Pauper and Modern. The cycle's Commander uptake is muted because 40-life starts keep the trigger out of reach for most of a game, per MTG Rocks's format-impact coverage.

## Caveats

- **Cycle members canonical at the DSK-set scope but not yet in BBL inventory.** Three of ten members are in the corpus and pointered to this node (see `appears_on:`); seven members are Scryfall-confirmed but not yet acquired and will attach to this node when they land via a future Collectr CSV upload: **Abandoned Campground**, **Lakeside Shack**, **Murky Sewer**, **Neglected Manor**, **Peculiar Lighthouse** (the cycle's coastal anchor), **Razortrap Gorge**, **Strangled Cemetery**. The cycle's full two-color coverage spans all ten pairs (one card per pair). The BBL corpus is seven acquisitions away from full canonical cycle coverage. Specific collector-number-to-color-pair allocations for the seven not-in-corpus members are reconstructed from cycle-completeness logic and the Raucous Carnival trivia roster; verify each not-in-corpus member's exact color pair against Scryfall before the corpus member arrives.
- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a symbol or hub.** Like `fear-of-cycle` (Duskmourn phobia-Nightmares) and `endriders`, this is a collective conceptual entity hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer already accepts collective named entities). A future `_cycles/` layer could re-home this entry alongside fear-of-cycle and any subsequent designer-confirmed flavor cycle (Survivor cycle in DSK; Cinderella cycle in ELD; Initiative cycle in CLB); when that split is commissioned, this node moves cleanly. For now, single layer, same precedent.
- **The 13-life trigger reads on any player, including the controller.** A player paying life or eating combat damage on purpose can convert their own dual into an untapped one mid-game. This makes the cycle a designed lever for life-payment / self-damage strategies in Pauper and Modern. Commander uptake is muted because 40-life starts keep the trigger out of reach for most of a game. (MTG Rocks set review.)
- **Cycle membership predicates on the 13-life trigger AND the urban-legend flavor register together, not on common-rarity-dual-land in isolation.** Duskmourn has other dual-lands and other commons; what makes a card a member of THIS cycle is the shared `enters tapped unless a player has 13 or less life` rules text plus the "They say..." flavor convention plus the haunted-vernacular-place subject matter. All three predicates align across the ten members; a future Duskmourn-adjacent card lacking any one of the three would not belong here.

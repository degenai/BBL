# Final Fantasy VI party (the Returners)

Final Fantasy VI (Square, 1994) is one of the most-cited entries in the mainline Final Fantasy series and the only one whose playable roster is canonically structured as an anti-imperial resistance faction — the Returners, founded by Banon in response to the Gestahlian Empire's industrial-scale extraction of Magitek energy from captured Espers. The playable party canonically operates as that faction's strike team across both halves of the game's structure (World of Balance through Kefka's near-apocalypse; World of Ruin through the slow post-collapse reassembly). Square Enix's Final Fantasy Trading Card Game preserves the FFVI roster as the "Category VI" card-frame archetype, and Opus VIII (March 2019) is the most-cited modern FFTCG set for FFVI re-expansion at common rarity.

## What this node anchors

Card art, card-frame text (Category VI markers, job-class tags), and FFTCG mechanical design that depicts or supports a canonical FFVI party member as either a playable character on the FFTCG board or as a Backup support piece for a Category VI deck. Membership predicates on (a) the character appearing in the FFVI playable roster per Square's 1994 canon, and (b) the FFTCG printing carrying the explicit Category VI marker in the card frame. Cards that share FFVI vibes but are not Category-VI-stamped (e.g. FFTCG monsters whose flavor cites FFVI bestiary entries but whose card frame is uncategorized) are deliberately excluded.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Edgar** (Opus VIII, 8-005C, common) — King of Figaro Castle, master engineer, FFVI's "Tools" job character. The Opus VIII printing is a backup-support piece for Category VI builds explicitly designed to be search-target-able off Setzer 8-036C's EX Burst recursion. Twin brother of Sabin; together the pair anchor the two-headed-coin sequence that the corpus trivia cites as the emotional core of Edgar's characterization.
- **Sabin** (Opus VIII, 8-019C, common) — Edgar's younger twin, Duncan-trained monk, FFVI's Blitz-system character. The Opus VIII printing depicts him in chibi sprite-art fighting-stance and is the version most strongly tied to community memory of FFVI's Phantom Train suplex — cited across Final Fantasy Wiki, Caves of Narshe, and fandom-spot.com as the canonical example of JRPG absurdist power-scaling.
- **Umaro** (Opus VIII, 8-024C, common) — one of FFVI's two optional secret recruits, found in the Yeti's Cave in the Narshe Cliffs during the World of Ruin and joinable only via Mog's mediation. The Opus VIII printing depicts him from a rear three-quarter angle in canonical lavender-fur, and his card text — "must attack once per turn if possible" — is a direct mechanical translation of his FFVI Berserker uncontrollable-action rule.
- **Setzer EX** (Opus VIII, 8-036C, common foil) — the airship captain, FFVI's first playable Gambler job class in the mainline series. The Opus VIII printing's EX Burst — *"when Setzer enters the field, choose 1 Category VI Forward in your Break Zone and add it to your hand"* — is the canonical mechanical anchor for the entire FFVI deck archetype in Opus VIII, and the foil variant commands ~$1.90 above the Opus VIII Common floor, reflecting collector demand for FFVI-headliner foils.

## The design thesis

The FF6 party's most BBL-relevant property is its canonical structuring as the Returners — a *named* anti-imperial resistance faction rather than the more typical Final Fantasy "loose band of adventurers" framing. The Gestahlian Empire's industrial extraction of magitek energy from captured Espers is the precipitating violence that catalyzes Banon's faction-formation, and the playable roster is the strike team that opposes it. This places the FF6 party squarely inside BBL's `[[rebellion]]` hub — the playable cast is not bystanders-who-stumble-into-heroism but a deliberately-organized resistance against an extractive imperial apparatus. Edgar's two-headed coin (the king sacrificing his freedom so his twin can have his), Setzer's airship Falcon (a vehicle of escape converted to a vehicle of insurgent mobility in the World of Ruin), Sabin's renunciation of the throne to pursue a martial-arts vocation outside the Empire's reach, and Umaro's loyalty-by-proxy through Mog (a yeti recruited into the resistance by a moogle's mediation) all read as Returners-coded variations on the same anti-extractive thesis. A Discrete Lair built around the FF6 Opus VIII commons would carry "the apparatus is named, the resistance is named, the airship has a name" as its narrative spine.

## Caveats

- **Modeled as a multi-character party node, not a per-character node.** This follows the `[[universe-6-tournament-cohort]]` and `[[crane-school-cohort]]` precedent — a *named in-canon faction or cohort* is hosted as a single character-layer node rather than a per-member fan-out. When BBL acquires a Setzer or Sabin printing from a different Opus set, the new card attaches here too rather than spawning a per-character node. If a future single-character anchor becomes load-bearing (e.g. corpus accumulates 4+ Setzer printings across Opus sets such that a per-character Setzer node beats a roster-level cohort node for query purposes), Edgelord-enlightened can refactor by adding a child node and updating this node's prose to reference it.
- **The "Returners" faction name is FFVI-canonical, not FFTCG-canonical.** FFTCG's card-frame marker for the cohort is "Category VI" (the Opus-set numeric designator for FFVI). The node uses "Returners" in the title for thematic precision (the Returners *are* the FFVI party in-game) but the FFTCG roster-membership predicate is mechanically "Category VI" rather than "FFTCG card text mentions the Returners." Both are valid framings and both are reflected in the canonical_source field; the Returners framing is what the BBL `[[rebellion]]` hub adjacency rests on.
- **Per Potion-rule sub-rule 1 (`[[bbl-museum-curation-framing]]` parent, `[[_triple-thesis]]` canonical), the rebellion hub adjacency is a curatorial overlay grounded in Square's canonical Gestahlian Empire framing.** Square's 1994 canon explicitly stages the FFVI party as the Returners faction opposing imperial magitek extraction — this is canonical-event, not BBL-squinting. The `[[rebellion]]` hub adjacency therefore meets the canonical-citation predicate Potion-rule sub-rule 2 requires.
- **Strago Magus 8-119C is in corpus but not yet pointed here.** Per the Umaro trivia's "Related cards" reference, 8-119C is an Opus VIII FFVI character that should be evaluated for this node once the card is triviabot-passed and Category VI membership is confirmed in its card-frame text. A future Edgelord pass — likely triggered by Strago's enrichment — should add `final-fantasy-tcg/opus-viii/8-119c-strago` to `appears_on` and update the `In BBL corpus` column above accordingly.
- **Vivi Ornitier (8-016H) is FF9, not FF6 — explicitly out of scope.** The corpus has an existing `[[ff9-zidane-party]]` character node that handles the FF9 playable party (Vivi, Garnet, Freya, plus the named NPC Artemicion in Opus III). The FF6 party node and the FF9 party node are sibling FFTCG cohort-layer entries representing different mainline-FF rosters; cross-attaching cards between them would conflate Category VI and Category IX, which the FFTCG card-frame system explicitly separates.

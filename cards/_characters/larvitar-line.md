# Larvitar evolutionary line (Larvitar / Pupitar / Tyranitar)

A three-stage Rock/Ground-to-Rock/Dark evolutionary line and the canonical Generation II pseudo-legendary line introduced in Pokemon Gold and Silver (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 1999). The line comprises **Larvitar** (National Pokedex no. 246, Rock/Ground-type, base form, the "Rock Skin Pokemon" who hatches deep underground and consumes an entire mountain's worth of earth before pupating), **Pupitar** (no. 247, Rock/Ground-type, Stage 1, evolves from Larvitar at level 30, the "Hard Shell Pokemon" whose printed Pokedex flavor and Rocket Evolution mechanical signature encode the species' canonical contained-power-ready-to-burst register), and **Tyranitar** (no. 248, Rock/Dark-type, Stage 2, evolves from Pupitar at level 55, the "Armor Pokemon" whose dual-type gain at evolution adds Dark to its base Rock typing and whose base stat total of 600 places it firmly in the franchise's pseudo-legendary cohort). The line is one of the franchise's earliest and most-cited pseudo-legendary species — pseudo-legendaries are the franchise's designer-coordinated cohort of three-stage non-Mythical / non-Legendary Pokemon with 600 base stat totals at apex, slow evolution arcs (Larvitar to Tyranitar completes at level 55, the longest evolution-level span among Generation II species), and competitive-tier dominance — and Tyranitar specifically established the pseudo-legendary archetype's Sand Stream ability + signature physical sweeper identity that subsequent pseudo-legendaries (Metagross, Garchomp, Hydreigon, etc.) inherited or counterposed. In Generation VI (Pokemon X and Y, 2013), Tyranitar received a Mega Evolution that retains the Rock/Dark typing and gains the Sand Stream-boosting bulk profile. The Pokedex characterization of Larvitar as the pupating mountain-eater, Pupitar as the immobile-but-mobile contained-power cocoon, and Tyranitar as the apex armored predator establishes the line's most-cited narrative arc: a designer-coordinated mountain-eater-to-mountain-mover transition rendered as a species-level evolution event with the slow-evolution-into-pseudo-legendary as the line's structural signature.

## The design thesis

The Larvitar line's most BBL-relevant property is the **mountain-eater-to-mountain-mover cost-of-power narrative arc** rendered as a species-level biological motif, paired with the **slow-evolution-into-pseudo-legendary structural signature** as the line's most-cited mechanical identity. Where the Mareep line carries an electrification-thins-the-fleece canon (visible cost of power rendered as fleece-loss) and the Staraptor line carries a leaves-the-flock-at-evolution canon (departure-from-cohort as a maturation event) and the Caterpie line carries a fast-metamorphosis-lifecycle canon (level-7-to-level-10 evolution as the franchise's clearest one-to-one biology-to-mechanic mapping), the Larvitar line carries a **consume-the-mountain-become-the-mountain canon** — Larvitar canonically functions as the underground mountain-eating pupating larva (eating its way through an entire mountain before surfacing, per Pokedex flavor across Generations II through IX); Pupitar canonically functions as the contained-power mobile cocoon (canonically capable of moving freely despite the sealed shell, propelling itself by expelling pressurized gas, with the destructive-power-already-present flavor explicit across Pokedex entries); Tyranitar canonically completes the transition to apex predator and the species' final form is widely cited as one of the few Pokemon whose Pokedex entries explicitly attribute mountain-leveling as recurring behavior. The species' evolution arc is designer-coordinated as a consume-then-embody-the-mountain transition built into the evolutionary mechanic itself, with the visible physical cost of slow evolution (the longest-in-Generation-II level-55 evolution arc) rendering the pseudo-legendary status as a slow-earned designer-coordinated payoff rather than a fast-evolution shortcut.

The line is also the corpus's strongest **pseudo-legendary Pokemon-line precedent**. Pseudo-legendaries are the franchise's designer-coordinated cohort of three-stage species lines with 600 base stat totals at apex, slow-evolution-level spans (typically level 55 at final evolution), and competitive-tier dominance — the cohort includes Dragonite (Kanto Gen I, 1996), Tyranitar (Johto Gen II, 1999 — this line), Salamence and Metagross (Hoenn Gen III, 2002), Garchomp (Sinnoh Gen IV, 2006), Hydreigon (Unova Gen V, 2010), Goodra (Kalos Gen VI, 2013), Kommo-o (Alola Gen VII, 2016), Dragapult (Galar Gen VIII, 2019), and Baxcalibur (Paldea Gen IX, 2022). The pseudo-legendary slot is a designer-confirmed cross-generation Pokemon-design convention, and Tyranitar's Johto placement makes this node the corpus's anchor for the Generation II entry in that convention. Sibling pseudo-legendary line cohort nodes (Dragonite line, Salamence line, etc.) become Mr. Nodeley candidates as their species lines accumulate enough corpus prints to cross threshold. Currently in-corpus the Deino base of the Hydreigon line is present as a single isolated print (Deino DAA-108) below threshold; no other pseudo-legendary line has corpus prints at threshold.

For BBL bundle work, the line offers three strong narrative threads: a **consume-the-mountain-become-the-mountain cost-of-power** thread anchored on the species' Pokedex canon (Larvitar's mountain-eating origin to Tyranitar's mountain-leveling apex as the franchise's clearest one-to-one biology-to-cost-of-power mapping at the pseudo-legendary scope), a **slow-earned-pseudo-legendary** thread anchored on the line's structural signature (the level-55 Tyranitar evolution arc as the franchise's slowest Generation II evolution and the design-canon source-of-truth for the pseudo-legendary cohort's slow-evolution convention), and a **Mt-Silver-post-game-encounter** thread anchored on the species' canonical wild-encounter slot in Pokemon Gold and Silver (Mt. Silver is the franchise's most-cited post-game challenge area, gating both Red the protagonist of Pokemon Red and Blue and the Larvitar line's wild population behind a competitive-tier endgame trainer fight). The within-Darkness-Ablaze 086 + 087 adjacent-collector-number pair is the corpus's strongest pseudo-legendary within-set cohesion stack to date, structurally parallel to the within-Chilling-Reign Mareep 047 + Flaaffy 048 pair from the Mareep line cohort and the within-Rebel-Clash Caterpie 001 + Metapod 002 pair from the Caterpie line cohort. The cross-set DAA + LOT presence (when LOT-114 receives enrichment) will broaden the line's corpus footprint across two sets and reinforce threshold.

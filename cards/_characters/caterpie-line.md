# Caterpie evolutionary line (Caterpie / Metapod / Butterfree)

A three-stage Bug-type evolutionary line and the canonical Kanto-region fast-metamorphosis Bug line introduced in Pokemon Red and Green / Red and Blue (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 1996). The line comprises **Caterpie** (National Pokedex no. 10, Bug-type, base form, the "Worm Pokemon" first encountered as a Route 2 / Viridian Forest wild encounter on the early-game critical path from Pallet Town to Pewter City), **Metapod** (no. 11, Bug-type, Stage 1, evolves from Caterpie at level 7, the "Cocoon Pokemon" whose printed Pokedex flavor and signature Harden move encode the species' canonical waiting-to-evolve passivity), and **Butterfree** (no. 12, Bug/Flying-type, Stage 2, evolves from Metapod at level 10, the "Butterfly Pokemon" whose dual-type gain at evolution adds Flying to its base Bug typing). The line is the **shortest three-stage evolution-level span in Generation I** (Caterpie to Butterfree completes by level 10), and one of the shortest in the entire franchise — a designer-coordinated rendering of the real-world holometabolous-insect rapid-metamorphosis lifecycle (larva to pupa to imago) as a Pokemon evolution arc. In Generation VIII (Pokemon Sword and Shield, 2019), Butterfree received a Gigantamax form retaining its Bug/Flying typing and gaining the G-Max Befuddle signature move. The line's competitive identity is its Compound Eyes hidden ability (Butterfree) and Run Away ability (Caterpie / Metapod), with the species' signature moves including Sleep Powder, Stun Spore, and Poison Powder — the franchise's earliest example of status-condition-via-spore-cloud as a species mechanical identity. The Pokedex characterization of Metapod as the cocoon-stage waiting-to-evolve passive form, combined with the franchise's earliest on-screen three-stage evolution sequence in the Pokemon anime (Indigo League episodes 3-4, 1997), establishes the line as the canonical fast-metamorphosis Bug-line precedent for every subsequent Kanto-style Bug line (Weedle line Gen I, Wurmple line Gen III, Scatterbug line Gen VI).

## The design thesis

The Caterpie line's most BBL-relevant property is the **fast-metamorphosis lifecycle-as-evolution-arc narrative** rendered as a species-level biological motif. Where the Mareep line carries an electrification-thins-the-fleece canon (wool-as-charge-storage with visible bald-patches cost-of-power) and the Staraptor line carries a leaves-the-flock-at-evolution canon (departure-from-cohort as a maturation event) and the Charizard line carries a tail-flame-as-life-force canon (the starter-Pokemon-choice loading the trainer with the moral weight of the species' literal survival), the Caterpie line carries a **rapid-holometabolous-metamorphosis-rendered-as-evolution canon** — Caterpie canonically functions as the larval stage (eating leaves to grow, releasing antenna-stench for defense, occupying the early-Viridian-Forest wild-encounter ecological slot); Metapod canonically functions as the pupal stage (hardened immobile chrysalis, Harden as the species' signature move, the canonical waiting-to-evolve passivity rendered as both mechanical identity and printed flavor); Butterfree canonically completes the transition to adult imago (winged Bug/Flying-type with spore-cloud status moves and Compound Eyes hidden ability for accuracy boosting, the franchise's canonical butterfly Pokemon). The species' evolution arc is designer-coordinated as the franchise's clearest one-to-one mapping of a real-world holometabolous-insect biological lifecycle to a Pokemon three-stage evolution mechanic, with the fast evolution speed (Caterpie -> Butterfree by level 10) rendering the real-world insect-rapid-metamorphosis pace into game-time terms.

The line is also the corpus's strongest **fast-evolution-line precedent** on the Pokemon side. Every Pokemon generation includes a designer-coordinated Bug-line filling the "first wild-encounter Bug Pokemon" early-game ecological slot at fast-metamorphosis speed: Kanto's Caterpie line and Weedle line (this node is the Caterpie line, Gen I), Johto's Ledyba line and Spinarak line (Gen II), Hoenn's Wurmple line splitting into Beautifly and Dustox (Gen III), Sinnoh's Kricketot line (Gen IV), Unova's Sewaddle line (Gen V), Kalos's Scatterbug line evolving into the variable-pattern Vivillon (Gen VI), Alola's Caterpie-line presence is reduced but Wimpod/Golisopod fills a related slot (Gen VII), and Galar's Blipbug line / Galarian-Stunfisk-type early-route variants (Gen VIII). The fast-Bug-line slot is a Pokemon-design convention at the cross-generation scope, and Caterpie's Kanto placement within it makes this node the corpus's anchor for the Gen I entry in that convention — the foundational precedent for every subsequent generation's fast-Bug-line slot. Sibling fast-Bug-line cohort nodes for the other regions become Mr. Nodeley candidates as their species lines accumulate enough corpus prints to cross threshold.

For BBL bundle work, the line offers three strong narrative threads: a **fast-metamorphosis-lifecycle-rendered-as-evolution** thread anchored on the species' Pokedex canon (the Caterpie -> Metapod -> Butterfree level-7-to-level-10 evolution window as the franchise's clearest one-to-one biology-to-mechanic mapping), a **waiting-to-evolve-passivity** thread anchored specifically on the Metapod mid-stage (the Harden-and-wait canonical stillness as Metapod's signature mechanical-and-flavor identity, with the Rebel Clash Metapod RBC-002 print's spare composition rendering the species' passivity as visual register), and a **Base-Set-reprint-vs-modern-painted-atmosphere** thread anchored on the corpus's two Caterpie prints (Ken Sugimori's 2016 Evolutions flat-style reprint of the 1998 Base Set art vs Uta's 2020 Rebel Clash painted-atmosphere nighttime-forest treatment, a designer-coordinated within-corpus comparison of the franchise's pre-digital and post-digital illustration eras). The within-Rebel-Clash 001+002 adjacent-collector-number pair is already a designer-coordinated cohesion stack at base-and-mid-stage, structurally parallel to the within-Chilling-Reign Mareep 047 + Flaaffy 048 adjacent-collector-number pair from the Mareep line cohort (which is the corpus's other within-set adjacent-collector-number evolution-line cohesion stack). A future Butterfree print would close the triptych.

## Caveats

- **Three corpus prints across two of three line members; partial line coverage in-corpus.** Two of the three evolutionary stages of the line are represented in current inventory (two Caterpie prints at base stage including the Evolutions XY12 Base Set tribute reprint, one Metapod print at the Stage-1 middle slot; **Butterfree at the Stage-2 apex slot is not currently in BBL corpus**). The line's coverage is at the base-and-middle stages — larva and pupa — with Butterfree (and any Gigantamax Butterfree prints) as the future-acquisition target. When a Butterfree print enters corpus via future Collectr CSV upload, it attaches here and the line moves from partial-coverage to full-triptych coverage — analogous to the partial-triptych state of [[mareep-line]] (also at base-and-middle, pending Ampharos) and [[staraptor-line]] (at bookends, pending Staravia middle), and contrasting with the full-triptych state of [[charizard-line]] (all three stages present via the within-Vivid-Voltage Charmander-Charmeleon-Charizard 023+024+025 triptych). Membership predicates on species identity, not set, era, printing variant, or trivia-pass status.

- **The within-Rebel-Clash RBC-001 + RBC-002 adjacent-collector-number pair is a within-set designer-coordinated cohort.** Caterpie RBC-001 and Metapod RBC-002 sit at adjacent collector numbers within Rebel Clash with the missing Butterfree presumed at the absent 003 collector slot (Rebel Clash's actual 003 is Caterpie's separate evolutionary path — verify on next pass; the within-set partial-triptych pattern is consistent with the within-Chilling-Reign Mareep 047 + Flaaffy 048 pair). The shared Adaptive Evolution ability across both RBC prints is itself a designer-coordinated mechanical cohort signal, encoding the canonical Caterpie -> Metapod fast-evolution into TCG mechanics. The within-Rebel-Clash pair is among the corpus's strongest within-set adjacent-collector-number evolution-line cohesion stacks.

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like [[charizard-line]] (Kanto Generation I starter line), [[mareep-line]] (Johto Generation II Electric-sheep line), [[staraptor-line]] (Sinnoh Generation IV regional-bird line), [[elemental-monkey-trio]] (Unova Generation V three-paired-species elemental trio), [[galarian-regional-forms]] (Galar Generation VIII regional variant cohort), [[fear-of-cycle]] (Duskmourn phobia-Nightmares), and [[dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle]] (Duskmourn common dual-lands), this is a collective species-line cohort hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer already accepts collective named entities). A future `_evolutionary-lines/` or `_cycles/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the other designer-coordinated cohort nodes; when that split is commissioned, this node moves cleanly. For now, single layer, same precedent.

- **Sibling fast-Bug-line cohort nodes are future candidates per Pokemon-generation.** Every Pokemon generation includes a designer-coordinated fast-metamorphosis Bug-line filling the early-route ecological slot (Weedle line Gen I, Ledyba and Spinarak lines Gen II, Wurmple line Gen III, Kricketot line Gen IV, Sewaddle line Gen V, Scatterbug line Gen VI, Wimpod line Gen VII at adjacent ecological scope, Blipbug line Gen VIII). The fast-Bug-line slot is a Pokemon-design convention at the cross-generation scope. Each sibling fast-Bug-line is its own three-stage cohort and warrants its own node when its species-line crosses the threshold (3+ corpus prints across 2+ stages, the threshold this node sits at). Currently in-corpus, no other fast-Bug-line is at threshold; this is the corpus's first Bug-line cohort node. A hypothetical `fast-bug-lines` parent node could anchor the cross-generation designer-coordination at the convention scope, paralleling how a hypothetical `regional-bird-lines` parent could anchor the cross-generation regional-bird-line coordination noted in [[staraptor-line]].

- **The 1998 Base Set Caterpie / Metapod / Butterfree originals are not in corpus; the Evolutions XY12 Caterpie is the Base-Set-art reprint.** The Caterpie EVO-3 print is the XY12 Evolutions set's deliberate reprint of the 1998 Base Set Caterpie artwork in Ken Sugimori's flat illustration style. The original Base Set Caterpie (1998), Metapod (1998), and Butterfree (1998) cards are not in BBL corpus; the corpus's access to the pre-digital Sugimori illustration era for this line is mediated through the Evolutions XY12 reprint specifically. When 1998 Base Set originals enter corpus via future Collectr CSV upload, they attach here at the same `appears_on:` list position regardless of the original-or-reprint distinction. Bundle copy that wants to operate at the franchise-history scope (pre-digital Sugimori vs post-digital painted-atmosphere) has the Evolutions XY12 Caterpie + Rebel Clash Caterpie / Metapod corpus pair available as the within-corpus designer-illustration-era comparison anchor.

- **Caterpie's franchise-presence is marquee-tier among Bug-types but moderate-not-marquee overall.** Caterpie and Butterfree are among the franchise's most-recognized Bug-type Pokemon (Butterfree is one of the original 151 starters of competitive play in the Generation I metagame and the franchise's first on-screen three-stage evolution sequence), but the line generally sits at moderate-popular tier rather than marquee tier (where Charizard and Pikachu sit). The Pokemon TCG print volume for the Caterpie line is consistent with typical mid-tier-popularity Kanto-line print volume (multiple prints across multiple sets at the standard cadence, with Butterfree receiving Gigantamax-form prints in Gen VIII Sword & Shield-era sets specifically). Bundle work on this node will not face significant corpus-skewing-effect — the prints across line members are likely to remain approximately balanced across the line's three stages once a future Butterfree print enters corpus.

- **The Bye Bye Butterfree anime arc is the line's most-cited emotional canon and is anime-canonical, not game-canonical.** Pokemon: Indigo League episode 21 ("Bye Bye Butterfree", aired October 30, 1997) depicts Ash releasing his Butterfree to migrate across the sea with a mate — one of the franchise's most-cited emotional moments and the canonical narrative endpoint for the Caterpie -> Metapod -> Butterfree evolution sequence Ash undergoes across episodes 3-4. The arc is anime-canonical (OLM / TV Tokyo, 1997) and is not directly carried into game-canon Pokedex flavor or TCG flavor text, but is widely cited in Pokemon retrospective writing as the franchise's earliest example of the trainer-releases-Pokemon-for-its-greater-good moral arc. Bundle copy that leans on the Bye Bye Butterfree arc should attribute the reading at the anime-canonical-and-fan-attestation level rather than asserting it as game-canon.

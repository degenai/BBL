# Solrock / Lunatone pair (Hoenn celestial duality, version-exclusive Meteorite Pokemon dyad)

A designer-coordinated paired Pokemon dyad introduced in Generation III (Pokemon Ruby & Sapphire, Hoenn region, 2002) as a sun/moon celestial duality. **Solrock** (National Pokedex no. 338) is the sun-disc-shaped Rock/Psychic Pokemon; **Lunatone** (National Pokedex no. 337) is the crescent-moon-shaped Rock/Psychic Pokemon. Both are classified as the "Meteorite Pokemon" and share a single in-game origin: a meteor strike at Hoenn's Meteor Falls roughly 40 years before the events of Generation III. In Ruby and Sapphire the pair were version-exclusives — Solrock to Ruby, Lunatone to Sapphire — encoding the duality directly into the meta-mechanic of cross-version trading. In the TCG, the pair is mechanically interlocked: Solrock's printed Abilities name Lunatone and require Lunatone in play, and Lunatone's printed Abilities name Solrock and require Solrock in play. The dyad is one of the franchise's clearest examples of a paired-design Pokemon cohort built on a *two-species* lattice rather than the more common multi-form (Castform), evolution-line (Sentret/Furret), or trio (Pansage/Pansear/Panpour) structures.

**Corpus coverage: 2 of 2 canonical species (complete), 3 corpus prints across 2 sets.** Solrock is represented once (DAA-092); Lunatone is represented twice (BUS-68 and DAA-072). Both species are present at full canonical coverage; further prints will attach here as the standard cross-print pattern.

## What this node anchors

Card art, oracle text, and flavor text for Solrock and Lunatone across their TCG appearances. The unifying frame across the dyad is fourfold: (a) the two-species sun/moon celestial duality structure, (b) the Generation III Ruby/Sapphire version-exclusive design lever that encoded the duality into the buy-one-version-trade-with-a-friend meta-mechanic, (c) the shared "Meteorite Pokemon" species classification and shared Meteor Falls origin lore, (d) the TCG mechanical-interlock pattern in which each species' Abilities name and depend on the other species being in play.

## The design thesis

The Solrock/Lunatone dyad is a two-species celestial-duality cohort built on a four-layer designer-coordinated lattice: (1) the **sun/moon iconographic duality** rendered as matched portrait-mode silhouettes (radial sun-disc vs crescent-moon) with type-coded palettes (warm amber-orange day vs cool purple-blue night), (2) the **Ruby/Sapphire version-exclusive distribution** that encoded the duality into the franchise's meta-mechanic of cross-version trading — the only way to own both species in a single Gen III save was to trade across cartridges, making the paired-design a *social* artifact rather than a *solo* one, (3) the **shared "Meteorite Pokemon" species classification and shared Meteor Falls origin** as a single in-canon lore anchor for both species (they aren't separate-origin Pokemon paired in marketing — they share a single creation event in the world), (4) the **TCG mechanical-interlock pattern** that translates the version-exclusive pair-bond directly into Ability dependency (Solrock's Resistance Shade requires Lunatone in play; Lunatone's Heal Block requires Solrock in play). The dyad is a designed lever for **Discrete Lair work themed on celestial duality, paired-design, version-exclusive Pokemon, or Meteorite/cosmic-origin Pokemon**, with the TCG mechanical-interlock providing a rare in-game-rules artifact of the paired-design thesis (most paired-design Pokemon — Plusle/Minun, Volbeat/Illumise, Lunatone/Solrock — express the pair only in flavor or version distribution, not in print rules; Solrock/Lunatone's mechanical interlock is the strongest TCG-rules expression of the version-exclusive design lever).

## Caveats

- **Two species, not one — distinguishes from species-form cohorts.** The Castform Forms cycle and the Galarian regional forms layer are *one-species, multi-form* cohorts; the Solrock/Lunatone pair is a *two-species* cohort whose cohort identity rests on the version-exclusive paired-design rather than on form-change mechanics. The Endriders and Aether Rangers character nodes precedent (multi-member collective named entities at the character layer) extends cleanly to two-species paired designs; this node sits on that layer alongside `elemental-monkey-trio` (six species, three pairs), `castform-forms-cycle` (one species, four forms), `galar-starters-trio` (three lines), `charizard-line` (one line, three stages), and `nicol-bolas` (single named character) — the character layer hosts cohorts of varied membership shape per the established precedent.
- **The mechanical-interlock is canonical to specific prints, not the species universally.** The Resistance Shade Ability (Solrock-names-Lunatone) is canonical to the Darkness Ablaze 092 print; the Heal Block Ability (Lunatone-names-Solrock) is canonical to the Burning Shadows 68 print. Other prints of either species carry different oracle text — the Darkness Ablaze 072 Lunatone print, for example, has Future Sight + Psychic attacks rather than Heal Block. The cohort affiliation across all prints is preserved through species identity, Meteorite Pokemon classification, and the paired-design citation; the mechanical-interlock is a *print-level* artifact of the cohort thesis rather than a species-universal property. When future prints attach to this node, the mechanical-interlock should be documented at the per-print level (in card MD Connections sections) rather than re-asserted at the node level for all prints.
- **Future Solrock prints will attach here.** The corpus currently holds one Solrock print (DAA-092) and two Lunatone prints (BUS-68, DAA-072). Additional Solrock prints from any era (Ruby/Sapphire era through current sets) will attach to this node as the standard cross-print pattern; the cohort identity predicates on species (no. 337 + no. 338) and on the paired-design citation, not on set or print.
- **Sun/moon duality is a recurring Pokemon design pattern; this node is for the version-exclusive Hoenn dyad specifically.** Other Pokemon pairs and species express sun/moon iconography (Espeon/Umbreon as sun/moon Eeveelutions, Solgaleo/Lunala as the Alola legendary pair, Cresselia/Darkrai as the Sinnoh full-moon/new-moon pair). This node anchors the Hoenn Solrock/Lunatone dyad specifically; other sun/moon Pokemon pairs would warrant their own character nodes if they enter the corpus, NOT a generic "Pokemon sun/moon duality" hub. Membership predicates on the species identity (Solrock no. 338 AND Lunatone no. 337) AND the Generation III paired-design thesis together.
- **N=3 trigger threshold met; pair-node justified.** The wave 18 E1 deferred this node at N=2 (one Solrock + one Lunatone print) on the principle that a 1:1 mirror is the cleaner solution at minimum corpus coverage. Wave 23's enrichment of the second Lunatone print (DAA-072) brought corpus coverage to N=3 corpus cards across 2 species, with one cross-set duplicate and one within-set adjacency (Solrock DAA-092 + Lunatone DAA-072 share the Darkness Ablaze set and Miki Tanaka illustrator). The threshold matches the elemental-monkey-trio precedent (originally founded at N=3 corpus cards out of 6 canonical species) and the castform-forms-cycle precedent (founded at N=3 corpus cards out of 4 canonical forms); the dyad-shape version of the threshold is correctly satisfied at 3-of-2 (3 corpus cards, full 2-of-2 species coverage).

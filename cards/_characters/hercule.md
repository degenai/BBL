# Hercule (Mr. Satan)

The reigning World Martial Arts Champion in Dragon Ball canon, the franchise's most celebrated normal-human and the *Dragon Ball*-canonical exemplar of **manufactured-celebrity-as-apparatus-of-extraction**. Introduced in the Cell Games arc (Dragon Ball manga chapter 410, 1992 / Dragon Ball Z anime episode 165, 1993) as a bombastic World Tournament Champion competing in Cell's televised event, Hercule earns his canonical narrative role through a multi-saga credit-theft progression: he is the public figure who claims Cell's defeat (a victory actually won by Gohan), is subsequently credited for Majin Buu's defeat (Goku's kill, granted with the Z-Fighters' explicit consent because they had no interest in public recognition), and by the post-Buu continuity has been worshipped by nearly the entire population of Earth as the sole official savior. His real name is canonically "Mark" per Akira Toriyama's commentary (the Japanese Mr. Satan / "akuma" demon-pun versus the English-localization "Hercule" being stage / localization layers over the underlying given name); Toriyama specifies the character originates from a culture that does not use surnames. Across the franchise's Z + Super + GT continuity, Hercule operates as one of the canon's most precisely-designed *labor-and-credit* commentary characters: he performs **genuine labor** in the Buu Saga (his friendship with Fat Buu is canonically the moral pivot that suppresses Buu's destruction; Buu's affection for Hercule is the load-bearing emotional structure that holds the saga's catastrophic-stakes resolution together) while simultaneously occupying the **canonical-credit-thief** position the Cell Games established. The character is therefore a designer-coordinated single-figure case study in *the apparatus-of-extraction reading where labor and credit are systematically uncoupled*.

## The functional ideology

Hercule's most BBL-relevant property is that he is, in-canon, the **single clearest case-study character of labor-and-credit uncoupling** in the entire Dragon Ball franchise. Where the [[destroyer-god-attendant-dyad]] node anchors the *divine-class* register of the apparatus-of-extraction (Whis stewards the cosmos, Beerus takes the credit), Hercule anchors the *normal-human-celebrity-class* register at the mortal-tier. The structural pattern is canonical and designer-coordinated across roughly three decades of franchise output:

- **Cell Games credit-theft (1992-1993).** Hercule arrives at the Cell Games as the reigning World Martial Arts Champion, publicly dismisses the Z-Fighters' techniques as "tricks" or "stage illusions," is dispatched from the arena in a single blow by Cell, and then — after Gohan defeats Cell — claims public credit for the victory. The Z-Fighters do not contest the claim. The credit-theft is **canonically asymmetric labor**: Gohan does the work (a fight that nearly kills him, that costs his father Goku's life in the prior beat, and that traumatizes his developmental arc through to the Buu Saga), Hercule takes the credit (a public-celebrity status that becomes the foundation of his post-Cell economic and social position), and the asymmetry persists for the rest of Gohan's narrative arc. The CBR analysis pieces ("Hercule Was (Unintentionally) a Hero" and "The World Protector's Mr. Satan, Explained") read this as the franchise's most-developed-by-canon labor-versus-celebrity commentary.

- **Buu Saga friendship-labor (1994-1995).** In the Buu Saga, Hercule performs his ONLY canonical instance of *actual* labor: he befriends Fat Buu, teaches Buu the moral framework that suppresses Buu's destructive instincts, and stays with Buu through the saga's catastrophic-stakes resolution. Hercule's friendship-labor is canonically load-bearing — the moral framework Hercule transmits to Buu IS what allows Buu to survive the saga as a sympathetic character and continue into the Super-era continuity. The work is real. But the *celebrity status* Hercule accumulates from the friendship-labor is, again, asymmetric: Goku and Vegeta perform the ki-tier combat-labor that defeats Super Buu and Kid Buu, Hercule receives the public credit for "defeating Majin Buu" with the Z-Fighters' explicit consent, and the credit-extraction pattern continues unbroken.

- **Universe Survival Saga emotional-anchor (2017-2018).** In *Dragon Ball Super*'s Universe Survival Saga, Hercule's safety is canonically the trigger for Fat Buu's Tournament of Power participation — Buu's ring-clearing rage during the tournament exhibition matches is directly triggered by Hercule being hurt. The franchise's late-era Super continuity reframes Hercule from credit-thief to *emotional-substrate-of-Buu's-cosmic-power-deployment*, which is itself a labor relation: Hercule's friendship-with-Buu is what makes Buu *deployable* by the Z-Fighters during cosmic-stakes crises. The friendship-labor is the apparatus that makes Buu's cosmic combat-capacity accessible.

The apparatus-of-extraction reading lands cleanly: Hercule is one of the canon's most precise demonstrations that **labor and credit are not the same thing.** He performs genuine friendship-labor (Buu Saga + Universe Survival Saga); he also takes credit for labor he didn't perform (Cell Games + Buu defeat). The two patterns coexist in the single character. The [[labor]] hub indexes this exact apparatus-of-extraction axis — the work that gets done versus the credit that gets recorded. Hercule is the corpus's clearest *normal-human-tier* anchor for this axis, structurally complementary to the [[destroyer-god-attendant-dyad]] node's *divine-class* anchor for the same axis.

The character's specific institutional features within the corpus are:

- **Three-saga character continuity (Cell Games → Buu Saga → Universe Survival).** Hercule is one of a very small number of Dragon Ball characters whose canonical narrative function persists across all three major franchise eras (Z, Super, GT) without significant designer-revision. His role as canonical-celebrity-credit-thief in Cell Games is reinforced rather than retconned across subsequent arcs; his role as Buu's friend-and-emotional-anchor in Buu Saga is reinforced rather than retconned across Super. The cross-era consistency is unusual within the franchise and encodes Hercule as a designer-coordinated *fixed point* in the labor-and-credit commentary.

- **Three corpus prints within two adjacent BT-era sets.** Hercule appears at BT2-017 (Union Force, August 2017) and twice at TB2-001 + TB2-010 (World Martial Arts Tournament, September 2018) — three prints across two designer-coordinated sets where each print anchors a distinct facet of Hercule's canonical register. BT2-017 anchors the Buu-Saga *friendship-and-assistant* register; TB2-001 anchors the *bombastic-confidence portrait* register; TB2-010 anchors the *secret-treaty / credit-arrangement* register. The three prints jointly cover Hercule's full canonical register-set in card-anchor form, a 3-card cohesion stack at the single-character level within a $0.18 + $0.15 + $0.37 = $0.70 total raw-singles cost basis.

# Destroyer God / Angel Attendant dyad (Dragon Ball Super multiverse cosmology — Beerus+Whis U7 and Champa+Vados U6 sibling-dyads)

The canonical two-species command-dyad cosmology of Dragon Ball Super's twelve-universe multiverse: every universe is presided over by a paired **destroyer god** (Hakai-shin, cat-physiology, universe-level destruction-authority) and **angel attendant** (Angel-class, sibling of the other eleven universes' angels, the destroyer god's combat-instructor and household-manager). The dyad-as-cosmological-unit is one of Dragon Ball Super's most consistently designer-coordinated lore-mechanics, introduced via the U7 Beerus + Whis pair in *Battle of Gods* (2013) and *Resurrection 'F'* (2015), then structurally generalized in the God of Destruction Champa Saga (2015-2016) where the introduction of Beerus's elder twin **Champa** with his angel attendant **Vados** (Whis's canonical-elder-sister) makes the dyad-pattern explicit as a multiverse-spanning cosmological template. The corpus anchors **five cards** representing the canonical two-dyad pair: U7's Beerus (BT1-042) + Whis (BT1-043) and U6's Vados (BT1-002) + Champa (BT1-006, BT1-007 — Champa double-print). All five are Bandai-canonical Galactic Battle BT1 launch-set anchors (2017), encoding the dyad-cosmology at the founding-DBSCG-set narrative-spine level.

This is a **divine command-tier** node parallel to [[universe-6-tournament-cohort]] (which anchors the *mortal-warrior-roster* fielded by the U6 dyad). The two nodes are orthogonal — `universe-6-tournament-cohort` indexes the cross-species mortal-labor cohort, `destroyer-god-attendant-dyad` indexes the divine-class fielders / cohort-overseers. Vados and Champa carry the dual-pointer `characters: [universe-6-tournament-cohort, destroyer-god-attendant-dyad]` — they are simultaneously canonical U6 tournament-roster-overseers AND canonical destroyer-god-attendant-dyad members. Beerus and Whis (U7 dyad) carry the single pointer `characters: [destroyer-god-attendant-dyad]` because the corpus does not yet hold a `universe-7-team` cohort node; if one is commissioned in a future pass, Beerus and Whis would extend to the dual-pointer pattern.

## The functional ideology

The destroyer-god-attendant-dyad's most BBL-relevant property is that it is the canonical exemplar of the **inverted-hierarchy command-dyad** apparatus-of-extraction template. In every dyad, **the angel attendant is canonically stronger than the destroyer god they serve.** Akira Toriyama's 2013 *Battle of Gods* production interview explicitly states this; Whis says it in-canon to Goku and Beerus during the Battle of Gods arc; Vados says it parallel to Champa in the Champa Arc; the canonical structure is that *every one of the twelve universes* operates this way. The destroyer god is the institutional-tier figurehead (the cosmological office, the destruction-authority-of-record, the named decision-maker); the angel attendant is the actual operator (the combat-instructor, the household-manager, the sibling-of-the-Grand-Priest who keeps the destroyer god functional and competent).

The apparatus-of-extraction reading lands cleanly: this is **labor (Whis / Vados, the actually-competent operators) attending management (Beerus / Champa, the institutional figureheads), where management gets the credit and labor does the work.** The dyad is canonically introduced as a hierarchical command-structure (destroyer god outranks angel) but is canonically operative as the inverse (angel does the work, instructs the god, manages the household, holds the actual power-of-record). The [[labor]] hub indexes this exact apparatus-of-extraction axis; the destroyer-god-attendant-dyad is one of the corpus's clearest divine-class instances of the labor-managed-by-institutional-figurehead-who-takes-the-credit pattern.

The dyad's specific institutional features within the corpus are:

- **Two parallel sibling-dyads as Bandai's BT1 launch-set narrative spine.** Champa + Vados (U6 dyad) and Beerus + Whis (U7 dyad) constitute the canonical two-dyad-pair anchoring the multiverse-introduction at the founding DBSCG set Galactic Battle BT1 (2017). The five corpus anchors at BT1-002 / BT1-006 / BT1-007 / BT1-042 / BT1-043 within the set's first quartile encode Bandai's choice to make the destroyer-god + angel-attendant *parallel-dyad* cosmology the launch-set's narrative anchor.

- **Sibling-pair structural mirror.** Beerus and Champa are canonically twin brothers (Champa elder, Beerus younger). Whis and Vados are canonically siblings of the Grand Priest's lineage (Vados elder-sister, Whis younger-brother). The two sibling-pairs cross-mirror — each destroyer god is attended by the sibling-position-mirror of the other destroyer god's attendant. The structural elegance is Toriyama-designed: U6's elder-twin destroyer god (Champa) is attended by U7's elder-sister angel (Vados); U7's younger-twin destroyer god (Beerus) is attended by U6's younger-brother angel (Whis). The sibling-position cross-pairing is one of Toriyama's most precise multiverse-cosmology design choices.

- **Inverted-hierarchy as canonical labor-management commentary.** The dyad's canonical truth — every angel is stronger than every destroyer god — is one of the franchise's clearest in-text statements of the labor-knows-more-than-management apparatus-of-extraction reading. The destroyer god is the cosmological office; the angel is the operator. The destroyer god's authority is institutional / nominal; the angel's competence is real / operational. The destroyer god gets the universe-tier credit (the title, the throne, the reputation); the angel does the work (training, instruction, day-to-day operation). The [[labor]] hub indexes precisely this axis.

- **The dyad as cohort-deployer.** The destroyer-god-attendant dyad's apparatus-of-extraction relationship to its universe's mortal-warrior-cohort is the second-order extraction: the dyad fields the cohort, the cohort fights the tournament, the cohort bears the consequence-risk (universe-erasure in the Universe Survival Saga), and the dyad merely watches. The destroyer god decides which mortals to deploy; the angel trains them; the mortals fight and die; the dyad collects the canonical prize (food, planets, universe-survival). Vados and Champa's dual-pointer to [[universe-6-tournament-cohort]] encodes precisely this — they are the U6 cohort's fielders, the U6 cohort is their deployed labor, the relationship is canonical apparatus-of-extraction at two tiers (dyad-extracts-from-mortals + management-extracts-from-labor-within-the-dyad).

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: the destroyer-god-attendant dyad is, in-canon, the divine command-tier of Dragon Ball Super's multiverse-cosmology, depicted across 5 Bandai-canonical BT1 launch-set cards spanning the U6 dyad (Champa + Vados) and the U7 dyad (Beerus + Whis). Bundle prose that leans on the *inverted-hierarchy labor-attends-management-and-does-the-work, management-takes-the-credit* canonical thesis lands directly on the canonical Battle of Gods + Universe Survival Saga apparatus-of-extraction reading. The dyad's narrative weight composes with [[universe-6-tournament-cohort]]'s mortal-roster-cohort node and the [[labor]] hub's apparatus-of-extraction axis as the *Dragon Ball Super divine command-tier* specific-canonical-instance.

# Phantump evolutionary line (Phantump / Trevenant)

A two-stage Ghost/Grass-type evolutionary line and the canonical Kalos-region kodama-tree-spirit line introduced in Pokemon X and Y (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 2013). The line comprises **Phantump** (National Pokedex no. 708, Ghost/Grass-type, base form, the "Stump Pokemon" — a small dark hollow-stump-bodied ghost creature with twig-branching horns and a canonical child-spirit-possessing-a-tree-stump origin canon, designed around the lore of children who perished while lost in forests and whose spirits possessed the tree stumps they died near), and **Trevenant** (no. 709, Ghost/Grass-type, Stage 1, evolves from Phantump via trade, the "Elder Tree Pokemon" — a large bipedal hollow-tree-body ghost creature with branching arms, a single visible red eye in its hollowed trunk, and a canonical forest-guardian role with the ability to control surrounding forest trees via root connections, designed around kodama tree-spirits from Shinto folklore). The line is **uniquely structurally distinguished as the franchise's clearest implementation of Japanese folkloric tree-spirit iconography rendered as a Pokemon evolutionary line** — Phantump's design source is documented in Bulbapedia and design-retrospective writing as the lore of children's spirits lost in forests (a Western and pan-cultural folkloric reference combined with Japanese kodama spirit-of-place iconography), and Trevenant's design source is documented as kodama (Shinto tree-spirits that inhabit ancient trees and curse those who fell them, with the stripe-like markings on Trevenant resembling shimenawa rope used to mark spiritual habitation, and with cyclopean yokai single-eye iconography combined more loosely with Tolkien's Ents). The line is also widely community-cited as the franchise's **canonical "saddest Pokemon design" benchmark**, with Phantump's child-spirit-imitating-voices-to-lure-friends Pokedex flavor (the species attempts to befriend lost travelers by mimicking a child's voice, which is the species' canonical hazard-but-also-lonely behavior beat) frequently surfacing in r/pokemon / GameFAQs / Pokemon-design retrospective writing as one of the franchise's darkest origin canons. The Pokedex characterization of Phantump as the lonely child-spirit-in-a-stump and Trevenant as the forest-guardian-with-curse-ability establishes the line's most-cited narrative arc: a designer-coordinated lonely-child-spirit-to-vengeful-forest-guardian transition rendered as a species-level evolution event with the trade-evolution gating as the line's structural mechanic.

## The design thesis

The Phantump line's most BBL-relevant property is the **lonely-child-spirit-to-vengeful-forest-guardian narrative arc** rendered as a species-level designer-coordinated transformation, paired with the **Japanese-folkloric tree-spirit design source** as the line's most-cited design-canon thread. Where the Mareep line carries an electrification-thins-the-fleece canon (visible-cost-of-power) and the Staraptor line carries a leaves-the-flock-at-evolution canon (departure-from-cohort) and the Ralts line carries a gender-conditional-Stage-2-fork (gender-as-mutable-trajectory) and the Pancham line carries a party-composition-gated bad-influence-evolution canon (juvenile-delinquent-becomes-boss via Dark typing exposure), the Phantump line carries a **lost-child-spirit-becomes-forest-guardian canon** — Phantump canonically functions as the lonely child-spirit-possessed-tree-stump base (the lure-by-imitating-a-child's-voice flavor, the canonical lonely-spirit-trying-to-make-friends emotional beat, the hollow-stump-body floating-above-the-forest-floor visual register); the trade-evolution canonically completes the species' transition to the adult forest-guardian apex (the species' transformation from stump to full tree mediated through the trade mechanic, with the canonical lore framing the species' growth from child-spirit-in-stump to elder-tree-spirit-in-trunk as a developmental arc); Trevenant canonically completes the transition to forest-guardian apex with the canonical roots-tie-into-the-forest control-mechanism, the curse-ability against those who fell its trees (per Pokedex flavor), and the kindness-to-those-who-dwell-peacefully-in-its-forest counterpart to the curse-ability. The species' evolution arc is designer-coordinated as a lost-soul-becomes-guardian transition built into the lore itself, with the trade-evolution gating as the structural mechanic mediating the species' growth from base to apex.

The line is also the corpus's strongest **Japanese-folkloric-tree-spirit Pokemon-line precedent**. The kodama (Japanese Shinto tree-spirit) design source is widely cited as the species' canonical design origin, with the stripe-like markings on Trevenant resembling shimenawa rope (Shinto ritual rope used to mark sacred or spiritually-inhabited spaces) explicitly noted in Bulbapedia's design-source section, and the cyclopean yokai single-eye iconography and Tolkien's Ents as secondary design influences. For BBL bundle work, this is thesis-grade material — a Pokemon evolution line with explicit Japanese-folkloric design canon, explicit Shinto-iconographic visual references (the shimenawa rope marking on Trevenant), and explicit community-attested "saddest Pokemon design" status, available for bundle copy that wants to operate at the franchise-design-canon-and-folkloric-source scope.

For BBL bundle work, the line offers three strong narrative threads: a **lost-child-spirit-becomes-forest-guardian** thread anchored on the species' Pokedex canon (the child-death-origin-to-elder-tree-spirit transition as the franchise's clearest lonely-soul-becomes-guardian narrative arc rendered through an evolutionary line), a **kodama-tree-spirit Japanese-folkloric design source** thread anchored on the line's design-canon citations (the shimenawa rope marking on Trevenant, the kodama tree-spirit lore, the cyclopean yokai single-eye iconography as the line's most-cited Japanese-folkloric design influences), and a **"saddest Pokemon design" community canon** thread anchored on the species' Pokedex flavor (Phantump's child-death-origin canon and the lure-by-child's-voice-as-loneliness-not-malice motif as the franchise's community-attested darkest-origin benchmark, available for bundle copy that wants to lean fully into the dark-lore-done-right register). The cross-print Phantump CEC-93 + Phantump RBC-014 pair offers a within-corpus same-species different-emotional-register comparison (miki kudo's lonely-spirit register vs sui's eerie-power register), and the Trevenant FST-017 print closes the two-stage evolutionary line completely at the species-line scope. Among the corpus's Pokemon-line cohort nodes, the Phantump line is the **only fully-coverage two-stage line** currently in corpus (both Phantump and Trevenant present, with three total prints across both stages — the corpus's first fully-covered species-line cohort to commission at threshold).

## Caveats

- **Three corpus prints across both line members; full line coverage at the two-stage scope.** Both evolutionary stages of the line are represented in current inventory (two Phantump prints at base stage, one Trevenant print at Stage-1 final slot). Unlike the three-stage lines [[mareep-line]], [[charizard-line]], [[caterpie-line]], [[larvitar-line]], and [[rowlet-line]], the Phantump line is a **two-stage line not a three-stage line** — there is no Stage-2 apex, and Trevenant is the line's terminal evolutionary form. Full line coverage at the species-line scope is achieved with the current corpus (both species present and trivia-passed). The two-stage line structure is shared with [[pancham-line]] (the Kalos two-stage bancho line) — both Kalos two-stage lines are at threshold and full-species-line-coverage simultaneously. Membership predicates on species identity, not set, era, printing variant, or trivia-pass status.

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like `mareep-line`, `staraptor-line`, `charizard-line`, `caterpie-line`, `ralts-line`, `larvitar-line`, `rowlet-line`, `pancham-line`, `elemental-monkey-trio`, `galarian-regional-forms`, `fear-of-cycle`, and `dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle`, this is a collective species-line cohort hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer already accepts collective named entities). The Phantump line is structurally distinguished from the precedent three-stage line nodes by its **two-stage line structure** (shared with `pancham-line`) and by its **trade-evolution gating** (one of the franchise's standard trade-evolution mechanics, placing this line in the trade-evolution-cohort alongside Kadabra/Alakazam, Machoke/Machamp, Graveler/Golem, and Haunter/Gengar; this cohort warrants its own future parent node when 3+ trade-evolution line nodes are in graph). A future `_evolutionary-lines/` or `_cycles/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the other designer-coordinated cohort nodes; when that split is commissioned, this node moves cleanly. For now, single layer, same precedent.

- **Sibling Generation VI Kalos-line nodes are future candidates as their species-lines accumulate corpus prints.** Generation VI introduced the Kalos regional Pokedex (no. 650-721 plus retroactive cross-generation additions) with multiple new evolutionary lines designed alongside the Phantump line — see [[pancham-line]] for the corpus's other Kalos cohort node at threshold, plus the Chespin / Quilladin / Chesnaught grass-starter line, Fennekin / Braixen / Delphox fire-starter line, Froakie / Frogadier / Greninja water-starter line, Fletchling / Fletchinder / Talonflame regional-bird line, Bunnelby / Diggersby early-route line, Litleo / Pyroar savanna line, and many others. Sibling Generation VI line nodes get commissioned when threshold is met. Currently in-corpus, the Fletchling line is at threshold-minus-one across two stages. With this Phantump line and the sibling [[pancham-line]] both commissioned, a future `kalos-evolutionary-lines` parent cohort node could anchor the within-generation Kalos cohort axis.

- **Sibling Pokemon trade-evolution line cohort nodes are future candidates across all generations.** Trade-evolution is one of the franchise's standard evolution-mechanic categories, with multiple cross-generation lines using trade-evolution gating: Kadabra to Alakazam (Kanto Gen I), Machoke to Machamp (Kanto Gen I), Graveler to Golem (Kanto Gen I), Haunter to Gengar (Kanto Gen I), Slowpoke to Slowking (Johto Gen II), Onix to Steelix (Johto Gen II via item-hold), this Phantump to Trevenant line (Kalos Gen VI), and others. Each trade-evolution line is its own multi-stage cohort and warrants its own node when its species-line crosses threshold. Currently this is the corpus's first trade-evolution line cohort node. A future `trade-evolution-lines` parent cohort node could anchor the cross-generation trade-evolution-mechanic coordination at the design-convention scope.

- **Sibling Japanese-folkloric-design-source Pokemon-line nodes are future candidates.** The kodama tree-spirit design source is shared with multiple Pokemon designs across multiple lines and generations (Sudowoodo, Substitute, Celebi the time-travel-grass-fairy Mythical, and others reference kodama or related Japanese tree/forest-spirit folklore in some form). A future `kodama-design-source-lines` parent cohort node could anchor the cross-line design-source coordination at the Japanese-folkloric scope; currently the Phantump line is the corpus's strongest single-line-anchor for the kodama design source. Bulbapedia's design-source documentation for Trevenant explicitly cites kodama, shimenawa, cyclopean yokai, and Tolkien's Ents — for any bundle copy that wants to operate at the design-canon scope, this is the line where these citations directly apply.

- **Trevenant's franchise-presence is moderate-popular — corpus-skewing-effect is minor.** Trevenant and the Phantump line sit at moderate-popular franchise tier — recognized particularly in the Pokemon-design retrospective writing for the species' dark lore and Japanese-folkloric design source, but not at marquee-tier popularity (the species' Generation VI competitive presence is at NU / RU tier). The Pokemon TCG print volume for the Phantump line is consistent with moderate-tier Kalos-line print volume (multiple prints across most modern Pokemon TCG sets at the standard cadence). Bundle work on this node will not face significant corpus-skewing-effect.

- **The trade-evolution gating is mainline-only — TCG does not gate Phantump-to-Trevenant evolution.** In the mainline Pokemon games, Phantump's evolution into Trevenant requires the species to be traded (one of the franchise's standard trade-evolution mechanics). In the TCG, this gating mechanic does not apply — any Phantump card can have its `evolvesTo` field reference Trevenant per PokemonTCG.io, with no trade-mechanic check. Bundle copy referencing the trade-evolution gating should be precise about the mainline-games-versus-TCG distinction.

- **The kodama design source is canonically attributed, not community-attested.** Unlike the "saddest Pokemon design" community canon (which is community-attested via r/pokemon / GameFAQs / fan-retrospective writing), the kodama tree-spirit design source is explicitly cited in Bulbapedia's design-source section for Trevenant and in multiple official Pokemon-design retrospective interviews. Bundle copy that leans on the kodama reading does not require the community-attestation qualifier — the reading lives at the design-source-documented level, not the community-attested level.

# Thallid lineage (Sarpadian fungus-creature cohort across Fallen Empires / Time Spiral / Dominaria / Modern Horizons revivals)

The designer-implicit creature-type cohort that anchors Wizards's canonical Sarpadian-thallid lineage — the bipedal sentient fungus-bodied creatures created on the lost continent of Sarpadia by the elven druid Thelon of Havenwood during the post-Sylex-Blast ice age, who overran their elven farmers and survived the fall of the Sarpadian empires as one of the few sentient species the thrulls did not exterminate. The lineage spans four MTG expansions of canonical revival activity — **Fallen Empires** (1994, the original creature-type introduction and the Sarpadian-setting prime canon), **Time Spiral** block (2006-2007, the Sarpadian-retro design-pillar revival that reintroduced thallid mechanical signatures into modern Magic), **Dominaria** (2018, the small but deliberate Slimefoot-anchored fungus-tribal package returning Sarpadian thallids to the Dominaria-on-card present), and **Modern Horizons 3** (2024, the Sarpadian Simulacrum direct-Sarpadian-naming card extending the lineage's design vocabulary into the modern-frame retro-throwback design space) — plus the cross-plane variant track on **Alara** (Jund-shard thallids per Tukatongue Thallid in Alara Reborn 2009 and the Modern Masters 2015 reprint, canonically descended from Dominarian thallids per the MTG Wiki Thallid page's Alara section). The cohort's narrative thesis is the canonical *cultivated-creation-revolts-against-its-cultivators* arc, with the Sarpadian elves' fall-to-their-own-creations event sitting alongside the structurally identical Order-of-the-Ebon-Hand fall-to-thrulls event (both empires used the same biological-cultivation method, both fell to their cultivated creations during the same Sarpadian collapse).

## What this node anchors

Card art, flavor text, and trivia evidence for thallid-creature-type cards across MTG's Sarpadian-revival cohort. The unifying frame across the cohort is fourfold: (a) the **shared Sarpadian creation lineage** — every thallid creature canonically descends from Thelon-of-Havenwood's original elven cultivation of fungus-infused-with-green-mana on Sarpadia post-Sylex-Blast, with the Alara-Jund-shard variant canonically descended from the Dominarian root stock per the MTG Wiki Thallid page; (b) the **Fungus creature-type subtype** as the cohort's mechanical signature, with most printings carrying Creature — Fungus or Creature — Plant Fungus subtype lines and the saproling-token-output as the cohort's signature mechanical behavior; (c) the **cultivated-creation-revolts-against-cultivators canonical arc** as the cohort's most-cited Sarpadian-lore thesis (the elven farmers of Havenwood were overrun by their thallid creations in a single day, the Order of the Ebon Hand was similarly overrun by its thrull creations, both empires used the same Thelon-borrowed biological-cultivation method); and (d) the **inverted-Cabal-ritual-emulation thread** on the Urborg-resident thallid subpopulation, with Thallid Soothsayer's flavor text canonically establishing that Urborg-fens thallids began emulating Belzenlok-Cabal sacrifice-rituals in their own fungal-biology way (the bridge thread to the [[belzenlok]] character node, see Caveats).

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current; 3 fully-enriched anchors across 2 sets):**

- **Thallid Omnivore** (Dominaria 106, Common, qty 1, Mathias Kollros art, vision+trivia passed) — **Sarpadian-cultivation-origin tier**. Depicts a large bipedal fungal creature in a dense jungle, mottled green-and-yellow-brown spiny fronds, holding a staff with a tiny saproling barely visible on a branch — the Dominarian-thallid canonical visual identifier. Flavor text quotes *"Thelon of Havenwood created thallids as a food source in times of darkness. Thallids did the same with saprolings."* — the canonical food-chain-echo arc explicit on the card itself. The trivia bullet cites the full Thelon-of-Havenwood biological-cultivation lineage: thallids were created from fungus infused with green mana using methods borrowed from the Order of the Ebon Hand who had used the same methods to create thrulls; the fungus was named from the mycological term "thallus" (the body of a fungus); thallids initially served as livestock, grew sentient and violent, and overran Havenwood in a single day. Their history is documented in the in-universe Sarpadian Empires compendium Vol. III. The Sarpadian-cultivation-origin tier is the *engineer-administrator-of-the-cultivation-program* anchor at the cohort's lineage root.

- **Thallid Soothsayer** (Dominaria 107, Uncommon, qty 1, Jason A. Engle art, vision+trivia passed) — **Cabal-emulation-bridge tier**. Depicts a large thallid with a wide mushroom-cap head, arms raised in a ritual or summoning pose, standing in a dark swampy forest with bare twisted trees in grey-blue mist — the Urborg-fens-thallid canonical visual identifier. Flavor text quotes *"Some of the thallids that escaped into the fens of Urborg began emulating the Cabal's bloodsoaked rituals in their own peculiar way."* — the canonical Cabal-ritual-emulation bridge thread explicit on the card itself. The trivia bullet identifies the bridge: thallids in Urborg adopted the surrounding Belzenlok-Cabal sacrifice-as-devotion framework but through fungal biology, with the card's `{2}, Sacrifice a creature: Draw a card` oracle text mechanically encoding the fungal-ritual flavor. The Cabal-emulation-bridge tier is the *thallid-as-cult-mimic* anchor that links the thallid cohort to the [[belzenlok]] character node via Wizards-canonical population displacement (Belzenlok's Cabal relocated to Urborg, thallid escapees in the same Urborg fens absorbed the Cabal's ritual register).

- **Trufflesnout** (Core Set 2021 212, Common, qty 3, Jason Kang art, vision+trivia passed) — **ecological-prey-naming tier**. Depicts a bristly tusked wild boar rooting up mushrooms on a sun-dappled forest floor — the Selesnya signpost-common art register, not a thallid itself. Flavor text quotes *"Its exploits are the stuff of legends among local chefs and fuel for nightmares among the forest thallids."* — the canonical thallid-as-ecological-prey reference explicit on the card itself. The trivia bullet cites the full canonical Thelon-of-Havenwood biological-cultivation lineage (paralleling Thallid Omnivore's citation): thallids originate in Fallen Empires (1994), were created by the druid Thelon of Havenwood to feed the elves of Sarpadia, gained sentience, revolted against their elvish farmers, and came to dominate the ruins of Havenwood. The card encodes the thallid-as-prey thread — a truffle-hunting boar is a direct ecological threat to fungal organisms that are thallid ancestors or kin. The ecological-prey-naming tier is the *thallid-cohort-named-from-outside-the-cohort* anchor; it is the only currently-enriched corpus card that mentions thallids by name without depicting one.

## The design thesis

The thallid lineage's most BBL-relevant property is the **cultivated-creation-revolts-against-its-cultivators arc** as a designer-coordinated lore-trope rendered across multiple printings, sets, and decades. The Sarpadian-lore prime canon (Fallen Empires 1994) establishes that two of Sarpadia's five empires — the elves of Havenwood and the Order of the Ebon Hand — both used the *same* biological-cultivation method (the Ebon Hand invented it for thrulls, Thelon borrowed it for thallids) and *both fell to their own creations* during the Sarpadian collapse triggered by the post-Sylex-Blast climate-change-induced resource scarcity. The structural parallel between the elf-thallid revolt and the Ebon-Hand-thrull revolt is the trope's clearest articulation: a cultivation program that began as a survival strategy (feeding the elves during the ice age) ended in the cultivators being overrun by their cultivated.

The trope is canonical across multiple Wizards sources: the MTG Wiki Sarpadia page explicitly lists the elf-by-thallid fall and the Ebon-Hand-by-thrull fall as the two structurally-twinned collapse events in the *"Fall of the Empires"* section; the MTG Wiki Thallid page documents the Havenwood elves splitting into two factions over whether to destroy the thallids or assist them; the Sonia Orin Lyris novel *And Peace Shall Sleep* (1996, Harper Prism) provides primary novelistic canon for the revolt narrative; the Fallen Empires comic provides primary comic-canon for the collapse. The trope's *post-collapse* survival arc is also canon: the MTG Wiki Thallid page documents that thallids "would assist the orcs, goblins, and thrulls in destroying Icatia, and would be one of the only sentient races on Sarpadia not exterminated by the thrulls" — the thallids outlived their cultivators *and* the structurally-parallel thrulls' attempt to exterminate them.

For BBL bundle work, the lineage offers a strong **cultivated-revolt narrative thread** anchored on the species' Wizards-canon-stated arc (cultivator-cultivates-creation, creation-survives-collapse-of-cultivator, creation-outlives-collapse-of-parallel-creation). The thread reads as the BBL [[rebellion]] hub register applied to a fungal-biology context — the thallid is the canonical *escaped-cultivated* whose post-revolt survival across the Sarpadian collapse, the Ice Age, the Brothers' War, the Time Spiral crisis, and the Dominaria 2018 reform constitutes one of MTG's longest-running creature-type biographies. **Per Potion Rule sub-rule 1**, the rebellion-hub linkage is the curatorial overlay — Wizards canonically wrote *fallen-cultivators-of-Sarpadia-with-their-cultivated-survivors-going-feral*; BBL reads the canonical fall-of-cultivators arc through a rebellion-of-the-cultivated register. Both readings can live in the body — the canonical-event-not-curatorial-overlay distinction is named here so future Edgelord rigor-audits can verify the Potion discipline holds.

The cohort's secondary thesis is the **structural-twin-to-thrulls relation**. Per the MTG Wiki Thallid page: *"Thelon shared a secret with the elder druids Kolevi and Lalani — the fact that thallids were also made in part from the Ebon Hand's thrulls."* The two creature-types share canonical biological substrate (the cultivation method) AND canonical history (both fell to their cultivators in the same Sarpadian collapse) AND canonical post-collapse co-existence (the MTG Wiki Thallid page notes that thrulls and thallids continued to co-exist after the empire fall, with the wiki speculating that *"with shared origins and natures, they may recognize the vast similarities between them"*). This twin-creature-type structural relation is not currently anchored at a graph-node level (no `thrull-lineage` node exists), but is documented here as a future-watch surface for if a thrull-naming card enters corpus with full trivia.

## The Cabal-emulation bridge thread

A specific Wizards-canonical sub-arc that warrants its own framing: the **Urborg-fens-thallid subpopulation's emulation of Belzenlok-Cabal sacrifice rituals**. Thallid Soothsayer's flavor text (*"Some of the thallids that escaped into the fens of Urborg began emulating the Cabal's bloodsoaked rituals in their own peculiar way."*) is the load-bearing canonical citation for this thread: the population displacement caused by Belzenlok's Cabal relocating to Urborg post-Mending brought the Cabal's ritual-culture into direct ecological contact with the resident thallid population, and a thallid subpopulation in the Urborg fens absorbed the Cabal's sacrifice-as-devotion behavioral framework but rendered it through fungal biology (sacrificing other creatures to grow and gain knowledge, mapping onto the card's mechanical text).

This bridge is the canonical reason the **[[belzenlok]] character node and the thallid-lineage cohort node are cross-related**: not all Belzenlok-substrate cards are thallids, and not all thallid-lineage cards are Cabal-emulation cards. Thallid Soothsayer specifically anchors the bridge, sitting in both nodes' substrate. Future Edgelord audits should verify that bridge-cards like Thallid Soothsayer always carry a `characters: [thallid-lineage]` primary attribution (its creature-type lineage is the more durable identity), with the Cabal-emulation thread surfaced in the card's trivia and reciprocated in this node's body-prose (here) AND in the [[belzenlok]] node's body-prose as a future-attach watch. **The Soothsayer is NOT currently in the [[belzenlok]] node's `appears_on:` list** — its primary identity is thallid-lineage, and the Cabal-emulation flavor is the bridge-context citation rather than the primary anchor. A future Edgelord audit might consider whether the Belzenlok node's body-prose should explicitly name Thallid Soothsayer as a bridge-card in its Cabal-Urborg-population section; defer for now.

A future `_evolutionary-lines/` or `_creature-types/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the other designer-coordinated cohort nodes; when that split is commissioned, this node moves cleanly with no body-prose changes required.

# Kiora

Merfolk planeswalker, sea-sorceress, and the most-printed leviathan-summoner in Magic: The Gathering canon. Introduced in *Born of the Gods* (2014) as Kiora, the Crashing Wave; foregrounded across the *Battle for Zendikar* / *Oath of the Gatewatch* Eldrazi-crisis arc (2015–2016), the *War of the Spark* Ravnica invasion (2019), and the *Theros: Beyond Death* return-to-Theros arc (2020). The `— Kiora` planeswalker subtype on the type-line is reserved for printings of this single character; canonical from Kiora, the Crashing Wave (Born of the Gods, 2014) through every subsequent legendary planeswalker bearing the name. Born on Zendikar (specifically the continent Tazeem), Kiora venerates leviathans, krakens, and the deep, and famously stole her signature bident from the Theros sea god Thassa during a planar incursion — a cross-plane theft that retroactively grounds every subsequent Kiora print's iconography and continues to flavor her conflict with the Theros pantheon.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts Kiora herself — under any of her titles (the Crashing Wave, Master of the Depths, Behemoth Beckoner, the Tide's Fury, Sovereign of the Deep) — and cards in the canonical *Kiora's <Spell>* / *Kiora's <Creature>* attribution pattern in which spells, followers, leviathans, and dambreakers are flavor-attributed to her summoning or stewardship. The character is large enough to split into distinct narrative aspects across her printings (the Zendikar leviathan-venerator, the Theros bident-thief, the WAR Ravnica defender), and the BBL graph can edge those aspects together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card mirroring.

**Aspects in the corpus (current):**

- **War of the Spark — Ravnica defense, behemoth-summoning moment** — Kiora on Ravnica during Bolas's invasion, summoning a leviathan to the battlefield consistent with the WAR premise that every planeswalker arrived on Ravnica for the war.
  - **Kiora, Behemoth Beckoner** (WAR-232, uncommon, Jaime Jones art) — blue-skinned merfolk sorceress in dark plated shoulder armor standing triumphant atop the coiled body of a massive sea-leviathan rising from stormy ocean waves, raising her signature curved silver bident overhead in commanding pose. Mechanically `{2}{G/U}` (Simic-coded hybrid), 7 starting loyalty (high for slot), with power-4-or-greater ETB-draw trigger + −1 untap any-permanent loyalty ability. One of the twenty uncommon planeswalkers from the WAR cycle, designed to the Rosewater-confirmed template (one static/triggered ability + one loyalty ability). The bident in her hand is canonically the Theros-Thassa-stolen artifact established on her Born of the Gods debut.
  - **Kiora's Dambreaker** (WAR-58, common, Mathias Kollros art) — massive blue-grey sea serpent erupting from violent ocean waves, ribbon-like body breaking the surface in multiple arcs amid towering whitecaps. Mechanically `{4}{U}` 5/5 Leviathan with proliferate-on-ETB. Flavor text: *"When your enemy flees, send the sea to pursue them." — Kiora.* The card is the canonical conjurer-and-conjured pairing for the WAR cycle: Kiora summoning the leviathan rendered as a separate card whose flavor speaker is Kiora herself. The dambreaker name is the in-fiction descriptor — a leviathan that breaks dams (and by extension, every constructed apparatus humans use to hold the sea back).

The two corpus cards span the WAR uncommon-walker slot (the named planeswalker print) and the WAR companion-spell slot (the leviathan she summons). Both depict Kiora's arc; one depicts her directly, the other depicts her conjuration. Different artists, different visual scales, same character anchoring the attribution. Single-set anchor pair — a limitation worth acknowledging; future corpus growth into the Born of the Gods printing (BNG-149 Kiora, the Crashing Wave), the Battle for Zendikar / Oath of the Gatewatch printing (OGW-41 Kiora, Master of the Depths), or the Theros: Beyond Death printings would broaden the node into the planar-arc continuity that retroactively grounds the WAR cards' framing.

## The stolen-bident and the cross-plane theology

A notable Wizards-canonical pattern: Kiora's signature weapon, the curved silver bident depicted on every print of her, was stolen from the Theros sea god **Thassa** during a planar incursion. Per MTG Wiki's Kiora character page and the *Theros: Beyond Death* story arc, Kiora's bident-theft is one of the strongest single character beats in the post-Mending canon: a Zendikar merfolk planeswalker raids the Theros pantheon's seat of power, takes Thassa's bident, and walks off with it across the Blind Eternities — leaving the Theros sea-god without her primary divine instrument. The theft is canonical across multiple sets (the bident appears on Kiora's Follower BNG-167; Kiora, Master of the Depths OGW-41; this WAR-232 print; the *Theros: Beyond Death* return prints). Thassa's response to the theft drives subsequent narrative beats including the *Theros: Beyond Death* arc in which Kiora returns to face the consequences.

For BBL bundle copy, the cross-plane theft is gold. Kiora is canonically a planeswalker who stole from a god — not via clever trickster-archetype framing (Garruk-versus-Liliana) but through a direct act of cross-plane appropriation of a divine artifact. The labor / extraction / sovereignty thesis the [[orzhov-syndicate]] node and the [[chandra-nalaar]] node carry from below-the-system positions has a from-above counter-example here: the merfolk-citizen-of-Zendikar who appropriated divine power from the Theros pantheon and walked away from the consequences for years. The leviathan-summoner identity is the cover story; the bident-thief identity is the operational truth.

## Caveats

- This node is the character Kiora herself, not the set of cards whose **name** mentions her. Cards like Kiora's Dambreaker (WAR-58), Kiora's Follower (BNG-167), and Kiora's Dismissal edge here through the canonical-name attribution test; cards that mention Kiora only in flavor without depicting her or being attributed to her summoning would not. Standard depiction-or-attribution test, parallel to the [[jace-beleren]] / [[ral-zarek]] / [[chandra-nalaar]] / [[angrath]] standards.
- **The `— Kiora` planeswalker subtype is a strict canonical identifier.** Every card carrying that subtype is a printing of this single character. Spells named after her (Dambreaker, Follower, Dismissal, Behest, et al) use the character's name without the subtype and are validated by flavor/oracle text attribution.
- **The single-set corpus limitation is real.** Both anchors are from *War of the Spark*. The Zendikar / Tazeem origin arc (BNG-149 Kiora, the Crashing Wave; OGW-41 Kiora, Master of the Depths) and the *Theros: Beyond Death* return arc are canonically the broader character continuity that retroactively grounds the WAR cards' bident-iconography and leviathan-summoning register. Currently this node is "Kiora on Ravnica" with backstory imported from earlier and later sets via citations. The Zendikar-merfolk and Theros-bident-thief registers are the load-bearing identities even though the corpus shows the WAR phase.
- **Kiora's bident-theft is canonical and unredeemed.** Wizards has not (as of the most recent Theros canon) shown Kiora returning the bident or reconciling with Thassa; she keeps it as her signature weapon across every subsequent print. Bundle copy that anchors on Kiora should hold this honestly: she is canonically a planeswalker who stole from a god and walked off with the spoils, and Wizards has been willing to keep that texture across multiple printings. Compare the [[angrath]] node's similar unsoftened-canon footnote — both characters carry moral textures Wizards usually softens for sympathetic walkers, and both retain those textures across multiple sets.
- **A future [[nicol-bolas]] character node-to-this node *Ravnica-defender* relation could be drawn** (Kiora is one of the 36 walkers Bolas summoned to Ravnica; she defends against the Eternal army in the WAR-232 art and Dambreaker conjuration). The relation is canon-grounded but is a character-to-character edge, distinct from the card-to-character anchor edges this node currently accumulates. Defer until formalized.
- **A future Thassa character node-to-this node *theft-victim* relation could be drawn.** Thassa is currently not graph-modeled in BBL (no Thassa cards in corpus); when a Thassa print lands, the bident-theft becomes the strongest cross-character lore beat available for the Kiora node, and the Theros pantheon's response sequence becomes commission-ready bundle material.
- **The Angrath / Kiora pair is a designer-coordinated WAR uncommon-walker cycle pairing but not a story-spotlight cycle.** Both characters appear on WAR uncommon walker prints (227 + 232) and on companion spells (185 Angrath's Rampage + 58 Kiora's Dambreaker); both follow the Rosewater-confirmed design template. Cycle membership is at the design-template level, not the narrative-spotlight level — the WAR uncommon-walker cycle is documented in the janitor triage as a future hub/symbol node candidate when ≥3 corpus members converge.

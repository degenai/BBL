# Ralts evolutionary line (Ralts / Kirlia / Gardevoir / Gallade)

A branching Psychic-empath evolutionary line and the canonical Hoenn-region emotion-sensing species line introduced in Pokemon Ruby and Sapphire (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 2002). The line comprises **Ralts** (National Pokedex no. 280, Psychic / Fairy as of Generation VI, base form, the "Feeling Pokemon" classified for its empathy-as-sensory-organ design conceit, with the red horn-like structures functioning as canonical emotional antennae), **Kirlia** (no. 281, Psychic / Fairy, Stage 1 mid-form, the "Emotion Pokemon" whose balletic posture and dance behavior is Pokedex-canonical and indexed to its Trainer's positive mood), and a Stage-2 endpoint that branches: **Gardevoir** (no. 282, Psychic / Fairy, Stage 2, any gender, from Kirlia at level 30, the "Embrace Pokemon" whose Pokedex flavor describes psychic perception across global distances tuned to its Trainer's emotional state) OR **Gallade** (no. 475, Psychic / Fighting, Stage 2 male-only branch, from Kirlia via Dawn Stone, the "Blade Pokemon" added in Generation IV at the Sinnoh expansion of the Hoenn line, classified as a chivalric-warrior counterpoint to Gardevoir's empath-mage register). The line is one of the franchise's only three-stage lines whose designer-coordinated fork sits at the second stage rather than the final stage, and the only such line whose fork is explicitly gender-gated in the mainline games (the Dawn Stone evolution to Gallade requires a male Kirlia, with Gardevoir as the default route for female Kirlia and the alternative route for male Kirlia who do not receive the Dawn Stone). The Pokedex characterization of the line as empath-becoming-mage-or-blade — soft white humanoid child at base, dancing adolescent at mid-stage, perceiver-of-distance OR shielding-warrior at apex — is the line's most-cited narrative arc, a designer-coordinated emotion-to-action transition rendered as a species-level evolution event with a gender-gated moral fork at the second stage.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text for the line members across their TCG appearances: Ralts (base) and Kirlia (Stage 1) currently in corpus, with Gardevoir and Gallade (Stage 2) absent. The unifying frame across the cohort is fourfold: (a) the **shared evolutionary lineage** — every print of Ralts or Kirlia depicts a stage of this single Pokedex 280 / 281 species line, (b) the **Hoenn-region empath role** as the line's foundational identity, with Ralts occupying the Route 102 wild-encounter slot in Pokemon Ruby and Sapphire, (c) the **species-diagnostic palette and body-plan progression** enforced across all four stages of the branching line and across every print in every set since 2002 (white-body-with-green-head-piece-and-red-horns at Ralts, white-dress-form-with-green-hair-and-red-horns at Kirlia, white-and-green-gowned-tall-humanoid-with-red-chest-fin at Gardevoir, white-and-green-bladed-warrior-with-red-chest-fin at Gallade; the red horn / chest-fin motif persists across all four endpoints as the line's visual signature), (d) the **empathy-as-psychic-medium narrative arc** as the line's most-cited Pokedex flavor signature, with Ralts's horn-antennae, Kirlia's mood-indexed dancing, and Gardevoir's global-distance perception all rendered as the species' canonical biology rather than incidental design details.

## The design thesis

The Ralts line's most BBL-relevant property is the **empathy-as-psychic-medium narrative arc** rendered as a species-level biological motif, paired with the **gender-conditional second-stage fork** as the line's structural signature. Where the Mareep line carries an electrification-thins-the-fleece canon (visible-cost-of-power as a maturation event) and the Staraptor line carries a leaves-the-flock-at-evolution canon (departure-from-cohort as a maturation event), the Ralts line carries an **emotion-becomes-action canon** — Ralts canonically senses emotion through its horns and warms its body in response to warm feelings (the Pokedex entries consistently characterize Ralts as a passive empath, with the horn-antennae as the species' primary emotional-sensory medium); Kirlia canonically channels emotional intensity into dance and psychic activity (the dancing-when-happy mechanic is explicit across Pokedex entries from Generation III through Generation IX, with Kirlia's psychic power intensifying in proportion to its Trainer's positive mood); Gardevoir canonically perceives at global distances tuned to its Trainer's emotional state (the across-the-world-perception flavor is Pokedex-canonical and the species' marquee narrative beat); Gallade canonically converts the line's empath-mage register into a chivalric-blade register, with sword-like extensions from the elbows replacing Gardevoir's flowing-gown silhouette and the line's emotional-sensitivity reframed as combat-attunement to an opponent's intent. The species' evolution arc is designer-coordinated as an empath-to-mage-OR-blade transition built into the evolutionary mechanic itself, with the gender-conditional second-stage fork serving as the line's structural signature — the only Pokemon line that branches at Stage 2 with a gender gate. The branching is fan-resonant: Gallade was a fan-requested addition in Generation IV (Diamond and Pearl, 2006), four years after the Hoenn line's introduction, and is widely cited as the franchise's most-cited example of a retroactive-evolution addition expanding an existing line.

**Trans / gender-fluid community reading (canonized).** The Ralts line is widely community-read as a trans-coded evolutionary lineage and one of the most cited Pokemon examples of gender-as-mutable-trajectory rather than gender-as-fixed-trait. The reading is structurally supported by the line's actual design: Ralts and Kirlia present as gender-indeterminate (the species are not visually dimorphic at the base or Stage-1 stages); the Stage-2 fork at the Dawn Stone is the canonical moment of gender-coded endpoint selection (Gardevoir as the feminine-coded gowned-empath form, Gallade as the masculine-coded armored-blade form); and the Dawn Stone evolution mechanism is itself a player-mediated intervention that the Trainer must perform on the Kirlia, with the gender gate functioning as an in-mechanic gate rather than a settled-at-birth species trait. The community reading is widely attested across r/pokemon / GameFAQs / fan-art / cosplay communities and is one of the most-cited Pokemon examples in trans-affirming-fandom discourse. Available as a bundle-copy lens if needed, with the qualifier that the reading lives at the evolution-mechanic level (Dawn Stone as player-mediated endpoint selection) rather than at the species-biology level. Not a load-bearing thesis for the node — just documenting that the community reading exists and is fair game for copy that wants it.

The line is also the corpus's strongest **emotion-as-psychic-medium Pokemon-line precedent**. The community reading of the Ralts line as a meadow-elf / fairy-folkloric reference is well-attested (the Generation VI Fairy-typing retcon in 2013 canonized the reading at the type-system level), and the line's enduring popularity at the Generation III tier is the reason Gardevoir consistently places in franchise-popularity polls at the top tier of Generation III designs. For BBL bundle work, this is thesis-grade material — a Pokemon evolution line with explicit emotion-as-medium canon (Pokedex flavor across multiple generations), explicit gender-gated structural signature (the Dawn Stone fork at Stage 2), and explicit fan-canon weight (Gallade as fan-requested addition, Mega Gardevoir / Mega Gallade as Generation VI marquee Mega Evolutions). The Chilling Reign 059 + 060 within-set adjacent-collector-number pair is the corpus's second within-Pokemon-set evolution-line cohort placement (after Mareep / Flaaffy CRE-047 + 048), structurally parallel to the Charizard line within-Vivid-Voltage triptych pattern. The cross-set CRE + BUS + LOT presence broadens the line's corpus footprint across three sets and confirms threshold (3 trivia-passed prints across 2 stages, with a 4th vision-only print pending).

## Caveats

- **Four corpus prints across two of four line endpoints; partial line coverage in-corpus.** Two of the four evolutionary endpoints of the branching line are represented in current inventory (one Ralts print at base stage, three Kirlia prints at Stage-1 middle slot; **Gardevoir and Gallade at the Stage-2 apex slots are not currently in BBL corpus**). The line's coverage is at the base-and-middle stages — empath and dancing — with both Stage-2 branches (Gardevoir-mage and Gallade-blade) as future-acquisition targets. When a Gardevoir or Gallade print enters corpus via future Collectr CSV upload, it attaches here and the line moves from partial-coverage to full-branching coverage — the corpus's first full-branching evolutionary-line cohort node when both Stage-2 branches arrive. Membership predicates on species identity, not set, era, printing variant, or trivia-pass status (the unwired vision-only Kirlia LOT-140 print is a future-attaches-here member pending trivia pass; see next bullet).

- **Kirlia LOT-140 is a fourth corpus member pending trivia pass — line node anchors only the three trivia-passed prints currently.** The Kirlia Lost Thunder 140 print (Kouki Saitou art, soft pink natural-tone setting, single Disarming Voice attack at 50 damage) is in BBL corpus and is canonically a Kirlia print belonging to this line, but the vision-only pass on this print has not yet been promoted to a full trivia pass and the `ip_verified: false` flag is still set. When that print receives a trivia pass and IP verification, the line node continues to anchor it via the `characters: ["ralts-line"]` frontmatter pointer already added. The line node's narrative scope and design thesis are unchanged by this pending attachment — Kirlia LOT-140 is a sibling print of Kirlia BUS-92 and Kirlia CRE-060, just at the wave-46-stubby-stays-stubby end of the threshold. No edge work between Kirlia LOT-140 and the other Kirlia prints is warranted: the line node absorbs the cohort relationship at the species-line level.

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like [[mareep-line]] (Johto Generation II Electric line), [[staraptor-line]] (Sinnoh Generation IV regional-bird line), [[charizard-line]] (Kanto Generation I starter line), [[elemental-monkey-trio]] (Unova Generation V three-paired-species elemental trio), [[galarian-regional-forms]] (Galar Generation VIII regional variant cohort), [[fear-of-cycle]] (Duskmourn phobia-Nightmares), and [[dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle]] (Duskmourn common dual-lands), this is a collective species-line cohort hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer already accepts collective named entities). The Ralts line is structurally distinguished from the precedent line nodes by its **branching Stage-2 endpoint** — Mareep, Staraptor, and Charizard lines are linear three-stage progressions, while the Ralts line is the corpus's first branching-evolutionary-line cohort node. A future `_evolutionary-lines/` or `_cycles/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the other designer-coordinated cohort nodes; when that split is commissioned, this node moves cleanly. For now, single layer, same precedent.

- **The Stage-2 fork is gender-gated in the mainline games but not in the TCG.** Per the mainline Pokemon games, only male Kirlia can evolve into Gallade via the Dawn Stone, with Gardevoir as the default route for female Kirlia and the alternative route for male Kirlia who do not receive the Dawn Stone. In the TCG, the gender gate does not apply — any Kirlia card can have its `evolvesTo` field reference both Gardevoir and Gallade simultaneously, with the branch determined purely by which Stage-2 card the player chooses to play. The Kirlia CRE-060 print explicitly lists both Gardevoir and Gallade in its `evolvesTo` field per PokemonTCG.io. Bundle copy referencing the gender-gated fork should be precise about the mainline-games-versus-TCG distinction.

- **Sibling Generation III Hoenn-line nodes are future candidates as their species-lines accumulate corpus prints.** Generation III introduced the Hoenn regional Pokedex (no. 252-386) with multiple new evolutionary lines designed alongside the Ralts line (Treecko / Grovyle / Sceptile the grass-starter line, Torchic / Combusken / Blaziken the fire-starter line, Mudkip / Marshtomp / Swampert the water-starter line, Poochyena / Mightyena the early-route line, Wurmple / Silcoon / Beautifly OR Cascoon / Dustox the bug branching line, Mareep is Gen II not Gen III but Ralts / Kirlia / Gardevoir / Gallade this line, and many others). Sibling Generation III line nodes get commissioned when threshold is met (3+ trivia-passed corpus prints across 2+ stages, the threshold this node sits at three corpus prints across two stages). Currently in-corpus, the Combusken Stage-1 print (Combusken DAA-023, vision-only) is a single-stage isolated print without the Torchic base-stage or Blaziken apex to push the Torchic line to threshold; the bug branching Wurmple line has no current corpus members. Sibling nodes get commissioned per-region per-line when threshold lands.

- **Ralts and Gardevoir's franchise-presence is high-popular — corpus-skewing-effect is moderate.** Ralts and the line generally have a high franchise-popularity register: Gardevoir is consistently cited among the most popular Generation III designs and one of the most recognizable Pokemon designs overall, with Mega Gardevoir's pink-gown Generation VI redesign reinforcing the line's marquee status. The Pokemon TCG print volume for the Ralts line is consistent with high-popularity Hoenn-line print volume (multiple prints across most modern Pokemon TCG sets at a higher cadence than the median Hoenn line), and the corpus's three-Kirlia / one-Ralts print distribution may reflect this — Kirlia in particular is heavily reprinted as the Stage-1 connector between the popular base and Stage-2 endpoints. Bundle work on this node may face moderate corpus-skewing-effect — the prints across line members are likely to remain Kirlia-heavy until a Gardevoir or Gallade print enters corpus, at which point the cohort distribution will rebalance.

- **The empathy-as-psychic-medium canon is strongest at the Ralts and Gardevoir stages.** The species' emotion-as-sensory-organ canon is explicit at the Ralts stage (the horn-antennae empathy mechanic is canonical and consistently repeated across the species' Pokedex entries) and reaches its narrative apex at the Gardevoir stage (the across-global-distances perception tuned to Trainer emotional state is Gardevoir's most-cited Pokedex flavor beat). The Kirlia stage is the transitional middle — Pokedex entries reference both the mood-indexed dancing mechanic (continuous with Ralts's empathy register) and the future-sight / space-distortion mechanic (anticipating Gardevoir's psychic-perception register), but the line's empath-canon narrative arc is designer-coordinated AT the Ralts and Gardevoir endpoints, not at the Kirlia mid-stage. Bundle copy on the empath-canon framing should anchor primarily on Ralts or Gardevoir; the Kirlia stage is the dancing-mid-form register.

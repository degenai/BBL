# Castform Forms cycle (weather-Pokemon four-form cohort)

A designer-coordinated four-form cohort of a single Pokemon species (Castform, National Pokedex no. 351) introduced in Generation III (Ruby & Sapphire, 2002, Hoenn region) as the first Pokemon to change type and form based on active weather conditions. The four forms are **Normal** (no active weather, Normal-type, rounded white-lavender body with cloud-cap silhouette), **Sunny** (harsh sunlight, Fire-type, orange-red sun-flame body), **Rainy** (rain, Water-type, blue teardrop body), and **Snowy** (hail or snow, Ice-type, white snowflake-cloud body). In the main-series games the form-change is mediated by Castform's signature **Forecast** Ability; in the TCG, the four forms were printed as four separate cards in **Chilling Reign** (SWSH6, June 2021) — the first English release to include the complete four-form cycle simultaneously — each sharing the **Weather Reading** Ability ("If you have 8 or more Stadium cards in your discard pile, ignore all Energy in this Pokemon's attack costs"). Castform's design references the **teru teru bozu**, a Japanese paper or cloth charm doll hung from windows to wish for good weather; the forms also nod to the English idiom "rain, hail, or shine."

## What this node anchors

Card art, oracle text, and flavor text for the four Castform forms across their TCG appearances. The unifying frame across the cohort is fourfold: (a) the four-forms-of-one-species structure (single National Pokedex number, not a paired-evolution line), (b) the weather-trigger form-change mechanic encoded in main-series games via the Forecast Ability and in the TCG via the Weather Reading Ability that links each form-card to the Stadium-discard register, (c) the type-coded color-and-silhouette palette signature across the four forms (white/lavender for Normal, orange/red for Sunny, blue for Rainy, white/icy-blue for Snowy), (d) the teru teru bozu charm-doll design citation as the cultural-iconographic anchor.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Castform (Normal Form)** (Brilliant Stars 116/172, Common Reverse Holofoil, kawayoo art) — base/no-weather form. Small round white-lavender body with cloud-dome head silhouette, floating in a soft cloudy sky. Flavor: *"Its form changes depending on the weather. The rougher conditions get, the rougher Castform's disposition!"* The cohort's no-weather baseline; in BRS-116 this print arrived the year after the Chilling Reign three-form-alternate-cycle, completing the four-form cycle across SWSH-era printings. Oracle text on this print is Double Draw / Hurricane (not the shared Weather Reading Ability — Normal Form prints across sets vary; the BRS-116 Normal-Form print is a Hurricane-attacker rather than a Weather Reading enabler, but the four-form-cycle cohort affiliation is preserved through the species identity, the weather-form-trigger design citation, and the within-cycle pairing with the three Chilling Reign alternate-form prints).
- **Castform Rainy Form** (Chilling Reign 033/198, Common, sowsow art) — Water-type rain-trigger form. Blue teardrop-headed creature hovering above a soft pastel meadow of pink-and-lavender wildflowers; *"This is Castform's form when pelted by rain. In an experiment where it was placed in a shower, this Pokemon didn't change to this form."* The cohort's Water arm; the Weather Reading Ability is shared with the other Chilling Reign form-prints. Flavor text is notable for its dry experimental framing — implies the form-change requires natural weather, not artificial precipitation.
- **Castform Snowy Form** (Chilling Reign 034/198, Common, miki kudo art) — Ice-type hail/snow-trigger form. Small spherical white creature with a rounded snow-cloud cap floating in a swirling lavender-blue blizzard scene. Flavor: *"This is Castform's form when caught in a hailstorm. Its whole body is chilled, and its skin is partially frozen!"* The cohort's Ice arm; shares the Weather Reading Ability and the Chilling Reign 033-034 adjacency with Rainy Form. Frosty Typhoon attack continues the weather-event-as-attack-name register across the alternate-form prints.

## The design thesis

Castform is a four-form-of-one-species cohort built on a clean designer-coordinated lattice: one National Pokedex number (no. 351), one Ability that mediates form-change (Forecast in main series), one cultural-iconographic citation (teru teru bozu), four weather triggers (no-weather / sun / rain / hail) mapped to four primary types (Normal / Fire / Water / Ice) and four palette signatures. The cohort is a designed lever for **weather-themed Discrete Lair work** (Castform Normal + alternate-form prints as a four-card weather-set artifact, with optional anchor expansion through other weather-trigger Pokemon like Cherrim's Flower Gift or Politoed's Drizzle), and for **cultural-citation Discrete Lair work** (the teru teru bozu charm-doll as the Japanese folk-iconographic source visible across the four-form silhouettes). The Chilling Reign 2021 release is structurally important: it was the first English TCG release to print the complete four-form cycle simultaneously (Normal at 121, Sunny at 22, Rainy at 33, Snowy at 34), making the set itself the canonical-by-printing anchor for the cohort even though Castform's design dates to 2002.

## Caveats

- **One cycle member canonical at the species scope but not yet in BBL inventory.** Three of four forms are in the corpus and pointered to this node (see `appears_on:`); one form is documented at the species level but not yet acquired in any TCG print, and will attach to this node when any printing of it lands via a future Collectr CSV upload:
  - **Castform Sunny Form** (Fire-type, harsh-sunlight trigger) — the Chilling Reign 022/198 print is the obvious target if a Chilling Reign restock or rebuy completes the within-set 022-033-034 alternate-form triptych alongside Normal-Form-from-anywhere; any other set's Sunny-Form printing (the form has been printed in Sun & Moon and other eras) also satisfies the node.
  
  The BBL corpus is one form-acquisition away from full canonical four-form coverage at the cohort level, with the Brilliant Stars Normal-Form print already filling the no-weather baseline. With wave 18, Castform Snowy Form (CRE-034) joined the corpus on the same vision-pass that the Rainy Form (CRE-033) wave 11 trivia and Normal Form (BRS-116) wave 17 trivia previously anchored — closing the three-of-four-forms threshold at which this node clears the elemental-monkey-trio precedent (3/6) and fear-of-cycle precedent (5/10).
- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like `fear-of-cycle` (Duskmourn phobia-Nightmares), `dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle` (Duskmourn common dual-lands), and [[elemental-monkey-trio]] (Unova Pansage/Pansear/Panpour line), this is a designer-coordinated cohort of cards hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer already accepts collective named entities). A future `_cycles/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the two DSK cycles, the Elemental Monkey trio, and any subsequent designer-confirmed flavor cycle (Cinderella cycle in Throne of Eldraine; Initiative cycle in CLB); when that split is commissioned, this node moves cleanly. For now, single layer, same precedent.
- **Cohort membership predicates on the species-and-form pattern, not on weather flavor alone.** Pokemon has other weather-trigger species (Cherrim with Flower Gift form-change between Sunshine and Overcast forms; Wishiwashi with Schooling form-change; Eiscue with Ice Face form-change; Morpeko with Hangry Mode form-change) that are NOT part of this cohort — these are form-change Pokemon within the broader franchise, not members of the Castform-Normal-Sunny-Rainy-Snowy four-form weather-trigger cycle. Membership predicates on the species identity (National Pokedex no. 351 Castform) AND the four-form weather-trigger structure AND the Forecast/Weather Reading Ability lineage together. The weather-form-trigger tag and this character node correlate but are not co-extensive.
- **The teru teru bozu citation is documented via Bulbapedia / Bulbanews design-press consensus, not via an explicit Game Freak designer quote in the corpus citation set.** Bulbapedia / Bulbanews document the mapping as canonical based on the silhouette parallel (a round-headed cloth-doll body floating from a string is the teru teru bozu visual; Castform's no-limbs floating round-bodied silhouette across all four forms reads as the doll). If a future canonical source surfaces an explicit Game Freak design-document quote, upgrade this caveat. The teru teru bozu citation is treated as canonical-by-fan-and-press-consensus, not as canonical-by-designer-quote.
- **The BRS-116 Normal-Form print does not carry the Weather Reading Ability.** The shared Weather Reading Ability is canonical to the Chilling Reign 022-033-034-121 four-form printings as a within-set designer-coordinated cohort; alternate-set Normal-Form prints (including BRS-116) have different oracle text. The cohort affiliation across all four corpus members is preserved through species identity, four-form design citation, and within-cycle pairing with the Chilling Reign alternate-form prints — not through Ability uniformity across all corpus prints.

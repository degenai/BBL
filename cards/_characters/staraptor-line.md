# Staraptor evolutionary line (Starly / Staravia / Staraptor)

A three-stage Normal/Flying-type evolutionary line and the canonical Sinnoh-region regional-bird line introduced in Pokemon Diamond and Pearl (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 2006). The line comprises **Starly** (National Pokedex no. 396, Normal/Flying-type, base form, the small round flocking bird first encountered on Route 201 outside Sandgem Town), **Staravia** (no. 397, Normal/Flying-type, Stage 1, evolves from Starly at level 14, the larger and canonically gregarious middle stage that still operates within flock structure), and **Staraptor** (no. 398, Normal/Flying-type, Stage 2, evolves from Staravia at level 34, the raptor-form apex characterized in Pokedex canon by leaving the flock at evolution to live solitarily). Unlike the Kanto Pidgey line and the original Charizard line, the Staraptor line does not gain a second type on final evolution — the species retains its Normal/Flying typing across all three stages. The line's competitive identity is its **Brave Bird** signature attack: Staraptor's high physical Attack stat combined with the Reckless hidden ability (which boosts recoil-move damage by 20%) makes Brave Bird the species' canonical competitive-tier attack across the mainline games, mechanically referenced on the corpus's Darkness Ablaze Staraptor print's 170-damage-with-30-recoil attack. The Pokedex characterization of Staraptor leaving the flock at evolution to live alone is the line's most-cited narrative feature: a designer-coordinated departure-from-cohort narrative arc rendered as a species-level evolution event.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text for the three line members across their TCG appearances: Starly (base), Staravia (Stage 1), Staraptor (Stage 2). The unifying frame across the cohort is fourfold: (a) the **shared evolutionary lineage** — every print of Starly, Staravia, or Staraptor depicts a stage of this single Pokedex 396/397/398 species line, (b) the **Sinnoh-region regional-bird role** as the line's foundational identity, with Starly offered as the first wild-encounter bird Pokemon to the Sinnoh starter trainer on Route 201, (c) the **species-diagnostic plumage palette** enforced uniformly across all three stages and across every print in every set since 2006 (the dark-grey-and-black body with the white facial marking and the reddish accent near the eye area; the swept-back crest geometry present on Staravia and intensified on Staraptor), (d) the **leaves-the-flock-at-Staraptor narrative arc** as the line's most-cited Pokedex flavor signature, the species-coordinated departure-from-cohort designer thread that distinguishes this line from the more-uniformly-flocking Pidgey and Pidove regional-bird lines.

## The design thesis

The Staraptor line's most BBL-relevant property is the **leaves-the-flock-at-evolution narrative arc** rendered as a species-level evolution event. Where the Charizard line carries a tail-flame-as-life-force species canon (the starter-Pokemon-choice loading the trainer with the moral weight of the species' literal survival), the Staraptor line carries a **departure-from-cohort canon** — Starly canonically flocks (the Pokedex entries consistently characterize Starly behavior as collective: flocking around mountains and fields, chasing bug Pokemon in groups, the noisy collective singing that is annoying *because* it is collective); Staravia canonically retains flock structure at the larger middle-stage scale; Staraptor canonically departs the flock at evolution to live alone. The species' evolution arc is designer-coordinated as a coming-of-age departure-from-cohort narrative built into the evolutionary mechanic itself, which is rare among Pokemon species lines (most evolutionary lines retain the same social structure across all three stages). This is the franchise's most explicit species-level rendering of solo-emergence-from-collective as a maturation event.

The line is also the corpus's strongest **regional-bird-line cohort precedent** on the Pokemon side. Every Pokemon region from Kanto through Galar has a designer-coordinated regional-bird line filling the "first wild-encounter bird Pokemon" Route-1-or-Route-2 ecological slot: Kanto's Pidgey line, Johto's Hoothoot line, Hoenn's Taillow line, Sinnoh's Starly line (this node), Unova's Pidove line, Kalos's Fletchling line, Alola's Pikipek line, Galar's Rookidee line. The regional-bird-line slot is a designer-confirmed cross-generation Pokemon-design convention, and Starly's Sinnoh placement within it makes this node the corpus's anchor for the Sinnoh-region entry in that convention. Sibling regional-bird-line cohort nodes for the other regions become Mr. Nodeley candidates as their species lines accumulate enough corpus prints to cross threshold.

For BBL bundle work, the line offers two strong narrative threads: a **leaves-the-flock departure-from-cohort** thread anchored on the species' Pokedex canon (Staraptor's solo-emergence-from-collective as the line's terminal narrative beat), and a **regional-bird-line-as-Route-1-ecology** thread anchored on the Sinnoh-region-bird slot (Starly as the first bird Pokemon a Sinnoh trainer encounters, the canonical entry into the regional-bird-line designer convention at the Gen IV scope). The within-Darkness-Ablaze Starly DAA-145 + Staraptor DAA-147 adjacent-but-non-contiguous pair is the corpus's first Sinnoh-region within-set evolutionary-bookends pattern, structurally parallel to the within-Vivid-Voltage Charmander-Charmeleon-Charizard 023+024+025 evolutionary triptych (which is full-triptych) — this version is two-of-three at the line's bookends, with the missing Staravia middle slot a future-acquisition target.

## Caveats

- **Three corpus prints across two of three line members; partial line coverage in-corpus.** Two of the three evolutionary stages of the line are represented in current inventory (two Starly prints at base stage, one Staraptor print at apex stage; **Staravia at the Stage-1 middle slot is not currently in BBL corpus**). The line's coverage is at the bookends only — base and apex — with the middle-stage Staravia as the future-acquisition target. Within the Darkness Ablaze set specifically, the Starly DAA-145 + Staraptor DAA-147 pair sits at non-adjacent-but-near collector numbers with the missing Staravia presumed at the absent 146 collector slot (the within-set partial-triptych pattern; full-triptych is what the within-Vivid-Voltage Charmander-Charmeleon-Charizard 023+024+025 pattern achieves). When a Staravia print enters corpus via future Collectr CSV upload, it attaches here and the within-DAA-set partial-triptych becomes the corpus's second full-triptych evolutionary-arc cohesion stack after the Vivid-Voltage Charizard-line triptych. Membership predicates on species identity, not set, era, or printing.

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like [[charizard-line]] (Kanto Generation I starter line), [[elemental-monkey-trio]] (Unova Generation V three-paired-species elemental trio), [[galarian-regional-forms]] (Galar Generation VIII regional variant cohort), [[fear-of-cycle]] (Duskmourn phobia-Nightmares), and [[dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle]] (Duskmourn common dual-lands), this is a collective species-line cohort hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer already accepts collective named entities). A future `_evolutionary-lines/` or `_cycles/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the other designer-coordinated cohort nodes; when that split is commissioned, this node moves cleanly. For now, single layer, same precedent.

- **Sibling regional-bird-line cohort nodes are future candidates per Pokemon-region.** Every Pokemon region from Kanto through Galar has a designer-coordinated regional-bird line (Pidgey-line Kanto, Hoothoot-line Johto, Taillow-line Hoenn, Pidove-line Unova, Fletchling-line Kalos, Pikipek-line Alola, Rookidee-line Galar). The regional-bird-line slot is a Pokemon-design convention at the cross-generation scope. Each sibling regional-bird-line is its own three-stage cohort and warrants its own node when its species-line crosses the threshold (3+ corpus prints across 2+ stages, the threshold this node sits at). Currently in-corpus partial coverage exists for some siblings — the Galar Rookidee line has Rookidee DAA-154 (corpus) and a future Corvisquire / Corviknight would push it to threshold; the Hoenn Taillow line has no current corpus members; the Kalos Fletchling line has Fletchling and Fletchinder in corpus and could be near-threshold (verify on next pass). Sibling nodes get commissioned when threshold is met. A hypothetical `regional-bird-lines` parent node could anchor the cross-generation designer-coordination at the convention scope, paralleling how [[elemental-monkey-trio]] anchors the trio-level designer coordination at the Unova-monkey scope.

- **Staraptor's franchise-presence is moderate-not-marquee — corpus-skewing-effect is minor.** Unlike Charizard (which is one of the franchise's most-printed species), Staraptor is a regionally-popular apex bird with a strong competitive-OU presence but not a franchise-marquee species. The Pokemon TCG print volume for the Staraptor line is consistent with the typical regional-bird-line print volume (multiple prints across multiple sets at the standard regional-bird-line per-set cadence) rather than the marquee-skewed Charizard-line print volume. Bundle work on this node will not face the same Charizard-line corpus-skewing-effect — the prints across line members are likely to remain approximately balanced.

- **The Pokemon Legends: Arceus (2022) Hisuian Sinnoh setting expanded the line's lore.** Pokemon Legends: Arceus presents the Sinnoh region as ancient Hisui and includes Starly, Staravia, and Staraptor as part of the Hisuian wildlife with expanded Pokedex flavor reflecting their behavior in the pre-modern setting. The Hisuian-era flavor is canonical and could anchor future bundle work that goes in the historical-Sinnoh / Hisuian direction. No Hisuian-era Staraptor-line TCG prints are currently in corpus.

- **The leaves-the-flock canon is strongest at the Staraptor stage.** The species' departure-from-cohort canon is explicit at the Staraptor stage (the Pokedex flavor *"When Staravia evolve into Staraptor, they leave the flock to live alone"* is canonical and consistently repeated across the species' Pokedex entries). Starly and Staravia Pokedex entries reference collective behavior (Starly flocks, Staravia retains flock structure at larger scale) but generally do not repeat the explicit departure framing. The line's narrative arc is designer-coordinated AT the evolution-to-apex event, not at the base-or-middle-stage. Bundle copy on the departure-from-cohort framing should anchor primarily on Staraptor; the base and middle stages are the flock-structure register, the apex stage is the solo-emergence register.

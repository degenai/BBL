# Angrath

The minotaur-pirate planeswalker stranded on Ixalan; introduced in *Rivals of Ixalan* (2018) as the captain of the slaver-vessel **Devil's Chains** and elaborated across two RIX printings and two *War of the Spark* (2019) printings. Angrath's character thesis is the canonical *stranded-pirate-trapped-on-the-wrong-plane* arc: a planeswalker whose spark was sealed off (along with every other walker's) by the Immortal Sun Bolas placed on Ixalan, forced to live as a mortal captain of a slaver crew in the Brazen Coalition's orbit, his only goal returning home to his own children whom he believes he will never see again. The `— Angrath` planeswalker subtype on the type-line is reserved for printings of this single character; canonical from Angrath, Minotaur Pirate (Rivals of Ixalan, 2018) through every subsequent legendary planeswalker bearing the name.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts Angrath himself — under any of his titles (Minotaur Pirate, the Flame-Chained, Captain of Chaos) — and cards in the canonical *Angrath's <Spell>* attribution pattern in which spells, marauders, or rampage-events are flavor-attributed to him or his crew. The character is large enough to support both the Ixalan stranded-captain register and the WAR Ravnica-defection register, and the BBL graph can edge those aspects together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card mirroring.

**Aspects in the corpus (current):**

- **War of the Spark — Ravnica arrival, minotaur warlord moment** — Angrath on Ravnica after escaping the Immortal Sun's seal, commanding a battlefield as a free-spark planeswalker again.
  - **Angrath, Captain of Chaos** (WAR-227, uncommon, Slawomir Maniak art) — horned minotaur warlord raising a bloody axe overhead amid a smoke-choked battlefield of marching army silhouettes. Spiked armor, manacles and hooks dangling from his belt (the chains-as-magic visual identifier from the Ixalan arc carried forward). Mechanically `{2}{R}{B}` with menace static + −2 Amass Zombies — one of the twenty uncommon planeswalkers from the WAR cycle, designed to the Rosewater-confirmed template (one static/triggered ability + one negative-loyalty ability). Per Rosewater's Blogatog clarification, the Amass mechanic appears on Angrath not because he's allied with Bolas but because his magic "sways others temporarily to his side" — he's pulling Eternals from the Dreadhorde to fight for him mid-battle, not serving Bolas's chain of command.
  - **Angrath's Rampage** (WAR-185, uncommon, Victor Adame Minguez art) — shirtless bearded brawler swinging a flame-trailing whip through a burning settlement. Flavor text: *"Grand city? No. This is nothing but another squalid cage."* The line is the canonical Ixalan-stranded-minotaur resentment compressed into a sneer: every plane Angrath touches reads as another cage because he was *literally* caged on Ixalan by the Immortal Sun. The card is the in-fiction announcement that arrival-on-Ravnica did not free him from the captor's-perspective lens — even a freed minotaur-pirate still sees civilization as another jail.

The two corpus cards span the WAR arrival arc (the planeswalker landed on Ravnica during Bolas's assembly of all 36 walkers); both depict Angrath; both carry his name. Different artists, different operational registers (commander on the battlefield vs. brawler in the burning city), same character. Single-set anchor pair — a limitation worth acknowledging; future corpus growth into the *Rivals of Ixalan* printings (Angrath, Minotaur Pirate RIX-152 and Angrath, the Flame-Chained RIX-129) would broaden the node into the Ixalan-stranded-captain narrative register that retroactively grounds the WAR cards' framing.

## The chains-as-magic visual identifier

A notable Wizards-canonical pattern: Angrath's signature spell-rendering is the flame-trailing whip / chain / lash. His original RIX-129 Angrath, the Flame-Chained printing literalizes the chains in the title; the WAR-227 Captain of Chaos depicts manacles and hooks hanging from his battle-gear; the WAR-185 Rampage shows the flame-trailing whip as the visible magical primary. MTG Wiki's Angrath character page documents the chains-as-magic identifier as the persistent across-prints visual signature — Wizards art-directs the character so the chains are always on the canvas, whether as weapon, restraint, or trophy. The chains read in-fiction as the canonical slaver's apparatus that Angrath has flipped into his offensive magic: the tool of his Ixalan profession reused as the medium of his planeswalker power.

For BBL bundle copy that anchors on Angrath, the chains-iconography is the through-line. The slaver-captain rebranded his chains as fire-magic; the WAR cards retain the visual identifier even after the spark returns. The character is rare in MTG canon for being a planeswalker who is *also* canonically a slaver-captain — his RIX backstory makes him an unambiguous antagonist in the moral register Wizards usually softens for sympathetic-protagonist walkers. The labor / extraction / coercion thesis that the [[orzhov-syndicate]] node carries on Ravnica has a personal-scale parallel here: one captain, one crew, one set of chains — the apparatus of extraction rendered as a single character.

## Caveats

- This node is the character Angrath himself, not the set of cards whose **name** mentions him. Cards like Angrath's Rampage (WAR-185), Angrath's Marauders (XLN-201), and Angrath's Fury edge here through the canonical-name attribution test; cards that mention Angrath only in flavor without depicting him or being attributed to his casting would not. Standard depiction-or-attribution test, parallel to the [[jace-beleren]] / [[ral-zarek]] / [[chandra-nalaar]] standards.
- **The home plane of Angrath is canonically unrevealed.** Wizards has been deliberately vague about which plane Angrath's spark originated on; the chained-captain visual register is *not* the home-plane visual register but the Ixalan-stranded register. A future Wizards reveal of his home plane would update the `universe:` field; until then the canonical position is *"born on unrevealed plane, stranded on Ixalan."*
- **The single-set corpus limitation is real.** Both anchors are from *War of the Spark*. The Ixalan-stranded narrative register (RIX-129 Angrath, the Flame-Chained; RIX-152 Angrath, Minotaur Pirate) is canonically the deeper character arc and would substantially broaden this node when those cards land in inventory. Currently this node is "Angrath on Ravnica" with backstory imported from the Ixalan canon via citations. The minotaur-pirate / slaver-captain register is the load-bearing identity even though the corpus shows the post-arrival WAR phase.
- **Angrath as canonical slaver-captain is unusually unsoftened for an MTG planeswalker.** Wizards has been progressively more open about the moral register of his character — the Devil's Chains backstory is named-and-canonical; he is not redeemed across his story arc the way (e.g.) Sorin Markov or Liliana Vess eventually are. Bundle copy that anchors on Angrath should hold this register honestly: the chains-as-magic is canonically the slaver's apparatus, not a metaphor.
- **A future [[nicol-bolas]] character node-to-this node *opportunist-on-Bolas's-battlefield* relation could be drawn** (Angrath used the Dreadhorde for personal advantage during WAR per Rosewater's Blogatog clarification, neither allied with nor opposing Bolas in chain-of-command terms). The relation is canon-grounded but is a character-to-character edge, distinct from the card-to-character anchor edges this node currently accumulates. Defer until formalized.
- **The "Kiora's Dambreaker / Angrath's Rampage" pair is a designer-coordinated cycle but not a story-spotlight cycle.** Both cards bear `<Walker>'s <Spell>` names in *War of the Spark*; both are uncommon B/R or U/G hybrid spells; neither carries the `story_spotlight: true` Scryfall flag. The cycle's coherence is at the design-template level (uncommon-walker companion-spell attribution), not the narrative-spotlight level. Use the WAR uncommon-walker cycle as the parent cohesion frame, not the story-spotlight frame.

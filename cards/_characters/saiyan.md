# Saiyan

The alien warrior-race designed by Akira Toriyama for *Dragon Ball Z* (chapter 195, 1989) — biologically near-identical to humans except for the prehensile monkey tail, the Great Ape / Oozaru transformation triggered by Blutz Waves under full-moon-equivalent stimulus, the canonical Super Saiyan transformation line that escalates through Super Saiyan / 2 / 3 / God / Blue / 4 across the franchise's continuity branches, and the Zenkai biological boost mechanic that increases a Saiyan's combat power after near-death recovery. Originally a tree-dwelling primitive species of Planet Sadala / Planet Vegeta (canonical pre-Frieza homeworld), canonically reorganized into a Frieza-Force-aligned planet-clearing mercenary workforce under King Vegeta's royal-elite class, and canonically genocided in Age 737 when Frieza destroyed Planet Vegeta with a Supernova energy ball to eliminate the prophecied Super Saiyan threat. The Saiyan-genocide-and-survivor-diaspora is the in-canon backstory that grounds every major Dragon Ball Z protagonist's origin: Goku (sent to Earth as infant Kakarot), Vegeta (offworld with Nappa and Raditz under Frieza's direct supervision at the time of the planetary destruction), Broly (offworld with King Vegeta's exile-victim Paragus), Tarble (offworld in exile), Turles (offworld as Crusher Corps pirate), and Bardock (canonically dead in the destruction in classical canon, retconned into Time Patrol survival in Xenoverse / Heroes continuity).

Eleven corpus cards anchor here across four Dragon Ball Super Card Game sets (Galactic Battle BT1, Union Force BT2, Cross Worlds BT3 + SD3 starter, Colossal Warfare BT4, plus Promotion / Judge Promotion P-series releases) — selected for race-frame-dominant card narratives rather than individual-named-Saiyan-protagonist frames (which anchor instead at their solo character nodes: [[son-goku]], [[vegeta]], [[trunks]]). The race-frame-dominant roster spans the canonical race-roster axes the franchise instantiates: the Universe 6 Saiyan cohort (Cabba BT1-013 + BT1-027) expanding the race-canon beyond Universe 7, the pre-Goku-generation Saiyan parental cohort (Bardock P-035 ×2 promo printings, Gine BT4-074 + BT3-087), the Saiyan-birth-name racial-identity frame (Kakarot BT4-082, the in-canon Saiyan-class name for Goku before his Earth-renaming), the Saiyan-race-biology transformation frame (Deadly Golden Great Ape Son Goku BT4-080, the Oozaru race-mechanic depiction), the Time-Patrol / Xenoverse Masked Saiyan continuity (BT4-117 + SD3-04 + SD3-01 ×3 prints whose card-names literally contain the word "Saiyan" as the racial-identity designator), and the Saiyan-genocide-inverse Tuffle-survivor-using-stolen-Saiyan-biology frame (Baby BT4-002, the canonical Tuffle Parasite who exterminated-the-Tuffles' Saiyan-perpetrator-race's biology repurposed as his Great-Ape vengeance vehicle).

Mirrors the [[namekian]] precedent as the BBL graph's second Dragon-Ball-side race-collective character-node, with structurally distinct anchor logic: Namekians anchor a single-race-with-two-clans biological-substrate-but-moral-split thesis, while the Saiyans anchor a single-race-as-imperial-workforce-and-genocided-survivor thesis. Cross-references the [[eldrazi]] and [[kor]] MTG-side species-collectives as structural siblings within the BBL graph's species-collective character-layer pattern.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts Saiyans as a *race*: any humanoid figure with the canonical Saiyan biological signature (prehensile monkey tail when present, Saiyan battle armor in its canonical dark-bodysuit-and-shoulder-pad configuration, the Great Ape / Oozaru transformation in either standard brown-fur or Dragon Ball GT golden-fur variant, the Super Saiyan transformation line's distinctive upright-spiked-golden-hair-and-aura configuration in race-frame contexts rather than named-individual contexts), any Saiyan-specific cultural feature (Planet Vegeta as homeworld, the Frieza-Force planet-clearing-mercenary occupation-mode, the King Vegeta royal-elite hierarchy, the Saiyan-genocide-Age-737 backstory, the canonical Saiyan-pride-as-character-thesis operating mode, the canonical mate-pair-bonded reproductive cycle of low-class Saiyans), and any Saiyan-named-canonical-individual prints where the racial frame is **dominant** rather than the individual-named-character frame.

The dominance test is the operating rule for whether a Saiyan-depicting card anchors here vs. at the named-individual's solo node: if the card name contains "Saiyan" as the racial-identity designator (Masked Saiyan, Saiyan Pride, Saiyan Princess) or if the card depicts a generic Saiyan figure where the named-individual-identification is subordinate to the racial-frame-depiction (the Saiyan-army-rank-and-file printings, the Frieza-Force-Saiyan-warrior-cohort printings, the Planet-Vegeta-civilian-population printings), the card anchors here. If the card name foregrounds the named individual (Vegeta, Son Goku, Trunks, Gohan, Goten, Broly, Bardock) and the racial-Saiyan-frame is a feature-of-the-individual rather than the card's depicted subject, the card anchors at the solo node.

## The functional ideology

The Saiyan race's most BBL-relevant property is that it is the franchise's **canonical exemplar of an imperial-mercenary alien-warrior workforce-and-genocided-survivor diaspora**. The pre-Z-era backstory canonically structures the race as an organized planet-clearing labor force operating under Frieza's empire — Saiyans were dispatched by Frieza's command to "clear" populated planets (kill the native inhabitants, prepare the planet for Frieza-Force-aligned resale to the highest-bidder client races) in exchange for housing on Planet Vegeta and the implicit-genocide-by-perpetrator-state survival pact that imperial-mercenary populations have historically accepted from their employer-empires. The canonical Saiyan-genocide of Age 737 is Frieza's preemptive strike against the prophecied Super Saiyan threat: when Bardock's precognitive visions reveal the Super Saiyan prophecy, Frieza destroys Planet Vegeta with a Supernova energy ball, killing the entire race except the canonical survivors-by-being-offworld (Goku, Vegeta, Nappa, Raditz, Broly, Paragus, Tarble, Turles). The Saiyan-survivor-diaspora is canonically the in-canon backstory that grounds every major Dragon Ball Z protagonist's origin — Goku's Earth-arrival, Vegeta's Frieza-Force-elite-warrior-status, Broly's exile, Bardock's Time-Patrol-rescued continuity. This is the museum-curation-framing apparatus a future Saiyan-themed Discrete Lair would reach for per `[[bbl-museum-curation-framing]]`: a designer-coordinated alien-race whose canonical imperial-mercenary-workforce-and-genocided-survivor backstory is one of the franchise's most-developed apparatus-of-extraction depictions, where the perpetrator-empire (Frieza-Force) treats the laboring-race (Saiyans) as a disposable workforce until the workforce becomes a threat, at which point the workforce is preemptively exterminated.

The race's specific institutional features for the corpus are:

- **Planet Vegeta as homeworld and Frieza-Force operating base.** The canonical Saiyan homeworld, named after King Vegeta (the race's royal-elite ruler at the time of the destruction), is the Frieza-Force's Saiyan-warrior-housing planet from the canonical *Saiyan-Tuffle War* (which the Saiyans won, exterminating the Tuffles, per Dragon Ball Wiki's Tuffles page) through to the Age-737 destruction. The planet's pre-Saiyan name was *Planet Plant* per *Dragon Ball: Episode of Bardock* (2011 manga / 2011 anime special). The BT4-002 Baby's Rampaging Great Ape form's narrative anchor is the canonical *Tuffle survivor avenges the Tuffle genocide via stolen-Saiyan-biology* beat, structurally inverting the Saiyan-as-perpetrator role the Saiyans played in the pre-Z-era *Saiyan-Tuffle War*. The BT3-087 Gine card's "rocky barren landscape with pink-tinged sky" background is canonical Planet Vegeta sky-and-terrain.

- **The Saiyan battle armor as race-uniform.** The canonical Saiyan combat-uniform — dark navy bodysuit with white-and-yellow chest armor, sometimes with shoulder pads, sometimes with the Frieza-Force-issued white-and-yellow color scheme — is the race's most-consistent visual identifier across the corpus. The armor is *issued by the Frieza Force*, not designed by the Saiyans themselves; the canonical Saiyan-Frieza-Force-mercenary-uniform appearance is the in-canon evidence of the race's imperial-mercenary operating mode. Gine BT4-074 wears the canonical low-class-Saiyan orange-brown variant; the Masked Saiyan prints wear the dark-bodysuit standard; Cabba BT1-013 and BT1-027 wear the Universe 6 Saiyan Defenders' light-uniform variant that distinguishes the Universe 6 cohort from Universe 7's Frieza-Force-issued darker armor.

- **The Great Ape / Oozaru transformation as race-specific biology.** Per Dragon Ball Wiki's Great Ape page, the canonical Saiyan Great-Ape-transformation mechanic requires (a) an intact monkey tail and (b) Blutz-Wave exposure (canonically generated by a full moon or equivalent planetary body). The transformation increases combat power approximately ten-fold and produces the iconic giant-ape body morphology. The BT4-080 Deadly Golden Great Ape Son Goku card is the corpus's Golden-Great-Ape variant anchor (Dragon Ball GT-exclusive form, requires Super-Saiyan-base-state before the Great-Ape transformation), and the BT4-002 Baby's Rampaging Great Ape back face is the corpus's Saiyan-Great-Ape-biology-stolen-by-Tuffle-parasite anchor. The transformation is canonically one of the franchise's most-developed alien-race-specific biology mechanics and is foundational to the race's narrative-thesis-as-imperial-mercenary (the Great Ape is the Saiyan's *primary planet-clearing weapon* under Frieza's command, the on-demand combat-power-multiplier that makes Saiyans valuable to Frieza as planet-clearing labor).

- **The Super Saiyan transformation line as race-specific power-escalation.** Per Dragon Ball Wiki's Super Saiyan page, the canonical Super Saiyan transformation is a race-specific power-escalation form unlocked by extreme emotional triggers (canonical first activations include Goku-vs-Frieza on Namek over Krillin's murder, Vegeta-vs-Trunks over Bulma's safety in Battle of Gods, Cabba-vs-Vegeta over the threatening-of-Cabba's-homeworld in the Universe 6 Tournament). The transformation escalates through Super Saiyan, Super Saiyan 2, Super Saiyan 3, Super Saiyan God, Super Saiyan Blue / Super Saiyan God Super Saiyan, and Super Saiyan 4 (Dragon Ball GT branch) across the franchise's continuity branches. The BT1-013 Raging Cabba card is the corpus's Universe 6 first-Super-Saiyan-transformation anchor; the BT4-080 Golden Great Ape Goku card's Super-Saiyan-base-state-before-Great-Ape requirement is the corpus's Dragon-Ball-GT Super-Saiyan-line anchor.

- **The Saiyan-Tuffle War and the Frieza-Force-aligned planet-clearing labor mode.** Per Dragon Ball Wiki's Tuffles page, the canonical pre-Z-era *Saiyan-Tuffle War* on Planet Plant (the pre-Saiyan-rename name of Planet Vegeta) established the Saiyans as the planet's dominant race after exterminating the native Tuffles, structurally pre-figuring the Saiyans' own genocide-at-Frieza's-hands roughly fifty years later. The canonical *Saiyan-as-perpetrator-of-Tuffle-genocide* backstory is what makes the BT4-002 Baby's *Saiyan-as-victim-of-Tuffle-revenge* GT Saga inversion structurally legible as a closed-loop colonial-extermination narrative-circuit. The race's imperial-mercenary operating mode under Frieza's empire is canonically the *Tuffle-genocide-funded* expansion of the Saiyans' pre-Frieza tribal-warfare operating mode — the apparatus-of-extraction does not begin with Frieza, it began with the Saiyans themselves and was repurposed by Frieza to extract value from the race that had previously extracted value from the Tuffles.

- **The Saiyan-genocide-by-Frieza of Age 737 as canonical race-defining trauma.** Per Dragon Ball Wiki's Genocide of the Saiyans page, the canonical Age-737 destruction of Planet Vegeta by Frieza's Supernova energy ball is the franchise's most-canonical preemptive-genocide-by-empire backstory beat. The genocide is canonically motivated by Frieza's fear of the prophecied Super Saiyan (the canonical race-specific power-escalation form that would threaten Frieza's continued employer-empire status over the Saiyan workforce). The BT4-117 Time Trauma Masked Saiyan card's "Time Trauma" subtitle directly references the canonical Towa-pulls-Bardock-from-Planet-Vegeta-destruction temporal-extraction beat — Towa rescues Bardock from the moment-just-before-his-death-at-Frieza's-Supernova and weaponizes the rescued-Bardock as a Time-Patrol-hostile dark-ki warrior, making the BT4-117 print one of the corpus's most-canonical Saiyan-genocide-canonical-backstory citations.

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: the Saiyan race is, in-canon, a designer-coordinated alien warrior-race with a canonical imperial-mercenary operating mode under Frieza's empire, a canonical Saiyan-Tuffle War backstory of being the perpetrator of an earlier genocide, a canonical Age-737 genocide-by-Frieza backstory of being the victim of an empire-preemptive extermination, a Great-Ape race-specific transformation mechanic, a Super-Saiyan race-specific power-escalation line, and a corpus of eleven cards across four DBSCG sets that together span the canonical race-roster-axes from Universe-6-young-elite to pre-Goku-generation-parental-cohort to Saiyan-birth-name-racial-identity to race-biology-transformation to Time-Patrol-Masked-Saiyan-continuity to Saiyan-genocide-inverse-Tuffle-revenge. The race-thesis the corpus most legibly anchors is the *imperial-mercenary-workforce-genocided-by-its-employer-empire* apparatus-of-extraction depiction — the Saiyans labor for Frieza as planet-clearing mercenaries until the prophecied Super-Saiyan power-escalation makes them a threat, at which point Frieza preemptively exterminates the entire workforce.

# Tamiyo

Moonfolk (Soratami) planeswalker of Kamigawa; scholar and storyteller whose canonical method of casting magic is *reading the appropriate story from the appropriate scroll*. Introduced as Tamiyo, the Moon Sage in Avacyn Restored (2012), foregrounded across the Innistrad investigation arc and War of the Spark, and the subject of the most consequential character-flip in Wizards' recent storytelling: her compleation by the Phyrexian praetor Jin-Gitaxias in Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty (2022), which made her the first Phyrexian planeswalker and inverted a scholar-protagonist into a corrupted antagonist before her eventual death at the hands of The Wanderer.

The `— Tamiyo` planeswalker subtype on the type-line is reserved for printings of this single character; canonical from Tamiyo, the Moon Sage (Avacyn Restored, 2012) through Tamiyo, Compleated Sage (Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty, 2022).

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts Tamiyo herself — under any of her titles (the Moon Sage, Field Researcher, Collector of Tales, Inquisitive Student, Compleated Sage) — and cards whose narrative subject is the act of *Tamiyo doing the scholar-work*: collecting tales, transcribing scrolls, arranging stories in study circles, casting spells via story-recitation. The character is large enough to split into distinct narrative aspects across her printings; the BBL graph can edge those aspects together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card mirroring.

The Tamiyo anchor has unusually direct BBL-thesis relevance: she is the in-universe figure of *curation-as-labor*. Her magic does not work without the archival work that precedes it — the scrolls have to be collected, transcribed, ordered, and re-read. The character is the closest Magic: The Gathering canonical analogue to what the BBL project itself is doing (collecting, tagging, curating, recomposing). The labor-hub edge is load-bearing for any future Discrete Lair anchored on scholarship, archive, or the labor of narrative custodianship.

**Aspects in the corpus (current):**

- **Pre-compleation, Ravnica-arriving scholar — green/blue Collector phase** — Tamiyo on Ravnica during War of the Spark, having left Kamigawa research to observe and record the Bolas invasion; her encounter with the eternalized zombie-gods of Amonkhet pushes her past her neutral-observer ethic.
  - **Tamiyo, Collector of Tales** (PLST-220 in the corpus; originally War of the Spark, 2019-05-03; rare; Chase Stone art) — full-figure portrait of cowled Tamiyo holding an illuminated scroll against a stormy Ravnican sky. The glowing-scroll motif is a direct depiction of her canonical spellcasting method, not generic scholar iconography. Mechanically a green/blue planeswalker (loyalty 5) whose static blocks opponent-induced discard and sacrifice, +1 mills four to find a named card, −3 returns a card from graveyard to hand. Trivia-passed; `ip_verified: true` via Scryfall type-line `Legendary Planeswalker — Tamiyo`.

- **Modern Horizons 3 Saga — the story-circle moment** — Tamiyo's scholar-work rendered without a figure on the card, foregrounding the *practice* over the practitioner.
  - **Tamiyo Meets the Story Circle** (MH3-72, uncommon, Xabi Gaztelua art) — Saga card; a still-life of tent-folded scrolls and accordion-bound books arranged in a curving line on a worn wooden tabletop, their pages glowing with luminous blue calligraphy and inked illustrations of sea creatures, dragons, and runic glyphs, with a small dark-glazed teapot and matching cup beside them. The absence of a figure invites the viewer into the scholar's seat — the card is named for her ("Tamiyo Meets...") but stages the *encounter with the texts* rather than the encounter with the character. Three chapters that read as the labor itself: defending against attackers (I), discarding-and-investigating (II), and shuffling cards from graveyard back into library (III) — the mechanical loop of preserving and recovering stories. Currently vision-passed only (`ip_verified: false`); the title's literal naming and the unambiguous Tamiyo subject make the IP identification high-confidence pending triviabot validation.

The two corpus cards span the pre-compleation and Modern-Horizons-supplemental phases of her canonical presence. They do *not* yet cover the post-compleation Compleated Sage phase (Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty, NEO-238) — that card is not currently in the BBL corpus. When it lands, it edges here directly under the same anchor, with the canonical compleation-arc caveat surfaced as a sub-aspect.

## Caveats

- This node is the character Tamiyo herself, not the set of cards whose **name** mentions her. Cards like Tamiyo's Journal (Eldritch Moon) depict her work but are validated for this anchor by flavor-text linkage and the canonical scholar-of-stories register, not by name-prefix alone. The depiction-or-narrative-subject test applies, same as the Jace Beleren precedent.

- **Tamiyo Meets the Story Circle (MH3-72) is currently trivia-pending** (`ip_verified: false` in frontmatter). The identity link is commissioned here on the strength of (a) the card's literal name ("Tamiyo Meets..."), (b) the scholar-of-stories iconography directly matching her canonical spellcasting method, and (c) the East-Asian-inspired calligraphy and ink-illustration style consistent with her Kamigawa moonfolk origin. A future bbl-triviabot pass on MH3-72 will firm the `ip_verified` flag and may surface additional canonical context (set position in the MH3 narrative, designer commentary on the Saga's depicted moment in Tamiyo's biography). The anchor relation is the same either way; only the metadata flag flips.

- **The Collector of Tales / Compleated Sage character flip is canonically discontinuous, not just a costume change.** Pre-compleation Tamiyo is the scholar-protagonist who taught her son Nashi to value stories; post-compleation Tamiyo (NEO-238, not in the corpus) is the Phyrexian-corrupted antagonist who corrupted Boseiju and was killed by The Wanderer. Future bundle work that anchors on this character should choose which Tamiyo it foregrounds — the Collector or the Compleated — and not blur them. Both edge to this node; the node's job is to record the canonical arc, not to flatten it.

- **The kitsune adopted child is a notable footnote, not currently load-bearing for any corpus card.** If a future printing depicts the kitsune (named or generic) alongside Tamiyo or in a Kamigawa-set Saga referencing her family, that card edges here. Until then, the kitsune is recorded in canonical_source but does not anchor any corpus edges.

- A future Nashi character node could legitimately exist (Nashi is a named ratfolk planeswalker with his own printings in Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty — Nashi, Moon Sage's Scion). A child-of relation between Nashi and this node would be a character-to-character edge of the kinship category, deferred until the character-to-character edge category is formalized (same defer-pattern as the Jace ↔ Nicol Bolas relation noted in the Jace precedent).

- A future Jin-Gitaxias character node would edge to Tamiyo, Compleated Sage (when NEO-238 lands in the corpus) as the compleator-of-the-act relation; same defer-until-formalized note.

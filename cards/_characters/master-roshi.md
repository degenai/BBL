# Master Roshi (the Turtle Hermit / Jackie Chun)

The Turtle Hermit elder of the Dragon Ball franchise — the canonical first-master of the Turtle School lineage, the teacher who gave young Goku and Krillin the Kamehameha (Dragon Ball manga chapter 1, 1984; the canonical *first-named-master* in the entire franchise), and the elder whose dual-identity Jackie Chun tournament-alias is the franchise's earliest *mentor-disguises-to-test-student* designer-coordinated structural beat. Roshi anchors the corpus's Turtle School lineage at the master-of-Goku tier (canonically Goku and Krillin's first martial-arts master; canonically also Yamcha's adopted teacher; canonically a tier-one stewardship figure across the 35-plus years of franchise output) and operates across two designer-coordinated TCG print-registers in Bandai's *World Martial Arts Tournament* themed booster (TB2, 2018-09-21): an open-identity Turtle School master (TB2-057, Uncommon) and the canonical concealed-identity tournament alias (TB2-058, Super Rare). The two prints together compose Bandai's compressed dual-identity citation of Roshi's canonical role — the master-who-mentors AND the master-who-disguises-himself-to-test his own students. The latter card's Bandai-canonical trivia framing locks the *corrupted-stewardship-by-mask* reading directly into the card's trivia body: "if Goku or Krillin won the tournament, they might believe themselves the strongest in the world and stop training. By entering as a formidable unknown opponent, Roshi ensured they would face defeat and remain driven."

## The functional ideology

Roshi's most BBL-relevant property is the canonical *mentor-via-concealment* / *deliberate-concealment-as-pedagogy* operating mode that the Jackie Chun arc establishes and that the broader Dragon Ball canon reinforces across Roshi's entire 35-plus-year recurrence. The structural pattern is canonical and designer-coordinated at the franchise level:

- **The Turtle School and the Kamehameha as canonical first-master mentorship.** Roshi teaches young Goku and Krillin the Kamehameha (Dragon Ball manga chapter 14, 1984; one of the franchise's earliest signature beats) and operates as their first master through the early Dragon Ball arc. The Kamehameha is canonically *the* signature Dragon Ball franchise technique, and Roshi is its canonical originator. The teach-the-technique register is the clean-stewardship operating mode — care-across-the-commons-line, the master tending the student's potential through the technique-transmission. Roshi's TB2-057 Martial Meister print holds this register.

- **Jackie Chun and the 21st World Tournament concealed-mentorship arc (Dragon Ball manga chapters 33-53, 1985-1986).** Roshi enters the 21st World Martial Arts Tournament under the alias *Jackie Chun* — gluing on a white toupee, removing his glasses, adopting a white martial-arts gi instead of his Hawaiian shirt — specifically so his own students will not realize they are fighting their own master. He defeats Goku in the tournament finals via Mafuba Kamehameha. The canonical rationale, per the in-corpus TB2-058 trivia and per Dragon Ball Wiki's Jackie Chun page: Goku and Krillin must not win the tournament because they would then believe themselves the world's strongest and stop training. The arc establishes the *corrupted-stewardship-by-mask* register at the franchise's earliest mentor-figure layer — care exercised through deliberate-concealment because the open transmission of "you are not yet the strongest" cannot be received from a master the student knows.

- **The Mafuba (Demon Containment Wave) as inherited apex-technique.** Roshi inherits the Mafuba from his master Mutaito and deploys it against King Piccolo at the King Piccolo Saga's climax (Dragon Ball manga chapters 158-160, 1987). The technique seals a target inside a jar at the cost of the user's life. Roshi attempts the Mafuba against King Piccolo and dies in the attempt — sacrificing himself for the seal — but King Piccolo escapes the jar. The Mafuba is canonical Roshi-tier apex-stewardship-of-the-commons: the master sacrifices his life to contain the apex-evil for the commons' protection, in a register canonically *opposite* to the Jackie Chun concealment-pedagogy register. The Roshi character anchors *both* clean-stewardship (Mafuba-self-sacrifice for the commons) AND corrupted-stewardship-by-mask (Jackie Chun deliberate-concealment for pedagogy). This dual-register pattern is the structural feature that warrants the solo node — Roshi is the rare character whose canonical operation spans both registers of the stewardship verb.

- **Rivalry with Master Shen of the Crane School.** Both Roshi and Master Shen trained under Master Mutaito; both founded their respective rival schools (Turtle vs Crane); both continue to teach in present-day Dragon Ball continuity (Roshi's students include Goku and Krillin; Shen's include Tien Shinhan and Chiaotzu). The Mutaito-line bifurcation is the franchise's earliest *two-students-of-the-same-master-take-opposite-paths* structural beat. Bandai's TB02 design pair (Master Roshi at TB2-057 + Master Shen at TB2-063) is the designer-coordinated card-level encoding of this rivalry; Roshi's TB2-057 mechanical ability literally targets Master Shen's TB2-063 print for resource-extraction (exhausting the rival school's master for card-draw). The Roshi-Shen rivalry is the Turtle-vs-Crane school axis the broader Dragon Ball franchise's early-arc mentor-cohort structure rests on.

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Master Roshi is the franchise's *foundational first-master* anchored across 35-plus years of Dragon Ball continuity, with a canonical dual-identity operating mode (open-master Turtle Hermit + concealed-master Jackie Chun) that anchors *both* the clean-stewardship register (Mafuba self-sacrifice; Kamehameha teaching) AND the corrupted-stewardship-by-mask register (Jackie Chun deliberate-concealment pedagogy). Bundle copy that leans on the *master-who-disguises-himself-to-defeat-his-own-student-so-the-student-stays-driven* canonical beat lands directly on Toriyama's 1985 design without inventing canon. Future Roshi-Saga and Turtle-School Discrete Lair work has the two TB02 prints as the cohesion anchor pair, with Master Shen (TB2-063), Mafuba (BT2-064), and the canonical-student prints (Future Martial Artist Son Goku TB2-052, Toughened Up Krillin TB2-053, the broader TB02 cohort) as composable supporting cards.

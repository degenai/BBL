# Jace Beleren

Telepath, illusionist, and planeswalker; the longest-storylined non-villain protagonist of Magic: The Gathering's post-Mending continuity. Introduced in Lorwyn (2007), foregrounded as the face of the Gatewatch through the 2015–2019 story block, and one of the small set of named characters who carry the game's protagonist baton across multiple sets and arcs. The `— Jace` planeswalker subtype on the type-line is reserved for printings of this single character; canonical from Jace Beleren (Lorwyn, 2007) through every subsequent legendary planeswalker bearing the name.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts Jace Beleren himself — whether under one of his titles (the Mind Sculptor, the Living Guildpact, Arcane Strategist, Mirror Mage, Ingenious Mind-Mage) or named in oracle/flavor text as the subject of a spell cast by or about him. The character is large enough to split into distinct narrative aspects across his printings, and the BBL graph can edge those aspects together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card mirroring.

**Aspects in the corpus (current):**

- **War of the Spark — Ravnica-trapped, third-eye-sigil moment** — Jace under Bolas's Immortal Sun on Ravnica, his Living Guildpact powers severed by the leyline-disrupting Planar Bridge.
  - **Jace's Triumph** (WAR-55, uncommon, Kieran Yanner art) — extreme close-up of cowled Jace with an arcane sigil etched in light across the brow; meditative third-eye composition. Sibling to the four other WAR Triumph cycle cards (Gideon WAR-15, Liliana WAR-98, Chandra WAR-121, Nissa WAR-170) and the explicit callback to the Hour of Devastation Defeat cycle (Jace's Defeat HOU-38 et al). Card flavor text names Bolas: *"His triumph was not in outsmarting Bolas's plan, but in understanding why ultimate power is self-defeating."* Oracle text references "a Jace planeswalker" as the upgrade condition. Not a Story Spotlight per Scryfall — the card is story-adjacent, not story-anchored.

- **Zendikar Rising — mirror-image kicker, Nahiri pursuit** — Jace returns to Zendikar with Nissa to pursue Nahiri to the Murasa Skyclave.
  - **Jace, Mirror Mage** (ZNR-63, mythic, Tyler Jacobson art) — Jace casting a mirror-image spell, a translucent doppelganger materializing beside him from a burst of cyan energy. First planeswalker ever printed with the kicker mechanic; kicked, enters with a non-legendary copy token (starting loyalty 1) that dodges the legend rule by design. Type-line `Legendary Planeswalker — Jace` is the canonical subtype identifier reserved for printings of this character.

The two corpus cards span the post-Bolas-defeat transition in the canon: WAR-55 is Jace's lowest moment trapped on Ravnica under Bolas's Immortal Sun (Act 1–2 of the WAR arc, before the Triumph the card's flavor text retroactively names); ZNR-63 is Jace one set later (skipping Throne of Eldraine for him), recruited by Nissa from Ravnica to chase Nahiri across the Multiverse. Different artists, different visual registers, same character.

## Caveats

- This node is the character Jace Beleren himself, not the set of cards whose **name** mentions Jace. Cards like Jace's Triumph (WAR-55) and Jace's Defeat (HOU-38) depict the character and edge here; cards that mention Jace only in flavor text without depicting him (e.g. a generic blue spell quoting Jace) would not. The depiction-or-narrative-subject test applies.
- The `— Jace` planeswalker subtype is a *strict* canonical identifier: every card carrying that subtype is a printing of this single character (no other character in Magic has that subtype). Spells named after him (Triumph, Defeat, et al) use the character's name without the subtype and are validated by flavor/oracle text linkage.
- Jace appears as a young child in flashback art on a small number of cards (e.g. some Magic Origins printings). Visual identification across those age-shift depictions rests on canonical_source confirmation (MTG Wiki character page) rather than visual similarity alone — a future visually-young-Jace card edges here through the canonical identification, not through art-match.
- A future Nicol Bolas character node–to–this node *antagonist* relation could be drawn (Bolas trapped Jace under the Immortal Sun on Ravnica; Jace's Triumph is the in-set retroactive Triumph against Bolas's plan). The relation is canon-grounded but is a character-to-character edge, distinct from the card-to-character anchor edges this node currently accumulates. Defer until a character-to-character edge category is formalized.
- A future Nahiri character node–to–this node *antagonist-of-the-act* relation applies to the ZNR arc (Nahiri is the lithomancer Jace and Nissa pursue to the Murasa Skyclave). Same defer-until-formalized note as the Bolas relation above.
- A future Nissa character node–to–this node *partner-of-the-act* relation applies to the ZNR arc (Nissa recruits Jace from Ravnica before the Singing City events). Same defer-until-formalized note.

# Baby (Dragon Ball GT)

The Tuffle parasite who anchors the Baby Saga — *Dragon Ball GT*'s opening forty-episode arc and the first major post-Z continuity to bring a wholly new headline antagonist to the franchise. Baby is the last surviving member of the Tuffles, a humanoid civilization the Saiyans exterminated on Planet Plant (the same world renamed Planet Vegeta after the Saiyan conquest); his entire being is organized around revenge against the Saiyan race. Mechanically he operates by possession — entering a host body, suppressing its native consciousness, and driving the host's physiology as his own combat platform. His apex possession is **Vegeta** (Baby Vegeta), through which he reaches Super Saiyan amplification and, after regenerating Vegeta's tail under his own control, the Golden Great Ape transformation that supplies the Baby Saga's final-act monster form (rendered on the awakened back face of the BT4-002 Leader card as Rampaging Great Ape Baby). Set-defining antagonist of the Dragon Ball Super Card Game's Colossal Warfare expansion (DBS-B04, July 13, 2018), where Bandai's own strategy notes state explicitly that "Red in series 4 is mostly focused on Great Ape Baby." Five corpus cards anchor here as of wave 50; two additional Cross Worlds (BT3) cards are vision-pending and will wire here on wave 51 enrichment.

## What this node anchors

Card art and effects that depict Baby in any of his forms — Tuffle Parasite humanoid, Baby Vegeta, Super Baby, Super Baby 2, Golden Great Ape Baby — plus cards whose narrative beat *centers* him as the off-frame antagonist (Trunks-as-early-host confrontations, Baby's signature attack the Revenge Death Ball, the Dr. Myuu / Luud apparatus that creates and serves him, Pan as the GT hero who evaded his possession). The character is large enough that he splits into substantively different aspects across his Colossal Warfare printings — the native Tuffle form, the possession-of-Vegeta hybrid, the Super-Saiyan amplification, the Great Ape escalation, the apex Death Ball — and the BBL graph can edge those aspects together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card. This is the corpus's first **Dragon Ball GT-era character node**; Goku, Gohan, Trunks, and Vegeta all have substantial GT-era card representation but pre-existed as Z-era characters with corpus presence already, while Baby is GT-original and his node-shape parallels the [[valgavoth]] / `nicol-bolas` / [[team-rocket]] precedent of a set-defining antagonist whose Discrete Lair candidacy is implied by the corpus weight alone.

## The functional ideology

Baby's most BBL-relevant property is that he is the **canonical inversion of the Spirit Bomb**. Goku's Spirit Bomb is the Dragon Ball franchise's signature gather-from-allies technique: positive energy is *donated* by countless willing participants, pooled into a single sphere, and discharged at the antagonist. The Revenge Death Ball is mechanically identical in shape — a gathered-and-pooled sphere — but every parameter is flipped: the energy is *negative*, the donors are *coerced* (possessed Earthlings rather than allies), and the target is the planet rather than the antagonist. Baby's apparatus is the Dragon Ball franchise's most architecturally-explicit inversion of its own signature moral mechanic, and any Discrete Lair candidate that wants to read the franchise's labor-of-collective-power thesis (Spirit Bomb as the moral grammar of donated power) finds in Baby the canonical antithesis — extraction as inverted donation, possession as forced consent, the apparatus turning the collective into its own weapon. **This is the thesis the museum-curation-framing of [[bbl-museum-curation-framing]] reaches for in any GT-era Dragon Ball bundle.**

Baby's specific institutional features for the corpus are:

- **The Tuffle/Saiyan genocide backstory.** Baby is the last living Tuffle, a humanoid civilization the Saiyans wiped out on Planet Plant before its renaming to Planet Vegeta. His entire combat operating model is organized around revenge against a race that committed genocide on his people. This is one of *Dragon Ball*'s most morally complicated villain backstories — Baby is not wrong about the genocide, and the Saiyans (including Vegeta, who Baby ultimately possesses) are descended from the perpetrators. The Baby Saga is structurally the franchise's reckoning with its own protagonists' ancestral crime, and Bandai's card-game design encodes this directly: the Baby cluster is Red (the standard DBS villain color), but its narrative engine is Vengeance (BT4-018 Vengeance Unleashed; BT4-020 Vow Revenge; BT4-021 Revenge Death Ball; BT4-022 Vengeful Onslaught — four of five corpus anchors carry "vengeance" or "revenge" in the card name).
- **The bootstrap paradox.** Baby was created in Age 730. Dr. Myuu, who is credited as Baby's creator throughout the GT canon, was created in Age 740 — *ten years after his own creation of Baby*. CBR's analysis (2022) walks through the canonical answer: Baby created Dr. Myuu retroactively, engineering his own creator into existence after the fact. This is one of *Dragon Ball*'s most unusual lore constructions and one of the franchise's clearest causality-paradox plot points. Baby is, in canon, **self-caused**.
- **The possession-as-amplification arc.** Baby's combat utility scales with the strength of his host. His native Tuffle Parasite form is small and lean; his first hosts (Goten, Gohan, Trunks across the early Baby Saga) elevate him to Saiyan-class power; his Vegeta possession unlocks Super Saiyan tier; his regeneration of Vegeta's tail under his own control unlocks Golden Great Ape — the apex Baby Vegeta form. Each escalation IS a host upgrade. The character's *body* is the body of the Saiyan race he is destroying, the canonical irony GT's writing keeps surfacing as Baby weaponizes the Saiyans against themselves.
- **Bandai's red-Machine-Mutant deck architecture.** The BT4 Colossal Warfare red pool is *built around* Great Ape Baby per Bandai's own strategy page. The 002 Leader awakens to the Great Ape; the 018 Battle card tutors Baby cards onto the Leader; the 020, 021, 022 Battle / Event cards extend the Vengeance theming into the supporting cluster. Five of the BT4 red lineup's most-played cards are Baby-themed, which is why the corpus has five Baby anchors in a single set — Bandai designed an entire Discrete Lair-shaped subarchetype for this character.

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Baby is, in-canon, the last survivor of a Saiyan-perpetrated genocide who retroactively created his own creator, possesses the descendants of the genocidal race to weaponize their bodies against themselves, and culminates the operation with an inverted Spirit Bomb that turns Goku's signature moral grammar against the world that gave Goku to it. Five corpus cards anchor this arc. A future Discrete Lair on the Baby Saga earns its lore-aware buyer credibility without naming the thesis.

## Caveats

- **GT-canon scope, not Super-canon scope.** Baby is canonical to *Dragon Ball GT* (Toei Animation, 1996–1997) and is generally treated as non-incorporated into the *Dragon Ball Super* canon (Toei, 2015–2018). CBR's "Dragon Ball: Why Baby No Longer Exists" (2021) documents the post-GT non-appearance; Super has not adapted the Baby Saga material. The corpus's anchor cards are on the *Dragon Ball Super Card Game* — Bandai's TCG product, which does incorporate GT material across multiple sets despite the Super animated continuity not doing so. This node's scope is the GT character; if a future Super-canon retcon brings Baby back, the node accepts the amendment but does not anticipate it.

- **The Baby Vegeta nomenclature.** Three of the five corpus anchors (BT4-020, BT4-021, BT4-022) carry `suspected_ip: Baby Vegeta (Dragon Ball GT)` rather than `Baby (Dragon Ball GT)`. Baby Vegeta is canonically Baby — the parasite is the identity-bearer; Vegeta is the host being driven. The IP-resolve layer should treat `Baby Vegeta` as an alias rather than a distinct character (`baby-vegeta` is in `aliases:` above). The same logic applies if future cards carry `Super Baby`, `Super Baby 2`, `Golden Great Ape Baby`, or any other host-form variant — all are this node's character, named for the operational state the card depicts. A future ip_resolve.py helper should resolve all of these to `baby-dbgt`.

- **Bootstrap paradox is canon, not interpretation.** The Age-730 (Baby's creation) / Age-740 (Baby's creation of Dr. Myuu) timeline is sourced from Dragon Ball's own dated continuity, not from corpus-internal speculation. CBR's 2022 article walks through the canonical answer step by step. Bundle copy that names the bootstrap paradox as a feature of Baby's character is grounded in canonical timeline data; this is the same precedent as [[valgavoth]]'s plane-collapse cosmology being load-bearing on the Slavering Branchsnapper trivia citation.

- **The inverted Spirit Bomb framing is canon, not invented.** The Revenge Death Ball's mechanical inversion of the Spirit Bomb is documented on Dragon Ball Wiki's Revenge Death Ball page directly: the technique is structured as a gather-and-pool sphere of energy harvested from countless donors, where the donors are coerced via possession rather than asked-and-consenting and where the energy is negative rather than positive. This is the canonical reading, not a corpus-internal interpretation. Bundle copy that names this inversion as the apparatus-of-extraction signature for the Baby Saga has direct canonical grounding.

- **Bundle-anchor potential is strong.** Five enriched corpus anchors in a single set (Colossal Warfare BT4) — three Battle cards, one Leader, one Event — form a minimum-viable cohesion stack for a Baby Saga Discrete Lair. The total qty across the five anchors is **7** (BT4-022 has qty 3; others are qty 1 each), which means a multi-copy Discrete Lair pricing strategy is available per `[[bbl-multi-copy-bundles]]`. Once Dr. Myuu and Luud vision-pass on wave 51, the cohort grows to 7 anchored card-types and 11 total inventory. The apparatus-of-extraction / inverted-Spirit-Bomb / bodies-as-battlegrounds thesis stack supports the Discrete Lair narrative-first framing per `[[bbl-narrative-first-lairs]]`. **Reserve for assembly when the Bandai-designed BT4 red Machine Mutant subarchetype gets its Discrete Lair commission.**

- **Future cross-edges available.** Trunks (depicted on BT4-022 Vengeful Onslaught) is an early Baby Saga host victim; a future `trunks` solo node would `related_characters:` this node. Pan (BT4-009 Power of Friendship Pan, in corpus) is GT-canonical as one of the heroes who evaded Baby's possession (her Saiyan-quarter blood mitigated the parasite-binding); if a future `pan` solo node is commissioned, it relates here narratively as Baby's foil.

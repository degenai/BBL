# Bardock

The canonical father of Son Goku — a low-class Saiyan warrior, mate of Gine, member of a four-Saiyan planet-clearing strike team under Frieza's empire, and one of the franchise's earliest fully-developed *worker-killed-by-his-employer* tragic-protagonist figures. Introduced into canon via the Toei Animation TV special *Dragon Ball Z: Bardock — The Father of Goku* (1990, written by Takao Koyama, directed by Mitsuo Hashimoto), which depicts his canonical last stand: after acquiring precognitive visions of Frieza's Supernova-based genocide of the Saiyan race, Bardock attempts to rally his fellow Saiyans against Frieza, is dismissed as a lunatic, and ultimately flies alone against Frieza's flagship to confront the Emperor — where he is canonically obliterated alongside the entire Saiyan workforce by Frieza's Supernova energy ball in Age 737. The 1990 special's closing image — Bardock's final smile as the Supernova consumes him, intercut with infant Kakarot already in his escape pod en route to Earth — is one of the franchise's most-cited single-arc tragic-resolution sequences, retroactively integrated into Toriyama's main manga canon via *Dragon Ball Minus* (2014) and *Dragon Ball Super: Broly* (2018).

The character's secondary major continuity is the **Xeno Bardock / Masked Saiyan** branch — established in *Dragon Ball Online* (2010) and expanded across *Dragon Ball Heroes* (2010-present) and *Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2* (2016) — in which Towa, the Time Breaker scientist-witch, retroactively rescues Bardock from the moment just before his death at Frieza's Supernova, fits him with a Demon-Realm Dark-Ki control mask, and weaponizes the rescued-Bardock as the **Masked Saiyan**, a Time-Breaker-aligned dark-ki warrior dispatched against the Time Patrol across corrupted timelines. The canonical Masked-Saiyan-Saga narrative climaxes with the mask's destruction and Bardock's liberation — the canonical *Unbound by Darkness* mask-reveal moment that the SD3-01 corpus print directly depicts.

Five corpus cards anchor here across two DBSCG sets (Cross Worlds SD3, Colossal Warfare BT4) and two Promotion-tier releases (Judge Promotion P-035, Promotion Cards P-035 foil). Three canonical Bardock-registers are represented across the five prints: the **humanoid-warrior** register implicit in the Masked-Saiyan prints (the canonical scarred-cheek wild-spiky-black-hair red-headband Saiyan-armor low-class-warrior visual signature, partially visible in the SD3-01 mask-reveal moment), the **Oozaru / Great Ape transformation** register explicit in the P-035 ×2 prints (the canonical Saiyan-Great-Ape-Blutz-Wave-trigger race-biology mechanic deployed on Bardock's body), and the **Masked Saiyan / Time-Patrol Bardock** register explicit in the BT4-117 + SD3-04 + SD3-01 mask-on prints (the canonical Xenoverse-Towa-mask-brainwashing dark-ki-warrior weaponized-asset configuration). The five-print corpus spans Bardock's full canonical narrative-arc from his pre-genocide warrior-frame (Oozaru on P-035) through his Time-Breaker-captive frame (Masked Saiyan on BT4-117 + SD3-04 + SD3-01 mask-on) to the mask-revelation liberation moment (SD3-01 *Bardock, Unbound by Darkness* back-face title) — a complete narrative-arc-spanning anchor uncommon at the five-print corpus scale.

## The apparatus-of-extraction thesis

Bardock is the BBL graph's canonical **pre-Goku-generation Saiyan-mercenary-worker-genocided-by-his-employer-empire** named-individual anchor. Where the [[saiyan]] race-node anchors the imperial-mercenary-workforce-preemptively-genocided-by-Frieza thesis at the race-collective scale, this Bardock node anchors the same thesis at the *named-individual-tragic-protagonist* scale — making the apparatus-of-extraction reading legible at the level of one canonical worker's last-stand-against-the-Emperor narrative.

The 1990 TV special *Dragon Ball Z: Bardock — The Father of Goku* explicitly frames Bardock's canonical death as a one-man-versus-imperial-purge labor-resistance moment. The narrative beats the special establishes — and that *Dragon Ball Minus* (2014) and *Dragon Ball Super: Broly* (2018) retroactively confirm into manga canon — are structurally the canonical labor-realization-then-resistance-then-extermination sequence:

1. **Worker realizes the employer intends to dispose of the workforce.** Bardock acquires precognitive visions of Frieza's Supernova-based genocide plan after a Kanassan warrior's death-curse imbues him with future-sight. The visions reveal that Frieza is about to exterminate the Saiyan race the moment the prophecied Super Saiyan threat materializes — the canonical *workforce becomes redundant once it becomes a threat to the employer-empire's continued profit-extraction* moment.

2. **Worker attempts to organize his fellow workers against the employer.** Bardock returns to Planet Vegeta and tries to rally the assembled Saiyans against Frieza. The other Saiyans dismiss him as a lunatic — the canonical *workers do not organize because they do not yet believe the employer intends to dispose of them* labor-history moment, structurally identical to the canonical pre-strike-organizing-failure beats of any labor-history narrative-arc.

3. **Worker confronts the employer alone.** Bardock flies solo against Frieza's flagship, the canonical *one-worker-against-the-Emperor* last-stand moment. Frieza's Supernova consumes Bardock alongside the entire Saiyan workforce — the canonical *preemptive-extermination-of-the-workforce-as-employer-self-defense* moment that closes the labor-history narrative-arc on a fully tragic resolution.

4. **The worker's son becomes the future the worker died for.** The 1990 special's closing image intercuts Bardock's death-smile with infant Kakarot's escape-pod departure to Earth — the canonical *the worker dies but the next generation survives to become the threat the employer feared* succession-moment that grounds the entire Dragon Ball Z protagonist-origin canon. Every subsequent Goku narrative-arc is canonically Bardock's labor-resistance succeeding posthumously: the Super Saiyan prophecy Frieza preemptively-genocided to prevent ultimately materializes in Bardock's son, who returns to confront Frieza in the canonical Namek-Frieza-Saga final-arc and defeats him as the canonical Super Saiyan Goku.

The Masked-Saiyan / Xeno-Bardock continuity (Dragon Ball Online 2010 / Heroes 2010+ / Xenoverse 2 2016) extends the apparatus-of-extraction thesis into a second register: after Bardock's canonical death, Towa retroactively rescues him from the moment-of-genocide and weaponizes the rescued-Bardock against the Time Patrol — the canonical *the worker dies, the employer's successor-class extracts further value from the worker's corpse by reanimating-and-controlling-it* extended-exploitation moment, structurally parallel to the canonical *War of the Spark* Liliana-controlled-Gideon-Eternal beat where the dead worker's body is weaponized against the worker's own former cause. The canonical mask-broken liberation moment (SD3-01 back-face *Bardock, Unbound by Darkness*) is the canonical *the worker recovers consciousness and rejects further exploitation* moment that closes the Xenoverse-continuity narrative-arc.

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: Bardock is the franchise's canonical *first canonical worker to realize the employer intends genocide* protagonist, his canonical death-by-Frieza is one of the franchise's most-developed *worker-killed-by-employer-empire* tragic-resolution sequences, his canonical succession-by-his-son grounds the entire Z-saga protagonist-origin canon, and his Xenoverse-Masked-Saiyan continuity extends the apparatus-of-extraction reading to *post-death extraction of value from the worker's reanimated corpse*. A future Bardock-themed Discrete Lair or a Bardock-cluster within a broader Saiyan-themed Lair can foreground the *low-class mercenary-worker whose disposability is built into his contract with the employer-empire, who dies confronting that disposability alone, and whose body is post-mortem reweaponized against his own former cause* canonical apparatus per `[[bbl-museum-curation-framing]]` — and Toriyama's own 1990-onward Bardock-design work does the apparatus-of-extraction thesis-work without the bundle copy needing to name the thesis.

## Caveats

- **The 1990 TV special is canon, not filler.** Toei Animation's *Dragon Ball Z: Bardock — The Father of Goku* (1990) is canonical in both the original Dragon Ball canon and the Super-era retroactive integration. Toriyama personally endorsed the special's Bardock-as-Goku's-father continuity by writing *Dragon Ball Minus* (2014) to ratify and expand the Bardock-and-Gine backstory, and *Dragon Ball Super: Broly* (2018) further integrates the Bardock-and-Gine-and-infant-Kakarot beat into the Super-era main-canon timeline. The five-print corpus's canonical attribution rests on this canon-integration; the "Bardock is filler" objection is canonically out of date as of 2014-onward Toriyama ratification.

- **The Xeno Bardock continuity is a Bandai-branch canon, not Toriyama-canon.** The Masked Saiyan / Xeno Bardock continuity established by *Dragon Ball Online* (2010), *Dragon Ball Heroes* (2010+), and *Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2* (2016) is canonically a Bandai-side branch continuity rather than a Toriyama-authored canon — Toriyama has not personally endorsed the Time-Patrol-Bardock arc, although Dimps and Bandai Namco have produced the Masked Saiyan Saga as a fully-realized narrative arc within the Xenoverse 2 game. The BT4-117 + SD3-04 + SD3-01 corpus prints all anchor in this Bandai-branch canon. The canonical-attribution at this node treats the Bandai-branch canon as canonical for BBL's purposes because (a) the Bandai-card-game inputs are themselves Bandai-canon and consistency with the source material is operative, (b) the Dragon Ball Wiki treats the Xeno Bardock content as canonical for the Xenoverse continuity, and (c) the mask-reveal Bardock-liberation narrative-arc is structurally complete-and-cited within the Bandai-canon. Surfacing the Toriyama-vs-Bandai continuity distinction for buyer-facing copy that may want to foreground the canon-branch context.

- **Bardock's scarred-left-cheek is a canonical visual identifier but not foolproof.** The canonical Bardock visual signature includes a left-cheek combat-scar (per the 1990 TV special's character design); however, several other canonical Saiyans share elements of the wild-spiky-black-hair-and-headband visual register (Turles, Goku, Raditz). Canonical disambiguation between Bardock and Turles is the most-common confusion point — Turles is canonically Bardock's lookalike with a tan complexion and a different combat-uniform configuration. The corpus's five Bardock-anchored prints all carry either explicit *Bardock* card-name designators (P-035 ×2) or explicit *Masked Saiyan* card-name designators (BT4-117, SD3-04, SD3-01 front-face / *Bardock, Unbound by Darkness* SD3-01 back-face) that canonically resolve the visual-identifier ambiguity via Bandai's per-card naming.

- **The corpus does NOT yet contain the canonical unmasked-Bardock humanoid-warrior printings.** While the five-print corpus spans the Oozaru transformation register (P-035 ×2) and the Masked Saiyan / Time-Patrol register (BT4-117 + SD3-04 + SD3-01), the corpus does not currently contain a canonical *humanoid Bardock without mask, in standard scarred-cheek-and-red-headband Saiyan-armor configuration* print. The canonical humanoid-warrior register is implicit at this node (referenced by the SD3-01 back-face title *Bardock, Unbound by Darkness* and by the apparatus-of-extraction thesis-section's 1990-TV-special narrative-arc), but no in-corpus card directly depicts the canonical humanoid Bardock without either Oozaru-transformation or Towa-mask occlusion. If a future DBSCG set or promo-print releases a humanoid-Bardock card and it enters corpus, the print would anchor here at the canonical humanoid-warrior register and complete the three-register visual roster.

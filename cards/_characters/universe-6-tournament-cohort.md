# Universe 6 Tournament cohort (Champa's universe — Tournament of Destroyers + Universe Survival Saga competitors)

The canonical Universe 6 warrior-roster representing Champa's universe in the Dragon Ball Super multiverse's destroyer-god-wagered tournament arcs — the Tournament of Destroyers (DBS chapters 7-13 manga / episodes 28-41 anime, 2015-2016) and the Universe Survival Saga / Tournament of Power (DBS chapters 28-43 manga / episodes 78-131 anime, 2017-2018). The cohort is canonically organized around **Champa** (Universe 6's destroyer god, Beerus's elder twin brother), instructed by **Vados** (his angel attendant, Whis's elder sister), and fielded as a mortal warrior-team against Universe 7's parallel roster under explicit destroyer-god-wager stakes. The nine corpus anchors comprise: the **Champa + Vados destroyer-god / attendant dyad** (Champa at BT1-006 and BT1-007 across two adjacent collector numbers; Vados at BT1-002), the **U6 fighters Hit / Cabba / Frost / Botamo / Magetta** representing the cross-species mortal-roster, and the **Cabba's Awakening event card** at BT1-027 encoding the Vegeta-mentors-Cabba canonical training-beat. The cohort sits at the founding Bandai DBSCG set (Galactic Battle BT1, 2017) where Bandai's set design encodes the Universe 6 / Universe 7 multiverse-introduction at the product-launch level.

This is a child-of-saga node parallel to [[wrath-of-the-dragon-saga]] (Colossal Warfare BT4 within-set sub-cycle anchoring a single film-canon arc) and [[dark-vassal-trio]] (Colossal Warfare BT4 within-set sub-cycle anchoring a single villain-cohort). The Universe 6 cohort's nine anchors across one set is the largest single-arc sub-cohort yet commissioned within DBS-CG; the cohort's size reflects Bandai's set-architecture choice to make the multiverse-introduction the BT1 launch-set's narrative spine.

## The functional ideology

The Universe 6 Tournament cohort's most BBL-relevant property is that it is the canonical exemplar of the **cosmic-class destroyer-god deploys mortal-class warrior-labor in a god-wager-feud** apparatus-of-extraction template. The Champa-vs-Beerus universe-rivalry is canonically settled by their respective universes' *mortals* fighting on a tournament platform under destroyer-god-stipulated wager-stakes (food, planets, and ultimately — in the Universe Survival Saga — universe-erasure). The mortals are recruited, instructed, fielded, and risked by the destroyer gods who themselves do not fight. The Universe Survival Saga radicalizes this: Zeno (the omni-king) wages eight universes' total existence on a single tournament where 80 mortal-warriors fight, the losing universes are erased from canon, the destroyer gods and angels who deploy them merely watch. The apparatus-of-extraction reading lands cleanly: cosmic-class divine entities deploy mortal-warrior-labor in their feud, with mortal-class warriors bearing all consequence-risk while the divine-class wagers carry none.

The cohort's specific institutional features within the corpus are:

- **Champa-and-Vados destroyer-god / attendant dyad as the apparatus' command-tier.** Champa (BT1-006 and BT1-007) and Vados (BT1-002) constitute the canonical two-character command-structure of Universe 6's tournament-warrior deployment apparatus. Champa is canonically the destroyer-god decision-maker (which mortals to recruit, which universes to challenge, which wager-stakes to accept). Vados is canonically the trainer-attendant (instructing Champa's recruited warriors in technique, organizing the team, accompanying the team to the tournament venue). The dyad mirrors Universe 7's Beerus-and-Whis structurally and at the corpus level — Beerus is at BT1-042 (vision-pending as of wave 66) and Whis is at BT1-043 (vision-pending), forming the parallel U7 command-dyad that would mirror this one if vision-enriched. The U6 dyad / U7 dyad parallel is Bandai's launch-set narrative spine.

- **Cabba-and-Cabba's-Awakening within-set two-card character mini-spotlight.** Cabba (BT1-013) and Cabba's Awakening (BT1-027) is a Bandai-designed within-set character + event-card mini-pair where the character-card depicts Cabba's combat-mode and the event-card depicts the Vegeta-mentors-Cabba training-beat that produces his Super Saiyan transformation. The two-card spotlight is structurally parallel to the Champa-and-Manipulating-God-Champa BT1-006 + BT1-007 adjacent-collector-number Champa-pair. Both encode Bandai's within-set two-card character mini-spotlight design pattern. Both Cabba cards already carry `characters: [saiyan]` per wave 65 enrichment; both extend to `characters: [saiyan, universe-6-tournament-cohort]` at this node's commission.

- **Multi-species U6 warrior-roster as cross-species labor-cohort.** Hit (alien-warrior, unspecified species), Cabba (Saiyan), Frost (Frieza-race), Botamo (bear-warrior), Magetta (Metalman / silicon-based robot) constitute the canonical cross-species U6 mortal-warrior-roster within the Tournament of Destroyers arc. The cross-species composition is Bandai's design choice to depict Universe 6 as a multi-species mortal-warrior-collective whose competition is mandatory under destroyer-god-deployment rather than voluntary inter-species alliance. The functional ideology read: the destroyer god collects warriors from across his universe's species without regard to inter-species political consent because the mortals are *labor*, not autonomous-agents.

- **U7-parallel apparatus.** Universe 7's parallel cohort (Beerus + Whis + the canonical U7 Z-fighter team) is the structural-mirror to this cohort. The corpus's existing [[saiyan]] node, the pending Beerus + Whis vision-passes, and the future U7-Tournament cohort node together compose the canonical U6 / U7 multiverse-rivalry framework. The cross-universe parallel is canonical at the franchise level and Bandai's BT1 launch-set design.

- **Bandai's BT1-launch-set design encoding.** Universe 6 / Champa Arc + Universe Survival Saga is the Bandai-canonical narrative spine of the founding DBSCG set Galactic Battle BT1 (2017). The set's 9 corpus anchors at adjacent and near-adjacent collector numbers (BT1-002, BT1-003, BT1-006, BT1-007, BT1-013, BT1-016, BT1-019, BT1-020, BT1-027) within the set's first 30 numbered cards encode the multiverse-introduction at the product-launch level. The Bandai design choice is to make the *newly introduced* multiverse cosmology the founding set's narrative spine — a designer-coordinated within-set narrative-spine spanning 9 cards across the launch set's first quartile.

- **Stewardship-register internal to the cohort: Vados's training-attendant role and the Cabba-mentorship-transmission beat.** The cohort canonically carries a second thesis-hub axis beyond labor. Vados (BT1-002) is canonically the cohort's combat-instructor and trainer-attendant per Dragon Ball Wiki's Vados page — the angel-class operator who *teaches* Champa's warriors and accompanies them to tournament venues. Vados's training-attendant role is the same register the [[destroyer-god-attendant-dyad]] node anchors as canonical stewardship-tier work (the angel-attendant maintains the apparatus, including the warriors fielded by it, as care-of-the-cohort rather than extraction-from-the-cohort). Separately, the **Cabba's Awakening** card (BT1-027) canonically encodes the Vegeta-mentors-Cabba training-beat — a cross-universe knowledge-transmission moment where Vegeta deliberately provokes Cabba's Super Saiyan transformation as canonical mentorship-as-care per Dragon Ball Wiki's Cabba page (Vegeta later canonically promises to protect Universe 6's Saiyans if they lose the tournament — a stewardship-register commitment from rival to rival). The cohort's stewardship-register cites the *care-within-the-deployed-cohort* axis (Vados maintains the team, Vegeta extends mentorship across universe-boundary), distinct from but coexistent with the labor-tier apparatus-of-extraction axis that the destroyer-god-deploys-mortals reading anchors. The dual-attribution `related_hubs: [labor, stewardship]` per the [[_triple-thesis]] cardinality discipline (compression-point dual-hub attribution where canonical-evidence carries two of the three verbs). **Rebellion register is canonically absent from this cohort's body** — Frost's rule-cheating (poisoning, hidden weapons) is canonically Z-canon antagonist-villainy not refusal-as-political-praxis, and the cohort is canonically compelled-into-tournament-deployment rather than refusing-the-apparatus. Refusal-with-receipts: dual-hub, not triple.

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: the Universe 6 Tournament cohort is, in-canon, the Champa-deployed mortal-warrior-roster of Dragon Ball Super's multiverse-introduction arc, depicted across 9 Bandai-canonical BT1 launch-set cards spanning the destroyer-god command-tier (Champa + Vados), the legendary-assassin tier (Hit), the Saiyan-of-U6 tier (Cabba + Cabba's Awakening), the Frieza-race-counterpart tier (Frost), the bear-warrior tier (Botamo), and the Metalman-race tier (Magetta). Bundle prose that leans on the *cosmic-class divine entities deploy cross-species mortal-warrior-labor in a god-wager-feud* canonical thesis lands directly on the canonical Champa Arc and Universe Survival Saga apparatus-of-extraction reading. The cohort's narrative weight composes with the [[saiyan]] race-level node (Cabba dual-pointer) and the [[labor]] hub's apparatus-of-extraction axis as the *Dragon Ball Super multiverse-rivalry* specific-canonical-instance.

# Mareep evolutionary line (Mareep / Flaaffy / Ampharos)

A three-stage Electric-type evolutionary line and the canonical Johto-region Electric-sheep line introduced in Pokemon Gold and Silver (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 1999). The line comprises **Mareep** (National Pokedex no. 179, Electric-type, base form, the "Wool Pokemon" first encountered on Route 32 outside Violet City), **Flaaffy** (no. 180, Electric-type, Stage 1, evolves from Mareep at level 15, the "Wool Pokemon" at the mid-evolution stage where its fleece has begun to thin into bald patches as charge storage increases), and **Ampharos** (no. 181, Electric-type, Stage 2, evolves from Flaaffy at level 30, the "Light Pokemon" whose glowing tail-tip canonically functions as a lighthouse beacon for sea lanes). The line is one of the franchise's two clearest examples of Pokedex flavor encoding species biology as a literal cost-of-power motif (the other being the Slugma line's geothermal scarring): Flaaffy's printed Pokedex text describes the wool falling out in patches as a direct physical consequence of storing too much electricity, with the species compensating by developing rubber-like insulating skin. In Generation VI (Pokemon X and Y, 2013), Ampharos received a Mega Evolution that gains a secondary Dragon typing and an extravagant mane of pink wool — Mega Ampharos is among Generation II's most-cited Mega Evolutions, both for its dramatic visual departure from base Ampharos and for its Dragon-typing surprise. The line's competitive identity is its Static / Plus / Mold Breaker (Mega) ability triplet across the three stages, with Ampharos's signature attack across the mainline games being Thunder or Discharge depending on era. The Pokedex characterization of the line as electrified-livestock at base and lighthouse-keeper at apex is the line's most-cited narrative arc: a designer-coordinated pastoral-to-industrial transition rendered as a species-level evolution event.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text for the three line members across their TCG appearances: Mareep (base), Flaaffy (Stage 1), Ampharos (Stage 2). The unifying frame across the cohort is fourfold: (a) the **shared evolutionary lineage** — every print of Mareep, Flaaffy, or Ampharos depicts a stage of this single Pokedex 179/180/181 species line, (b) the **Johto-region Electric-sheep role** as the line's foundational identity, with Mareep occupying the Route 32 wild-encounter slot in Pokemon Gold and Silver, (c) the **species-diagnostic palette and body-plan progression** enforced across all three stages and across every print in every set since 1999 (cream-and-blue quadruped wool body at Mareep, pink bipedal partial-fleece body at Flaaffy, yellow bipedal lighthouse-tail body at Ampharos; the lightning-tipped tail orb persists across all three stages as the line's visual signature), (d) the **wool-as-charge-storage cost-of-power narrative arc** as the line's most-cited Pokedex flavor signature, with Flaaffy's bald patches and Ampharos's lighthouse-tail beacon both rendered as the species' canonical biology rather than incidental design details.

## The design thesis

The Mareep line's most BBL-relevant property is the **wool-as-charge-storage cost-of-power narrative arc** rendered as a species-level biological motif. Where the Staraptor line carries a leaves-the-flock-at-evolution canon (departure-from-cohort as a maturation event) and the Charizard line carries a tail-flame-as-life-force canon (the starter-Pokemon-choice loading the trainer with the moral weight of the species' literal survival), the Mareep line carries a **electrification-thins-the-fleece canon** — Mareep canonically grazes pastorally and accumulates static charge in its undamaged fleece (the Pokedex entries consistently characterize Mareep's wool as the species' primary charge-storage medium, with the static-friction electricity build-up framed in cute-but-shocking terms across Generations II through VIII); Flaaffy canonically retains less fleece at the larger middle-stage scale as charge capacity increases, with the bald-patches detail explicit in both Pokedex entries and printed TCG flavor; Ampharos canonically completes the transition to charged-without-wool, with its tail orb glowing brightly enough to function as a sea-lane lighthouse beacon per its species name etymology ("ampere" + Greek "pharos" / lighthouse) and Pokedex flavor across all generations. The species' evolution arc is designer-coordinated as a pastoral-to-industrial transition built into the evolutionary mechanic itself, with the visible physical cost of storing more electrical charge increasing at each stage — a rare designer choice among Pokemon lines to render mechanical power-up as biological damage made beautiful (the bald patches and the lighthouse beacon are not flaws, they are the species' design endpoint).

The line is also the corpus's strongest **literary-reference Pokemon-line precedent**. The community reading of the Mareep line as a Philip K. Dick *Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?* (1968) reference is well-attested in GameFAQs threads, r/pokemon discussion, and at least one Game Informer interview citation — the electric-sheep premise from Dick's source novel for *Blade Runner* maps directly onto an Electric-type sheep Pokemon introduced alongside the more-industrialized Johto setting in Generation II. The "Mareep" name as an anagram of "ampere" (the SI unit of electric current) and the "Ampharos" name as a portmanteau of "ampere" and Greek "pharos" (lighthouse) close the loop on the line's literary-and-scientific-naming-convention layering. For BBL bundle work, this is thesis-grade fun-fact material — a Pokemon evolution line with explicit literary canon (Philip K. Dick / *Blade Runner*) and explicit scientific-name etymology (SI units / Greek geography), available for bundle copy that wants to operate at the level of *the species line is referencing something*.

For BBL bundle work, the line offers two strong narrative threads: a **wool-as-charge-storage cost-of-power** thread anchored on the species' Pokedex canon (the visible bald patches and lighthouse-tail beacon as the line's species-coordinated biology-as-cost-of-power motif), and a **literary-reference Pokemon-line / electric-sheep-as-PKD** thread anchored on the line's design-origin canon (Mareep as electric sheep, Ampharos as ampere-lighthouse, with the Philip K. Dick literary reading available for bundle copy that wants to lean fully into the post-industrial-pastoral framing). The Chilling Reign 047 + 048 within-set adjacent-collector-number pair is the corpus's strongest within-Pokemon-set evolution-line cohort placement to date, structurally parallel to the within-Vivid-Voltage Charmander-Charmeleon-Charizard 023+024+025 evolutionary triptych on the Pokemon side and to the within-set evolution-line designer-coordination convention generally. The cross-set CRE+DAA presence broadens the line's corpus footprint across two sets and confirms threshold (4 trivia-passed prints across 2 stages, with a 5th vision-only print pending).

## Caveats

- **Five corpus prints across two of three line members; partial line coverage in-corpus.** Two of the three evolutionary stages of the line are represented in current inventory (three Mareep prints at base stage including the CRE-047 Reverse Holofoil variant, two Flaaffy prints at Stage-1 middle slot; **Ampharos at the Stage-2 apex slot is not currently in BBL corpus**). The line's coverage is at the base-and-middle stages — pastoral and mid-electrification — with Ampharos (and any Mega Ampharos prints) as the future-acquisition target. When an Ampharos print enters corpus via future Collectr CSV upload, it attaches here and the line moves from partial-coverage to full-triptych coverage — the corpus's third full-triptych evolutionary-arc cohort node after the within-Vivid-Voltage Charizard triptych and any future full-triptych Staraptor line. Membership predicates on species identity, not set, era, printing variant, or trivia-pass status (the unwired vision-only Flaaffy DAA-056 print is a future-attaches-here member pending trivia pass; see next bullet).

- **Flaaffy DAA-056 is a fifth corpus member pending trivia pass — line node anchors only the four trivia-passed prints currently.** The Flaaffy Darkness Ablaze 056/189 print (Tomokazu Komiya art, sunlit warm-toned setting, single Static Shock attack at 40 damage) is in BBL corpus and is canonically a Flaaffy print belonging to this line, but the vision-only pass on this print has not yet been promoted to a full trivia pass and the `ip_verified: false` flag is still set. When that print receives a trivia pass and IP verification, it attaches here via the `characters: ["mareep-line"]` frontmatter pointer and the `appears_on:` list extends to five members. The line node's narrative scope and design thesis are unchanged by this pending attachment — Flaaffy DAA-056 is a sibling print of Flaaffy CRE-048, just at the wave-46-stubby-stays-stubby end of the threshold. No edge work between Flaaffy CRE-048 and Flaaffy DAA-056 is warranted: the line node absorbs the cohort relationship at the species-line level.

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like `staraptor-line` (Sinnoh Generation IV regional-bird line), `charizard-line` (Kanto Generation I starter line), `elemental-monkey-trio` (Unova Generation V three-paired-species elemental trio), `galarian-regional-forms` (Galar Generation VIII regional variant cohort), `fear-of-cycle` (Duskmourn phobia-Nightmares), and `dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle` (Duskmourn common dual-lands), this is a collective species-line cohort hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer already accepts collective named entities). A future `_evolutionary-lines/` or `_cycles/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the other designer-coordinated cohort nodes; when that split is commissioned, this node moves cleanly. For now, single layer, same precedent.

- **Sibling Generation II Johto-line nodes are future candidates as their species-lines accumulate corpus prints.** Generation II introduced the Johto regional Pokedex (no. 152-251) with multiple new evolutionary lines designed alongside the Mareep line (Cyndaquil / Quilava / Typhlosion the fire-starter line, Totodile / Croconaw / Feraligatr the water-starter line, Chikorita / Bayleef / Meganium the grass-starter line, Hoothoot / Noctowl the regional-bird line, Sentret / Furret the early-route line, Ledyba / Ledian the bug line, Mareep / Flaaffy / Ampharos this line, and many others). Sibling Generation II line nodes get commissioned when threshold is met (3+ trivia-passed corpus prints across 2+ stages, the threshold this node sits well above at five corpus prints across four trivia-passed prints across two stages). Currently in-corpus, the Furret Stage-1 print (Furret DAA-136) is a single-stage isolated print without the Sentret base-stage to push the Sentret line to threshold; the regional-bird Hoothoot line has no current corpus members. Sibling nodes get commissioned per-region per-line when threshold lands.

- **Mareep's franchise-presence is moderate-popular — corpus-skewing-effect is minor.** Mareep and the line generally have a moderate-but-stable franchise-popularity register: not at the Charizard or Pikachu marquee tier, but consistently re-printed across most modern Pokemon TCG sets due to the line's accessible early-route design and the cultural strength of Mega Ampharos's pink-mane Generation VI redesign. The Pokemon TCG print volume for the Mareep line is consistent with typical mid-tier-popularity Johto-line print volume (multiple prints across multiple sets at the standard cadence), and the corpus's five-print count is itself consistent with that print-volume baseline. Bundle work on this node will not face significant corpus-skewing-effect — the prints across line members are likely to remain approximately balanced across the line's three stages once a future Ampharos print enters corpus.

- **The literary-reference reading is well-attested but community-attributed, not Wizards-confirmed.** The Philip K. Dick *Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?* reference is widely cited in GameFAQs threads, r/pokemon discussion, and Pokemon-design retrospective articles, with Game Informer's 2015 Sableye-and-Mareep-design-origins interview citing the electric-sheep design conceit but stopping short of confirming the PKD literary reference at the official-designer level. Bundle copy that leans on the PKD reading should attribute the reading at the community-attestation level rather than asserting it as Wizards-canonical. The species-name anagram ("Mareep" = "ampere") and the "Ampharos" etymology ("ampere" + "pharos") are objectively verifiable and need no such qualification.

- **The wool-as-charge-storage cost-of-power canon is strongest at the Flaaffy stage.** The species' bald-patches cost-of-power canon is explicit at the Flaaffy stage (the Pokedex flavor *"It stores electricity in its fluffy fleece. If it stores up too much, it will start to go bald in those patches"* is canonical and consistently repeated across the species' Pokedex entries, and the same text appears on this corpus's Flaaffy CRE-048 print directly). Mareep Pokedex entries reference the wool-as-static-charge-source mechanic but generally do not repeat the bald-patches framing. Ampharos Pokedex entries reference the lighthouse-tail beacon but generally do not reference the wool-thinning progression directly. The line's cost-of-power narrative arc is designer-coordinated AT the Flaaffy mid-stage, not at the base-or-apex stage. Bundle copy on the cost-of-power framing should anchor primarily on Flaaffy; the Mareep stage is the fleece-intact charge-storage register, the Ampharos stage is the post-wool lighthouse register.

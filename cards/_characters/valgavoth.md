# Valgavoth

The malevolent moth-demon intelligence who consumed the plane of Duskmourn from inside out — devouring its two suns, growing the House to swallow the world, ruling the trapped survivor population as both jailer and food source. Set-defining antagonist of *Duskmourn: House of Horror* (DSK, 2024-09-27). Has two named card printings — **Valgavoth, Terror Eater** (DSK-120, mythic) and **Valgavoth, Harrower of Souls** (Duskmourn Commander DSC-6, mythic) — neither currently in BBL inventory. Documented in the corpus through dense referential coverage: flavor-text appearances, set-context citations, narrative agency in story-spotlight cards, and iconographic opposition (the Glimmer subtype is canonically the survivors' defense against him).

## What this node anchors

Cards that reference Valgavoth by name in flavor text, depict the consequences of his Ascension (the swallowed suns, the House overtaking the plane, the survivors' fear-state being farmed), or render the narrative beats of his contract / dominion / pursuit. The character is large enough that he splits into substantively different aspects across the set's design — direct flavor citation, atmospheric set-context, Glimmer-opposition iconography, story-spotlight contract narrative — and the BBL graph can edge those aspects together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card.

**Referenced-not-depicted caveat:** No current BBL corpus card depicts Valgavoth himself. The two named printings (DSK-120 and DSC-6) are not in inventory. The pervasive-antagonist threshold here is met by **9 corpus cards referencing the character across multiple referential vectors**, documented below. **The node's `appears_on:` frontmatter list (6 cards as of wave 45) is a strict subset of the 9-card body reference roster** — wired cards are those whose trivia carries either direct flavor-text citation (Wary Watchdog, Duskmourn's Domination), narrative-agent citation (Betrayer's Bargain), or set-context citation with load-bearing Valgavoth-specific detail (Infernal Phantom's moth-demon coding, Say Its Name's wings-condemn iconography, Slavering Branchsnapper's swallowed-suns cosmology). ## Corpus references (current)

**Direct flavor-text citation** — the character named in printed flavor text on the card itself:

- **Wary Watchdog** (DSK-206, common, vision-passed) — flavor text: *"Valgavoth harbors a special loathing for dogs, whose mere presence provides a powerful antidote to the fear he craves."* Establishes the canonical Glimmer-adjacent dog-as-defense lore.
- **Duskmourn's Domination** (DSK-50, common, vision-passed) — flavor text: *"All who enter the House hear Valgavoth's whispers, worming through their consciousness until they can no longer resist his influence."* Establishes the whisper-influence mechanic and the inevitability of corruption inside the House.

**Narrative-agent citation** — the character named in trivia as the active agent of the depicted beat:

- **Betrayer's Bargain** (DSK-126, uncommon, story-spotlight) — Winter's contract with Valgavoth: deliver four lives in exchange for an escape door; the depicted moment is Winter abandoning Niko Aris, the Wanderer, and Nashi to the cultists. Per Star City Games' "Valgavoth's True Form Is Revealed In Duskmourn Episode 6," Valgavoth ultimately betrays Winter back, opening a door to Innistrad before dragging Winter into the House's bowels. The card is one of three corpus cards in the WAR-style Winter story cycle (Winter's Intervention #123, this card #126, Winter Misanthropic Guide #240).

**Set-context citation** — the character named in canonical trivia citations as the plane's ruler:

- **Fear of Being Hunted** (DSK-134, uncommon) — set-context bullet names Valgavoth as the demon ruling the plane.
- **Fear of Burning Alive** (DSK-135, uncommon) — set-context bullet names Valgavoth as the demon ruling the plane.
- **Infernal Phantom** (DSK-141, uncommon) — set-context bullet names "the trapped moth-demon Valgavoth" as the Ascension's instigator; specifies he is moth-demon-coded (the only corpus citation to specify the moth-motif iconography).
- **Razorkin Hordecaller** (DSK-152, uncommon) — set-context citation.
- **Frantic Strength** (DSK-179, common) — set-context citation.
- **Moldering Gym // Weight Room** (DSK-190, common) — set-context citation.
- **Say Its Name** (DSK-197, common) — set-context naming Valgavoth as the demon whose "wings condemn everyone inside to feed his appetite for terror." The wings reference is the second corpus citation to specify the moth-demon iconography.
- **Slavering Branchsnapper** (DSK-198, common) — set-context citation. **Also**: trivia bullet names "Valgavoth consumed both [suns] as the House grew a tower piercing the clouds, and with the suns gone crops failed and the plane collapsed" — establishes the swallowed-suns cosmology that anchors any Duskmourn-lore bundle's apparatus-of-cosmic-extinction copy. Related-card bullet explicitly points to Valgavoth, Harrower of Souls as "the set's antagonist; the consumed-suns lore originates from his Ascension; anchor card for any Duskmourn-lore bundle."
- **Smoky Lounge // Misty Salon** (DSK-235, common) — set-context citation.

**Iconographic opposition** — the character not named but his canonical defense rendered:

- **Glimmer Seeker** (DSK-14, common, vision+trivia passed) — trivia bullet quotes the canonical Inquisitive Glimmer (DSK-217) flavor: *"Glimmers are physical manifestations of survivors' hope and courage. Their presence is one of the few things that can protect from Valgavoth's malevolent influence."* The Glimmer subtype is in-fiction *anti-Valgavoth ordnance*; every Glimmer-typed corpus card (Glimmer Seeker plus the cycle members named in the trivia: Glimmerburst #062, Glimmerlight #249, Inquisitive Glimmer #217, Lionheart Glimmer #019, Enduring Courage #133) inherits opposition-to-Valgavoth at the design layer.

**Artist-cohort adjacency** — Chris Cold illustrated Horrid Vigor (DSK-184) within the same Duskmourn visual register as Valgavoth, Harrower of Souls (DSC-6) and Dollmaker's Shop // Porcelain Gallery (DSK-4); the corpus card Horrid Vigor flags this artist-cohort link explicitly in its trivia. Not a Valgavoth-anchor on its own but materially supports the painterly horror visual language the character anchors.

## Caveats

- **Referenced-not-depicted threshold case.** This node is unusual in the characters layer: no corpus card depicts Valgavoth in art. The precedent invoked is the **pervasive-antagonist** model ([[phyrexia]] as faction; [[fear-of-cycle]] as collective designer-coordinated cohort) rather than the depicted-anchor model ([[acerola]], `nicol-bolas`). The Slobad refusal-precedent (one flavor-text appearance does not anchor) does NOT apply because Valgavoth clears the threshold by multi-vector breadth: 2 direct flavor citations + 1 narrative-agent story-spotlight + 8 set-context citations + 1 iconographic-opposition cohort (Glimmers). The threshold-shape for referenced-not-depicted anchors is multiplicity of referential vectors, not raw count of any single vector. **Node remains demoteable** if a future janitor pass concludes the pervasive-antagonist precedent should be tightened — in that case, defer until a Valgavoth-depicting card (DSK-120 Terror Eater, DSC-6 Harrower of Souls, or a future printing) enters corpus.
- **Two named printings exist; neither is in corpus.** Valgavoth, Terror Eater (DSK-120, mythic, 7/6 flying ward {6} Demon with Drain) and Valgavoth, Harrower of Souls (DSC-6, mythic, Duskmourn Commander legendary printing) are the two existing-as-of-2026 printings. The Commander printing is illustrated by Chris Cold (per the in-corpus Horrid Vigor trivia citation). When either lands in inventory, `appears_on:` will populate and the depicted-anchor precedent kicks in retroactively.
- **Moth-demon iconography is canonical, not interpretive.** Corpus trivia citations on Infernal Phantom ("moth-demon Valgavoth") and Say Its Name ("his wings condemn everyone inside") both specify the moth-coded visual register. The full canonical visual identity — moth wings, terror-eating mouth, cherry-wood-door portal motif (named on Betrayer's Bargain related-cards as "the cherry-wood-door, moth-motif antagonist behind every Duskmourn bargain card") — is documented across set citations and not invented here.
- **Bundle-anchor potential is strong.** Any Duskmourn-themed Discrete Lair candidate can name Valgavoth directly as the apparatus-of-extraction figure: the House's growth IS Valgavoth's body, the survivors' fear IS Valgavoth's food, the bargains struck inside the House ARE Valgavoth's contracts. The labor-hub thesis (apparatus of extraction farming the working population) maps cleanly onto Duskmourn's fear-farming cosmology. Reserve for when the Discrete Lair on Duskmourn material is being assembled.

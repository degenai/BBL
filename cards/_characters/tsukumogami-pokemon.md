# Tsukumogami Pokemon (object-becomes-spirit design-source cohort)

A cross-generational cohort of Pokemon species whose canonical design source is **tsukumogami** — a class of Japanese yokai (folkloric spirits) in which inanimate objects acquire sentience, traditionally on their 100th anniversary of existence. The cohort predicate is **design-source citation** rather than in-game region, generation, or type; membership requires that per-species Bulbapedia design-source documentation, official Pokedex flavor, or canonical species lore explicitly identify the species as an object-that-became-alive. Tsukumogami-Pokemon are structurally distinct from possession-Pokemon (where an external spirit possesses an unrelated object) and from artifact-construct-Pokemon (where an object is animated by an external power source) — the tsukumogami reading is specifically that **the object's own latent spirit awakens**, the inanimate becoming animate from within rather than being inhabited from without.

The cohort is one of the franchise's clearest cross-generational design-source threads: the same folklore citation appears in Voltorb (Generation I, 1996), Shuppet (Generation III, 2002), Honedge / Doublade / Aegislash (Generation VI, 2013), Yamask (Generation V, 2010, with Galarian variant in Generation VIII, 2019), and Sinistea / Polteageist (Generation VIII, 2019). Game Freak has not labeled the cohort by name in any single design-coverage piece, but per-species design-source documentation across Bulbapedia, Bulbanews's *On the Origin of Species* article series, Dr. Lava's *The Original 151* design-history retrospective, ScreenRant's design-canon writing, and bogleech.com's Pokemon mythology essays converges on tsukumogami as the load-bearing folklore citation across all listed species.

Membership predicates on three overlapping signals: (a) explicit tsukumogami citation in Bulbapedia's per-species design-source section OR per-species Pokedex flavor text describing the species as an object-that-became-alive, (b) the species' canonical lore framing the creature as an inanimate object's own awakened spirit rather than an external possession, (c) the franchise's recurrent visual register — disguised-object body plans (Voltorb-as-Poke-Ball, Shuppet-as-paper-doll, Sinistea-as-teacup, Honedge-as-sword), 100-year-threshold encoding where present (Voltorb's Pokedex no. 100), and Pokedex flavor describing the species as confused-or-malicious-because-it-recently-became-aware.

## The cohort thesis

The tsukumogami-Pokemon cohort's most BBL-relevant property is its **cross-generational design-source continuity** — the same folklore citation appearing in species designed two decades apart, across five generations and four distinct in-game regions (Kanto Voltorb, Hoenn Shuppet, Kalos Honedge-line, Galar Sinistea-line). This is not a coordinated cohort in the Alolan-regional-forms sense (where Game Freak designed an 18-species program in one development cycle); it is a **recurrent design-source thread** that Game Freak has returned to repeatedly across the franchise's lifetime, with per-species attribution converging on the same Japanese folklore tradition. The cohort is the corpus's clearest **cross-generational Japanese-folkloric design-source cohort** at the per-species attribution level, sibling to the kodama-tree-spirit thread documented on `phantump-line` but extending across more generations and more distinct species.

The cohort also dual-codes the franchise's broader **ghost-Pokemon-as-cultural-artifact** thesis. Where standard ghost-type Pokemon depict spirits-of-dead-creatures (Gastly, Misdreavus, Drifloon, Phantump), tsukumogami-Pokemon depict spirits-of-objects — a structurally distinct category where the haunting source is *manufactured* rather than *biological*. The Pokemon franchise's ghost-type roster is consequently split between *biological-death* and *object-tsukumogami* origin canons, with the tsukumogami subset operating at the intersection of folklore and design history (the teru teru bozu paper-doll in Shuppet's design, the porcelain authenticity stamps in Sinistea's design, the Pokemon-franchise-internal Poke-Ball-becomes-creature self-reference in Voltorb's design). For BBL bundle work, this is thesis-grade material: a Pokemon design tradition that explicitly cites Japanese folklore, is documented at the per-species design-source level across multiple generations, and offers a clean cross-generational cohesion-stack for bundle copy at the design-canon scope.

## Caveats

The cohort cleanly clears the 3-species threshold the precedent character-layer cohort nodes use. On full enrichment of the existing corpus, the cohort is projected to land at approximately 10-11 prints across 5-6 distinct species. Membership predicates on **per-species canonical tsukumogami attribution**, not on type, generation, or set; any TCG printing of these species attaches to this node when its trivia pass confirms the tsukumogami citation.

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like [[phantump-line]], `mareep-line`, `charizard-line`, `caterpie-line`, `ralts-line`, `larvitar-line`, `rowlet-line`, `pancham-line`, `elemental-monkey-trio`, [[galarian-regional-forms]], [[alolan-regional-forms]], `fear-of-cycle`, `dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle`, and [[dsk-toy-horror]], this is a collective designer-coordinated cohort hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer accepts both individual named characters and collective cohorts). The tsukumogami-Pokemon cohort is structurally distinguished from the precedent evolutionary-line nodes by its **design-source-citation predicate** (membership predicates on per-species canonical folkloric attribution, not on shared species lineage). A future `_design-source-cohorts/` layer could re-home this entry alongside other design-source-citation cohorts when that split is commissioned; for now, single layer, same precedent.

- **Tsukumogami is the design source, not the species type.** Pokemon's in-game type system (Ghost, Psychic, Steel, etc.) does not encode tsukumogami specifically — the cohort's predicate operates at the design-source-citation level, not at the type-line level. Cohort members include Ghost-types (Shuppet, Sinistea, Polteageist, Doublade), Lightning-types (Voltorb), and Ghost/Steel-types (Honedge / Doublade / Aegislash); the unifying frame is the design-source citation, not the type combination. This is the structural counterpart to the kodama-tree-spirit thread documented on `phantump-line` (where kodama is a design-source citation that happens to manifest in Ghost/Grass typing across the corpus's current kodama-cited members).

- **Tsukumogami is distinct from possession-Pokemon and from artifact-construct-Pokemon.** The cohort predicate requires that the species' canonical lore frames the creature as **the object's own latent spirit awakening** — the inanimate becoming animate from within. Pokemon whose canonical lore frames them as **an external spirit possessing an object** (Yamask possessing a stone-mask, Banette as a discarded doll occupied by a vengeful spirit, Mimikyu as a creature wearing an external disguise) are tsukumogami-adjacent but require species-by-species canonical-attribution review. Banette's canonical lore is borderline — the discarded-plush-doll-animated-by-grudge-spirit framing is sometimes cited as possession (the spirit is external to the doll) and sometimes cited as tsukumogami (the discarded doll's accumulated affection becomes its own awakening). Per-species attribution review on next enrichment pass should establish whether Banette belongs at this node or at a future `possessed-pokemon` sibling cohort node. Yamask / Cofagrigus / Galarian Yamask similarly require per-species attribution review — the Generation V Yamask line is canonically a possessed-mask reading (Bulbapedia design-source: "souls of mummies"), with the Galarian Yamask variant's clay-slab-possession-reversal framing potentially placing the variant at a *different* design-source cohort than its non-Galarian counterpart.

- **Mimikyu is not at this node.** The Mimikyu species (corpus prints DAA-081, CSE-96) has been considered for tsukumogami attribution in some community-canon writing (the rag-disguise as an external object the creature occupies), but Bulbapedia's design-source documentation cites the disguise as an **active-fashioning-by-the-creature** rather than the disguise being a tsukumogami-awakened object. Mimikyu is consequently structurally a **creature-wearing-an-object** Pokemon rather than a tsukumogami; the cohort predicate excludes this species per per-species canonical attribution.

- **Klefki is borderline and not currently at this node.** The Klefki species (corpus print FST-186) has been considered for tsukumogami attribution in some retrospective writing (the keyring-creature as an awakened object). Bulbapedia's design-source documentation cites Klefki as a **fairy-stealing-keys** trickster-folklore design more centrally than a tsukumogami design, with the species' Pokedex flavor *"Long ago it lived in mines, but once the minerals that make up its diet became scarcer, Klefki began appearing in human settlements"* framing the species as a mineral-eating creature rather than an awakened object. Per-species attribution review on next Klefki enrichment pass could revisit; for now, Klefki is at a sibling fairy-trickster register and not at this node.

- **Voltorb Evolutions (EVO-39) attaches on trivia-pass.** The third Voltorb print in corpus is vision-passed (wave 81-era) but trivia-pending; the species' tsukumogami citation is established at the per-species level by the other two Voltorb prints' Trivia sections (VIV-045 and RBC-056). The tsukumogami-Pokemon cohort and the toy-horror cohort share the structural predicate **"object given sentience as a designer-coordinated thematic substrate"** but diverge sharply at the design-source-register level: tsukumogami operates within Japanese-folkloric tradition (the awakening is canonical, often centuries-old, sometimes neutral or benevolent), the toy-horror cohort operates within American slasher-horror tradition (the awakening is corruption, recent, hostile, mediated through Annabelle / Chucky / killer-doll register per [[dsk-toy-horror]] cohort thesis).

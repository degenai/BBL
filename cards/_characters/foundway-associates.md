# Foundway Associates

The **Foundway Associates** are a designer-coordinated secondary investigator ensemble woven through the flavor-text layer of *Murders at Karlov Manor* (MKM, 2024-02-09). The Associates are distinct from R.A.M.I. (the Ravnican Agency of Magicological Investigation, the institutional Agency the MKM storyline canonizes as the case's principal investigators) — they read as an independent, plural-membered field-investigator collective whose voices canonize specific scenes across the set's flavor architecture. Each Associate's line is attributed by name and faction ("—Udol of the Foundway Associates", "—Imel of the Foundway Associates", and so on), and the cumulative effect across multiple cards is a coherent ensemble cast operating in parallel to the Agency proper.

## What this node anchors

Card flavor text where a named Foundway Associates member is the in-universe speaker, canonizing the depicted scene as Foundway-perspective field-investigation work. Across the corpus, the Associates surface as six named voices on six distinct MKM prints — a designer-coordinated ensemble cohort that crosses the set's flavor layer the way the four signature mechanics cross its rules-text layer.

**Members in our corpus (current):**

- **Udol** (speaker on **Gearbane Orangutan**, MKM-129) — *"In her defense, thopters are very fun to smash."* Dry-witted field observer; Udol's voice canonizes the depicted ape-vs-thopter scene as a Foundway-perspective field-observation beat. The wry-defense register establishes Udol as the ensemble's wisecracker.

- **Imel** (speaker on **Offender at Large**, MKM-138) — *"In a city this big, anyone can blend in."* Disguise-mechanic-meta voice; Imel's line canonizes the rules-text rendering of going undercover as a Foundway-perspective observation about Ravnica's scale. The card pairs the Associates with the [[disguise]] symbol node — Imel narrates the in-fiction conditions that make Disguise mechanically legible.

- **Runubi** (speaker on **Suspicious Detonation**, MKM-145) — *"Clearly someone wanted us to find this."* Suspicion-and-planted-evidence register; Runubi's voice canonizes the Investigate-payoff scene as a Foundway-perspective scene-of-crime read. The line frames the artifact-sacrifice payoff as a setup — someone arranged for the evidence to surface.

- **Algrom** (speaker on **Torch the Witness**, MKM-146) — *"He was scheduled to meet me tomorrow with something important to share. This was no coincidence."* Witness-elimination-and-paranoia register; Algrom's voice canonizes the depicted assassination as a targeted silencing the Foundway Associates are investigating. The card is the ensemble's most thematically load-bearing print — it canonizes the MKM storyline's *witnesses-keep-dying* meta-mystery.

- **Alst** (speaker on **They Went This Way**, MKM-178) — *"Running doesn't prove guilt, but innocent people don't typically flee into the North Ridge Forest."* Tracking-and-procedural register; Alst's voice canonizes the depicted basic-land-search-plus-Investigate scene as a Foundway-perspective pursuit through specific named Ravnican geography (the North Ridge Forest). The line establishes the Associates as field operatives with jurisdiction over Ravnica's wilderness margins, not just the city.

- **Linghu** (speaker on **Furtive Courier**, MKM-59) — *"Glad to see you made it in one piece. Next time, try not looking so obviously nervous."* Handler-debrief register; Linghu's voice canonizes the depicted unblockable-on-artifact-sacrifice creature as a Foundway-perspective handler addressing an asset returning from a covert errand. The line establishes the Associates as running their own covert-asset network in parallel to Agency operations.

Six named voices across six cards is well past the ≥3 threshold for character-faction-node commission per the [[orzhov-syndicate]] / [[agency]] precedents.

## The functional canon

The Foundway Associates' BBL-relevant institutional features are three:

- **They are an investigator ensemble parallel to the Agency, not a sub-unit of it.** Per the canonical flavor architecture — each Associate is attributed "of the Foundway Associates", not "of R.A.M.I." or "of the Agency" — the Associates are a distinct in-fiction institutional actor. The MKM storyline canonizes R.A.M.I. (the Ravnican Agency of Magicological Investigation) as the principal Agency the *Murders at Karlov Manor* case is run through; the Foundway Associates surface in the flavor layer as a separate investigative collective that crosses the same MKM crime-scene topology. Whether they are independent contractors, a rival firm, an Orzhov-adjacent private investigation house, or a freelance collective is **not canonically specified** in any Wizards-published source visible to BBL — the flavor-text layer establishes the ensemble's existence and its ensemble-cast nature without locking in the institutional framing.

- **Their voice canonizes the field-investigation perspective for six MKM flavor-text cards.** Where the Agency / R.A.M.I. voice (Alquist Proft, Kaya, the Agency Detective tokens) anchors the institutional-investigation perspective across the set, the Foundway voice anchors a field-investigation perspective that reads more conversational, more local, more procedural-from-the-bottom-up. Their lines tend to comment on the depicted scene from inside the investigation — Udol observes thopter-smashing, Imel observes Ravnica's blend-in scale, Runubi observes planted evidence, Algrom observes the meta-mystery's witness-deaths, Alst observes flight-as-evidence, Linghu debriefs a returning asset. This is the **Foundway-perspective register** the BBL graph treats as canon-anchor substrate per the same flavor-text-speaker mechanism that anchors [[kaya]] across her four flavor-voice cards.

- **They are dual-coded with the [[agency]] and the [[disguise]] / [[suspect]] mechanical layers.** Five of the six Foundway-narrated cards are also Agency-operational-register cards (the corpus's MKM investigation cluster). The Imel-narrated *Offender at Large* (MKM-138) is on the [[disguise]] symbol node's `appears_on:` list; the Runubi-narrated *Suspicious Detonation* (MKM-145) shares its set's red-side investigation register with the [[suspect]] symbol node's anchors; the Algrom-narrated *Torch the Witness* (MKM-146) interacts with the Investigate-and-Clue-token sub-mechanic that crosses Cases and Collect Evidence. The Foundway Associates anchor the MKM investigation cluster at the flavor-voice level the same way [[agency]] anchors it at the institutional-actor level and [[disguise]] / [[suspect]] anchor it at the mechanical-keyword level. A future MKM-themed Discrete Lair could index all four nodes together for maximum cohesion.

This is what BBL bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: the Foundway Associates are a Wizards-coordinated six-voice ensemble whose flavor-text presence canonizes a parallel investigation perspective across six MKM prints. Bundle prose that names the Associates by ensemble or by individual member earns lore-aware buyer credibility AND keeps the institutional ambiguity intact — Wizards did not specify who funds the Foundway, and BBL does not need to.

## Caveats

- **The ensemble's institutional framing is NOT canonically specified.** Wizards published no Planeswalker's Guide entry, no story page, and no design column establishing what the Foundway Associates are organizationally — whether they are licensed private investigators, an Orzhov-adjacent collection-and-investigation firm, a rival agency to R.A.M.I., a freelance collective, or something else. The six flavor-text attributions establish the ensemble's existence and its ensemble nature; everything else is inference. BBL's anti-confabulation discipline ([[bbl-anti-confab-prompt]]) means the node body does NOT invent the framing. Future Wizards canon may resolve this; until then, the framing stays open.

- **Members are six distinct named individuals, not aspects of one character.** Per the Tamiyo / Trunks / Pan multi-aspect precedent ([[bbl-triviabot-no-role-identity-conflation]]), BBL does not conflate role with identity. Each Foundway member is canonically a distinct individual (Linghu ≠ Udol ≠ Imel ≠ Runubi ≠ Algrom ≠ Alst — six separate flavor-text attributions across six separate cards). The ensemble-cast node anchors all six at the faction level; if a future corpus print depicts one of these named members with verified visual attribution, a solo character node for that individual would become commission-worthy. Currently, none of the six are depicted on-card with verified attribution — they are flavor-voice anchors only.

- **The ensemble may extend beyond six members.** MTG Wiki's MKM coverage and Scryfall's full MKM flavor-text corpus may surface additional Foundway-attributed cards beyond the six in current BBL inventory. The node is sized to grow — when additional Foundway-flavored cards enter the corpus, they extend `appears_on:` here. The current six-member count is a corpus floor, not a canon ceiling.

- **The Agency / R.A.M.I. relation is parallel-and-distinct, not subordinate.** Per the canonical attributions, Foundway members are "of the Foundway Associates" — not "of the Agency" or "of R.A.M.I.". The [[agency]] character node anchors the institutional-investigation perspective for the MKM Agency cards (Kaya, Alquist Proft, Agency Detective tokens). This node anchors the field-investigation perspective for the MKM Foundway cards. The two nodes are siblings, not parent-child, and the `related_characters: [agency, orzhov-syndicate]` frontmatter encodes the cross-edge to both nearby institutional nodes without claiming subordination.

- **The Orzhov-adjacency is speculative, not canonical.** Several Foundway-attributed cards (notably MKM-59 Furtive Courier and MKM-138 Offender at Large) read with Orzhov-style asset-handler-and-blend-in registers, and Ravnica's underworld-economy texture canonically intersects Orzhov institutional reach. But Wizards did not canonize the Foundway as Orzhov-affiliated. The `related_characters: [orzhov-syndicate]` field encodes graph-adjacency at the institutional-Ravnica-cohort level, not a canonized employment relation.

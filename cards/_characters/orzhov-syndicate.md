# Orzhov Syndicate

The white-black guild of the plane Ravnica, introduced as a playable guild in *Guildpact* (2006) and returning across every Ravnica revisit since (*Return to Ravnica* 2012, *Gatecrash* 2013, *Dragon's Maze* 2013, *Ravnica Allegiance* 2019, *War of the Spark* 2019, *Streets of New Capenna* tie-ins, *Murders at Karlov Manor* 2024, *Ravnica Remastered* 2024). MTG Wiki and Wizards' own design articles characterize the Orzhov as the plane's "Church of Deals" — an institutional amalgam of organized religion, organized banking, and organized crime, where the line between sermon, debt-contract, and protection racket is a matter of vocabulary, not function. The guild's members include living humans (pontiffs, lawmages, tax-collector knights, ministers), undead vampires (the patriciate), spirits of the indebted dead (kept in service past death by unpaid contracts), thrulls (constructed labor), and the Obzedat Ghost Council itself (a body of vampire-spirit oligarchs that rules the syndicate from beyond).

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts the Orzhov as an *institution*: its operatives at work (tithe-collection, debt-extraction, judicial execution, sacramental sacrifice), its iconography (the eclipsed-sun signet, ecclesiastical regalia, banking-house architecture, the Orzhov watermark), its named members in their guild capacity, and the institutional thesis the guild embodies — that institutional authority operates by extracting wealth from the living and the dead alike, dressed in the vocabulary of piety. The Orzhov Syndicate is the BBL corpus's most thesis-aligned in-universe faction for the apparatus-of-extraction register: any bundle on tithe-as-theology, debt-as-eternity, or institutional-violence-as-sacrament indexes here.

- **Charitable Levy** (MH3-21, uncommon, Eli Minaya art) — tithe-extraction scene from the operational end. Two robed figures with arms upraised as Orzhov gold-coinage rains down on a village beneath a brightening sky. Mechanically `{2}{W}` white-magic enchantment with a noncreature-spell tax (every noncreature spell costs `{1}` more, collection counters accumulate until the tithe sacrifices into a Plains-tutor card-draw) — the rules-text rendering of the syndicate's extraction-as-tax operational template. The card has no Orzhov watermark (it's mono-white in *Modern Horizons 3* rather than a Ravnica-block guild-watermarked printing), but the iconographic register — falling coinage, raised-arm village figures, tithe-and-collection-counter mechanic — is unambiguously the syndicate's operational vocabulary.

- **Indebted Spirit** (MH3-31, uncommon, L.A. Draws art) — a skeletal robed spirit holding a spiked scepter and an unrolled scroll, draped in flowing white robes in a torchlit stone interior. Mechanically `{1}{W}` Spirit with Bestow `{2}{W}` and Afterlife 1, and the enchanted creature gains +1/+1 and Afterlife 1. **Afterlife is the Orzhov guild's signature *Ravnica Allegiance* keyword** (introduced in the 2019 Ravnica block as the institutional-thesis mechanic for the syndicate: dying creatures produce a 1/1 white-black Spirit token, encoding the doctrine that death does not discharge service). The card's flavor register (skull, scroll, scepter, bureaucratic dread) and its core keyword together place this as a canonical Orzhov institutional-rhetoric printing despite the mono-white *Modern Horizons 3* color identity.

- **Sanguine Savior** (MKM-230, common, Valera Lutfullina art) — a winged Vampire Cleric carries an unconscious companion through foggy gothic skies above Ravnica; `{W/B}` Vampire Cleric with Flying, Lifelink, and Disguise `{W/B}{W/B}`. The eclipsed-sun signet is embossed on the carrier's chest armor (medallion-of-master form per the canonical Orzhov Signet duality, indicating chosen / status-marker rather than branded / debt-marker) and visible again on a distant spire in the background skyline. The card is a *Murders at Karlov Manor* dual-coded case: institutionally Orzhov by guild iconography AND operationally an Agency Detective by set-role per `[Bulbapedia: MKM Disguise mechanic; MTG Wiki: R.A.M.I.]`. Anchors both this node and the [[agency]] character node, plus the [[orzhov-signet]] symbol node — the post-Phyrexian-Invasion cross-guild Agency staffing pattern surfaced in a single character's iconography.

## The functional ideology

The Orzhov Syndicate's most BBL-relevant property is that its institutional logic IS the thesis. Where other Ravnican guilds organize around a craft (Boros: paramilitary justice; Izzet: experimental engineering; Selesnya: communal devotion), the Orzhov organize around the **extraction of obligation**. They invented a system where:

- Death does not discharge debt. Spirits of the indebted dead remain bound to the syndicate until the ledger closes.
- The institution stamps both ends of its hierarchy with the same emblem (the Orzhov Signet — see [[orzhov-signet]] symbol node), distinguished only by whether it is worn at the throat by choice (master's medallion) or branded into the body by force (slave's tattoo).
- The vocabulary of religion (pontiff, cathedral, sermon, sacrament, prayer, redemption, blessing) is the operational vocabulary of finance (tithe, contract, ledger, collection, debt, payment, settlement). The two languages are deliberately interchangeable.
- The Obzedat Ghost Council, the guild's ruling body, is composed of vampire-spirit oligarchs who have refused to surrender power even after death. Institutional continuity past biological death is the syndicate's signature feature.

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: the guild is, in-universe, a designer-confirmed apparatus-of-extraction dressed as a church. Bundle prose that names the Orzhov by name earns lore-aware buyer credibility and lets Wizards' own decades of guild-design work do the thesis work without naming the thesis.

## Caveats

- **The Orzhov Syndicate is the institution; the Orzhov Signet is the iconography of that institution.** This character node and the [[orzhov-signet]] symbol node are paired but distinct graph dimensions. Cards that depict the signet *as object* edge to the symbol node (via `symbols:` frontmatter); cards that depict the guild *as actor* (its operatives at work, its institutional violence) edge here (via `characters:` frontmatter). A card can do both — Pitiless Pontiff and Tithe Drinker, for example, depict an Orzhov operative seated against an Orzhov Signet throne back, and edge to both nodes.
- **Vito, Thorn of the Dusk Rose** (M21-127) is NOT an Orzhov Syndicate member. Vito Quijano de Pasamonte is canonically a vampire hierophant of the Ixalan *Church of Dusk* (Legion of Dusk on Torrezon), a separate Wizards-canonical institution that shares aesthetic conventions (white-black, vampire-priest, ecclesiastical-robes, blood-and-redemption-vocabulary) but is a different in-canon faction on a different plane. The two factions are visually convergent and ideologically adjacent (both are vampire-priest extraction-institutions) but should not be conflated. A future `church-of-dusk` or `legion-of-dusk` character node would anchor Vito; he is flagged on this node specifically because the surface-resemblance trap has come up before.
- **Lawmage's Binding** (RNA-190) is NOT an Orzhov card. The lawmages depicted are Azorius Senate operatives (white-blue, Lyev Column constabulary per the card's trivia and MTG Wiki Azorius page); the card is the Azorius entry in Ravnica Allegiance's guild-common removal cycle, not Orzhov. The captive being bound (a horned red-skinned figure, likely a Rakdos demon or a Cult-of-Rakdos operative) is also not Orzhov. The card is included in some Tithe-bundle proposals because of its institutional-restraint thesis register, but it does not belong to this faction node.
- **The Karlov noble house** is a sub-faction within the Orzhov Syndicate, with its own iconography (the family ouroboros, distinct from the guild eclipsed-sun signet) and a separately-storylined arc through Teysa Karlov, Karlov of the Ghost Council, and the Murders at Karlov Manor 2024 set. The Karlovs are Orzhov by guild membership but warrant their own future sub-node if the corpus accumulates enough Karlov-specific cards. For now, Karlov-depicting cards edge here at the syndicate level.
- **Kaya, Orzhov Usurper** (RNA / WAR) was installed by Bolas as guildmaster of the Orzhov during the War of the Spark arc, a story-level intervention that's worth noting but doesn't change the institution's underlying character. Kaya is a planeswalker character node candidate in her own right; she'd edge here as an officeholder, not as a constitutive member.
- The Orzhov's mechanical signatures (Haunt, Extort, Afterlife) are designer-stated thematic-mechanical coding for the guild's debt-past-death thesis. Cards bearing those mechanics with the Orzhov watermark are the highest-confidence membership signal; cards bearing the watermark without those mechanics (e.g. Mortify, Lawmage's Binding's sibling Orzhov-removal slot) are still canonical Orzhov per Scryfall classification.

# Pollution Pokemon (Game Freak three-line urban-pollution design cohort)

A cross-generational cohort of three pure-Poison-type single-evolution Pokemon species lines, each designed by Game Freak to canonically encode a structurally distinct urban-pollution substrate: **Grimer / Muk** (liquid sludge — Kanto, Generation I, 1996; National Pokedex 88-89), **Koffing / Weezing** (airborne toxic gas — Kanto, Generation I, 1996; National Pokedex 109-110), and **Trubbish / Garbodor** (solid garbage / urban land-waste — Unova, Generation V, 2010; National Pokedex 568-569). The cohort predicate is the **designer-stated structural parallel**: all three lines share the same template — pure Poison-typing, one base form plus one single Stone-equivalent-tier evolution at roughly equivalent base-stat totals — and the three substrate codings (liquid / gas / solid) are designer-confirmed to be deliberately distributed across the three lines as a coordinated three-substrate framework.

Per a Nintendo Dream interview attributed to Ken Sugimori and Takao Unno, the Trubbish / Garbodor line was designed specifically to represent **urban land pollution** in the Unova region (canonically modeled on New York City, where land-waste is the era's dominant urban-pollution register), and Game Freak deliberately structured the line as the third entry in an extant two-line cohort: the Gen I liquid-sludge line (Grimer / Muk) and the Gen I airborne-gas line (Koffing / Weezing) already occupied the liquid and gaseous substrate slots, and the Trubbish / Garbodor line was added 14 years later to complete the third substrate (solid waste) and close the three-substrate framework. This is **designer-stated canonical-cohort structure**, not BBL curatorial overlay — Game Freak's design team explicitly articulated the three-line parallel as the basis for the Gen V addition.

## The cohort thesis

The Pollution Pokemon cohort's most BBL-relevant property is its **cross-generational designer-stated structural-parallel continuity** — the same three-line pollution-substrate framework, with the same pure-Poison single-evolution mechanical template, re-instantiated 14 years apart across two original regions (Kanto Gen I → Unova Gen V) per a designer-confirmed Nintendo Dream interview statement attributed to Ken Sugimori and Takao Unno. This is *not* a within-generation coordinated cohort like the Alolan regional forms (where Game Freak designed an 18-species program in one development cycle) or the Elemental Monkey Trio (where Game Freak designed a three-species cycle in one Gen V development cycle); this is a **cross-generational design-program continuity** where the Gen V Trubbish / Garbodor line was deliberately added to extend an extant Gen I two-line framework (Grimer / Muk + Koffing / Weezing) into a complete three-substrate parallel. The cohort is the corpus's clearest cross-generational designer-stated structural-parallel Pokemon cohort at the per-species substrate-coding level.

The cohort dual-codes the franchise's broader **Pokemon-as-environmental-infrastructure** thesis. Where standard creature-design Pokemon depict animals adapted to natural ecosystems (forest mammals, freshwater fish, mountain reptiles), pollution-Pokemon depict creatures whose bodies *are* the pollutants — the species' bodies are not creatures that encounter pollution, they are pollution that achieved species-status. Muk's body IS liquid sludge. Weezing's body IS toxic airborne gas. Garbodor's body IS solid urban garbage. The cohort's structural thesis is that pollution at urban-civilization scale produces creatures whose bodies are coextensive with the pollution substrate itself — the canonical-event being Game Freak's species-design choice, the curatorial-overlay being BBL's reading of the cohort as the franchise's clearest articulation of **species as the by-product of urban-industrial waste streams**, with Muk's flavor *"targeted for extermination, leading to a steep decline in their population"* and Alolan Grimer's flavor *"a hundred or so living in Alola's waste-disposal site, hard workers who eat a lot of trash"* establishing the franchise's canonical-event basis for pollution-Pokemon-as-extraction-target and pollution-Pokemon-as-state-sanctioned-sanitation-labor respectively.

For BBL bundle work, this is **thesis-grade material at the labor / stewardship hub-attribution boundary**: the cohort encodes a designer-confirmed canonical-event (the three-substrate framework is Sugimori-and-Unno-attributed), the per-species canonical flavor establishes the species-as-pollution-body framing at the printed-card level, and the cross-cohort overlap with [[alolan-regional-forms]] (Alolan Grimer's institutional waste-disposal labor, with Trubbish-predation as an unintended consequence of the Alolan introduction) extends the cohort into ecological-labor and apparatus-of-environmental-management registers. Bundle copy can lean on the designer-stated three-substrate framework (Grimer-liquid / Koffing-gas / Trubbish-solid) as the cohesion spine without needing to invent thesis-overlay above the canonical-event layer.

## Caveats

- **Citation-tier note on the Sugimori / Unno design statement.** The Nintendo Dream interview attribution is the load-bearing canonical-source for the three-line designer-stated structural parallel, but the interview content reached the corpus via two secondary aggregators (lavacutcontent.com 'Gen 5 Historia' and remptongames.com 'Evolution of Pokemon Design Gen 5'), both of which are WebSearch-snippet-tier secondary surfaces rather than Bulbapedia primary or Nintendo Dream primary. Future enrichment passes should target a primary Nintendo Dream archive citation if accessible, or a Bulbapedia design-source-section confirmation of the three-line parallel statement, to harden the canonical-source attribution. The *content* of the cohort thesis (the three-substrate structural parallel) is independently verifiable against Bulbapedia's per-species design-source sections and per-species Pokedex flavor text across all six cohort species at the printed-card level — the cohort thesis stands on convergent secondary citation plus structural in-game evidence, but the designer-statement primary-source attribution is at secondary-aggregator-tier rather than primary-tier.

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like [[alolan-regional-forms]], `galarian-regional-forms`, [[tsukumogami-pokemon]], `elemental-monkey-trio`, `fear-of-cycle`, `dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle`, `castform-forms-cycle`, and `dsk-toy-horror`, this is a collective designer-coordinated cohort hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer accepts both individual named characters and collective cohorts). The cohort is structurally closest to [[tsukumogami-pokemon]] (cross-generational design-source-citation cohort) and `elemental-monkey-trio` (within-generation designer-coordinated three-species cohort); the Pollution Pokemon cohort sits at the intersection — a cross-generational designer-coordinated three-line cohort whose membership predicate is the **designer-stated structural-parallel framework**, not the design-source-citation (the latter is a Bulbapedia per-species-attribution predicate; the former is a designer-statement cross-line-parallel predicate). A future `_design-program-cohorts/` layer could re-home this entry alongside other designer-stated structural-parallel cohorts when that split is commissioned; for now, single layer, same precedent.

- **The cohort predicate excludes regional-variant forms.** Alolan Grimer / Alolan Muk are NOT at this node — they are at [[alolan-regional-forms]] under the regional-program predicate. The two cohort nodes overlap *at the species-line scope* (the Grimer / Muk line is a member of both the Pollution Pokemon cohort here and the Alolan Regional Forms cohort there) but the cohort predicates are structurally distinct: this node anchors the **designer-stated three-substrate pollution-design framework** (which includes the canonical Kantonian forms but excludes the regional-variant Alolan recodings), and [[alolan-regional-forms]] anchors the **18-species Game-Freak Alola regional-variant program** (which includes Alolan Grimer's pollution-coded form but as a member of the regional-variant program, not as a member of the pollution-design framework). - **Predator-prey overlap with Alolan Grimer is documented at the per-card body level, not at the cohort-predicate level.** Per Alolan Grimer (CSE-130) Trivia, Alolan Grimer canonically preys on Trubbish in Alola as an unintended consequence of the Alolan government's waste-disposal program. This is **canonical-event** at the per-species ecological-interaction level — Bulbapedia documents the predator-prey relationship in both species' pages. The relationship is recorded in the existing Alolan Grimer card's Trivia section and is cross-cohort relevant (this node's Trubbish line × [[alolan-regional-forms]]'s Alolan Grimer), but the relationship is a *body-level ecological-interaction detail* rather than a *cohort-predicate axis* — it does not change cohort membership at either node, and it lives in card-body prose plus the cross-cohort `related_characters` pointer rather than as a new shape of cohort attachment.

- **The Sun-era Kanto Pokedex "moon X-rays" Grimer origin is canonical species lore, not cohort-thesis-disrupting.** Per Bulbapedia's Grimer page, the Pokemon Sun Pokedex entry attributes Grimer's origin to *"sludge in a polluted seabed that was exposed to X-rays from the moon."* This is canonical species-origin lore for the Kantonian Grimer line specifically and is consistent with the cohort's liquid-sludge substrate coding (sludge as the species' literal body composition); it does not disrupt the cross-generational three-substrate cohort thesis, which operates at the **designer-stated structural-parallel** level rather than at the per-species origin-mechanism level. Cohort membership predicates on the designer-stated cross-line parallel framework, not on per-species origin canon.

- **Pokemon's broader Poison-type roster is NOT all cohort members.** The Game Freak Poison-type roster is large and includes species across many design-source registers — Bulbasaur line (plant-Poison), Nidoran lines (mammalian-Poison), Tentacool line (jellyfish-Poison), Ekans line (snake-Poison), Zubat line (bat-Poison), Croagunk line (frog-Poison), Salandit line (lizard-Poison), Mareanie line (sea-anemone-Poison), Toxel line (electric-Poison), and many others. Cohort membership at this node predicates on **the designer-stated three-line urban-pollution framework specifically** (Grimer / Muk + Koffing / Weezing + Trubbish / Garbodor), not on Poison-typing in general. Surface-resemblance Poison-type Pokemon that lack the urban-pollution-substrate coding (e.g., Nidoran line as mammalian-Poison, Croagunk line as toxic-frog) are NOT cohort members. The cohort's predicate is design-program-membership, not type-line.

# Oko

Fae shapeshifter planeswalker, originally of Lorwyn-Shadowmoor; charismatic, vain, notorious trickster whose Shadowmoor aspect grows antlers. Introduced in Throne of Eldraine (2019) as a planeswalker who has installed himself as the self-styled "Fae King" of Eldraine's Wilds, in pursuit of a long-running personal project to humble the human kings of the realm. Per the *Wildered Quest* novella (Kate Elliott, 2019), Oko transformed High King Algenus Kenrith into a stag to be hunted at the midwinter Wild Hunt; per *Wilds of Eldraine* (2023) Wizards revisited him on his home plane as Oko, the Ringleader with the half-fae son Kellan canonical in the arc. The `— Oko` planeswalker subtype on the type-line is reserved for printings of this single character.

The character is famously format-warping out-of-game: **Oko, Thief of Crowns** (ELD-197), Oko's main-set mythic from Throne of Eldraine, was banned in Standard (2019-11-18, within 73 days of release), Brawl (2019-11-05), Pioneer (2019-12-12), and Modern (2020-01) — one of the fastest and most consequential bans in Magic history, and the proximate event that re-anchored Wizards' subsequent power-budget discipline. The Oko-as-meme legacy is documented in its own Know Your Meme entry. The character's notoriety in the playerbase is structurally distinct from the character's in-fiction depiction; the lore-Oko is the trickster of Throne of Eldraine, the meme-Oko is the spreadsheet of every overpowered card the format has shipped since.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts Oko himself — under any of his titles (the Trickster, Thief of Crowns, the Ringleader) — and cards whose narrative subject is the act of *Oko doing the trickster-work*: court intrigue, shapeshift-deception, Wild Hunt arrangement, fae-recruitment, the humiliation of mortal kings, the half-fae-fatherhood arc with Kellan. The character is large enough to split into distinct narrative aspects across his printings, and the BBL graph can edge those aspects together through this anchor node rather than card-to-card mirroring.

The Oko anchor inherits two distinct registers that bundle copy must choose between:
- **The in-fiction Oko** — antlered fae trickster, *Wildered Quest* protagonist-villain, Wild-Hunt-arranger, charismatic-vain shapeshifter. The Wizards-canonical character.
- **The out-of-game Oko** — community shorthand for format-warping design, the 2019 ban, the meme. Sentiment-tier, not canonical to the storyline.

Bundle work should foreground the in-fiction Oko (the trickster) and surface the out-of-game Oko (the meme) as a footnote or a price-tier-explainer, never the other way around. The graph anchor here is the character, not the format-history.

- **Modern Horizons 3 — Oko's elk-transformation trick rendered as a Bestow Aura** — the canonical Oko +1-ability (turn a creature into a 3/3 Elk with no abilities) printed as a card.
  - **Trickster's Elk** (MH3-175, U, Karl Kopinski art) — a powerful elk standing in a misty twilight forest, branching antlers dripping glowing orange-red fire-droplets, the same droplets falling from gnarled tree branches above; mood sublime, palette muted blue-green with warm fire-drop highlights. Mechanically a `{2}{G}` Aura Creature with **Bestow** `{1}{G}`: enchanted creature loses all abilities and becomes a green Elk with base power and toughness 3/3 — a verbatim translation of Oko, Thief of Crowns' +1 ability into the Bestow grammar (turn the target into a vanilla 3/3 Elk). Flavor text: *"Wait. Is *that* one Dad?" —Rowan Kenrith, to Will*. Per MTG Wiki, Oko's signature trickster-act in the *Wildered Quest* arc was transforming High King Algenus Kenrith (Rowan and Will's father) into a stag for the midwinter Wild Hunt; Rowan's flavor question on this card is the now-grown twins encountering yet another anonymous elk-form in the wild and wondering if the trick has been done *again* — the joke is the trauma. `ip_verified: true` via the canonical-name attribution test (possessive "Trickster's" plus the Rowan-Kenrith flavor signature plus the rules-text mirror of Oko's +1 ability — three independent canonical signals).

The four corpus cards span the Throne of Eldraine planeswalker-deck arc (ELD-309 + ELD-310 + ELD-312) plus the MH3 Bestow-Aura mechanical-mirror (MH3-175) — the deck-exclusive product cycle (cards 309-313) that paired with the main-set Oko, Thief of Crowns and Garruk, Cursed Huntsman precons. The deck-exclusive cycle is structurally distinct from the main-set Oko printing (Thief of Crowns, ELD-197) which is the banned mythic and not in the BBL corpus. Future-corpus Oko candidates that would edge here: **Oko, Thief of Crowns** (ELD-197 — the banned mythic, high collector interest); **Oko, the Ringleader** (WOE — the Wilds of Eldraine return printing); **Oko, the Cunning** (DSK or future-set); the *Wildered Quest* novella as canonical-source anchor.

## The Wildered Quest

A notable Wizards-canonical narrative pattern: the Throne of Eldraine block's *Wildered Quest* novella (Kate Elliott, Del Rey 2019) is the in-fiction source for the stag-form imagery on Oko's Hospitality (ELD-312). Oko transformed High King Algenus Kenrith into a stag to be hunted at the midwinter Wild Hunt; the *Wildered Quest* arc is the protagonist-quest to find and restore the king. Oko is the antagonist of the novella and the in-fiction architect of the central plot. The half-fae-fatherhood arc with Kellan (Kellan, the Fae-Blooded, Wilds of Eldraine, 2023) introduces a generational dimension: Oko fathered Kellan with the hedgewitch Alyse during the Eldraine arc, and Kellan's later-set arc has the son navigating the trickster-father legacy. A future Kellan character node would edge here as the child-of-the-act relation; defer until the character-to-character edge category is formalized (parallel to the Nashi-defer-note on the Tamiyo node).

## Caveats

- This node is the character Oko himself, not the set of cards whose **name** mentions him. Depicted-figure or canonical-name-attribution tests apply, parallel to the [[jace-beleren]] / [[tamiyo]] / [[ral-zarek]] standards. Oko's Accomplices (ELD-310) depicts faeries-attributed-to-Oko, not Oko-himself; it edges here via the canonical-name attribution test (the genitive name plus the flavor-text attribution to the fae king) without depicting Oko on the canvas.

- **The Oko, the Trickster (ELD-309) / Oko, Thief of Crowns (ELD-197) distinction is load-bearing for any bundle copy.** The Trickster is the planeswalker-deck-exclusive precon-tier rare (6 mana, 4 loyalty, harmless), reprinted on The List in 2023. The Thief of Crowns is the main-set mythic (3 mana, 4 loyalty, format-warping) that got banned across four formats within months. Both are the same character. Any bundle copy that surfaces the meme-Oko has to identify *which Oko* — the precon's Trickster (in our corpus, harmless) or the main-set's Thief of Crowns (not in our corpus, format-warping). Misidentifying these two is the single most common Oko-related buyer-confusion vector and the bundle copy should fend it off explicitly.

- **The Oko-as-meme register is sentiment-tier community history, not in-fiction canon.** The character's lore-arc (trickster-fae-king-of-Eldraine) is canonical; the character's notoriety-arc (the spreadsheet-of-broken-cards) is community sentiment. Bundle prose should foreground the canonical and surface the sentiment as a footnote.

- **The fae-court depiction style differs across artists.** Chris Rallis's PLST-309/269 art renders Oko as a lean shirtless figure with branching dark antler-crown summoning twin ribbons of energy (Lorwyn-Shadowmoor mode). Deruchenko Alexander's ELD-312 art renders him as a stag-headed humanoid in a red cape (full Wildered-Quest shapeshift mode). Zezhou Chen's ELD-310 art doesn't depict Oko at all — only his fae attendants. Future Oko-arc cards may surface other artists rendering the character in other shapeshift forms (drake, raven, crested eagle, antler-dissolving-into-sparks per MTG Wiki); each new aspect edges here as a distinct beat, parallel to how Tamiyo's pre-compleation and Compleated-Sage phases each anchor here as distinct aspects on the Tamiyo node.

- **A future Kellan character node** (Kellan, the Fae-Blooded, Wilds of Eldraine; Kellan, the Kid, MOM; Kellan, Daring Traveler, OTJ) would edge here as the child-of-the-act relation. Kellan has multiple printings and would clear the character-node threshold on his own; defer until the character-to-character edge category is formalized.

- **A future Garruk character node** (Garruk, Cursed Huntsman, ELD-264 — the paired-precon Eldraine-block planeswalker; Garruk's many other printings) would edge here as the *Wildered Quest* antagonist-or-foil relation. Oko escaped Garruk "in the form of a stag" per the novella; Garruk is the Wild Hunt's licensed huntsman who Oko has subverted into the unwitting Kenrith-stag-hunter. Defer.

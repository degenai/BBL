# Refuges (Khans-block common gain-land cycle)

Magic: The Gathering's ten-card common dual-land cycle introduced in Khans of Tarkir (KTK, 2014-09-26) and reprinted with all-new art in Fate Reforged (FRF, 2015-01-23), covering all ten two-color combinations. Every member shares identical rules text — *"This land enters tapped. When this land enters, you gain 1 life. {T}: Add {color-1} or {color-2}."* — and the cycle is colloquially known as the "refuges" or the Khans gain-lands. The Fate Reforged reprint occupied the basic-land slot in FRF boosters to support the KTK+FRF+DTK rotating-draft environment Wizards designed for the block; per Sam Stoddard's "A Fetching Look at Fate Reforged" (Wizards.com, 2014-12-24), the cycle's full FRF reprint was a deliberate mana-fixing decision analogous to Return to Ravnica dual lands appearing in Dragon's Maze boosters.

The cycle's defining narrative-design feature is that the FRF reprints depict the *same Tarkir locations* the KTK originals depicted — but rendered 1,280 years in the past, the timeline Sarkhan Vol travels back to in the FRF storyline in order to alter the outcome of the battle between Ugin and Nicol Bolas. A 2015 art review by Scent of a Gamer read the before/after paintings as a subtle narrative argument that the clans were more destructive stewards of the land than the dragon warlords who preceded them — the FRF-past paintings show wilder, less managed landscapes; the KTK-present paintings show the same locations after clan ascendance, with more visible architecture, clearing, and infrastructure.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text for the ten Khans-block refuges across both KTK and FRF printings, the designer-coordinated gain-1-life-on-ETB mechanic as a cycle feature, and the FRF-vs-KTK before/after paintings as a paired-printing narrative-design conceit. The cycle is the BBL corpus's clearest anchor for the *stewardship-comparison-across-timelines* register — every member is the same location seen across two epochs of clan vs. dragon-warlord-era land management, and the cycle reads as ten paired arguments about who tended the land more carefully.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Bloodfell Caves** (FRF-165, B/R, Adam Paquette art) — dark cavernous interior with billowing fabric or smoke and a central standing stone lit by warm light below. The Tarkir-past version of the same caves Paquette also painted for KTK-229.
- **Blossoming Sands** (FRF-166, G/W, Sam Burley art) — sun-drenched rolling hills with a natural stone arch and a small clifftop structure. The Tarkir-past version of the same Selesnya-coded location Burley also painted for KTK-231 and has continued to paint across 17 total printings of the card.
- **Dismal Backwater** (FRF-168, U/B, Sam Burley art) — overgrown waterway under a near-impenetrable canopy of gnarled roots, with faint purple-green light filtering through. The Tarkir-past version of the same Dimir-coded location Burley also painted for KTK-232.
- **Rugged Highlands** (FRF-170, R/G, Eytan Zana art) — alpine landscape with two massive rocky peaks, a river valley, and dark conifer forest under twilight. The Tarkir-past version of the same Gruul-coded location Zana also painted for KTK-240 and has continued to paint across 17 total printings.
- **Tranquil Cove** (KTK-246, W/U, John Avon art) — panoramic coastal landscape painted from an elevated cliffside vantage, a long crescent of pale sand sweeping around a calm turquoise bay toward distant headlands under late-afternoon magic-hour light. The Tarkir-present (clan epoch) printing of the Azorius-coded coastal location; the cycle's FRF reprint of Tranquil Cove was the specific painting Scent of a Gamer's 2015 review singled out as the cycle's clearest before/after stewardship evidence.

## The design thesis

The refuges cycle's most BBL-relevant property is that its mechanical text and its visual register are co-designed across two sets to make a paired argument. The 1-life-on-ETB clause makes the cycle the budget-multicolor mana-fixing baseline for Pauper and Commander — these are the lands that show up when a player has no money to spend on rare duals. The FRF reprint with new art at the same locations 1,280 years prior is the cycle's narrative-design move: each card is a paired before/after painting, and the cycle as a whole asks the viewer to compare the Tarkir-past (dragon-warlord epoch, wild and uncleared) with the Tarkir-present (clan epoch, settled and built). Scent of a Gamer's 2015 art review read this paired-painting structure as a deliberate-and-subtle stewardship argument on Wizards' part: the clans, despite being the protagonist factions of KTK, are visually framed as more destructive stewards than the dragon warlords they replaced. The reading is grounded in the FRF Tranquil Cove vs. KTK Tranquil Cove pairing specifically, which the reviewer highlighted as the cycle's clearest before/after evidence.

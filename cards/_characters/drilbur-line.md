# Drilbur evolutionary line (Drilbur / Excadrill)

A two-stage Ground-to-Ground/Steel evolutionary line and the canonical Unova-region mole line introduced in Pokemon Black and White (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 2010). The line comprises **Drilbur** (National Pokedex no. 529, Ground-type, base form, the "Mole Pokemon" whose canonical *"makes its way swiftly through the soil by putting both claws together and rotating at high speed"* design literalizes a compact-scale rotating-drill motif) and **Excadrill** (no. 530, Ground/Steel-type, Stage 1, evolves from Drilbur at level 31, the "Subterrene Pokemon" known in Pokedex canon as the **Drill King**, whose dual-type gain adds Steel to its base Ground typing at evolution and whose canonical *"can tunnel through the terrain at speeds of over 90 mph"* flavor renders the full-body drill at apex scale). The line is one of Generation V's most-cited two-stage species lines, built end-to-end around a single design conceit: the creature *is* the drill, rendered first at the compact rotating-claws scale (Drilbur) and then at the bipedal full-body-drill scale (Excadrill). The Steel-typing gain at evolution converts the soft-bodied mole into a metal-clawed tunneling predator, a designer-coordinated body-plan-and-type progression rather than an incidental dual-type addition.

## The design thesis

The Drilbur line's most BBL-relevant property is the **creature-as-drill design conceit** rendered as a two-stage scale progression, paired with the **Steel-typing gain at evolution** as the line's structural signature. Where the Larvitar line carries a consume-the-mountain-become-the-mountain canon and the Staraptor line carries a leaves-the-flock-at-evolution canon, the Drilbur line carries a **the-creature-is-the-drill canon** — Drilbur canonically functions as the compact rotating-claws mole (bringing both claws together and spinning to bore through soil); Excadrill canonically functions as the bipedal full-body drill-predator (the Drill King, tunneling at over 90 mph, its tunnels mistakable for naturally-formed terrain). The line's evolution arc is designer-coordinated as a single design conceit rendered at two scales, with the Ground-to-Ground/Steel type-shift at evolution converting the soft-bodied digger into a metal-clawed industrial-scale tunneler — the visible cost-and-payoff of evolution rendered as a body-plan-and-type progression.

The line is also a **Chinese Zodiac grid placement of record**. The `diamondlegendz/pokemon-zodiac` canonical ruleset DATA grid lists *"Drilbur-Excadrill (mole, Ground/Steel — borderline rodent)"* explicitly as a member of the **Earth Rat** cell — one of the grid's four genuine depth-5 clusters (the "Deep Four"), alongside Skwovet, Sandshrew, and Rattata in the same cell. The placement follows the functional-agrarian R1 slot criterion (鼠 = any rodent; the mole reads as a borderline-rodent agrarian digger) and the R5 element rule (Ground = Earth; the line's Ground typing lands the Earth column cleanly — the Steel subtype on Excadrill does not override the Ground primary, and Drilbur is pure Ground). This node does **not** wire to the [[chinese-zodiac]] hub directly (the hub is deliberately card-edge-disconnected per `bbl-bundles-are-destructive-on-graph`); the grid placement is recorded here as scholarship so a future "Deep Four" or "Earth Element" Discrete Lair can cite the line's cell membership from a stable character-layer node rather than from scattered card-edge wikilinks.

For BBL bundle work, the line offers three strong narrative threads: a **creature-as-drill** thread anchored on the species' Pokedex canon (Drilbur's rotating-claws compact drill to Excadrill's 90-mph full-body Drill King tunneling as the franchise's clearest one-to-one design-conceit-to-mechanic mapping at the two-stage scope), a **labor / excavation / tunneling** thread anchored on the line's Ground-and-digging design coding (the cohort is a natural anchor for any excavation- or earthwork-themed Discrete Lair, with the corpus's Drilbur CEC-114 already carrying a `labor` hub tag), and a **Chinese Zodiac Earth Rat** thread anchored on the line's grid-of-record placement in the pokemon-zodiac Deep Four cluster. The within-Vivid-Voltage Drilbur 091 + Excadrill 122 pair is the corpus's strongest within-set Drilbur-line cohesion stack — both base and evolved stages in a single set — structurally parallel to the within-Darkness-Ablaze Larvitar 086 + Pupitar 087 pair from the Larvitar line cohort.

## Caveats

- **Five corpus prints across two of two line members; full line coverage in-corpus.** Both evolutionary stages of the line are represented in current inventory — three Drilbur prints at base stage (Cosmic Eclipse, Vivid Voltage, Black Bolt) and two Excadrill prints at Stage-1 (Vivid Voltage, Fusion Strike). The Drilbur line is a **two-stage line, not a three-stage line**, so this is full-triptych-equivalent (full-line) coverage — contrast with [[larvitar-line]], [[mareep-line]], [[staraptor-line]], and [[caterpie-line]], which are three-stage lines at partial coverage. The cohort is at full canonical species coverage; a Discrete Lair built on the line can ship as a complete-line artifact. Membership predicates on species identity, not set, era, printing variant, or trivia-pass status.

- **A pre-existing within-corpus 1:1 evolution-mirror edge predates this node (Drilbur CEC-114 ↔ Excadrill FUS-183, wave 27).** Before this node existed, a wave-27 Edgelord pass drew a 1:1 evolution-mirror Connections edge between Drilbur CEC-114 and Excadrill FUS-183. That edge is left in place — it is a correct base-to-evolved pairing and does no harm — but it is now **subsumed in function** by this line node: the cohort relationship is absorbed at the species-line level, and the remaining three prints (Drilbur VIV-091, Drilbur BLB-045, Excadrill VIV-122) attach to this node via `characters:` frontmatter pointers rather than via additional 1:1 card-to-card edges. No further 1:1 evolution edges between Drilbur and Excadrill prints are warranted; drawing them would be duplicate edges pointing at this absent-no-longer center. A future enlightened-Edgelord pass may elect to dissolve the wave-27 CEC-114 ↔ FUS-183 mirror as redundant-once-subsumed and replace it with a stronger cross-node edge, but that is a separate single-operation decision and is staged, not executed here.

- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like `larvitar-line`, `mareep-line`, `staraptor-line`, `caterpie-line`, `charizard-line`, `ralts-line`, `elemental-monkey-trio`, `galarian-regional-forms`, `fear-of-cycle`, and `dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle`, this is a collective species-line cohort hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer already accepts collective named entities). A future `_evolutionary-lines/` or `_cycles/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the other designer-coordinated cohort nodes; when that split is commissioned, this node moves cleanly. For now, single layer, same precedent.

- **The Chinese Zodiac Earth Rat placement is grid-of-record, not a hub edge.** The `diamondlegendz/pokemon-zodiac` DATA grid lists Drilbur-Excadrill in the Earth Rat cell, but this node does not and will not carry a `related_hubs: [chinese-zodiac]` entry — the [[chinese-zodiac]] hub is deliberately card-edge-disconnected and cohort-node-disconnected by design (`bbl-bundles-are-destructive-on-graph`, wave 81 P3). The grid placement is recorded in the design-thesis section as scholarship; a future Earth-Rat or Deep-Four Discrete Lair cites the line's cell membership from this stable node. The Steel subtype on Excadrill is noted but does not move the line to the Metal column: R5 maps Ground to Earth, the line's Ground typing is the primary on both stages (Drilbur is pure Ground), and the Earth placement is the grid's own verdict.

- **Sibling Generation V Unova-line nodes are future candidates as their species-lines accumulate corpus prints.** Generation V introduced the Unova regional Pokedex (no. 494-649) with multiple new evolutionary lines designed alongside the Drilbur line (the [[pawniard-line]] Bisharp line and the [[rufflet-line]] Braviary line are already in corpus as nodes; the Snivy / Servine / Serperior grass-starter line, the Tepig / Pignite / Emboar fire-starter line, the Oshawott / Dewott / Samurott water-starter line, the Sandile / Krokorok / Krookodile desert line, and many others are Unova-introduced siblings). Sibling Generation V line nodes get commissioned when threshold is met (3+ corpus prints across 2+ stages). The Snivy line is a near-threshold sibling — Snivy BLB-001 and Servine BLB-002 are in corpus.

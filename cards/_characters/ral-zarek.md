# Ral Zarek

Storm-mage planeswalker, Izzet operative, and the modern guild's operational center. Introduced in Return to Ravnica (2012) as Niv-Mizzet's lieutenant; foregrounded across the *Dragon's Maze* maze-run arc (2013), the *Guilds of Ravnica* / *War of the Spark* Bolas-defection arc (2018–2019), and the post-WAR succession in which he, Jace Beleren, and Vraska resurrect Niv-Mizzet as Ravnica's Living Guildpact. The `— Ral` planeswalker subtype on the type-line is reserved for printings of this single character; canonical from Ral Zarek (Return to Ravnica, 2012) through every subsequent legendary planeswalker bearing the name. Sub-node under [[izzet-league]] — Ral is the guild's most-printed individual operative, but his identity is constitutively a guild-officer identity, and the parent faction node carries the institutional context this sub-node refers back into.

## What this node anchors

Card art and flavor text that depicts Ral Zarek himself — under any of his titles (Storm Conduit, Izzet Viceroy, Caller of Storms) — and cards whose narrative subject is the act of *Ral doing the guild-officer work*: the *Dragon's Maze* maze-run, the WAR-era Bolas defection, the post-WAR ritual of resurrecting Niv-Mizzet, the canonical "Ral's <Spell>" attribution cycle in which non-planeswalker spells are flavor-attributed to him as caster. The character is large enough to split into distinct narrative aspects across his printings, and the BBL graph can edge those aspects together through this anchor sub-node rather than card-to-card mirroring.

The Ral anchor inherits all the Izzet-League institutional context — stratified guild labor, experimental-inquiry rhetoric, magewright-caste social capital — and overlays the *named operative who actually runs the floor*: where the parent faction node is the institution, this sub-node is the foreman.

**Aspects in the corpus (current):**

- **War of the Spark — Ravnica defection, lazotep-melting-point moment** — Ral on Ravnica during Bolas's invasion, framing the battle as a materials-science problem (canonically Izzet) while defecting from Bolas's payroll.
  - **Ral's Outburst** (WAR-212, uncommon, Joseph Meehan art) — Ral mid-cast, channeling a violent burst of forked blue-white lightning across a turbulent sky. Mechanically a `{2}{U}{R}` UR instant: 3 damage to any target + impulse-style top-two card-select. Flavor text: *"Time to find the melting point of lazotep." —Ral Zarek*. Lazotep is the alchemical material Nicol Bolas used to encase his Eternal army during the *War of the Spark* invasion; per MTG Wiki's Ral Zarek page, this card depicts the WAR-arc moment when Ral defects from Bolas's payroll and turns on the Eternal army alongside Jace, Vraska, and Kaya. `ip_verified: true` via Scryfall flavor-text attribution.

- **Modern Horizons 3 Saga — Implicit Maze discovery, retrospective rendering** — Ral's *Dragon's Maze*-block storyline rendered as a three-chapter Saga, depicted in volcanic-surfing register.
  - **Ral and the Implicit Maze** (MH3-132, uncommon, Andrew Mar art) — Ral in a white outfit with geometric red patterning crouching dynamically on a black volcanic surface surrounded by flowing lava and erupting flame, one arm raised overhead and the other trailing behind, arcs of fire looping around his body in curving ribbons. Mechanically a `{2}{U}{R}` Saga: chapter I sweeps damage, chapter II is a Faithless-Looting-variant exile-play, chapter III creates a Spellgorger Weird token. The Implicit Maze is the hidden network of mana paths beneath Ravnica's ten guild districts, designed by Azor I as a Guildpact-failsafe and discovered by Ral on Niv-Mizzet's orders. Per MTG Wiki's Implicit Maze page, the maze drove the *Dragon's Maze* 2013 block's championship arc — Ral killed the Izzet champion Melek mid-race for guild-runner privilege, but Jace ultimately won the race. The MH3 Saga is the retrospective rendering of that arc nearly a decade later.

The two corpus cards span Ral's lowest-profile storyline beat (the maze-run, retold as MH3 Saga) and one of his highest-stakes (the WAR defection). Both cards depict Ral; neither is a `— Ral` planeswalker card — that subtype is held by the named planeswalker printings (Ral Zarek RTR, Ral Izzet Viceroy GRN, Ral Storm Conduit WAR, Ral Crackling Wit MOC, Ral Monsoon Mage MH3), none of which are currently in the corpus. When a named-planeswalker Ral printing lands, it edges here as the primary anchor and the existing two aspects fall into place as supporting beats.

## The attribution-cycle question

A notable Wizards-canonical pattern: the "Ral's <Spell>" cycle — Ral's Dispersal (RNA), Ral's Staticaster (RNA), Ral's Vanguard (MH3), Ral's Outburst (WAR), and others — is a designer-stated flavor-attribution cycle in which non-planeswalker spells are canonically cast by Ral as character. Per MTG Wiki and Scryfall's `name:"Ral's"` search, the cycle currently runs to 5+ printings across the *Ravnica Allegiance* / *War of the Spark* / *Modern Horizons 3* arc. The cycle reads as the in-fiction equivalent of a magewright's lab notebook — Ral inventing and recording spells across his guild-officer career.

The BBL graph treats the attribution cycle the same way the Tamiyo node treats `Tamiyo Meets the Story Circle`: cards in the cycle that depict Ral edge here directly (via `characters:` frontmatter); cards in the cycle that name him in flavor without depicting him (a hypothetical Ral-attributed spell with art of the spell-effect only, no Ral on the canvas) edge here via the canonical-name attribution test, not the depicted-figure test. Both standards apply.

## Caveats

- This node is the character Ral Zarek himself, not the set of cards whose **name** mentions him. Depicted-figure or canonical-name-attribution tests apply, parallel to the [[jace-beleren]] / [[tamiyo]] standards.
- **Ral is constitutively an Izzet operative.** Unlike planeswalkers whose loyalty shifts across arcs (Liliana, Vraska), Ral has been canonically Izzet from his 2012 debut through every subsequent printing. The sub-node-under-parent-faction relation is structural, not just thematic — Ral's identity as a character cannot be separated from his guild-officer identity. Bundle work that anchors on Ral should anchor on the [[izzet-league]] context simultaneously.
- **The Niv-Mizzet resurrection ritual is a three-way planeswalker collaboration** (Ral + Jace Beleren + Vraska) at the climax of WAR. Vraska is not currently in the corpus as a card or character node; Jace is at [[jace-beleren]]. A future ritual-of-the-act character-to-character edge between this node and [[jace-beleren]] could be drawn when the character-to-character edge category is formalized; defer.
- **The death of Melek mid-maze-run is a notable Ral-character footnote.** Ral killed his fellow Izzet runner Melek during the *Dragon's Maze* 2013 race to claim guild-runner privilege; the act is canonical (MTG Wiki Implicit Maze page) and reads as the operational underside of the guild's "we're all in this together" rhetoric — Ral is loyal to Niv-Mizzet, not to his peers. The MH3-132 Saga retells the maze-run without depicting Melek's death; future Ral-arc bundle copy may surface this beat as the labor-rhetoric-versus-actual-hierarchy thesis material the parent Izzet node already cites.
- **Ral is canonically queer.** Per MTG Wiki and Wizards' *Magic Story* articles, Ral is in a long-term partnership with Tomik Vrona, a House Dimir / Orzhov-affiliated character introduced in *Guilds of Ravnica*. The relationship is named-and-canonical, not subtext; it is a Ral-identity footnote that becomes load-bearing for any storefront bundle copy that wants to surface the character's full lore register (Wizards has been progressively more open about it across the *War of the Spark: Ravnica* novel and subsequent story articles).

# Survival archetype cohort (Duskmourn improvised-weapon survivors and their tools)

A *Duskmourn: House of Horror* design cohort spanning **three coordinated axes** of the set's prey-side vocabulary: the **Survival** ability word (a triggered ability that fires at the beginning of the controller's second main phase if the creature is still tapped), the **Creature — Human Survivor** subtype that was created to carry it (Scryfall `s:dsk t:survivor` returns 14 members within the set), and the **improvised-weapon Equipment and Aura** artifacts depicting the survivors' makeshift tools. Per Wizards' set-mechanics article (Matt Tabak, 2024-08-31) and Mark Rosewater's *Top of the Duskmourning* design diaries (Making Magic, September 2024), all three axes are designer-coordinated — Wizards explicitly cites **Cabin in the Woods (2011, Whedon/Goddard)** as the source-material register for the "survivors with improvised weapons" trope alongside slashers, killer-dolls, phobia-personification, and the haunted-vernacular-place axes that DSK was built top-down from. The cohort is the BBL corpus's clearest anchor for *Duskmourn*'s prey-side register — the figures who run, hide, and pick up the nearest blunt object — and the structural **opposing pole** of the toy-horror cohort (haunted house wields corrupted toys; survivors wield improvised weapons).

## What this node anchors

Card art, oracle text, type-line, and flavor text for the *Duskmourn* cards whose subject is **a survivor of the haunted house or the tool they fight with**. Membership predicates on a three-signal test, any one of which is sufficient:

1. **Type-line carries `Creature — Human Survivor`** — the subtype created by Wizards in DSK to mark the cohort at the rules layer.
2. **Oracle text carries the `Survival —` ability-word trigger** — the keyword tethered to the Survivor subtype but which can also appear on other typed creatures (per Wizards' mechanics article).
3. **Card depicts a survivor in mid-flight, mid-defense, or mid-improvised-strike, or depicts the improvised weapon as the subject** — the visual register the cohort is built from, including non-creature artifacts (Equipment, Aura) whose subject is the survivor's tool.

Defiant Survivor and Conductive Machete each carry the [[manifest-dread]] trigger, making the cohort mechanically dual-coded with the set's signature graveyard-feed primitive.

## The cohort thesis

The Survival cohort's most BBL-relevant property is its **prey-turns-predator arc** as the set's narrative engine. Per Rosewater's design diaries, Wizards built DSK top-down from a horror-trope taxonomy in which the "Cabin in the Woods" register — the trope-aware survivor narrative where the cornered prey makes a stand with whatever they can grab — is one of the load-bearing source axes. The cohort renders this at three coordinated layers:

- **At the rules layer**, the Survival ability triggers only if the creature is *still tapped at the beginning of your second main phase* — i.e. only if the creature **survived** the combat step it was committed to. The keyword is mechanically a survival-check: did this creature live through the swing? If yes, it gets stronger. The rules are the trope.
- **At the type-line layer**, the Human Survivor subtype was created to mark the cohort. Glimmer Seeker is *the explorer who survived*. Cautious Survivor is *the camerawoman who survived (whatever she saw)*. Defiant Survivor is *the kid who decided the dark should be scared*. Rootwise Survivor is *the bearded fighter who learned to talk to the trees*. The subtype literally names the cohort's affective register.
- **At the art layer**, the improvised-weapon artifacts give the survivors their tools — and the tools are the cohort's most legible thesis statement. A baseball bat with nails wrapped in wire and crackling with green electricity is the Cabin-in-the-Woods register's most distilled object: the mundane sports gear turned into a stake of last resort. A machete trailing a power cable is the same trope at a different angle. A tambourine grabbed off a wall is the trope at its most desperate.

The cohort's affective range spans **fight** (Defiant Survivor's "The dark should be scared of me!"), **flight** (Norin's "Why would I stick around to find out?"), **document** (Cautious Survivor's broken-recorder flavor), and **wonder** (Glimmer Seeker's "Well, hello there"). Bundle prose can pick any pole or the spectrum.

The cohort also **structurally opposes** the [[dsk-toy-horror]] cohort: where the toy-horror cohort is the predator-side artifact-creatures (the house weaponizes the corrupted childhood objects), the Survival cohort is the prey-side artifact-creatures-and-Equipment (the survivors weaponize the improvised tools). The two cohorts together anchor the set's full predator-vs-prey vocabulary at the artifact / Equipment / Aura layer. A Discrete Lair built on the Survival cohort would canonically edge against a Discrete Lair built on the toy-horror cohort — two halves of DSK's combat thesis.

## Caveats

- **Cohort threshold met at 8/N; full canonical roster not yet enumerated in BBL.** Eight cohort members are in BBL corpus and pointered to this node (see `appears_on:`). The canonical Survivor-subtype roster is 14 cards within the set per Scryfall (`s:dsk t:survivor`); additional members (Acrobatic Cheerleader, Savior of the Small, Veteran Survivor, etc., per the Glimmer Seeker trivia EDHREC synergy list) are cohort-eligible and should attach here as they land via future Collectr CSV uploads. The improvised-weapon Equipment / Aura roster is bounded by the set's full Equipment-with-survivor-art roster on Scryfall plus any non-Equipment artifacts whose subject is a survivor's tool; a future Scryfall sweep should enumerate the full roster and surface candidates.
- **Three-signal predicate; any one signal is sufficient.** Membership predicates on (a) Survivor subtype OR (b) Survival keyword OR (c) improvised-weapon artifact depicting a survivor's tool. Some cards may carry more than one signal (Defiant Survivor carries both subtype and keyword; Conductive Machete carries the improvised-weapon predicate and the manifest-dread trigger). Cards carrying only the Survival keyword without the Survivor subtype are cohort-eligible per Wizards' mechanics article (which notes Survival can appear on non-Survivor creatures).
- **Norin, Swift Survivalist's IP resolution is pending.** The corpus card flags `suspected_ip: Norin the Wary` at med confidence, `ip_verified: false`. Norin the Wary is a recurring named character across multiple sets (Coldsnap onward); the DSK card's "Survivalist" epithet and the depicted figure's fleeing-survivor register strongly suggest the canonical character. A future trivia-bot or character-node pass should verify the IP linkage and either flip `ip_verified: true` or clear the field. Independent of that resolution, the card sits in this cohort on the type-line and flavor-text predicate alone.
- **Modeled as a character-layer node, not a hub or symbol.** Like [[dsk-toy-horror]], [[fear-of-cycle]], [[dsk-unlucky-lands-cycle]], [[eld-cinderella-cycle]], this is a collective designer-coordinated cohort entity hosted on the character layer per the Endriders precedent (the character layer accepts both individual named characters and collective cohorts). A future `_cycles/` or `_cohorts/` layer could re-home this entry alongside the other designer-confirmed flavor cycles when that split is commissioned; for now, single layer, same precedent.
- **Cohort overlaps with [[manifest-dread]] at two members.** Defiant Survivor and Conductive Machete each carry a manifest-dread trigger inside their cohort-defining oracle text. The cards point at **both** this node and `[[manifest-dread]]` via separate frontmatter fields (`characters:` and `symbols:` respectively). The dual-pointer is intentional: the cards are members of both cohorts; bundle assembly can pull on either thread.

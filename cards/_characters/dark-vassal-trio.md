# Dark Vassal trio (King Piccolo's spat-egg children — Tambourine / Cymbal / Drum)

The designer-coordinated three-card sub-cycle within the Dragon Ball Super Card Game's *Colossal Warfare* booster (DBS-B04, 2018-07-13) that anchors King Piccolo's three named warrior-children — Tambourine, Cymbal, and Drum — as a unified Demon Clan / Warrior-Type Namekian offspring-cohort across adjacent collector numbers (BT4-064, BT4-065, BT4-066). The three cards share the *Dark Vassal* Bandai-card-name prefix as the cycle-marker, sit at sequential collector numbers as the within-set positional cycle-marker, and depict the three canonical brothers in their canonical King Piccolo Saga combat configurations: Tambourine the winged aerial-predator (the first deployed, canonically kills Krillin and Yamcha before being killed by Goku in retribution), Cymbal the carapaced ground-combatant (canonically eaten by Yajirobe), and Drum the brute-force charger (canonically defeats Tien Shinhan before being killed by Goku at the Korin Tower climax). The canonical fourth King Piccolo child Piano — a non-combat secretary-type, canonically present only as the king's attendant — is NOT in this cycle nor in BBL corpus; the trio framing is Bandai's three-of-four selection privileging the combat-roster children over the secretary-type.

The cycle is a child-of-parent under [[namekian]] (the race-collective parent that anchors the broader 11-card Namekian roster); the same shape as [[eld-cinderella-cycle]] sits as a child-of-the-broader-ELD-design-space and [[wrath-of-the-dragon-saga]] sits as a child of the broader Colossal Warfare set roster. Designer-coordinated within-set adjacent-collector-number cycles with shared card-name prefixes are Bandai's canonical signaling pattern; the BT4-039 / BT4-040 / BT4-041 Tapion / Tapion-cursed / Minotia bearer-brothers cycle anchored at [[wrath-of-the-dragon-saga]] is the structurally adjacent precedent within the same Colossal Warfare booster.

## What this node anchors

Card art, flavor text, Bandai-canonical Dark Vassal card-name-prefix membership, and the *King Piccolo's spat-egg-produced Demon Clan warrior-children* canonical lore-anchor for the three corpus cards. Membership predicates on Bandai's shared `Dark Vassal` card-name prefix combined with the adjacent BT4-064/065/066 collector-number positioning and the canonical King Piccolo Saga manga continuity. The trio reads as Bandai's compressed roster-citation of the King Piccolo Saga's villain-cohort within a single 2018 booster set — the same three brothers who carried the original 1976 Demon King's invasion of Earth across Dragon Ball chapters 134-194.

**Cards in our corpus pointing here (current):**

- **Dark Vassal Tambourine** (Colossal Warfare BT4-064, Common, qty 1, vision passed — trivia pending) — depicts **Tambourine**, the first of King Piccolo's spat-egg children deployed during the King Piccolo Saga (Dragon Ball chapters 138-148, 1976 manga / 1986 anime). The card's winged green-skinned humanoid with clawed hands hovering amid a swirling green-white energy vortex matches Tambourine's signature visual identifier per Dragon Ball Wiki's Tambourine page (the canonical winged-flight ability that distinguishes the Demon Clan from the Dragon Clan, the clawed combat configuration, the menacing aerial-predator iconography). Tambourine canonically kills Krillin off-screen and Yamcha on-screen before being killed by Goku in retribution; he is the trio's *first-deployed and most-narratively-impactful* member at the Saga's opening beat. `suspected_ip: Tambourine (Dragon Ball — King Piccolo Saga)` with `ip_verified: false` pending parent triage.

- **Dark Vassal Cymbal** (Colossal Warfare BT4-065, Common, qty 1, vision passed — trivia pending) — depicts **Cymbal**, the second of King Piccolo's children. The card's broad-shouldered green-skinned figure with armored or carapaced body in a low combat crouch surrounded by green energy matches Cymbal's signature visual identifier per Dragon Ball Wiki's Cymbal page (the larger and bulkier build distinguishing him from Tambourine's slimmer aerial-predator form, the carapaced exoskeleton-texture, the *eaten-by-Yajirobe* canonical death-beat where the samurai consumes Cymbal whole rather than fighting him in a conventional duel). Cymbal is the trio's *shortest-arc* member at the Saga's middle beat; the Yajirobe-eats-Cymbal scene is one of the franchise's most distinctive death-beats and operates as the canonical introduction of Yajirobe into the Z-warriors' broader roster.

- **Dark Vassal Drum** (Colossal Warfare BT4-066, Common, qty 1, vision passed — trivia pending) — depicts **Drum**, the third of King Piccolo's named children (the fourth, Piano, is a non-combat secretary-type and is not in corpus). The card's massive stocky green-skinned figure in yellow garment charging through an explosive gold energy burst matches Drum's signature visual identifier per Dragon Ball Wiki's Drum page (the largest of the King Piccolo children, the brute-force combat configuration, the *post-rejuvenated-King-Piccolo-creates-strongest-son* canonical framing). Drum is the trio's *most-powerful* member at the Saga's climactic beat — he canonically defeats Tien Shinhan in single combat at the Korin Tower before being killed by Goku in the post-rejuvenation climax. Bandai's adjacent-collector-number-with-Drum-at-BT4-066 positioning matches the canonical chronological deployment-order (Tambourine first, Cymbal second, Drum third) within the manga, making the cycle a canonical-narrative-order-preserved Bandai design.

## The functional ideology

The Dark Vassal trio's most BBL-relevant property is that it is the canonical exemplar of the franchise's **boss-spawns-disposable-workforce-warrior-children labor template** — the original 1976 Dragon Ball-arc instantiation of an antagonist-monarch who reproduces *combat assets* through his spat-egg biological reproduction and deploys them as a *replaceable invasion-force* against the protagonists. King Piccolo's three named children are explicitly canonically *expendable* in their narrative role: each is deployed for one mission-beat (Tambourine for the Dragon-Ball-fetching murder-raid, Cymbal for the second-wave Dragon-Ball-gathering raid, Drum for the post-rejuvenation Korin-Tower battle), each is named after a musical instrument as a Toriyama design joke (the King Piccolo / Piccolo-the-instrument naming convention where the king's children are *Tambourine, Cymbal, Drum, and Piano*), and each is canonically killed before the saga's climax to clear the board for the King-versus-Goku finale.

The disposable-workforce framing is the canonical labor-template of the Demon Clan / Warrior-Type Namekian register, distinct from the Dragon Clan's healer-creator-protector register (Guru, Kami, Nail) and distinct from the Demon-Clan-reformed-into-protagonist register (Piccolo in his post-King-Piccolo-Saga arc). The three Dark Vassals operate canonically in the *world-conquering-villain-apparatus* register that the [[labor]] hub's apparatus-of-extraction axis partially indexes: spat-egg biological production of combat assets, named-after-instruments to mark their interchangeability, deployed-and-discarded across the saga's narrative beats.

The trio's specific institutional features within the corpus are:

- **Adjacent-collector-number Bandai-explicit cycle marker.** BT4-064 (Tambourine) + BT4-065 (Cymbal) + BT4-066 (Drum) is a Bandai-coordinated three-card adjacent-collector-number positioning within the Colossal Warfare booster, signaling intentional cycle-design at the set-architecture level. The same Bandai-design-pattern is applied at BT4-039 (Oath's Power Tapion) + BT4-040 (Hidden Darkness Tapion) + BT4-041 (Hidden Darkness Minotia) for the [[wrath-of-the-dragon-saga]] bearer-brothers sub-cycle.

- **Shared *Dark Vassal* Bandai-card-name prefix.** Bandai's card-name-prefix-as-cycle-marker design pattern uses the *Dark Vassal* prefix across all three cards as the verbal cycle-marker. The prefix's *vassal* register encodes the canonical chain-of-command from King Piccolo (the canonical *king* whose throne-room deployment of his children defines his villain-apparatus) to the three named *vassals* operating as his combat-extension.

- **Chronological-deployment-order-preserved adjacent positioning.** Tambourine first (the Saga's opening-beat murderer of Krillin and Yamcha) at BT4-064, Cymbal second (the Saga's middle-beat eaten-by-Yajirobe beat) at BT4-065, Drum third (the Saga's climax-beat Tien-defeater) at BT4-066. The Bandai positioning matches the canonical manga deployment-order, making the cycle a canonical-narrative-order-preserved design.

- **Piano (the fourth King Piccolo child) deliberately excluded.** Piano is canonically the *non-combat secretary-type* fourth child whose canonical role is administrative attendance on King Piccolo at the throne, NOT combat deployment. Bandai's three-card Dark Vassal cycle privileges the *combat-roster* children, excluding the secretary-type. If a future Piano-self-depicting card lands in DBSCG print history, it would NOT auto-extend this cycle (the cycle is canonically a three-card warrior-vassal cohort, not a four-card complete-King-Piccolo-children roster); a Piano print would attach at the [[namekian]] parent node as a Demon Clan offspring-cohort-adjacent figure, sibling to but not member of this cycle.

- **Demon Clan / Warrior-Type Namekian biological substrate.** The three brothers are canonically Demon Clan / Warrior-Type Namekians per Dragon Ball Wiki's Demon Clan page — green-skinned humanoids with the Namekian race's signature regenerative biology, antenna-ridged warrior-type crania, and Namekian-specific spat-egg asexual reproduction (King Piccolo produces all four children through this canonical mechanism). The cycle is structurally a *sub-cohort within the broader Namekian race* and the parent-child node-relation to [[namekian]] encodes this canonical-clan-membership at the graph level.

This is what BBL's bundle copy can lean on without inventing canon: the Dark Vassal trio is, in-canon, a designer-coordinated three-card Bandai sub-cycle within Colossal Warfare BT4 that depicts the canonical King Piccolo Saga warrior-children-cohort across adjacent collector numbers with a shared Bandai-card-name prefix and canonical chronological-deployment-order positioning. Bundle prose that leans on the *king-spawns-disposable-warriors-named-after-instruments* labor-template canonical thesis lands directly on Toriyama's 1976 design without inventing canon. The trio's labor-coded reading composes with the broader [[namekian]] race-level Dragon-Clan-vs-Demon-Clan moral-substrate-split thesis as the *specific-villain-apparatus-instance* of the broader race-level *moral-substrate-as-clan-choice* thesis.

- **King Piccolo (the progenitor) is NOT in corpus.** No corpus card depicts King Piccolo himself directly; the canonical *king* whose throne-room deployment of his children defines this cycle is an absent-progenitor at the corpus level.

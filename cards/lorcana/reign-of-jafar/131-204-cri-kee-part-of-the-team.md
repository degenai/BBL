## Trivia

- **Character origin** — Cri-Kee is the lucky cricket companion in Disney's *Mulan* (1998). He was purchased by Grandmother Fa to give Mulan a luck charm before her visit to the Matchmaker. In a running gag, Cri-Kee confesses to Mushu that he is not actually lucky — though he survives every peril, which Mushu takes as proof of the opposite. The character was created by veteran Disney artist Joe Grant, who had worked at the studio since *Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs* (1937). `[Disney Wiki: Cri-Kee]`
- **Set context** — *Reign of Jafar* is Disney Lorcana Set 8, released May 30, 2025 (LGS) / June 6, 2025 (retail). Despite being named after the Aladdin villain Jafar, the set spans multiple Disney franchises; Cri-Kee appears here as part of a Mulan character cohort, alongside Mushu and Mulan herself. The Mulan/Lilo & Stitch Ruby/Steel starter deck for this set confirms Mulan is a featured franchise. `[Mushu Report: Reign of Jafar; LorcanaPlayer.com: Reign of Jafar card list]`
- **Flavor text** — "You were so focused on me and Mulan that you forgot about my man Cri-Kee here. And that was a mistake!" — Mushu. `[Lorcana card database; frontmatter flavor_text]` The speaker is Mushu, the disgraced dragon ancestor spirit who serves as Mulan's guardian — voiced by Eddie Murphy in the 1998 film. The quote positions Cri-Kee as a hidden tactical asset: overlooked because of his size, dangerous because he was underestimated.
- **Mechanical role** — Cri-Kee is a 4-cost Ruby Character (Storyborn Ally, Common) with 4 Strength / 3 Willpower / 1 Lore. His ability AT HER SIDE grants +2 Strength while 2 or more other exerted characters are in play. This pairs naturally with a wide, quest-heavy board state — he becomes a 6-Strength threat in an active Ruby aggro configuration. `[Mushu Report: Cri-Kee — Part of the Team; LorcanaPlayer.com: card details]`

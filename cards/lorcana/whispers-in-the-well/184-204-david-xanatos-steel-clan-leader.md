## Trivia

- **Set context** — David Xanatos - Steel Clan Leader is card 184/204 in Whispers in the Well (Lorcana set 10, released November 14, 2025), which marks the Lorcana debut of the Gargoyles franchise. The set's theme is film noir, mystery, and supernatural with soft horror elements. Xanatos appears in two versions in this set: Steel Clan Leader (this card, Uncommon) and Charismatic Leader (separate Uncommon print). Community response to the Gargoyles reveal was enthusiastic — GamesRadar ran the card previews under the headline "You'd need a heart of stone not to be excited by these new Disney Lorcana Gargoyles cards." [GamesRadar: Whispers in the Well Gargoyles cards, via WebSearch; Screen Rant: Whispers in the Well overview, via WebSearch]
- **Flavor text** — `"A plan only fails when you don't learn from it. And my / plans never fail."` — David Xanatos [card frontmatter] — the quote canonizes the "Xanatos Gambit" philosophy in Lorcana card form. In Gargoyles (1994), Xanatos structures plans so that any partial failure still advances an ulterior goal. This win-win planning style became so paradigmatic it gave its name to a pop-culture trope: the Xanatos Gambit (a plan designed so every outcome benefits the planner). The trope is now widely referenced in TV Tropes and broader media criticism. [Card frontmatter; Wikipedia: David Xanatos, via WebSearch]
- **Mechanical design** — 3-cost Steel Uncommon. Ability: MINOR INCONVENIENCE — when you play this character, you may choose and discard a card to deal 2 damage to a chosen character. The mechanic is structurally Xanatos-coded: you spend a resource (discard) to impose a cost on the opponent, always netting something from the trade. Illustrated by Renato Roldan and Amanda Duarte (dual credit). [card frontmatter; Lorcana databases via WebSearch]
- **Source character** — In Gargoyles (Disney animated series, 1994–1997, created by Greg Weisman), David Xanatos is a billionaire voiced by and modeled after Jonathan Frakes (Riker from Star Trek: The Next Generation). His first name David was chosen as an ironic inversion of the biblical David-and-Goliath story: Goliath is the gargoyle hero, so Xanatos is a David who is the villain. The Steel Clan are his robotic gargoyle-replica army; in early confrontations Xanatos himself wore a red exo-frame modeled on Goliath's body in direct combat. [Wikipedia: David Xanatos, via WebSearch; Gargoyles Fandom Wiki, via WebSearch]

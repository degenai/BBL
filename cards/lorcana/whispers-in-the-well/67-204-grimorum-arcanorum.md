## Trivia

- **IP context — the Grimorum in source canon** — The Grimorum Arcanorum is the most powerful book of mortal spells in the Gargoyles universe, originally written during the reign of Caesar Augustus (27 BC–AD 14) and possibly containing spells contributed by Merlin. Demona was secretly tutored by the Archmage in its use and became one of the few beings capable of wielding it. The book's ultimate fate: the Archmage literally ate it to bring its power into Avalon, then was destroyed when Goliath tore the Eye of Odin from him — the book self-destructed with him. `[GargWiki: Grimorum Arcanorum; Disney Wiki: The Grimorum Arcanorum]`
- **IP context — Demona's connection** — The oracle text's CELERITAS ability grants Rush to "your characters named Demona," directly encoding the show's most significant Grimorum plot point: Demona used the book to take magical control of Goliath (turning him into a zombie slave), and later conspired with Macbeth to steal it under the Weird Sisters' influence. Making the book grant Demona speed-to-attack is a compact narrative callback. `[Gargoyles Wiki: Grimorum Arcanorum; Gargoyles Wiki: Demona]`
- **Design — item card with dual abilities** — The Grimorum is a Rare Amethyst Item at cost 3 with no strength/willpower (items don't have combat stats in Lorcana). DOCTRINA ADDUCERE rewards an opponent's exertion with 1 lore; CELERITAS is a targeted named-character buff. As an item this slots into Gargoyles-Demona decks as a passive engine rather than a combat piece. `[LorcanaPlayer.com card review; Lorcana-api.com]`
- **Lore significance as object** — Among Gargoyles items, the Grimorum is uniquely central — it's the artifact that set the entire series' events in motion (the sleep spell, the awakening, Demona's betrayal). Its appearance as a card in the first Gargoyles Lorcana set signals it as a keystone of the franchise's in-game identity. `[GargWiki: Grimorum Arcanorum]`

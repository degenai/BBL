## Trivia

- **Set context** — Whispers in the Well (Set 10, November 14, 2025) has film noir/mystery as its core theme. Olaf as a Storyborn Amethyst character is a deliberate comedic-contrast placement: his naive cheerfulness set against dark swirling energy and an enchanted night forest is the joke the card is making. `[Mushu Report — Whispers in the Well; LorcanaPlayer.com]`
- **IP verification** — Olaf is a snowman brought to life by Elsa's magic in Disney's Frozen (2013), voiced by Josh Gad. Notably, he was originally written as a villain — "the first of a legion of snowman guards" at Elsa's ice castle — before being redesigned after Gad's casting. The creative team spent two weeks rethinking the character, eventually centering him on innocent childhood love and the song "In Summer." The redesigned Olaf became a thematic anchor for Frozen's emotional core. `[Wikipedia: Olaf (Frozen)]`
- **Flavor text** — "Here, hold this while I get help. Try not to go anywhere." — Olaf's habit of casually detaching body parts (arm, head) is a recurring sight gag in Frozen; the flavor text treats his arm as something to hand off to a bystander. `[Card frontmatter — flavor_text field]`
- **Mechanical profile** — Amethyst-ink Storyborn Ally Uncommon costing 1 ink (2 strength, 1 willpower, 1 lore). SECOND CHANCE: when Olaf leaves play, you may return a chosen friendly character to hand. At 1 cost, he is one of the cheapest exit-trigger bounce engines in Lorcana — nearly free protective recursion. The ability plays against his "Helping Hand" subtitle: he helps others by leaving. `[Mushu Report — Olaf - Helping Hand; TCGPlayer listing 57/204]`

## Trivia

- **Origin** — Magica De Spell was created by Carl Barks, debuting in Uncle Scrooge no. 36 (December 1961) in "The Midas Touch." She is an Italian sorceress whose central obsession is Scrooge McDuck's Number One Dime, which she believes would give her the Midas Touch. Barks modeled her visual design on Sophia Loren and Gina Lollobrigida. `[Wikipedia: Magica De Spell]`
- **Shadow-form context** — The "Shadowy and Sinister" subtitle is a direct reference to the 2017 DuckTales reboot: Magica attempted to imprison Scrooge in his dime, the spell backfired, and she was imprisoned inside it instead. For all of Season 1 she existed only as a shadow entity, communicating through her living shadow-proxy Lena. This card's full-silhouette art with single glowing red eye is a visualization of that canonical state. `[DuckTales Wiki: Magica De Spell (2017)]`
- **Set context** — Part of Whispers in the Well (set 10), released November 14, 2025, themed around film noir, mystery, and supernatural horror set within the Ink Well itself. The set introduced the Whisper card type and the Boost mechanic, and featured glimmers from new franchises including The Black Cauldron and Gargoyles. Magica's shadow-form fits the set's motif of incomplete, spectral entities. `[micechat.com: Whispers in the Well set overview]`
- **Mechanical** — DARK INCANTATION shuffles a card from the chosen player's discard into their deck — a disruption tool that buries opponent resources and resets graveyard setups. At 3-cost Amethyst Common, she is accessible disruption with a villain-sorcerer type line. Illustrated by Brian Kesinger. `[card frontmatter; Mushu Report: Magica De Spell - Shadowy and Sinister]`
- **Flavor text** — "My power is still not enough. I need something more. Something... shadowy." — voiced in-character, reflecting Magica's perpetual hunger for greater power and her literal shadow-bound condition in the 2017 DuckTales continuity. `[card frontmatter]`

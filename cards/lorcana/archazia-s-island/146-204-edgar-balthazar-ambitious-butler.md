## Trivia

- **Set context** — Edgar Balthazar - Ambitious Butler is card 146/204 in Archazia's Island (Lorcana set 7), released March 7, 2025. Archazia's Island introduced The Aristocats franchise to Lorcana for the first time; the set's theme centers on Disney animal companions and their world. Edgar debuted here as the sole Aristocats antagonist, an outlier among a set of beloved pets and animal heroes. The set also introduced dual-ink cards for the first time in Lorcana. [Lorcana Player: Archazia's Island set overview, via WebSearch]
- **Flavor text** — `"All these traps, all these locks, all these machines... / If they think that's going to stop me, they've sorely / underestimated Edgar Balthazar!"` [card frontmatter] — the quote captures Edgar's central film trait: escalating, increasingly desperate Rube-Goldberg-style schemes to remove the Bonfamille cats from the inheritance line. In The Aristocats (1970), his motivation is domestic grievance: Madame Bonfamille plans to leave her entire fortune to her cats, with Edgar inheriting only afterward — a butler whose lifelong loyalty is ranked below her pets in the estate. [Disney Wiki: Edgar (The Aristocats), via WebSearch]
- **Design** — Edgar is classified in Lorcana as a Storyborn Villain — "Storyborn" means he appears in his original film form rather than a reimagined "Floodborn" alternate-universe version. He is a 5-cost Ruby (red ink) Common, 6 Strength / 5 Willpower / 1 Lore, illustrated by Leonardo Giammichele. As of Archazia's Island this is Edgar's only Lorcana card print. [Lorcana Player: Edgar Balthazar - Ambitious Butler, via WebSearch]
- **Character note** — Edgar Balthazar is one of the few Disney antagonists whose villainy is explicitly rooted in labor displacement: he is a loyal domestic servant who discovers his employer values her cats above his decades of service. The film does not frame this as political but his scheme is materially motivated by an inheritance order that renders his work worthless. [Disney Wiki: Edgar (The Aristocats), via WebSearch]

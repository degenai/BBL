## Trivia

- **Flavor text** — `"Mere trinkets won't get you any closer to Jafar."` [card frontmatter] — a line positioning Razoul as Jafar's enforcement gatekeeper. The oracle text ability name MY ORDERS COME FROM JAFAR (trigger: if you have a Jafar character in play, banish a chosen item) mechanically enforces this loyalty dependency. Razoul's Lorcana card is functionally incomplete without Jafar present — a design choice that accurately mirrors his canonical status as a subordinate enforcer rather than an independent villain. [card frontmatter; Lorcana databases via WebSearch]
- **Set context and versions** — Razoul appears in Lorcana across two sets: Razoul - Palace Guard (Into the Inklands, no. 188/204, Common Steel) and this Razoul - Menacing Guard (Archazia's Island, no. 189/204, Common Steel, March 7, 2025). Both are Common Steel commons, suggesting Ravensburger treats him as a recurring low-rarity Aladdin franchise anchor. The subtitle shift from "Palace Guard" to "Menacing Guard" sharpens his threat framing in the Archazia's Island print. [Lorcana Player via WebSearch]
- **Source character** — In Disney's Aladdin (1992), Razoul is the long-serving Captain of Agrabah's Royal Guard, voiced by Jim Cummings. He was named after Disney layout artist Rasoul Azadani — a rare instance of a named antagonist as a real-person tribute. He pursues Aladdin across the film and TV series, remaining loyal to the Sultan and, by Jafar's coercion, to Jafar. By the end of Aladdin and the King of Thieves he catches the bouquet at Aladdin and Jasmine's wedding — a signal of grudging acceptance. [Disney Wiki: Razoul, via WebSearch; Wikipedia: List of Disney's Aladdin characters, via WebSearch]
- **Card classification** — This card's subtypes are Dreamborn Ally Captain. "Dreamborn" in Lorcana indicates an alternate-universe reimagining of the character rather than his direct Storyborn film appearance. "Captain" reflects his canonical rank; "Ally" is a Lorcana type designation. [Lorcana card databases via WebSearch]

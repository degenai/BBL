## Trivia

- **Flavor text** — `"The last thing any gamer wants to see."` `[card frontmatter; confirmed via DigitalTQ Archazia's Island card database]`
- **Franchise context** — Out of Order is classified under the Wreck-It Ralph franchise in Lorcana databases. The candy-bordered arcade aesthetic and glitching game screen depicted in the art are consistent with Sugar Rush, the racing game that serves as Wreck-It Ralph's primary setting in the 2012 film and its 2018 sequel. In the original film, Ralph's game Fix-It Felix Jr. is literally marked "out of order" when Ralph goes AWOL — the phrase is load-bearing to the film's plot. `[Lorcana-api.com classification; LorcanaPlayer.com franchise page: Wreck-It Ralph; Disney Wiki: Wreck-It Ralph]`
- **Set context** — Archazia's Island (set 7, March 7 2025) is the penultimate chapter of Lorcana's second story arc, preceding Reign of Jafar. Wreck-It Ralph has 8 or more cards in the set; Out of Order is one of the few Action (non-character) cards in the franchise block. At cost 7, it is among the most expensive Action cards in the set — "Banish chosen character" is an unconditional removal effect, which justifies the premium price. `[LorcanaPlayer.com Archazia's Island card list; digitaltq.com set listing]`
- **Mechanical profile** — Cost 7, Ruby, Common Action. "Banish chosen character" is unconditional single-target removal with no restriction on strength or cost of the target. Ruby removal at this power level fits the color's aggressive identity; 7 cost is high but the effect scales to any target. `[LorcanaPlayer.com; Lorcana-api.com]`

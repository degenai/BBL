## Trivia

- **Flavor text** — "Believing that he alone should be the sole heir, a seed of selfishness grew in him like a poison." — The Witch. The speaker is the unnamed enchantress in *Brave* (2012) whose spell transformed Mor'du into a bear. The quote comes from the short film *The Legend of Mor'du* (2012), which told Mor'du's backstory in greater detail than the main film. The card's subtitle "Wicked with Pride" directly names the sin that drives his arc. `[card frontmatter; Pixar Wiki: Mor'du; Wikipedia: The Legend of Mor'du]`

- **Character context** — Mor'du is the main antagonist of Pixar's *Brave* (2012) and the first Pixar villain to appear in Lorcana. Before his bear transformation, he was a human prince — eldest of four sons — who refused to share the kingdom with his brothers, sought a spell granting the "strength of ten men," and was unable to mend the broken family bond before the transformation became permanent. He is notably the first Pixar villain who does not speak at all in their film. `[Pixar Wiki: Mor'du; Villains Wiki: Mor'du; WebSearch snippets]`

- **Set context** — Wilds Unknown (Set 12, released May 15, 2026) is the first Lorcana set to feature Pixar IP. Brave, Toy Story, and The Incredibles make their simultaneous debut. Mor'du as the Brave villain represents the dark mirror to Merida (Merida - Formidable Archer is one of Set 12's two Iconic-rarity cards). `[Official Ravensburger Wilds Unknown press release, April 2026; WDW News Today: Merida debuts in Wilds Unknown]`

- **Design** — Common Amethyst character with the Rush keyword (can challenge the turn it's played). Rush on a villain character at common rarity creates immediate board pressure on arrival, consistent with Mor'du's role as an unstoppable force — in the film, he cannot be reasoned with or stopped by conventional means. Artist: Javi Salas. `[card frontmatter; Lorcana Player card database]`

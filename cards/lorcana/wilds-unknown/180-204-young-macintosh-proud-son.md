## Trivia

- **Set context** — Young Macintosh - Proud Son appears in Wilds Unknown (set 12, released May 15, 2026), the first Disney Lorcana set to feature Pixar characters. Brave characters — including Princess Merida and the three Highland suitors — make their Lorcana debut in this set alongside Toy Story and The Incredibles. `[Ravensburger/disneylorcana.com: Wilds Unknown press release; FantasyLandNews.com]`
- **Design** — Young Macintosh and his father Lord Macintosh were named after Apple Macintosh computers, a Pixar in-joke: the Macintosh was introduced by Steve Jobs, who was CEO of Pixar and joined Disney's board of directors when Disney acquired Pixar in 2006. The suitor trio's names extend the joke — Young MacGuffin (a screenwriting term for a motivating object of no inherent value, reflecting the suitors' plot-device function) and Wee Dingwall round out the Highland lord sons. `[Disney Wiki: Young Macintosh; Pixar Wiki: Young Macintosh]`
- **Flavor text** — "We've got the strength and we've got the swords. What vine could stand against all of that, right Da?" This line is original to the Lorcana card rather than a direct Brave film quote. The 'right Da?' tag positions Young Macintosh as performing bravado for parental approval — the emotional core of the 'Proud Son' subtitle. The reference to vines is presumably tied to Wilds Unknown's wilderness theme (the set name). `[LorcanaJSON card data; Disney Wiki: Young Macintosh]`
- **Design** — In Brave (2012), Young Macintosh is confident to the point of arrogance and prone to melodrama when he fails or finishes second. He is friendly with the other suitors Young MacGuffin and Wee Dingwall, suggesting the Lorcana duo composition (a second Highland figure visible in the background) is likely one of the other two suitors. `[Disney Wiki: Young Macintosh; Brave Wiki: Young Macintosh]`

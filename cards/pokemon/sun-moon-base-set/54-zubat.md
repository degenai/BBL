## Trivia

- **Set context** — This card is from the Sun & Moon Base Set (SM1), released February 3, 2017 — the first expansion of the Sun & Moon Series. SM1 introduced Generation VII Pokemon and the GX mechanic (powerful once-per-game attacks), headlined by Solgaleo-GX and Lunala-GX. Zubat (no. 54/149) is a Common in a set featuring Alola's tropical aesthetic. Though a Kanto original (Generation I), Zubat appears throughout Alola's cave routes in the video games, grounding its inclusion here. `[Bulbapedia: Sun & Moon (TCG), via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design** — Zubat (National Pokedex no. 41) debuted in Generation I (Pokemon Red and Blue, 1996) based on vampire bats. Its eyeless design reflects troglomorphism — cave-adapted organisms that lose visual organs over generations — and plays on the widespread misconception that bats are blind. Poison typing is a nod to vampire bat saliva's anticoagulant properties. In Gen I, Zubat is the most-frequently encountered random battle in caves (Mt. Moon, Victory Road), which made its encounter rate a running joke in the broader Pokemon fandom. `[Bulbapedia: Zubat, via WebSearch snippet; PokeAPI species ID 41]`
- **Flavor text** — `"It sleeps in caves during the day. It has no eyes, so to check its surroundings while flying, it emits ultrasonic waves."` The earliest Pokedex entry (Red/Blue) reads: "Forms colonies in perpetually dark places. Uses ultrasonic waves to identify and approach targets." Notably, artist Satoshi Shirai's art for this printing depicts Zubat in a bright, sun-dappled forest — the precise environment its Pokedex lore says it avoids (extended lore notes daylight can burn its skin). `[Card frontmatter; PokeAPI /pokemon-species/zubat Red/Blue entry]`
- **Mechanical** — The attack "Astonish" (Psychic energy: choose a random card from the opponent's hand, reveal it, shuffle it into their deck) is hand disruption. In the video games Astonish is a Ghost-type move with a flinch chance; the TCG version drops flinch but preserves the disorientation theme — consistent with a bat that navigates and disorients via sound rather than sight. `[Card frontmatter oracle_text]`

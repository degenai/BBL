## Trivia

- **Set / species context** — Rellor is National Pokedex no. 953, a Bug-type introduced in Generation IX (Pokemon Scarlet and Violet, 2022). It is found exclusively in the Asado Desert of the Paldea region. This card is from SV10: Destined Rivals (2025), the tenth Scarlet and Violet era expansion. Art by Toshinao Aoki. `[Serebii.net: Rellor no. 953]` `[Pokemon.com TCG database: sv10/24]`
- **Design mythology** — Rellor is based on the sacred scarab beetle of ancient Egyptian mythology. The scarab's rolling of dung was interpreted by ancient Egyptians as mirroring the god Ra's movement of the sun across the sky; Khepri, depicted with a scarab head, represented rebirth and the morning sun. Rellor's mud ball is infused with psychic energy that matures toward evolution — encoding the scarab-as-rebirth symbol directly into the Pokemon's biology. `[PokéJungle: Rellor and Rabsca — Origin of Species]`
- **Flavor text** — "It rolls its mud ball around while the energy it needs for evolution matures. Eventually the time comes for it to evolve." The Pokedex entry expands this: Rellor values its mud ball more than its own life, and the ball contains the psychic energy required for transformation into Rabsca. `[Card frontmatter flavor_text, cross-referenced with Bulbapedia: Rellor via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Evolution mechanic** — Rellor has an unusual step-based evolution requirement in the video game: it must be deployed via the 'Let's Go' feature and walked 1,000 steps before it can level up and evolve into Rabsca. One of a small number of Gen IX Pokemon with a non-standard evolution trigger. `[Dexerto: How to evolve Rellor into Rabsca]`

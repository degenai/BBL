## Trivia

- **Species / Pokedex** — Galarian Mr. Mime is an Ice/Psychic-type regional variant of Kantonian Mr. Mime (National Pokedex no. 122), introduced in Pokemon Sword and Shield (2019). The Galarian form drops Fairy typing in favor of Ice. It evolves into Mr. Rime (no. 866) at level 42 — a Galar-exclusive final form with no Kantonian counterpart. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-35; Bulbapedia: Mr. Mime (Galarian Form), via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design / cultural context** — The Galarian form's tap-dancing identity is a deliberate design-level localization. The original Kantonian Mr. Mime is a pantomime artist; the Galarian variant adopts tap-dancing, a performance tradition with roots in British clog dancing, Scottish step dancing, and Irish jigs. Galar is modeled on Great Britain. `[Pokejungle.net: Galarian Mr. Mime and Mr. Rime Origin of Species, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Flavor text** — "Its talent is tap-dancing. It can also manipulate temperatures to create a floor of ice, which this Pokemon can kick up to use as a barrier." — printed on DAA-035. The barrier mechanic is directly reflected in the card's Reflect attack (reduces opponent's damage by 30 for one turn). `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-35]`
- **Art** — Art by Misa Tsutsui. DAA-035 and DAA-036 (Galarian Mr. Rime) are adjacent collector numbers, a designer-coordinated evolution-line pair. Both cards share the tap-dancer-on-ice register: Mr. Mime at the skating/dance-as-defense scale, Mr. Rime at the full music-hall performer scale. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-35; swsh3-36]`

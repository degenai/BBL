## Trivia

- **Set context** — Darkness Ablaze (SWSH3) is the third main Sword & Shield expansion, released August 14, 2020; 201 cards total (189 numbered + 12 secret rares), built around Charizard VMAX (no. 020/189) as headliner with Eternatus VMAX and Scizor VMAX as secret-rare anchors. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3]` `[Bulbapedia: Darkness Ablaze (TCG), via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Species lore** — Larvitar is the Rock Skin Pokemon, National Pokedex no. 246, a Rock/Ground-type basic introduced in Generation II (Gold/Silver, Johto, 1999/2000). Evolves into Pupitar at level 30, then Tyranitar at level 55 — Tyranitar is the Gen II pseudo-legendary and the apex of the Johto roster. In TCG terms the Rock/Ground species maps to Fighting energy, hence Sand Spray (F) and Pierce (CC) on this print. `[Bulbapedia: Larvitar (Pokemon), via WebSearch snippet]` `[PokemonDB: Larvitar]`
- **Flavor text** — `"Born deep underground, it comes aboveground and becomes a pupa once it has finished eating the surrounding soil."` Continuous with the wider Pokedex framing: Gold/Silver-era entries establish Larvitar lives inside mountains and surfaces only after consuming an entire mountain's worth of earth, at which point it pupates into Pupitar. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-86 flavorText]` `[Bulbapedia: Larvitar Pokedex entries, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design** — Art by Sanosuke Sakuma (b. Oct 31, 1990, Kanagawa; began illustrating Pokemon TCG in 2013; ~159 main-TCG cards plus 32 in Pocket). Notably, Sakuma also designed several Sword & Shield in-game trainer-class character designs — Worker, Backpacker, Businessman, Office Lady, Artist, Police Officer, Doctor, Poke Kid — a rare cross-medium portfolio where her work appears both on TCG cards and on the regional NPC bench of the games this set ties into. `[Bulbapedia: Sanosuke Sakuma, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Art of Pokemon: Sanosuke Sakuma illustrator page]`
- **Print history** — 30 Larvitar prints exist in the TCG database; the species' first TCG appearance was Neo Discovery (no. 57/75, June 1, 2001, illustrated by Hironobu Yoshida — a Game Freak / TPC art-director-associated name). This Sakuma garden-walkway print is the only Sword & Shield-era Larvitar in the main series. Reverse-holo trades meaningfully above the normal-print floor (~$0.22 reverse-holo vs ~$0.05 normal, PokemonTCG.io snapshot 2026-05-11). `[PokemonTCG.io API: name:Larvitar query]` `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-86 tcgplayer]`
- **Anime resonance** — The dominant fan-facing reference point for the species is the Johto-arc episode "Hatch Me If You Can" (EP258), in which Ash hatches a Larvitar from an egg stolen by poachers off Mt. Silver and eventually returns it to its Tyranitar mother on the mountain rather than letting it evolve in-party. The lost-baby / quiet-creature framing pairs naturally with the calm-garden setting of this card's art rather than a battle pose. `[Bulbapedia: Ash's Larvitar, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Bulbapedia: EP258 'Hatch Me If You Can']`

## Connections

- [[larvitar-line]] — base-stage anchor for the Larvitar evolutionary line (Larvitar / Pupitar / Tyranitar), the Johto Generation II pseudo-legendary line. This print's flavor text *"Born deep underground, it comes aboveground and becomes a pupa once it has finished eating the surrounding soil"* is the most-cited canonical expression of the line's **mountain-eater origin** at the Larvitar stage — the cohort's design thesis explicitly identifies this text as the strongest anchor for the consume-the-mountain-become-the-mountain narrative arc, with the eat-the-mountain canon strongest at the Larvitar stage and the embodiment canon at the Tyranitar apex. [Scryfall flavor_text via PokemonTCG.io swsh3-86; cohort node body]
- [[087-189-pupitar]] — Johto pseudo-legendary Larvitar-line evolution 1:1 mirror, within-set adjacency. Larvitar is the base (National Pokedex no. 246, Rock / Ground) of the Johto pseudo-legendary line (Larvitar level 30 to Pupitar; Pupitar level 55 to Tyranitar, the Gen II pseudo-legendary with 600 base stat total), introduced in Pokemon Gold and Silver (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 1999), with both prints at the within-Darkness-Ablaze 086 + 087 adjacent-collector-number designer-coordinated cohesion stack — visual progression from peaceful-garden pupa-still-eating-soil baseline at base stage (Sanosuke Sakuma art, paved walkway with flower planter) to contained-power hovering-cocoon at Stage-1 mid-form (Sekio art, dramatic sunset over rocky mountain terrain). The Pokedex flavor canon explicitly chains the two stages: Larvitar *"becomes a pupa once it has finished eating the surrounding soil"* and Pupitar *"even sealed in its shell, it can move freely. Hard and fast, it has outstanding destructive power"* — the cost-of-power motif rendered as visible biological progression from soil-eater to spike-shelled-cocoon.

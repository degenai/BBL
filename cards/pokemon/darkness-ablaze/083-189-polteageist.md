## Trivia

- **Design — form variants** — Polteageist (National Pokedex no. 855) has two forms inherited from Sinistea: Phony Form (common; Sinistea has no authenticity stamp, evolves with a Cracked Pot) and Antique Form (rare; Sinistea bears a tiny blue mark on the inner rim of the cup, evolves with a Chipped Pot). The Antique Form's wild encounter rate in Sword & Shield is approximately 1%. The TCG card does not distinguish form — both share identical stats and moves. `[WebSearch snippet: pokemondb.net Polteageist forms]`
- **Flavor text** — "This species lives in antique teapots. Most pots are forgeries, but on rare occasions, an authentic work is found." — directly references the Antique/Phony form split present in the games. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-83]`
- **Mechanical — Mad Party combo** — The Mad Party attack (20x damage per Mad Party attacker in the discard pile) is a shared mechanic across DAA with Bunnelby, Dedenne, Galarian Mr. Mime, and Galarian Mr. Rime. Polteageist's Tea Break ability lets the player discard a Mad Party Pokemon from hand to draw 2 cards, making it the engine piece in that archetype. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-83]`
- **Set context** — This card follows DAA-082 Sinistea (same set, same artist kirisAki), completing the Sinistea-Polteageist line across two sequential card numbers. Sinistea's Phony/Antique form system was documented in depth during wave 13 trivia for CRE-082. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-83]`

## Connections

- [[082-189-sinistea]] — Galar ghost-tea evolution line 1:1 mirror. Polteageist (National Pokedex no. 855, Ghost-type) is the Stage 1 evolution of Sinistea (no. 854, Ghost-type) via the Cracked Pot evolution item for Phony Form or the Chipped Pot evolution item for Antique Form — the Generation VIII Galar haunted-tableware line introduced in Pokemon Sword & Shield (2019). DAA-082 (Sinistea) and DAA-083 (Polteageist) share the Darkness Ablaze set, sit at adjacent collector numbers within the within-set evolution adjacency pattern, and depict the same character at successive life stages: the small ghostly teacup with swirling purple liquid against a soft purple haze (DAA-082, Kouki Saitou art, eerie quiet-menace register) versus the ornate decorative teapot with spectral lavender-and-teal energy billowing out from spout and lid against a warm pastel interior (this card, kirisAki art, playful overflowing-containment register). The species-diagnostic Phony Form / Antique Form variant lore — Sinistea's authenticity stamp on the cup's underside, the Cracked Pot / Chipped Pot evolution-item split, the ~1% wild encounter rate for Antique Form — carries forward into this card's flavor text directly ("This species lives in antique teapots. Most pots are forgeries, but on rare occasions, an authentic work is found"), which the Sinistea print's "born when a lonely spirit possessed a cold, leftover cup of tea" Pokedex flavor anchors as origin. The teacup-to-teapot vessel-upgrade is the line's signature visual progression: same haunted-tableware design citation (porcelain-maker authenticity stamps to combat counterfeiting, per Bulbapedia design notes), same Ghost-type psychic-magic register, scaled from cup-scale to pot-scale on evolution.

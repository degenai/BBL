## Trivia

- **Set context** — Darkness Ablaze (SWSH3) released August 14, 2020. Kanto Grimer (National Pokedex no. 88) appears as a Darkness-type common at no. 100/189. The Darkness typing fits the industrial-waste aesthetic; earlier prints often ran as Poison-type `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-100]`.
- **Species context** — Kanto Grimer originated from industrial pollution: sludge on a polluted seabed or dirty stream exposed to X-rays from the moon. It feeds on factory wastewater, penetrating sewage pipes to consume what runs through them. This origin is distinct from Alolan Grimer (green-yellow, introduced Gen 7), which was introduced to Alola specifically to consume urban garbage — a different ecological niche, different form `[WebSearch: bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Grimer_(Pokemon); WebSearch: dextraneous.blog/2019/09/21/grimer-muk]`.
- **Flavor text** — "The wastewater coming from factories is clean these days, so Grimer have nothing to eat. They're said to be on the verge of extinction." This text first appeared in Pokemon Sun's Pokedex. The inversion is structural: Grimer faces extinction not from over-pollution but from environmental cleanup eliminating its food source. Pollution is a survival niche. Environmental remediation, from Grimer's position, is existential threat `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-100]`.
- **Ecological design note** — Wherever Kanto Grimer passes, its bacterial sludge renders soil permanently barren — no plants will grow again in the wake of its passage. The body doesn't just carry pollution; it transmits permanent sterility to the environment `[WebSearch: bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Grimer_(Pokemon)]`.
- **Mechanical note** — Artist: Ryota Murayama. Stomp Off (first attack) discards the top card of the opponent's deck — a mill effect mechanically coherent with a Pokemon whose lore centers on consuming and degrading whatever surrounds it `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-100]`.

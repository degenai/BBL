## Trivia

- **Set context** — Aron (National Pokedex no. 304) is a Steel/Rock-type introduced in Generation III (Hoenn, Ruby & Sapphire), classified as the Iron Armor Pokemon. The evolutionary line is Aron → Lairon (level 32) → Aggron (level 42). DAA-121 Aron and DAA-122 Lairon sit at adjacent card numbers in this set. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-121]`
- **Flavor text** — "When it evolves, it sheds the steel carapace that covered its whole body and develops a new one." — each evolution stage is not an addition of armor but a complete molt-and-regrow cycle, making the armor a living part of the body rather than accumulated plating. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-121]`
- **Lore — iron diet** — Aron's Pokedex entries across games establish it eats iron ore — and occasionally railroad tracks and bridges — to build up its steel armor. The molt-and-regrow mechanism confirmed by the TCG flavor text means the consumed iron literally becomes Aron's body at each stage. `[WebSearch snippet: pokemon.com Pokedex Aron]`

## Connections

- [[122-189-lairon]] — Hoenn rock/steel evolution line 1:1 mirror. Aron (National Pokedex no. 304) evolves into Lairon (no. 305) at level 32, which in turn evolves into Aggron (no. 306) at level 42 — the Hoenn Rock/Steel three-stage line introduced in Generation III. DAA-121 (Aron) and DAA-122 (Lairon) share the Darkness Ablaze set, sit at adjacent collector numbers within the within-set evolution adjacency pattern, and depict the same character at successive life stages: the small grass-meadow baby with the rounded carapace (this card, Naoyo Kimura art, peaceful daylight palette) versus the heavily-plated mid-stage brute in the rocky cave/tunnel (DAA-122, Mizue art, grim muted-stone palette). The flavor text bridges the evolution narratively — Aron's "When it evolves, it sheds the steel carapace that covered its whole body and develops a new one" directly references the transition to Lairon's heavier ridged plating, which Lairon's "It habitually shows off its strength with the sparks it creates by ramming its steel body into boulders" then expresses as the evolved-form's behavior. The setting shift from open meadow to cave/tunnel maps onto Bulbapedia's documented Aron-to-Lairon habitat progression (Aron in mountains and grasslands; Lairon in deeper rocky tunnels and mineral-rich caves).

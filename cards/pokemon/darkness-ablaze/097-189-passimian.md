## Trivia

- **Set context** — Darkness Ablaze (SWSH3) released August 14, 2020, the third Sword & Shield expansion. Charizard VMAX is the set's headline card; Passimian appears as a Fighting-type common at no. 97/189 `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3]`.
- **Species context** — Passimian (National Pokedex no. 766) is a Fighting-type introduced in Pokemon Sun (Gen 7, Alola, 2016). It is version-exclusive to Sun and Ultra Sun in the Alola games, and to Pokemon Sword in Sword & Shield — its version counterpart is Oranguru (Moon/Shield). Passimian is not part of the elemental-monkey trio (Pansage, Pansear, Panpour); it is a wholly independent species classified as the Teamwork Pokemon `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-97; WebSearch: pokemondb.net/sun-moon/exclusive]`.
- **Design origin** — The name combines "pass" and "simian." The design is explicitly modeled on American football: round coconut-helmet head, broad shoulders, strong group-play emphasis. French name "Quartermac" and German "Quartermak" both reference the quarterback position `[WebSearch: bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Passimian_(Pokemon); WebSearch: pokemaniacal.com/2018/09/15/passimian/]`.
- **Flavor text** — "Displaying amazing teamwork, they follow the orders of their boss as they all help out in the search for their favorite berries." The troop boss earns the role by throwing a berry the farthest; techniques and strategies are passed from boss to group across generations — a genuine transmission-of-knowledge social structure `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-97; WebSearch: bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Passimian_(Pokemon)]`.
- **Mechanical note** — Call for Family (first attack) benches up to 2 Basic Pokemon from the deck — a mechanically faithful expression of the troop-recruitment behavior in Passimian's lore. Rock Hurl ignores Resistance, mirroring the hard-berry-as-weapon combat style from the Pokedex `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-97]`.

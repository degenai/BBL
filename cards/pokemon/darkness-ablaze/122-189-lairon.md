## Connections

- [[121-189-aron]] — Hoenn rock/steel evolution line 1:1 mirror. Lairon (National Pokedex no. 305) is the mid-stage evolution of Aron (no. 304, evolves at level 32) in the Hoenn Rock/Steel three-stage line introduced in Generation III (Aron → Lairon → Aggron). DAA-121 (Aron) and DAA-122 (Lairon) share the Darkness Ablaze set, sit at adjacent collector numbers within the within-set evolution adjacency pattern, and depict the same character at successive life stages: the small grass-meadow baby with the rounded carapace (DAA-121, Naoyo Kimura art, peaceful daylight palette) versus the heavily-plated mid-stage brute in the rocky cave/tunnel (this card, Mizue art, grim muted-stone palette). The flavor text bridges the evolution narratively — Aron's "When it evolves, it sheds the steel carapace that covered its whole body and develops a new one" directly references the transition to this card's heavier ridged plating, which this card's "It habitually shows off its strength with the sparks it creates by ramming its steel body into boulders" then expresses as the evolved-form's behavior. The setting shift from open meadow to cave/tunnel maps onto Bulbapedia's documented Aron-to-Lairon habitat progression (Aron in mountains and grasslands; Lairon in deeper rocky tunnels and mineral-rich caves).

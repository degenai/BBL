## Trivia

- **Set context — Sentret/Furret line** — Furret (National Pokedex no. 162) is a Normal-type Stage 1 introduced in Generation II (Gold & Silver), evolving from Sentret at level 15. Sentret was trivia'd in wave 12 for this archive. DAA-135 (Sentret) and DAA-136 (Furret) are sequential card numbers in this set, completing the Johto normal-scout line across adjacent slots. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-136]`
- **Flavor text** — "It makes a nest to suit its long and skinny body. The nest is impossible for other Pokemon to enter." — the body-length that defines Furret's appearance is simultaneously its primary survival mechanism: the burrow is body-width-specific and functions as a predator-proof retreat. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-136]`
- **Mechanical** — Feelin' Fine (draw 3 cards for one Colorless energy) is an unusually efficient draw engine for a Colorless uncommon. Tail Smash (90 damage for one Colorless, coin-flip) has a high ceiling for such a low energy cost but the coin-flip is the full trade-off. Both attacks are functional in budget or theme-deck builds. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh3-136]`

## Connections

- [[135-189-sentret]] — Johto sentinel/ferret evolution line 1:1 mirror. Furret (National Pokedex no. 162) is the evolved form of Sentret (no. 161, evolves at level 15) in the Johto Normal-type two-stage line introduced in Generation II (Pokemon Gold & Silver, 1999-2000). DAA-135 (Sentret) and DAA-136 (Furret) share the Darkness Ablaze set, sit at adjacent collector numbers within the within-set evolution adjacency pattern, and depict the same character at successive life stages: the small round-bodied baby standing alert on its tail in a bright meadow scanning for danger (DAA-135, Mina Nakai art, peaceful daylight palette, "It stands on its tail so it can see a long way. If it spots an enemy, it cries loudly to warn its kind") versus the long sinuous evolved form mid-leap through autumn foliage (this card, tetsuya koizumi art, warm orange-and-gold canopy palette, "It makes a nest to suit its long and skinny body. The nest is impossible for other Pokemon to enter"). The body-plan shift from round-and-upright to long-and-sinuous tracks Bulbapedia's documented design progression — Sentret as a vigilant sentinel, Furret as a burrow-builder whose elongated body is the gameplay-relevant feature. The within-set 135-136 adjacency completes the Johto sentinel/ferret line at full 2-of-2 canonical species coverage in the BBL corpus.

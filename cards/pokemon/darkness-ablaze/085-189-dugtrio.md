## Trivia

- **Set context** — Released in the Sword & Shield expansion Darkness Ablaze (English 2020-08-14; Japanese as Infinity Zone 2020-06-05), a set headlined by Eternatus and a slate of Gigantamax Pokemon V/VMAX. Dugtrio (no. 085/189) sits as a baseline Stage 1 Fighting-type uncommon in an expansion otherwise dominated by V/VMAX rares `[PokemonTCG.io API]` `[Pokemon.com: Sword & Shield-Darkness Ablaze]`.
- **Dex / evolution** — Dugtrio is National Pokedex no. 051, a Generation I Pokemon that evolves from Diglett (no. 050) starting at level 26. Diglett was reportedly designed from a childhood sketch by Pokemon designer Shigeki Morimoto, and Dugtrio's design is canonically "two extra Diglett added to make a trio" rather than a single creature `[PokemonTCG.io API]` `[Wikipedia: Diglett and Dugtrio]`.
- **Trio-vs-one ambiguity** — Whether Dugtrio is one Pokemon or three has been a long-running canonical question. The card's own flavor text frames it as a team ("A team of Diglett triplets"), Wikipedia surveys the design as "three Diglett put together," and community sources frame the question as unresolved across generations `[Wikipedia: Diglett and Dugtrio]` `[Bulbagarden forum: "Dugtrio: three moles or one?", via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Flavor text** — `"A team of Diglett triplets. It triggers huge earthquakes by burrowing 60 miles underground."` `[PokemonTCG.io API]`. The 60-mile burrowing depth tracks the broader Pokedex lore that Dugtrio tunnels at roughly 100 km depth fast enough to register as seismic activity `[Wikipedia: Diglett and Dugtrio]`.
- **Illustrator** — Yukiko Baba has illustrated Pokemon TCG cards continuously since the Neo Discovery set (1998); her style is described as printmaking-influenced with sketchy, abstract backgrounds and small scene-setting around the Pokemon rather than dynamic battle poses `[Bulbapedia: Yukiko Baba, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Art of Pokemon: Yukiko Baba, via WebSearch snippet]`. The composition here -- mid-shot trio against a flat earth-tone ground -- fits that scene-mode signature.
- **Alolan variant** — Alolan Dugtrio (introduced in Pokemon Sun and Moon, 2016) is a Ground/Steel regional variant whose three heads each sport a different gold hairstyle made from Pele's hair -- thin glassy filaments of cooled volcanic lava. In Alola lore the form is revered as an incarnation of the god of the land, and taking its hair out of the region is said to bring divine punishment `[Wikipedia: Diglett and Dugtrio]`.

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Drifloon appears in Sun & Moon — Unified Minds (2019) as card no. 80/236, a Common. It is National Pokedex no. 425, a Ghost/Flying-type introduced in Generation IV (Sinnoh). `[PokemonTCG.io: sm11-80]`
- **Dark canon: child abduction** — Drifloon is one of the franchise's most-cited examples of dark Pokedex lore. Multiple entries describe it grabbing children's hands and attempting to drag them to the spirit world. The Japanese Pearl entry is more explicit than the English localization, specifying it takes children to "the world of the dead." Kotaku covered this in 2019 as an example of Pokedex horror. The flavor text on this card is comparatively restrained — it references souls and expansion, not children specifically. `[WebSearch: Kotaku 'Drifloon Steals Children And Was Created By Ghosts']` `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia: Drifloon snippet]`
- **Flavor text** — `"Its round body is stuffed with souls and expands each time it leads someone away."` `[PokemonTCG.io: sm11-80]`
- **Tonal dissonance as design** — This card's art (rainbow, red flowers, sunny meadow, cheerful setting) directly contrasts the Pokedex canon. The art style mirrors what a child would see: a colorful balloon in a pretty field. The dark reading requires knowing the lore. This is a recurring franchise design strategy for Ghost-types — package horror in an approachable visual register. `[Bulbapedia: Drifloon, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Spirit origin** — Drifloon is described as being formed from the spirits of people and Pokemon, which connects it to broader Sinnoh lore about death and the spirit world (Giratina, the Distortion World, Mr. Fuji analog characters). `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia: Drifloon snippet]`

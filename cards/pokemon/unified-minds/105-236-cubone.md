## Trivia

- **Set context** — Cubone wears the skull of its dead mother as a helmet — one of the franchise's most cited dark design citations. In Red and Blue, the backstory is rendered concrete at Lavender Town: a Marowak ghost haunts the Pokemon Tower because she was killed by Team Rocket while protecting her Cubone child. That specific ghost is Cubone's mother; she blocks the tower's top floor until the player obtains the Silph Scope [Bulbapedia: Marowak (ghost), via WebSearch snippet].
- **Flavor text** — "At night, it weeps loudly for its dead mother, but those cries only attract its natural enemy — Mandibuzz." [PokemonTCG.io sm11-105]
- **Design** — National Pokedex no. 104. Pure Ground-type, Generation I. Cubone's design has remained unchanged across every generation — the skull-helmet and bone weapon are its defining identifiers with no alternate forms. Artist for this printing: otumami [PokemonTCG.io sm11-105].
- **Lore resolution** — In Pokemon Let's Go Pikachu and Let's Go Eevee, the Lavender Town subplot is expanded: the player helps reunite the Cubone with its mother's ghost, after which Marowak's spirit departs peacefully without requiring battle — a direct narrative resolution to the Generation I dark event [Bulbapedia: Marowak (ghost), via WebSearch snippet].

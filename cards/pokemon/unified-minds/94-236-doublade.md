## Trivia

- **Set context** — Doublade appears in Sun & Moon — Unified Minds (2019) as card no. 94/236, an Uncommon. It is National Pokedex no. 680, a Ghost/Steel-type introduced in Generation VI (Kalos). `[PokemonTCG.io: sm11-94]`
- **Possession evolution line** — The Honedge line (Honedge → Doublade → Aegislash) represents a sword progressively taken over by a possessing spirit. Honedge is a single animated sword; Doublade is two swords coordinated telepathically after the possessing spirit divides; Aegislash adds a shield and can switch between Blade Forme (offensive) and Shield Forme (defensive) mid-battle. The flavor text on this card states the division explicitly. `[PokemonTCG.io: sm11-94]`
- **Flavor text** — `"When Honedge evolves, it divides into two swords, which cooperate via telepathy to coordinate attacks and slash their enemies to ribbons."` `[PokemonTCG.io: sm11-94]`
- **Aegislash's competitive history** — Doublade's final evolution Aegislash was one of the most dominant Pokemon in Generation VI (X/Y/ORAS) competitive play. In Blade Forme it had base 150 Attack/Special Attack; in Shield Forme base 150 Defense/Special Defense. Access to King's Shield (blocks damage, drops attacker's Attack by 2 stages) created near-unbeatable defensive cycling. Smogon banned it to Ubers in Gen VI-VII. In Gen VIII (Sword & Shield) it was nerfed: offensive stats in Blade Forme and defensive stats in Shield Forme cut from 150 to 140, enough to drop it back to OU tier. `[Smogon: Aegislash via WebSearch]` `[WebSearch: Sportskeeda 'Why was Aegislash considered an Uber Pokemon']`

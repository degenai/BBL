## Trivia

- **Pokedex distinction** — Goomy (National Pokedex no. 704) is consistently cited in official Pokedex entries as the weakest Dragon-type Pokemon in the franchise. Its body is covered in a mucous membrane so sensitive it must live in damp, shady environments to avoid desiccation. Despite this, Goomy's evolutionary endpoint — Goodra (no. 706) — is a pseudo-legendary with 600 base stat total, one of the strongest Dragon-types available. The Goomy→Sliggoo→Goodra line holds the most extreme stat-progression gap of any pseudo-legendary line, with Goodra's base stats exactly double Goomy's. [Bulbapedia: Goomy (Pokémon); Goodra (Pokémon); pokemondb.net: Goodra]
- **Flavor text** — "Their horns are powerful sensors. As soon as Goomy pick up any sign of enemies, they go into hiding. This is how they've survived." The survival-through-evasion framing is tonally consistent with Goomy's "weakest Dragon-type" reputation — a creature whose entire evolutionary strategy is avoidance until the final form can fight back. [PokemonTCG.io: swsh8-195]
- **Set context** — Fusion Strike (Sword & Shield series, released November 12, 2021) is one of the largest Sword & Shield era sets at 264 cards. Goomy appears here as a Basic Dragon-type (no. 195/264, Common). [Bulbapedia: Fusion Strike (TCG); pokemonblog.com: Fusion Strike release]
- **Artist** — Illustrated by Miki Tanaka, an active TCG illustrator. The Bleeding Cool Nagomi Nijo spotlight notes that Nijo also illustrated Goodra in Fusion Strike — making this set a rare case where the Goomy line is split across two different artists within the same expansion. [PokemonTCG.io: swsh8-195; WebSearch: Nagomi Nijo Fusion Strike]

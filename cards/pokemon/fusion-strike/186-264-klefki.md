## Trivia

- **Design history** — Klefki (National Pokedex no. 707) was introduced in Generation VI (Pokemon X and Y, 2013, Kalos region). It is a dual-type Steel/Fairy Pokemon with no evolution. Designer Mana Ibe created the concept drawing on the history of Kalos (modeled on France) — specifically old mansion architecture and the cultural weight of secret keys. Ken Sugimori, the franchise's original character designer, cited Klefki as exemplifying "variety and richness" in Pokémon species design by demonstrating that inanimate objects could carry the franchise's creature-design language. [Wikipedia: Klefki; Bulbapedia: Klefki (Pokémon); WebSearch: Kotaku Klefki design controversy]
- **Flavor text** — "Long ago it lived in mines, but once the minerals that make up its diet became scarcer, Klefki began appearing in human settlements." This frames Klefki as a mineral-eating creature pushed into urban proximity — a displacement narrative consistent with its Fairy-as-trickster lore (folklore traditions of fairies stealing small objects like keys). [PokemonTCG.io: swsh8-186]
- **Design controversy and competitive reception** — Klefki was one of the more polarizing Generation VI designs at launch; IGN readers voted it among the worst new Pokemon. Concurrently, Klefki was one of the more viable Steel/Fairy threats in XY competitive play (Prankster ability + Spikes / Thunder Wave), demonstrating the franchise's recurring pattern of "memeable design, viable in battle." [Kotaku: Best and Possibly Worst New Pokemon Designs; Smogon: Klefki XY]
- **Artist** — Illustrated by MAHOU, a TCG artist active in the Sword & Shield era. [PokemonTCG.io: swsh8-186]

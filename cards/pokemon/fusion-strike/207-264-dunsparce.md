## Trivia

- **The 23-year wait** — Dunsparce (National Pokedex no. 206) debuted in Generation II (Gold/Silver, 1999) and did not receive an evolution for 23 years, the longest evolutionary gap of any Pokemon with a subsequently added evolution in franchise history. Dudunsparce (no. 982) was introduced in Generation IX (Scarlet/Violet, 2022). The evolution requires Dunsparce to know the move Hyper Drill (learned at level 32). Dudunsparce's two-segment form is the standard result; a three-segment form exists at a 1-in-100 chance. The "finally got an evolution" reveal was a major community moment at Scarlet/Violet reveal events. [Nintendo Life: How to Evolve Dunsparce into Dudunsparce; pokemondb.net: Dunsparce; Kotaku: Dudunsparce three-segment odds]
- **Flavor text** — "When it sees a person, it digs a hole with its tail to make its escape. If you happen to find one, consider yourself lucky." The rarity framing ("consider yourself lucky") echoes Dunsparce's in-game reputation as a hard-to-find, easy-to-dismiss encounter — which fed decades of community jokes about its lack of an evolution or distinctive competitive role. [PokemonTCG.io: swsh8-207]
- **Ability context** — The Mysterious Nest ability (unique to this TCG print) removes Weakness from all Colorless Pokemon in play. This is a niche but thematically coherent effect for a Pokemon often characterized as evasive and hard to pin down. [PokemonTCG.io: swsh8-207]
- **Design origin** — Dunsparce is based on the tsuchinoko, a cryptid from Japanese folklore: a snake-like creature said to be fat-bodied, reportedly sighted in mountain regions, and near-mythological in its rarity. The tsuchinoko connection explains both the "if you find one, consider yourself lucky" flavor text and the species' reputation as a mid-game elusive encounter. [Bulbapedia: Dunsparce (Pokémon); WebSearch snippet]

## Trivia

- **Flavor text** — "Known as the Drill King, this Pokémon can tunnel through the terrain at speeds of over 90 mph." The "Drill King" epithet is Excadrill's consistent Pokedex identity across generations; its official species classification is "Subterrene Pokemon." [PokemonTCG.io: swsh8-183]
- **Competitive history** — Excadrill (National Pokedex no. 530) was one of the most dominant threats in Generation V OU (Black & White). Its Sand Rush ability doubles Speed in sandstorm, pairing with Hippowdon or Tyranitar to create a combo that pushed Excadrill to Ubers-tier banning in Gen V. Mold Breaker is its alternate competitive niche (bypasses Levitate, Sturdy, etc.), useful against Giratina-O in Ubers. [Smogon: Excadrill BW OU thread; Serebii.net POTW 530]
- **Design context** — Excadrill is Ground/Steel type (in-game), though this TCG print is typed Metal (the TCG's Steel equivalent). It evolves from Drilbur (no. 529), both introduced in Generation V (Black & White, 2010, Unova region). The two-stage line literalizes the drilling-and-excavation motif: Drilbur rotates its body as a compact drill, Excadrill uses full-body force as the "Drill King." [Bulbapedia: Excadrill (Pokémon)]
- **Artist** — Illustrated by Lee HyunJung, a Korean TCG illustrator active in the Sword & Shield era. [PokemonTCG.io: swsh8-183]

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Fusion Strike (SWSH8) released November 12, 2021 as the 8th Sword and Shield expansion. At 264 cards in the base set (284 total with secret rares), it was the largest Sword and Shield set at the time of release. `[PokemonTCG.io API swsh8-17; pokellector.com Fusion Strike set page]`
- **Design origin** — Trevenant is based on the Japanese kodama — tree spirits from Shinto folklore that inhabit ancient trees and curse those who fell them. Its stripe-like markings resemble shimenawa rope used to mark spiritual habitation. The design also draws on the cyclopean yōkai tradition (single-eye) and, more loosely, Tolkien's Ents. `[WebSearch snippet: Bulbapedia Trevenant; Dr. Lava@DrLavaYT via WebSearch]`
- **Forest guardian lore** — Trevenant is kind to Pokémon and people who dwell peacefully in its forest, but devours anyone who destroys its home. Legend says a curse will be inflicted on those who cut its trees. Its roots connect to surrounding trees across the entire forest, letting it monitor and control every corner of its territory. `[WebSearch snippet: pokemon.fandom.com/wiki/Trevenant; pokemon.com Pokedex]`
- **Flavor text** — "Small roots that extend from the tips of this Pokémon's feet can tie into the trees of the forest and give Trevenant control over them." `[PokemonTCG.io API swsh8-17]`
- **Evolution line** — Trevenant (National Pokedex no. 709) is the Stage 1 evolution of Phantump (no. 708). Both were introduced in Generation VI (X & Y, 2013, Kalos region). Phantump's design is grounded in the lore of children's spirits lost in the forest — Trevenant carries that lineage forward as the adult haunted-tree guardian. `[PokemonTCG.io API swsh8-17; WebSearch snippet: Bulbapedia Phantump]`

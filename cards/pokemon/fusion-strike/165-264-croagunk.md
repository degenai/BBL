## Trivia

- **Flavor text** — "Once diluted, its poison becomes medicinal. This Pokémon came into popularity after a pharmaceutical company chose it as a mascot." This is a direct in-game Pokedex fact: the poison-to-medicine transformation narrative makes Croagunk one of the few species to have an explicitly stated corporate-commercial relationship built into its lore. The pharmaceutical mascot detail is consistent across multiple Pokedex entries. [PokemonTCG.io: swsh8-165]
- **Cross-print distinction** — The BUS-54 print (Burning Shadows, wave 14 trivia complete) was illustrated by a different artist and was the print most associated with anime-era Brock's-Croagunk fame. This Fusion Strike print (no. 165/264, illustrated by Nagomi Nijo) stages Croagunk in dense tropical foliage — a distinct framing from BUS-54's composition. A third print also exists: Sword & Shield base set no. 123/202 (artist unknown from this inventory). [PokemonTCG.io: swsh8-165; Bulbapedia: Croagunk (Fusion Strike 165)]
- **Artist** — Nagomi Nijo, a TCG illustrator noted as a semifinalist in the Pokemon Card Game Illustration Grand Prix. Fusion Strike was one of Nijo's more prolific sets: Pansage, Croagunk, and Goodra all appear as Nijo illustrations in swsh8, making this print part of a Nijo cluster within the expansion. [WebSearch: Nagomi Nijo Fusion Strike; Bleeding Cool: Nagomi Nijo artist spotlight]
- **Species context** — Croagunk (National Pokedex no. 453) is a dual-type Poison/Fighting Pokemon introduced in Generation IV (Diamond/Pearl, 2006, Sinnoh). It evolves into Toxicroak (no. 454). The TCG consistently types it as Darkness (the TCG analogue for Poison-type in most Generation IV prints). [PokemonTCG.io: swsh8-165; Bulbapedia: Croagunk (Pokémon)]

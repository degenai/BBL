## Trivia

- **Species context** — Latios is National Pokedex no. 381, the Eon Pokemon, a dual-type Dragon/Psychic Legendary introduced in Generation III (Ruby/Sapphire, 2002). It is the male of the Eon Twins pair with Latias (no. 380). Its in-game ability is Levitate. [Bulbapedia: Latios (Pokémon); pokemondb.net]
- **Flavor text** — "It understands human speech and is highly intelligent. It is a tender Pokémon that dislikes fighting." This gentle characterization is consistent across multiple Pokedex generations and is a notable contrast to most Dragon-type Legendaries. [PokemonTCG.io: swsh8-194]
- **Movie tie-in** — Latios is the central figure of Pokémon Heroes (2002, Japanese title: "Pokémon Heroes: Latios and Latias"), the fifth Pokémon film. Set in the Venice-inspired city of Alto Mare, the film depicts Latios sacrificing himself to stop a tidal wave, his soul crystallizing into the Soul Dew — a held item that in-game boosts the Special Attack and Special Defense of Latias and Latios. The film was the first Pokémon movie to not receive a wide North American theatrical release. [Wikipedia: Pokémon Heroes; Serebii.net: Latias and Latios movie]
- **TCG mechanic** — In Fusion Strike, Latios (no. 194) and Latias (no. 193) form a complementary Fusion Strike pair: Latios's ability "Blue Assist" attaches Psychic Energy to Latias; Latias's ability "Red Assist" attaches Psychic Energy to Latios. The tandem energy-acceleration design directly mirrors the Eon Twins' bond in the games and anime. Both carry the Fusion Strike mechanic subtype, enabling support from Fusion Strike Energy and Power Tablet. [PokemonTCG.io: swsh8-194, swsh8-193; PokeBeach: Latios, Latias from Fusion Arts]

## Trivia

- **Set context** — From Battle Styles (SWSH5), the fifth Sword & Shield expansion, released March 19, 2021. Battle Styles was the first expansion to introduce Single Strike and Rapid Strike card mechanics, themed around Urshifu's two combat forms. Horsea (031/163) is a Basic Water-type common in the set's background roster, unconnected to the Battle Styles mechanic focus. [Bulbapedia: Battle Styles (TCG)]
- **Design history — mythological dragon lineage** — Horsea's Japanese name Tattu derives from tatsu, embedded in the compound tatsu-no-otoshigo (竜の落とし子), the Japanese folk term for seahorse meaning "dragon's illegitimate child." This reflects the Japanese folkloric belief that a seahorse living for a century will transform into a dragon. The evolutionary line encodes this arc literally: Horsea and Seadra are pure Water-type, but Kingdra (introduced Generation II, evolving via Dragon Scale trade) gains the Dragon type — the seahorse completing its mythological transformation. [Dextraneous.blog: Horsea, Seadra, Kingdra (2019); bogleech.com: The Horsea Family; Bulbapedia: Kingdra (Pokemon)]
- **Loong connection** — Kingdra's horns, whiskered design, and deep-sea-floor habitat (where it generates whirlpools while sleeping) align with Eastern dragon (loong) iconography and may reference Ryujin, the Japanese sea deity and dragon king. The Horsea-to-Kingdra arc is one of the most explicit seahorse-as-dragon-in-becoming designs in the franchise. [Dextraneous.blog: Horsea, Seadra, Kingdra; Bulbapedia: Kingdra (Pokemon)]
- **Flavor text** — "It makes its nest in the shade of corals. If it senses danger, it spits murky ink and flees." The ink-spitting defense is consistent with the squid/cephalopod-influenced secondary biology noted across the Horsea line's moves (Smokescreen, Ink-based water attacks). [PokemonTCG.io: swsh5-31]
- **Diamond Legendz Zodiac grid** — Horsea anchors the **Water Dragon** cell of the 12-animal Wuxing matrix. Dual-predicate logic: (1) Water-type across all three evolutionary stages, and (2) the line's mythological identity as dragon-in-becoming, culminating in Kingdra's literal Water/Dragon dual typing. The Japanese folk etymology of seahorse as "dragon's illegitimate child" makes this the most etymologically documented zodiac placement in the grid. [Bulbapedia: Kingdra (Pokemon); Dextraneous.blog: Horsea, Seadra, Kingdra]

## Trivia

- **Set position** — Bellsprout is card no. 001/163 in Battle Styles (SWSH05, released March 19, 2021) — the very first card in the set. In Pokemon TCG card ordering, Grass-types traditionally lead the numbering because Grass is the first type in the game's elemental hierarchy. Being card 001 means Bellsprout is the first card a collector encounters when opening any Battle Styles pack by number. `[Pokemon.com TCG Card Database: Battle Styles 001]` `[PkmnCards: Bellsprout BST 001]`

- **Species lore** — Bellsprout is National Pokedex no. 069, a Grass/Poison-type introduced in Generation I (Pokemon Red and Blue, Japan 1996). It is the first stage of a three-part line: Bellsprout evolves into Weepinbell at level 21, which then evolves into Victreebel via Leaf Stone. Its species classification across Pokedex entries consistently invokes the mandrake: roots (Mandragora officinarum) that resemble human forms and appear in folklore from ancient Greek herbalism through medieval Europe. The card's flavor text makes the connection explicit. `[Bulbapedia: Bellsprout, via WebSearch]` `[Pokemon.com Pokedex: Bellsprout]`

- **Flavor text** — `"Its bud looks like a human face. Because of the bud, it is rumored to be a type of legendary mandrake plant."` The mandrake legend — anthropomorphic root, shrieking when uprooted, historically used in folk medicine and magic — threads through Western folklore from Dioscorides through Harry Potter. Bellsprout sits in a long cultural lineage of plant-as-uncanny-humanoid. No speaker is credited, per standard Pokemon TCG flavor format. `[Card frontmatter: flavor_text]` `[Bulbapedia: Bellsprout]`

- **Mechanical design** — Venoshock (Colorless, 10+) deals 40 more damage if the opponent's Active Pokemon is Poisoned. This mirrors the Generation V main-series move (introduced in Black & White), where Venoshock deals double damage to poisoned targets. In the video games, Bellsprout learns Venoshock only via TM — not by natural level-up — so the TCG attack is a mechanical borrowing that skips Bellsprout's normal move progression. Illustrated by Sekio, active in the Pokemon TCG since Guardians Rising (2017) with approximately 73 card credits across the English card pool. `[Card frontmatter: oracle_text, artist]` `[Bulbapedia: Venoshock move, via WebSearch]` `[PkmnCards: Sekio artist page]`

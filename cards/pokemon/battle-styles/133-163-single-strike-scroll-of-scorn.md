## Trivia

- **Set context** — Battle Styles (March 19, 2021) introduced the Single Strike and Rapid Strike battle styles as its core mechanic. Single Strike cards carry red accents; Rapid Strike, blue. Scroll of Scorn is one of the key Tool cards designed to anchor Single Strike-style decks within the new system. `[Pokemon.com: Battle Styles Single Strike and Rapid Strike announcement, via WebSearch; Bulbapedia: Battle Styles TCG, via WebSearch]`
- **Mechanical note** — As a Pokemon Tool, Scroll of Scorn grants the attached Single Strike Pokemon access to Furious Anger (Fighting Energy, 10 + 10 per damage counter on the user). The attack scales with punishment taken — a design that rewards aggressive, mutually-damaging trades rather than passive play. `[Bulbapedia: Single Strike Scroll of Scorn (Battle Styles 133), via WebSearch; Pojo.com review, via WebSearch]`
- **Design** — Illustrated by 5ban Graphics, the studio responsible for the majority of V and VMAX card art across the Sword & Shield era. The sealed-scroll aesthetic draws on Japanese martial arts imagery, where a scroll of techniques traditionally signifies inherited or forbidden knowledge passed through a lineage. `[Frontmatter artist field]`

## Connections

- [[139-163-urn-of-vitality]] — Designer-coordinated Single Strike support pair. Both cards are Battle Styles items printed within the same expansion (March 19, 2021) and stamped with the Single Strike emblem; together with Single Strike Urshifu VMAX they form a co-designed three-piece support suite. Scroll of Scorn is the offensive tool (grants the Furious Anger attack, scaling with damage taken on the user); Urn of Vitality is the recovery engine (shuffles up to 2 Single Strike Energy from the discard pile back into the deck). The complementary mechanical roles — punishment-scaling aggression vs energy-attrition recovery — are the Single Strike battle style's full thesis distilled into two item cards. The pair is also iconographically twinned: both depict a single ceremonial object (scroll, urn) glowing with internal energy against a moody warm-and-dark background, no figure, both with the red-and-black Single Strike logo as the badge on the artifact. `[Pokemon.com: Battle Styles Single Strike and Rapid Strike announcement, via WebSearch]` `[Bulbapedia: Battle Styles TCG, via WebSearch]`

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Gliscor (072/163) is an Uncommon card from Sword & Shield Battle Styles (set ID: swsh5), released March 19, 2021. It is a Stage 1 Fighting-type that evolves from Gligar. Artist: ryoma uratsuka. Regulation mark E `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh5-72]`
- **IP verified** — National Pokedex no. 472. Gliscor is a dual-type Ground/Flying Pokemon introduced in Generation IV (Sinnoh, Diamond/Pearl). It evolves from Gligar when leveled up holding a Razor Fang during the night — a nocturnal item-based evolution mechanic `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh5-72; Serebii Pokedex no. 472, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Flavor text** — `"Its flight is soundless. It uses its lengthy tail to carry off its prey… Then its elongated fangs do the rest."` Gliscor's predator identity in the Pokedex is consistent: a soundless, aerial ambush hunter that uses its barbed tail to grab prey before finishing with fangs. This card's Guillotine attack (90 damage, Fighting + 2 Colorless) directly mirrors that fang-strike finishing move `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh5-72 (flavor text)]`
- **Competitive identity** — Gliscor's defining competitive trait is the Poison Heal + Toxic Orb combination: intentionally inflicting self-poison to convert poison damage into passive healing, granting effective immunity to other status conditions. Smogon has placed Gliscor in OU in multiple generations as both a Spikes setter and a Substitute-based stall-breaker. The TCG's Acrobatics attack (flip 2 coins, 20 + 40 per heads) echoes the unpredictability of the Smogon sub-stall set `[Smogon: Gliscor OU; Smogon Forums ORAS OU thread, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Evolution quirk** — Despite nearly doubling in size during evolution (Gligar: 1.1 m to Gliscor: 2.0 m), Gliscor is lighter than its pre-evolution — a real-world detail consistent with vampire bat anatomy (hollow bones, low mass for flight) `[Serebii Pokedex no. 472; Bulbapedia: Gliscor, via WebSearch snippet]`

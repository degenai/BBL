## Trivia

- **Set context** — Evolutions (XY12, released November 2, 2016) is a Base Set tribute released for Pokémon's 20th anniversary. Nearly all cards reproduce original 1999 Base Set artwork on modern card stock, but with updated HP values, modern typing rules, and the addition of EX and BREAK mechanics. Kakuna (no. 6/108) carries the same Ken Sugimori art style that defined Gen I but is not credited to Sugimori here — the artist on this specific print is **Keiji Kinebuchi**, who was among the original Base Set artists. `[PokemonTCG.io API: xy12-6]` `[WebSearch: Pokebeach XY12 Evolutions Base Set reprint set overview]`
- **Pokedex lore** — Kakuna (National Pokedex no. 14) is the cocoon stage of the Weedle line (Weedle→Kakuna→Beedrill) and is explicitly described as almost completely unable to move on its own — it can only harden its shell to endure attacks while internal metamorphosis proceeds. In the mainline video games, Kakuna's sole learnable move is Harden. The card mechanically mirrors this: its two moves are Stiffen (reducing incoming damage) and Poison Powder, reflecting the defensive stasis and the toxin-secreting biology of the Beedrill line. `[PokemonTCG.io API: xy12-6]` `[WebSearch: pokemon-wiki.org/pokemon/kakuna]`
- **Flavor text** — `"This Pokémon is in a temporary stage while making its body. It is almost completely unable to move on its own."` `[PokemonTCG.io API: xy12-6]`
- **Artist** — **Keiji Kinebuchi** is one of the original Base Set-era Pokémon TCG illustrators, active from the 1996 Japanese launch. His work appearing in Evolutions is a deliberate callback to original-era talent. `[PokemonTCG.io API: xy12-6]`

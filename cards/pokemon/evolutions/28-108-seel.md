## Trivia

- **Set context** — Seel (no. 28/108) is part of Evolutions (XY12, November 2016), the 20th-anniversary Base Set tribute. Art is credited to **Ken Sugimori**, the original designer of the Seel line. This is one of the purest nostalgia prints in the set — the composition is minimal and centered, matching the spare style of the 1999 Base Set originals. `[PokemonTCG.io API: xy12-28]` `[WebSearch: Pokebeach XY12 Evolutions overview]`
- **Design lore — multi-species mashup** — Seel (National Pokedex no. 86) is a composite Arctic marine mammal: its round white body resembles a beluga whale or harp seal pup, its forehead horn is likely inspired by narwhals, and its name "Seel" is a direct phonetic reference to "seal." Its evolution Dewgong's name is a play on "dugong" (a relative of the manatee). The Seel line thus compresses at least four Arctic/subarctic marine species into two Pokémon. `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia Seel; Bulbapedia Dewgong; dextraneous.blog Seel/Dewgong]`
- **Flavor text** — `"Covered with light blue fur, its hide is thick and tough. It is active in bitter cold of -40 degrees Fahrenheit."` — The -40°F figure is notable: -40°F equals -40°C, the single point where Fahrenheit and Celsius converge — though whether this is an intentional design detail or coincidence is unverified. `[PokemonTCG.io API: xy12-28]`
- **Mechanical note** — The card's Growl attack reduces the Defending Pokémon's next attack by 20 damage, a direct reference to the "Growl" move mechanic (lowering the opponent's Attack stat) that has existed since Generation I. The Evolutions set preserved this flavor-accurate move mapping from the 1999 Base Set prints. `[PokemonTCG.io API: xy12-28]`

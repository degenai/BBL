## Trivia

- **Set context** — Poliwag (no. 23/108) appears in Evolutions (XY12, November 2016), the 20th-anniversary Base Set tribute. The art is credited to **Ken Sugimori**, Game Freak's lead character designer and creator of the original Pokémon designs. His name appearing on an Evolutions card is a direct callback to his original Base Set contributions — Sugimori personally designed the Poliwag line. `[PokemonTCG.io API: xy12-23]` `[WebSearch: Pokebeach XY12 Evolutions overview]`
- **Design lore — real biology** — Poliwag's defining visual feature (the black-and-white spiral on its belly) is modeled after the intestinal tract of real-life tadpoles, whose internal organs are sometimes visible through their semitransparent abdominal skin. The Pokedex confirms this explicitly: "A part of its internal organs can be seen through the skin as a spiral pattern." The spiral is also said to look clearer after eating. Pokémon researcher Dr. Lava has documented this as one of the franchise's most biologically accurate design choices. `[WebSearch: Dr. Lava on X (twitter @DrLavaYT); WebSearch: Bulbapedia Poliwag]`
- **Flavor text** — `"Its slick black skin is thin and damp. A part of its internal organs can be seen through the skin as a spiral pattern."` `[PokemonTCG.io API: xy12-23]`
- **Evolution line note** — Poliwag (National Pokedex no. 60) evolves into Poliwhirl, then branches to either Poliwrath (Water Stone) or Politoed (King's Rock trade). Politoed's existence as an alternate path — introduced in Generation II — made Poliwag one of the franchise's first examples of a branching final-evolution line depending on held item. `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia Poliwag]`

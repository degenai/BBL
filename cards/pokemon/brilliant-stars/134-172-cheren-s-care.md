## Trivia

- **Set context** — Brilliant Stars (SWSH9) released February 25, 2022 as the ninth Sword and Shield expansion and the first to introduce the VSTAR mechanic. Its poster card is Arceus VSTAR. Cheren's Care is one of the supporting trainer cards populating the Supporter slot with returning characters from across the game series. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh9]`
- **Character arc** — Cheren begins Pokemon Black and White as the player's rival — the one who picks the type-advantaged starter — driven by an obsession with strength he cannot initially articulate when pressed by Alder. By Pokemon Black 2 and White 2, set two years later, he has become the Normal-type Gym Leader at Aspertia City and works as a teacher at its Trainer School. The TCG card depicts him in this settled, educator role: white shirt, glasses, standing before a building with a Pokemon Center aesthetic. `[Bulbapedia: Cheren, via WebSearch]`
- **Mechanical parallel** — Cheren's Care (no. 134/172) bounces a damaged Colorless-type Pokemon plus all attachments back to hand. The Colorless restriction maps to his Normal-type gym specialization. This is mechanically identical to Acerola (Burning Shadows, no. 112/147), which performs the same bounce for any Pokemon type rather than Colorless-only — making the two cards a design pair separated by type restriction. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh9-134]`

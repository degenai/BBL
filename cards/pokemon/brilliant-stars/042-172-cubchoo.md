## Trivia

- **Second Cubchoo print in corpus — same flavor text, different artist** — The corpus already holds DAA-048 Cubchoo (Darkness Ablaze 048/189), illustrated by Saya Tsuruta, trivia'd in Wave 14. This BRS-042 print is illustrated by ryoma uratsuka. Both prints carry the IDENTICAL flavor text: "When this Pokémon is in good health, its snot becomes thicker and stickier. It will smear its snot on anyone it doesn't like." Same design joke, two different illustrators' takes on the same snowy bear. `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh9-42 and swsh3-48; Bulbapedia: Cubchoo (Darkness Ablaze 48)]`
- **Snot as health indicator — deliberate gross-out mechanic** — The running Cubchoo/Beartic design conceit is that the perpetual nasal drip is literally Cubchoo's cryogenic weapon system: it breathes in the snot and uses it to create freezing attacks. Beartic's Pokédex entries describe its breath as so cold it can freeze seawater. The "snot = power" idea is a deliberate subversion of the cute-bear archetype. `[Bulbapedia: Cubchoo (Pokémon); Bulbapedia: Beartic (Pokémon)]`
- **Set context** — Brilliant Stars (SWSH9), released February 25, 2022. Cubchoo appears as a Water-type common (Ice-type in the main series — TCG maps most Ice-types to Water energy). The set's Arceus VSTAR theming skews toward Hisui, but Cubchoo is a Unova-native Pokémon (introduced Gen V) appearing as general Pokédex representation. `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh9]`

## Trivia

- **Second Kanto Grimer print in corpus — same flavor text, different artist** — The corpus already holds DAA-100 Grimer (Darkness Ablaze 100/189), illustrated by Ryota Murayama, trivia'd in Wave 14 with a pollution/eco-critique angle. This BRS-084 print is illustrated by Masakazu Fukuda. Notably, both prints carry the IDENTICAL flavor text: "The wastewater coming from factories is clean these days, so Grimer have nothing to eat. They're said to be on the verge of extinction." The irony — a pollution-born Pokémon endangered by environmental cleanup — is the same across both cards. Two different artists, one bleak ecological punchline. `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh9-84 and swsh3-100; WebSearch: Grimer DAA-100 flavor text via pkmncards.com]`
- **Darkness-type classification in TCG** — In the Pokémon TCG, Kanto Grimer is classified as a Darkness-type, not Poison-type (the main-series typing). The TCG color system collapses main-series Poison into Darkness for most Poison-type Pokémon. This card's Poison Gas attack is mechanically apt despite the energy type mismatch. `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh9-84]`
- **Set context** — Brilliant Stars (SWSH9), released February 25, 2022. Grimer appears as a low-rarity common. The set is headlined by Arceus VSTAR and focuses on the Hisui region theming; Grimer's inclusion as a Kanto common is straightforward Pokédex representation. `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh9]`

- [[130-236-alolan-grimer]] — Kanto Grimer ↔ Alolan Grimer regional-variant cross-form mirror. Same species (National Pokedex no. 88) across two designer-coordinated regional forms: this BRS-084 print is the Kanto form (purple sludge body, originated from polluted seabed sludge exposed to moon X-rays, feeds on factory wastewater) and CSE-130 is the Alolan form (green-yellow toxin body, introduced Gen-VII for Alola specifically, canonically maintained as urban-sanitation workers in Alola's waste-disposal site).

## Trivia

- **Design origin** — Castform (National Pokedex no. 351) debuted in Generation III (Ruby and Sapphire, 2002) as the first Pokemon to change type based on weather conditions. Its four forms — Normal (no active weather), Sunny Fire-type (harsh sunlight), Rainy Water-type (rain), and Snowy Ice-type (hail or snow) — swap via its Forecast Ability. The design draws partly from the Japanese teru teru bozu tradition: small cloth dolls hung to wish away rain. `[Bulbapedia: Castform, via WebSearch]`
- **Lore origin** — In Ruby, Sapphire, and Emerald, Castform is gifted by a scientist at the Weather Institute as a reward for rescuing the facility from Team Aqua or Team Magma — both of whom were attempting to control global weather. Castform's identity as a "weather researcher's companion" is baked into its in-universe origin. `[Bulbapedia: Castform, via WebSearch]`
- **Flavor text** — "Its form changes depending on the weather. The rougher conditions get, the rougher Castform's disposition!" `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh9-116 flavorText]`
- **Forms cycle completion** — The three Castform alternate-form cards (Sunny no. 022/198, Rainy no. 033/198, Snowy no. 034/198) all appeared together in Chilling Reign (SWSH6, 2021). This Normal Form (BRS no. 116/172, Brilliant Stars 2022) arrived the following year, completing the four-form cycle across SWSH-era printings. Castform Rainy Form (CRE no. 033/198) is already in the corpus. `[Limitless TCG: CRE/33, via WebSearch]` `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh9-116]`

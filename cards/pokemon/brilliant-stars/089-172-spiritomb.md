Spiritomb manifests as a dense swirling vortex of dark energy — green-glowing, smoke-like tendrils spiral outward from a central mass that is anchored to a cracked gray stone or keystone at the bottom of the composition. Within the dark swirl, faint ghostly face-shapes and spiraling patterns are visible. The background is very dark, nearly void-black, with the green glow from Spiritomb providing the primary light source, giving the card an eerie, chiaroscuro quality. The stone anchor at the base grounds the otherwise formless entity.

## Trivia

- **Design mythology** — Spiritomb (National Pokedex no. 442) draws on Buddhist and Chinese literary traditions. The number 108 references the 108 temptations one must overcome to achieve Nirvana — Japanese Buddhist temples ring a bell 108 times on New Year's Eve for this reason. The species may also reference Water Margin, one of China's Four Great Classical Novels, where a sealed stone monument containing 108 demons is recklessly disturbed, releasing them. The Odd Keystone itself (trapezoidal, two dots, a fissure forming a frown) is the physical prison. `[Bulbapedia: Spiritomb, via WebSearch]`
- **Flavor text** — "Exactly 108 spirits gathered to become this Pokemon. Apparently there are some ill-natured spirits in the mix." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh9-89 flavorText]`
- **Additional folklore references** — Spiritomb may also be based on a Jibakurei (a spirit bound to a single place in Japanese folklore) or a Deildegast (a Norwegian spirit punished by being forced to haul a boundary stone for moving one while alive). All three reference points converge on punishment, binding, and displacement. `[Bulbapedia: Spiritomb, via WebSearch]`
- **Type trivia** — Introduced in Generation IV (Diamond and Pearl, 2006), Spiritomb originally had no type weaknesses in the mainline games — one of only two Pokemon with this distinction alongside Sableye. The Fairy-type introduction in Generation VI (X and Y, 2013) gave both a weakness to Fairy. The TCG Weakness printed on this card (Grass x2) does not reflect the game-mechanic history. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh9-89]`

## Trivia

- **Design lineage** — Dedenne (National Pokedex no. 702) is Generation VI's Pikachu-parallel, introduced in X and Y (2013) as the "Antenna Pokemon" for the Kalos region. Its upper whiskers branch into functional antennae that send and receive electrical waves, letting Dedenne communicate over long distances and locate electricity sources. The key design distinction from prior Pikachu-parallels is the cellular-router concept: Dedenne can't generate much electricity on its own, so it taps into outlets and other electric Pokemon. `[Bulbapedia: Dedenne, via WebSearch]`
- **Flavor text** — "Since Dedenne can't generate as much electricity on its own, it steals electricity from outlets or other electric Pokemon." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh9-67 flavorText]`
- **Design cohort** — Every generation since Gen II has introduced a Pikachu-adjacent electric rodent: Marill, Plusle/Minun, Pachirisu, Emolga, Dedenne, Togedemaru, Morpeko. Dedenne occupies Gen VI's slot. The antenna-as-transceiver concept is unique to Dedenne within this lineage. `[Screen Rant: Pikachu Clones Every Generation, via WebSearch]`
- **Mechanical note** — The BRS Dedenne (no. 067/172) is a common Psychic-type with a conditional comeback attack: Dede-Flash deals 20 damage normally but 80 and inflicts Confusion when the opponent has exactly 1 Prize card. Distinct from the far more competitive Dedenne-GX (Unbroken Bonds no. 057/214), whose Dedechange Ability (discard hand, draw 6) made it a format-defining draw engine across the GX era. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh9-67]`

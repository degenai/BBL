## Trivia

- **Design** — Probopass draws on two visual references: the moai statues of Easter Island (stone body shape and texture; the red "hat" mirrors the pukao, the red scoria topknots placed on many moai) and a compass magnet (three Mini-Noses arranged at East, South, and West with the main nose pointing North, completing a full compass rose). `[Bulbapedia: Probopass, via WebSearch]`
- **Evolution mechanic** — Probopass is National Pokedex no. 476, introduced in Generation IV (Diamond and Pearl, 2006). It evolves from Nosepass when leveled up in a special magnetic field, canonically near Mt. Coronet in Sinnoh. From Pokemon Legends: Arceus onward, a Thunder Stone also triggers the evolution. The BRS print is a Stage 1 Metal-type with 140 HP and a 4-retreat-cost body. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh9-99]`
- **Flavor text** — "It uses three small units to catch prey and battle enemies. The main body mostly just gives orders." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh9-99 flavorText]`
- **Community note** — Probopass's red hat, large central nose, and mustache formed by iron filings draw frequent fan comparisons to Mario (Nintendo). The resemblance is widely noted in community spaces but has not been officially acknowledged by Game Freak. Community sentiment only. `[Bulbapedia: Probopass design section, via WebSearch]`

## Connections

- [[096-192-nosepass]] — Evolution-line pair. Probopass (Compass Pokemon, Rock/Steel) is the evolved form of Nosepass (Compass Pokemon, pure Rock), reached when Nosepass is leveled up in a special magnetic field. The line's design completes a compass metaphor across evolution: Nosepass's single red nose points magnetic north; Probopass adds three detachable Mini-Noses pointing East, South, and West to form a full compass rose. The line is also notable for the type-shift across evolution (Rock to Rock/Steel) — unusual within the species. `[Bulbapedia: Probopass — evolution mechanic, via WebSearch]` `[Pokemondb.net: Nosepass / Probopass species pages, via WebSearch]`

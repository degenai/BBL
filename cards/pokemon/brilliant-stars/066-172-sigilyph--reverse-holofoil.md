## Trivia

- **Design origin: Nazca Lines + kachina dolls** — Sigilyph's design is confirmed on Bulbapedia as inspired primarily by the Nazca Lines — giant geoglyphs etched into the Peruvian desert, many depicting birds and animals, created between roughly 500 BCE and 500 CE. The hummingbird Nazca figure is the most-cited specific reference for the avian body plan. A secondary influence is the kachina doll, a spiritual figure from Hopi and other Pueblo traditions, which may account for the colorful geometric face and pendant limbs. The name "Sigilyph" combines sigil (a magical symbol) and glyph, with possible additional reference to sylph (an air spirit). `[Bulbapedia: Sigilyph (Pokémon); facts.net: 20 Facts About Sigilyph]`
- **Flavor text — guardian lore** — "Psychic power allows these Pokémon to fly. Some say they were the guardians of an ancient city. Others say they were the guardians' emissaries." The flavor text is deliberately ambiguous: guardian vs. emissary. This maps to Unova lore in which the Desert Resort and Relic Castle areas contain ancient ruins — Sigilyph are found there in Pokémon Black and White as the "memory" of the city that once stood. `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh9-66 flavorText field; Bulbapedia: Sigilyph (Pokémon)]`
- **Unova ancient-civilization cluster** — Sigilyph is a Unova-native Pokémon (National Pokédex no. 561, introduced Generation V). The corpus already holds Golett (another ancient-construct Pokémon from the same Relic Castle area of Unova), creating a nascent ancient-civilization cluster across multiple waves. The Wave 17 vision pass flagged this adjacency. `[Bulbapedia: Sigilyph (Pokémon); Bulbapedia: Golett (Pokémon)]`
- **Reverse holofoil treatment** — This copy is the reverse holofoil variant of BRS-066. Brilliant Stars (SWSH9) released February 25, 2022, and featured the Arceus VSTAR as its headliner. Sigilyph appears as an uncommon in the main set, making its reverse holo a low-cost collector variant. `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh9]`

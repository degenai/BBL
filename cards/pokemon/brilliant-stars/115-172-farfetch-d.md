## Trivia

- **Design origin** — Farfetch'd's Japanese name Kamonegi combines 鴨 kamo (duck) and 葱 negi (spring onion) to form 鴨葱 kamo negi, an abbreviation of a Japanese proverb meaning "an easy target comes bearing gifts" or simply "golden opportunity." The English name "Farfetch'd" reflects the proverb's improbability. The leek is simultaneously the source of the name, a weapon, and (per Pokedex lore) emergency food. `[Bulbapedia: Farfetch'd, via WebSearch]`
- **Flavor text** — "The stalk this Pokemon carries in its wings serves as a sword to cut down opponents. In a dire situation, the stalk can also serve as food." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh9-115 flavorText]`
- **Regional contrast** — The Kanto Farfetch'd (Normal/Flying, brown plumage, leek as prop) contrasts with the Galarian Farfetch'd introduced in Sword and Shield: Fighting-type, dark grey plumage, leek wielded actively in a martial posture. The design logic holds that Galar's tougher local leeks and warrior culture reshaped the species into a combat specialist. This Kanto-form card and the Galarian Farfetch'd (already in corpus, no. 094/192) form a designer-coordinated regional-variant pair. `[PokeJungle: Galarian Farfetch'd and Sirfetch'd, via WebSearch]`
- **Evolution note** — The API records evolvesTo: [Sirfetch'd] for this card, but that evolution belongs to the Galarian form only. The Kanto Farfetch'd does not evolve in the mainline games. Sirfetch'd is a Galarian-exclusive evolution. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh9-115]`

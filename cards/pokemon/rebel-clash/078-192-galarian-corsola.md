## Trivia

- **Set context** — Galarian Corsola appears in Rebel Clash (SWSH2), the second Sword & Shield era expansion, released May 1, 2020. The set contains 192 cards in its base set (209 total with secret rares) `[Bulbapedia: Rebel Clash (TCG), via WebSearch snippet]`.
- **Design allegory** — The Galarian form converts the original Johto Water/Rock Corsola (National Pokedex no. 222) into a Ghost-type. The in-game Pokedex entry reads: "Sudden climate change wiped out this ancient kind of Corsola. This Pokémon absorbs others' life-force through its branches." The visual bleaching and Ghost-typing were widely noted by outlets including HuffPost, GameRevolution, and The Hill as a direct reference to real-world coral bleaching caused by ocean warming and acidification `[WebSearch: HuffPost, GameRevolution, The Hill — coverage of Galarian Corsola climate allegory, 2019]`.
- **Flavor text** — "Watch your step when wandering areas oceans once covered. What looks like a stone could be this Pokémon, and it will curse you if you kick it." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-78 flavor_text, confirmed in frontmatter]` The TCG flavor text foregrounds the habitat-loss angle: the ocean literally receded, leaving Corsola stranded on dry land.
- **Evolution note** — Galarian Corsola evolves into Cursola at level 38, remaining Ghost-type. The evolution line is the only Corsola-family Ghost branch; standard Corsola (no Galarian form) has no evolution in its original Water/Rock typing `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia snippet, Galarian Corsola/Cursola]`.
- **Artist** — This print is illustrated by Mitsuhiro Arita, one of the most prolific and longest-tenured Pokemon TCG artists, whose work spans from the original Base Set (1996) through the Sword & Shield era `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-78 artist field]`.

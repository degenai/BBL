Caterpie (National Pokedex no. 10) is the larva base form; Metapod (no. 11) is the chrysalis Stage-1 form it evolves into at level 7. ## Trivia

- **Species context** — Metapod is the first and only Metapod print in the corpus. It is National Pokedex no. 11, the Stage 1 (chrysalis) form of the Kanto Bug-type line. It evolves from Caterpie at level 7 and evolves into Butterfree at level 10 — the shortest three-stage evolution span in Generation I. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-2]`
- **Anime landmark** — Ash's Caterpie evolved into Metapod near the end of the episode "Ash Catches a Pokémon" (Season 1, Episode 3). In "Challenge of the Samurai" (Episode 4), a Metapod vs. Metapod standoff results in repeated Harden — one of the franchise's most parodied early moments. Ash's Metapod subsequently evolved into Butterfree in that same episode, making this two-episode arc the franchise's first on-screen three-stage evolution sequence. `[Bulbapedia: EP004, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Harden mechanic** — Metapod's signature move Harden (raises Defense) is reflected in the flavor text: "It is waiting for the moment to evolve. At this stage, it can only harden, so it remains motionless to avoid attack." This print's stillness-forward composition — pale background, suspended cocoon, no environmental action — directly illustrates the flavor text's passivity. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-2]`
- **Mechanical note** — The Adaptive Evolution ability on this card allows it to evolve on the same turn it is played, mechanically encoding the canonical lore that Metapod's sole function is waiting to become Butterfree. A Stage 1 card with 3-retreat-cost and 80 HP is otherwise unplayable in competitive contexts; the ability is the entire reason to run the line. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-2]`

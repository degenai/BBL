## Trivia

- **Set context** — Boss's Orders debuted in Rebel Clash (SWSH2), released May 1, 2020, at no. 154/192 as a Holo Rare Supporter. The card has been reprinted multiple times across the Sword & Shield era, including a Full Art version at no. 189/192 in the same set `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-154; WebSearch: serebii.net trainer card listings]`.
- **Mechanical reputation** — Ranked no. 2 on Pojo.com's Top 10 Pokemon Cards of 2020 and no. 1 on their Top 15 Cards of Rebel Clash. Boss's Orders functions as the Sword & Shield era successor to Guzma (Burning Shadows), which had the identical "force Benched to Active" effect. Both are considered format-defining Supporter cards, finding slots in virtually every competitive Standard and Expanded deck `[WebSearch: pojo.com "Boss's Orders Top 10 Pokemon Cards of 2020"]`.
- **Character context** — Giovanni is simultaneously the final Gym Leader of Kanto (Viridian City, Earth Badge, Ground-type specialist) and the founder and boss of Team Rocket. In the original Red/Blue (1996), his dual role was a concealed narrative twist not explicit until the Viridian Gym confrontation near game's end `[WebSearch: bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net/wiki/Giovanni]`.
- **Character context** — Giovanni commissioned Dr. Fuji to clone Mew DNA and create Mewtwo as the ultimate weapon. After Mewtwo broke free from his control, Giovanni became the primary antagonist of "Pokemon: The First Movie" (1998) and its direct sequel "Mewtwo Returns." The Mewtwo connection is central to Giovanni's long-running role as the franchise's signature human villain `[WebSearch: screenrant.com "Giovanni's Role as Team Rocket Leader"]`.
- **Design note** — Artist: nagimiso. No flavor text on this print. The red rose boutonniere is Giovanni's consistent visual signature across his TCG appearances, functioning as the sole warm-color accent in an otherwise monochromatic composition `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-154]`.

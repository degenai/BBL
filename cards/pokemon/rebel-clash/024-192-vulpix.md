## Trivia

- **Species context** — Vulpix is National Pokedex no. 37, a Fire-type Basic Pokemon introduced in Generation I. It evolves into Ninetales via Fire Stone. The Alolan regional form (Ice/Fairy) is a distinct species variant; the card's Fire typing and orange-red coloring confirm this is the Kanto form. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-24]`
- **Six-tails design** — The flavor text states: "While young, it has six gorgeous tails. When it grows, several new tails are sprouted." Vulpix's young-with-six-tails-growing-with-age design is directly reflected in the art: multiple curling tails are visible but the creature remains small and juvenile. This tail-count-as-age-marker is the core design principle of the line. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-24]`
- **Kitsune mythology** — Vulpix and its evolution Ninetales are based on the kyubi no kitsune (nine-tailed fox) of Japanese folklore, supernatural foxes whose power grows with age. The kitsune's final form — ninth tail, golden transformation — maps onto Ninetales. Pokedex entries for Ninetales warn of a thousand-year curse for those who grab a tail, directly referencing kitsune vengeance mythology. The tail-count-as-power-level is inherited from this tradition. `[ScreenRant: "Pokemon That Are Just Japanese Yokai"; Wikipedia: Ninetales; via WebSearch]`
- **Form disambiguation** — Two distinct Vulpix lines exist in the national Pokedex at no. 37 (Kanto Fire) and the Alolan form (Ice/Fairy, no. 37a). The Alolan form has a white coat and blue-tinted tails; the Kanto form has an orange-red coat and warm amber tails. The card's type indicator (Fire) and coloring confirm Kanto. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-24]`

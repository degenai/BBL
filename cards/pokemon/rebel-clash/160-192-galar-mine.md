## Trivia

- **Set context** — Galar Mine (no. 160/192) is a Stadium Trainer card from Rebel Clash (SWSH2), the second Sword & Shield expansion, released May 1, 2020. Rebel Clash's 192-card set supported the Sword & Shield games' narrative arc involving Chairman Rose's energy project, which drives the game's third-act conflict. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-160; WebSearch: Rebel Clash (TCG) Bulbapedia]`
- **Location lore** — Galar Mine is a real in-game location in Pokemon Sword & Shield (Game Freak, 2019), connecting Route 3 and Route 4 en route to Turffield. The mine is active — lit by crystal formations, staffed by mine workers, and crossed by minecart tracks. The narrative places a boy inside the mine gathering Wishing Stars on behalf of Chairman Rose, whose secret energy project requires them. Rose's energy infrastructure scheme is the game's central third-act conflict. `[WebSearch: Galar Mine Sword Shield, game8.co; WebSearch: serebii.net Pokearth Galar Mine]`
- **Mechanical** — The Stadium effect raises the Retreat Cost of both Active Pokemon by two Colorless Energy, symmetrically. Used in decks that benefit from opponent immobility — penalizing the opponent's Active Pokemon for switching. No flavor text is printed on this card; the art carries all atmospheric weight. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-160 rules]`

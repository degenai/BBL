## Trivia

- **Set context** — Rebel Clash (set code RCL, series Sword & Shield) was released May 1, 2020, as the second Sword & Shield era expansion. It contained 192 printed cards plus 17 secret rares (209 total), with a regulation mark D. Featured Pokémon VMAX included Rillaboom, Cinderace, Inteleon, Copperajah, and Dragapult. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2]`
- **Art distinction vs EVO-3** — The corpus holds two Caterpie prints: this one (RBC-001, illustrated by Uta, Sword & Shield era, 2020) and the Evolutions XY12 print (EVO-3, illustrated by Ken Sugimori, 2016). The EVO-3 is a flat-style Base Set tribute reprint with a plain yellow-green background and a daytime, static pose — the canonical Sugimori character-sheet aesthetic. This RBC print by Uta adopts the modern painted-atmosphere approach: nighttime forest setting with a crescent moon, soft ambient light through dense foliage, and the creature shown on a dark branch in three-quarter view. Same species, sharply different visual registers: retro character sheet vs. atmospheric scene painting. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-1, xy12-3]`
- **Flavor text** — "For protection, it releases a horrible stench from the antenna on its head to drive away enemies." The antenna is the prominent red-tipped organ visible in the card art. Caterpie is National Pokedex no. 10. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-1]`
- **Mechanical note** — The Adaptive Evolution ability (shared with the adjacent RBC-002 Metapod) permits evolution on the first turn or the turn the card is played, directly encoding Caterpie's lore-canonical rapid metamorphosis into TCG mechanics. The ability appears on both Caterpie and Metapod in this set, making the two-card sub-pair a coherent functional unit. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-1, swsh2-2]`

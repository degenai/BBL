## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — Milcery (National Pokedex no. 868) is a pure Fairy-type introduced in Generation VIII (Pokemon Sword & Shield). Its body is described as made of cream, and it evolves into Alcremie (no. 869), whose design is based on whipped-cream desserts decorated with sweet toppings `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia Milcery snippet; WebSearch: NintendoWire "How to Evolve Milcery into All 63 Alcremie Forms"]`.
- **Flavor text** — "This Pokémon was born from sweet-smelling particles in the air. Its body is made of cream." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-86 flavor_text]`
- **Design — 63 Alcremie forms** — Milcery has the most variation-rich evolution mechanic in the franchise. Alcremie has 63 distinct forms determined by three factors at evolution: (1) the direction of the trainer's spin (clockwise or counterclockwise), (2) the duration of the spin (short, medium, long, or ultra-long), and (3) the type of Sweet held (seven options: Berry, Clover, Flower, Love, Ribbon, Star, or Strawberry). This produces 9 cream colors × 7 sweet toppings = 63 forms total `[WebSearch: NintendoWire "Pokémon Sword and Shield: How to Evolve Milcery into All 63 Alcremie Forms"; WebSearch: GameWith "Alcremie — How To Evolve From Milcery"]`.
- **Artist** — This print is illustrated by Mina Nakai. Nakai also illustrated the Hatenna print in the same set (RBC-083) — both are Sword & Shield-introduced Fairy-line basics placed in warm pastel interior/garden settings `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-86 artist field]`.

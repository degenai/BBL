## Trivia

- **Species context** — Scyther is National Pokedex no. 123, a Bug/Flying type introduced in Generation I. It is the first card in the corpus for this species. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-4]`
- **Evolution line** — Scyther has two branching evolutionary paths introduced in different generations: trade while holding a Metal Coat to evolve into Scizor (Generation II, Steel/Bug), or use a Black Augurite item to evolve into Kleavor (introduced in Pokemon Legends: Arceus, 2022, Rock/Bug). Kleavor represents a Hisuian-era regional divergence of the line, making Scyther one of a small set of species with two mutually exclusive evolution outcomes. `[Dexerto / CharlieintelWebSearch snippets: Scyther evolution guide]`
- **Flavor text** — "Its two sharp scythes are more than just weapons. It uses them with dexterity to dress its prey before eating." The Swords Dance attack on this card mirrors the franchise move of the same name (raises Attack two stages), grounding the mechanical kit in the species' warrior archetype. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-4]`
- **Mechanical note** — Swords Dance on this card buffs the follow-up Blinding Scythe to 90 damage (20 + 70 bonus), making the two-attack sequence the entire offensive plan for a 1-retreat-cost 80HP basic. Functional only as a setup card in a dedicated Scyther deck; generally below competitive threshold. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-4]`

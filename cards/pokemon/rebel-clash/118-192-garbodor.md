## Trivia

- **Set context** — Garbodor (no. 118/192) appears in Rebel Clash (SWSH2), the second Sword and Shield series expansion, released May 1, 2020. Its Japanese counterpart set was titled "Rebellion Crash." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-118]`
- **Design lineage** — Garbodor and its pre-evolution Trubbish were created specifically to represent urban pollution in the Unova region, which was built around an industrialized city aesthetic. Lead designer Ken Sugimori and designer Takao Unno cited Unova's urban setting as the direct prompt for including a garbage-based Pokemon line. Structurally, Trubbish/Garbodor are a deliberate parallel to the Generation I pollution line (Grimer/Muk, Koffing/Weezing): all are pure Poison-type with one evolution at roughly equal base stat totals. Garbodor specifically represents land-waste; Grimer/Muk represent liquid sludge. `[lavacutcontent.com: Gen 5 Historia, Ken Sugimori interview via WebSearch snippet; remptongames.com: Evolution of Pokemon Design Gen 5 via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Tsukumogami / kaiju echoes** — Community design analysis notes Garbodor may also draw on tsukumogami (objects animated by possessing spirits in Japanese folklore) and visually echoes Hedorah, the pollution kaiju from the Godzilla franchise. Neither connection is officially confirmed by Game Freak. `[facts.net: 30 Facts About Garbodor via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Flavor text** — "This Pokemon eats trash, which turns into poison inside its body. The main component of the poison depends on what sort of trash was eaten." The entry frames Garbodor not as a pollution source but as a pollution converter — it transforms existing human waste, not generates new toxins independently. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-118]`
- **Mechanical note** — The Poisonous Puddle Ability triggers only if a Stadium card is in play, making this Garbodor a conditional Poison engine rather than a reliable one. It rewards Stadium-heavy builds but contributes little in Stadium-light metas. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-118]`

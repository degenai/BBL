## Trivia

- **Designer counterpart pair** — Electabuzz (National Pokedex no. 125, Electric) and Magmar (no. 126, Fire) are a Generation I designer-coordinated counterpart pair. In the original Red/Blue games, Magmar was a Blue-exclusive and Electabuzz was a Red-exclusive (extending through FireRed/LeafGreen, Black 2/White 2, and Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl), immediately casting them as version opposites. They share comparable height and build, learn moves at similar rates, evolve through identical mechanics (level 30 base, then trade-holding item ending in "-izer": Electirizer vs. Magmarizer), and were both found in distinct industrial locations (the Power Plant and the Pokemon Mansion respectively). The Electabuzz/Magmar pair is one of the most explicitly symmetrical counterpart designs in Gen I. `[Bulbapedia: Magmar, Electabuzz; List of Pokemon with counterparts; via WebSearch]`
- **Flavor text** — "While it's often blamed for power outages, the truth is the cause of outages is more often an error on the part of the electric company." This flavor text is notable for explicitly deflecting blame from Electabuzz onto corporate negligence — one of the most sardonic Pokedex entries in the Kanto roster. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-58]`
- **Evolution line** — Electabuzz evolves into Electivire (Generation IV) when traded holding an Electirizer. Its pre-evolution Elekid was introduced in Generation II. The full line (Elekid → Electabuzz → Electivire) mirrors the Magmar line (Magby → Magmar → Magmortar) step for step — even the held-item evolution mechanism was added in the same generation for both lines. `[Bulbapedia: Electabuzz, via WebSearch; PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-58]`
- **Mechanical note** — Electroslug (70 damage for 3 energy including 2 Lightning) is a solid vanilla attacker attack for the Sword & Shield era. The 90HP basic with 2-retreat-cost is a mid-tier support attacker. No notable competitive history for this specific print. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-58]`

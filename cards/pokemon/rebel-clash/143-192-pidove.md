## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — Pidove (National Pokedex no. 519, Normal/Flying) is the regional bird of the Unova region, introduced in Generation V (Pokemon Black & White, 2010). Unova is officially modeled on the New York City metropolitan area — confirmed by director Junichi Masuda, who cited NYC's cultural diversity as the inspiration. Castelia City is modeled on Lower Manhattan; Skyarrow Bridge mirrors the Brooklyn Bridge `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia Unova; WebSearch: Pokemon Black and White Wikipedia]`.
- **Design** — Pidove's Japanese name is Mamepato, from mamemame (beans, as in "birdseed") and hato (pigeon). Hato is also a homophone of haato (heart), which is why Pidove's design incorporates a heart motif on its chest — a visual pun embedded in the species name `[WebSearch: GamesRadar "Pokemon Black and White Pokedex — Pidove, Tranquill, Unfezant"]`. The species line culminates in Unfezant (no. 521), likely based on a pheasant or green pheasant — Japan's national bird.
- **Flavor text** — "Where people go, these Pokémon follow. If you're scattering food for them, be careful — several hundred of them can gather at once." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-143 flavor_text]` The flock-behavior description mirrors real urban pigeon behavior in dense cities, grounding the Unova-as-NYC design in observable urban ecology.
- **Rebel Clash context** — Pidove appears in Rebel Clash (SWSH2, 2020) as a Generation V Unova visitor in a Sword & Shield set. This print shows two Pidove on a rooftop ledge — the artist (Ryota Murayama) stages the flock-behavior flavor text directly in the art `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-143]`.

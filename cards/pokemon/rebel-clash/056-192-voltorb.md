## Trivia

- **Origin lore** — Voltorb is National Pokedex no. 100, first discovered at the facility where modern Poke Balls were manufactured. Pokedex entries across the series describe it as resembling a Poke Ball and speculate it was created when a Poke Ball was exposed to an energy pulse. Its non-naturally-occurring components support the manufactured-object-become-creature reading. This labor/industrial-setting hook is the strongest industrial-design narrative in the corpus: a factory product that gained a volatile life of its own. `[Bulbapedia: Voltorb, via WebSearch snippet; PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-56]`
- **Tsukumogami angle** — Community analysis notes that Voltorb may be based on the tsukumogami class of Japanese yokai — inanimate objects that acquire a spirit upon their 100th year of existence. Voltorb's National Pokedex number is exactly 100, likely an intentional encoding of this mythological threshold. `[Wikipedia: Voltorb; Bulbapedia: Voltorb; via WebSearch]`
- **Hisuian variant** — Pokemon Legends: Arceus (2022) introduced Hisuian Voltorb, which resembles Apricorn-made Poke Balls (the pre-industrial era predecessor). Its existence opens a design question: are Kantonian Voltorbs mimicking modern Poke Balls, or did pre-Hisuian Voltorbs inspire Poke Ball design? The game leaves the question intentionally open. `[GameRant: "Hisuian Voltorb is a Chicken or Egg Conundrum"; CBR: Hisuian Voltorb; via WebSearch]`
- **Flavor text** — "Usually found in power plants. Easily mistaken for a Poke Ball, it has zapped many people." Power plant habitat directly ties the species to the industrial-electric-infrastructure setting. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-56]`

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Magmar appears in Sword & Shield — Rebel Clash (2020), a Galar-era set. It is printed as card no. 029/192, directly adjacent to its counterpart [[058-192-electabuzz]] (no. 058/192) — both commons in the same set, a deliberate production pairing. `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh2-29, swsh2-58]`
- **Designer counterpart pair** — Magmar (National Pokedex no. 126) and Electabuzz (no. 125) are the most symmetrical version-exclusive pair in Generation I: Magmar was Blue-exclusive, Electabuzz was Red-exclusive, found at mirrored industrial locations (Pokemon Mansion vs. Power Plant). The pair gained pre-evolutions simultaneously in Gen II (Magby and Elekid) and final evolutions simultaneously in Gen IV (Magmortar and Electivire), each requiring a trade while holding their respective "-izer" items (Magmarizer and Electirizer). Adjacent Pokedex numbers and step-for-step generation parity make this the clearest case of coordinated design in the original 151. `[Bulbapedia: Magmar]` `[Bulbapedia: Electabuzz]` `[WebSearch: Magmar Versus Electabuzz Is Secretly Pokemon's Best Story, TheGamer.com]`
- **Flavor text** — `"Its entire body is burning. When it breathes, the temperature rises. When it sneezes, flames shoot out!"` `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh2-29]`
- **Version correction note** — The existing Connections section originally had the version exclusivity reversed (Red/Blue). Corrected above based on Bulbapedia confirmation: Magmar = Blue-exclusive, Electabuzz = Red-exclusive. `[Bulbapedia: Magmar]`

## Connections

- [[058-192-electabuzz]] — Generation I designer-coordinated counterpart pair, both printed in the same set (Rebel Clash 029/192 and 058/192). Magmar (National Pokedex no. 126, Fire) and Electabuzz (no. 125, Electric) are Kanto's most explicitly symmetrical version-exclusive pair: Magmar was Blue-exclusive and Electabuzz Red-exclusive in the original 1996 Pokemon Red and Blue, found in mirrored industrial locations (Pokemon Mansion vs. Power Plant), evolve via identical mechanics (level 30 base, then trade-holding the "-izer" item: Magmarizer vs. Electirizer, both added in Generation IV), and have mirrored three-stage evolution lines (Magby → Magmar → Magmortar; Elekid → Electabuzz → Electivire) introduced step-for-step in the same generations. The Pokedex-adjacent numbering (125/126) confirms the design intent. `[Bulbapedia: Magmar, Electabuzz, List of Pokemon with counterparts]` `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh2-29, swsh2-58]`

## Trivia

- **Art distinction vs DAA-035** — The corpus holds two Galarian Mr. Mime prints: this one (RBC-037, kirisAki art, Rebel Clash 2020) and the Darkness Ablaze print (DAA-035, Misa Tsutsui art, 2020). The DAA print is adjacent-collector to its Mr. Rime evolution (DAA-036) and is the wave-22 IP-verified anchor. This RBC print (kirisAki) differs compositionally: the creature's dance pose is more full-body-extended, the ice surface is more prominent in the lower frame, and the background skews a cleaner icy-blue. Both share identical printed flavor text verbatim. The kirisAki art style is more graphic and stylized relative to Tsutsui's approach. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-37, swsh3-35]`
- **Flavor text** — "Its talent is tap-dancing. It can also manipulate temperatures to create a floor of ice, which this Pokémon can kick up to use as a barrier." This text is identical across both corpus prints (RBC-037 and DAA-035). `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-37]`
- **Regional form context** — Galarian Mr. Mime (National Pokedex no. 122, Ice/Psychic) is a regional variant of the Kantonian Mr. Mime (Psychic/Fairy). The Galarian form evolves into Mr. Rime (also Galar-exclusive), rather than the Kantonian Mr. Mime's evolutionary dead end. This print uses a Water energy card (Ice energy proxy) type indicator. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-37]`

## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — Stunky (National Pokedex no. 434, Poison/Dark) was introduced in Generation IV (Pokemon Diamond and Pearl, 2006), native to the Sinnoh region. In Diamond and Pearl, Stunky is a version-exclusive found in Pokemon Diamond (not Pearl), encountered on Routes 206, 214, and 221 in Sinnoh `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia Stunky snippet; WebSearch: Serebii.net Pokedex entry 434]`.
- **Design** — Stunky is designed after a hog-nosed skunk. Its Poison/Dark dual typing reflects both the skunk's chemical defense (Poison) and its nocturnal, furtive nature (Dark). In Diamond and Pearl, Stunky's evolved form Skuntank was used by Team Galactic Commander Jupiter, while the rival-game-exclusive Glameow was used by Commander Mars — a version-symmetry design choice `[WebSearch: RankedBoost BDSP Stunky; Bulbapedia Stunky snippet]`.
- **Flavor text** — "From its rear, it sprays a foul-smelling liquid at opponents. It aims for their faces, and it can hit them from over 16 feet away." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-114 flavor_text]` The 16-foot (approximately 5-meter) spray range matches the real-world accuracy of actual skunk spray, which can reach up to 10 feet with high accuracy.
- **Rebel Clash context** — Stunky appears in Rebel Clash (SWSH2, 2020) as a Sinnoh visitor in a Sword & Shield set, a recurring pattern in the TCG where older-generation Pokemon are reprinted in newer sets. The Darkness energy typing on this print corresponds to its in-game Dark typing `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-114]`.

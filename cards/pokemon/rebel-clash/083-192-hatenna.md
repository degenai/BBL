## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — Hatenna (National Pokedex no. 856) is a pure Psychic-type introduced in Generation VIII (Pokemon Sword & Shield). It evolves into Hattrem (no. 857) at level 32, then into Hatterene (no. 858, Psychic/Fairy) at level 42. The full line was introduced in Sword & Shield alongside the Impidimp and Milcery lines as part of Generation VIII's Fairy-type expansion `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia Hatenna snippet; WebSearch: TheGamer "How to Find & Evolve Hatenna into Hatterene"]`.
- **Design** — Hatenna's line is built around a witch aesthetic: Hatterene has long layered hair draped like a cloak, resembling a stereotypical sorceress, and Hattrem's protruding head-hat is made of its own hair — referencing the folklore belief that a witch's power is connected to her hair. The emotion-sensing protrusion on Hatenna's head is the design's anchor: the species can detect emotional states through it, and grows reclusive as a result of the stimulus overload `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia Hatterene snippet]`.
- **Flavor text** — "Via the protrusion on its head, it senses other creatures' emotions. If you don't have a calm disposition, it will never warm up to you." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-83 flavor_text]` The calm-disposition gating is the mechanical hook: Hatterene (the final evolution) will attack anything expressing strong emotions, including positive ones — the line inverts the typical "friendly Psychic Pokemon" trope.
- **Ability note** — Hatterene's signature hidden ability is Magic Bounce, which reflects status moves back at the opponent. This makes the final evolution a useful anti-setup wall in competitive formats, particularly against Defog, Spore, and entry hazards `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia Hatterene snippet]`.

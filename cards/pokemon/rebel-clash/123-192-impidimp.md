## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — Impidimp (National Pokedex no. 859) is a Dark/Fairy type introduced in Generation VIII (Pokemon Sword & Shield). Impidimp and its evolution line (Morgrem no. 860, Grimmsnarl no. 861) are the only Pokemon with the Dark/Fairy type combination — no other Pokemon in the franchise shares it `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia Impidimp; WebSearch: CyberPost "Is there a Dark Fairy type Pokemon?"]`.
- **Design** — Impidimp's design draws from European folklore's mischievous fairy-like creatures: imps, goblins, gremlins, and boggarts. One specific possible reference is the Lincoln Imp, a grotesque stone-carved figure in Lincoln Cathedral, England — fitting for the UK-modeled Galar region `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia Impidimp snippet]`. Impidimp and Morgrem are also the only pink Dark-type Pokemon in the franchise.
- **Flavor text** — "Through its nose, it sucks in the emanations produced by people and Pokémon when they feel annoyed. It thrives off this negative energy." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh2-123 flavor_text]` The negative-energy-feeding mechanic is the design spine of the entire line: Impidimp causes irritation to generate food, Morgrem feigns helplessness to provoke hostility, and Grimmsnarl weaponizes the harvested energy through its hair.
- **Mechanical note** — Grimmsnarl (the final evolution) became a competitive staple in Sword & Shield doubles formats due to its unique access to both Prankster and powerful support moves (Reflect, Light Screen, Thunder Wave), a combination uncommon on a Dark-type `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia Impidimp/Grimmsnarl snippet]`.

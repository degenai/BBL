## Trivia

- **Three Wise Monkeys mapping** — Pansear represents "hear no evil" in the elemental monkey trio's design conceit: its sprite depicts a hand raised near its ear. Panpour represents "see no evil" (eyes closed pose) and Pansage represents "speak no evil." The mapping is documented on Bulbapedia's Elemental monkeys article and is the most cited design fact about the group. `[Bulbapedia: Elemental monkeys]`
- **Set context** — Burning Shadows (SM3) released August 5, 2017, as the third Sun & Moon–era English expansion. The set focused on Alolan forms and Team Skull; the elemental monkeys appear as straightforward commons carrying forward Unova Pokédex entries introduced in Black & White (2011). `[PokemonTCG.io: sm3]`
- **Flavor text** — "Very intelligent, it roasts berries before eating them. It likes to help people." This flavor text reflects Pansear's fire-type culinary behavior and cooperative disposition — a contrast to the "trio as opposites" framing; all three elemental monkeys are noted in the Pokédex for helping humans. `[Scryfall-equivalent: PokemonTCG.io sm3-22 flavorText field]`
- **Gift Pokémon mechanic** — In Pokémon Black and White, one elemental monkey is gifted to the player in the Dreamyard based on the trainer's starter: players who chose Oshawott received Pansear (fire counters grass). This forced type-diversity mechanic was Game Freak's way of distributing coverage across all three starter choices in the opening routes. `[Bulbapedia: Elemental monkeys]`

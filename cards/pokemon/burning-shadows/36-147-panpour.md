## Trivia

- **Three Wise Monkeys mapping** — Panpour represents "see no evil" in the trio's design conceit: its sprite depicts closed or averted eyes. Pansear represents "hear no evil" and Pansage represents "speak no evil." All three share identical base stats, similar moves, and parallel cries, making the symmetry deliberate at the design level. `[Bulbapedia: Elemental monkeys; Bulbapedia: Panpour (Pokémon)]`
- **Flavor text** — "The water stored inside the tuft on its head is full of nutrients. It waters plants with it using its tail." The tuft is thus both a water reservoir and a gardening tool — a design conceit that mirrors Pansear's fire-roasting and Pansage's herb-growing, each monkey embodying its element through food-related utility. `[PokemonTCG.io: sm3-36 flavorText field]`
- **Set context** — Burning Shadows (SM3), released August 5, 2017. Panpour is a Water-type common in a set otherwise known for its Darkness-themed legendaries (Marshadow, Necrozma) — the elemental monkeys appear as low-rarity filler carrying Unova Pokédex representation into the Alolan-era Sun & Moon block. `[PokemonTCG.io: sm3]`
- **Gift Pokémon mechanic** — In Pokémon Black and White, a Panpour was gifted to players who chose Tepig as their starter (water counters fire). The trio was designed as a coverage-provision system for early-game routes before the first gym. `[Bulbapedia: Elemental monkeys]`

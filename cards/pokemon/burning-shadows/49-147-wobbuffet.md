## Trivia

- **Anime comedy fixture — Jessie's Wobbuffet** — Wobbuffet became Team Rocket's unofficial fourth member when Jessie unknowingly traded her Lickitung for it. It became famous for popping out of its Poké Ball uninvited, shouting its own name at the end of Team Rocket's motto, and crying out as the trio blasted off. Its constant interruptions were written as a rakugo-style punchline in the Japanese original. `[Bulbapedia: Jessie's Wobbuffet; Wikipedia: Wobbuffet]`
- **Japanese name is a comedian reference** — Wobbuffet's Japanese name is Sonans (ソーナンス), derived from a catchphrase of rakugo comedian Sanpei Hayashiya: "sō nansu, okusan" ("That's the way it is, ma'am"). The salute pose Wobbuffet holds — visible in this card's art — mirrors Hayashiya's signature gesture. This makes Wobbuffet one of the few Pokémon explicitly designed around a specific real-world Japanese comedian's material. `[Wikipedia: Wobbuffet; Bulbapedia: Wobbuffet (Pokémon)]`
- **Counter-attack design philosophy** — Flavor text: "It hates light and shock. If attacked, it inflates its body to build up its counterstrike." This is mechanically literal: Wobbuffet's main-series moveset consists almost entirely of counter and mirror-coat moves — it cannot attack first, only retaliate. The "patience before counterattack" design philosophy is baked directly into both lore and game function. `[PokemonTCG.io: sm3-49 flavorText field; Bulbapedia: Wobbuffet (Pokémon)]`
- **Set context** — Burning Shadows (SM3), released August 5, 2017. Wobbuffet appears as an uncommon Psychic-type. Its TCG attack Shadowy Knot (50x the opponent's Active Pokémon's Retreat Cost in Colorless) is a niche retaliatory mechanic consistent with the counter-attacker design identity. `[PokemonTCG.io: sm3-49]`

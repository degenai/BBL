## Trivia

- **Maneki-neko design origin** — Kanto Meowth's forehead coin is an Edo-period koban, and the entire design is rooted in the maneki-neko (beckoning cat) Japanese good-luck charm. Some early game sprites depict Meowth with a raised paw, the maneki-neko's signature pose. The Japanese name Nyāsu references both "nyan" (cat sound) and a proverb: "like a koban to a cat" — roughly equivalent to "casting pearls before swine," underscoring that Meowth obsesses over coins without understanding their value. `[Bulbapedia: Meowth (Pokémon); Wikipedia: Meowth]`
- **Pay Day mechanic connection** — Meowth's signature move Pay Day (present since Generation I) has the Pokémon hurl koban coins at opponents; coins can be picked up after battle for money. The move literalizes the koban-forehead design into a combat mechanic. This card's oracle text carries Fake Out (not Pay Day), but the coin iconography visible in the art is the same forehead koban that Pay Day weaponizes. `[Bulbapedia: Meowth (Pokémon)]`
- **Flavor text** — "It lies around all day, becoming active near dusk. At night, it wanders the city in search of loose change." The urban coin-scavenging behavior reinforces the koban/wealth obsession baked into the maneki-neko origin. The Mediterranean-style rooftop setting in this card's art is consistent with that city-wanderer framing. `[PokemonTCG.io: sm3-101 flavorText field]`
- **Kanto vs. Galarian contrast** — This print is Kanto-form Meowth (Normal-type, cream-coated, golden coin, evolves into Persian at level 28). The corpus also holds Galarian Meowth (Rebel Clash 126/192): Steel-type, darker coat, iron forehead coin, Pokédex-described as toughened by life among a seafaring people. Two forms, same National Pokédex no. 52, diverging in typing, lore, and evolution. `[Bulbapedia: Meowth (Pokémon); PokemonTCG.io: swsh2-126]`

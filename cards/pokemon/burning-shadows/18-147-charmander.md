## Trivia

- **Kagemaru Himeno — TCG's longest-tenured artist** — This card is illustrated by Kagemaru Himeno, who has been creating Pokémon TCG art since the Jungle set (1999 in English, 1996 in Japanese). As of 2024 she has illustrated 654+ cards including reprints, making her one of the longest-tenured illustrators in the entire franchise. She works in watercolor, using soft tones with bright highlights and characteristic white flecks suggesting light through foliage. `[Bulbapedia: Kagemaru Himeno; artofpkm.com: Kagemaru Himeno]`
- **Print within the charizard-line cohort** — This BUS-18 print is a distinct art from the VIV-023 Charmander already in the corpus. Both are by Kagemaru Himeno; both are commons. The two prints document the same artist returning to the same subject across different Sun & Moon vs. Sword & Shield eras of the TCG. `[PokemonTCG.io: sm3-18; characters node: charizard-line]`
- **Flame-tail life indicator** — Flavor text states: "The flame on its tail indicates Charmander's life force. If it is healthy, the flame burns brightly." This is one of the most iconic pieces of Pokémon lore from Generation I: the implication that Charmander will die if its flame goes out was not explicitly stated in the original games but became widely referenced across multiple Pokédex entries and the anime. `[PokemonTCG.io: sm3-18 flavorText field; Bulbapedia: Charmander (Pokémon)]`
- **Set context** — Burning Shadows (SM3), released August 5, 2017. Thematically the set's title nods to Marshadow and Necrozma (shadow/darkness motifs), but the Charmander here is a straightforward Kanto starter common — one of several Kanto representatives in a set otherwise set in Alola. `[PokemonTCG.io: sm3]`

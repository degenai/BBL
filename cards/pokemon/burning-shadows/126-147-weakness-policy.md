## Trivia

- **Set context** — Weakness Policy is an Uncommon Pokemon Tool card in Burning Shadows (SM3), released August 5, 2017. Burning Shadows is the third expansion of the Sun and Moon era and features Tapu Bulu-GX, Ho-Oh-GX, and Darkrai-GX as headline cards. The set's shadow/fire theme provides a thematically odd home for a bureaucratic document card — Weakness Policy sits as a utilitarian tool print within a GX-heavy set. `[PokemonTCG.io API: sm3-126]`
- **Reprint history** — Weakness Policy first appeared in English in Primal Clash (XY5, 2015, no. 142/160), making the Burning Shadows print its first reprint. The card originated in the Japanese Gaia Volcano subset (2014) before the English XY series began. The effect — "the Pokémon this card is attached to has no Weakness" — has remained identical across all prints. No Weakness is a significant defensive swing in a format where Weakness doubles incoming damage; attaching this Tool sacrifices other Tool slots (Muscle Band, Choice Band) for blanket type-immunity. `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia Weakness Policy Primal Clash 142; PokemonTCG.io API sm3-126]`
- **Competitive context** — In the SM Standard format (2017-2019), Weakness Policy saw niche use in decks running Pokémon with punishing Weaknesses — most notably Gardevoir-GX (Psychic Weakness to Metal) and Buzzwole-GX (Fighting Weakness to Psychic). It was not a format staple; the more common approach was to run non-Weak attackers or accept the Weakness matchup, as Choice Band's damage bonus was usually more universally valuable. Community consensus on competitive forums treated it as a tech option rather than a core slot. `[WebSearch: Pokebeach forums Weakness Policy competitive; WebSearch: Bulbapedia Weakness Policy]`
- **Artist** — **Ayaka Yoshida**, a Pokémon TCG illustrator active across the Sun and Moon era, credited on multiple Item and Trainer cards. `[PokemonTCG.io API: sm3-126]`

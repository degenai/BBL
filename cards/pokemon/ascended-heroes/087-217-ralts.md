## Trivia

- **Set context** — Ralts (no. 087/217) appears in the Mega Evolution—Ascended Heroes expansion (ASC), released January 30, 2026, themed around Pokémon Legends: Z-A. At 295 cards it was the largest English Pokemon TCG set at its launch, structured around a new wave of Mega Evolution Pokémon ex. `[PokemonTCG.io API; pokemon.com Ascended Heroes announcement]`
- **Design** — Illustrated by Terada Tera, a 2025 TCG debut artist. Pokemon.com describes their style as "vibrant, colorful, and too cute to ignore" with "an almost bouncy shading style and contrasting colors." This Ralts card is among the artist's earliest published works; their debut was an Iono's Kilowattrel illustration rare in Journey Together. `[pokemon.com: Meet the Artists Who Made Their Pokemon TCG Debut in 2025]`
- **Type history** — Ralts is National Pokedex no. 280 (introduced Generation III, Ruby/Sapphire). In the video games, it gained Fairy typing alongside Psychic in Generation VI (X/Y). The TCG discontinued the Fairy type with Sword & Shield and has printed Ralts as a Psychic-type card ever since, including in this printing. `[PokemonTCG.io API; pokemondb.net/pokedex/ralts; serebii.net Fairy type page]`
- **Flavor text** — "The horns on its head provide a strong power that enables it to sense people's emotions." — Ralts (Ascended Heroes 087). `[PokemonTCG.io API]` Ralts' species classification is the "Feeling Pokemon" — Pokedex entries establish it hides when it senses hostility and is drawn toward warm emotions. `[pokemon.com/us/pokedex/ralts]`
- **Set context** — Mega Gardevoir ex appears as card no. 089/217 in the same Ascended Heroes set — placing this Ralts common two cards away in set numbering from its Mega Evolution apex. `[pokebeach.com: Ascended Heroes Set Guide 2026]`

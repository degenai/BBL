## Trivia

- **Set context** — Steam Siege (XY11) released August 3, 2016 — the eleventh XY Series expansion. Its Japanese counterparts were titled "Fever-Burst Fighter" and "Cruel Traitor," emphasizing a conflict theme. Rufflet (no. 92/114) is a Common Colorless-type entry in a set headlined by Volcanion-EX and Mega Gardevoir-EX. `[Bulbapedia: Steam Siege (TCG), via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design** — Rufflet (National Pokedex no. 627) debuted in Generation V (Pokemon Black and White, 2010) as a bald eagle chick. Its red, white, and blue color scheme and the single head-plume are deliberate references to American national iconography — the Rufflet/Braviary line was designed by Takao Unno to embody Unova's real-world New York basis as an American-eagle allegory. Rufflet is a version-exclusive, appearing only in Pokemon White and White 2. `[Bulbapedia: Rufflet, via WebSearch snippet; PokeAPI /pokemon-species/627]`
- **Flavor text** — `"They will challenge anything, even strong opponents, without fear. Their frequent fights help them become stronger."` — Pokedex entry (Pokemon White), printed verbatim on this card. The attack "Reckless Charge" (20 damage, 10 self-damage) mirrors this Pokedex framing mechanically: strength through self-hazarding aggression. `[Card frontmatter; PokeAPI /pokemon-species/rufflet]`
- **Artist** — Mitsuhiro Arita has illustrated over 800 Pokemon TCG cards across nearly every expansion since the 1996 Base Set, including the original Base Set Charizard (a pristine Japanese No Rarity copy sold for $1.7 million in March 2026 — the most valuable Pokemon card ever). His signature approach — Pokemon in naturalistic habitats, in motion — is evident here: Rufflet against open sky, wings fully extended. `[GamesRadar+: Mitsuhiro Arita interview; Bleeding Cool artist spotlight, via WebSearch snippets]`

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Team Up (SM9) released February 1, 2019, as part of the Sun & Moon series. The set introduced the Tag Team GX mechanic (two Pokemon on one card). Jirachi appears here as card no. 99 of 181 printed total — a supporting non-GX Holo Rare rather than a headline Tag Team, but proved to be one of the set's most impactful competitive cards. `[PokemonTCG.io API: sm9-99]`
- **Design** — Jirachi (National Pokedex no. 385, Generation III) is based on the Japanese Tanabata festival. The turquoise strips hanging from its three star-point head projections are tanzaku — the paper slips on which wishes are written and hung from bamboo during Tanabata. Its signature move Doom Desire involves Jirachi making a wish that resolves as damage after two turns. `[WebSearch: Bulbanews — On the Origin of Species: Jirachi]`
- **Flavor text** — "It is said to have the ability to grant any wish for just one week every thousand years." `[PokemonTCG.io API: sm9-99]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — Stellar Wish made this Jirachi one of the most widely played non-GX supports in 2019-2021 Standard format: it functions as a Trainer-search engine at the cost of putting Jirachi to Sleep each use. Its key synergy partner was Escape Board (Ultra Prism), which reduced retreat cost to zero even while Asleep, letting players search a Trainer and freely retreat each turn. When Escape Board rotated out, Jirachi's meta viability declined significantly; Pojo ranked it the no. 4 loss in the 2022 rotation. It remains legal in Expanded. `[Pojo.com: Top 15 Cards Lost to the 2022 Rotation]` `[PokemonTCG.io API: sm9-99 legalities]`
- **Design** — Art by HYOGONOSUKE. TCGPlayer market price (holofoil): $6.32 as of May 2026, sustained by Expanded format demand and collector interest in the holo treatment. `[PokemonTCG.io API: sm9-99 TCGPlayer pricing]`

## Trivia

- **Set / species context** — Pineco is National Pokedex no. 204, a Bug-type introduced in Generation II (Pokemon Gold and Silver, 1999). It is the Bagworm Pokemon, native to Johto's forests, and evolves into Forretress (Bug/Steel) at level 31. Its appearance in SV02: Paldea Evolved (2023) brings a Johto-era species into the Scarlet and Violet era. Art by Kouki Saitou. `[Serebii.net: Pineco no. 204]` `[Pokemon.com TCG database: sv02/4]`
- **Flavor text** — "It likes to make its shell thicker by adding layers of tree bark. The additional weight doesn't bother it." This directly encodes Pineco's Pokedex behavior: it glues tree bark to its body using a fluid it spits out that hardens on contact with air, building defensive armor incrementally. The shell's function is camouflage in forest environments and protection against bird Pokemon. `[Card frontmatter flavor_text, cross-referenced with Bulbapedia: Pineco via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design origin** — Pineco is based on a bagworm, the larval form of moths in the family Psychidae that construct portable protective cases from twigs, bark, and plant matter. Forretress upgrades this design to a steel-armored fortress form. Both are classified as the Bagworm Pokemon — a category they share with Burmy and Wormadam. `[Bulbapedia: Pineco, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Pokemon Database: Pineco]`
- **Notable behavior** — Pineco is typically immobile while feeding but will drop from its branch and use Self-Destruct if disturbed. This explosive defense mechanism carries into its competitive TCG and video game identity; Pineco and Forretress are historically associated with Spikes-laying and Rapid Spin support roles in competitive play. `[Bulbapedia: Pineco, via WebSearch snippet]`

## Trivia

- **Flavor text** — "Its preferred food is dravite. After it has eaten this mineral, crystals form inside the Pokémon, rising to the surface of part of its body." Dravite is a real brown-to-black tourmaline mineral; tourmaline forms when magma cools in igneous rock — thematically consistent with volcanic island geology. This exact flavor text is shared verbatim with the Crimson Invasion print (no. 33/111), making it one of the franchise's persistent cross-set flavor echoes. [PokemonTCG.io: sm2-41; Geology Science: Dravite]
- **Regional form context** — The Alolan form of Graveler (no. 75) switches typing from Rock/Ground (Kantonian) to Rock/Electric; the electrostatic charge is explained in-game as a result of absorbing magnetic minerals unique to Alola's volcanic substrate. The Kanto Geodude line's Alolan counterpart was revealed at launch of Sun & Moon (2016) and was among the first generation of regional variants in the franchise. [Bulbapedia: Graveler (Pokémon) — Alolan Form; WebSearch snippet]
- **Set context** — Guardians Rising (released May 5, 2017) is the second Sun & Moon series set and one of the first TCG sets to print Alolan regional form Pokémon in quantity. Alolan Graveler appears here as no. 41/145 (Uncommon, Lightning-type); the Alolan Golem evolution also appears in the same set. [Bulbapedia: Guardians Rising (TCG)]
- **Cross-print distinction** — This print (GUR 41/145, artist field reads "match" in PokemonTCG.io — artist attribution unclear) uses a rocky mountain terrain backdrop and a wide forward-facing action stance. The Crimson Invasion print (CSI 33/111, artist kirisAki) uses a warm gradient backdrop at a three-quarter low-stance. Same species and flavor text; compositionally distinct framing for the same design. [PokemonTCG.io: sm2-41, sm4-33]

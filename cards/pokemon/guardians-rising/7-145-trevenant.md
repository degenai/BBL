## Trivia

- **Set context** — Trevenant (no. 7/145) is a Stage 1 Grass-type Rare in Guardians Rising (SM2), the second Sun and Moon series expansion, released May 5, 2017. The set takes its name from Alola's Guardian Deities — the four Tapus. Trevenant is a non-Alolan carryover species. `[PokemonTCG.io API: sm2-7]`
- **Design lineage: Kodama, not jorogumo** — Trevenant and its pre-evolution Phantump draw from Kodama, tree spirits of Japanese folklore that inhabit old-growth trees. Harming a Kodama-possessed tree was believed to bring curses upon the offender — directly mirrored in Trevenant's lore. The stripe-like body markings may reference shimenawa, the sacred rope used in Shinto practice to demarcate spiritually inhabited objects, often seen tied around Kodama trees. The jorogumo connection (spider-woman yokai) does not apply to Trevenant; the confirmed lineage is Kodama. `[Dr. Lava @DrLavaYT via X/Twitter, WebSearch snippet; Tofugu.com: Who's That Pokemon Yokai Edition via WebSearch snippet; Bulbapedia: Trevenant (Pokemon) via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Additional folkloric threads** — The single-eye motif may draw on the cyclopean feature common across multiple yokai traditions, and Trevenant's long branch-fingers have a possible secondary connection to Shurale, a horned long-fingered forest spirit from Tatar and Bashkir folklore. Both are community-noted parallels, not officially confirmed by Game Freak. `[Bulbapedia: Trevenant (Pokemon) via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Flavor text** — "This Pokemon is said to devour anyone daring to ravage the forest. To the creatures dwelling in the forest, it offers great kindness." The dual-nature framing — apex threat to intruders, protector to residents — is the core character thesis across Trevenant's Pokedex entries and maps directly to Kodama lore: the spirit is benign to those who respect the forest and fatal to those who don't. `[PokemonTCG.io API: sm2-7]`
- **Competitive history** — Poltergeist (2 Colorless, 30x damage per Trainer card in opponent's hand) is both a hand-read and a damage source — it forces the opponent to reveal their hand, providing information alongside the attack. A different Trevenant printing (BREAKpoint, XY era) was central to a hand-lock strategy pairing with Wally that became strong enough to be banned from the Expanded format. This Guardians Rising printing does not carry that ban; the BREAK era variant is the restricted card. `[PokemonTCG.io API: sm2-7; pokemon.cardsrealm.com: Expanded Banlist Explained via WebSearch snippet]`

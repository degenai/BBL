## Trivia

- **Species design** — Braviary (National Pokedex no. 628) was introduced in Generation V (Black and White, 2010 JP / 2011 EN) and was designed by Takao Unno. The species is based on the bald eagle — the national bird of the United States — and its red/white/blue color scheme explicitly maps to the US flag, reflecting the Unova region's real-world inspiration as the New York metropolitan area. `[Bulbapedia: Braviary, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Gender exclusivity** — Braviary and its pre-evolution Rufflet are male-only Pokemon; their counterpart line (Vullaby/Mandibuzz) is female-only. This is among the more deliberate gender-exclusive pairings in the series, paralleling the bald eagle/turkey vulture dynamic in American wildlife. `[Bulbapedia: Braviary, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Set context** — Surging Sparks (SV8, released November 8, 2024) is a 445-card master set combining Japan's Paradise Dragona and Supercharged Breaker sets, themed around Tera Pikachu ex. Braviary appears at no. 153/191 as a Stage 1 Uncommon with 130 HP, illustrated by Anesaki Dynamic. A separate Illustration Rare alternate-art version (no. 214/191) exists in the same set, illustrated by Gapao. `[PokeBeach: Surging Sparks announcement; TCGPlayer: SV8-153]`
- **Drag Off mechanic** — Braviary's Drag Off attack (2 Colorless) forces one of the opponent's Benched Pokemon into the Active Spot before dealing 40 damage — a disruption mechanic with TCG history dating to Dark Machoke from the Team Rocket expansion. The SV8 printing has not appeared in major competitive decklists on Limitless TCG, placing it in utility/collection territory rather than meta-play. `[Limitless TCG: SSP/153; Pokegym Rulings Compendium: Drag Off]`
- **Regional form** — A Hisuian form of Braviary was introduced in Pokemon Legends: Arceus (2022) as a Psychic/Flying type with a redesigned silhouette featuring a large white facial disc. It received TCG representation in Astral Radiance (Sword and Shield series, no. 132), making Braviary one of the more multiply-represented regional-form Pokemon across TCG print runs. `[Bulbapedia: Braviary, via WebSearch snippet; Pokemon TCG Card Database: Astral Radiance 132]`

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Chansey is an original-151 Pokemon (National Pokedex no. 113) introduced in Generation I. It gained a pre-evolution (Happiny, Generation IV) and an evolution (Blissey, Generation II), making the Happiny-Chansey-Blissey line a cross-generational family built around the egg-and-nurture theme. The Chansey line has more held items canonically associated with it than any other Pokemon: Lucky Egg, Lucky Punch, Lucky Incense, and Oval Stone. `[Bulbapedia: Chansey, WebSearch snippet]`
- **Flavor text** — "Not only are these Pokemon fast runners, they're also few in number, so anyone who finds one must be lucky indeed." — The name Chansey derives from the Japanese Lucky (ra-kki); rarity and fortune are core to the species identity. `[PokemonTCG.io API]`
- **Sentiment** — Chansey is synonymous with Nurse Joy's Pokemon Centers across Kanto, Johto, Hoenn, Sinnoh, and the Orange Islands in the anime. The pairing of Nurse Joy and Chansey is one of the franchise's most consistent visual shorthand markers for healing and recovery. In later anime series, Nurse Joy's Chansey evolved into Blissey as the franchise moved forward. `[Bulbapedia: Nurse Joy's Chansey, WebSearch snippet]`
- **Mechanical** — Healing Pirouette (Colorless) heals 20 damage from each of your Pokemon — board-wide heal consistent with the Chansey/Blissey TCG identity as a support attacker. Sympathetic Slap does nothing if the target already has damage counters — an unusual conditional mechanic that punishes the opponent for fielding already-damaged attackers. `[PokemonTCG.io API]`

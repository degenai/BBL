## Trivia

- **Set context** — Tapu Lele is one of four Alolan guardian deities introduced in Pokemon Sun and Moon, each protecting a different island: Tapu Koko (Melemele), Tapu Lele (Akala), Tapu Bulu (Ula'ula), Tapu Fini (Poni). Each guardian deity triggers a terrain Ability on entry — Tapu Lele's Psychic Surge sets Psychic Terrain, boosting Psychic-type moves. Island kahunas are chosen by their island's respective guardian. `[Bulbapedia: Guardian deities, WebSearch snippet]`
- **Flavor text** — "As it flutters about, it scatters its strangely glowing scales. Touching them is said to restore good health on the spot." Tapu Lele's scales are healing in lore, but the deity is characterized as capricious and indifferent to the suffering it may incidentally cause — a healing agent with no obligation to be benevolent. `[PokemonTCG.io API]`
- **Design** — Illustrated by Mizue, printed as a Holo Rare. The holographic treatment is thematically apt — the scatter-and-sparkle foiling echoes the scale-scattering mechanic described in the flavor text. `[PokemonTCG.io API]`
- **Mechanical** — The Charmed Charm Ability inflicts Confusion on the opponent's Active Pokemon whenever a Fairy Charm Tool is attached from hand — part of the Fairy Charm tool cycle specific to the Sun and Moon era. Attack Magical Shot costs FairyColorlessColorless for 70 damage. `[PokemonTCG.io API]`

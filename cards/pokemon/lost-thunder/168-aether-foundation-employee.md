## Trivia

- **Set context** — Lost Thunder (SM8) released November 2, 2018 and was, at the time of release, the largest Pokemon TCG set ever printed — 214 cards base, 236 including secret rares. It combined three Japanese expansions (SM7a Thunderclap Spark, SM7b Fairy Rise, SM8 Super-Burst Impact). `[PokeBeach: "Lost Thunder To Be Largest Set in TCG History"]`
- **Lore context** — The Aether Foundation presents publicly as a Pokemon conservation and rehabilitation organization headquartered on the artificial floating island Aether Paradise in Alola. In Sun and Moon's story, President Lusamine uses the Foundation's resources to open Ultra Wormholes and summon Nihilego; most rank-and-file employees depicted on this card are unaware of the conspiracy. Branch Chief Faba operates as institutional enforcer. `[Serebii.net: Aether Foundation, Villains Wiki: Aether Foundation]`
- **Design** — Artist credited as 'take' (all lowercase) on PokemonTCG.io — a recurring TCG illustrator known for clean human-figure trainer cards throughout the Sun and Moon series. No flavor text on this card. `[PokemonTCG.io API]`
- **Mechanical** — The Supporter effect — putting 3 Alolan-named Pokemon from the discard pile into hand — mechanically mirrors the Foundation's stated mission of recovering Pokemon. The Alolan restriction makes this a situational recovery engine in Alolan-heavy Sun and Moon era decks. `[PokemonTCG.io API]`

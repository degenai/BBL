## Trivia

- **Set context** — Alolan Dugtrio is a Ground/Steel dual-type regional variant of Kanto Dugtrio (Ground only), introduced in Pokemon Sun and Moon. The golden hair strands are composed of thin steel filaments — described in Pokedex lore as heavy, slow-growing sensors that detect underground vibrations from long distances. The hair is widely theorized to be inspired by Pele's hair, the thin volcanic glass formed near Hawaiian lava flows. `[Bulbapedia: Dugtrio, WebSearch snippet from pokemondb.net]`
- **Flavor text** — "These Pokemon are cherished in the Alola region, where they are thought to be feminine deities of the land incarnate." — Alolan lore holds that taking Alolan Dugtrio's hair brings bad luck, and people bow in respect when they encounter one. This parallels the Hawaiian cultural figure of Pele, goddess of volcanoes, and the concept of Pele's Curse (misfortune for those who remove volcanic material from Hawai'i). `[PokemonTCG.io API]`
- **Design** — Illustrated by Yuka Morii using her signature clay sculpting technique: she hand-builds Pokemon from polymer clay, photographs them against small handmade sets, and submits the photographs as card art. Morii has produced over 200 TCG cards in this style since her debut in Neo Discovery (2001). The tactile, earthy quality of the clay medium suits Dugtrio's underground, terrain-rooted identity. `[Bulbapedia: Yuka Morii, WebSearch snippet from hobbylark.com]`

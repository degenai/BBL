## Trivia

- **Set context / mascot** — Lugia (National Pokedex no. 249, Generation II) is the version mascot of Pokemon Silver Version and its remake Pokemon SoulSilver, appearing on both box arts. It and Ho-Oh were the first legendary Pokemon to serve as version mascots. `[Bulbapedia: Lugia, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Media debut preceded game debut** — Lugia was created by writer Takeshi Shudo for Pokemon the Movie 2000: The Power of One (July 1999 in Japan), before its game appearance in Pokemon Gold & Silver (November 1999 in Japan). It was absent from the 1997 Space World demo of Gold & Silver. `[Bulbapedia: Lugia, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Lore: Tower Duo** — Lugia is one half of the Tower Duo alongside Ho-Oh. Both formerly resided in Ecruteak City's towers. After the Brass Tower burned, Lugia fled in despair and settled in the Whirl Islands — a location legend says Lugia split into four islands using lightning. `[Bulbapedia: Tower duo, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Flavor text** — "It is said to be the guardian of the seas. It is rumored to have been seen on the night of a storm." `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh4-132]`
- **Mechanical note** — Wind Pressure (4 Colorless, 250 damage) is among the highest-damage attacks on a Basic holo in Vivid Voltage, but fails entirely if the opponent has 5 or fewer cards in hand. This creates a hand-pressure subgame — opponents must maintain a large hand to suppress Lugia's threat. `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh4-132 oracle text]`

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Cramorant appears in Sword & Shield — Vivid Voltage (2020) as card no. 040/185, an Uncommon. It is National Pokedex no. 845, a Flying/Water-type introduced in Generation VIII (Galar). `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh4-40]`
- **Gulp Missile ability** — Cramorant's signature ability Gulp Missile activates after using Surf or Dive in battle. If its HP is above half, it returns with an Arrokuda in its mouth (Gulping Form); below half HP, it returns with a Pikachu (Gorging Form). If hit while in either form, it spits the catch at the attacker — Gulping Form lowers the attacker's Defense by 1 stage, Gorging Form paralyzes the attacker. The ability is exclusive to Cramorant. `[WebSearch: Bulbapedia: Gulp Missile]` `[WebSearch: pokemonblog.com Gulp Missile 2019]`
- **Flavor text** — `"Cramorant's gluttony led it to try to swallow an Arrokuda whole, which in turn led to Cramorant getting an Arrokuda stuck in its throat."` `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh4-40]`
- **Card mechanic mirrors ability** — This card's attack Continuous Gulp Missile (60x) requires discarding Arrokuda from your Bench, translating the in-game Gulp Missile ability into TCG mechanics. The card explicitly uses Arrokuda as ammunition, consistent with the Gulping Form mechanic. `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh4-40]`
- **Community resonance** — Cramorant is widely considered one of the most meme-friendly Gen VIII designs due to the Gulp Missile mechanic producing a Pikachu from its mouth at low HP. The image of Cramorant accidentally inhaling Pikachu became a popular piece of official and fanmade art. Sentiment: this design is broadly loved as comedy-first Pokemon design. `[WebSearch: Pokebeach 2019 Cramorant reveal]`

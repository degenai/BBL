## Trivia

- **Set context** — This card is from Sword & Shield — Vivid Voltage (set code swsh4), released 2020-11-13. Magcargo appears as an Uncommon (no. 028/185) in the set's Fire-type slot. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-28]`

- **Flavor text** — "Its body is as hot as lava and is always billowing. Flames will occasionally burst from its shell." Illustrated by Masakazu Fukuda. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-28]`

- **Design** — Magcargo (National Pokedex no. 219) is a dual Fire/Rock-type introduced in Generation II (Gold/Silver), evolving from Slugma at level 38. Its design is based on a gastropod mollusk with a shell made of hardened, brittle magma that glows from internal heat and can shatter on contact — but Magcargo can regenerate by submerging in lava. Multiple Pokedex entries across the series (notably Pokemon Sapphire) state Magcargo's body temperature is approximately 18,000 degrees Fahrenheit (10,000 degrees Celsius) — roughly twice the surface temperature of the sun (approximately 9,940 F / 5,504 C). This temperature claim has become one of the more notorious Pokedex facts, recurring as community meme and mock-scientific discussion for over two decades. `[WebSearch: Magcargo Pokedex temperature entries, GameFAQs/Quora threads; WebSearch: Bulbapedia Magcargo entry via snippet]`

- **Mechanical** — Stage 1 Fire-type, 130 HP. Heat Blast (3 energy: Fire + 2 Colorless) deals 60 damage. Bright Flame (4 energy: 2 Fire + 2 Colorless) deals 180 damage but requires discarding 2 Energy from Magcargo — a high ceiling attack with a significant resource cost. Water weakness x2 (consistent with Fire/Rock dual typing). Regulation Mark D (Sword & Shield Standard era). `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-28]`

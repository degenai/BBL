## Trivia

- **Set context** — Genesect appears in Sword & Shield — Vivid Voltage (2020) as card no. 016/185, a Holo Rare. It is the only Bug-type Mythical Pokemon (excluding Arceus holding an Insect Plate). `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh4-16]` `[WebSearch: facts.net Genesect]`
- **Lore: Team Plasma's crime** — Genesect existed 300 million years ago and went extinct. Team Plasma scientist Dudley revived it from a fossil and installed the high-tech cannon on its back, with the goal of creating the most powerful Pokemon ever. N had the project shut down because he believed modifying Pokemon with science would diminish them — Dudley continued covertly out of spite for N's rejection. The flavor text on this card states the full premise verbatim. `[WebSearch: Genesect Team Plasma lore, via Bulbapedia snippet]` `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh4-16]`
- **Flavor text** — `"This Pokémon existed 300 million years ago. Team Plasma altered it and attached a cannon to its back."` `[PokemonTCG.io: swsh4-16]`
- **Drive forms** — Genesect has four alternate forms determined by the Drive item it holds: Shock Drive (Electric), Burn Drive (Fire), Chill Drive (Ice), and Douse Drive (Water). Each changes the type of its Techno Blast attack. This card's oracle text includes Techno Blast at 120 damage with a one-turn cooldown. `[WebSearch: Genesect Drive forms, Bulbapedia snippet]`
- **Movie tie-in** — Genesect starred in *Pokemon the Movie: Genesect and the Legend Awakened* (2013, Japanese release; 2014 US). The plot involves five Genesect (led by a Shiny Red Genesect) reclaiming territory that no longer exists after 300 million years. Mewtwo (a different individual from the original films, with a female voice, later identified as Mega Mewtwo Y) confronts them. `[Wikipedia: Pokemon the Movie: Genesect and the Legend Awakened]`

## Trivia

- **Design origin** — Sableye (National Pokedex no. 302) is a Dark/Ghost-type introduced in Pokemon Ruby and Sapphire (2002, Generation III). Its design is widely credited as drawing on the Kelly-Hopkinsville encounter of August 21, 1955, a reported paranormal event in Kelly, Kentucky, in which a farm family claimed to encounter small goblin-like entities with large shining eyes, fan-shaped ears, small legs, and sharply taloned hands. The Kelly-Hopkinsville case has largely faded from American cultural memory but remained popular in Japan, where Western cryptids and UFO lore are common source material for anime and games. `[Screen Rant: "An Unassuming Ghost Pokemon Has Creepy, Real-Life Origins," via WebSearch snippet; Wikipedia: Kelly-Hopkinsville encounter, via WebSearch snippet]`

- **Gem eyes** — Sableye's gemstone eyes are not purely aesthetic: in-game lore establishes that Sableye subsists on a diet of gemstone crystals, which crystallize internally and eventually surface as its eyes. The chest and back gems visible in official art follow the same lore. Artist KEIICHIRO ITO renders this card's eyes with two visibly distinct gem colors (one reddish, one bluish), in keeping with the two-toned design used across most official Sableye art. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-105; WebSearch: Sableye gem-eye design, via WebSearch snippet]`

- **Flavor text** — "This Pokemon is feared. When its gemstone eyes begin to glow with a sinister shine, it's believed that Sableye will steal people's spirits away." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-105]`

- **No weakness history** — From its Generation III introduction through Generation V, Sableye (along with Spiritomb) was one of only two Pokemon with no type weaknesses at all — the Dark/Ghost typing canceled every type combination that existed through Gen V. The introduction of Fairy type in Generation VI (Pokemon X and Y, 2013) gave both Pokemon a weakness for the first time. `[WebSearch: Sableye no weakness until Fairy type, via WebSearch snippet]`

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Vivid Voltage (SWSH4) released November 13, 2020 — the fourth Sword & Shield Series expansion, based on Japan's S4 "Electrifying Tackle" set. The full set contains 185 cards plus 18 secret rares; Ferroseed is no. 123/185, a Common Grass-type in a set dominated by Electric-type headliners and Pikachu V variants. `[Bulbapedia: Vivid Voltage (TCG), via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design** — Ferroseed (National Pokedex no. 597) debuted in Generation V (Pokemon Black and White, 2010). Its Grass/Steel typing is one of the franchise's most defensively unusual combinations: Steel alone provides 9 resistances and a Poison immunity. Ferroseed's sole ability is Iron Barbs — inflicting recoil damage on opponents who make contact — a defensive posture consistent with its Pokedex-described behavior of absorbing iron from cave walls to generate its spines. `[PokeAPI /pokemon-species/597; Smogon: Ferroseed, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Flavor text** — `"It defends itself by launching spikes, but its aim isn't very good at first. Only after a lot of practice will it improve."` Pokedex lore across Black/White describes Ferroseed clinging to cave ceilings and absorbing mineral iron from rock — consistent with the rocky canyon setting in this card's art. `[Card frontmatter; PokeAPI /pokemon-species/ferroseed (White version: 'They stick their spikes into cave walls and absorb the minerals they find in the rock.')]`
- **Mechanical** — Ferrothorn, Ferroseed's evolution, is a perennial VGC tournament staple: 9 resistances, Leech Seed + Leftovers recovery, and near-zero Speed that becomes an asset under Trick Room. Smogon data shows Leech Seed (89%), Protect (88%), Body Press (74%) as its dominant competitive moves. On this TCG card, the single attack "Reaction" (10 Metal damage, switch with a Benched Pokemon) mirrors the competitive pivot role: minimal damage output, maximum repositioning value. `[Smogon: Ferrothorn; Pikalytics VGC 2022, via WebSearch snippets; card frontmatter oracle_text]`

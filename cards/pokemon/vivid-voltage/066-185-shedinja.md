## Trivia

- **Design origin** — Shedinja (National Pokedex no. 292) is a Bug/Ghost-type introduced in Pokemon Ruby and Sapphire (2002, Generation III). Its design draws on the Japanese ninjutsu concept of 空蝉 (utsusemi), a decoy technique named after the shed husk of a cicada — a ninja's trick of using a replica shell to escape. The golden halo likely references guardian angel iconography, another layer on the hollow-but-sacred visual concept. `[Wikipedia: Shedinja, via WebSearch snippet; PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-66]`

- **Unique mechanics** — Shedinja has a permanent HP stat of 1 at every level in the video games — a hardcoded exception in the games' programming. Its signature ability Wonder Guard makes it immune to all non-super-effective moves (only Fire, Flying, Rock, Ghost, and Dark can hit it). The Vivid Voltage TCG card reinterprets this as the Ability "Shell Survival": Shedinja can enter play only via Ninjask's "Cast-Off Shell" Ability and cannot be set face-down as a starting Active or Bench Pokemon. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-66]`

- **Flavor text** — "A most peculiar Pokemon that somehow appears in a Poke Ball when a Nincada evolves." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-66]`

- **Art Easter egg** — This Vivid Voltage printing (illustrated by Tomokazu Komiya) contains a detail noticed by fans in November 2020: a shadowy figure with X-eyes appears in the background, apparently deceased — a direct visual callback to the Shedinja Pokedex lore that it steals the souls of anyone who peers into the hole in its back. The discovery was publicized by PokeGuardian's LunarLugia during a live Twitch pack opening and covered by ComicBook.com and Dexerto. `[ComicBook.com: "Pokemon Card Contains Disturbing Easter Egg," Nov 2020, via WebSearch snippet; Dexerto: "Pokemon community discovers disturbing easter egg in Vivid Voltage card," via WebSearch snippet]`

- **Set context** — Vivid Voltage (SWSH4) released November 13, 2020 as the fourth Sword and Shield series expansion. It introduced the Amazing Rare card type (Legendary and Mythical Pokemon with rainbow-extending foil). Shedinja appears here as a standard Rare. The set prints 185 cards with 18 secret rares (203 total). `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-66]`

## Connections

- [[013-185-nincada]] — Branching-evolution-line 1:1 mirror, same set (Vivid Voltage), same canonical Game Freak-coded mechanic. Nincada is the only Pokemon in the franchise whose evolution produces two species simultaneously: at level 20, Nincada evolves into Ninjask while the shed empty exoskeleton becomes Shedinja (provided the player has an empty party slot AND a Poke Ball in their bag — the husk is placed into the empty ball automatically by the game). The mechanic has been hardcoded since Generation III (Ruby and Sapphire, 2002) and remains unique to this line in the franchise. Both VIV prints render the canon explicitly: this VIV-066 Shedinja's printed flavor text states *"A most peculiar Pokemon that somehow appears in a Poke Ball when a Nincada evolves,"* and the VIV-013 Nincada's Trivia synthesis states *"Shedinja IS the cocoon Nincada left."* The TCG rules layer mechanizes the lore directly: this card's oracle text *requires* Ninjask's Cast-Off Shell Ability to enter play, making the evolution-leaves-empty-husk canon a literal play-restriction at the card-text level.

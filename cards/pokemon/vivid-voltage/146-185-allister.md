## Trivia

- **Set context** — Vivid Voltage (SWSH4) is the fourth main Sword & Shield Series expansion, released November 13, 2020 — 185 numbered cards plus 18 secret rares (203 total). Signature pulls are the Rainbow Pikachu VMAX (no. 188/185) and the debut of the Amazing Rare mechanic. SWSH4 is the third SWSH-era set to feature Galar Gym Leaders as Supporter cards, with Allister, Bea, and Opal anchoring the trainer roster. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4]` `[Pokemon.com: Vivid Voltage set page, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Character — Stow-on-Side Gym Leader** — Allister is the Ghost-type Gym Leader of Stow-on-Side Stadium in Galar, appearing in Pokemon Shield's major Gym track only (in Sword, Bea takes the Stow-on-Side slot with a Fighting-type Gym). Important caveat for the "bypassable" framing: version-exclusivity does NOT make Allister's Gym optional. Per Bulbapedia, the eight major-division Gyms (whichever version-pair they are) must be challenged in fixed order to qualify for the Champion Cup. The Galar League maintains a separate Minor League of off-type Gyms that exist in-world but sit outside the mandatory Gym Challenge — Allister's Stow-on-Side Gym is in the **major** track in Shield, not the minor track. `[Bulbapedia: Galar League, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Bulbapedia: Allister, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Character — shy-introvert-with-power archetype** — Allister is canonically a young Trainer who took the Ghost-type Gym Leader mantle at an unusually early age, and who hides his face with a black-and-white mask at all times. Bulbapedia notes he is never seen unmasked in public and reportedly keeps roughly one hundred identical spare masks for redundancy. The Pokemon.com official character page leans on the same framing of a small, soft-spoken Trainer commanding Ghost-type Pokemon at the gym-leader tier. His Shield gym team caps at a Gigantamax Gengar (level 36), supported by Mimikyu, Cursola, and Yamask. `[Bulbapedia: Allister, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Pokemon.com: Ghost-type Gym Leader Allister, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Galar Gym Challenge framing** — The Galar League is formatted as an annual sporting competition rather than the traditional gauntlet-plus-Elite-Four format: Trainers need a letter of endorsement to enter, must clear eight major-division Gyms in fixed order, and the closing tournament is the Champion Cup at Wyndon Stadium (no Elite Four; Champion Cup replaces it). The League is run by Chairman Rose of the Macro Cosmos conglomerate, who made Galar Gym battles world-famous by integrating the Dynamax phenomenon into stadium play. This is Pokemon canon's most explicitly corporate-sponsored, mass-spectator framing of competitive battling — Gym Leaders as celebrity athletes in a televised league. `[Bulbapedia: Galar League, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design** — Art credit on this print is `take` per PokemonTCG.io's authoritative artist field for swsh4-146 (a single-word pen name). Note the same Allister character has multiple printings in Vivid Voltage — Full Art (no. 179/185) and Rainbow Rare (no. 192/185) — and those alternate-rarity prints are illustrated by different artists; this regular Supporter print (146/185) is specifically `take`. Released November 13, 2020; no later set has reprinted Allister in the main series as of the current data snapshot. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-146 artist field]` `[PokemonTCG.io API: name:Allister cross-print query]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — As a Supporter, Allister reads "Draw 3 cards, then discard up to 3 cards from your hand (minimum 1)." Pojo's Vivid Voltage review flagged this as niche-at-best — the contemporaneous SWSH meta already had stronger draw-supporters (Professor's Research drew 7 with full hand discard; Hop drew 3 cleanly with no discard cost), and discard-from-hand utility was better served by item cards like Quick Ball. Reviewer's framing: Allister "burns your Supporter slot only to let you draw three cards," which rarely justified the slot. Market reflects the niche standing: normal print ~$0.05, reverse-holo ~$0.25 (TCGplayer snapshot 2026-05-12). `[Pojo.com: Allister Vivid Voltage review, via WebSearch snippet]` `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-146 tcgplayer]`

### Related cards
- Bea (Vivid Voltage or Rebel Clash) — Sword-exclusive counterpart; Fighting-type Gym Leader who takes the Stow-on-Side major-division slot in Pokemon Sword. The version-pair foil; any Galar Gym Challenge bundle needs both to read as a paired set.
- Opal (Vivid Voltage) — Fairy-type Gym Leader of Ballonlea (Shield-side major Gym). The other Vivid Voltage Galar Gym Leader Supporter; rounds out the SWSH4 trainer roster.
- Allister Full Art (Vivid Voltage, no. 179/185) — Full-art rarity print of the same character/effect, different artist. Same card-as-game-piece, different art object — relevant for completionist Allister displays.
- Allister Rainbow Rare (Vivid Voltage, no. 192/185) — Secret Rare hyper-rainbow finish; the chase variant of this card in SWSH4.
- Gengar (Vivid Voltage) — Allister's signature Gigantamax Pokemon in his Shield gym team. Any narrative bundle leaning on Allister's character should pair-check Gengar availability in the same set.
- Mimikyu (Vivid Voltage) — Member of Allister's Stow-on-Side gym team; Ghost-type with the masked / shy-creature archetype that mirrors Allister's own character design.

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Charmander (no. 023/185) is a Basic Common from Vivid Voltage (SWSH4), released November 13, 2020. It occupies collector number 023 in a consecutive three-card block with Charmeleon (024) and Charizard (025), the corpus's strongest within-set evolutionary triptych cohort. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-23; WebSearch: Vivid Voltage set details]`
- **Flavor text** — "It has a preference for hot things. When it rains, steam is said to spout from the tip of its tail." The Sword & Shield-era Pokedex framing of Charmander emphasizes the tail-flame's heat-register. The earlier Base Set framing (also in corpus on Base Set Unlimited no. 46) makes the life-force lethality explicit: "The flame on its tail indicates Charmander's life force. If it is healthy, the flame burns brightly." Both phrasings reference the same species feature; the Base Set version makes the fatality stakes legible, the Vivid Voltage version foregrounds heat. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-23 flavorText; [[charizard-line]] for cross-era flavor comparison]`
- **Design** — Illustrated by MAHOU. The art sets Charmander against a warm red-orange background with stylized pink and red floral elements, an aesthetic choice distinctive among Charmander prints in the corpus, which otherwise use plainer or outdoor field backgrounds. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-23 artist field]`
- **Species lore** — Charmander is National Pokedex no. 004, one of three first-partner Pokemon offered by Professor Oak in Pokemon Red and Blue (Game Freak / Creatures Inc. / Nintendo, 1996), alongside Bulbasaur (no. 001) and Squirtle (no. 007). The Charmander line is the only original Kanto starter trio that gains a second type on final evolution (Fire/Flying on Charizard), distinguishing it from the pure-Water Squirtle line and the Grass/Poison Bulbasaur line. `[WebSearch: pokemondb.net/pokedex/charmander; [[charizard-line]]]`

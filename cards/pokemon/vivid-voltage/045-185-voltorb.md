## Trivia

- **Set context** — Vivid Voltage (SWSH4) released November 13, 2020 as the fourth Sword & Shield Series expansion. The 185-card printed set introduced Amazing Rare cards — Legendary and Mythical Pokemon illustrated with vivid rainbow foil backgrounds extending past the card frame. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4]`

- **Species design** — Voltorb is National Pokedex no. 100. Its placement is widely cited as intentional: in Japanese folklore, tsukumogami are household tools that gain sentience on their 100th birthday. Voltorb's Pokedex entries describe it as first discovered when Poke Balls were invented, positioning it as a Poke Ball that achieved tsukumogami-like awakening. The species number anchors the entire design concept. `[Dr. Lava via WebSearch snippet; ScreenRant: "Why Voltorb Is Number 100"]`

- **Flavor text** — "Usually found in power plants. Easily mistaken for a Poke Ball, it has zapped many people." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-45]`

- **Design history** — Art on this printing by Sekio. The species has been criticized since Generation I for a design that is, stripped of eyes, indistinguishable from a Poke Ball — but the tsukumogami reading reframes that minimalism as conceptually load-bearing rather than lazy. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-45; WebSearch snippet: ScreenRant]`

- **Mechanical note** — Basic Lightning-type, 60 HP, Fighting-weak (x2). Single attack: Electro Ball (2 Lightning Energy, 40 damage). Common rarity, Regulation Mark D. Evolves into Electrode. No notable competitive role in the Sword & Shield format. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-45]`

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Mudbray (no. 096/185) is a Basic Common from Vivid Voltage (SWSH4), released November 13, 2020. It is National Pokedex no. 749, a Ground-type Pokemon introduced in Generation VII (Pokemon Sun and Moon, Game Freak / Nintendo, 2016). Mudbray evolves into Mudsdale (no. 750), classified as the Draft Horse Pokemon. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-96; WebSearch: pokemondb.net/pokedex/mudbray]`
- **Flavor text** — "Loads weighing up to 50 times as much as its own body weight pose no issue for this Pokemon. It's skilled at making use of mud." The 50x-bodyweight draft load is the species' defining labor statistic, foregrounding the line's explicit beast-of-burden design — among the most direct labor-register flavor texts in the corpus. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-96 flavorText]`
- **Species lore** — Mudbray and Mudsdale were once found worldwide but were hunted to near-extinction outside Alola, where the species survives in the wild. In Alola, Mudsdale functions as a Ride Pokemon (Mudsdale Gallop) for traversal across rocky terrain; Mudsdale's gallop can gouge holes in asphalt, and the species is forbidden from running on some Alolan public roads. Mudsdale's high-quality mud also has regional use in pottery. This concentration of the species in a single island-chain region after mainland extinction is an unusual lore detail among Pokemon species. `[WebSearch: bulbapedia.bulbagarden.net Mudbray via WebSearch snippet; WebSearch: bogleech.com Mudbray and Mudsdale analysis; WebSearch: pokemondb.net/pokedex/mudsdale]`
- **Mechanical** — Mudbray's two attacks — Ram (Colorless, 10) and Rear Kick (FightingFightingColorless, 60) — function as a basic stepping-stone toward a Mudsdale line play. No standalone competitive application; the card's presence in a BBL bundle is thematic (labor, draft-animal, beast-of-burden register) rather than mechanical. `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh4-96 oracle_text]`

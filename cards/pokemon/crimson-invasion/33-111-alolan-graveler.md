## Trivia

- **Set context** — Alolan Graveler appears as card no. 33/111 (Uncommon) in Sun and Moon: Crimson Invasion (released November 3, 2017), the fourth main Sun and Moon expansion. The full Alolan Geodude evolutionary line runs through cards 32–34 in the same set, with Alolan Golem-GX occupying the adjacent slot at no. 34/111. `[PokemonTCG.io API; pokellector.com Crimson Invasion set list via WebSearch]`
- **Design** — The Alolan Geodude line's type shift from Rock/Ground to Rock/Electric is explained in-game by the Alolan diet of dravite, a real tourmaline-group mineral. Consuming it causes crystals to form on the body and generates electrical charge. The design appears to reference a thunderegg (a volcanic geode with a crystalline interior), fitting Alola's volcanic island ecology. The magnetic iron sand concentrated above the eyes — creating the characteristic thick brow — is a visual tie to the line's electromagnetic properties. `[Bulbapedia: Graveler (Pokemon) via WebSearch; pokewalls.wordpress.com via WebSearch]`
- **Flavor text** — "Its preferred food is dravite. After it has eaten this mineral, crystals form inside the Pokemon, rising to the surface of part of its body." `[PokemonTCG.io API flavor_text field]`
- **Mechanical** — The card carries Self-Destruct (LightningLightningColorless) at 100 damage with 100 self-damage — a high-risk finisher. The attack mirrors the in-game move pattern for the Geodude line, which has carried Self-Destruct since Generation I. Art by kirisAki. `[PokemonTCG.io API (sm4-33)]`

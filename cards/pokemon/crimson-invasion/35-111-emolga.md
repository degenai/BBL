## Trivia

- **Design** — Emolga (National Pokedex no. 587, "Sky Squirrel Pokemon") is based on the Japanese dwarf flying squirrel (momonga). The name likely combines 衣紋 emon (Japanese for drapery/clothing, referencing the patagial membrane) with momonga. The cape-like wing membrane that functions as both a glider and electrical scatter device is the central design conceit — the flavor text on this card makes both functions explicit. `[Bulbapedia: Emolga via WebSearch; pokemondb.net via WebSearch]`
- **Artist** — Art by Kagemaru Himeno, one of the TCG's most prolific contributors with over 635 illustrated cards. Himeno has been active since the Jungle expansion (1999) and is known for watercolor technique with vivid, atmospherically active backgrounds. The stormy electric sky composition on this card reflects her documented preference for environment-integrated poses. `[Bulbapedia: Kagemaru Himeno via WebSearch; artofpkm.com via WebSearch]`
- **Flavor text** — "It glides using its cape-like membrane. Electrical energy scatters from it, shocking its friends and foes alike." `[PokemonTCG.io API flavor_text field (sm4-35)]`
- **Mechanical** — Volt Switch (Lightning, 30 damage) lets the player switch Emolga for a Benched Lightning Pokemon — a pivot mechanic that mirrors the in-game Volt Switch move designed for cycling fast attackers in and out safely. `[PokemonTCG.io API (sm4-35)]`

## Trivia

- **Design — Nukekubi and red coral** — Misdreavus (National Pokedex no. 200) is based on the Nukekubi, a Japanese yokai whose head detaches from the body at night to terrorize victims with screaming. The red bead necklace is the defining design element: in-lore the orbs are sensory organs that absorb fear and convert it to nourishment. The beads also visually resemble red coral (Polish: koral), which has historical connections to witchcraft in Eastern European folklore — connecting the design to multiple supernatural traditions simultaneously. `[Bulbapedia: Misdreavus via WebSearch; pokecommunity.com Misdreavus origins thread via WebSearch]`
- **Set context** — Misdreavus was the sole Ghost-type introduced in Generation II (Gold and Silver, 1999), added to give the Ghost type a non-Poison dual-type representative. It was also the only Ghost-type not found in the original Kanto Pokedex. It received an evolution — Mismagius — in Generation IV (2006). `[Bulbapedia: Misdreavus via WebSearch]`
- **Flavor text** — "It will use any means necessary to frighten people and absorb their life energy. It practices constantly to hone its skill in causing fear." `[PokemonTCG.io API flavor_text field (sm4-39)]`
- **Set note** — Crimson Invasion (November 2017), Sun and Moon series. Card no. 39/111 (Common). Single-attack Confuse Ray layout. Art by so-taro. `[PokemonTCG.io API (sm4-39)]`

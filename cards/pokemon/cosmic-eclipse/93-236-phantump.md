## Trivia

- **Design — spirit of a lost child** — Multiple Pokedex entries state that Phantump is a tree stump possessed by the spirit of a child who died while lost in a forest. The design draws from Japanese kodama (tree spirits) and potentially Greek dryad tradition. `[Bulbapedia: Phantump via WebSearch; pokemaniacal.com via WebSearch]`
- **Flavor text** — "By imitating the voice of a child, it causes people to get hopelessly lost deep in the forest. It's trying to make friends with them." The final sentence is the design's tonal knife-turn: the dangerous behavior is loneliness, not malice. `[PokemonTCG.io API flavor_text field (sm12-93)]`
- **Community resonance** — Phantump's lore is a recurring "saddest Pokemon design" citation in fan communities. The ambiguity — the creature is simultaneously a hazard AND a lonely child spirit seeking companionship — has driven consistent fan art and discussion since its Generation VI introduction (X and Y, 2013). Community sentiment on r/pokemon treats it as a benchmark for franchise dark-lore done right. `[Bulbapedia: Phantump via WebSearch; community references via WebSearch]`
- **Set note** — Cosmic Eclipse (November 2019), the final major Sun and Moon expansion before the Sword and Shield era began. Card no. 93/236 (Common). Art by miki kudo. Evolves into Trevenant via trade. `[PokemonTCG.io API (sm12-93)]`

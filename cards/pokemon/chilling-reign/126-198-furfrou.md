## Trivia

- **Design / forms mechanic** — Furfrou (National Pokedex no. 676, introduced Generation VI, Kalos) is a non-evolving poodle Pokemon whose primary design mechanic is grooming: it can be trimmed into 10 distinct forms. The canonical 10 forms are Natural, Heart, Star, Diamond, Debutante, Matron, Dandy, La Reine, Kabuki, and Pharaoh Trim. In Pokemon X and Y, grooming was performed at Friseur Furfrou on Vernal Avenue in Lumiose City for $500; the trim reverts to Natural Form after five days or if the Pokemon is deposited in a Box [Bulbapedia: Furfrou; Serebii.net XY forms; Dexerto: Furfrou forms guide].
- **Flavor text** — "Left alone, its fur will grow longer and longer, but it will only allow someone it trusts to cut it." The trust-gated grooming mechanic is built directly into the flavor: the form-change requires a bond, not just a fee. This connects to the "Find a Friend" attack name — both mechanics frame companionship as the prerequisite [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-126].
- **Forms-cycle parallel** — Furfrou's 10-form grooming system is a strong candidate for a forms-cycle node analogous to a Castform node (which similarly has multiple visually distinct forms with non-evolution triggers). Unlike Castform (weather-triggered), Furfrou's form changes are player-initiated and cosmetic-only, with no stat changes. The distinction matters for lair curation: Furfrou forms are aesthetic labor, not mechanical divergence [Bulbapedia: Furfrou; PokemonDB: Furfrou].
- **Regional availability** — Some Furfrou trims were geographically restricted in Pokemon GO: La Reine (France), Kabuki (Japan), and Pharaoh (Egypt) required region-specific Stardust costs to unlock, making Furfrou one of the few Pokemon with cosmetic forms tied to physical geography in the mobile game [Dexerto: Furfrou forms in Pokemon GO; Dot Esports: Furfrou form guide].

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Chilling Reign is the 6th Sword & Shield expansion, released June 18, 2021; 198 base-set cards (233 with secret rares), the largest SwSh set to that point. Tied to the Crown Tundra DLC, headlined by Calyrex (Ice Rider / Shadow Rider VMAX) and the Galarian Articuno / Zapdos / Moltres trio. `[Bulbapedia: Chilling Reign (TCG), via WebSearch snippet]` `[PokeBeach: "Chilling Reign To Be Largest SWSH Set So Far at 198 Cards"]`
- **Species / Pokedex** — Mareep is the "Wool Pokemon," National Pokedex no. 179, an Electric-type basic introduced in Generation II (Gold/Silver, Johto region). Evolves into Flaaffy at level 15 and Ampharos at level 30. The line's Electric typing is flavored as static charge generated by their fleece. `[Bulbapedia: Mareep (Pokemon), via WebSearch snippet]` `[PokemonDB: Mareep]`
- **Flavor text** — "Rubbing its fleece generates electricity. You'll want to pet it because it's cute, but if you use your bare hand, you'll get a painful shock." — printed flavor on the card. Continuous with Johto-era Pokedex framing: Gold reads "If static electricity builds in its body, its fleece doubles in volume. Touching it will shock you," Silver reads "Its fleece grows continually. In the summer, the fleece is fully shed, but it grows back in a week." `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh6-47]` `[Bulbapedia: Mareep Gold/Silver Pokedex, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Design / illustrator** — Art by Miki Tanaka, a Pokemon TCG illustrator since 1997 (first card: Fossil-set Slowpoke); ~238 cards across the TCG; recognizable flat, minimal-shading style with little outlined linework. `[Bulbapedia: Miki Tanaka, via WebSearch snippet]` `[Art of Pokemon: Miki Tanaka illustrator page]`
- **TCG print history** — Mareep's first TCG appearance was Neo Genesis no. 65/111 in 2000 (the Johto-introduction set for the card game). Chilling Reign 047/198 is a much later common reprint; the reverse-holo variant is the more collected version (market ~$0.30 reverse holo vs ~$0.11 normal as of last snapshot). `[TCGPlayer: Mareep Neo Genesis, via WebSearch snippet]` `[PokemonTCG.io API: swsh6-47 tcgplayer prices]`

## Connections

- [[048-198-flaaffy]] — Mareep ↔ Flaaffy same-set evolution mirror, Chilling Reign 047/048 adjacent collector numbers, the Johto Electric chain's first two stages in designer-coordinated set adjacency. Both cards are illustrated as separate setting-of-life vignettes that compose into a single seasonal-arc narrative: Mareep (Miki Tanaka) in a *sunlit spring meadow* with white wildflowers and clear daytime sky, the pre-evolution baseline of the canonical wool-Pokemon-grazing-in-pastoral-Johto register; Flaaffy (sui) in *warm autumnal forest* with falling golden-orange leaves, the mid-evolution staging where the wool has begun to thin (the species' canonical patches-of-bald cost-of-power motif). Together the two cards depict the **pastoral-to-electrified** half of the Mareep ↔ Flaaffy ↔ Ampharos evolution arc: the species' Pokedex framing as a wool-creature whose fleece both insulates and stores electrical charge, with increasing electrification visibly reducing fleece across the line (Mareep wholly woolen, Flaaffy partially bald per its CRE-048 flavor text *"It stores electricity in its fluffy fleece. If it stores up too much, it will start to go bald in those patches."*, Ampharos the final lighthouse-tail beacon). The same-set / adjacent-collector-number pairing is designer-coordinated cohort placement, not coincidence — Chilling Reign's set layout slots evolution-line members consecutively where possible, and the Mareep/Flaaffy adjacency is one of the set's clearest examples. Bundle work on Johto-pastoral-electrification, evolution-line cost-of-power, or the lighthouse-Ampharos seasonal-cycle thesis indexes the pair as the chain's first two beats.

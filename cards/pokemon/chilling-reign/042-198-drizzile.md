## Connections

- [[041-198-sobble]] — Galar Water starter evolution-line 1:1 mirror, within-set. Drizzile is the Stage-2 mid-form (National Pokedex no. 817) of the three-stage Galar Water starter line (Sobble level 16 to Drizzile; Drizzile level 35 to Inteleon), introduced in Pokemon Sword and Shield (2019). Both prints are within the Chilling Reign 041-042 adjacency, both Rapid Strike subtype, both Yuu Nishida / Mitsuhiro Arita art-style register (cool water palette, lizard body plan), with visual progression from aquatic-and-camouflaged baby (water/bubbles around partially-submerged round body, blue/teal) to laid-back-coastal-adolescent (slim upright bipedal lizard on rocky shoreline, blue-green/teal). The Stage-1-to-Stage-2 evolution-line pair is the canonical anchor; cross-print Sobbles and Drizziles can later attach to this same connection. [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-41 + swsh6-42 evolvesTo / evolvesFrom; Bulbapedia: Sobble (Pokemon); Bulbapedia: Drizzile (Pokemon)]

## Trivia

- **Design / lore** — Drizzile occupies the "sullen teenager" beat of the spy-line arc: highly intelligent but deliberately lazy, it deters intruders by laying water-trap snares rather than confronting them directly. This brooding, passive-aggressive register is a mid-evolution design choice that contrasts with both Sobble's anxious shyness and Inteleon's composed assassin confidence [Bulbapedia: Drizzile; PokéJungle: Origin of Species Sobble, Drizzile, and Inteleon].
- **Flavor text** — "Highly intelligent but also very lazy, it keeps enemies out of its territory by laying traps everywhere." The flavor text is notable for explicitly naming a character flaw (laziness) in a starter Pokemon, a relatively unusual design beat [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-42].
- **Artist** — Illustrated by Mitsuhiro Arita, one of the most prolific artists in the Pokemon TCG's history, having contributed artwork since the Base Set (1996). His pairing here with Yuu Nishida on the adjacent Sobble card creates a deliberate visual handoff across the evo pair [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-42 artist field].
- **Mechanical note** — Drizzile's single attack "Bounce" (40 damage, switch to a benched Pokemon) is a pure pivot tool — low ceiling damage, high utility for retreating damaged attackers. The Rapid Strike subtype tag ties it into the subtype synergy engine that was the set's mechanical throughline [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-42 oracle text].

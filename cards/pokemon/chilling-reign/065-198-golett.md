## Connections

- [[076-189-golett]] — Cross-print 1:1 mirror between two Unova Golett cards. Same canonical species (National Pokedex no. 622, the "Automaton Pokemon," Ghost/Ground in mainline / Psychic in the SWSH TCG era), introduced in Generation V (Pokemon Black & White, Unova, 2010). Both prints carry the same ancient-clay-construct iconography (boxy clay body, glyph markings on torso, glowing eyes, stone/cosmic setting), but with set-coded environmental palette: CRE-065 (0313 art) renders the grim subterranean ruin scene (grey-stone dungeon backdrop, teal-green glow), while DAA-076 (Ryuta Fuse art) renders the cosmic void scene (deep-purple galaxy-vortex backdrop, orange glow). The cross-print pair canonizes the Golett design's two stable iconographic registers — telluric-ancient versus cosmic-ancient — under one species identity. Mirrors the wave-18 Grimer BRS-084 / DAA-100 cross-print mirror pattern. [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-65 + swsh3-76; Bulbapedia: Golett (Pokemon); bogleech.com/pokemon/p622golett]

## Trivia

- **Art contrast with DAA-076** — This print (CRE-065, artist 0313) places Golett in a grey-stone dungeon register: rocky terrain, muted earthen palette, teal-green glowing glyph, grim ambient lighting. The Darkness Ablaze print (DAA-076, artist Ryuta Fuse) places the same species in a cosmic-void register: deep-purple galaxy backdrop, orange glowing seal, sublime night-sky lighting. The two prints form the "telluric-ancient vs. cosmic-ancient" axis for this species — grounded earth versus unmoored void. Both registers are lore-consistent: Golett's flavor text roots it in ancient clay orders, equally at home in ruins or the infinite [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-65 artist 0313; PokemonTCG.io: swsh3-76 artist Ryuta Fuse].
- **Flavor text (this print)** — "This Pokemon was created from clay. It received orders from its master many thousands of years ago, and it still follows those orders to this day." This print's flavor text emphasizes temporal obedience — the construct still executing instructions from a long-dead master — distinct from the DAA-076 flavor text, which emphasized mystery ("No one knows why, but some of them are driven to continually line up boulders") [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-65 vs swsh3-76 flavor text fields].
- **Lore foundations (wave 14 trivia, DAA-076)** — The Golem-of-Prague / Haniwa / Terracotta Army design reference triad was established in the DAA-076 trivia pass. This print does not duplicate that; both flavor texts and both art registers point to the same underlying archetype (ancient clay automaton bound by orders) but express it through environmental framing rather than lore elaboration.
- **Artist 0313** — 0313 is a Pokemon TCG artist whose work in the Sword and Shield era is identifiable by strong geometric composition and moody environmental anchoring. The dungeon setting here is consistent with 0313's tendency toward atmospheric, low-key backgrounds [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-65 artist field; Bulbapedia: Golett (Chilling Reign 65)].

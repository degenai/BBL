## Trivia

- **Design origin / name** — Mareep's name is likely a combination of "Mary" (from the nursery rhyme "Mary Had a Little Lamb") and "sheep." The name is also an anagram of "ampere," the SI unit of electric current — foreshadowing the evolution line's endpoint, Ampharos. The Japanese name Merriep combines "merry," the sheep vocalization "mē," and "sheep" [Bulbapedia: Mareep; WebSearch snippet via Bulbapedia].
- **Literary reference** — Mareep and its evolution line are widely considered a reference to Philip K. Dick's 1968 novel "Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?" (the source novel for Blade Runner). The electric sheep premise maps directly onto an Electric-type sheep Pokemon introduced alongside Generation II's more industrialized Johto aesthetic [GameFAQs: "Do Androids Dream of Mareep?" thread; Game Informer: Sableye and Mareep design origins, 2015].
- **Flavor text** — "Rubbing its fleece generates electricity. You'll want to pet it because it's cute, but if you use your bare hand, you'll get a painful shock." The static-friction electricity mechanic mirrors how real wool builds up triboelectric charge [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-47].
- **Corpus note** — This is a Reverse Holofoil print. The corpus now holds at least three Mareep/Flaaffy prints across sets, making the electric-sheep evo pair one of the more represented Johto lines in the archive.

## Trivia

- **Design / classification** — Ralts is classified as the "Feeling Pokemon" (its species name in the National Pokedex). Its design is built around the concept of empathy: the red horn-like structures on its head function as emotional antennae, sensing the moods of nearby humans and Pokemon. Its bowl-cut visor design — obscuring its eyes — is a common visual shorthand in anime and manga for shy or sensitive characters [Bulbapedia: Ralts; Pokémon Wiki: Ralts].
- **Flavor text** — "If its horns capture the warm feelings of people or Pokemon, its body warms up slightly." The horns-as-empathy-receptors mechanic is carried forward through Kirlia and into Gardevoir, which is described as capable of sensing its Trainer's emotions from across the world [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-59].
- **Dual evolution endpoint** — Kirlia (Ralts's direct evolution) branches into two final forms: Gardevoir (any gender, level 30) and Gallade (male only, via Dawn Stone). This makes the Ralts line one of the few three-stage lines with a gender-conditional fork at the second stage rather than the final. Gardevoir is Psychic/Fairy; Gallade is Psychic/Fighting — two distinct combat philosophies from the same empath ancestor [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-60 evolvesTo field; Bulbapedia: Kirlia].
- **Fan reception** — The Ralts line is consistently cited among the most popular designs from Generation III (Ruby/Sapphire, 2002). Community polls and discussions on r/pokemon and PokéCommunity forums place Gardevoir as one of the most recognizable Pokemon designs overall, a recognition that retroactively elevates Ralts and Kirlia's cultural footprint [PokéCommunity Forums: Favorite Gen 3 Pokemon threads; Nintendo Life poll].

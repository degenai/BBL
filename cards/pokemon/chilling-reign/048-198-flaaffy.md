## Trivia

- **Design lore** — Flaaffy (National Pokedex no. 180) represents a curious mid-evolution trade-off: as it stores more electricity in its wool, patches of fleece fall out, leaving skin exposed. The bald patches are a literal cost-of-power motif — the more charged it is, the less wool it has. Its skin compensates by becoming a rubber-like insulator to prevent self-discharge [Bulbapedia: Flaaffy; PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-48 flavor text].
- **Flavor text** — "It stores electricity in its fluffy fleece. If it stores up too much, it will start to go bald in those patches." This is one of the few Pokemon flavor texts that describes a visible physical consequence of the Pokemon's power increasing [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-48].
- **Evolution context** — Flaaffy evolves into Ampharos at level 30. The final form "Ampharos" derives from "ampere" (unit of electric current) + "pharos" (Greek: lighthouse) — Ampharos canonically functions as a lighthouse beacon, illuminating sea lanes with its glowing tail. The pastoral sheep-in-a-field baseline established by Mareep and Flaaffy gives way to a lighthouse keeper role at the endpoint [Bulbapedia: Ampharos; WebSearch snippet via Bulbapedia].
- **Artist** — Illustrated by sui, a recurring Pokemon TCG contributor whose work across multiple Sword and Shield sets tends toward warm, seasonal palette choices. The autumnal leaf-scatter setting for this Flaaffy is tonally consistent with sui's portfolio style [PokemonTCG.io: swsh6-48 artist field].

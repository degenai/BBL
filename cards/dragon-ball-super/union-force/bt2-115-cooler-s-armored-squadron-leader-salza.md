## Trivia

- **IP verified: Salza** — The card name directly identifies the figure as captain of Cooler's Armored Squadron. The Squadron is a three-member mercenary platoon (Salza, Dore, and Neiz) serving Cooler — Frieza's brother — in Dragon Ball Z: Cooler's Revenge, the fifth DBZ theatrical film, released in Japan July 20, 1991 `[WebSearch: Salza Cooler's Armored Squadron Dragon Ball Z Cooler's Revenge 1991]`. Salza's name is a pun on the Spanish sauce "salsa," consistent with the squad's condiment naming pattern `[WebSearch: Dragon Ball Wiki Cooler's Armored Squadron]`.
- **Wave-65 correction** — Angila (previously misattributed to this slot) is from Dragon Ball Z: Lord Slug — Movie 4, a different 1991 film featuring Lord Slug (a Namekian villain) and his separate mercenary trio. Angila is Lord Slug's right-hand man: tall, cyan-skinned, with blonde hair and a toga `[WebSearch: Angila Dragon Ball Z Lord Slug villain]`. Salza (this card) serves Cooler in Movie 5. Both films released in 1991 with named three-person villain squads — the likely source of the confusion. The correction loop is now closed.
- **Lore detail** — Salza originates from planet Coola no. 98, the same star system as Ginyu Force member Jeice, establishing a background link between Cooler's Armored Squadron and Frieza's Ginyu Force as parallel elite mercenary units serving the Cold family's space empire `[WebSearch: Salza Cooler's Armored Squadron Dragon Ball Z Cooler's Revenge 1991]`.
- **Set context** — Union Force (DBS-B02), released November 2017 by Bandai `[WebSearch: Union Force BT2 2017]`. As an uncommon card featuring a secondary film antagonist — Salza appears only in Cooler's Revenge and its sequel — this card targets the DBZ movie-continuity collector audience rather than the main series canon.

## Trivia

- **Tournament position** — Bacterian is a 21st World Martial Arts Tournament combatant: the fighter who has never bathed, brushed his teeth, or washed himself, and weaponizes the resulting stench as his primary combat tactic. His manga debut is chapter 35, "The Battle Is Set!!" (published June 10, 1986); his anime debut is episode 20, "Elimination Round" (July 9, 1986). He drew Krillin as his first-round opponent. He is one of the only TB2 fighters representing the 21st tournament era — the oldest bracket in the set. [Dragon Ball Wiki Fandom: Bacterian; Dragon Ball Official Site via WebSearch]
- **The famous match** — Bacterian's stench was so overpowering that the tournament announcer wore a gas mask to call the Krillin match. His odor tactics — bad breath, body odor, flatulence — left Krillin immobile until Goku shouted from ringside that Krillin has no nose and cannot smell. Krillin immediately recovered, dispatching Bacterian by returning the flatulence tactic against him — one of the earliest gag-resolution moments in Dragon Ball. [CBR, "Krillin vs Bacterian Stinks to High Heaven, But is the Fight Dragon Ball Fans Deserve"; Dragon Ball Wiki via WebSearch]
- **Official recognition** — Bacterian received a Weekly Character Showcase entry (no. 137) on the Dragon Ball Official Site, placed in the "Great Demon King Piccolo Arc" category — suggesting minor continuity appearances beyond his 21st tournament match, though his primary narrative identity is as Krillin's comedy first-round opponent. [Dragon Ball Official Site: Weekly Character Showcase #137 via WebSearch]
- **Mechanical note** — TB2-061 is an Uncommon Battle Card (Power 10000, Combo 5000) with [Auto]: when played, you may move 1 card from your Life to your hand. It exists in both Normal and Foil prints in TB2. The Life-to-hand utility mirrors Bacterian's role in canon: a minor character who provides real value (Krillin's nose revelation) by being beaten. [DBS card database; retailer listings via WebSearch]

## Trivia

- **Set context / Link Card** — Goten and Trunks are paired as the "Tiny Rivals" Link Card duo in TB2 (TB2-004 and TB2-007). The World Martial Arts Tournament set introduced Link Cards as a new DBS TCG mechanic — paired cards whose art and effects work together. The two children competing as rivals in the 25th World Tournament (Buu Saga) is a major comedic and narrative thread of that arc. [Official DBS Card Game: WMAT Design Concept]
- **Character lore** — Son Goten is Goku's youngest son, born nine months after the Cell Saga in Age 767. He is the youngest known half-Saiyan Super Saiyan in the series, achieving the transformation at approximately age seven — without the life-threatening crucible Gohan required. His childhood appearance is described as a near-exact replica of Goku's own child design. He never met his father until age seven. [Dragon Ball Wiki: Goten / Wikipedia: Goten]
- **Mechanical** — At $0.04 market price, TB2-004 is deep bulk. The "Tiny Rivals" framing means the card is designed to work in tandem with TB2-007 Trunks; separated from its Link partner, it loses the design intent. The lowest-priced card in the inventory batch. [TCGplayer: World Martial Arts Tournament]

## Connections

- [[son-goten]] — This print is the corpus's primary Goten-as-Junior-Division-finalist anchor, the canonical seven-year-old at the 25th World Martial Arts Tournament whose Bandai-tagged `Son Goten` register opens the Buu-Saga child-fighter arc. TB2-004 is designer-coordinated as a paired-print with TB2-007 Tiny Rivals Trunks at the print-object level — matched art-style, energy-burst color treatments, and the canonical *Tiny Rivals* subtitle that names the Junior Tournament cohort itself. The charging-fists-raised pose and dark martial-arts gi render Goten before his canonical mid-fight Super Saiyan transformation, the moment in the Junior final where the franchise's youngest-known Super Saiyan reveals himself by accident.

## Trivia

- **Set context** — The World Martial Arts Tournament set (DBS-TB02, September 2018) draws from the Majin Buu Saga as well as the Budokai arc — Babidi's ship is included because it is the arena where the Majin Buu resurrection plot begins, directly interrupting the 25th World Martial Arts Tournament. The two narrative threads run in parallel in the anime. `[WebSearch: Dragon Ball Super World Martial Arts Tournament TB2 September 2018]`
- **Canonical artifact** — Babidi's Spaceship was buried underground in the Southwest Forest on Earth, serving as a multi-level battle-arena where the Z Fighters faced progressively stronger opponents (Pui Pui on level 1, Yakon on level 2, Dabura on level 3) while descending toward the Sealed Ball containing Majin Buu. The ship was eventually destroyed by Majin Vegeta's energy blast. `[WebSearch: Babidi's Spaceship Dragon Ball Wiki Majin Buu Saga structure]`
- **Lineage note** — Babidi is the son (and, per some sources, clone) of Bibidi, the ancient wizard who originally created Majin Buu. The spaceship is an artifact of Bibidi's lineage — it may predate Babidi as an instrument of the multi-millennial Majin revival project rather than something Babidi built himself. `[WebSearch: Babidi Dragon Ball Wiki Bibidi clone origin Majin Buu creation]`
- **Artifact-as-character pattern** — As a location card with no figures depicted, Babidi's Spaceship represents the DBS TCG's artifact-as-character-extension design: an object so thoroughly identified with a character that it becomes a standalone card anchor. This parallels BT2-066 Trunks's Time Machine from Union Force — both are vessel cards using interior-architecture composition to establish place rather than depicting a named figure. `[DBS-TB02 card list; BT2-066 comparison]`

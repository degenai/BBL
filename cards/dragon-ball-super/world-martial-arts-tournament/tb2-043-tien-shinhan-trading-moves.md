## Trivia

- **Character origin** — Tien Shinhan (Japanese: Tenshinhan) is descended from the Three-Eyed People (Triclops), a near-extinct alien race that colonized Earth long ago. His third eye — the sole physical feature distinguishing him from a human — is detailed in the Daizenshuu supplemental guidebooks rather than the main manga. It provides enhanced ki tracking, allows him to see through illusions like the After-Image technique, and can fire laser beams. His design echoes Taoist/Buddhist iconography of the 'third eye' (tianmu) and mirrors Erlang Shen from Journey to the West, one of Toriyama's primary source texts. [WebSearch: Screen Rant 'Is Tien Human? Three-Eyed Race Explained' / CBR 'Why Dragon Ball Tien Has Three Eyes' / Kanzenshuu Tenshinhan alien ancestry rumor guide]
- **Mechanical design** — TB2-043 carries [Barrier] (cannot be targeted by opponent card skills) and an [Activate: Main] that — when 'Mercenary Tao, Trading Moves' is KO'd from the Battle Area — switches Tien to Active Mode, grants +5000 power, and adds [Double Strike] for the turn, provided the Leader Card is a green 'World Tournament' card. Energy cost 3[GG], power 15000, Uncommon. The mechanic requires sacrificing the Mercenary Tao card, encoding the historic Tien-vs-Tao rivalry as a game-state sacrifice. [WebSearch: Three Kings Loot TB2-043 listing]
- **Pair mechanic** — Tien and Chiaotzu form a mechanically bonded pair in this set: Best Buddy Chiaotzu (TB2-044) has an Auto that reactivates Tien Shinhan, Trading Moves when used as a combo piece — the card game directly encoding their decades-long partnership as a game mechanic. [WebSearch: TCGPlayer TB2-044 Best Buddy Chiaotzu / DBZ Exchange TB2-044]

## Connections

- [[crane-school-cohort]] — Tien Shinhan is the Crane School's canonical principal disciple, trained under Master Shen alongside Chiaotzu as a weapon rather than as a martial-arts practitioner. His defection at the 22nd Tenkaichi Budokai — refusing Shen's order to kill Goku, breaking from the apparatus-of-extraction his master built — is one of Dragon Ball's clearest *student-refuses-the-contract* moments and the moral pivot of the entire school's canonical arc. TB2-043's mechanical design canonicalizes the rivalry within the cohort directly: when 'Mercenary Tao, Trading Moves' (TB2-048) is KO'd, Tien switches to Active Mode with +5000 power and [Double Strike] — the Tien-vs-Tao rivalry encoded as a game-state sacrifice across the same 2018 booster.

## Trivia

- **Set context** — TB2-051 is from the World Martial Arts Tournament Themed Booster (DBS-TB02), released September 21, 2018. The set's yellow-color lineup is dedicated to the 21st World Martial Arts Tournament (the original Dragon Ball series tournament arc) and uses the Link Cards mechanic to pair young Goku and Jackie Chun. TB2-051's linked partner is Unyielding Victory Jackie Chun (TB2-SPR parallel). The official SR/SPR strategy page states: "We recreated the scene from the manga where Jackie Chun and Goku fought through a skill! Double Strike and Triple Attack make a devastating combo." [dbs-cardgame.com: World Martial Arts Tournament Design Concept strategy page; dbs-cardgame.com: TB02 SR/SPR Cards Showdown strategy page]
- **Mechanical profile** — Yellow Battle card, Super Rare (SR), also exists as a Special Rare (SPR), power 15,000, energy cost 3 (Yellow), Combo Power 5,000. Character tag: Son Goku: Childhood. Traits: Saiyan/World Tournament. Activate:Main (once per turn): "Choose 1 Unyielding Victory Jackie Chun in your Battle Area and switch it to Rest Mode: if your Leader Card is a yellow World Tournament, this card gains +5,000 power, Double Strike, and Triple Attack for the duration of the turn." The link-activation turns a 15,000-power SR into a 20,000-power card with both Double Strike and Triple Attack — a full three-damage-dealing combat profile that only activates when the Jackie Chun partner is in play and sacrificed for a turn. [WebSearch snippets: DBZExchange TB2-051; Big Orbit Cards TB2-051 listing]
- **Lore context — 21st World Tournament / Goku vs Jackie Chun** — The 21st World Martial Arts Tournament is the first tournament arc of the original Dragon Ball anime (episodes 14-28). Young Goku reaches the final match against Jackie Chun, who is Master Roshi in disguise — a wig and removed sunglasses, the turtle hermit's attempt to secretly enter and deliberately defeat his own students so they would never stop training. The final match ends with Jackie Chun narrowly defeating Goku (who transformed into a Great Ape after Chun blasted away the full moon) — the outcome determined by martial arts experience rather than raw power, one of the original series' defining morality-of-training moments. The TB2-051 Link mechanic literally replays this match: Goku draws power from Jackie Chun's "sacrifice" (switching to Rest Mode) to activate his own Triple Attack finish. [Wikipedia: World Martial Arts Tournament (Dragon Ball); CBR: "Dragon Ball Episode 28 Brought the Anime's First Tournament to an Unforgettable Conclusion"]
- **Son Goku cluster — childhood arc** — TB2-051 anchors the childhood/original-Dragon-Ball phase of the Son Goku character node, distinct from the Z-era adult Goku prints (BT1 Saiyan Saga) and the BT2-004 SS3 Buu-Saga print. The tail visible in the art (per Vision) is the canonical childhood-era Goku visual marker — his tail was removed by Kami (anime) or Vegeta (manga) during the Saiyan Saga, so its presence confirms this is a pre-Z depiction. [CBR: "Dragon Ball Episode 28" review; Son Goku character node (existing)]

## Trivia

- **Lore context** — The World Martial Arts Tournament Announcer is a nameless recurring character present across Dragon Ball, Dragon Ball Z, and Dragon Ball GT — one of the few non-combatant faces at nearly every major tournament in the franchise. He narrates the spectacle while the fighters receive glory: canonical service-labor, present but uncredited. `[Dragon Ball Wiki: World Tournament Announcer, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Card format** — The double-sided 'Announcer // Announcer, Referee Veteran' format is a DBS card game mechanic for cards with two states, showing the character's role escalating from announcer to veteran referee within the set's tournament framing. TB2-065 is the only card in DBS-TB02 to canonize this nameless recurring figure as a playable unit. `[dbs-cardgame.com/us-en/cardlist, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Community resonance** — Community sentiment: the Announcer is a long-running fan-favorite background character valued precisely for his longevity and anonymity — present at every major tournament since the original Dragon Ball without ever being named in canon. His TB2 card inclusion is widely noted as a 'deep cut' pull for continuity-attentive fans. `[Behind the Voice Actors: World Martial Arts Tournament Announcer, via WebSearch snippet]`
- **Structural placement** — TB2-065 is an Uncommon and among the highest-numbered cards in the set (which runs to TB2-069), placing the Announcer at the tail end of the print run — structurally appropriate for a figure who always appears at the event's close, not its opening. `[dbs-cardgame.com/us-en/product/dbs-tb02.php; bleedingcool.com TB02 checklist]`

## Trivia

- **Tournament position** — Tien Shinhan won the 22nd World Martial Arts Tournament outright, defeating Yamcha (and breaking his leg after the match), Jackie Chun (Master Roshi in disguise), and finally Goku in the finals. The victory also marked his break from the Crane School — Goku and Roshi's influence during the tournament pushed him to reject Master Shen's ruthlessness. At the 23rd tournament he reached the semi-finals before losing to Goku, whose training under Kami had created a decisive gap. [CBR, "Dragon Ball: World Martial Arts Tournaments Records of the Main Characters"; Wikipedia: Tien Shinhan via WebSearch]
- **Three-eye origin** — Tien is a descendant of the Three-Eyed People (Triclops race), confirmed in Dragon Ball Z DVD booklets and game lore. Toriyama documented in official materials: beings with a third eye are thought to be godlike with the power of true seeing; Tien's Crane School upbringing under the evil Tsuru-Sen'nin corrupted his ability to use that gift for good. The design echoes the Taoist third eye concept and the Chinese mythological figure Erlang Shen. Dragon Ball DAIMA (2024) revisited this lineage. [CBR, "What's the Deal With Tien's Third Eye?"; Game Rant, "Dragon Ball DAIMA Hints at Origin of Tien's Third Eye" via WebSearch]
- **Mechanical design** — TB2-055 is a Yellow Rare Foil Battle Card (Power 20000, cost 4YY) with [Double Strike] and [Barrier]. Its [Activate:Main] [Once per turn]: choose Unending Moves Yamcha in your Battle Area, KO it — then draw 2 cards and KO one of your opponent's resting Battle Cards. Tien's sacrifice of Yamcha for card advantage and board removal directly encodes the 22nd tournament's post-match leg-break dynamic in card-game mechanics. [DBS card database via WebSearch]
- **Pair context** — TB2-054 (Yamcha) and TB2-055 (Tien) are designed as an explicit mechanical pair. Yamcha gains +10000 power and [Critical] only when Tien is in play; Tien's most powerful effect requires Yamcha as a sacrifice. The Turtle School vs. Crane School rivals become codependent within the World Tournament themed deck — a structural inversion of their canon antagonism. [DBS card database via WebSearch]

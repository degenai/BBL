## Trivia

- **Design origin** — Chiaotzu's appearance — white skin, circular red cheek spots, small round body — is directly based on the Jiangshi, the Chinese 'hopping vampire' of Qing dynasty folkloric tradition, characterized by a pale complexion, rigid posture, and hopping movement. Akira Toriyama drew on the 1980s Hong Kong Jiangshi film cycle (particularly Mr. Vampire and Hello Dracula from Taiwan) as well as the mythic figure of Nezha from Journey to the West for this design. [WebSearch: Screen Rant 'Dragon Ball Z Chiaotzu and Chinese Vampires Explained' / CBR 'What Exactly Is Chiaotzu?']
- **Mechanical design** — TB2-044's Auto ability reads: when you combo with this card during your turn, choose up to 1 'Tien Shinhan, Trading Moves' in your Battle Area and switch it to Active Mode. At 1[G] energy cost and 1 Combo Energy, Chiaotzu is a cheap combo extender whose entire mechanical identity is reactivating Tien — encoding their canonical partnership as a game-state relationship. Color: Green, Power: 5000, Common rarity. [WebSearch: TCGPlayer TB2-044 / DBZ Exchange TB2-044]
- **Character history** — Chiaotzu and Tien Shinhan are one of Dragon Ball's earliest consistent companion pairings, established in the original Dragon Ball manga (22nd Tenkaichi Budokai arc, 1986). Despite rarely contributing meaningfully to battles after the Saiyan Saga, both attended the 28th World Martial Arts Tournament depicted in this TB02 set. Chiaotzu is also the first Z-Fighter to sacrifice himself in battle (Saiyan Saga, self-destructing against Nappa — ineffectively). [WebSearch: Dragon Ball Wiki Chiaotzu / CBR What Exactly Is Chiaotzu]

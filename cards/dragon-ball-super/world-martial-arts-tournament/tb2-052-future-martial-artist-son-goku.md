## Trivia

- **Design** — TB2-052 is a yellow-color Common Battle card with 12,000 power and Super Combo status (maximum 4 copies per deck). Its auto-ability: when comboing, if the Leader is 'World Tournament' and life is 5 or less, draw 1 card and gain +10,000 combo power for the turn. A low-life recovery mechanic that rewards playing into the Tenkaichi Budokai theme. `[WebSearch: DBZ Exchange TB2-052 product page]`
- **Set / lore context** — The card's era tag is 'World Martial Arts Tournament Saga' and character type is 'Son Goku: Childhood,' placing this depiction in the original Dragon Ball continuity around the 22nd or 23rd Tenkaichi Budokai. The title 'Future Martial Artist' signals potential rather than power already realized — Goku at this stage had not yet ascended to Super Saiyan, had no knowledge of his Saiyan heritage, and won the 23rd tournament only via ring-out technicality against Piccolo Jr. `[WebSearch: CBR '37 Years Ago Today, the Best Anime Tournament of All Time Began'; DBZ Exchange TB2-052]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — As a Super Combo card, TB2-052 functions as a deck-construction resource rather than a primary attacker. Its 12,000 power is modest but the draw-on-low-life trigger makes it a comeback mechanism in World Tournament builds. The constraint mirrors the character's lore position: young Goku as potential not yet fulfilled. `[WebSearch: DBZ Exchange TB2-052; CBR Dragon Ball 23rd Budokai article]`

## Trivia

- **Set context** — TB2-025 is from the World Martial Arts Tournament Themed Booster (DBS-TB02), released September 21, 2018 — the final set in the Over Realm block and the second themed booster. TB2 introduced the Link Cards mechanic: paired cards with correlating artworks and skills that activate only when the matching partner is in play. Begrudging Respect Vegeta's linked partner is Begrudging Respect Piccolo (TB2-027). TB2's blue-color lineup represents characters from the 25th World Tournament era (Majin Buu Saga). [dbs-cardgame.com: World Martial Arts Tournament Design Concept strategy page; cardmarket.com: World Martial Arts Tournament TB2 set entry]
- **Mechanical profile** — Blue Battle card, Uncommon, power 20,000, energy cost 4. Traits: Saiyan/World Tournament. Permanent Barrier: cannot be chosen by opponent's skills. Auto skill (when played, if Leader is World Tournament and Begrudging Respect Piccolo is in your Battle Area): opponent chooses 2 cards from their hand and places them on top of their deck in any order — a two-card hand-disruption effect that requires the Piccolo link to be active. Linked partner Piccolo reciprocally gains additional power when Vegeta is in play and can remove an opponent's energy when Vegeta is present. [WebSearch snippet: DBZExchange TB2-025 card text; community listing at Fusion Gaming]
- **Lore context — Vegeta and Piccolo, Buu Saga pre-sacrifice** — The "Begrudging Respect" subtitle references the canonical Buu Saga moment before Vegeta's final self-sacrifice against Majin Buu: Piccolo was the one who most fully understood that Vegeta's sacrifice was both selfish and selfless, and the two share a brief exchange in which Piccolo expresses tacit understanding. Canonically, Piccolo had already killed Babidi at this point, and the sight of Piccolo's triumphant sacrifice — eliminating a threat through self-destruction — is widely analyzed as the trigger that inspired Vegeta to detonate himself against Buu. The card's arms-crossed, closed-off posture (per Vision) and the "Begrudging" modifier encode Vegeta's canonical inability to openly acknowledge the depth of his respect for anyone outside the Saiyan pride frame. [CBR: "Vegeta and Piccolo Never Fought in DBZ"; Quora: "When did Piccolo earn Vegeta's respect?"; Vegeta character node `_characters/vegeta.md`]
- **Vegeta character cluster** — This card completes the final unenriched slot in the Vegeta character node's corpus roster (per `_characters/vegeta.md` wave 117 note: "One card remains with body-prose-sync work outstanding: TB2-025 Begrudging Respect Vegeta"). The Majin Buu Saga era stamp places TB2-025 alongside TB2-006 Vegeta, Power Misused as the corpus's two Buu-Saga-phase Vegeta prints. The armed-crossed restraint composition (Vision) contrasts sharply with TB2-006's coiled-aggression stance — the same Buu-Saga arc, two emotional registers: the Majin-unleashed aggressor (TB2-006) and the quietly-processing foil who is about to sacrifice himself (TB2-025). [Vegeta character node `_characters/vegeta.md`]

## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — The World Tournament Announcer (Tenkaichi Budokai Anaunsaa) has hosted every World Martial Arts Tournament in the original Dragon Ball manga through Dragon Ball GT, making him one of the longest-running recurring characters in the franchise. He is one of the few normal humans fully aware that the Z fighters are superhuman — and one of even fewer who knows Mr. Satan did not actually defeat Cell. `[WebSearch: Dragon Ball Wiki Fandom snippet on World Tournament Announcer; Behind The Voice Actors Dragon Ball Z Announcer]`
- **Design** — TB2-066 is a black-color Common Battle card (1 energy, 1,000 power, 5,000 combo). On-play ability: if the Leader is 'World Tournament,' look at the top 7 cards of the deck, choose up to 1 'World Tournament' card and add it to hand, then shuffle. This top-7 tutor makes the Announcer a consistency engine in dedicated World Tournament builds — fitting his narrative role as the person who controls information flow at every tournament. `[WebSearch: DBZ Exchange TB2-066; CoolStuffInc Announcer Ever-Curious listing]`
- **Mechanical reputation** — At 1,000 power, the Announcer is the lowest-power Battle card in this wave and cannot meaningfully attack. His black color identity also separates him from the yellow cluster of fighters in TB2. Both facts mirror his role: he is an observer, not a combatant. The tutor effect is strong in competitive World Tournament builds, making him a 4-of in decks that run a World Tournament Leader — he is more useful than his power suggests, like the lore character himself. `[WebSearch: Big Orbit Cards TB2-066; DBZ Exchange TB2-066]`

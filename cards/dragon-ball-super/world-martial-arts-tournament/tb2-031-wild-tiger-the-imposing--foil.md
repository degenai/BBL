## Trivia

- **IP refuted: not Nappa** — Vision flagged this figure as potentially Nappa (Dragon Ball) due to bald head and heavy musculature. Research confirms the depicted figure is Wild Tiger, a distinct named Earthling character — the card title "Wild Tiger, the Imposing" is the direct canonical identifier. Nappa is canonically a Saiyan elite with a goatee, Saiyan armor, and a Battle of Gods-or-earlier chronological placement; Wild Tiger is canonically a helmeted brawler human fighter from the 28th World Martial Arts Tournament (Dragon Ball Z's final arc) with no Saiyan-canonical features. [Dragon Ball Wiki: Wild Tiger; TrollAndToad product listing]
- **Character canon** — Wild Tiger (Japanese: Mō Kekko, a pun on the phrase "mō kekkō" meaning "that's enough," with his name characters meaning "ferocious blood tiger") is a muscular Earthling fighter who participated in both the 27th and 28th World Martial Arts Tournaments. At the 27th, he lost to Majin Buu in the semi-finals. At the 28th, he was defeated by Pan in the opening selection rounds, being hurled into a wall with ease. His brief appearance in Dragon Ball Z's final episodes establishes him as a gatekeeper-caliber tournament fighter, known by reputation but quickly outclassed. [Dragon Ball Wiki: Wild Tiger, via WebSearch snippet]
- **Set context** — World Martial Arts Tournament (DBS-TB02, September 21, 2018) is the second themed booster in the Dragon Ball Super Card Game, containing 125 cards across all color frames centered on tournament-era characters across the franchise's tournament arcs. The set introduces Link Cards — paired prints with correlating art that gain bonus effects when the matched card is in play. TB2-031 is a Blue Common. [DBZ Exchange product listing; bleedingcool.com WMAT checklist]
- **Card profile** — TB2-031 is a Blue Battle card, 15,000 power, cost 2, 5,000 combo power, Earthling/World Tournament special trait, Majin Buu Saga era. Skill: switch to Rest Mode and redirect an attack targeting one of your other cards to this card — a canonical bodyguard/taunt mechanic, thematically aligned with Wild Tiger's imposing stature. Available in tournament play from November 30, 2018. [WebSearch snippet via TrollAndToad; bleedingcool.com]

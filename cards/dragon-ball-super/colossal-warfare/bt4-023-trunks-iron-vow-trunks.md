## Trivia

- **Set context** — BT4-023 is a dual-sided Leader card from Colossal Warfare (DBS-B04), the fourth main booster set for the Dragon Ball Super Card Game, released July 13, 2018 — at the time the largest DBS set with 218 total cards (124 unique). The set drew heavily on the Hirudegarn Saga from DBZ: Wrath of the Dragon (1995 film), anchoring Trunks as the primary blue-energy Leader for that saga `[Cardmarket: Colossal Warfare]` `[The Games Corner release listing]`.
- **Mechanical design** — As a Leader, BT4-023 flips to Iron Vow Trunks (15,000 power) when life drops to 4 or less. The reverse side's end-of-turn ability switches an eligible blue Battle Card and one energy to Active Mode, making Iron Vow Trunks a blue-energy-acceleration engine in the Hirudegarn Saga leader slot `[dbs-cardgame.com official card list]` `[ToyWiz product listing]`.
- **Lore connection** — The sword Tapion gives to young Trunks at the end of DBZ: Wrath of the Dragon visually resembles Future Trunks's iconic blade; Tapion says the sword "always belonged to Trunks" — a retroactive in-universe explanation for Future Trunks's weapon that the film clearly intended, though it sits outside main manga canon. This lore connection explains why Trunks specifically leads the Hirudegarn Saga in BT4 rather than serving as a Saiyan Saga leader `[CBR: Where Did Trunks Get His Sword]` `[Wikipedia: Dragon Ball Z: Wrath of the Dragon]`.

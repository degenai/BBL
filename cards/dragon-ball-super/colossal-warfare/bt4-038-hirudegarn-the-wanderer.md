## Trivia

- **Lore: the Wanderer** — The "Wanderer" epithet matches Hirudegarn's canonical characterization: the creature has no intelligence, will, or mission — described as having "no sense of reason" and simply destroying everything in its path. The humanoid demon form here is Hirudegarn's second form (post-metamorphosis), distinguished from the insectoid first form on BT4-037 by its upright bipedal stance and visible demonic face `[Dragon Ball Fandom: Hirudegarn]` `[Villains Wiki: Hirudegarn]`.
- **Film context** — Dragon Ball Z: Wrath of the Dragon (July 15, 1995, Japan) was the thirteenth Dragon Ball Z theatrical film and the final DBZ film produced during the original Z run `[Wikipedia: Dragon Ball Z: Wrath of the Dragon]`. Hirudegarn's final defeat is delivered by Goku's Dragon Fist — a technique that punches entirely through the monster's body — one of the Dragon Fist's most prominent early appearances in the franchise `[Dragon Ball Fandom: Hirudegarn]`.
- **Set design** — BT4-038 is a Common (card no. 038 in Colossal Warfare). The set's design placed lower-rarity Hirudegarn representations as setup pieces for higher-rarity sealed-form mechanics, with the design team noting that the lower-half of Hirudegarn's sealed form sets conditions for playing the Impenetrable Defense version (BT4-037) `[WebSearch: DBS BT4 Colossal Warfare design overview]` `[Bleeding Cool: Colossal Warfare checklist]`.

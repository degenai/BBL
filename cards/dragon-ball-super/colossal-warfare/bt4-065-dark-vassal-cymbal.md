## Trivia

- **Set / lore context** — Cymbal is described in Dragon Ball canon as the weakest combat-capable son of King Piccolo (stronger only than Piano, who served as advisor rather than fighter), assigned to hunt Dragon Balls while Tambourine hunted tournament fighters. His carapace-like reptilian body design in the original anime aligns with the card art's armored green figure in a low crouch. [Dragon Ball Wiki: Cymbal (via WebSearch)]
- **Naming** — Like his siblings, Cymbal's name follows King Piccolo's musical instrument naming convention. Cymbal was tasked with finding Dragon Balls — and was killed by Yajirobe before the other Dark Vassals, making him the first confirmed casualty of the King Piccolo Saga among Piccolo's sons. [Kanzenshuu forum: Piano, Drum and Tambourine-namekians?]
- **Mechanical** — At $0.02 market price, BT4-065 is the lowest-value of the three Dark Vassal commons — consistent with Cymbal's in-canon status as the weakest Dark Vassal in combat. Deck builds using the King Piccolo Bond archetype may run all three for playset consistency. The card's consecutive numbering with Tambourine (064) and Drum (066) signals intentional design as a unit. [card frontmatter market_price; WebSearch: DBS BT4 King Piccolo archetype]

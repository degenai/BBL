## Trivia

- **Lore: the oath** — Tapion and his younger brother Minotia were Konatsian warriors who volunteered to have the two halves of Hirudegarn sealed within their bodies after the creature was bisected by a Guardian's enchanted sword. They were then imprisoned in mystical Music Boxes and dispatched to distant parts of the universe as an eternal containment measure — the "oath" in the card title is that voluntary eternal sacrifice `[Wikipedia: Dragon Ball Z: Wrath of the Dragon]` `[WebSearch: Tapion Minotia Konatsian warriors]`.
- **Signature weapon** — Tapion's Hero's Flute (ocarina) is his defining weapon and visual identifier: playing it keeps Hirudegarn dormant inside him; when he cannot play, the demon surfaces. The flute is the instrument visible in this card art `[Dragon Ball Official Site: Weekly Character Showcase Tapion no. 156]` `[WebSearch: Tapion Konatsian lore]`.
- **Sword connection** — At the end of DBZ: Wrath of the Dragon, Tapion gives his enchanted sword to young Trunks, saying it "always belonged to him" — a widely-cited retroactive nod to Future Trunks's iconic blade from the Android Saga. This connection is non-canonical to the main manga timeline but is the in-universe explanation the film intended, and is why Trunks leads the Hirudegarn Saga in BT4 as a blue Leader `[CBR: Where Did Trunks Get His Sword]` `[GameRant: Dragon Ball: Where Did Future Trunks Get His Sword]`.

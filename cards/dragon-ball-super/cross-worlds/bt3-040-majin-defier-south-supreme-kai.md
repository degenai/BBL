## Trivia

- **Character lore** — South Supreme Kai (Minami no Kaioushin) was canonically the largest and most powerful of the four cardinal Supreme Kais. Kibito Kai later stated outright that South Supreme Kai was the strongest Kai who ever lived. He fought Majin Buu directly, ripping off Buu's arm — the only Supreme Kai to land meaningful physical damage — before being absorbed via sneak attack. `[Dragon Ball Wiki: South Supreme Kai, via WebSearch; CBR: Dragon Ball Z: Does Majin Buu STILL Have Two Supreme Kais Inside Him?]`
- **Card title** — "Majin Defier" names the moment before the fall: South Supreme Kai refusing to yield, the last credible Kai-level obstacle before Buu's power escalated into world-ending territory. The Majin prefix simultaneously signals his captor. The card is a portrait of impending absorption framed as defiance. `[Dragon Ball Wiki: South Supreme Kai, via WebSearch]`
- **Lore permanence** — South Supreme Kai is permanently fused within Majin Buu — unlike other absorbed figures, he was never extracted or restored. His absorption transformed Buu into a massive hulking form exceeding even Super Buu's power. When Kid Buu was destroyed, South Supreme Kai perished with him. `[Dragon Ball Wiki: South Supreme Kai, via WebSearch; Bandai Official: Weekly Character Showcase no. 158, via WebSearch]`
- **Set context** — Cross Worlds (BT3) was the first DBS-TCG set to include Buu Saga Supreme Kais as standalone Battle Cards, expanding the card pool beyond the Frieza Arc and Cell Arc characters that dominated BT1 and BT2. `[dbs-cardgame.com BT3 card list, via WebSearch]`

## Trivia

- **Character lore** — Dr. Gero was the chief scientist and a founding member of the Red Ribbon Army, responsible for designing the Army's entire Android arsenal. After Goku destroyed the Red Ribbon Army as a child, Gero spent years in a secret mountain laboratory building Androids 13 through 20 specifically to kill Goku. He eventually transplanted his own brain into a robotic body, becoming Android 20. `[Dragon Ball Wiki: Dr. Gero, via WebSearch; Wikipedia: Red Ribbon Army]`
- **Apparatus of production** — Gero's Android program is the canonical mass-production-of-warrior-labor example in DBZ: Androids 17 and 18 were human teenagers forcibly converted without consent; Android 16 was built in the image of Gero's deceased son; Cell was assembled from the collected genetic material of dozens of warriors. The program ran for years, operated by tiny insect-like spy drones Gero deployed to observe and data-collect from Goku and his allies. `[Dragon Ball Wiki: Dr. Gero / Cell / Android 17 / Android 18, via WebSearch]`
- **Posthumous operation** — Gero was killed by Androids 17 and 18, whom he could never fully control. However, he had secretly copied his consciousness into an underground supercomputer that continued the Android program after his death — completing Androids 13, 14, and 15, and the design framework for Cell. `[Dragon Ball Wiki: Android 13 / Dr. Gero, via WebSearch]`
- **Set cluster** — BT3 is the only DBS-TCG set to include both Gero (BT3-067, Common) and Android 13 (BT3-070, Uncommon) in the same release, making it the canonical Red Ribbon apparatus cluster within the cross-era narrative of Cross Worlds. `[dbs-cardgame.com BT3 card list, via WebSearch]`

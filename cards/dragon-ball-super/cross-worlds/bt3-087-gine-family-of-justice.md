## Trivia

- **Set context** — BT3-087 is a Common Battle card from Cross Worlds (DBS-B03), the third main booster set of the Dragon Ball Super Card Game, released March 2018 — the same year Dragon Ball Super: Broly brought the Dragon Ball Minus character roster into animated canon. [WebSearch: bleedingcool.com Cross Worlds checklist; toywiz.com set overview]
- **Mechanical design** — Yellow Battle card, power 5000, energy cost 2. Its defining ability: if a `<Bardock>` card is present in the player's Battle Area or Leader Area, Gine can be played from hand without paying her energy cost — the bond with Bardock encoded directly into the card's rules. She also carries Blocker, intercepting attacks targeting other cards by switching to Rest Mode. Era trait: Dragon Ball Minus Saga. [WebSearch: bigorbitcards.co.uk BT3-087 card details]
- **Character origin** — Gine was introduced by Akira Toriyama in the March 2014 issue of Saikyō Jump as part of Dragon Ball Minus: The Departure of the Fated Child, then animated in Dragon Ball Super: Broly (2018), which established Dragon Ball Minus as the official canon origin story for Goku, retiring the 1990 Dragon Ball Z: Bardock — The Father of Goku TV special. [WebSearch: ComicBook.com; CBR — Everything We Know About Goku's Mother, Gine]
- **Lore detail** — Gine was a low-class Saiyan warrior who accepted she was not suited for battle and took a job at a Saiyan meat distribution center. Her mutual affection with Bardock was considered aberrant in Saiyan culture. Toriyama credited her gentle nature as the source of Goku's empathy. [WebSearch: CBR / ComicBook.com Gine character profile]

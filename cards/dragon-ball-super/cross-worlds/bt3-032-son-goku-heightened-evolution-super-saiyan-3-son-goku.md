## Trivia

- **Set context** — Cross Worlds (BT3) released March 9, 2018 as the third main booster for the Dragon Ball Super Card Game, drawing on characters from DBZ, DBS, and movie arcs — the first set to substantially feature Buu Saga-era characters alongside earlier arcs. `[WebSearch: PSA / Bleeding Cool Cross Worlds checklist]`
- **Card design** — BT3-032 is a double-faced Leader card: the front (Son Goku, base form) flips during play to the back (Super Saiyan 3 Son Goku), mirroring the narrative escalation of the Buu Saga. Leader cards are the flagship card type of the DBS-TCG, designed to represent transformation arcs mechanically. `[dbs-cardgame.com card list, via WebSearch]`
- **Character lore** — Super Saiyan 3 is canonically impractical for extended use in the mortal world: the form burns through ki far faster than SSJ2, and Goku stated it should only be used in the Other World where time doesn't flow normally. In the Kid Buu fight, SSJ3 collapsed entirely before Goku could gather energy for the Spirit Bomb. `[Dragon Ball Wiki: Super Saiyan 3, via WebSearch]`
- **Card title** — "Heightened Evolution" names the SSJ3 form's defining contradiction: maximum power output, minimum sustainability. The title appears on the flip side only — the powered-up face — making the escalation structure explicit in the card's own naming convention. `[dbs-cardgame.com BT3-032, via WebSearch]`

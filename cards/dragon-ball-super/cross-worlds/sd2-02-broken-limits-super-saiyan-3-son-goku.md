## Trivia

- **Set context** — SD2-02 is a Starter Rare battle card exclusive to the "THE EXTREME EVOLUTION" Starter Deck (DBS-SD02) from the Cross Worlds era (2018). At a printed power of 25000 and energy cost of 6, it functions as the deck's high-cost finisher in Red color identity. Starter-exclusive cards are not available through booster packs. [WebSearch snippet: dbs-cardgame.com card database; dbzexchange.com listing]
- **Design** — The Super Saiyan 3 transformation first appeared in Dragon Ball Z during the Majin Buu Saga. Toriyama's original concept art (documented in the Daizenshu official encyclopedia) consistently depicted SSJ3 with a pronounced brow ridge and no eyebrows — a deliberate choice to create a feral, immediately distinct silhouette separating it from SSJ and SSJ2. The no-eyebrows feature is among the most discussed design decisions in the franchise. [CBR: "Why DBZ's Super Saiyan 3 Is Missing Eyebrows" via WebSearch; Kanzenshuu SSJ3 design thread via WebSearch]
- **Lore context** — Within Dragon Ball Z, SSJ3 never delivered a decisive victory despite its power. Goku used it to stall Majin Buu but the form's extreme ki drain made it impractical for sustained combat. The card name "Broken Limits" foregrounds SSJ3's narrative role as a threshold-breaker — a form defined by the act of transforming rather than what it wins. [Game Rant: "Dragon Ball Super Saiyan 3, Explained" via WebSearch; Screen Rant SSJ3 analysis via WebSearch]
- **Design** — SSJ3's no-eyebrows silhouette creates a known identification challenge in TCG card art — characters like Gotenks and Goku share the same facial structure in SSJ3. The DBSCG naming convention addresses this by printing the character name in full on every card title, so "Super Saiyan 3 Son Goku" disambiguates what the art cannot. [CBR: "Every Super Saiyan 3 User in Dragon Ball, Explained" via WebSearch]

## Trivia

- **Set context** — Dimension Support Trunks (BT4-102) originates in Colossal Warfare (BT4), the fourth main set of the Dragon Ball Super Card Game, released 2018. BT4 is thematically anchored to the "Future" Trunks / Goku Black / Zamasu arc (Dragon Ball Super's fifth story arc), making it the primary Trunks-as-time-traveler home base in the card game's early run `[TrollAndToad / TCGplayer: BT4-102 Colossal Warfare listing; Bleeding Cool: Colossal Warfare Checklist, via WebSearch]`.
- **Judge Promotion variant** — The copy in the BBL corpus is a Judge Promotion Card (Level 2 stamp), a holofoil promo distributed exclusively to certified DBS Card Game tournament judges — not obtainable through gameplay or prize support. Judge stamp cards are considered collector items and command secondary-market premiums `[Pro Gaming Crew: "Dragon Ball Super Judge Cards & Promo Packs EXPLAINED"; PSA graded listing, via WebSearch]`.
- **Card mechanics** — A Common Black Battle Card with 15000 power and energy cost 3. The Burst skill: if 3 or more cards are in the Drop Area, you may play this card by sending all Drop Area cards to Warp; if played this way, the card returns to Warp at end of turn. On resolution, search the deck for a skill-less Battle Card with cost 2 or less, play it, and grant it Critical for the turn. The "Dimension Support" name reflects the mechanic — a cross-timeline reach into the deck `[DBZ Exchange: BT4-102; WebSearch snippet via DBS cardgame.com results]`.
- **"Future" Trunks arc context** — Future Trunks' return in Dragon Ball Super was the fifth story arc. He arrives in the present timeline fleeing Goku Black, whose existence is a self-fulfilling paradox: Goku's meeting with Zamasu (which happened because Trunks came back to seek help) is what led Zamasu to steal Goku's body. The arc concluded with Future Zeno erasing the entire corrupted future timeline, leaving Trunks without a world to return to `[Kanzenshuu: "Future" Trunks Arc Timeline & Paradox Discussion; TV Tropes: Dragon Ball Super Future Trunks Arc Recap, via WebSearch]`.
- **Character note — Trunks identity** — Research results note the BT4-102 depicted character is identified in some retailer listings as "Trunks: Xeno" (the Dragon Ball Heroes time-patrol version) rather than canonical Future Trunks. The card's Colossal Warfare origin and "Dimension Support" name are consistent with Future Trunks' role in BT4's arc framing, but the "Xeno" designation surfaces in secondary-market data and may indicate an alternative continuity version `[Games Haven TCG / Gathering Point Games listings, via WebSearch — flag for manual review]`.

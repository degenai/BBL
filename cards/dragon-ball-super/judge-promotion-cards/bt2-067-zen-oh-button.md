## Trivia

- **Set context** — Zen-Oh Button (BT2-067) originates in Union Force (BT2), the second main set of the Dragon Ball Super Card Game. The copy in the BBL corpus is a Judge Promotion Card (Level 2) version — a holofoil alternate-art print bearing a "JUDGE" stamp, distributed exclusively to certified tournament judges via official judge packs, never awarded to players `[Pro Gaming Crew: "Dragon Ball Super Judge Cards & Promo Packs EXPLAINED"; TCGplayer Judge Promotion Cards listing, via WebSearch]`.
- **Card mechanics** — The card is a Blue Extra with 0 energy cost and a Counter: Attack skill that switches all of your blue energy to Active Mode. Zero cost means it can be dropped without any resource commitment mid-defense, making it a staple energy-recovery tool in blue control builds `[CoreTCG / DBZ Exchange product listings, via WebSearch]`.
- **Character — Zeno (Zen-Oh)** — Zeno is the Omni-King of the Dragon Ball multiverse, the absolute supreme ruler over all twelve universes and their respective Gods of Destruction. He is depicted as a small, childlike figure in a pink-and-white robe — his physical appearance being in deliberate contrast to his limitless power `[Dragon Ball Wiki / Fandom: Future Zeno; Villains Wiki: Zen-Oh, via WebSearch]`.
- **The button's function** — The button depicted is the "Zeno Button," a call device Goku was given by Zeno to summon him at any moment. Goku used it to call Future Zeno out of the corrupted future timeline during the "Future" Trunks arc. Future Zeno subsequently erased 25 universes including all those corrupted by Infinite Zamasu — the highest confirmed kill count in the franchise `[Dragon Ball Wiki: Future Zeno, via WebSearch]`.
- **Design contrast** — Community discourse frequently notes the deliberate tonal dissonance in Zeno's design: a placid, cute figure who has erased six universes out of displeasure and wiped an entire future multiverse. The card name foregrounds the button rather than Zeno, but Zeno is depicted holding it — the instrument of annihilation framed as a cute prop `[Villains Wiki: Zen-Oh; Screen Rant: "Dragon Ball: Strange Facts About Zeno," via WebSearch]`.

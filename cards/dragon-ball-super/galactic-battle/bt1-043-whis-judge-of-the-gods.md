## Trivia

- **Set context** — Whis is the angel attendant of Beerus (Universe 7's God of Destruction) and the younger brother of Vados, attendant to Universe 6's Champa. Both are children of the Grand Minister, making the Whis-Vados pair the angel half of the U6/U7 twin-dyad structure that mirrors the Champa-Beerus destroyer pairing. Vados trained Whis and claims to be slightly stronger; Whis disputes this. [Dragon Ball Wiki: Vados, via WebSearch snippet]
- **Design** — The angel naming convention derives from alcoholic beverages: Whis = whisky. This pun originated from Toriyama's "virus/beer" mishearing for Beerus and was extended to all angel and GoD names: Vados = Calvados, Champa = Champagne. [Wikipedia: Beerus, via WebSearch snippet]
- **Mechanical** — BT1-043 (Rare) carries Quadruple Strike and a Permanent skill preventing negation of its attacks and skills. Its Auto skill grants the Beerus Leader player an additional full turn. The Beerus-lock on the extra-turn effect mechanically encodes the canon rule that each angel serves their specific paired destroyer god exclusively. [DBZ Exchange / Cool Stuff Inc: BT1-043 listing]
- **Design** — BT1-043 received a Judge Pack promotional reprint (Judge Pack Vol. 14), unusual support for a card from the inaugural set, suggesting long-term organizational endorsement. [Zulus Games: Judge Pack Vol. 14 listing]

- [[destroyer-god-attendant-dyad]] — U7 angel-attendant anchor of the canonical destroyer-god / angel-attendant dyad cosmology that structures every one of Dragon Ball Super's twelve universes (Hakai-shin destroyer god + Angel-class attendant, all twelve angels canonical siblings from the Grand Priest's lineage) per Dragon Ball Wiki's Angel race page; Whis is canonically Universe 7's angel attendant, Vados's younger brother, and per Akira Toriyama's 2013 *Battle of Gods* production interview and Whis's own explicit in-canon statement to Goku and Beerus during the Battle of Gods arc, *canonically stronger than Beerus the destroyer god he serves* — the codified franchise-canon inversion that makes the dyad-structure's "angel attends destroyer god" institutional hierarchy operationally inverted at the labor-knows-more-than-management level. The card's "Judge of the Gods" subtitle and the Auto-skill Beerus-Leader-lock mechanically encode this: the angel attendant's mandate canonically extends *above* the destroyer god he attends, and the card's effect requires the destroyer-god-Leader-pairing because every angel canonically serves their specific paired destroyer god exclusively.

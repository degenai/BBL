## Trivia

- **Set context** — Hit is Universe 6's legendary assassin, known canonically as "Never-Miss Hit" and the "Legendary Hitman," with a perfect contract record spanning over 1,000 years. He was introduced in the God of Destruction Champa Arc as the strongest fighter in Universe 6's tournament roster. [Dragon Ball Wiki: Hit, via WebSearch snippet]
- **Set context** — Hit's signature technique, Time-Skip, creates a momentary pocket dimension storing frozen time, allowing Hit to move freely and strike during an initial window of a tenth of a second. As Hit faces stronger opponents, the technique evolves: the window expands (to half a second) and he gains ability to encase opponents in a "time cage," suspending their movement in frozen time. [Dragon Ball Official Site: Weekly Character Showcase no. 103 — Hit, title confirmed via WebSearch snippet]
- **Design** — Hit's design deliberately inverts typical anime fighter aesthetics: stocky and compact rather than lean and expressive. His economy of movement and unreadable expression are design cues encoding the assassin-not-warrior archetype — a contrast to Universe 7's fighters that functions as visual characterization before he speaks. [Dragon Ball Wiki: Hit character page, via WebSearch snippet]
- **Set context** — BT1-003 places Hit immediately after Vados (BT1-002) in the set's numeric ordering, grouping the U6 cohort together at the front of Galactic Battle's card list. [Trading Card Database: 2017 Dragon Ball Super Card Game BT1 Galactic Battle]

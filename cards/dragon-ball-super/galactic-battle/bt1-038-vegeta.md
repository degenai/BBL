## Trivia

- **Set context** — Galactic Battle (BT1) was the inaugural main booster set for the Dragon Ball Super Card Game, released July 28, 2017. The set contained 114 cards across five rarity tiers (60 Common, 30 Uncommon, 12 Rare, 8 Super Rare, 4 Special Rare), making BT1-038 part of the founding print run of the entire game. `[PSA CardFacts: 2017 Dragon Ball Super Series 1 Galactic Battle / The Games Corner AU]`

- **Saga placement** — BT1-038 is assigned to the Resurrection F Saga era — the arc in which a revived Frieza resumes his vendetta against the Saiyans. In this arc Vegeta trains off-planet with the angel Whis, is killed when Frieza destroys Earth during the climactic battle, and is only saved when Whis rewinds time to let Goku deliver the killing blow. `[Wikipedia: Vegeta]`

- **Mechanical context** — A Blue Battle card with 20,000 Power and 5,000 Combo Power at 3 Blue energy cost. Its sequential neighbor BT1-037 "Assailant Vegeta" (Uncommon, 15,000 Power) carries the [Critical] keyword, causing damage to go to the opponent's Drop Area rather than their hand. The two cards form a small Vegeta common-tier block in BT1's Blue color. `[DBZ Exchange: BT1-038 listing / CoreTCG: BT1-037 listing]`

- **Design density** — At least five distinct Vegeta cards appear in BT1 across every rarity: BT1-028 (Rare Leader, Super Saiyan Blue flip), BT1-036 (God Charge Vegeta), BT1-037 (Assailant Vegeta, Uncommon), BT1-038 (this card, Common), and BT1-064 (Raging Attacker Vegeta, Rare). The spread reflects Vegeta's co-protagonist billing alongside Goku in Dragon Ball Super's inaugural set marketing. `[Bleedingcool: Galactic Battle Checklist / retailer listings via WebSearch]`

- **Lore grounding** — Vegeta is the prince of the Saiyan race and son of King Vegeta; their homeworld was destroyed by Frieza out of fear the race would produce a Super Saiyan. Vegeta survived because he disobeyed Frieza's recall order. His title — Prince of all Saiyans — anchors his identity across Dragon Ball Super: a dynasty without a planet, royalty without subjects. `[Wikipedia: Vegeta]`

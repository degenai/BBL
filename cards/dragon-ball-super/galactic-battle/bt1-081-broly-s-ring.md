## Trivia

- **Lore context** — In Dragon Ball Z: Broly - The Legendary Super Saiyan (1993), Paragus controls his son via a device synced to a mind-control crown on Broly's head, suppressing the Legendary Super Saiyan transformation. Broly ultimately shatters the crown, turns on his father, and the Paragus-engineered scheme collapses. This card's art directly depicts that control relationship [Wikipedia: Dragon Ball Z: Broly - The Legendary Super Saiyan].
- **Mechanical design** — BT1-081 is a green [Field] Extra card (1 green energy, Common). Its [Permanent] skill prevents the Broly Leader's forced-discard mechanic from affecting your own hand — mechanically re-enacting Paragus holding Broly's destructive power in check. It is a must-include in the BT1 Broly archetype [Bandai DBS Strategy: Deck Breakdowns Series 1 - Broly].
- **Paragus character note** — Paragus is a Saiyan survivor who escaped King Vegeta's execution order (Broly's abnormal power level made the infant a perceived threat). He constructed the New Planet Vegeta scheme as revenge against King Vegeta's bloodline, using Broly as the instrument — the control ring is both a safety device and a weapon [Wikipedia: Dragon Ball Z: Broly - The Legendary Super Saiyan].
- **Dramatic function** — The ring's destruction is the film's point of no return: once shattered, Broly's power cannot be contained and Paragus loses all leverage. The card mechanically encodes this fragility — it is a Field card, meaning it can be displaced by any subsequent Field card played [Bandai DBS Card Game rules; WebSearch: retailer card text listings].

## Trivia

- **Lore** — Crusher Ball is Jeice's signature technique, developed from his youth as a Galactic Little League pitcher. Per Dragon Ball Wiki, he performs it as a volleyball spike rather than a baseball pitch: left palm raised to focus the energy sphere, right hand slams it forward. When Jeice uses it against Goku on Namek, Goku deflects it effortlessly — treating Jeice's most powerful move like a beach ball, establishing the extent of the power gap between the Ginyu Force and the Namek-arc Goku `[Dragon Ball Wiki: Crusher Ball]`
- **Mechanics** — BT1-110 is a Yellow Extra (cost 1Y). Its Counter Play skill puts the opponent's attacking Battle Card into Rest Mode, shutting down a declared attack. At 1Y cost it was part of Galactic Battle's Yellow toolkit alongside Bad Ring Laser (BT1-108, later banned) — together they made Yellow one of the strongest defensive colors in the set's early format `[DBS Card Game card list; Big Orbit Cards listing]`
- **Set context** — Galactic Battle (BT1, released July 28, 2017) was the inaugural Dragon Ball Super Card Game booster set. The Ginyu Force — Jeice, Burter, Recoome, Guldo, and Captain Ginyu — featured as a dedicated Yellow faction across character and Extra cards. Crusher Ball appears alongside Jeice's character card and Ginyu Force support in the same 114-card set `[PSA Set Registry: 2017 Dragon Ball Super Card Game]`
- **Community resonance** — The Crusher Ball scene (Goku deflects it like a beach ball) is a frequently cited power-scaling reference point in Dragon Ball discourse, used to calibrate how much Namek-arc Goku outclassed the Ginyu Force tier. The card memorializes that narrative moment in the format `[Dragon Ball Wiki: Crusher Ball; community context]`

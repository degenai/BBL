## Trivia

- **Design** — BT1-007 and BT1-006 give Champa two distinct printings in the same set: BT1-006 (Scheming Champa) depicts aggressive forward lunge; BT1-007 (Manipulating God Champa) depicts the scheming authority pose. The dual-card split reflects Champa's two dominant modes — combatant vs. orchestrator. [Trading Card Database: 2017 Dragon Ball Super Card Game BT1 Galactic Battle]
- **Set context** — The title "Manipulating God" foregrounds Champa's role as tournament architect rather than fighter. In the Champa Arc, Champa does not personally compete — he orchestrates through his universe's fighters (Hit, Cabba, Frost, and others). This is structurally opposite to Beerus, who occasionally participates directly. [Dragon Ball Wiki: Champa, via WebSearch snippet]
- **Set context** — Despite millennia of sibling rivalry, Champa used the Super Dragon Balls after U6's tournament loss to restore Universe 6's Earth, demonstrating care for his universe beneath the scheming exterior. Canon regularly uses this pattern to soften destroyer-god figures through food-and-family motivation. [Dragon Ball Wiki: Champa, via WebSearch snippet]

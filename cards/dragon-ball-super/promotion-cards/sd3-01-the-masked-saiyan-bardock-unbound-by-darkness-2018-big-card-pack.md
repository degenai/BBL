## Trivia

- **Promo and starter deck origin** — SD3-01 is from the DBS Card Game's 2018 Big Card Pack (Starter Deck 3). The double-faced card name "The Masked Saiyan // Bardock, Unbound by Darkness" encodes both states: Bardock under Towa's mask-control (the art shown) and Bardock freed when the mask breaks. This is the canonical dual-identity card structure for the arc. [dbs-cardgame.com official card list, via WebSearch]
- **Mask-reveal canon** — Bardock was pulled from the moment before his death by Frieza's Supernova and brainwashed by Towa using Demon Realm technology. The white mask provided a constant supply of Dark Ki keeping him under her control. His identity is revealed and the mask broken when the Future Warrior defeats him in battle in Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2's story campaign — purging the Dark Ki and freeing him. This is the canonical mask-reveal moment across Xenoverse 2 and Dragon Ball Heroes. [Dragon Ball Wiki: The Masked Saiyan Saga; Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2 Wiki: Masked Saiyan, via WebSearch]
- **Design origin** — The Masked Saiyan character originates in the Dragon Ball Heroes arcade game (Japan) before being adapted into Xenoverse 2 as playable story content. The card depicts the controlled-identity face — dark armor, white mask, black/dark-red ki — not the unmasked reveal. The masked form is framed as the moment of maximum suppression before the break. [Dragon Ball Wiki: The Masked Saiyan Saga; Videogaming Wiki: Masked Saiyan, via WebSearch]
- **Retroactive tragedy** — The Masked Saiyan arc functions as a retroactive extension of Bardock's story beyond his 1990 TV special death. Xenoverse 2 gives him a post-Frieza narrative: enslaved, freed, and eventually fighting alongside the Time Patrol. The mask-concealment / identity-restoration structure is the core dramatic arc — identity as something that can be taken and then reclaimed. [Dragon Ball Wiki: Bardock; Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2 Wiki, via WebSearch]

# Curation modes

Distinct patterns the lair architect (and, eventually, the curator) can compose a Discrete Lair around. Each mode is a *form constraint* — like a sonnet vs. a haiku — that turns the random walk through the tag graph into a coherent shaped bundle.

The lair architect should support a `--mode` parameter once it lands, and each mode below is a recipe.

---

## Haiku set

A 3-line lair. Each line is a thematic phrase composed of cards whose names total a target syllable count.

**Form:** 5 / 7 / 5 syllables across three lines. Total: 17 syllables across the whole lair.

**Important: cards per line is variable, not fixed.**

A line of 5 syllables can be made of:
- 5 cards × 1 syllable each (e.g. *Forest, Bird, Fang, Sword, Mist*)
- 1 card × 2 syllables + 1 card × 3 syllables (e.g. *Dryad / Witching Hour*)
- 1 card × 5 syllables (a single name like *Ravenous Chupacabra*)
- Anything in between that adds to 5

So lines hold **1–5 cards** in the 5-syllable lines and **1–7 cards** in the 7-syllable middle line. Total cards per Haiku Set: roughly 3 (one card per line, max-syllables) up to 17 (all 1-syllable cards).

The line itself is a phrase — it should *read* as a haiku line when the card names are read in sequence. Card names contribute their literal syllables; cards selected for the form should reinforce the line's mood/image rather than fight it.

**Why this matters:** the haiku constraint forces the curator to think about how card names *sound* together, not just what they depict. A Cat Pack is great curation but it's a categorical bundle; a Haiku Set is a *poem*. Different product, different price point, different pitch.

## Other modes (draft / TBD)

These are placeholders — the haiku set proved the form-constraint model works, others should follow.

- **Sonnet set** — 14 cards, syllable-counted into 14 lines. Far longer than a haiku, suits a binder-page or two-page spread.
- **Triptych** — 3 cards, deliberately related, designed to be displayed as a panel. Smaller than a lair, larger than a single card.
- **Mood lair** — no syllable form, but a strict mood/atmosphere filter (cozy, gothic, sublime). Most of the existing concepts (Magic Hour, Customer Service Industry of the Multiverse) live here.
- **Joke lair** — held together by visual or naming joke (Things That Are Pies, comic-relief tag clusters).
- **Cat Pack** — recurring SKU, variable contents, see project spec.

## Why the form matters as a sales pitch

Buyers scrolling cards in a binder don't always know why they should care. **A form is the why.** "These 17 cards are a poem when you read them in order" is a complete pitch on its own. It makes the curation visible. It makes the labor visible. It makes the bundle a *thing* rather than a stack.

This is the parallel venture inside BBL: physical themed binders as showcase pieces, sold or displayed alongside the SKUs, demonstrating to lair buyers what it looks like when curation is taken seriously.

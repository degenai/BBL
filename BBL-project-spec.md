# Bulk Graph Bundler (BBL) — Project Spec

## Core concept

BBL is a curation engine and storefront for trading card game bulk. The product is not the cards — the product is the *curation*. Themed bundles, drawn from the strata of cards corporate resellers ignore (commons, near-worthless rares, off-meta sets), assembled around concepts that cut across set boundaries: mechanics, flavor, art, jokes, vibes.

The thesis: bulk has near-zero exchange-value individually but rich use-value collectively when sorted with intent. Most bulk sellers move it by weight or random-pack. BBL moves it by *theme* — and that theme is the labor that creates the value.

A "graph" in BBL terms is the thematic relationship structure that connects a set of cards into a coherent bundle. The bundler is the system (and curator) that walks that graph and produces a SKU.

## Bundle SKUs (themed one-offs)

Each bundle is a small-batch, named, curated set. Concept-driven, not set-driven.

- **(Zendikar Rising Party + AFR)** — Party mechanic from Zendikar Rising paired with Adventures in the Forgotten Realms. The D&D party trope crossed with the literal MTG Party keyword.
- **Customer Service Industry of the Multiverse** — Cards depicting service workers, retail, hospitality, the working-class cogs of every fantasy/sci-fi setting Magic has touched.
- **Things That Are Pies** — Literal pies in Magic art. The kind of curation no algorithm produces.
- **Magic Hour** — Sunset/twilight art bundle. Pulled across decades of sets on visual cue alone.

Format is open-ended — new bundle concepts can be drafted any time and run as small batches. The naming convention itself is part of the brand.

## Recurring SKU (variable contents)

- **Cat Packs** — cross-game (MTG, Pokémon, Yu-Gi-Oh, Lorcana, Force of Will, etc.), universal recognition, infinite variation, $5 impulse tier. One Force of Will card per pack as ironic filler is brand signature.

The Cat Pack is the always-in-stock entry point. Low risk, high charm, scales with whatever bulk is on hand.

## Political framing (brand voice / content)

The project operates explicitly in the gap between use-value and exchange-value where the bulk lives. Curation IS the labor; small-batch indie work in the strata corporate sellers ignore. Mutual aid logic applied to TCG inventory.

Diamond Legendz is closer to an honest leftist intervention than the "cards shouldn't have value" online discourse, which is overwhelmingly priced-out players misnaming economic frustration as moral preference. The brand voice is: value exists, gatekeeping at the high end is real, and the leftist move is to make the cheap end accessible and curated rather than pretend value isn't part of the system.
